Volunteer firefighters, who make up about 65% of all U.S. fire crews, have dropped from 827,000 in 2008 to 635,000 in 2023. The loss is hitting places hard, with New York reporting its lowest volunteer numbers in four decades. The decline has forced six departments to shut down this year, meaning residents wait longer for help. State officials are pushing for small payments per shift to help keep volunteers afloat.
