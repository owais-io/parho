Nasry “Tito” Asfura, a construction businessman backed by former U.S. President Donald Trump, was announced as Honduras’s new president after a month‑long vote that was marred by fraud claims. He won with 40.27% of the vote, barely beating rival Salvador Nasralla, who got 39.53%. The electoral council declared him winner before all ballots were fully checked, sparking protests from Nasralla, Congress and the OAS. The result is being challenged in court.
