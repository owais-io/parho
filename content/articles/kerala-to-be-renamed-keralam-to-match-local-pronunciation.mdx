The Indian government has approved changing the state’s name from Kerala to Keralam. The new name matches how locals say it in Malayalam, the state’s main language. Kerala, known for its beaches and coconut plantations, has always been called Keralam in Malayalam. Supporters say it respects local culture. The change is part of a trend of renaming cities and states after their original names, like Mumbai from Bombay.
