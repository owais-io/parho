The Guardian published a list of corrections for recent articles. It pointed out mistakes such as calling Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney the head of state instead of the monarch, misspelling the name of photographer Larry Sultan’s widow, and other errors in unrelated pieces about politics, food, and TV. The notice explains how readers can submit complaints or requests for corrections, underscoring the outlet’s commitment to accuracy.
