Keith McDowall was a British journalist who became a key public‑relations figure for both the Conservative and Labour governments. He helped reshape communications in Northern Ireland, created the first confidential line to the public, and later led information services at the CBI and British Shipbuilders. He also ran a successful PR firm, advised unions during the Wapping dispute, and supported his wife Brenda Dean in her rise to Labour peer. He died at 96, leaving a legacy of media insight.
