Australia’s prime minister announced the biggest gun buyback in three decades to follow the Bondi beach attack. The government will collect and destroy hundreds of thousands of firearms, sharing costs with the states. New rules will limit how many guns most people can own and tighten licensing. The move aims to prevent future violence and improve safety after the shooting. Lawmakers are debating stricter gun limits across the country.
