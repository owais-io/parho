A deadly meningitis B outbreak started at a Canterbury nightclub and now has 20 confirmed cases, two deaths, and dozens of hospitalised students. Health officials treated 30,000 people with antibiotics and are vaccinating 5,000 Kent students. The NHS is debating wider free vaccination, while private pharmacies see a surge in demand and a shortage of shots. Experts say the disease is rare but can spread quickly among young adults.
