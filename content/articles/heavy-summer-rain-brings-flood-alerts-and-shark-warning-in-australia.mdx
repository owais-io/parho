Late‑summer storms have dumped heavy rain across Australia, putting South Australia and parts of Victoria on flood watch and sparking a bull‑shark warning in Sydney because murky water can lure sharks. Record rainfall in South Australia, with up to 100 mm in some towns, could trigger flash floods in Adelaide. Victoria saw double‑or‑triple its usual February rain. Queensland also received heavy falls, and scientists warn climate change is making such events more common.
