During the 2024 anti‑immigration riots in the UK, St Anne’s church in Liverpool was on a far‑right hit list. Attackers aimed at the church’s tenant, a refugee charity, and nearby the mosque was also watched. Instead of burning, thousands of locals, priests, Muslims and refugees marched outside the church to defend it. The protest showed that love and unity can beat hate. Community leaders say this united front will help stop future violence.
