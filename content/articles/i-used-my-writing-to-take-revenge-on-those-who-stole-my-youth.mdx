Mircea Cărtărescu, a celebrated Romanian writer, says he used his novels as a form of revenge against people who took his youth. A fan of Nabokov, he loves butterflies and even built his best book, the 1,400‑page *Blinding* trilogy, around a butterfly shape. Born under communism, he has lived in several European cities and is a long‑time Nobel hopeful. His work blends surreal scenes, criticism of his homeland, and a love of religion.
