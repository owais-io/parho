Polanski gave a speech at the New Economics Foundation, standing against the Chancellor’s modest plans. He warned the UK’s economy favours rich people and called for rent controls, a 1‑2% wealth tax and nationalised water services. He wants new fiscal rules, replacing the OBR with “fiscal referees”, and proposes a windfall tax to help families pay energy bills. He avoided credit‑spending promises and criticised Labour’s limited action, hoping to win left‑wing voters.
