Martin Kettle reminds readers that Britain once felt like a broken nation in the 1980s, but it pulled together by letting parties cooperate. He compares today’s low confidence with that past period, saying the lesson is to work together instead of splitting over differences. Kettle believes that if politicians again seek common ground, Britain can overcome current challenges just as it did after the 1980s.
