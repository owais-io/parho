The BBC says it will cut about £600 million from its budget over the next three years because fewer households are paying the licence fee. The move means some jobs will be lost and certain programmes could be axed or cut back. Top bosses say the cuts are needed to keep the broadcaster competitive. Staff and unions are worried about the impact on services and jobs.
