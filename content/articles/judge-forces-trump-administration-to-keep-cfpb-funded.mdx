A federal judge has ordered the Trump administration to keep money flowing to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, which protects consumers from bad banking practices. The agency had been threatened with closure after the new president promised to shut it down. The court said the administration’s plan to cut funding was a made‑up excuse. The decision stops the agency’s dismantlement and keeps it running.
