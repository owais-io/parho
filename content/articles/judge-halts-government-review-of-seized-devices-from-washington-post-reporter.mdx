A federal judge in Virginia stopped the government from searching a reporter’s seized laptops, phone, and watch during a January raid. He warned that letting the government sift through the material could hurt press freedom and public trust. Lawyers for The Washington Post say the seizure destroyed the reporter’s work, while the government says it needs the data to investigate a contractor. The judge will decide in March.
