Experts warn that climate change is putting the world’s food supply at risk. Rising extreme weather is already flattening crop yields, and future models predict up to a 24 % drop in calories produced under high‑emission scenarios. Maize, the biggest crop, could lose up to 24 % of its yield by century‑end. About 600 million people could face food insecurity by 2030. Scientists call for emissions cuts, stronger farming systems, and less food waste.
