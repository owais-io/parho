Ghana’s President John Dramani Mahama used the country’s 69th Independence Day speech to propose that the United Nations officially recognise the trans‑Atlantic slave trade as a crime against humanity and begin a process of reparations. He highlighted support from the African Union, CARICOM and other southern nations, and explained that acknowledging the past is the first step toward fixing long‑lasting inequalities through investment in education, health, culture and jobs.
