Prison governors say inmates with long or whole‑life sentences are increasingly attacking high‑profile prisoners. They have nothing to lose, so they seek status by harming famous child killers or paedophiles. The Ministry of Justice is reviewing the high‑security estate after several homicides, including the death of the Soham killer at Frankland. Staff shortages, overcrowding and a lack of experienced officers fuel tension, raising concerns about safety for all inmates.
