The Guardian article follows Penny Lancaster, a 50‑year‑old ex‑model who now works as a special constable to cope with past trauma. It points out that many 50‑to‑64‑year‑olds are on sickness benefits, often due to health issues like menopause, and feel left behind at work. Politicians, especially Labour, must focus on decent jobs and good care for this group, not just on attacking immigrants. Reform is gaining votes by spreading fear, but real solutions are needed.
