Diabetes drugs may help keep anxiety and depression from getting worse. A study of 95,000 patients with depression or anxiety who took diabetes medicines between 2009 and 2022 found that semaglutide cut the risk of mental‑health decline by 42%, and liraglutide by 18%. Other similar drugs didn’t show the same benefit. Experts say the findings are reassuring but don’t prove the drugs treat mood disorders on their own, and warn of higher preterm birth risk when taken during early pregnancy.
