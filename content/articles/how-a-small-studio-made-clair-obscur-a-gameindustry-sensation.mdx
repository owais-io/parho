Guillaume Broche started “Clair Obscur” as a one‑man demo while working at Ubisoft. He later teamed up with a handful of friends found on Reddit, SoundCloud, and ArtStation. Together they turned the demo into a Belle Époque‑style RPG that sold over two million copies and earned 12 award nominations. The game’s emotional story, written from personal family experiences, helped players cope with loss, and the studio’s tight budget and indie spirit made it a surprisingly big success.
