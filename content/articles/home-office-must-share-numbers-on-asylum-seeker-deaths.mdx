Human rights groups want the Home Office to publish how many asylum seekers die in its care, instead of hiding the data behind Freedom of Information requests. In 2024, 51 people died in Home Office accommodation, up from 30 the year before. Critics say the government should release the figures each quarter like it does for prisons and hospitals. Campaigners fear that keeping the numbers hidden hurts families and stops needed improvements.
