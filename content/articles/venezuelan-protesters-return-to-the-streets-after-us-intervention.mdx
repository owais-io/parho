After US forces removed Maduro, students and activists in Caracas and other cities marched for freedom, the release of political prisoners and a full democratic transition. The first rallies in weeks were shown on Venezuelan TV and welcomed foreign journalists. The interim government freed some prisoners and allowed protests, but many opposition leaders stay in jail and no election date is set. The US pledged investment, but skeptics fear the reforms could stall.
