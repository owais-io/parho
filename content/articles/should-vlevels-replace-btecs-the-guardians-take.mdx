The UK government paused cuts to BTecs after college leaders pushed back. But new V‑levels, meant to simplify post‑16 choices, are being rolled out instead. Experts worry the rush could hurt students, especially those who might otherwise study A‑levels or go to university. With fewer T‑levels taking off, there’s fear of more young people becoming NEETs. The debate is on whether a single A‑level‑style system will really help.
