An everyday birdwatching story: A male great spotted woodpecker is using the plastic cover on an electrical junction box on a roadside as a drum. The narrator hears its rhythmic pecks each morning, turning the box into a percussion instrument. The bird keeps coming back, using the noisy surface to attract mates and mark territory. The article celebrates this quirky behaviour and the bird’s adaptation to avoid injury while striking.
