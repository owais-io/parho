In a 2016 Fox News interview, former defense secretary Pete Hegseth said U.S. soldiers must refuse orders that are illegal. That comment is being cited now as the Biden‑era government carries out strikes on suspected drug boats off Venezuela and Colombia, killing dozens. Critics call the attacks unlawful, while Hegseth and some Democrats argue the military should obey. The debate over who can give or refuse orders has reignited over recent killings.
