A sewage plant in Eastbourne broke a screen that held millions of plastic beads, releasing them into the sea. The beads have drifted to Camber Sands and other parts of the Sussex coast, ruining beaches and threatening wildlife. Residents, fishermen and tourism businesses worry about the environmental damage and the impact on visitors. The water company will pay for cleanup, but some costs will come from customers’ bills. Officials hope the cleanup will protect the beach and local economy.
