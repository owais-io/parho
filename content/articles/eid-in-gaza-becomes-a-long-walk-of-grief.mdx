Eid, the holiday that usually brings family gatherings, turned into a long, sorrowful walk for people in Gaza. After months of conflict, families visit relatives, but many homes are empty, filled with grief over lost loved ones. The author describes how visits meant celebration but felt like a procession through death, with broken homes, missing people, and the quiet weight of mourning. The day ended with quiet remembrance.
