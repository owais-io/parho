John Barkham, an 82‑year‑old ecologist, was a pioneering teacher at the University of East Anglia. He introduced student‑led syllabuses and championed kindness in the classroom. Despite a difficult childhood, he earned a first at Birmingham and a PhD on Cotswold woodlands. Later, he helped wildlife trusts and ran community allotments. Even in illness, he stayed positive, loved birds, and left a legacy of caring education.
