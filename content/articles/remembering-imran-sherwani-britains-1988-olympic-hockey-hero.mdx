Imran Sherwani, who died at 63 from Alzheimer’s, was a key player in Great Britain's 1988 Olympic hockey team that won gold. He scored twice in the final, sparking famous BBC commentary. After a long club career and work as a police officer, newsagent, financial adviser, teacher, and coach, he also carried the Olympic torch in 2012. He left behind his wife Louise and three sons.
