Matthew Doyle, a former communications chief for Labour leader Keir Starmer, was nominated for a life peerage in December. After it emerged he had campaigned for a friend who was later convicted of holding indecent images of children, the party withdrew his membership in the House of Lords. Starmer said Doyle failed to give a full account of his actions, prompting calls for stricter peer‑appointment rules.
