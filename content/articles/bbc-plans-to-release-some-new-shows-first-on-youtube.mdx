The BBC is considering putting some of its new programmes on YouTube before they air on its own services. This move would help reach younger audiences, who mainly watch on the platform. Politicians and the regulator Ofcom want the BBC to share more content online, while the BBC wants YouTube to give its shows more visibility. The deal could raise questions about the licence fee and how it might affect other BBC services.
