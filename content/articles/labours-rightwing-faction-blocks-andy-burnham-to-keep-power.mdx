Keir Starmer’s right‑wing inner circle is blocking Andy Burnham from standing in a Greater Manchester by‑election, saying he could threaten Labour’s hold on the area. The move shows the faction prefers to keep control even if it means hurting the party’s chances. They’ve also snubbed other left‑leaning hopefuls, fearing progressive rivals could win seats. In short, power‑driven politics trump progress in the current Labour struggle.
