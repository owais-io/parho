Venezuela's former president Nicolás Maduro was taken to a Manhattan court after a U.S. raid that rescued him from Caracas. He walked into the courtroom in ankle cuffs, saying "Happy New Year" to the gallery. Maduro and his wife, Cilia Flores, were arraigned on narcoterrorism charges from the Trump‑era justice department. In court, he denied the accusations, calling himself a "kidnapped president." The judge, Judge Hellerstein, set a future conference for the case.
