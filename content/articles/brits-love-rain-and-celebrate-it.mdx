Brits have long treated rain as a normal part of life. From funny sayings to tea‑towel souvenirs, the UK’s culture turns wet weather into a shared joke. Even when rivers flood or farms struggle, people shrug and say things like “the garden needed it” or “no bad weather, only wrong clothes.” The article explains how weather talk becomes a ritual, a way to stay positive and even romantic about rain.
