AstraZeneca announced it will invest £11 billion ($15 billion) in China by 2030, focusing on new research centres and bigger factories. The move comes after the company paused a £200 million expansion in Cambridge and stopped a £450 million vaccine plant near Liverpool. The UK Prime Minister said the Chinese investment would boost AstraZeneca and support UK jobs. The company is also under Chinese investigations over tax and data‑privacy issues.
