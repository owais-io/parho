Gerry Gable died at 88 after a life of fighting fascism in Britain. He founded the anti‑fascist magazine Searchlight, which uncovered the secrets and crimes of far‑right groups like the National Front and the BNP. Gable’s team used undercover agents, gathered evidence, and helped activists expose Nazi symbols and violent plans. His work kept Britain’s far‑right from gaining power and inspired similar campaigns across Europe and the U.S.
