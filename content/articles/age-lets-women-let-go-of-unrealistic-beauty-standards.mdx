Zoya Patel recalls being shy in a school pool room because of her body, watching older women who didn’t care about how they looked. She explains how that moment made her feel judged by beauty rules that last into adulthood. Now, at 36, she feels free to enjoy life without worrying about appearance, even though she still feels the pull of those standards. The piece argues that age can bring freedom from chasing an impossible ideal.
