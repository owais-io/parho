France’s 35,000 local elections on March 15‑22 will test the far‑right National Rally (RN) and the alliances that could stop it. The RN is fielding a record 650 lists and hopes second‑round deals can win key cities like Toulon, Nîmes and Marseille. Macron’s centrists, the Socialists and the left will try to unite against the RN. Results in Paris, Nice and Le Havre will decide if the RN can grow beyond its current strongholds.
