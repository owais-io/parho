Ireland’s fly‑half (No 10) position has become a revolving door, stirring fans and critics alike. After dropping star Tony Ward in 1979, the spot has seen a string of contenders—Ollie Campbell, Ronan O’Gara, Johnny Sexton, and now Jack Crowley and Sam Prendergast. Coach Andy Farrell faces pressure to choose the right fit while dealing with fan backlash, injuries, and the need to keep the team focused. This ongoing battle shows how a single spot can ignite national debate.
