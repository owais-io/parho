Worcestershire county council, led by the Reform party, has been approved to raise its council tax by up to 9% in April – the largest increase in England. The move is part of a wider plan that lets several councils lift rates above the usual 5% cap. Meanwhile, the government will clear about £5 bn of debt that local councils owe for special educational needs and disability services, helping many avoid bankruptcy.
