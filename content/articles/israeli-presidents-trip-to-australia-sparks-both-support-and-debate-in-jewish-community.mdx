Israeli President Isaac Herzog travelled to Australia after the Bondi beach tragedy. The visit was welcomed by many Jews who felt comforted by his words of solidarity, but some community members worried it would deepen divisions. Critics feared the trip could fuel anti‑Palestinian sentiment and stir antisemitic tropes about dual loyalty. Inside Australia, protests and celebrations ran side by side, reflecting a split in attitudes toward Israel and its policies.
