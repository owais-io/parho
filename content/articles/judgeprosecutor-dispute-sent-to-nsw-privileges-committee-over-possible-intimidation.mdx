Judge Penelope Wass used a parliamentary‑privilege statement to criticize the NSW Director of Public Prosecutions, Sally Dowling. In turn, prosecutors have tried to have Wass recuse herself from trials, citing her criticism as bias. An upper‑house inquiry has asked the powerful privileges committee to check if the ODPP abused privilege and whether the move intimidates witnesses. The dispute could affect future cases and raises questions about court and parliament boundaries.
