David Lammy, the justice secretary, said the UK will no longer be a safe haven for dirty money. He revealed a new plan that will give extra money to an anti‑corruption police unit, set up a global summit on illicit finance, and add £15 million to the City of London’s corruption squad. The plan also targets “enablers” who help dictators hide money, calls for tougher rules on political donations, and says some cases will be tried by specialist judges.
