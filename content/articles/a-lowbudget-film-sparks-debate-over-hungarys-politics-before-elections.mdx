Feels Like Home is a cheap Hungarian thriller that came out just before the April 12 parliamentary elections. Audiences read it as a story about an abusive family, which many saw as a stand‑in for the country’s authoritarian leader, Viktor Orbán. The film got no state money and was promoted by the actors’ own social media. Critics say it’s a high‑quality movie that reflects real political tension, and it’s helping a new wave of independent Hungarian films.
