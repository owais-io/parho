A brutal snowstorm hit Petropavlovsk‑Kamchatsky in eastern Russia, dumping over 1.8 metres of snow and creating drifts over 3 metres high. Cars were buried, and two people died when a roof collapsed. Snow fell for almost a week, blocking roads for several weeks. At the same time, Storm Harry dumped record rain across the Mediterranean, flooding Tunisia, Algeria, Libya and parts of Italy, killing several people.
