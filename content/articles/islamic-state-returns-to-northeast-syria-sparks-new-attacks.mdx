After years of being pushed out, the Islamic State is re‑emerging in Syria’s Deir ez‑Zor province. Its leader released a long speech calling for attacks on the government. Nine new raids hit checkpoints in the northeast, including a gunfight that killed four security officers. Local residents feel uneasy because old IS supporters still live nearby. The group is trying to win hearts by hiding its past brutality, hoping to recruit frustrated young people.
