Thames Water’s lenders have proposed a £10bn rescue plan that gives the company a cash injection of £3.35bn and adds £6.65bn in debt. The deal would wipe out about 30 % of its senior debt and all junior debt, while paying off fines for pollution leaks. It would stop dividends until 2035, keep water bills steady and requires regulator approval to avoid nationalisation.
