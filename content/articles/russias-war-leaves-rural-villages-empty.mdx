In a small Komi village, most young men have gone to fight in Ukraine, turning the once busy streets into almost deserted lanes. The war pulls people out of farms, shops and schools, leaving fewer hands to work. Prices rise, and families mourn lost sons. Russia’s army keeps recruiting from poorer rural areas because a military contract pays far more than local jobs. The front’s high casualties are outpacing new recruits, threatening the war effort.
