A woman in Sydney was arrested during a protest for wearing a jacket that said “globalise the intifada.” Police told her the slogan was illegal, but no law actually bans it. She was released after about an hour with no charge. The state wants to add the phrase to hate‑speech laws. Supporters argue it’s a call for resistance, while opponents see it as violent. The incident sparked debate over free speech and policing.
