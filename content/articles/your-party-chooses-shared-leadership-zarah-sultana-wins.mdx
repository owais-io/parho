A new left‑wing group, led by former Labour boss Jeremy Corbyn, decided to run on a shared‑leadership model after a close vote. Zarah Sultana, an ex‑Labour MP who has fought with Corbyn, won a key role. Members voted 51.6% to 48.6% for a collective executive with a chair, deputy chair and spokesperson. The party also allowed members to join other left parties. The decision comes amid internal fights and a shaky start.
