NatWest has agreed to pay £2.7 bn to acquire Evelyn Partners, one of Britain’s biggest wealth‑management firms. The deal adds about 2,400 staff and £69 bn of client assets to the bank’s portfolio, boosting its private‑banking reach. NatWest beat Barclays in the race for the company, which was up for sale by its owners Permira and Warburg Pincus. The purchase signals a push to grow wealth‑management services after NatWest returned to private ownership last year.
