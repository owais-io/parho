Friends have very different rules for wearing reading glasses. Some think you should always take them off, others keep them on the head, and some hate any visible eyewear at all. Zoe jokes about how these quirks reveal personality traits—like laziness or vanity. The essay shows that even small habits can cause quirky disagreements and that nobody actually changes how they wear glasses.
