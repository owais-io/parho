Labor ministers introduced urgent changes to Australia’s immigration laws, letting the government block temporary visa holders from certain regions, mainly Iran, from entering. The move follows the Middle East war and aims to protect the immigration system. Critics argue the bill is unfair and targets one group, while supporters say it closes loopholes. The debate has highlighted tensions over asylum seekers and the Iranian women’s soccer team.
