JPMorgan Chase has begun a trial that compares the hours junior investment bankers say they work with computer‑generated estimates based on their daily digital activity. The bank will send each junior a report showing any gaps between the two. The move aims to raise awareness, improve wellbeing and spark open talks about workload. The firm already limits junior staff to 80 hours a week and is rolling the tool out across its investment division.
