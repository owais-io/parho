The UK government has made sweeping changes to the asylum system. Refugees will now have their status reviewed every 30 months and could be sent back if their home country is no longer unsafe. New student visas for four countries have been halted, and a pilot scheme offers up to £40,000 to families in hotels to return voluntarily, with removal powers on the table. Support for asylum seekers is being trimmed, and the time needed to gain settlement status has been doubled to 10‑20 years.
