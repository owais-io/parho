The UK government has sharply cut or shut down several climate‑aid programmes aimed at protecting nature in poorer countries. Funding for the Biodiverse Landscapes Fund, the Coast and Pact projects, and even the £500 million Blue Planet Fund has been reduced or halted. The cuts were made without clear explanations, and transparency about how money is spent has been lacking. Experts say the move hurts local communities and weakens the UK’s climate commitments.
