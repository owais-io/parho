The government plans to cut Britain's road deaths by 65% by 2035 and is considering lowering the drink‑drive limit in England and Wales from 35 to 22 micrograms of alcohol per 100 ml of breath, matching Scotland’s level. Transport Minister Lillian Greenwood says studies show the limit change in Scotland didn’t hurt pubs, so rural pubs shouldn’t close. She urges people to choose public transport or a designated driver when going out.
