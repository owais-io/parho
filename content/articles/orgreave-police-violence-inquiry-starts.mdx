The UK government has officially launched an inquiry into the police violence that happened at the Orgreave coking plant during the 1984‑85 miners’ strike. The inquiry will look at the clashes, the later prosecutions of 95 miners and the evidence that was later found to be unreliable. A panel of experts will gather information and deliver a report by spring 2028. The campaign for truth says the investigation must be shaped by miners’ experiences.
