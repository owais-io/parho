A short DHS shutdown began Saturday, leaving about 13% of federal civilian workers, including TSA staff, unpaid. The pause mainly affects airport security and could slow travel, but most other agencies keep running. Congress is on a 10‑day recess, though talks continue. The break follows a dispute over new rules for immigration agents after fatal shootings, with Democrats demanding more oversight and Republicans opposing the changes.
