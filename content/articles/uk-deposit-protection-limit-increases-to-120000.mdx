Starting Dec. 1, the UK will protect up to £120,000 of a person's money if their bank or building society fails, up from £85,000. That's a 41% jump, the biggest rise in eight years. The Financial Services Compensation Scheme will pay customers back within about a week. Temporary big balances—like a house sale or inheritance—now get up to £1.4 million for six months. Most people save far less than the new limit, so the change mainly reassures savers.
