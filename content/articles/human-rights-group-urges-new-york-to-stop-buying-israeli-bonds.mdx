The human rights group Dawn sent a 26‑page letter to New York officials, saying that buying Israeli bonds breaks international law and fiduciary duties. They warn the state and city could face legal, ethical, and financial risks. The message comes after a split in New York politics, with some officials wanting to stop, others wanting to keep investing. The debate reflects growing U.S. calls to divest from Israel following the Gaza war.
