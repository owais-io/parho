Antonio Tejero, the former civil guard who stormed Spain’s parliament in 1981, has died at 93. He led the brief armed takeover that threatened the young democracy, holding lawmakers hostage for 18 hours. After the coup was stopped by King Juan Carlos, Tejero was jailed and later released in 1996. He spent his later years painting and gardening, but his legacy remains tied to Spain’s struggle between dictatorship and democracy.
