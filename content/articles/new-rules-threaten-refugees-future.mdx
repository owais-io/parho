The letters argue that the Home Secretary’s new rule to re‑check asylum status every 30 months will leave refugees in constant uncertainty, making it hard to buy homes, keep jobs or plan schooling. Writers say the policy hurts the country’s moral reputation and could push Labour supporters to quit. They stress that refugees need a stable, compassionate system rather than periodic reviews that risk turning them vulnerable and undocumented.
