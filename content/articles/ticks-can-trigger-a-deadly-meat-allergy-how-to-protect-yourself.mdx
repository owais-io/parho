Jeremy Webb’s son died from a sudden allergic reaction to meat after a tick bite, a rare but deadly link now recognized in New South Wales. The case shows that two or more tick bites can trigger an alpha‑gal allergy that makes people react to mammalian meat, even airborne particles. Prevention means avoiding tick bites with repellents, long clothing, and quick tick removal. If you suspect an allergy, seek an immunology test and carry an auto‑injector.
