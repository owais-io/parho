Actor Timothée Chalamet said no one cares about opera or ballet, sparking a backlash from performers, fans and arts organisations. Opera houses and ballet companies pointed out that audiences still turn up, even though ticket prices are high and younger people are less familiar with these art forms. Experts say the key to survival is making shows more accessible and exciting for young people, not dismissing them as irrelevant.
