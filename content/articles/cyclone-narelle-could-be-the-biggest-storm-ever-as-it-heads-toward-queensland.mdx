North Queensland faces what could be the biggest tropical cyclone in recent memory, as Narelle, a powerful storm that formed near the Solomon Islands, heads toward the Cape York coast. Meteorologists say it will likely hit the town of Coen as a Category‑4 cyclone with winds of 160‑200 km/h. The state has evacuated resorts, moved vulnerable people out of the danger zone, and emergency teams are on standby to deal with flooding and high tides.
