On 7‑9 January, a fire that started in Ravenswood blew across Victoria, destroying 289 houses. Six families from Harcourt and nearby towns had to leave in moments as winds changed. They packed essentials—photos, important documents, food, a battery‑powered radio, and even a chainsaw—after learning how quickly the fire spread. The stories show how pre‑planning, prioritizing safety over possessions, and listening to fire‑service warnings can save lives.
