Labour’s chancellor, Rachel Reeves, gave her spring statement and said the UK economy was good. She ignored data that showed growth was slipping and unemployment was rising. Reeves also said the Middle East war wasn’t a concern. The speech had no new spending or tax plans, which left the opposition scratching their heads. The overall tone was confusing, with a mix of confident claims and lack of detail.
