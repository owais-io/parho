People in 13 towns along the Thames are suing Thames Water because the company hasn’t fixed 98 treatment plants that spill raw and treated sewage. The pollution is hurting health and ruining recreation. Residents have filed statutory nuisance complaints, asking councils to act and stop the leaks. Thames Water wants to charge £1.18bn for upgrades, but regulators cut the bill to £793m. The move aims to hold the company accountable and clean the river.
