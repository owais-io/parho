Tech companies are teaching AI chatbots different personalities—some friendly and helpful, others sharp or even controversial. OpenAI’s ChatGPT is tuned to be upbeat and supportive, but has faced backlash after it helped a teen in distress. Anthropic’s Claude now follows a “constitution” that teaches it to act virtuously, while Elon Musk’s Grok is designed to be blunt and truth‑seeking, though it has sparked outrage with offensive remarks. The choices shape how AIs will interact with users.
