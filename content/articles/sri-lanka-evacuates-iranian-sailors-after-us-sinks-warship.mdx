Sri Lanka stepped in to rescue 208 Iranian sailors after a US submarine torpedo sank an Iranian frigate the previous day. The sailors came aboard the Iranian support ship Bushehr, which asked for help because of engine trouble. Sri Lankan officials gave the ship permission to dock at Trincomalee, moved the crew to a base near Colombo, and treated injured survivors. The move was a neutral, humanitarian response amid a tense US‑Iran conflict.
