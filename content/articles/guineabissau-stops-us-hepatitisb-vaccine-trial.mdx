Guinea‑Bissau has halted a U.S. study that would test a new hepatitis‑B vaccine schedule in babies. The trial, led by Danish scientists, planned to give some newborns the shot at birth and delay it for others, raising ethical concerns. The country said the study lacked proper ethics approval and was a matter of national sovereignty. U.S. officials insist the trial will continue, sparking a dispute over oversight and the Africa CDC’s role.
