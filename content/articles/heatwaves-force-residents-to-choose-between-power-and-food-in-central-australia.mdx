Since summer began, the heat in central Australia has made it hard for families in town camps around Alice Springs to keep their homes powered. With prepaid electricity meters, a single drop in credit cuts off lights and fridges. People like Vanessa Davis have to decide whether to spend money on power or on food. Experts say the cost is rising and the number of outages is high. Governments are considering rules to stop disconnections when it’s over 40 °C.
