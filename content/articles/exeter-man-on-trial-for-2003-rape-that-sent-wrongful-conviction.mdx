Paul Quinn, 51, is on trial in Manchester for raping and attempting to strangle a woman in 2003. New DNA tests now link him to the crime, overturning a 17‑year wrongful conviction of Andrew Malkinson. Quinn denies the charges, claiming his DNA was found by mistake. The case shows how new science can correct past injustices and is still ongoing. The trial will decide whether Quinn is guilty or not.
