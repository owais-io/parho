Universal Studios has been given the green light to build its first UK theme park on a former brickworks site in Bedford. The government approved the plans after a special deal that skips normal local planning checks. The park, expected to open in 2031, could host some of Europe’s tallest rides and attract over 8 million visitors a year. It will create about 20,000 construction jobs and 8,000 permanent roles, boosting the local economy by billions.
