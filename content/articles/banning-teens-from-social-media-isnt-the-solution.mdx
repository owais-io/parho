The article argues that while social media can harm young people, a blanket ban on those under 16 won’t solve the problem. It says tech companies drive anxiety and self‑harm for profit, and that parents or teens can’t simply turn off a phone to fix it. Instead, we need real change at the source—regulating the companies themselves, not just limiting kids’ screen time.
