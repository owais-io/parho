The letter argues that Britain’s new “affordable” housing is only a theory because the only way a home can be cheap is if you pay for a costly commuter season ticket. The writer points out that these towns are just spill‑over from big cities, offering cheap prices but no proper infrastructure. The real fix would be more homes, better planning, and not just encouraging people to move far away.
