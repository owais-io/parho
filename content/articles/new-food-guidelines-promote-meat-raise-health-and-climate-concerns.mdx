Robert F. Kennedy Jr.’s new Dietary Guidelines put red meat and cheese higher on the food pyramid than plant proteins, confusing experts. Health advocates say the picture contradicts the written rules that say saturated fats should stay under 10% of calories. Environmental groups warn that more meat would harm the planet through deforestation and methane. Critics worry the change could push schools and the public toward unhealthy, high‑fat diets.
