Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni is pushing a referendum that would change Italy’s courts and prosecutors. Supporters say it will make the justice system fairer, but critics say it will let the government control judges and slow investigations. The campaign has been heated, with Meloni attacking judges and even using a rapper’s podcast to reach young voters. The result will test her popularity before the 2027 general election and could reshape how Italy’s judiciary works.
