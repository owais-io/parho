Australia’s new environment minister, Murray Watt, plans to add about 524,000 square kilometres of ocean to the country’s marine protected areas, taking the total to 30% of the coast. He will review 44 national marine parks, starting with the Coral Sea and some islands, and hopes to finish the work by 2028. The move aims to stop fishing and drilling in these zones and to heal the political rift left by the former government.
