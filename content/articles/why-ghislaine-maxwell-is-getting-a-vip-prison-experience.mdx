Ghislaine Maxwell, serving 20 years for her part in Jeffrey Epstein’s sex‑trafficking scheme, has been moved to a federal prison in Texas that treats her like a celebrity. She gets unlimited toilet paper, special meals, tech, and even puppies. Critics say her transfer shows a two‑tier legal system that favors the wealthy. The article also notes how many women in U.S. jails, often low‑income, get far harsher conditions.
