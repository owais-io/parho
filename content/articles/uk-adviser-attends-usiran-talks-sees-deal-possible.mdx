UK adviser Jonathan Powell attended the final Geneva talks between the US and Iran and thought Iran had offered a real deal. He said there was still time for diplomacy, not a war. Two days later the US and Israel launched a strike on Iran, which the UK said was too early. The article shows how Britain’s involvement in the talks made it reluctant to support the attack, stressing the tension in UK‑US relations.
