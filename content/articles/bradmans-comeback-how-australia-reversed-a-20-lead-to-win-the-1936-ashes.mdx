In 1936, England led the Ashes series 2‑0 and were on the brink of victory. Australia’s captain, Don Bradman, turned the tide with a brilliant 270‑run innings at Melbourne amid rain‑slick wickets. His team won the third Test, level the series, and then captured the final two matches to claim the Ashes. The story shows how determination and skill can overturn a daunting deficit.
