Seattle’s defense, called the Dark Side, shut down the Patriots and won 29‑13 in the Super Bowl. GM John Schneider rebuilt the unit by trading away quarterback Russell Wilson and adding key linebackers, defensive linemen, and pass rushers. Coach Mike Macdonald’s aggressive game plan led to 11 sacks and a record‑breaking performance. The victory cements Seattle’s status as a title‑winning team with a hard‑hitting defense.
