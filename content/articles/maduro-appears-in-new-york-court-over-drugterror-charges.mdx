Venezuela’s former president Nicolás Maduro appeared in a Manhattan federal court on Thursday for charges of “narco‑terrorism.” He and his wife are accused of running a corrupt government that used state power to protect drug trafficking. Maduro’s lawyers say the U.S. is blocking Venezuelan government money from paying his legal fees, a move they call a violation of his rights. The case has sparked protests and comes amid a reshaping of Venezuela’s leadership after Maduro’s capture.
