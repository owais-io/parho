A UK charity, Sex Matters, tried to stop trans people from using Hampstead Heath swimming ponds the way they identify, claiming it was sex discrimination. The High Court threw out the case, saying the right to bring such a claim belongs in a lower court. The ruling keeps the current, trans‑inclusive arrangements in place, a move supported by 86% of people in a city-wide consultation.
