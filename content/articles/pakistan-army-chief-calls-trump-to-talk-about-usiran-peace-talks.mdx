Pakistan's army chief, Asim Munir, called former President Donald Trump to discuss possible talks between the United States and Iran. Pakistani officials say Islamabad could host a meeting, but the venue isn’t confirmed. The U.S. may send Vice‑President J.D. Vance to negotiate, instead of Trump’s Middle‑East envoys. Iran’s officials are wary of U.S. intentions and want a different negotiator. Talks could help end the war and lower oil prices.
