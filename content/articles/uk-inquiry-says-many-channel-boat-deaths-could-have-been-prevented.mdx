The inquiry into the 2021 Channel boat tragedy found that many of the 33 people who died could have survived if better safety and rescue steps had been taken. It blamed overcrowded, poorly equipped boats, faulty life‑jackets, coastguard staff shortages and a delayed response. The report calls for ending dangerous small‑boat crossings, more rescue staff, better equipment and quicker, smarter search tactics.
