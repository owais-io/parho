After 36 years, Iran’s Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei died, sparking both joy and sorrow across the country. In Tehran’s squares, some people wept and chanted slogans, while others danced, blew horns and set off fireworks. The split reaction shows how the protests that killed dozens of citizens last year split the nation. Many mourn for the victims, but others see the death as a relief and a chance for change.
