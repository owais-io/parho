Netflix’s upcoming Wuthering Heights adaptation by Emerald Fennell has drawn criticism over its casting. The film replaces the book’s mysterious, ethnically ambiguous Heathcliff with white Australian Jacob Elordi, sparking debates about representation. Fennell says the choice reflects her teenage vision of the character. Critics argue that casting a white actor erases the story’s themes of class and otherness, while defenders note the novel’s public‑domain status allows creative freedom.
