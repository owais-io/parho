The U.S. has slapped sanctions on eight people and companies that helped hire Colombian ex‑soldiers to fight for Sudan’s Rapid Support Forces, a group accused of genocide. The sanctions target recruiters, financiers, and a few key workers linked to the network. Colombia’s foreign ministry had already apologized after a report exposed the soldiers’ involvement. The move aims to cut off money and support for the RSF and curb the use of Colombian mercenaries abroad.
