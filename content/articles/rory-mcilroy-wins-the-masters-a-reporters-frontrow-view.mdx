On a cool April morning in Augusta, I woke up to the sight of a green jacket on a chair. That meant Rory McIlroy had just won the Masters, his fourth major and the last piece of a career grand slam. I’d followed him for years, so watching his final shots felt like a personal victory. The win filled me with joy, tears, and a sense of historic triumph.
