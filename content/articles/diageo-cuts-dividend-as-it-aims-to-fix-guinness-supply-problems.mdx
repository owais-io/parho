Diageo, the world’s biggest spirits maker, lowered its dividend by half and pulled back sales and profit forecasts again in just four months. New boss Dave Lewis says the company is cutting costs and investing in its London bottling plant to ease Guinness supply problems. Weak demand in the US and China, rising consumer costs, and slimmer drinks per visit are hurting sales, so Diageo plans smaller packs to keep people buying.
