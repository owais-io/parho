Anna Gillett (88) was a lifelong feminist who spent 24 years at the Council of Europe working on language learning for migrants and school programs. She later led the social policy steering committee, tackling social exclusion, family, elderly, and children issues across Europe. Born in Rugby, educated at Cambridge, she married a lawyer, moved to Strasbourg, taught kindergarten, then joined the Council after divorce. After retiring she earned further degrees, loved piano, gardening, and encouraged her family’s love of learning.
