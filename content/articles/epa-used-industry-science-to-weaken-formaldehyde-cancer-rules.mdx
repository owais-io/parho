New documents show the Trump‑era EPA backed down on strict formaldehyde limits, using studies from a chemist funded by chemical groups. Those studies claimed a small amount of the toxic gas is safe, even though newer research says any exposure can raise cancer risk. The move involved meetings with big chemical companies and cherry‑picking data. Critics warn the change could loosen rules on many other dangerous chemicals, putting people at risk.
