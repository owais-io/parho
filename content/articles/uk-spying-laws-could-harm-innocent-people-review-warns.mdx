A review of Britain’s new spying law warns it is too broad and could wrongly target ordinary people. Jonathan Hall says the law could reach into politics, journalism, protests and normal daily life, leading to false arrests or investigations. He points to examples like lobbying, journalism or foreign aid work that could be labelled espionage. The review also notes police powers over protest sites and suggests extra safeguards and a policing code.
