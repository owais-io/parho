Sussan Ley served as the leader of Australia's Liberal Party for just 276 days before being replaced by Angus Taylor. That makes her the second‑shortest time anyone has held the post—only Alexander Downer served for less than a year. By contrast, founding leader Robert Menzies stayed on for over 20 years. The article compares the lengths of all Liberal leaders' terms, showing how brief Ley’s time at the top was.
