New York City’s brutal cold snap has claimed 18 lives, officials said. The freezing storm, one of the longest sub‑zero spells since 1961, forced the mayor to declare a “code blue,” opening 60 hotel rooms and 62 warming centres for the homeless. Temperatures hit as low as –20°F, sparking an extreme‑cold warning. The city urged anyone out on the street to seek shelter.
