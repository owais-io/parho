UK borrowing costs hit their lowest level in over a year as investors feel the government’s finances are improving and expect more rate cuts. 10‑year government bond yields fell to 4.34%, down from 4.41%. This supports Treasury Secretary Rachel Reeves, who aims to shrink the deficit from over 5% to below 2% by 2029‑30, and reflects confidence in future Bank of England rate cuts.
