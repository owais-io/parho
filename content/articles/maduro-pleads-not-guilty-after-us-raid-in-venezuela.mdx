After a surprise raid by US forces, former Venezuelan president Nicolás Maduro was taken to a Manhattan courtroom where he pleaded not guilty to charges of drug trafficking, weapons possession and narco‑terrorism. The short arraignment drew worldwide criticism, with the UN calling the move a breach of international law. Maduro insists he was illegally captured and is still Venezuela’s president. His wife was also seized, and the incident has heightened tensions in the region.
