A new study in Trondheim tested a simple change: turning off blue‑light in psychiatric wards during the evening. In a controlled trial with 476 patients, those in the blue‑filtered ward had better mood improvements and less aggressive behavior, even though the length of stay stayed the same. The results suggest that matching hospital lighting to our natural circadian rhythm can make care safer and more effective.
