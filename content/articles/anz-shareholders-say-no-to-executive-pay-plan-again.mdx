ANZ shareholders voted down the bank’s executive pay plan for the second year in a row. They were angry after ANZ was hit with a $240 million penalty for many rule‑breaking mistakes, like charging dead customers fees. More than a third of voters opposed the pay scheme, and shareholders also rejected a vote to replace the board. The bank is also dealing with staff cuts that have drawn criticism from the Finance Sector Union.
