New South Wales has seen four shark attacks in two days, including a surfer who was hospitalized after a bite off Limeburners Creek. Authorities say dirty, murky water invites bull sharks, so they’re closing beaches from Port Macquarie to Sydney’s northern shores until at least Thursday. They urge people to use local pools, while lifeguards and drones boost patrols. The incidents remind everyone that heavy rain and cloudy water can attract sharks.
