Eleanor Holmes Norton, Washington D.C.’s 88‑year‑old non‑voting delegate, said she will leave Congress after 33 years. She fought for D.C. residents’ voting rights and statehood, using her limited power to push reforms that helped revitalize the city. Her career saw successes in housing, health care and anti‑crack measures, but she faced criticism and health concerns in recent years. Norton will step down at the end of this term, passing the baton to a new generation.
