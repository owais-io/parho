The author, a former public‑school student, says he was surprised at first but not shocked by allegations of Nigel Farage’s racism from his school days. He notes that elite schools like Dulwich and Eton often have students using racist language and that many of those attitudes stick into adulthood. The piece links Farage’s past behavior to his current political power, Brexit, and the rise of far‑right sentiment in the UK.
