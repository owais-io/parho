Jihadist attacks in Nigeria and the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) rose sharply last year, even while worldwide terror deaths dropped. In Nigeria, deaths jumped 46% to 750, the fourth‑highest in the world, with groups like Boko Haram and new "bandit" militias active. In the DRC, attacks from the IS‑linked Allied Democratic Forces increased 28% to 467. Global terrorism deaths fell 28% to 5,582, showing a mixed trend.
