Dozens of flood monitoring stations across northern Australia are now showing major flooding after a week of record‑breaking rain. The Bureau of Meteorology says at least 50 sites have breached the ‘major flood’ level, especially on the Katherine, Daly and Burnett rivers. Residents in towns like Katherine and Bundaberg have been warned to stay away, and some have been air‑lifted. The water levels are still climbing in many places, and officials say more flooding could come.
