A protest in a Minneapolis church led the Trump administration to use the 1994 Freedom of Access to Clinic Entrances (Face) Act, originally aimed at abortion‑clinic blockades, to charge 39 people, including a former CNN anchor, for interrupting a service. The move sparked criticism from free‑speech groups and prompted several states to propose buffer‑zone bills around places of worship. The case raises questions about the balance between religious freedom and the right to protest.
