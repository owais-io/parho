Researchers say Ukrainian civilians suffered a 26% jump in deaths in 2025, with 2,248 killed and 12,493 injured by bombs. The worst hit came on June 24 in Dnipro, when a missile struck a train, apartments and schools, killing 21 and wounding 314, including children. Russia’s nightly missile and drone raids left millions without power, water and heat. Worldwide, 45,358 people were hit by bombs, with Israel leading and Russia second.
