Two 65‑ and 71‑year‑old pilots were killed when their helicopters collided over a farm near Hammonton, New Jersey, on Sunday. The aircraft, both Enstrom models, were flying close together and likely hit each other. The men, who had been regular breakfast customers at a local café, had earned their private pilot licences in 2014. Only the pilots were on board. Authorities and the NTSB are investigating the crash, while senators and the community mourn the loss.
