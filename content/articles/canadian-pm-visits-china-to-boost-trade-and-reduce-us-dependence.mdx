Canada’s new prime minister Mark Carney flew to Beijing this week to try and fix trade ties with China. He wants to grow sales to China while cutting how much Canada sells to the United States, which has slapped tariffs on Canadian goods. China has already hit Canada with high duties on canola oil and other products. The visit also gives Canada a chance to talk about political prisoners and human‑rights concerns.
