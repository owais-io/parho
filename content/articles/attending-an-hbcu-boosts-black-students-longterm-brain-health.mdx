Researchers looked at almost 2,000 Black adults who went to college between 1940 and 1980. Those who attended historically Black colleges (HBCUs) had better memory and thinking skills when they were about 62 than people who went to predominantly white schools. The study shows that the supportive, culturally‑relevant environment of HBCUs can protect brain health for years after graduation. It highlights the importance of keeping and supporting these schools.
