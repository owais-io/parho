A former UN boss, María Fernanda Espinosa, used to send copies of the UN charter to every member state’s representative to remind them of their commitments. Now, as she seeks a higher role, the article suggests the UN should again distribute the charter to key leaders—Trump, Putin, Netanyahu—and to the UK’s Attorney General, who has introduced a controversial “UK right.” The piece critiques how these officials handle international law.
