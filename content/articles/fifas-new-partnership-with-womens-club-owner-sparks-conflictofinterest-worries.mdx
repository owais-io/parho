FIFA has named Kynisca, the ownership group of several women’s football clubs, as the presenting partner for its first Women’s Champions Cup. The deal comes after owner Michele Kang gave $30 million to US Soccer and founded a women’s football institute. Critics say Kang could profit if her teams win or sign players, raising doubts about the tournament’s fairness. The partnership is only for this year, but the conflict of interest remains a hot topic.
