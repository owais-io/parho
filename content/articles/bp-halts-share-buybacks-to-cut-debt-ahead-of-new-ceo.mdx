BP’s board has stopped buying back shares to free up cash for debt reduction, after heavy write‑downs on its low‑carbon assets. With the company’s debt highest among major oil firms, the move aims to trim about £6 billion a year while it waits for new CEO Meg O’Neill to take charge in April. Share prices fell 6%, and investors are watching to see if BP will shift focus back to oil and gas projects or divest its renewables holdings.
