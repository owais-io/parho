María Corina Machado, a Venezuelan opposition leader, fled her country in a risky journey that began with a secret boat trip to the Dutch island of Curaçao. From there she travelled by plane to Norway, where she will receive the Nobel Peace Prize. The escape was helped by U.S. officials and was delayed by bad weather. Machado has been in hiding for almost a year, hoping to continue her fight for democracy from abroad.
