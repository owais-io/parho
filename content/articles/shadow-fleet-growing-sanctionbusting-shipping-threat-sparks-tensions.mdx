Russia, Iran and Venezuela use a network of old, hidden oil tankers to dodge Western sanctions. The fleet has ballooned to around 1,000 ships, and governments have begun boarding or stopping them, including a U.S. raid on a Venezuelan tanker and Ukrainian drone hits on Russian ships. Fake flags and shady owners help hide the vessels, but the growing crackdown risks turning into military clashes. The EU has slapped sanctions on related companies.
