A Hong Kong judge found the father of pro‑democracy activist Anna Kwok guilty under the city’s new national‑security law. He was charged for attempting to shut down her life‑insurance policy and withdraw the funds. The ruling drew international criticism for targeting relatives of wanted dissidents. The conviction may bring up to seven years in prison, highlighting how Hong Kong is tightening its security laws.
