The US defence secretary, Pete Hegseth, told Latin American leaders in Miami that the United States wants them to help fight drug cartels. He warned that Washington might act alone if the region does not join. The talk followed a decade of US military support in the region and growing drug production. Some key countries, like Colombia, Mexico and Brazil, did not attend the conference. Critics argue that using only the army will not solve the complex drug problem.
