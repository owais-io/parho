Cambridge University has uncovered a treasure of plant samples, drawings and watercolours that taught Charles Darwin in the 1820s. These rare materials, once used by his mentor John Henslow, will now be featured in a new four‑week summer botany course. The course is designed to give students hands‑on experience like Darwin’s, filling a modern gap in plant‑science training and reminding us of the roots of evolutionary theory.
