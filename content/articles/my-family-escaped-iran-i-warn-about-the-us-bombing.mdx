In 1980 my grandfather got a warning that the Iranian regime wanted him dead, so he, my grandmother and mother fled to London with forged passports. After years in asylum, they moved to Australia. With the death of Iran’s leader, celebrations erupted worldwide, but I’m alarmed that the US’s sudden bombing of Iran could kill innocent civilians. The attack would hurt ordinary Iranians who already face torture, limited shelters and a hostile government. I urge the US to protect civilians and seek peace.
