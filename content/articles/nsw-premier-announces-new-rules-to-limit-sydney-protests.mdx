Chris Minns, the premier of New South Wales, said the state will tighten rules on protests in Sydney’s city centre, after the Bondi beach terror attack. He plans tougher approval processes, will ban certain slogans like “globalise the intifada,” and could allow a 14‑day ban on protests after a declared terrorist event. The changes aim to keep the city safe and let residents enjoy public spaces without constant demonstrations.
