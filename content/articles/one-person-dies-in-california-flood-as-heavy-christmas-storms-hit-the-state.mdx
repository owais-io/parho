One person died when a car flooded in Redding, California, as the state prepares for a week of heavy Christmas storms. Officials say the driver was trapped in a water‑filled vehicle and police could not rescue them. Meteorologists warn that the atmospheric river—called the Pineapple Express—will bring 4 to 12 inches of rain, raising flood and mudslide risks, especially in burn‑scarred areas. Local governments are urging residents to stay alert and plan ahead.
