Three pro‑democracy activists in Hong Kong are on trial for organising an annual vigil that remembers the 1989 Tiananmen Square crackdown. Charged with inciting subversion under Beijing’s national security law, they face up to ten years in prison. The case shows how the law is used to stop protests and free‑speech events. Critics say the trial is a tool to silence history and democratic voices.
