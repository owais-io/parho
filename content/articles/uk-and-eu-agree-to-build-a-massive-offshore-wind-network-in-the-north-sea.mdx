Energy ministers from the UK and nine other European nations signed a deal to turn the North Sea into a big clean‑energy hub. They will build 100 gigawatts of offshore wind farms and link them with a new high‑voltage submarine grid. The plan includes 5 GW of new wind capacity each year until 2040 and is expected to create 91,000 jobs. The project could attract €1 trillion of investment and help Europe meet its 2050 wind goals.
