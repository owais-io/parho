New Zealand’s “golden visa” is attracting a wave of wealthy foreigners after the government loosened rules last April. The Active Investor Plus visa now costs less, skips the English test and cuts the residency time from three years to three weeks. The scheme has already drawn 573 applications (1,833 people), 40 % from the U.S., and China’s entries have doubled. Investors are lured by New Zealand’s natural beauty, business climate and a chance to avoid political turmoil abroad.
