Glp‑1 injections, once used for diabetes, are now being sold as weekly weight‑loss shots. In 2025, the World Health Organization said the drugs can treat obesity, sparking cheers from doctors and criticism from public‑health experts. While the shots can help people lose weight and lower heart‑disease risks, they don’t replace healthy food or exercise, and long‑term safety and cost are still uncertain. The debate shows that medicine is only part of the solution.
