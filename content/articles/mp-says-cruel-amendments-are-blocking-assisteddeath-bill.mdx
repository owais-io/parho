House of Lords MPs are putting forward over 1,000 amendments to the assisted‑death bill. MP Kim Leadbeater says many are unnecessary and cruel, such as banning people who traveled abroad last year, checking family finances, and requiring a death to be filmed. She fears the bill won’t finish before the parliamentary session ends. Opponents say the changes protect against coercion, but debate is still ongoing.
