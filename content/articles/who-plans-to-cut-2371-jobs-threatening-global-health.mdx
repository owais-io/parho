WHO plans to cut about 2,371 jobs, or almost a quarter of its staff, by June 2026 after the U.S. left the organization. The cuts will hit Geneva and Africa most, hurting the WHO’s ability to track disease, supply medicine and respond to emergencies. Staff are angry and worry about health threats worldwide. Leaders say more money is needed to keep WHO effective.
