The UK announced £1bn for quantum computing, but at the same time it is cutting money for basic science in particle physics, astronomy and nuclear physics. Those cuts threaten the pool of early‑career scientists who could develop the ideas that feed quantum tech. Many researchers are leaving for countries with steadier funding. Without a solid science base, the UK’s ambitions for quantum leadership are at risk.
