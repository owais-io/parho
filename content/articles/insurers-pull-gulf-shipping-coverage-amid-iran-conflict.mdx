Maritime insurers are pulling war‑risk coverage for ships in the Gulf after Iran‑related tensions grow. With the strait of Hormuz closed, many tankers are stuck or taking longer routes, pushing freight rates higher. Insurance costs could jump by up to 100%, and big shipping lines are diverting traffic around Africa. This rattles insurers like Beazley, whose shares briefly fell, and customers may see higher prices for goods shipped through the Middle East.
