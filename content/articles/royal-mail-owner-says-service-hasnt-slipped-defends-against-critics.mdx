Czech billionaire Daniel Křetínský, who bought Royal Mail’s parent company last year, told MPs that the service hasn’t dropped, even though customers complain about late letters and higher prices. He apologized for delays, blamed the UK’s high delivery expectations and competition with parcel couriers, and denied that Royal Mail favours profit‑making parcels over letters. He also said regulators will lower delivery targets next week, and he welcomed better driver wages elsewhere.
