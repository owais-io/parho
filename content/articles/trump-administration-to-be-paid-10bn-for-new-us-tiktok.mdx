Investors who bought TikTok’s U.S. operations from Chinese owner ByteDance are set to pay the Trump administration a $10 billion fee for making the deal happen. The deal, closed in January, involves firms like Oracle, MGX and Silver Lake. Trump signed an executive order to keep TikTok in U.S. hands, saying the platform would be “American‑operated.” The fee is unusually large—about 70% of the U.S. TikTok’s $14 billion value—and shows the administration’s rare direct role in a private‑sector transaction.
