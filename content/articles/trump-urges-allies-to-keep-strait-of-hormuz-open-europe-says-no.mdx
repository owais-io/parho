President Trump has warned European allies that the U.S. will face a “very bad future” if they don't help keep the Strait of Hormuz open. The strait, vital for oil shipments, was closed after Iran used drones and mines in response to U.S.-Israeli attacks. Britain, Germany, France, Italy, Australia and Japan all said they have no plans to send warships. European leaders want more detail on U.S. strategy before committing.
