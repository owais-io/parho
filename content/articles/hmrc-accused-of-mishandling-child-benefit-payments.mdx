HMRC wrongly stopped child benefit payments for nearly 4,000 parents after using faulty travel data that said they left the UK. Some parents had only taken holiday or work trips, yet HMRC cut their benefits. Officials will testify before a Treasury committee. HMRC says it missed checks, apologises, and is fixing the system. Parents now have extra time to prove they stayed in the UK.
