Trump said the White House will pull 700 federal immigration agents out of Minnesota, leaving about 2,000 on the ground—still more than normal. He said the cut is a “softer touch” after backlash over hard enforcement. Minnesota’s governor says the move hurts the state politically and operationally. Senate minority leader Chuck Schumer and others criticize the agency, saying its presence feels like a military patrol.
