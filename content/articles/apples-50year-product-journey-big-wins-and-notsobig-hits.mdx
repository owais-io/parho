Apple started in a garage in 1976 and has made and broken millions of products. Its biggest successes – the Apple II, Macintosh, iMac G3, iPod and iPhone – set new standards for ease of use and control over the whole ecosystem. But the company also shipped flops like the fan‑less Apple III, the heavy Macintosh Portable, the clunky Newton, the game‑console Pippin and the pricey Vision Pro. Apple’s story shows that great design can win, but a lack of polish or market fit can hurt.
