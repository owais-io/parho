In 1786, Quobna Ottobah Cugoano, a free Black man living in London, sent the Prince of Wales and King George III a pamphlet and his own book, urging Britain to stop the slave trade. He warned of the cruelty, exposed the monarchy’s ties to slavery, and demanded immediate abolition. The royal family kept the book but never acted, leaving his urgent appeal unanswered.
