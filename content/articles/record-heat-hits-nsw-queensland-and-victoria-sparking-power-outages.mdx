Australia’s heatwave has hit parts of New South Wales, Queensland and Victoria, setting record highs. Temperatures spiked into the high 40s, with Borrona Downs hitting 49 °C. Even alpine resorts saw over 30 °C for the first time. The surge strained power lines, leaving 11,000 homes without electricity, and left people scrambling for air‑conditioning relief. The heat also fuels ongoing bushfires, forcing school closures and threatening community services.
