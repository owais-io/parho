The House rejected a motion that would have ordered President Trump to pull U.S. troops out of Venezuela. The vote was tied, so a Texas congressman rushed in to break the tie, keeping the bill alive. Only two Republicans joined Democrats, while most GOP members backed Trump’s foreign‑policy actions. The measure aimed to limit presidential military moves after a surprise raid captured Venezuelan President Maduro. The fight highlights a split in Congress over Trump’s aggressive stance abroad.
