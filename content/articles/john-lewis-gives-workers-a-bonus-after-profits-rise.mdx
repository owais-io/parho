John Lewis has paid its 69,000 workers a bonus for the first time in four years, sharing £35 million (about one week’s pay). Profits rose 6 % to £134 m and sales up 5 % to £13.4 bn. The group is still trimming stores and jobs, but is investing in new tech, AI and better shelves. Management says disciplined spending helped the bonus return. Customers are buying more, and the company plans to keep investing in brands and services.
