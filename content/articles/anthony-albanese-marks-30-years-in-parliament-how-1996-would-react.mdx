Anthony Albanese celebrates 30 years as an Australian MP, first winning Grayndler in 1996 when Labor was out of power. The party marked the anniversary with a quieter celebration than his 1996 debut night. Now Prime Minister, he backs tough foreign moves like the Israel‑Iran strikes, but critics say he’s slow on social reforms. Inside Labor, he holds tight control, though some fear he’s too cautious to push bold change.
