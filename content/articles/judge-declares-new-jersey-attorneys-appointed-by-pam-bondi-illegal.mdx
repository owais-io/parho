A federal judge said the three lawyers Pam Bondi chose to run New Jersey’s attorney‑general office were appointed illegally because they never got Senate approval. The judge warned that the lack of proper confirmation could lead to criminal cases being dismissed. Bondi had split the job into three roles to avoid the usual checks. This ruling follows a string of court decisions that say Trump‑era appointments without Senate confirmation are unlawful.
