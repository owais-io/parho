A food‑policy expert says Britain is too dependent on imports and has no emergency food reserve. Only 54 % of food is produced locally, much less than the U.S., France or Australia. Most supplies travel through a few big supermarkets and a handful of distribution centres, making the system a single point of failure. With climate change and possible war, the government should build stockpiles and strengthen local farming, backed by new law.
