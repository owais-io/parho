Palawan, a Philippine island known for its reefs and forests, is facing damage from a nickel mine. The mine releases red silt that clogs rivers, kills fish, and makes rice grow poorly. Villagers and indigenous groups say the mine pollutes water, hurts health, and destroys wildlife. The mining company says the ore isn’t harmful and it keeps clean drainage, but scientists warn it can spread nickel and chromium into the environment.
