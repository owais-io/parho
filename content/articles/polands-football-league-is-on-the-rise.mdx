Polish football is on the upswing. The Ekstraklasa’s teams are more competitive than ever, with the gap between first and eighth places only four points. A new wave of private owners and better stadiums—thanks to Euro 2012—has lifted crowds to over 13,000 per game. Clubs are moving out of city‑board control, getting more money, and attracting foreign talent. With a growing TV deal and more European matches, Poland could soon break into the top 10 leagues.
