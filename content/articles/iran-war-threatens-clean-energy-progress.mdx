The fighting in Iran has hit global oil supplies and created the biggest oil shock in history. While some countries have turned to solar, wind and electric cars to stay safe, the war also stops the flow of metals needed for new panels and pushes nations to burn more coal and oil. Experts say the conflict could slow the switch to clean power unless governments change tax rules, cut subsidies and stop new fossil‑fuel projects.
