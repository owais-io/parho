The London office of Clifford Chance is cutting about 10 % of its business‑support staff—roughly 50 people in finance, HR and IT—as more work gets done by artificial intelligence. The firm says AI can handle tasks like research, scheduling and contract reviews, so fewer people are needed. PwC is seeing a similar trend, holding back on hiring and saying AI will change the types of roles they need.
