Will Lewis, who took over the Washington Post five months after the previous publisher left, tried to fix the paper’s finances but faced backlash from staff and heavy layoffs. He was criticized for clashing with reporters, skipping meetings, and attending high‑profile events right after mass job cuts. After a controversial year of changes and employee anger, Lewis stepped down in June 2024, leaving the Post in a shaky spot.
