Tashkent Supermarket, opened by Uzbek immigrants, brings Central Asian staples like manty, plov, and cheap figs to New York. Originally in Brooklyn, the shop expanded to a West Village spot, drawing food bloggers and curious New Yorkers. The store offers a mix of homemade breads, spices, and ready‑to‑eat dishes, giving people from former Soviet states a taste of home. Prices have risen a bit due to Manhattan costs, but the shop remains a cultural hub for longtime patrons and newcomers.
