In London’s Little Tehran and Manchester, Iranians celebrated the death of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei after U.S.–Israel strikes hit Iran. While some threw parties, many were also shocked by the bombing’s toll, especially the 148 children killed in an elementary school strike. The community feels a mix of relief and uncertainty, hoping for a new government—some name Reza Pahlavi, the Shah’s son—yet fearing more chaos. The death has sparked hope that the regime’s rule might end, but many worry about ongoing violence.
