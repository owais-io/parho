The UK competition watchdog the CMA has started investigations into five companies – Autotrader, Just Eat, Dignity, Feefo and Pasta Evangelists – over claims that they are hiding or inflating reviews. The probes look at things like one‑star reviews being removed, staff asked to write positive comments, and customers being paid for five‑star ratings. The CMA can now fine firms up to 10% of their global turnover if they break the law.
