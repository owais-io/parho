Gulf states warn that Iran‑backed militias and their proxies are a growing threat. They say attacks from Iran—missiles, drones, and covert cells—could destabilise the region. Qatar, Kuwait, the UAE, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, and Jordan have arrested suspected members and accused Iran of training sleeper cells. The Gulf leaders fear a repeat of the 1980s Iran‑Iraq war and are urging Iraq to stop these groups from launching attacks.
