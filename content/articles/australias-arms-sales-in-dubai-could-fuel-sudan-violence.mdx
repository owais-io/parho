Australia’s arms sales at a Dubai airshow raise concerns about weapons ending up in Sudan’s brutal conflicts. The UAE is Australia’s top buyer, and evidence suggests the UAE has supplied the Sudanese RSF with guns that have killed thousands. Human‑rights groups want a review of Australia’s export rules, while the government insists its controls are strict. The link between Australian weapons and Sudan’s violence is unclear but worrying.
