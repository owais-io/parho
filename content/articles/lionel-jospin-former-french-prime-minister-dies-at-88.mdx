Lionel Jospin, the former French prime minister, died at 88. He led France from 1997‑2002, introducing the 35‑hour workweek, universal health care and civil partnerships. In 2002 he was unexpectedly beaten by the far‑right, ending his political career. Born in Paris, he was a long‑time Socialist leader and later served on France’s Constitutional Council until 2019. His death marks the end of an era.
