After the top Iranian official was killed, the country chose his son, Mojtaba Khamenei, to take his place. He’s a quiet security‑focused figure with little public fame. The move shows that Iran’s leaders value stability and the power of the Revolutionary Guard over traditional religious credentials. While it may keep the regime steady now, the decision could stir future tensions as it hints at a family line of leaders.
