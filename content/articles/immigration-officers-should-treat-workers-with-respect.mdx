A group of readers write to the Guardian about how immigration officers in the UK raid restaurants. They say the visits are too loud and intimidating. The letters argue that while enforcing the law is important, it should be done politely and quietly. Officers could check paperwork behind closed doors instead of making a scene. They want a fair balance between firm enforcement and treating people with dignity.
