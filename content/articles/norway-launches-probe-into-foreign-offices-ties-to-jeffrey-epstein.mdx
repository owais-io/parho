Norway’s parliament voted unanimously to set up an independent commission to investigate the foreign office’s links to Jeffrey Epstein. Prime Minister Jonas Gahr Støre said the leaked US Justice Department files show rich people can buy influence, and named high‑profile figures like former ambassadors Mona Juul and Terje Rød‑Larsen, former PM Thorbjørn Jagland and ex‑foreign minister Børge Brende. The probe will also look at how Norway picks leaders in international bodies and spends aid, aiming to restore public trust.
