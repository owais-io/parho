At the Bafta awards, Tourette’s activist John Davidson shouted the N‑word while a prize was being presented. The outburst drew angry comments from stars like Jamie Foxx and Wendell Pierce, who demanded apologies. Davidson said the words were involuntary tics from his condition. The incident sparked a wider debate about Tourette’s, racism and how media handle involuntary speech, with some calling for more understanding. Officials and advocacy groups said the event highlighted the need for better preparation at public events.
