In north Wales, two 19‑ and 20‑year‑olds from Norfolk went missing while hiking Snowdon’s Yr Wyddfa. After a harsh winter search involving rescue teams, dogs and a helicopter, their bodies were found the next day. Families mourn the loss, describing them as caring and loving, while authorities praised the volunteers’ hard work and urged others to stay safe in the mountains. Their tragedy reminds hikers of the risks of winter climbs.
