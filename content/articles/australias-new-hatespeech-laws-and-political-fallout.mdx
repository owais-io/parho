Australia passed hate‑speech laws after a terror attack at Bondi Beach. The bill bans membership in hate groups, adds jail terms for leaders and members, and lets the government cancel visas for extremist activity. It strengthens rules about hate symbols and creates stricter penalties for religious “hate preachers.” The law split the original bill and sparked a political split that broke up the ruling coalition. Critics fear it could limit free speech and give the government too much power.
