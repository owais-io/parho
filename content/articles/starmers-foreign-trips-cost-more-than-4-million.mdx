Prime Minister Keir Starmer has spent more than £4 million on 39 overseas trips since taking office. The biggest spend was a three‑day COP climate conference in Brazil, costing £413 000. Other costly trips include India and the G20 in Johannesburg. Critics say he’s “never here Keir” and that he should stay in the UK. Starmer says the travel secures investment and jobs for Britain. Compared with, his spend is higher than Rishi Sunak’s or Boris Johnson’s, but similar to Tony Blair’s.
