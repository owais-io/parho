President Cyril Ramaphosa closed the G20 summit in Johannesburg, rejecting a U.S. request that he hand over the presidency to a junior diplomat for next year. The United States boycotted the event, accusing South Africa of discrimination. Ramaphosa stressed multilateralism, climate change, and gender equality. The G20 issued a declaration on these issues. Key members such as the U.S., Russia, China, and Argentina were absent, while the next summit will be in Florida next year.
