Brazil opened a 186‑km hiking trail called Caminhos da Ibiapaba that links two national parks and passes through three ecosystems. The trail, marked with black‑yellow footprints, is part of a strategy that turns paths into conservation tools. By giving people easy access to forests, it encourages locals to protect the land, helps control fires and poaching, and creates jobs for businesses that serve hikers. The project aims to connect all protected areas with trails.
