Okunoshima, a tiny Japanese island, is famous for its large rabbit population that tourists love to feed and photograph. The island was secretly used by the Japanese army in WWII to produce chemical weapons, and rabbits were once part of gas tests. Today, the rabbits depend on visitors for food because there is little natural food left. With fewer tourists and predators like boar, the future of the island’s rabbits is uncertain.
