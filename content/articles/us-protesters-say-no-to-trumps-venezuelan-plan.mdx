Protests erupted in cities like Chicago, New York, and Seattle as people opposed President Trump's plan to capture Venezuelan leader Nicolás Maduro. Many supporters of Maduro celebrated his removal, but protestors warned that the U.S. action could spark war and violate Venezuelan sovereignty. They argued Trump lacked congressional approval, and called the move illegal. Democrats and anti‑war groups criticized the plan, while some also called for Maduro’s release.
