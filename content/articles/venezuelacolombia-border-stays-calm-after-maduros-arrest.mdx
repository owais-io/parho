After US forces captured Venezuelan leader Maduro, traffic across the Simón Bolívar Bridge to Colombia stayed normal, but a tense calm settled. Military commanders said orders didn't change, and Colombian officials raised security amid fears of crime and rebel spill‑over. The Colombian government warned of possible mass crossings and set up Red Cross emergency kits. Venezuelan shops were emptied, and refugees in Colombia celebrated but remained unsure about returning home.
