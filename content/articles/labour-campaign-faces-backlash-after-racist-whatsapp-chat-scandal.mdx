Labour’s campaign for a key byelection is in trouble after a WhatsApp chat full of racist and antisemitic jokes was exposed. Six local councillors broke conduct rules, and two were expelled. The scandal, involving former MP Andrew Gwynne and others, sparked protests, calls for resignations, and may hurt Labour’s chances in the February election. The investigation will be reviewed before the vote.
