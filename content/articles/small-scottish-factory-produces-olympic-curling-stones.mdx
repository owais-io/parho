At Kays Scotland in Mauchline, a 15‑person family firm has been making curling stones for the Winter Olympics since 1924. Using special granite from the island of Ailsa Craig, each 18‑kg stone is hand‑shaped, drilled, polished and stamped with a unique number. This year the shop produced 132 stones for the Games in Italy and also created Olympic gift‑ware. The workshop can make 48 stones a week.
