Rhoda Roberts was a Bundjalung elder who spent her life sharing Aboriginal stories, music, and dance with Australia and the world. She helped put Indigenous culture front‑and‑center at the Sydney Olympics, launched the Dreaming festival, and ran the first Aboriginal TV show. Roberts also created radio programs that gave First Nations voices a platform and led major arts initiatives at the Sydney Opera House. She died at 66.
