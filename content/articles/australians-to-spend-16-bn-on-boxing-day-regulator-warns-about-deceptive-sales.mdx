Australians are expected to spend about $1.6 billion on Boxing Day, but the consumer watchdog warns that many stores use tricks to make discounts look bigger than they are. It says shoppers should look at the final price, not the advertised sale. Sales for the whole post‑Christmas week are forecast at $3.8 billion, with household goods and fashion leading. Confidence is low and higher interest rates could push sales lower.
