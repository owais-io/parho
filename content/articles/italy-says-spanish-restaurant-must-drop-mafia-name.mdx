Spain’s restaurant chain “The Mafia Sits at the Table” may have to change its name after Italy’s government asked the country’s patent office to cancel the trademark. Both the EU and Spanish trademark offices agreed that the name glorifies organised crime and could upset victims. The chain says the word “mafia” is just a cultural reference, but Italy argues it still represents a global criminal group. The firm can appeal the ruling.
