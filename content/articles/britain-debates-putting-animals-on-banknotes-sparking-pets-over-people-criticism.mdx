Britain is debating putting animals on its banknotes. The idea, backed by public polls, clashes with critics who say it shows a “woke” bias and creates a false choice between caring for animals and caring for people. They cite examples like the “pets over people” phrase used during a Kabul animal‑charity evacuation. The debate highlights that many, especially working‑class groups, love wildlife and see environmental care as part of everyday life.
