Jono Ridler, a New Zealand swimmer, is attempting the world’s longest unassisted staged swim along the east coast. He covers 1,350 km, swimming 18–30 km a day, while his support boats track him. The journey is more than a challenge – it’s a campaign to warn about jellyfish, pollution and bottom trawling. Ridler’s petition has already gained recently over 40,000 signatures. His effort hopes to inspire policy change.
