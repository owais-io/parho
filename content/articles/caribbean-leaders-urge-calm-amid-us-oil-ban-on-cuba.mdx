At a four‑day Caricom summit in St Kitts and Nevis, Caribbean leaders asked the United States to ease its oil embargo on Cuba and stop deadly military strikes on suspected drug boats. They warned that Cuba’s energy shortages and humanitarian crisis could spill over into the region, hurting migration, security and trade. The leaders also urged the U.S. to open dialogue, hoping the new Caricom chair and the U.S. secretary of state will meet soon.
