Mike Collins, who taught town‑planning at UCL for over three decades, passed away at 92. He began his career in London’s county council, then spent 20 years as UCL’s director of studies, researching how statutory planning works in practice. After retiring he advised on environmental reports and stayed active in local planning debates. He published two books in his 90s on London shopping history and planning education, and he loved golf with his partner Sheila.
