Police are questioning a man in his 60s over claims that he helped Mohamed Al Fayed, the late former Harrods owner, carry out human‑trafficking and rape. The suspect was interviewed this month after 154 people said they were abused by Fayed. No arrests have been made yet, but detectives plan to ask more people in the coming months. The probe, called Operation Cornpoppy, covers more than 400 alleged offences from 1977‑2014.
