Voters in Costa Rica head to the polls as crime rises and fears grow of a shift toward authoritarian rule. The election will pick a new president and 57 congress members. Laura Fernández, hand‑picked by outgoing President Rodrigo Chaves, is leading the polls with a tough‑on‑crime promise. The opposition is split and about a third of voters are still undecided, leaving the outcome uncertain.
