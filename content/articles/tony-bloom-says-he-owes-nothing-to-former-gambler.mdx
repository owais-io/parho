Tony Bloom, the billionaire owner of Brighton & Hove Albion and a big gambling figure, says he does not owe money to his ex‑employee Ryan Dudfield. Bloom admits his betting syndicate used the accounts of political adviser George Cottrell to place huge bets. Dudfield claims the group owed him £13.1 m, but Bloom argues any payments should come from Cottrell, not him. The dispute shows how secretive large‑scale betting operations can be.
