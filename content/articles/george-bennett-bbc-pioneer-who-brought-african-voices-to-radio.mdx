George Bennett (1924‑2015) was a BBC radio boss who built the African Service into the world’s biggest listenership in Africa. He hired local journalists, created shows like Focus on Africa, and made the BBC’s news reach more than any other continent’s radio. After leaving the BBC, he ran UN and NGO radio stations in Somalia, Liberia and Sierra Leone, and trained Red Cross teams. His work left a lasting legacy for African broadcasting.
