A group of charities and a law firm have filed a rare super‑complaint against police in England and Wales, saying sexual‑offence investigations are taking too long. Over the past decade, more than 37,000 cases took over three years to finish, with some lasting up to nine years. Survivors say the delays make them feel ignored and they may stop reporting again. The complaint calls for a full audit of all forces and warns of legal liability.
