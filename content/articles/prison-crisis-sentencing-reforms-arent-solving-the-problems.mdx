England’s prisons are in crisis, with unsafe conditions, violence, staff shortages and limited education. New sentencing laws and a push to cut jury trials add pressure. The government’s plans—expanding electronic tags and reducing prison use—are hampered by weak probation services and budget gaps. The result is a record prison population, high staff attacks, and doubts that reforms will improve rehabilitation. Critics say the changes risk turning prisons into just another punishment tool.
