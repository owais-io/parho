Russia has opened a criminal case against Telegram founder Pavel Durov, accusing him of helping terrorists. Authorities say the app was used for attacks on Russian military and civilians. The Kremlin wants Telegram to comply with Russian law, which would mean opening private chats and removing opposition content. Durov, who lives abroad, says the probe is a way to stifle privacy and free speech. Russia has already slowed Telegram traffic and blocked other apps.
