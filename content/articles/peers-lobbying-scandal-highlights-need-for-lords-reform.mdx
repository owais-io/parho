Two members of the House of Lords were suspended after undercover reporters caught them offering to use their positions to benefit private clients. The incidents show the Lords is too big, poorly watched and full of political insiders. Peers get a free daily allowance, yet many use the job mainly for networking and money. The scandal underlines why the Lords must be reformed to restore public trust.
