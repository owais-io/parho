On St. Helena Island, South Carolina, the Gullah Geechee community fought to regain access to the Big House Cemetery, a historic burial ground that was locked off by private landowners. In February 2025, a judge ordered the gates opened for funerals and maintenance, but families must now request access and post a $5,000 bond for road repairs. The case highlights how development threatens the community’s cultural and spiritual ties to the sea‑side cemetery.
