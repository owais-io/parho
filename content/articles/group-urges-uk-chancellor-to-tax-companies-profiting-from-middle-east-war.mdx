Charities, unions and campaign groups have asked UK chancellor Rachel Reeves to tax companies making extra profits from the US‑Israel conflict over Iran. They want new levies on energy, banks, agriculture, defence and tech firms to raise billions for cost‑of‑living help and boost energy security. Reeves already has a North Sea windfall tax but faces pressure to tighten it and stop price gouging as the war drives up energy costs.
