Federal agents arrived in New Orleans and started a large immigration crackdown, arresting many people in a mainly Latino suburb and at places like Lowe’s and Home Depot. The operation, called Catahoula Crunch, targets those released after crimes. The city’s mayor and residents fear racial profiling and lack of due process. Businesses closed, protests marched, and local police said they won’t enforce federal immigration law. The crackdown could last until January.
