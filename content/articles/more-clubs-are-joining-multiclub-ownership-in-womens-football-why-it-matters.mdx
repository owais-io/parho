In women's football, a growing trend of multi‑club ownership is reshaping the sport. Investors like Michele Kang and groups such as Mercury 13, Bay Collective, and Monarch Collective are buying stakes in multiple clubs, from London City Lionesses to Bristol City, Montpellier and Viktoria Berlin. They argue the model brings much‑needed money and expertise, helping teams compete and grow. While some worry about control and profit motives, many see it as a practical way to level the playing field.
