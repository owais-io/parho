Gail Slater was fired from her role leading the U.S. Justice Department’s antitrust division after a rocky stint under President Trump. Tensions with Attorney General Pam Bondi erupted when Slater fought a $14 billion Hewlett Packard Enterprise‑Juniper Networks merger, arguing it would hurt competition and raise security risks. Bondi said Slater lied, while critics said she was too strict on corporate deals. The decision leaves the antitrust office to appoint an interim head.
