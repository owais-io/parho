Sarah Mullally, the first woman to become archbishop of Canterbury, said she will rebuild confidence in how the Church of England deals with child and adult abuse. She stresses “proper independence,” strict safeguarding rules, and putting victims at the centre of all actions. Mullally, who has faced past criticism, vows transparent, timely investigations and compassionate leadership.
