Before Christmas the UK government told 63 councils that could be re‑structured to decide if they want to postpone their May elections. By the deadline, 23 councils asked for delays, 34 said they would hold the vote, and 7 have not yet replied. The move is aimed at saving money and resources while councils reorganise. Labour backs the delays; the Conservatives call them a problem and plan to challenge the decision.
