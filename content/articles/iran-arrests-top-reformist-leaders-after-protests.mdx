The Revolutionary Guards have detained the head of Iran’s Reformists Front and other key reformist figures, tightening control after the country’s latest street protests. Officials accuse them of collaborating with the U.S. and Israel and of undermining the nation. The move follows the killing of thousands of protestors and the sentencing of human‑rights activist Narges Mohammadi. It shows the president’s weak influence over the government’s hard‑line policies.
