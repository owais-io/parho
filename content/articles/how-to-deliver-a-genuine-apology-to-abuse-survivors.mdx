On March 11, 2022, five Northern Ireland ministers apologized to children who had been abused in care homes and other institutions. Experts say a good apology should have five parts: admit harm, take responsibility, show remorse, promise it won’t happen again, and offer repair. Survivors said the apology felt “downgraded” because it wasn’t delivered by a single head of state. The next step is real restitution—money, support services, and a permanent memorial.
