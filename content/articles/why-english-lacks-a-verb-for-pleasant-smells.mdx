Adrian Chiles remembers the scent of a 1977 bike shop and wonders why English has no word for nice odors. He explains Welsh and Croatian words that capture pleasant scents, like Welsh clywed (sense) and Croatian mirišiti (smell pleasantly). He shares how listeners use smell to recall memories, and how some still sense scents despite losing their sense of smell. The article shows how language shapes our experience of scent.
