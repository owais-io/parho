In the 1970s, oil prices tripled and Europe had to act fast. France built a nuclear grid, Denmark turned to wind, the Netherlands made cycling everywhere, and Sweden expanded district heating. Those moves cut fossil‑fuel use and made energy more reliable. Today, after Russia’s war in Ukraine, the same ideas are back on the table, but politics and industry make it hard to shift away from gas.
