A Los Angeles jury awarded $6 million to a young woman who said she was addicted to YouTube and Instagram, finding that Meta and YouTube had deliberately built addictive features. The verdict shows courts will hold tech firms responsible for harm. Australian lawyers are now looking into whether similar cases could be brought in Australia. The decision comes as the Australian government plans new rules to protect young people online.
