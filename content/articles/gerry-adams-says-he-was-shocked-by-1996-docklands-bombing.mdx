Gerry Adams, former Sinn Féin leader, told a London court he was stunned by the 1996 Docklands bombing and denies any role or prior knowledge. He rejects claims he was part of the IRA’s leadership and says the British government misrepresented him. The civil case accuses him of being involved in several bombings, but his lawyers argue no concrete evidence links him to the attacks. The trial is still ongoing.
