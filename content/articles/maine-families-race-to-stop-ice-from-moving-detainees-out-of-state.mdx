ICE has begun sending over 200 people who were arrested in Maine to prisons in other states. Families and lawyers are scrambling to find where the detainees are and to file legal papers that could keep them in Maine. When ICE moves someone quickly, the chance to fight the detention shrinks, so the legal aid teams are under pressure. Non‑profits like ILAP are stepping in to help those who can’t afford private lawyers.
