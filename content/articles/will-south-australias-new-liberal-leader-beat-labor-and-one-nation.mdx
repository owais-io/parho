South Australia heads to a March election. New Liberal leader Ashton Hurn has just 14% of the primary vote, while Labor leads at 44% and One Nation is at 24%. The Liberals have faced scandals and internal fights. Hurn says she will try to win a seat, but polls suggest Labor could take all seats and One Nation might become the main opposition. The outcome could show how One Nation could shape future politics.
