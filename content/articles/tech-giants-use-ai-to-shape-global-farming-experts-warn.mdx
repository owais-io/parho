A new report says big tech companies like Google, Microsoft, Amazon, IBM and Alibaba are teaming up with large farms to steer what farmers grow, using AI and data from satellites. The experts argue this top‑down approach favors a few profitable crops and pushes farmers to buy expensive seeds and chemicals. They warn that such a global, tech‑driven system could hurt food security and hurt local, diverse farming practices.
