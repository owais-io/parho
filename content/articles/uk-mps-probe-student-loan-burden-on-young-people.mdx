UK MPs have started a cross‑party inquiry into student loans after worries that young people are caught in a “perfect storm” of debt, high rents, low pension savings and a job market threatened by AI. The Treasury Select Committee, chaired by Meg Hillier, wants stories from the public and says the frozen repayment threshold is a problem. Hillier says these pressures make it hard for 20‑to‑30‑year‑olds to buy homes, save for the future and keep up with a changing workforce.
