A new flat 25 % tax on Australian gas exports is gaining traction, even among Labor MPs. The idea, led by Senator David Pocock and the Australia Institute, aims to take windfall profits from the Middle‑East war. Gas companies, including the AEP, fight back, saying the tax hurts trade with Japan and South Korea. The government sees a chance to shift public opinion and move ahead with the levy.
