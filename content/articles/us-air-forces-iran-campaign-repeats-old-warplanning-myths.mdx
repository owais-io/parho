The article shows how Trump’s plan to use the U.S. air force against Iran repeats ideas from a 100‑year‑old Italian general who said wars could be won by bombing civilian targets to break morale. U.S. leaders have long claimed air power can finish wars without ground troops, but history—from WWII to Iraq and Kosovo—has shown that huge bombing campaigns rarely end conflicts. Trump’s new campaign, with promises of precision bombs and AI, follows the same old pattern.
