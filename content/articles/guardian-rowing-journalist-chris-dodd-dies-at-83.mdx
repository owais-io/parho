Chris Dodd, a Guardian reporter who made rowing stories engaging, died at 83. He was known for fair, plain‑spoken coverage and a famous 1987 Boat Race mutiny piece that earned the Oxford crew’s respect. Dodd also founded the River and Rowing Museum in Henley, becoming its first curator. He wrote many books, helped shape modern rowing journalism, and stayed active despite Parkinson’s disease. He left behind a lasting legacy.
