Former US senator Joe Manchin urged lawmakers to stop fighting and keep the political climate calm after the recent wave of violence, including the death of conservative activist Charlie Kirk. He said Democrats and Republicans should leave the extremes behind and work together. Manchin, who became an independent, called for respectful debate and a sensible majority to push policy. Republican Senator Rand Paul also warned that gerrymandering could spark more tension.
