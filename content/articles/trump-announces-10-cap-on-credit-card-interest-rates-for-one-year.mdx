Donald Trump said he will limit credit card interest rates to 10% for a year, starting Jan. 20. He tweeted the plan after years of high rates that hit $1.17 trillion in debt. Some lawmakers, like Bernie Sanders and Josh Hawley, had already introduced a similar bill, but banks and some senators say a cap would hurt lenders and could lead to card cancellations. Trump’s move got mixed reactions, with some support and some warnings about the impact.
