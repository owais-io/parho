People still love old DVDs, CDs, and even coffee makers because they’re tangible and require a bit of effort. In a world where everything is instant, pulling a DVD off a shelf feels like a small ritual. The article explains how the physical touch and limited choice of old tech give us a sense of control, nostalgia, and a break from endless scrolling. It shows that even broken gadgets have charm and teach us to appreciate simplicity.
