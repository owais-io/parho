President Trump told the US cabinet that any nation producing illegal drugs could face military strikes, naming Venezuela and even Colombia. Colombian President Gustavo Petro slammed the threat as a war declaration, insisting it would damage 200 years of friendship and inviting Trump to watch drug‑lab raids. The two leaders’ relationship has already frayed, with the US revoking Petro’s visa and criticizing his anti‑drug campaign. The tension reflects a new US‑Latin America conflict over drug enforcement.
