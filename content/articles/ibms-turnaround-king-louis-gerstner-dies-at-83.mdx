IBM’s chief, Louis Gerstner, who saved the company in the 1990s, died at 83. He took over when IBM was losing money and facing rivals like Microsoft. Instead of breaking the company into separate units, he kept it whole, focusing on integrated solutions customers wanted. His bold move to drop the OS/2 project and push for profits steadied IBM, turning it from a loss‑maker into a global tech leader.
