Chile is about to open its 47th national park, Cape Froward, covering 200,000 hectares of wilderness. The park will finish a 2,800‑km wildlife corridor that runs from the world’s northernmost point to the southern tip of the Americas. It hosts animals like the huemul deer and endangered river otter, plus peat bogs that store carbon. Conservation groups and the government protected the land, and the new park will feature a restored lighthouse museum and café for visitors.
