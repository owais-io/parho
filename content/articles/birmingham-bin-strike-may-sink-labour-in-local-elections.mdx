Unite’s general secretary Sharon Graham warned that the Birmingham bin‑collection strike could wipe out Labour seats in the May local elections. The strike began over pay cuts that could cost workers £8,000 a year and has turned into an indefinite walk‑out. Graham said voters are turning away from Labour, citing the council’s handling of the dispute and the union’s decision to cut its affiliation fee by 40%. The council and union have yet to reach a deal.
