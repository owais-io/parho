Cuba is on the brink of a humanitarian crisis. Decades of a U.S. embargo have left the island with shortages of fuel, electricity, medicine and food. Health services are under pressure, and new diseases like dengue and yellow fever are rising. The population has dropped below 8 million as young people emigrate, and the country is increasingly isolated and vulnerable.
