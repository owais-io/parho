Met police officers secretly watched the family of Jean Charles de Menezes, who was mistakenly shot by police in 2005. The family had been campaigning for justice after his death, and the surveillance records show officers tracked their meetings and plans, including a flower‑laying ceremony. The Met admitted the spying was unjustifiable. A public inquiry is looking at how undercover officers have long monitored grieving families and activists.
