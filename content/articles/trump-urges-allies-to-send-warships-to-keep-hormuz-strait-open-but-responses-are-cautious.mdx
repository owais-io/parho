After Iran blocked the Hormuz waterway, President Trump asked countries like the UK, Japan, South Korea and others to send warships to protect the route. The call came on Truth Social, and he said all oil‑shipping nations should help. Most governments replied with caution, saying they would discuss options but were reluctant to commit to a naval presence that could risk their crews. Some considered drones or escort missions, but no firm plans were announced.
