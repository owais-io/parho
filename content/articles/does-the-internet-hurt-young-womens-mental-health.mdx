The Guardian writer Isabel Brooks reacts to Freya India’s book “Girls,” which blames social media for harming young women’s mental health. Brooks argues the story is one‑sided, noting that depression trends began before the internet. She points to lost independence, stricter supervision, and other social pressures. The piece calls for a balanced view, urging empowerment rather than doom‑minded narratives. It urges young people to use social media thoughtfully and seek supportive communities.
