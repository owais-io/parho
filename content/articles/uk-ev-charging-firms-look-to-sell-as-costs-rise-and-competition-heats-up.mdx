British electric‑vehicle charging firms are struggling with higher costs and fierce competition, prompting many to seek buyers. Investors who poured money into the sector during the pandemic are now looking for returns, pushing consolidation. The market could shrink from about 150 operators to just five or six major players. Smaller companies are buying niche sites or merging to save money and improve scale, while big names like Shell still dominate.
