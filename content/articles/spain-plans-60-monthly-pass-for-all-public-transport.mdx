Spain’s government is rolling out a public‑transport pass that lets anyone travel by bus or train anywhere in the country for €60 a month. The plan, announced by Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez, starts in late January and aims to cut commuters’ costs, especially for those under 26, who could pay just €30. The move comes while Sánchez’s coalition faces corruption scandals and calls for early elections, but he insists it shows the government’s focus on everyday life.
