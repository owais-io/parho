Because of the US‑Israel war on Iran, many holidaymakers from the UK and mainland Europe are ditching the eastern Mediterranean and heading to places like Italy, Spain, Malta, Croatia, and even the Caribbean. Travel companies say demand for western European and Caribbean trips has jumped, while flights to Cyprus, Turkey and Greece are being cancelled. Prices are soaring, and airlines are reshuffling routes. The conflict is costing the region millions in lost tourism revenue.
