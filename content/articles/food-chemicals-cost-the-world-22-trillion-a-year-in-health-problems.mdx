Scientists warn that common chemicals in food packaging and farming are linked to cancers, birth defects, and brain damage. A study estimates the health cost at up to $2.2 trillion each year—about as much as the world’s largest companies earn. It also projects 200‑700 million fewer births by 2100 if exposure stays the same. The report calls for urgent action, saying chemical pollution is as serious as climate change.
