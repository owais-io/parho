Four young men linked to a far‑right “active club” group were found guilty of attacking three men in Stockholm on a night in August. The court said the attacks were hate crimes because the victims were chosen for their ethnicity. The men received prison terms ranging from six months to three years and must pay damages. This is the first time Sweden has punished a group’s racist assaults, highlighting the country’s fight against extremist violence.
