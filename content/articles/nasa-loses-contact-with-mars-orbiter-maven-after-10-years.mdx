NASA lost contact with the Maven spacecraft that has orbited Mars for more than ten years. The probe stopped communicating after passing behind the planet. NASA says the vehicle was working normally before the eclipse and is looking into why the signal vanished. Maven has studied Mars' upper atmosphere and helped send messages for the Curiosity and Perseverance rovers. Two other active Mars orbiters remain.
