Jenni Murray was a BBC broadcaster who ran Woman’s Hour for decades. She was known for her friendly, warm voice and her mix of light chat, deep interviews, and tough topics like domestic abuse and sexism. Murray was a natural storyteller, often mixing humour with serious stories, and she used her platform to spotlight real women’s issues. She faced sexism early in her career, but she broke barriers and became a beloved radio personality.
