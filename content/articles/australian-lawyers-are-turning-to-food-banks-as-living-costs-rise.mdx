In Australia, even people who earn six‑figure salaries are struggling to keep food on their tables. Jess Townsend, a $90,000 lawyer, has had to use a food bank once a year. Prices for groceries and houses have gone up faster than wages, pushing many middle‑class families into food insecurity. A Food Bank Australia report says one in five high‑income households faced hunger last year. Rising rent, energy bills and car costs are driving the crisis.
