Cape Town’s housing market has gotten out of control, pushing up prices and rents by over 30 % in five years. Many residents blame Airbnb listings for squeezing the middle class out of the city. The city still has huge gaps left over from apartheid, with 18 % of people living in informal settlements and 400 000 on a social‑housing wait list. The mayor has released new affordable‑housing land, and a new tax rule will make short‑term rentals pay commercial rates.
