People and charity volunteers are working to stop a suspected bird flu outbreak among swans in the Thames Valley. Since October, 324 swan deaths have been recorded, with 39 in the first four weeks of 2026 alone. Volunteers report dozens of dead swans, and tests are underway. Officials warn the public not to touch sick or dead birds and to wash hands. The aim is to stop the virus from spreading to other species.
