Penelope Putz, a member of the Quaker Fox family, went to Mount School in York and was active in student protests. She joined the 2003 anti‑Iraq war march in London and later took part in a 1940s demonstration about food inequality at the school. She taught there from 1987‑2006 and wrote a history of the school. Her life shows how student activism and wartime events shaped her.
