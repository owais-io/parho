A deadly fire tore through a three‑storey South Korean car‑parts factory, killing 14 people and injuring nearly 60. The blaze spread so fast that workers were seen leaping from windows before firefighters could arrive. Firefighters had to first remove sodium, a reactive chemical, before attacking the flames. 170 workers were inside. The company is cooperating with an investigation as officials look into safety failures.
