In Gisborne, New Zealand, a guided tour lets visitors touch and feed large stingrays. In a shallow reef, gentle rays glide close, eager for pats. The operator, Dean Savage, limits contact so the animals stay independent, making the visit both a thrilling chance to see a 300‑kg creature up close and a conservation lesson that teaches respect for marine life and Māori cultural values.
