Kemi Badenoch, the Conservative leader, kicked off the party’s campaign for local elections in Westminster. The event was crowded with party members, activists and a few old politicians. Badenoch spoke about plans to support the North Sea, add police, cut drug use and change welfare. Her speech felt disjointed, with mixed messages and unclear policy details. The crowd cheered, but many questions remained about her realistic strategy and the party’s chances in the upcoming elections.
