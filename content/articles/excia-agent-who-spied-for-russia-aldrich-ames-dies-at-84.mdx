Aldrich Ames, the CIA spy who sold secrets to the Soviet Union and Russia, died in a Maryland prison at age 84. He and his wife were caught in 1994 and pleaded guilty to espionage. Ames spent almost a decade passing names of Western agents and other intelligence to Moscow, earning $2.5 million. He said money problems drove him, and later claimed his spying didn’t really hurt the U.S., a view the court did not accept.
