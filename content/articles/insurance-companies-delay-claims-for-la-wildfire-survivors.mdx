After the LA wildfires, many homeowners lost their homes. Their insurance companies, especially State Farm, promised help but then delayed payments, offered low estimates, and sent multiple adjusters. Survivors like the Conkles had to fight for months for fair payouts and rebuilding money. The problem is widespread, showing a bigger crisis where insurers profit while customers struggle. Regulators and activists demand fairer rules.
