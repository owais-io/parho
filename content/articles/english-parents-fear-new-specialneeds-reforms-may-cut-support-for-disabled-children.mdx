A survey of 1,000 parents of disabled children in England found that half are nervous about the government’s upcoming reforms to special‑needs education. Many fear that their children’s legal support plans could be cut or weakened. The changes, set to be released in February, would change how schools and local authorities fund and provide care. Parents say the current system is stressful, under‑funded, and lets some children fall through the cracks.
