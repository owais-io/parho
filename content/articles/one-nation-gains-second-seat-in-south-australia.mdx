Pauline Hanson’s One Nation party has won a second seat in South Australia, topping the polls with 22.3% of the primary vote—more than the Liberals. Labor won a landslide, but many voters chose One Nation as a protest against the major parties. The new seat comes in the lower house, and the party will now have a voice in the state parliament. The result shows One Nation can turn polls into seats.
