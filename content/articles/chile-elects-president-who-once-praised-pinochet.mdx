Chile’s new president, José Antonio Kast, has won by appealing to people who still admire the late dictator Augusto Pinochet. Kast, who campaigned in the 1980s for Pinochet’s regime, has made the past a key part of his political brand. A study shows about a third of Chileans still view Pinochet positively. This election shows a lingering support for authoritarian ideas and highlights the need for better education about the country's violent past.
