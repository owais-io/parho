Ross Coleman talks about starting a speech therapy program and finding motivation in people who don’t stammer, like Jonathan Groff. He also points out that many well‑known figures actually have stammered, from Marilyn Monroe to Bruce Willis, and even literary giants like Charles Darwin and Lewis Carroll. The Guardian’s article lists these names to show that stammering doesn’t stop success, hoping to give hope and inspiration to young people who stammer.
