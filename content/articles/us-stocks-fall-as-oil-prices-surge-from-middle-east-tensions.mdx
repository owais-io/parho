US stock indices slid again on Friday, marking a fourth week of losses as worries about the US‑Israel conflict with Iran spiked oil prices. The Dow dropped over 400 points, the S&P 500 fell 1.5% and the Nasdaq slipped 2%. Small‑cap stocks hit a correction, and the Russell 2000 fell 10% from its recent peak. Brent crude hit $107 a barrel and U.S. gasoline rose to nearly $4 a gallon, raising costs for commuters and businesses. The Strait of Hormuz is blocked, and the Pentagon has moved 2,200 Marines to the region amid escalating tensions.
