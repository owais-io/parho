The war in Iran has shut major Middle‑East airports like Dubai and Doha for days, jamming global flights. Airlines are racing to find backup routes and spare planes, sending some flights on odd paths or stopping over in other cities. Passengers are stuck and airlines face huge costs from idle planes and hotel bills. The chaos may keep hurting travel for weeks, even after the fighting ends.
