Mary Ann Patten was a 19‑year‑old, pregnant woman who became the first female to command a merchant clipper. When her husband fell ill and a mutinous crew threatened to take over, she stepped up, gave a powerful speech, and convinced the sailors to let her lead. She navigated the ship through a deadly storm at Cape Horn, avoiding ice and keeping everyone safe. Historian Tilar Mazzeo is telling her story in a new book.
