Storms in southwest England are happening faster and with heavier rain, 20 years ahead of climate models. Scientists say flooding is already a problem that the government isn’t funding enough to fix. Local councils are scrambling to pump water out, but budgets are shrinking. Without more money and nature‑based defenses, some communities may have to be abandoned or lose their homes.
