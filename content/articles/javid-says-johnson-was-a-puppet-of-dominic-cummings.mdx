Javid, former UK chancellor, said he quit twice during Boris Johnson's time because he felt Johnson was a puppet of Dominic Cummings. In 2020 he left after Johnson wanted to replace his Treasury advisers with ones picked by Cummings; in 2022 he resigned again after learning lockdown party allegations were true. Javid compared Johnson unfavorably to earlier PMs and praised David Cameron. He also stressed that ministers need time to eat and travel between meetings.
