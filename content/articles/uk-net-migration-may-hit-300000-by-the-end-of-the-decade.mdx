Prof. Brian Bell, head of the UK’s migration advisory group, says net migration could rise to about 300,000 by the decade’s end, up from 204,000 today. He expects a rebound as more overseas students and workers return after the pandemic slowdown. Although the Office for National Statistics sees a drop this year, the Office for Budget Responsibility and Bell predict a mid‑term bounce‑back. The report also warns that family visas can strain public finances.
