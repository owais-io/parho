The Guardian, with Israeli partners, investigated how Microsoft, Google, Amazon and other tech companies helped Israel collect and store Palestinian phone calls and use AI to pick targets for its Gaza war. After Oct 7 the army demanded more data storage and analysis tools, leading to huge use of AI scoring civilians as enemies. Microsoft cut some services, and workers inside the firms are uneasy about their role. The story shows how tech powers modern warfare and sparks big ethical questions.
