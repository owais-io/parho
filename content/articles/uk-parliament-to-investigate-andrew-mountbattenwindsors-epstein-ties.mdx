Andrew Mountbatten‑Windsor, a former trade envoy and member of the British royal family, was linked to financier Jeffrey Epstein and was arrested last week for alleged misconduct in public office. Parliament, not the government, will probe his Epstein connections and his role as trade envoy from 2001‑2011. Australia and New Zealand say they would back removing him from the line of succession, but that would need a new law and Commonwealth approval.
