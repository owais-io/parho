Detroit police decided not to fire two officers who called border patrol during a stop. Residents and activists say the police should not help federal immigration. The officers were suspended for 30 days without pay, but the chief said he would not terminate them. Republicans, ICE and a Michigan governor supported the officers, while local leaders demanded the department follow city rules. The case highlights tension between local law enforcement and federal immigration policy.
