In December, the U.S. added about 50,000 jobs, the smallest monthly increase since the pandemic, falling short of the 73,000 economists expected. The unemployment rate dipped to 4.4%. This slowdown marks the slowest job‑growth year since 2020. The White House accidentally shared early data on social media, sparking a protocol review. The Federal Reserve may keep interest rates steady as it watches the weak labor market.
