The article marks the 30th anniversary of the Women’s Fiction Prize, started to give women a voice after an all‑male Booker shortlist in 1991. It interviews critic Catherine Taylor, who remembers the 1990s as a male‑dominated scene. Over three decades the prize helped more women win major awards, though some still say men are being sidelined. The piece looks at reading habits, notes women buy most fiction, and questions why men’s work is seen as less “interior”.
