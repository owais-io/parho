The writer explains how animals have been sharing gifts for millions of years and gives fun examples. Cuckoos hand out protein‑rich insects, shrikes create “kebab” gifts, and penguins drop pebbles that become nest‑building presents. Crows love shiny bottle caps, while dolphins wear sea sponges as hats that protect and moisturise. The piece ends with a light‑hearted wish to receive a dolphin‑made sponge hat.
