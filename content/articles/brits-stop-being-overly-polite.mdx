The writer argues that Brits habitually avoid directness and over‑think politeness, which can make social situations awkward. She uses examples like checking house temperatures, specifying tea preferences, and refusing to pay a bill. By encouraging blunt honesty—saying no when you can’t or being clear about your needs—she believes people can feel more comfortable and avoid unnecessary awkwardness in everyday life.
