Human‑rights lawyer Michael Mansfield has slammed the UK government for refusing to talk with prisoners from Palestine Action who are on hunger strike. He says the government’s “perverse incentive” argument is nonsense and that refusing to meet is a dismissal of the strikers’ serious health risks. Mansfield urges the justice secretary to allow the prisoners to speak with officials and consider early release or proper treatment.
