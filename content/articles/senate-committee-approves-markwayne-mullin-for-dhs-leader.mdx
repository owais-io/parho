A Senate committee on Thursday voted nearly along party lines to move forward Markwayne Mullin’s nomination to head the Department of Homeland Security. All eight Republicans, except chair Rand Paul, and one Democrat, John Fetterman, voted for him. Mullin faced questions over his support for Trump‑style immigration enforcement and past remarks about encouraging violence. The nomination now heads to the full Senate for a confirmation vote.
