The novel Noughts & Crosses, now 25 years old, was a game‑changer for young‑adult fiction, tackling racism and class in a reversed‑order Britain. It inspired a stage play, a BBC series, and the National Year of Reading. Yet a recent study shows fewer Black protagonists in children’s books, highlighting a need for real, lasting diversity in publishing.
