The article explains why many towns, like Hartlepool, are running out of spots for memorial benches—public seats with names on them to remember loved ones. These benches keep memories alive in everyday places instead of quiet cemeteries, letting people sit together or alone while still feeling close to the person. The trend shows a shift toward public mourning and a desire to keep people present in daily life.
