Michael Pham, a former Vietnamese refugee, now leads San Diego’s Catholic diocese. He and about 500 volunteers walk with immigrants into federal courthouses, praying and offering a human presence that can calm ICE officers and reduce arrests. The program, called Faith, started after he saw too many innocent people being taken away. Pham’s own journey and the diocese’s past abuse scandals shape his mission to protect and listen to vulnerable people.
