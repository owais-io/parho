Mr. Dugal Nisbet‑Smith, who died at 90, was a journalist who worked in New Zealand, London, the Caribbean, Scotland and the UK. He started as a cub reporter, moved to the Daily Express, then ran the Barbados Advocate. Later he led the Scottish Daily Record and Times Newspapers, sparking a strike that paved the way for Rupert Murdoch. He advised the Aga Khan, headed the Newspaper Society, and fought for press freedom. He loved painting, sculpting and watching All Blacks games.
