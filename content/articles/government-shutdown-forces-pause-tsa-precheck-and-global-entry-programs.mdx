Due to a government shutdown, the Department of Homeland Security has temporarily stopped the TSA PreCheck and Global Entry programs, which normally let approved travelers move through security faster. The pause starts at 6 a.m. ET Sunday, giving travelers and airlines very little warning. Airlines say the cutback hurts the industry, citing a $6.1 billion loss last year. DHS says it is prioritizing regular passengers while the shutdown continues.
