An 80‑kg bronze eagle, once on a German warship, was recovered from the River Plate in 2006. It was briefly shown in Montevideo, but the government moved it to a military base to avoid it becoming a neo‑Nazi shrine. Now Uruguay is debating whether to melt it, put it in a museum, or display it on a seaside resort. The issue has sparked legal battles and public protests over how to handle a Nazi symbol.
