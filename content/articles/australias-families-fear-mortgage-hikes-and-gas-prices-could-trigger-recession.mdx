Australia’s Reserve Bank has raised interest rates twice this year, and gas prices are climbing because of the war in Iran. The combined effect means families are losing more than $1 billion a month from their budgets. Mortgage payments are up by around $200 for many, while petrol costs have jumped over 50 cents a litre. Economists warn the country could slip into recession unless spending slows.
