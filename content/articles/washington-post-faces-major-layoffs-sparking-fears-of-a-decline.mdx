The Washington Post has cut almost one‑third of its 2,500‑person staff, shutting down its sports, local news, audio‑video and commercial teams. Former editor Marty Baron warned the cuts could hurt subscribers and set the paper on a “death spiral.” New editor Matt Murray said the paper will now focus on politics, but it faces strong competition. Union leaders and some journalists are calling for more investment or a sale.
