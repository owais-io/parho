A federal judge in North Carolina is hearing a case that questions a new law tightening rules for people who register on election day. Cleta Mitchell, a Trump ally who ran for a 2020‑election‑fraud commission, says the law is meant to cut away same‑day registration, especially for college students, claiming it could let fraud happen. The trial shows the activists’ plans and the lawsuit’s argument that the change unfairly targets young voters.
