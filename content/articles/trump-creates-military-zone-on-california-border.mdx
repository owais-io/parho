Trump's administration announced that 760 acres of land in California will be put under Navy control for three years as a new military zone to help stop illegal crossings. The area, in San Diego and Imperial counties, will allow the military to arrest migrants and use more charges. While it says it fixes security gaps, border arrests have dropped to a 60‑year low. A judge also blocked a federal takeover of California's National Guard.
