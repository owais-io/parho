Australia keeps cricket open to everyone, with games in parks, on beaches, and at big stadiums that are owned by the public, not private clubs. The article shows how fans visit Bradman Oval, play backyard matches, and enjoy big crowds at the MCG. In contrast, England has turned cricket into a paid, privatized sport. The piece highlights the friendly, inclusive Aussie cricket culture and why it matters.
