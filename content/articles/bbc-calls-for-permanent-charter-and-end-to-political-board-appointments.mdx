The BBC says it needs a permanent charter and wants to stop political people on its board. The move comes after the director‑general and news chief resigned, which staff blamed on board politics. Fans want the BBC to stay independent, so the broadcaster is pushing for a charter that won’t need re‑approval every decade. It also faces money cuts and a need to stay relevant amid streaming rivals.
