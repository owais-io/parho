Many people secretly wish they could date their therapist, and it’s more common than you think. Psychologists explain this as transference – the client’s feelings and fantasies get projected onto the therapist. While it can reveal deep emotional needs, it can also lead to messy boundaries. Therapists are urged to talk openly about such feelings, and professional groups set rules to keep relationships ethical. Still, some cases end badly.
