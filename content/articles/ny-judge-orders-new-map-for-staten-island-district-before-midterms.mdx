A New York judge said the 11th congressional district, which covers Staten Island and parts of Brooklyn, must be redrawn because its lines dilute Black and Latino voters. The decision requires the state's bipartisan commission to produce a new map by Feb. 6, giving Democrats a better shot at winning the seat. Republicans say the change is a political trick. The ruling is part of a nationwide battle over redistricting before the 2026 midterms.
