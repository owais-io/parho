ABC employees announced a 24‑hour strike on Wednesday, the first since 2006. The strike will stop most TV, radio and online news, affecting about 65% of Australia’s viewers. Unions rejected the broadcaster’s offer of a 10% pay rise over three years, saying it’s below inflation and doesn’t cover job security, career progress and night‑shift pay. Management says the deal is sustainable, while workers fear losing jobs to automation and low wages.
