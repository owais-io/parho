South Korea’s court has handed former president Yoon Suk‑Yeol a life sentence with labour for trying to seize power by declaring martial law on 3 Dec 2024. Yoon’s plan to block Parliament, arrest rivals and stop elections was ruled an insurrection. He was impeached and removed from office before the trial. The judge noted his refusal to apologise and the damage to the country’s stability. Yoon will appeal, and several of his aides also received prison terms.
