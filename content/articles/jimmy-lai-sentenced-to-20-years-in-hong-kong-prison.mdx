Jimmy Lai, the founder of the now‑closed Apple Daily and a key pro‑democracy voice, was given a 20‑year prison term after a national‑security trial. The court found him guilty of colluding with foreign forces and publishing seditious material. Human‑rights groups say the sentence is a death sentence and call for his release. Lai’s health is worsening in solitary, and his family urges the UK and US to intervene.
