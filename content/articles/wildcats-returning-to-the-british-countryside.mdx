The author walks through pine forests after a snowfall and finds tracks of a red squirrel, hare, badger and, most excitingly, a wildcat. He shares what the Saving Wildcats project has done since 2015: breeding cats in captivity, fitting them with GPS collars and releasing them into the wild. So far 46 cats have survived, some roam huge distances and even have kittens in the countryside.
