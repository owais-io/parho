Relx, a big UK data company, saw its shares fall after AI plug‑ins called Claude were released. Investors feared the company’s high profits might shrink. Despite this, Relx posted solid growth, raised its dividend, and announced a larger share‑buyback program. The CEO said AI will keep boosting value, and the company plans to keep buying back shares at the lower price to support earnings.
