Hungary’s Prime Minister Viktor Orbán has written to the EU, asking for a fact‑finding team to examine the Druzhba oil pipeline that runs through Slovakia and Hungary. He says the pipeline is a key route for Ukraine’s energy and that its status is blocking a €90 bn loan to Ukraine and new sanctions against Russia. Orbán wants the mission to include Hungarian and Slovak experts and says Hungary will accept any findings. Meanwhile, Nordic countries are on high alert after a threat that a foreign‑linked actor could attack their energy networks. The story also notes that US and Ukrainian negotiators are meeting in Geneva to discuss reconstruction after the war.
