Peter Tatchell, a well‑known gay‑rights activist, was taken into custody in London for holding a sign that said “globalise the intifada” during a pro‑Palestine march. Police said the word intifada was unlawful, but Tatchell says it simply means uprising, not violence. He calls the arrest a crackdown on free speech and is planning legal action. The incident follows other arrests of protesters in the city.
