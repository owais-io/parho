The Trump administration has nominated Darin Smith, a former Wyoming state senator who has never tried a case, to become the US attorney for the state. Smith’s record shows opposition to LGBTQ+ rights, support for January 6 protests, and a history of pushing anti‑trans bills. Democrats say he lacks the legal experience needed and could bias federal law enforcement. The nomination is now before the Senate for confirmation.
