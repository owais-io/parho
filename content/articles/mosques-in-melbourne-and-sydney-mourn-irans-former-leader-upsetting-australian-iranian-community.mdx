After Iran’s supreme leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, died at 86, a handful of Shia mosques in Melbourne and Sydney held memorials. Many Australian Iranians felt hurt, saying Khamenei was a tyrant who killed thousands. NSW Premier Chris Minns called the vigils “atrocious.” Some community leaders said the mournings were religious, not political, but the events sparked strong debate among Australians of Iranian background.
