In Storeton Wood on the Wirral, a former quarry now a woodland, I searched for a replica of ancient footprints. Those footprints, left by a Triassic reptile called Chirotherium, were first found in 1838 and sent to museums. Today, a carved replica marks the site, connecting visitors to a creature that lived 240 million years ago. The walk turned into a time‑travel adventure through ancient history and local heritage.
