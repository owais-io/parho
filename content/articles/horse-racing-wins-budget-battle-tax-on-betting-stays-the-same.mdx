Thanks to a campaign called “Axe The Racing Tax,” the UK government decided not to raise the tax on horse‑race betting. The rate stays at 15%, while other gambling taxes will jump – online casino games go to 40% and non‑race sports betting rises to 25%. The change was praised by racing officials and the BHA, who say the move keeps the sport competitive and helps it grow.
