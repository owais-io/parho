A former Australian Border Force officer, Rohan Pike, calls himself the country’s top illicit‑tobacco expert while also advising nicotine‑product groups. Public‑health voices say he should disclose these industry ties, arguing that his advice—against higher cigarette taxes and for nicotine pouches—mirrors tobacco companies’ positions. Experts warn that hidden links let the industry shape policy and that governments need to see who is speaking on the record.
