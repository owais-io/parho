A former Canadian snowboarder who once competed in the 2002 Olympics has been arrested by U.S. authorities as the alleged head of a huge cocaine ring that sold up to $1 billion a year. The man, named Ryan Wedding, is accused of ordering murders, using a lawyer to cover up crimes, and operating in Mexico with Sinaloa cartel help. His case shows how quickly a sports star can fall into crime.
