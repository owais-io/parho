A freshwater crocodile, normally living in northern Australia, was spotted swimming in Ironbark Creek in Newcastle—about 1,000 km from its natural home. Teenagers called the police on Saturday, and wildlife experts from the Australian Reptile Park and the State Emergency Service worked overnight to capture the about 1‑metre‑long female. She was taken to a park on the Central Coast for health checks, likely after escaping from a pet.
