Kenyan courts sentenced two men to 15 years for robbing and beating two gay men in Nairobi. LGBTQ+ groups say it’s a rare moment of justice in a country where homosexuality is still criminalised and where queer people face hate crimes and blackmail. The case follows a recent blackmail attack that left victims injured and forced to pay money. Activists hope the ruling will deter future attacks, though fear and lack of police protection remain high.
