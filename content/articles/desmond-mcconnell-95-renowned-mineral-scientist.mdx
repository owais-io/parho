Desmond McConnell, 95, was a leading mineral scientist who taught at Cambridge and Oxford and worked in industry. He helped reveal unusual crystal behaviours, studied lunar dust, and advanced understanding of plagioclase feldspars. He led the Schlumberger Cambridge research building’s design and later chaired Oxford’s earth‑science department. A Fellow of the Royal Society and recipient of the Humboldt prize, he also sang, painted, and loved nature. He is survived by his wife, children, grandchildren and great‑grandchildren.
