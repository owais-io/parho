The letter argues that putting all children with SEND into mainstream schools is not always helpful. Many disabled kids face higher exclusion and lower attendance, and they often lack the support they need. Parents and teachers say special schools give smaller classes, trained staff and a sense of community that mainstream schools sometimes miss. The writer calls for clear policies that recognize both mainstream inclusion and the value of special schools.
