Alabama Gov. Kay Ivey decided to cancel the death sentence of 75‑year‑old Charles “Sonny” Burton, who was set to be executed for a 1991 robbery‑related murder. Ivey said it was unfair that the real shooter got a life sentence while Burton faced death. Protesters, the victim’s family and critics urged her to stay the execution, but she acted to make the punishment fair.
