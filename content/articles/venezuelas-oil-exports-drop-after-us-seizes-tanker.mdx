Venezuela's oil exports fell sharply after the U.S. seized a tanker. The Skipper, seized near Venezuela, is the first time the U.S. captured Venezuelan oil cargo since 2019 sanctions. Now only a U.S. company, Chevron, is moving Venezuelan oil. Roughly 11 million barrels are stuck in Venezuelan waters. The U.S. plans more seizures, and Venezuela calls the action theft and vows to protest.
