Five Stanford students are on trial for felony charges after a June protest where they locked themselves in the president’s office to demand the university divest from Israel. They vandalized the room, were suspended, and were later charged by the county district attorney. The defendants plead not guilty and want the case to spotlight what they say is Stanford’s role in the Gaza conflict. If convicted, they could face three years in prison. The university also seeks restitution.
