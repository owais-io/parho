Prosecutor Thien Ho asked the judge to make Joseph DeAngelo, the Golden State Killer, wear a clear face shield when he pleaded guilty. Ho, who led the case, wrote a book that tells how investigators used new DNA genealogy to catch DeAngelo after 40 years of unsolved crimes. He was convicted for over 100 burglaries, 51 rapes, and 13 murders, and is now serving life without parole. The book also honors the victims and shows how modern science changed policing.
