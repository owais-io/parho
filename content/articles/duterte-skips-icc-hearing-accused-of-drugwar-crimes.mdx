Former Philippine president Rodrigo Duterte, 80, is being charged by the International Criminal Court with crimes against humanity for the death‑filled drug crackdown that killed an estimated 12,000–30,000 people. The court will hold a pre‑trial hearing this week, but Duterte says he won’t attend because of age and memory problems, claiming the court has no authority. Victims’ families call the move cowardly and urge the ICC to bring him to justice.
