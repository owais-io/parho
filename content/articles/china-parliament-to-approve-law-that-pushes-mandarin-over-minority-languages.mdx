China’s top lawmakers will vote on a new ‘ethnic unity’ bill that will make Mandarin the main language in schools and public signs, pushing minority tongues like Tibetan, Uyghur and Mongolian to the background. The law, part of a broader package that also includes an environmental code and a five‑year plan, is seen as a formal step toward the Beijing policy of cultural assimilation. Critics say it legalises forced assimilation.
