In the United States, almost every experience is split into price tiers—cheap, mid‑range, and luxury—so that people pay more for “better” options. Airlines now charge extra for checked bags and snacks, movie theatres offer VIP seats, and ski resorts sell fast‑track passes. Even health care has “membership medicine” that costs up to $50,000 a year. These layers of premium pricing make everyday activities more expensive for most people.
