The Pentagon has put about 1,500 active‑duty soldiers in Alaska on standby for a possible trip to Minnesota, where protests against immigration raids are growing. The order comes after President Trump threatened to use the Insurrection Act if Minnesota officials do not curb protest activity. Local leaders say a military presence would only heighten tensions. The soldiers specialize in cold‑weather ops and could be sent if violence escalates.
