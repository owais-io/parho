A federal judge ruled that Robert F. Kennedy Jr.'s appointment of a new vaccine advisory group broke federal law. As a result, all the committee’s recent votes are void, and its meeting was postponed. The judge said the committee’s changes to the U.S. vaccine schedule were “arbitrary and capricious.” The ruling means the previous vaccine rules will stay in place for now, though the future of vaccine policy is still uncertain.
