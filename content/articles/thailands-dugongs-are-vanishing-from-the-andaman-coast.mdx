Thailand’s dugongs are disappearing from the Andaman coast, with the population dropping from about 270 to less than 140 in recent years. Experts blame the loss of seagrass, pollution, silt, and rising temperatures for the animals’ food crisis. A local photographer called Pop is using Instagram to raise awareness, while the UN’s new high‑seas treaty offers a chance to protect these marine mammals.
