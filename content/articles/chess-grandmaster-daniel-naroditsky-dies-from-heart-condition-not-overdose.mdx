American chess grandmaster Daniel Naroditsky died last year from a sudden heart rhythm problem caused by an undiagnosed heart‑involving disease called sarcoidosis. The medical examiner said drugs found in his blood—methamphetamine and a kratom compound—did not kill him. His death was ruled accidental, not suicide or overdose. Chess leaders are reviewing comments by former world champion Vladimir Kramnik, and FIDE may sanction him.
