A Sutton Trust study found that the country’s 500 best secondary schools accept only half as many students with special educational needs (Send) and low incomes as average state schools. Many leaders say schools are deliberately turning away these pupils to keep exam scores high and finances strong. The report calls for more staff, specialist help, and government funding to make schools truly inclusive.
