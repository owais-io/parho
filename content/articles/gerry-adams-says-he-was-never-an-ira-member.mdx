77‑year‑old Gerry Adams appeared in a London high‑court hearing to deny claims that he was an Irish Republican Army member or involved in its bombings. He insisted that Sinn Féin, the political party he led, is separate from the IRA. The case, brought by three victims of IRA attacks, seeks £1 in symbolic damages. Adams also warned the judge not to make off‑hand remarks and reiterated his stance that he never joined the IRA.
