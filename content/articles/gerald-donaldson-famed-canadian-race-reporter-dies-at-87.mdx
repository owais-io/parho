Gerald Donaldson was a Canadian journalist and broadcaster who spent 30 years covering Formula One. He is best known for his close, thoughtful interviews with famous drivers like Ayrton Senna, James Hunt and Juan Manuel Fangio. Donaldson wrote books about racing and helped bring the world of motorsport to ordinary readers. He died at 87, leaving a legacy of insightful writing about racing.
