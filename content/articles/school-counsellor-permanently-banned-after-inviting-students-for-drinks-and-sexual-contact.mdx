A New South Wales school counsellor, Victoria Worsley, was permanently banned by the Health Care Complaints Commission after she invited students to her home for alcoholic jelly shots and had sexual contact with them. The investigation found she crossed professional boundaries by meeting students outside school, offering drinks, and engaging in sexual acts. The ban applies to all health‑related jobs in NSW and across other Australian states, preventing her from any future counselling or social work duties.
