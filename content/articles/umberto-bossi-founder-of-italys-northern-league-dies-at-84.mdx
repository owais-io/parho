Umberto Bossi, who founded Italy’s Northern League and championed a separate region called Padania, died on March 18, 2026 at 84. He was known for provocative speeches, anti‑immigration comments, and a flamboyant style that shocked voters. Bossi helped shift Italy from the First to the Second Republic, served as a minister in Berlusconi’s governments, and was a controversial but influential figure in northern Italian politics.
