At a Melbourne protest last month, a police officer threw a crowd‑control grenade that exploded near a protester's umbrella. The blast burned Scout’s legs and arms and left bruises. No warning was given. Two protesters, including Scout, are thinking of suing the police for the injuries. Police say the device was used as allowed, but critics say it was dangerous and the use of such weapons at protests is risky.
