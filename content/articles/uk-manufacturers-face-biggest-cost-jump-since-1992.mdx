UK manufacturers are seeing the biggest jump in production costs in decades, driven by rising oil prices from the Middle East conflict. A PMI survey shows slower growth and the highest input‑price inflation since 1992. Fuel, transport and energy‑heavy materials are the main culprits. The slowdown hurts new orders and exports, leaving the Bank of England wary of future rate moves.
