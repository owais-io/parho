U.S. stocks slipped early Monday but bounced back after the U.S.–Israel strike on Iran. The Dow fell 0.15%, the S&P 500 nudged up 0.04%, and Nasdaq rose 0.36%. Tech names like Nvidia did well, while airlines lost ground. Global markets fell harder, and gas prices jumped about 40% after Iranian drone attacks hit QatarEnergy and tankers in the Strait of Hormuz. Oil rose nearly 7%, and JPMorgan’s Jamie Dimon said the war shouldn’t hit inflation unless it drags on.
