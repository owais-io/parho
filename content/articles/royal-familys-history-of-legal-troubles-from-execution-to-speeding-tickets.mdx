The British royal family has had a long history of legal troubles, from the execution of Charles I to modern scandals. Andrew Mountbatten‑Windsor was recently arrested, but similar incidents have occurred for decades. Edward VII was caught in a gambling trial, Princess Anne faced repeated speeding fines, Prince Philip crashed his Land Rover, and Prince Charles was questioned about Diana’s death. These events show a pattern of rule‑breaking across generations.
