Back in 1933, a Western Australian referendum saw 66% voting to break away, but the idea was shelved by war and legal hurdles. Today, a new book argues the state should become its country, citing Covid mismanagement, low mining taxes, and high living costs. It proposes a 20% tax on resource companies and compares WA to Norway or Qatar. Federal leaders and experts say secession would hurt the state’s economy, defense, and unity.
