President Trump wants the United States to stop buying rare‑earth metals from China, which make batteries, phones, and weapons. He has worked with other countries and built a new plant in South Carolina to start making the magnets in the U.S. The plan faces big hurdles: China controls most mining and refining, and U.S. projects need time and money to become profitable. The goal is to secure the metals that power the nation’s tech and defense.
