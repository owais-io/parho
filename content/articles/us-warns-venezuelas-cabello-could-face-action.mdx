U.S. officials warned Venezuelan interior minister Diosdado Cabello that if he refuses to support acting president Delcy Rodríguez, he could face the same fate as former leader Nicolás Maduro. Washington wants to first stabilize the country, then open the oil market to foreign firms and eventually hold elections. The U.S. has seized oil tankers carrying Venezuelan crude. Cabello controls police and armed militias, rivals Rodríguez, and has a record of human‑rights abuses.
