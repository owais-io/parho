After Bashar al‑Assad was removed, Syria is a year into a new era but still plagued by killings and lack of justice. Veterans and families of victims say evidence is missing and that former regime members keep disappearing. The new president has made friends with the West and lifted sanctions, but inside the country, sectarian attacks continue and people fear that if people take revenge, the country could unravel. The government has begun trials, but progress is slow.
