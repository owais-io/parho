Japan and the United States have announced a joint investment of about $36 billion in projects that include a huge natural‑gas power plant in Ohio, a deep‑water oil export terminal in Texas, and a factory for making synthetic diamonds in Georgia. The deals are part of a larger $550 billion commitment aimed at cutting reliance on China for critical minerals and strengthening economic ties. Trump said the projects will give the U.S. more energy security and industrial capacity.
