Greggs, Britain’s biggest bakery, keeps adding factories and opening new shops even as sales growth slows and its shares fall. The company is building new plants in Derby and Kettering to hit 3,500 outlets, but analysts worry the market might be saturated and prices are rising. The chief executive says demand is still strong, while investors question whether more stores will bring more customers or just shift them around.
