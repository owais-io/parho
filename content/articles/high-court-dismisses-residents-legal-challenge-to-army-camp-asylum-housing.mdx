Residents in East Sussex who wanted to stop the Home Office from turning an army training camp into a home for up to 540 asylum seekers lost their case in the High Court. A judge said the challenge was too early – the policy wasn’t set yet. The Home Office defended the move, while local residents plan to fight again. They argue the site’s wildlife and the men’s safety could be harmed.
