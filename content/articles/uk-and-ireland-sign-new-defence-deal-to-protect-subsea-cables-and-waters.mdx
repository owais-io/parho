The UK and Ireland have signed a new defence agreement in Cork, aiming to protect undersea cables and other critical infrastructure in Irish waters. The deal adds cyber‑threats and sabotage of submarine cables to the 2016 framework and follows incidents such as a Russian spy ship entering the sea. While the Royal Navy won’t patrol the area, the two sides will share information and coordinate responses to any attacks.
