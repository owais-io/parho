Scotland has just made water cremation – called hydrolysis – legal, becoming the first UK region to offer an eco‑friendly alternative to burial or traditional cremation. The process immerses a body in a strong alkaline solution for a few hours, leaving only bone dust that can be kept, scattered or buried. It emits no toxic fumes, saves space, and reduces coffin use. The first facility may open in about nine months.
