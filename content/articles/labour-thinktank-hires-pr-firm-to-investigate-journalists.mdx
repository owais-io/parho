A former Labour Together director, now a Cabinet Office minister, hired the PR agency APCO to look into journalists who exposed the thinktank’s money problems. The thinktank, tied to Keir Starmer’s rise, had paid money it didn’t declare, and its former chief of staff, Morgan McSweeney, is under scrutiny. MPs want a full inquiry, raising doubts about the minister’s involvement and the integrity of the party’s operations.
