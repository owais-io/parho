Sir Geoffrey Bindman was a human‑rights lawyer who fought for better race‑relations laws and helped refugees in Britain. He met weekly with black, Asian and Jewish friends at a breakfast table in London, sharing stories and planning action. His sharp mind and warm heart made him a champion for equality. He died at 89, leaving a legacy that reminds us to keep working for fairness and unity.
