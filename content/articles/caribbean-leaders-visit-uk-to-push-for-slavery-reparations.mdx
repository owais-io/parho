Caribbean leaders are travelling to the UK next week to ask the British government for apologies and money to make up for the slave trade. They’ll meet MPs, academics, and civil‑society groups to raise awareness and push for a formal apology and financial reparations. The visit follows the UK’s recent refusal to pay money for slavery and a growing public interest in the issue. The leaders want Britain to admit its past and help Caribbean countries recover.
