A California court said the state attorney general couldn’t stop the Riverside County sheriff from counting ballots taken from a 2025 election that passed a redistricting law. The sheriff, who ran for governor, says the results were wrong by about 45,000 votes, but officials say the difference is only about 100. The court said the AG should have sued in a lower court, and it backed the sheriff’s right to investigate.
