After seven years of a limit that let families claim support for only two kids, the Labour government has removed the cap. From April, low‑income households can get about £300 a month for each child beyond the second. The move should help 483,000 families, easing food, school and housing costs. The change costs £2.3bn next year but is seen as a cheap way to cut child poverty and improve kids’ health and education.
