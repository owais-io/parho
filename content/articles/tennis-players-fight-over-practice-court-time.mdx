At the US Open, top players like Medvedev, Zverev, Gauff, and Raducanu all have to share practice courts. Some stretch their time past the hour, which annoys others who need to play. Players debate how to politely ask someone to stop or how to handle it quietly. The article shows how this “court‑time fight” can create tension and how players try to balance respect with their training needs.
