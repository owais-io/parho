Plants and animals are disappearing at a scary pace—up to 150 species a day. The species that thrive with humans, like pigeons and rats, fill the empty spots. Scientists call this the “homogenocene.” It started with hunting in the ice age and continues today with farming and cities. But if we manage land wisely and control invasives, we can restore diversity.
