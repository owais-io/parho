China has removed travel bans and other restrictions on six UK MPs and peers who were barred in 2021 for criticizing the country’s treatment of Uyghurs. The move came after Prime Minister Keir Starmer met President Xi Jinping in Beijing. Starmer said the sanctions were lifted immediately, but he warned that China still keeps some academics and former MPs under restrictions. The decision signals a warming, but still cautious, relationship between the UK and China.
