Samia Baho runs a café in Sunshine, Melbourne, where she offers food and a job‑training hub for migrant and refugee women. After arriving from Eritrea in 1970, she started the Centre of Advancing Women to help women get work, tackle domestic violence, and learn skills. The café gives customers‑service experience, while volunteers share support and grant‑writing help. Despite no funding, 80% of 27 women gain paid jobs and feel empowered.
