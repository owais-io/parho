Reza Pahlavi, son of Iran’s former king, says he can lead a new Iran after the current regime falls. He hasn’t lived in the country since 1979 and says protests have killed thousands, but believes the regime will collapse anyway. Pahlavi wants Western support to weaken the Revolutionary Guard, but he’s silent on whether he’d restore a monarchy. Trump praised the regime’s pause on executions, adding to the debate.
