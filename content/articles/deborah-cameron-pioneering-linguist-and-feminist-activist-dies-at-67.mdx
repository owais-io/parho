Deborah Cameron, a pioneering linguist who showed how language shapes gender, died from pancreatic cancer at 67. She taught at Oxford and wrote influential books such as *Feminism and Linguistic Theory* and *The Myth of Mars and Venus*. Cameron campaigned against violence toward women, fought for freedom of speech and critiqued “political correctness.” Her students, including Caroline Criado Perez, credit her with sparking new ways to think about gender and language.
