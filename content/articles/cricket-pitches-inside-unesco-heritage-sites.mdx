Alex Preston recounts a cricket match he played with the Lord’s Taverners on a unique pitch in Corfu that sits inside a UNESCO world‑heritage area. He points out that other famous historic venues also have world‑heritage status, like Bath’s pitch and possibly two there, the Galle cricket ground next to Sri Lanka’s Fort, and even a field in London’s Greenwich Park. The piece celebrates how sport can thrive within protected heritage sites.
