A retired US Navy chief writes a passionate letter condemning President Trump’s remarks that UK troops who fought in Afghanistan were “a little off the frontlines.” He says the comments insult the bravery of those soldiers and their families, recalls his own service with the Royal Navy and RAF, and urges Trump to show real contrition by apologising publicly, perhaps at the National Memorial Arboretum. He warns that the nation’s shared values are at risk if leaders dismiss such sacrifices.
