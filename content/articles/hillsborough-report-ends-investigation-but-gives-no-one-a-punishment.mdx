The Independent Office for Police Conduct finished its 14‑year probe of the 1989 Hillsborough disaster. Its final report says 97 people died unlawfully but no police officer will face any punishment. Twelve officers could have been charged with gross misconduct, including former chief Peter Wright, but all are retired or dead. Families feel a mix of closure and disappointment, as no one is held accountable.
