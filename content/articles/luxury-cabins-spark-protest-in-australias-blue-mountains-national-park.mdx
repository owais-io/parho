Australia’s Blue Mountains national park, officially protected in 2022, is at the center of a heated debate. The state wildlife service wants to add 18 luxury cabins, calling them low‑impact “bush camps.” Conservationists call the move a resort and say it could lead to more private projects in wild places. Tourism firms argue such projects fund protection, while critics worry about losing untouched nature. Experts warn that private development could erode conservation standards.
