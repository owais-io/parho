A Guardian letter debate shows people split over how often we say “thank you.” Some readers say it’s a simple way to show respect to baristas, shop clerks, bus drivers and others who do hard jobs, while others think the phrase is over‑used or even insincere. Commenters from Dublin, Geneva, France, New York and the UK share personal anecdotes about gratitude, apology, and cultural habits.
