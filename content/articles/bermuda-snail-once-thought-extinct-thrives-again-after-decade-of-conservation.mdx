A tiny snail that lived only in Bermuda was feared gone forever until scientists found a few left in an alley in 2014. Over ten years, teams bred more than 100,000 snails in a UK zoo, then released them back on the island. Today the snails are thriving in six areas, proving that careful breeding and protection can save a species from extinction.
