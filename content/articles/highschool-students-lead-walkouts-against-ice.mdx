Teenagers in schools across the U.S. are walking out to protest Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). From Wisconsin to Texas, they plan and lead protests, sometimes without teachers’ permission, and share information about their rights. Many fear that ICE could target their families or classmates. Despite possible discipline or school opposition, the students keep their voices up, telling classmates that bravery means standing up for those who feel unsafe.
