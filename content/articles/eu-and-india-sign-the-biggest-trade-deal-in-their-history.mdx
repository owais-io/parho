India and the European Union have just signed the biggest trade deal in their history, opening markets for cars, wine and services. The deal came after 20 years of stalled talks and was driven by fears of US protectionism under Trump. It also signals a shift toward stronger ties outside the US, with new cooperation on security, research and the Indo‑Pacific. The agreement shows a growing move toward a multipolar world.
