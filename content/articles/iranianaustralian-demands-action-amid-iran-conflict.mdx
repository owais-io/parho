A second‑generation Iranian‑Australian and Baháʼí member of a persecuted minority in Iran shares his deep helplessness as war spreads. He recalls his family fleeing in 1979, ongoing persecution, forced confessions, and recent attacks on his community. While prayer offers comfort, he urges concrete action—raising awareness, supporting civil society, and working for peace. His message: faith must pair with tangible steps to protect rights.
