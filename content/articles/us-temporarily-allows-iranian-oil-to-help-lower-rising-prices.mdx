The U.S. has temporarily lifted sanctions on Iranian oil for 30 days to add about 140 million barrels to the global market and curb soaring prices caused by the war with Israel. The move is meant to protect U.S. businesses and voters ahead of the mid‑term elections, but critics worry it could help Iran fund its war. The waiver doesn’t let new Iranian oil enter the U.S., and the real impact will depend on the Strait of Hormuz reopening.
