Winter Storm Fern swept across the eastern United States, leaving over a million homes without power and killing seven people. At least 20 states declared emergencies and major airports cancelled thousands of flights. Schools in New York City were shut, while most subway lines stayed open. Many roads were icy, leading to fuel shortages and calls for people to stay home. The storm brought record cold and heavy snow to areas unaccustomed to such conditions.
