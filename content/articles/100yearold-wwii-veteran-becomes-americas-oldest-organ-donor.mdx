Dale Steele, a 100‑year‑old U.S. World War II veteran, died after a head injury but donated his liver before he passed. Live On Nebraska says that a healthy liver stays essentially “new” even in old donors. Using a warm‑blood perfusion technique, his liver was recovered and transplanted the next day, making him the oldest known organ donor in the United States. Steele had a long life on a Nebraska farm and in the military.
