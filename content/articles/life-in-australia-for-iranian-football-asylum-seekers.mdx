A group of Iranian women footballers have been granted humanitarian visas to Australia after leaving their country. They can now settle, learn English, and access job and education help. Support from community groups and cricket clubs helps them adjust. They face emotional ups and downs, but can eventually play for local teams and apply for permanent residency after four years. Their journey shows how sport and support services aid refugees.
