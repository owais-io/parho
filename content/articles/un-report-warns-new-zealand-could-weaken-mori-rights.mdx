The UN’s human‑rights committee said New Zealand is at risk of eroding Māori rights. In a 14‑page report, it criticised cuts to the Māori Health Authority, reduced funding for Māori services, and a shrinking role for the Treaty of Waitangi in schools and government. The report called for stronger protection of Māori land, language revitalisation and better anti‑discrimination laws. The government said it would look at the findings, while Māori leaders and protests have erupted over the reforms.
