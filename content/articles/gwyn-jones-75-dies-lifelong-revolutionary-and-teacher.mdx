Gwyn Jones, 75, was a lifelong activist who taught English in Spain, Italy, Romania, and Hungary while working with trade unions and socialist groups. After growing up in Wales, he studied law at King’s College London, joined the International Marxist Group, and later the Socialist Workers Party. He lived in the post‑Franco era, helped workers in Britain, and joined street protests during the fall of communism. His dedication to change shaped many people.
