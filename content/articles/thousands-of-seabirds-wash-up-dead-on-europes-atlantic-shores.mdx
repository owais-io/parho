Thousands of seabirds, mostly puffins, wash up dead on Europe’s Atlantic coasts after a series of severe storms. This is the worst wave since 2014, when 54,000 birds were found. Scientists warn many more died at sea. The RSPB says climate‑driven storms, avian flu, food shortages and pollution are crushing seabird populations. Puffins normally spend winter far out in the North Atlantic, so many more may have died at sea without reaching shore. Rising temperatures also cause flooding in Britain.
