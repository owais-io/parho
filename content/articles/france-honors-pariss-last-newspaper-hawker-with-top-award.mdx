Ali Akbar, a 73‑year‑old man from Pakistan, has been selling newspapers on the streets of Paris for more than 50 years. He was made a Knight of the National Order of Merit by President Emmanuel Macron in a ceremony at the Élysée Palace. The award recognises his role as a beloved figure in the Left Bank, where he greeted people with jokes and news. Although newspaper sales have dropped, he keeps walking the city and hopes the tradition lives on.
