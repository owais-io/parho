The UK has enough gas for only two days in storage, but experts say that is not enough for future shocks. A war in the Middle East and a shift to renewables mean Britain needs a bigger gas reserve. The government has resisted more storage because of cost, but a strategic reserve could protect prices and keep supply steady. This extra storage would also help during rare but severe winter demand spikes.
