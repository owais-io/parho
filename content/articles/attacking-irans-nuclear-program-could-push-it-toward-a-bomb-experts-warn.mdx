Experts say that US‑Israeli bombings aimed at stopping Iran’s nuclear program might instead push Tehran toward building a bomb. Iran’s nuclear deal ended in 2018, after which it built a stockpile of 440 kg of highly enriched uranium—enough for over 10 warheads. The strikes destroyed some facilities but left deep underground sites intact, and inspectors can’t verify what Iran is doing now. The risk is that a weakened regime could become even more dangerous.
