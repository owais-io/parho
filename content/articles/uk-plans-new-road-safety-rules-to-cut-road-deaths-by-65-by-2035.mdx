The UK government will introduce stricter road safety rules to cut road deaths by 65% by 2035. Planned measures include lowering the legal alcohol limit, requiring breath‑test devices for convicted drink‑drivers, and double fines for uninsured drivers. New cars will have automatic emergency braking, and drivers over 70 will need eye exams every three years. Young drivers will face longer training, and drug‑driving offenders will be pulled from the road early.
