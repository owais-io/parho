After a harsh internet shutdown began on 8 January, Iranian authorities have begun easing restrictions but still keep the blockade in place. Connectivity is uneven, with about 60 % of normal traffic returning in some areas. Major sites like Google, Bing and ChatGPT are now accessible in a few provinces, while most social media and messaging apps stay blocked. The regime says the blackout costs around $36 million a day, hurting its economy.
