In a Guardian column, the writer shows how JG Ballard’s 2004 novel *Kingdom Come* predicted the political and social mess we see in Britain today. The book follows a man visiting a suburb near the M25, where he sees rising racism, protests, and economic decline that mirror current events. The article argues that Britain’s recent shift to right‑wing extremism is not a foreign import but the result of decades of domestic change.
