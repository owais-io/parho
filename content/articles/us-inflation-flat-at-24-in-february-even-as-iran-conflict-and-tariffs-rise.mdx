Inflation in the U.S. stayed steady at 2.4% in February, the same as in January, while the new U.S.–Israel‑Iran war and trade tariffs keep prices shaky. Core inflation (minus food and energy) is 2.5%. House‑rent, health care, and utilities hit the biggest rises, while gas prices fell. Economists say oil price swings could lift inflation, so the Fed is likely to keep rates unchanged for now.
