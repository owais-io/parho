Delcy Rodríguez, Venezuela’s vice‑president, told U.S. officials she would cooperate if President Nicolás Maduro left the country. She and her brother Jorge spoke privately with Trump’s team months before the January raid that stole Maduro. Their pledge was aimed at keeping the country stable, not at overthrowing him. U.S. officials saw the offer as a way to prevent chaos after the coup.
