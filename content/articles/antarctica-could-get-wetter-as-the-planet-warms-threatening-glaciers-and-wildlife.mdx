Antarctica is a dry desert, getting just 16 cm of rain each year. But scientists say global warming could turn it into a wetter place. If temperatures rise 2 °C or more, rain could jump over 20 %, melting glaciers faster, thinning ice shelves, and creating more icebergs. That would shrink the food chain, lose penguin and seal nesting spots, invite invasive crabs and mussels, and damage historic huts. Limiting warming to 1.5 °C could give the continent a chance to adapt.
