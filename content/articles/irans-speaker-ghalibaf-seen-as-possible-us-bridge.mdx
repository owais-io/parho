Parliament speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf, a hard‑liner with a military past, is being eyed by the U.S. as a potential contact with Iran. He has led the IRGC, served as Tehran’s mayor, and is known for harsh crackdowns on protests. While he sometimes supports cooperation, his record of corruption and brutality makes it unclear whether he can truly represent Iran’s interests. Washington’s interest may reflect a misunderstanding of Iran’s power structure.
