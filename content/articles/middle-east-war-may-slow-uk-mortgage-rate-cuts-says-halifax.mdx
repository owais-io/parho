Halifax says the war in the Middle East could keep UK mortgage rates from dropping quickly. House price growth slowed in February, and higher energy costs and swap rates are pushing borrowing costs up. Some lenders have already raised fixed mortgage rates, and first‑time buyers still find it hard to afford homes. The slowdown could keep interest rates higher for longer.
