In cold December, New York organizers handed out rights guides and whistles to street vendors worried about ICE raids. With over 7,000 arrests citywide, vendors—most immigrants—feel threatened. Groups like Street Vendor Project and NYC Ice Watch use social media to warn and block raids, while other activists train volunteers and lobby for better permits. The effort shows how community action can help keep vendors and neighborhoods safe.
