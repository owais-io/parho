UK banks and payment firms are meeting to launch a new national payment system called DeliveryCo, giving Britain a backup if Visa or Mastercard were shut down by the US. The move follows Trump’s threats that could halt foreign‑owned payment networks. Led by Barclays CEO Vim Maru, the group includes major banks and even Visa and Mastercard, which will remain part of the new rail. DeliveryCo aims to be ready by 2030, adding resilience to UK commerce.
