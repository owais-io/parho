Adrian Chiles wonders when people started hating dog poop left on the ground. He recalls times when owners didn’t clean up because dogs roamed freely. A real change came in 1978 when New York City mayor Ed Koch introduced the Pooper‑Scooper Law, making it illegal to leave dog waste on sidewalks. Today, leaving poop is seen as uncivilised, and the law has helped keep streets clean.
