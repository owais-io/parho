Oil prices have jumped above $100 a barrel because the Middle East war has blocked the Strait of Hormuz, a key shipping route. The rise hurts kerosene the most, and governments are stepping in: India is forcing refineries to produce more LPG and limiting sales to households; Sri Lanka is rationing fuel by vehicle type; Pakistan has warned of school closures and remote work to save fuel. People are being asked to share how they’re cutting fuel use.
