Europe’s pension plans are under pressure because fewer workers are paying for more retirees. France is paying a huge share of its GDP and has seen big strikes over reforms. Germany’s worker‑retiree ratio is falling to 2‑to‑1 and it plans to raise the retirement age. Spain has protests over low minimum pensions. Denmark will lift its age to 70 by 2040, while the Netherlands uses a mixed, more flexible system.
