In Greece, the government has recovered a set of rare photographs showing 200 communist fighters executed by Nazi soldiers on May 1, 1944. The images, once hidden, were sold on eBay before the Greek culture ministry bought them. They confirm the bravery of the men and spark new debate about the country’s wartime history and the post‑war civil conflict. The discovery has drawn far‑right backlash and renewed public interest.
