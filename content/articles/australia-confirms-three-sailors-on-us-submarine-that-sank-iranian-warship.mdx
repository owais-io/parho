Australian Prime Minister Anthony Albanese confirmed that three Australian sailors were on a U.S. submarine that torpedoed an Iranian warship in the Indian Ocean. They were there for Aukus training and did not take part in the attack. The claim sparked debate about Australia’s involvement in overseas conflicts and raised questions about international law.
