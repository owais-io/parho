The article gathers letters from people who have loved ones who died with assisted help and from health experts. They warn that letting people choose assisted death could pressure those who lack good palliative care. The writers say we need better hospice services first, and that the bill should be carefully checked so no vulnerable person feels forced into it. The debate shows how hard it is to balance personal choice with protecting everyone.
