Gold prices climbed to a new record of $5,000 an ounce, the first time the metal has hit that level. Investors rushed to gold after President Trump announced risky trade moves and threatened tariffs, sparking fears that the U.S. dollar could weaken. The jump came as markets rattled by a possible U.S. shutdown and Japan’s tax‑cut plans also worried traders. Analysts say the surge shows gold is still the go‑to safe‑haven when politics get shaky.
