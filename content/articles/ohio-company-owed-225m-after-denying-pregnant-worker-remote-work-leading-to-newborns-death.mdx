A Ohio freight‑broker, Total Quality Logistics (TQL), was ordered to pay $22.5 million after a judge found it wrongfully denied a pregnant employee, Chelsea Walsh, the chance to work from home. Walsh was told to return to the office while her pregnancy was high‑risk. She later gave birth prematurely to a baby who died. The jury said TQL’s refusal caused the loss and that the company should have respected her doctors’ instructions.
