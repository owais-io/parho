Australia’s new Integrated Assessment Tool (IAT) uses a computer algorithm to decide how much home help and support people get. It was meant to make the process faster and fairer, but many seniors and people with disabilities feel it ignores their real needs. The system leaves no room for human override, leading to frustration, delayed care, and even losing independence. The story warns that letting machines decide care can create unfair, inhuman outcomes.
