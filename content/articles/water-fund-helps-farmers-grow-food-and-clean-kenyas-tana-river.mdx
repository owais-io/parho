The Upper Tana‑Nairobi water fund, started in 2015, partners Kenyan farmers with the private sector to protect the Tana River’s watershed. Farmers plant trees, build water pans and use cover crops to stop soil erosion, reducing river sediment and improving water quality. The fund has cut Nairobi’s water‑treatment costs by 41 % and added 27 million litres of clean water each day. Farmers now grow profitable crops, earn extra income, and help feed their families.
