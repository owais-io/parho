Walter Sofronoff, who led an inquiry into the Bruce Lehrmann case, tried to overturn a finding that he acted corruptly by leaking the inquiry to reporters. A federal judge said he couldn’t prove the commission was wrong, so the report stands. The commission said Sofronoff shared confidential documents with two journalists and acted in their interest, not the law’s. The ruling keeps the integrity commission’s findings on the books.
