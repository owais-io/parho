Spain's Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez faces a big test in Extremadura as voters decide after a wave of corruption and harassment scandals hit his party. The region, once a PSOE stronghold, is now run by the conservative PP, who may need Vox support. Trials involve socialist leaders and Sánchez's brother. Results could show a national swing against the socialists, hurting Sánchez's hopes for 2027.
