Kellie Sloane, a 52‑year‑old former TV journalist, was picked without opposition to head the New South Wales Liberal Party. She’s only a year into parliament and is known for clear, engaging speech, but critics point to her lack of experience. With the party sitting at just 28% in polls, Sloane aims to focus on housing, cost‑of‑living issues and climate targets while hoping to bring fresh energy and more women into the Liberals.
