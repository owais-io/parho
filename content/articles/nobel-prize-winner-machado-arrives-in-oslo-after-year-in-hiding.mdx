María Corina Machado, the Venezuelan opposition leader who won the 2025 Peace Prize, escaped Caracas after 11 months hiding. She appeared on the balcony of Oslo’s Grand Hotel, greeted by cheering supporters who sang the Venezuelan anthem. Her daughter accepted the award in her place on Wednesday. The visit comes while Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro still claims victory in the contested election, and he slammed the award as a “failure.” Machado’s arrival signals a hopeful moment for democracy in Venezuela.
