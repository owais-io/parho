Every year the word chosen as “Word of the Year” tends to reflect darker, more troubling trends—think “fake news,” “post‑truth,” or “climate emergency.” 2025’s winners include “parasocial,” “rage bait,” “slop,” and the teen slang “six‑seven,” which means “so‑so” and is used to annoy adults. The article compares these words to museum pieces, showing how they capture society’s mood and how the playful “six‑seven” might offer hope.
