Indigenous leaders from Alberta flew to London to warn King Charles that a growing movement is pushing for the province’s independence, saying it ignores Treaty Six, an 1876 agreement that gives First Nations rights over the land. Alberta groups are collecting signatures for a referendum and even seeking a $500 bn U.S. loan if it succeeds. Chiefs stressed that secession would only be possible with treaty‑holder consent, and the king pledged to learn more.
