Former Labour minister Louise Haigh has urged the government to back delivery workers who were fired by courier firm DPD after they protested a big pay cut. DPD said it would cut drivers’ rates by up to £8,000 a year, while the company made almost £200 m in pre‑tax profit. Workers walked off the job for three days, and DPD has threatened to enforce the cuts next year. Haigh says Labour must protect vulnerable workers and enforce new employment rights laws.
