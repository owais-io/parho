Claudette Colvin was an 86‑year‑old civil rights activist who, at age 15, refused to give up her bus seat in Montgomery, Alabama in 1955, sparking a movement that later led to Rosa Parks' famous protest. Despite being arrested, she later joined a lawsuit that ended bus segregation. She lived most of her life as a nurse, and only recently has her role been formally recognized. She died on January 13, 2026.
