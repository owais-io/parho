Liz Hurley told a High Court judge that the Daily Mail illegally tapped her home landline and used hidden microphones, while Prince Harry said the paper’s covert spying hurt him and his wife. Both claim the publisher used private investigators and hacking to gather stories. The Daily Mail, owned by Associated Newspapers Ltd, denies the allegations. The case is part of a broader lawsuit by several celebrities, including Harry, against the paper for privacy breaches.
