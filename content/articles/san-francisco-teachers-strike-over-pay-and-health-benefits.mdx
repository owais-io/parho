About 6,000 San Francisco public‑school teachers walked out Monday, the city’s first teachers strike in nearly 50 years. They’re fighting for higher pay, better health benefits and more help for special‑needs students. The district shut all 120 schools and will offer online lessons for some of its 50,000 pupils. Teachers want a 9% raise over two years; the district offers 6% over three, citing a $100 m deficit. Talks are set to resume.
