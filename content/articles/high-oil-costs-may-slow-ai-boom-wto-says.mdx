The WTO chief economist says that if oil prices stay high because of the Middle East war, the energy‑heavy AI industry might see slower growth. AI investments were a major part of trade gains last year, but a long‑term energy shock could cut global goods trade growth by another half‑percentage point and hurt food security. Even with US tariffs, trade still grew, but a sharp rise in energy costs could push the economy lower.
