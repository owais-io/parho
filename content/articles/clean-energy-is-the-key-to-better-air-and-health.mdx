Europe’s new Cancer‑Prevention Code says governments must tackle air pollution by fixing the energy system that fuels it. Most greenhouse gases come from burning fossil fuels, which also spew fine dust and nitrogen oxides that harm lungs and raise cancer risk. Switching to cleaner electricity, electrifying transport, industry and homes, and using heat pumps can cut emissions and improve air. In schools, simple air‑filter upgrades lower dust, cut sickness and even boost pupils’ test scores.
