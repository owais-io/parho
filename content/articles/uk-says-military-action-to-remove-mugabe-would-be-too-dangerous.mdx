In 2004 the UK Foreign Office looked at ways to deal with Zimbabwe’s dictator Robert Mugabe. Papers show officials thought about using force, stronger sanctions or re‑engaging with him. They decided that a military coup would be too risky, would likely fail, and would hurt British people and regional stability. So the UK chose to keep pressure from the outside and plan a longer‑term diplomatic approach.
