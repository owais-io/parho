Survivors of Ireland’s mother‑and‑baby homes living in Britain risk losing state benefits if they take compensation from Dublin. The Irish scheme pays €5,000‑€125,000 to over 13,000 women and children who were abused, but the money counts as income and can wipe out universal credit, housing and pension credit. Campaigners, including actors Siobhán McSweeney and Steve Coogan, are pushing for a UK law that would protect these benefits.
