Families of victims from Hillsborough, the Manchester arena bombing and other disasters are furious because Keir Starmer’s government hasn’t introduced a law that would make police and public bodies honest about mistakes. Labour promised the bill, but it was dropped in January over national‑security worries. The families want the rule to cover MI5 and MI6 too and have written to Starmer calling the delay an insult. The government says it’s still working on an amendment and hopes to finish it next session.
