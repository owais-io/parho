Alba, the pro‑independence group founded in 2021, has decided to dissolve and will not field any candidates in the 2026 Scottish parliament vote. The move follows a steep drop in members and a financial crisis that has prompted police scrutiny of its accounts. Leader Kenny MacAskill said the decision was made with regret, as debts far exceed available cash. Some members say the party had a unique stance on oil, gas and gender identity.
