The recent fighting between Iran, the US and Israel has shaken the Gulf countries—Saudi Arabia, Qatar and the UAE. These nations have been trying to modernise, build tourism and stay close to the US and Israel. But new attacks from Iran and clashes between the Gulf states themselves are threatening that stability. The war could hurt their oil trade, water supplies and their plans to become global business hubs. The region may face bigger political and economic problems.
