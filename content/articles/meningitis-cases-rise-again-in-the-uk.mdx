Meningitis cases have jumped again in the UK, with 13 new cases and two deaths so far. The rise is mainly caused by a bacteria called MenB, which most teens and university students haven’t been vaccinated against. Hospitals now give antibiotics to anyone who may have been exposed and are starting a special MenB vaccination drive for students in Kent. The government says quick treatment and extra shots are key to stopping the spread.
