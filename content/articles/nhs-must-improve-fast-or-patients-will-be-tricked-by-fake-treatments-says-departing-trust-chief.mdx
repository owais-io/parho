NHS chief Nick Hulme, who has spent 46 years in the system, is leaving his trust. He warns the service is in danger, with long waiting lists and high costs. Hulme says politicians’ promises of ‘choice’ hurt the NHS, and private practice is booming but risky. He criticises the slow, bureaucratic reforms and says if the NHS doesn’t get better soon, patients will turn to fake cures. He hopes urgent changes will fix the system.
