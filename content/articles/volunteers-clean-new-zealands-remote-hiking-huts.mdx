In New Zealand, a huge network of 950 huts lets hikers rest in the backcountry, but the government can’t keep them all clean. Volunteers, led by the ‘Love our Huts’ campaign, haul tools, wipe windows and pull weeds. Families and friends, including kids, spend hours on foot to tidy huts. It keeps a cultural heritage alive and teaches stewardship for future generations.
