Polls show more Americans than ever see US health care as a crisis. 23 % think the system is in trouble, 47 % say it has big problems, and 29 % say cost is the biggest worry. Rising drug prices, insurance delays and high bills make people skip needed care. The problem is worse in some states, like Mississippi, and ties to mental‑health costs. Experts say fixing affordability and adding mental care to routine visits could help.
