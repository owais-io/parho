Author urges new laws to protect children from unsafe circumcision. He supports criminalising unqualified practitioners but stops short of banning the ritual entirely, citing Jewish and Muslim traditions. The proposal would require training, accreditation, and monitoring of all circumcisions. This aims to balance child safety with respect for religious customs, while cracking down on rogue operators. The debate highlights tensions between tradition and medical ethics.
