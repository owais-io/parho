Labour is fighting to keep power but is losing ground because the voting system favours only the two biggest parties. A recent by‑election showed how a small change in votes can swing many seats, risking a right‑wing leader like Nigel Farage winning on a minority vote. The party has also made unpopular immigration moves and a weak message that hurts voter feelings. Experts call for a commission to reform the first‑past‑the‑post system so voters can choose freely.
