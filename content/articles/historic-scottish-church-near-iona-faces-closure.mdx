A small, 18th‑century church on Loch Insh in the Scottish Highlands has been a site of Christian worship since the 7th century, linked to monks from Iona. Its bronze bell, one of only five in Scotland, dates back to AD 900. The church, surrounded by wildlife and beautiful scenery, is now threatened with closure, but locals are campaigning to keep it open so that worship, bells and birds can continue to echo.
