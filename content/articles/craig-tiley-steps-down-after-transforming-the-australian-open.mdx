Craig Tiley, who has led Tennis Australia since 2005 and re‑imagined the Australian Open as a world‑class entertainment event, will leave the role in 2026. Under his guidance, the Open grew from 550,000 to 1.4 million attendees, added new stadiums, expanded the schedule, and made the tournament a major cultural highlight. He’s faced criticism over player handling and local talent development but remains praised for turning the event into a profitable, globally recognized spectacle.
