In late October, the Brazilian police launched Operation Containment in Rio’s Penha and Alemão favelas. The 17‑hour raid ended with 122 people dead, most of them Black residents. The operation was chaotic, with many officers injured, few body‑cam recordings, and no clear evidence of who was targeted. Only 17 of the 100 people the police had warrants for were arrested, and many victims had no criminal record.
