Newcastle United’s takeover by Saudi Arabia’s Public Investment Fund (PIF) promised huge spending and new stadiums, but the club’s big plans haven’t materialised. New players like Woltemade and Elanga are struggling, and the club’s top‑spot hopes are fading. Meanwhile, Saudi mega‑projects such as Neom and a Riyadh skyscraper have been scaled back. Fans remain sceptical, and the PIF’s cash crunch makes future promises uncertain.
