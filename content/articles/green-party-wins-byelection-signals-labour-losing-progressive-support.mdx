Green Party wins the Gorton and Denton byelection, showing that progressive voters are turning away from Labour and leaning toward the Greens. The win came after months of government moves—tax promises, immigration cuts and a crackdown on left‑leaning politics—that left many younger, minority and professional voters dissatisfied. Labour’s strategy of ignoring these voters has backfired, while Reform UK’s hardline stance failed to capture the same support. The result suggests a bigger shift for the next general election.
