Peter Mandelson, a former Labour cabinet minister, slipped back into the party’s inner circle and was later named Britain’s ambassador to the United States. His return sparked anger because of his past ties to the disgraced financier Jeffrey Epstein and because senior staff had warned about his character. Critics say Starmer brought him in to add political firepower but it left many Labour MPs uneasy and exposed a lack of clear leadership.
