A London charity called Dose of Nature prescribes outdoor time to treat mental health problems. GPs refer patients, who get eight weekly walks or sits in parks, guided by volunteers. A study found the program lifts serotonin, reduces stress, and improves mood better than usual NHS therapy – 64% recovery vs 50% for talk‑talk. It’s free, works for diverse groups, and could save the NHS money.
