Alibaba’s boss announced a $40 billion AI push and talked about super‑intelligent machines, sparking headlines. But most Chinese firms focus on practical AI, not the futuristic “general AI” the U.S. is racing to build. U.S. sanctions limit China’s access to top chips, while China is pouring money into data centers and domestic chips. Experts say the gap is shrinking, and China could close it quickly if chip tech improves.
