West Australia’s premier, Roger Cook, spoke out against divisive politics after a 20‑year‑old man was arrested for planning a terrorist attack. Cook joined Muslim leaders at a mosque, saying politicians should avoid “dog‑whistling” over immigration and stop blaming whole communities. He urged people to condemn hate and stand together. Police say the young man was driven by white‑supremacy, and they are still looking for anyone who may have influenced him.
