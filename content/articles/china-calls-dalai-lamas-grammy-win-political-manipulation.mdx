The Dalai Lama won his first Grammy for a spoken‑word album. China’s foreign ministry called the win “anti‑China political manipulation” and said the award was a tool for political propaganda. The 90‑year‑old Buddhist, who lives in exile in India, has long been a symbol of Tibetan resistance. His win has added a new chapter to the ongoing clash between the Dalai Lama’s supporters and Beijing. The Chinese government still sees the Dalai Lama as a separatist.
