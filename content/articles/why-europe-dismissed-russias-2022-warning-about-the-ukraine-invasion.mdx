On the fourth anniversary of Russia’s full‑scale invasion of Ukraine, a Guardian journalist looks back at the war’s opening days and the intelligence that warned of an attack. While the U.S. and U.K. were convinced Russia would strike Kyiv, France, Germany and other European leaders doubted the warnings because of past mistakes and a belief that Putin would act rationally. The piece uncovers the intelligence gaps and reminds us that “impossible” threats can still happen.
