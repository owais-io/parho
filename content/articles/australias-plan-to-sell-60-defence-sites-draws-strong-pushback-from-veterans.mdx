The Australian government announced it will sell more than 60 defence sites, aiming to raise up to $1.8 billion. Veterans, the RSL, opposition MPs and local leaders warn the process could take a decade, cost billions, and damage historic sites. They argue some properties are heritage landmarks, not empty paddocks, and should be protected or redeveloped for housing and parks. The plan has sparked debate over value, timing and cultural preservation.
