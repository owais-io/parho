Authorities plan to keep the world’s internet closed to most Iranians. Only people cleared by the government will get a limited, filtered global web. The rest will use a domestic network that has its own sites and apps. The move follows a 16‑year push to control online traffic, and it could hurt the economy and culture. The plan comes after a severe shutdown during protests that killed thousands.
