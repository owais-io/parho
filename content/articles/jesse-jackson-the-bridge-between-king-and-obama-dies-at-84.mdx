Jesse Jackson, a civil‑rights leader who helped shape the modern Democratic Party, died at 84. He saw Martin Luther King’s murder in 1968 and later ran twice for the Democratic nomination in 1984 and 1988, opening doors for Black candidates. Jackson built the Rainbow Coalition, pushed for voting rights, marriage equality and anti‑apartheid sanctions, and his work paved the way for Barack Obama’s 2008 victory. His influence lives on in today’s politics.
