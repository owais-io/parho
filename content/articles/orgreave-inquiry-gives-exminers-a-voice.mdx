South Yorkshire’s former miners can now tell the story of the 1984 Orgreave clash after a new public inquiry was launched. Bishop Pete Wilcox said the investigation will give miners a voice that was silenced for four decades. The inquiry will collect witness accounts, documents and videos about the heavy‑handed police attack that left many injured and created lasting trauma. Police are expected to cooperate and the hope is that the truth will help the community heal.
