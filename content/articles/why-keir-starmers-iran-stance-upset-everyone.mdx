Keir Starmer’s stance on a possible UK role in a U.S. strike on Iran has left many politicians and voters uneasy. At first he said the UK had no legal right to join a pre‑emptive attack, but when Iranian missiles threatened British assets he reversed course, allowing forces to act only in “collective self‑defence”. Critics claim he’s too weak, while supporters fear a costly war.
