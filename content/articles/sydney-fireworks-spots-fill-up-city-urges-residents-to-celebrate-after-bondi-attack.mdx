On New Year’s Eve, Sydney’s popular fire‑work viewing spots were full. After the Bondi attack, Premier Chris Minns urged residents to celebrate, saying they should show strength and solidarity. Police said they were on high alert and that over 2,500 officers would be on the streets, many with long‑arm tasers. Transport for NSW added 1,000 extra services and ran trains for 46 hours to keep people moving safely.
