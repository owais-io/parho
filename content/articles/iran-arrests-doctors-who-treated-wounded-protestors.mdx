Iran has arrested several doctors, including surgeon Alireza Golchini, who treated wounded protestors during the country’s violent crackdown. Human‑rights groups call the arrests part of a revenge campaign and warn that Golchini faces a death sentence for refusing to ignore injured protesters. At least nine medical staff have been detained, and security forces raided makeshift clinics. The U.S. and international groups are demanding the doctors’ release and condemning the persecution.
