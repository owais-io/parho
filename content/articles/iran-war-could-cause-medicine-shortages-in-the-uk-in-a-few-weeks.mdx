Experts warn that if the Iran conflict keeps going, Britain may run out of key medicines like painkillers and cancer drugs within weeks. The war has closed the Strait of Hormuz, cutting off the main shipping route. Air freight is more expensive and flights are fewer, so medicine deliveries are delayed. Higher costs may push drug prices up, hurting the NHS and patients.
