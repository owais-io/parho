The article follows the author’s attempt to enjoy a fancy salad outside in London’s spring weather. After waiting in line for a salad bar, they try to eat on a wall because there are no tables or benches nearby. The messy situation—leaves flying, a wooden fork clumsy, and curious onlookers—shows how urban spaces often lack proper seating for picnics. Readers learn that finding a table first can save an outdoor eating disaster.
