EU foreign‑policy chief Kaja Kallas said the US‑EU relationship has taken a major hit after President Trump threatened to take over Greenland and impose tariffs. An emergency summit was held to calm things down, and Trump later backed off the tariffs. EU leaders stressed they won’t abandon 80 years of friendship, but they’re still wary of Trump’s moves and are ready to use economic tools if needed. The EU also reaffirmed its support for Ukraine and is re‑examining its trade deal with the US.
