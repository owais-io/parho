Tim Davie, the BBC’s director‑general, quit after months of pressure over coverage, bias claims and board fights. Staff blamed a mix of political pressure, a controversial edit of a Trump speech and accusations of liberal bias. Davie’s resignation, along with news head Deborah Turness, exposed deep divisions inside the BBC. The board is now being reviewed, and a new chief will need to deal with government talks over the BBC’s licence fee and charter. The future looks uncertain.
