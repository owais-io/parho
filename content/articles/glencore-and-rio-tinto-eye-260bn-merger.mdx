Glencore and Rio Tinto are in talks to merge, a deal worth about $260 bn, including debt. The news sent shares up and down – Glencore jumped 9%, Rio fell 2%. The pair could combine mining strength with Glencore’s trading reach, but cultural fit and coal issues remain. A bigger copper focus could boost earnings, and the move may tempt rivals like BHP. The deal still faces questions about pricing and structure.
