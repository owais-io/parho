The Small Business Administration, under new administrator Kelly Loeffler, announced it will no longer approve loans for small‑business owners who are not U.S. citizens, even if they are legal residents. Critics say the move is political, not economic, and will hurt immigrant entrepreneurs who rely on capital to grow jobs. The article argues the SBA should stay out of politics, perhaps becoming a semi‑private agency that serves all small businesses. This change could reshape how loans are distributed.
