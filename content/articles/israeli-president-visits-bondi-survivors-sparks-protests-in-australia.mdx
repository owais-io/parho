Israeli president Isaac Herzog visited Australia to honor Bondi terror attack survivors, laying wreaths and stones at the memorial and speaking with victims’ families. His visits were meant to strengthen ties, but the trip sparked protests. Thousands gathered in Sydney and Melbourne, some supporting Palestinian rights and criticizing Israel’s actions. Police mobilized for the visit, and a Jewish community letter said Herzog was unwelcome. The event highlighted tensions over Israel’s role in the conflict.
