In the quiet of mid‑winter, Cornwall’s ancient churches become a secret trail for history lovers. From St Winnow’s stone tower to St Mabyn’s weather‑worn carvings, each site hides stories – a bear carved into a stained‑glass window, heraldic beasts on stone, and family crests that still echo in the walls. The tour skips crowds, letting visitors explore legends, myths, and the quiet charm of these historic halls.
