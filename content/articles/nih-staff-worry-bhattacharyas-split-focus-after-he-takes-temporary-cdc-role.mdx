Jay Bhattacharya is the permanent director of the National Institutes of Health (NIH) but will also run the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) for a few months. NIH scientists worry he won’t have time for their problems. Early‑career researchers complain about higher health‑insurance costs and a steep drop in grant success. Many NIH institutes have acting leaders, and employees say decisions are slow and unclear.
