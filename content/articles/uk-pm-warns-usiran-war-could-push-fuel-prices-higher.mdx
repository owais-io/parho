UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer said a long‑term US‑Iran war could raise fuel prices and hurt households. Oil prices spiked above $100 a barrel, pushing petrol to around 140p a litre. Starmer said the government is watching the situation and looking for ways to limit cost rises, even cancelling a planned fuel duty hike. He urged drivers to cut non‑essential trips to save money.
