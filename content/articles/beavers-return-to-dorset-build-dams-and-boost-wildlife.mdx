Last year, the National Trust re‑released beavers in Dorset, the first in England in 400 years. The animals have built a 35‑metre dam that slows a stream, creating a deep pool that attracts fish, birds, bats and even an otter. The new wetlands let more light reach the ground, supporting insects and amphibians, and may help keep a nearby road from flooding. Some early setbacks occurred, but the beavers are settling in and may soon have young kits.
