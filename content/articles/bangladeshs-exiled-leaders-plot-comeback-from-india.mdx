After Bangladesh’s 2024 uprising, many Awami League leaders fled to India, where they now meet in Kolkata and Delhi to plan a return. The party is banned and its senior members face charges of murder, corruption and crimes against humanity. India lets them stay, ignoring extradition requests. Ahead of February’s election, the exiles hope to disrupt the vote and re‑enter politics once the ban lifts.
