Despite a law that forced the Justice Department to release all Epstein investigative files by December 19, the department only published about 3 million pages—half the 6 million it said were relevant. The late release and heavy redactions angered victims’ lawyers and civil‑rights groups, who say many more documents are still hidden. Congress and Democrats accuse the department of a cover‑up, while officials claim they complied with the law.
