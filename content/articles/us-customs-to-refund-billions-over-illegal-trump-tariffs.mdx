U.S. customs officials are preparing a new system that will refund roughly $166 billion collected from Trump‑era tariffs that the Supreme Court ruled illegal. In just 45 days they aim to return the money to about 330,000 importers, using existing processes that normally settle duties after shipments clear. The plan was discussed in a closed meeting with trade lawyers and a judge, who said the refunds should be automatic and include interest.
