Rich Ruohonen, a 54‑year‑old Minnesota lawyer, made Olympic history when he played for the U.S. men’s curling team at the Beijing Winter Games. He joined as an alternate, stepped onto the ice after a teammate’s injury, and became the oldest American to compete in a Winter Olympics. While juggling a full‑time law practice and long training sessions, Ruohonen also spoke out about ICE raids in his home state, using his platform to highlight community concerns.
