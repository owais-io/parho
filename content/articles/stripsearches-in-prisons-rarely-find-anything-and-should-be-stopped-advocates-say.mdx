Prison strip‑searches happen a lot in Australia but only about 1 in 200 find any hidden items. Victims, many of whom have a history of abuse, say the searches are humiliating and re‑traumatise them. A Human Rights Law Centre report argues the practice should end, proposing body scanners and trauma‑informed checks instead. The data shows women, especially Indigenous ones, are searched far more often than their male peers.
