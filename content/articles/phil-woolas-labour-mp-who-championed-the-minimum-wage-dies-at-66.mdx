Phil Woolas, the Labour MP who helped bring the national minimum wage to the UK, died at 66 after a battle with brain cancer. Born in Scunthorpe, he was known for his blunt speeches and hard‑talking stance on immigration, which earned him praise and controversy. Woolas lost his seat in 2010 after an election court found him guilty of lying in a campaign leaflet. He remained a polarising figure until his death.
