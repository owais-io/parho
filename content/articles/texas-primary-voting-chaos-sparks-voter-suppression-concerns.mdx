Texas held its Democratic and Republican primaries on Tuesday, but voters in Dallas and Williamson counties faced long lines, confusing signage and limited machines. Democrats said the chaos was an attempt to suppress votes. A Texas court ordered separate counts for people out of line, adding more confusion. Advocacy groups warn that the problems could spread to the November midterms and hurt people of color and working‑class voters.
