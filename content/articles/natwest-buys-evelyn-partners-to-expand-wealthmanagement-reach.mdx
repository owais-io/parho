NatWest announced a £2.7 bn purchase of Evelyn Partners, a wealth‑management firm with £69 bn under management. The deal targets the growing “mass‑affluent” market—people with £50 k+ to invest—by adding fee income and tapping older generations’ wealth transfers. While the share price dipped after the news, NatWest expects cost savings and a larger client base. Risks include strong competition and slower growth than hoped.
