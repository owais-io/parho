Toronto is still clearing the largest snow storm ever, with about 60 cm covering the city. The storm knocked out 500 flights, shut schools for a second snow day, and caused dozens of crashes and one death. Transit was delayed, a streetcar derailed, and main roads are being plowed first. Workers use shovels and GPS to track progress, while residents enjoy the snowy streets. The storm was a rare Arctic‑tropical mix.
