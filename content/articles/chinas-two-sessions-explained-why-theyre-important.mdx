China’s annual “Two Sessions” bring thousands of delegates to Beijing to approve laws, budgets and leadership changes. They consist of the National People’s Congress, a 3,000‑member “rubber‑stamp” parliament, and the advisory China People’s Political Consultative Conference. The meetings set economic targets, announce military spending and confirm party appointments. This year the focus is on the 15th five‑year plan to boost domestic demand and high‑tech growth, while a recent purge rattles the military leadership.
