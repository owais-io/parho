The RMT union, representing about 1,800 London Underground drivers, will strike for six 24‑hour days (12 days in total) in March, April, and May. The strikes target the company’s plan to move to a four‑day work week, which the drivers say raises safety, fatigue, and work‑life balance problems. London Underground says the changes are voluntary and will improve reliability, but the drivers insist they’re unacceptable.
