A federal judge has stopped the Trump administration from cutting more than $230 million in homeland security money for states that didn’t cooperate with immigration enforcement. The cuts would have affected eight states and DC. Judge Mary McElroy said the cuts were political and unlawful, saying the funds support vital law‑enforcement and counter‑terrorism programs. State attorneys general welcomed the ruling, and the Department of Homeland Security plans to appeal.
