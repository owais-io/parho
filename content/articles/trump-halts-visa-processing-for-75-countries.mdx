Trump has stopped visa processing for 75 countries, starting Jan. 21, in a push to limit who can legally enter the U.S. The move targets people the administration thinks may become a “public charge” and covers nations worldwide, from allies to conflict zones. Exceptions apply for dual nationals or those who can prove an “America First” national interest. The list was revealed in a State Department cable.
