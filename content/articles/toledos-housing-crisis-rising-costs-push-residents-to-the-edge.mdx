Toledo, Ohio, is turning into a hard‑to‑afford city for many families. Rent has jumped 43% since 2021, and water bills are climbing, like Kwiona Sprott’s $200‑a‑month bill. Her apartment in Executive Towers has broken gyms, leaking water and mold, yet the property’s out‑of‑state owners and managers ignore repairs. Investors from abroad are buying most homes, driving up prices and leaving locals with fewer affordable choices.
