Venezuela’s top dictator, Nicolás Maduro, was taken by US special forces last week. The U.S. says the move will bring change, but the new leader, Delcy Rodríguez, is still an ally of the old regime. Many political prisoners were released, but security forces remain tight, with checkpoints and raids. The opposition, especially María Corina Machado, is sidelined. People fear for safety, and the country may be a new, US‑friendly dictatorship.
