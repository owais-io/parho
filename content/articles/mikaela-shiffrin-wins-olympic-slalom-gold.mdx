Mikaela Shiffrin won the women's slalom at the Winter Olympics, becoming the first U.S. skier to claim three Olympic gold medals. She finished in 1 minute 39.10 seconds, beating the second‑place runner by 1.50 seconds—the biggest winning gap in any Olympic alpine event since 1998. Her win marks a historic moment for U.S. alpine skiing and cements Shiffrin’s status as one of the sport’s greatest athletes.
