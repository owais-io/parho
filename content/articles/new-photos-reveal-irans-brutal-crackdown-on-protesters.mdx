New photos show how Iran violently suppressed anti‑government protests after cutting the internet. A Tehran photographer shares pictures and survivor stories. Protesters faced teargas, bullets, and the Basij militia. Many were shot, injured, some killed, and some taken away. Witnesses describe soldiers shooting civilians and families hiding in fear. The crackdown left many injured with bullets in their bodies, and authorities stayed silent while the truth surfaced through the photos.
