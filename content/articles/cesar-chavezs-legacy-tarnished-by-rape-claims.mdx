A New York Times report says Cesar Chavez, the famous U‑Farm Workers leader, sexually abused several women for decades. The allegations come from former UFW organizers, including Dolores Huerta, who said Chavez forced her to have sex and raped her, causing pregnancies. Two young women say Chavez molested them from age 12 and even raped them in motel rooms. The revelations hurt Chavez’s reputation and raise questions about the UFW’s legacy.
