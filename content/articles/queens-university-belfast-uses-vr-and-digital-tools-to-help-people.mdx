Queen’s University Belfast is using virtual reality and digital tools to help people in everyday life. They built a VR pub where people with social anxiety can practice chatting with AI characters. The university also created VR experiences to teach empathy for abuse victims and to treat trauma. They’re designing digital instruments so disabled musicians can play. And researchers are making AI run on small devices to give doctors real‑time help during surgery.
