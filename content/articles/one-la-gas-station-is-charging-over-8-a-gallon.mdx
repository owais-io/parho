One Los Angeles Chevron station on Alameda Street is charging $8.31 a gallon, more than double the city average of about $5.37. The owner says the high rent and downtown location explain the price hike, but customers are few and some think it’s price‑gouging. California law allows high prices unless an emergency is declared. Buyers are encouraged to compare prices with AAA’s free app and remember gas margins are thin, with most profit coming from drinks and snacks.
