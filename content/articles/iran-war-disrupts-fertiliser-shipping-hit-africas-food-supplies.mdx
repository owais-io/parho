The Iran war has blocked shipping through the Hormuz Strait, a route for fertiliser and oil. Africa relies on these imports, with Sudan getting 54% of its fertiliser that way and Kenya about 30%. Prices have jumped, raising food costs and hurting household budgets. Governments in Kenya, Tanzania, Ethiopia and Zambia are boosting fuel reserves or subsidies to keep supplies steady. The disruption slows African food exports to the Middle East, while oil‑exporting neighbours benefit from higher crude prices.
