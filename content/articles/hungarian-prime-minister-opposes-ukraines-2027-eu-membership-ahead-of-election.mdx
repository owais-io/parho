Prime Minister Viktor Orbán is using the debate over Ukraine’s planned EU membership in 2027 as a campaign issue for Hungary’s upcoming parliamentary election. He claims Ukraine is trying to meddle in Hungarian politics, calls the country a threat, and says EU funds would be taken away if Ukraine joins. Orbán’s anti‑Ukraine rhetoric echoes past anti‑immigration messages. Ukraine’s president, Volodymyr Zelensky, insists the country will be ready in 2027, while EU officials say the timeline is uncertain.
