Culture Secretary Lisa Nandy announced a national youth strategy worth £500 million, aimed at helping 500,000 more young people get a trusted adult mentor, improve resilience, and learn online safety. The plan will build to 250 youth clubs, open 50 hubs for professional support, and boost training for youth workers. Nandy said the strategy is needed because young people feel isolated, unsafe online, and disconnected from government. She warned that cuts to youth services have left many without help.
