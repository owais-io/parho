FEMA staff are furious after a draft plan from the Trump‑era administration shows the agency could cut more than half its workforce by 2026. Current and former employees say the cuts would weaken disaster response. The plan, led by Homeland Security Secretary Kristi Noem, was leaked and says to slash jobs and move staff from Washington to regional offices. FEMA officials say the document is not a final decision.
