The UK government plans a new special educational needs and disabilities (SEND) policy, but charities warn it could cut legal protections for disabled children. They fear losing Education, Health and Care Plans, the SEND tribunal, and post‑18 support. A letter from the Disabled Children’s Partnership and other groups demands reforms keep enforceable plans, preserve the tribunal, and safeguard children’s rights. The government says it will fund teacher training, specialist places, and SEND leads.
