Police in London have questioned three women on suspicion that they helped the late former Harrods owner, Mohamed Al Fayed, who is accused of raping or sexually assaulting up to 154 women over 40 years. The women, aged in their 40s to 60s, were interviewed last fortnight. No arrests have been made yet, but detectives plan to question more people who may have enabled Al Fayed’s crimes. Victims can still provide information, and Harrods has a compensation scheme ending March 31.
