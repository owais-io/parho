A hacker group linked to Iran, called Handala, says it hacked a U.S. medical‑device maker, Stryker, after a school was bombed in Iran. The cyber attack hit the company’s Microsoft systems and stopped many employees from working. Stryker’s stock fell about 3% and the company said it’s still figuring out the damage. Cyber‑security experts warn the group could strike again soon.
