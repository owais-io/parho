Japan's defence minister announced that the country will install surface‑to‑air missiles on Yonaguni, its far‑west island, by March 2031. The move, part of a broader shift to counter China’s growing power in the East China Sea, follows comments from Japan’s prime minister that Japan could join a Taiwan defence fight. China has warned its citizens against visiting Japan and tightened restrictions on dual‑use exports. Yonaguni residents, already hosting a defence base, fear becoming a frontline in a possible clash.
