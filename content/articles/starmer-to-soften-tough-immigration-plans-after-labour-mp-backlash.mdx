Keir Starmer is looking to soften an immigration package that would lengthen the wait for settled status from 5 to 10 years. Labour MPs, led by Angela Rayner, protested, saying the changes are unfair to people who have lived in the UK for years. The government is debating exemptions for workers and those near settlement. The plan also includes stricter rules for refugees and even offers money to make families leave. Starmer met MPs to keep the party together.
