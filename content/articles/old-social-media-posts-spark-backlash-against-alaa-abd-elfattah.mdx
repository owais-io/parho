A human rights activist, Alaa Abd el‑Fattah, returned to the UK after being released from an Egyptian jail. His old tweets from 2010‑2012, which urged violence and used hateful language, resurfaced online and caused a stir. Abd el‑Fattah apologized, saying the posts were the product of a frustrated youth during the Arab Spring. Some politicians were unaware of the tweets, but there are calls from Conservatives and UK leaders to strip him of his citizenship.
