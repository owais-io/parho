After a bomb blast in Tehran, the author hears people telling each other “Don’t die” as war escalates. Israel’s attacks on Iran’s capital hit schools, hospitals, and homes, forcing many to flee. The city feels empty and dangerous, yet small groups still meet in parks to share jokes and hope. The writer urges readers not to see Iran only as a victim, but as people fighting for survival and peace.
