Arthur Stockwin, a leading scholar who shaped how Britain studies Japan, died at 90. He founded the Nissan Institute of Japanese Studies at Oxford and turned it into a top centre, created a big PhD program, and published over 100 books. His work linked UK and Japan scholars and earned him Japan’s Order of the Rising Sun and an OBE. He taught, mentored, and inspired generations of students.
