UK politicians are racing to limit the voting rights of refugees. They worry about bloc or family voting and feel it threatens democracy. Labour plans to make refugee status temporary, needing renewal every 30 months. Politicians like Nigel Farage, Kemi Badenoch, and Shabana Mahmood are involved, each using the debate to score political points. The debate is also fueled by fears of “sectarian voting” and the idea that non‑British‑born voters could be given too much influence.
