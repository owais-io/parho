After a high‑speed train crash in southern Spain that killed at least 41, a lottery ticket seller named Gonzalo Sánchez used his quad bike to ferry rescuers and injured passengers across the rugged tracks for six hours. He helped move people from both trains, even when the scene was dark and chaotic. The town praised his quick action, while officials began investigating the cause. The country is in mourning.
