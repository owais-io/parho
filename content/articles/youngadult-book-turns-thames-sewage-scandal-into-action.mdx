A new YA novel called *Floaters* turns the Thames sewage problem into a coming‑of‑age revenge story. Written by C M Taylor, who and his daughters saw too much waste in their river, the book sells only 215 copies—one for each mile of the Thames. Half the profits go to the charity Surfers Against Sewage, and the book hopes to spark public pressure ahead of a new water‑reform bill.
