The news cycle is busy. A few big stories are grabbing everyone’s attention. --- ### 1. The Justice Department and the Fed * Jerome Powell is the Fed’s top boss.   * He says the Justice Department is looking into his work on Fed building renovations.   * Trump has been loud about Powell. He says the Fed should lower interest rates.   * Powell says he will keep making money‑market decisions from data, not politics.   * Democrats fear this probe could hurt the Fed’s independence. --- ### 2. Trump’s Talk About Greenland * Greenland is a large island that belongs to Denmark.   * Trump says the U.S. might help defend it or even use force.   * He says the island is strategic for fighting Russia and China.   * The U.S. Secretary of State will meet with him on the matter.   * No one has decided yet if the U.S. will take action. --- ### 3. Iran and the Protesters * Iran is cracking down on people who want change.   * About 10,000 people were arrested, more than 500 killed.   * President Trump says he is ready to negotiate.   * He also warns that U.S. military or cyber action might happen if the violence continues.   * The U.S. is thinking about sanctions or direct strikes.   * It is a tough call because the country has many cities where a strike could harm civilians. --- ### 4. Minneapolis and the ICE Controversy * A federal officer shot a woman, Renee Good, in her car last week.   * The city is seeing a wave of protests in several U.S. cities.   * The Homeland Security Secretary said more officers will be sent for protection.   * The city says local police are “outnumbered” by the federal agents.   * Some people call it a cover‑up. Others say it was an act of self‑defence. --- ### 5. How Democrats and Republicans Are Talking * Democrats say the focus should be on lowering living costs.   * Republicans, led by Trump, say we need to spend money on overseas adventures.   * The two sides have different priorities.   * The U.S. Congress is working on a new rule about fighting in Venezuela.   * That rule tries to stop the U.S. from taking more action without Congress. --- ### Bottom Line The U.S. government is juggling many important issues at once: 1. Trump’s new probe of the Fed’s boss. 2. A possible U.S. move to help Greenland. 3. The U.S. stance on Iran’s crackdown. 4. Rising protests after a violent incident in Minneapolis. Every piece of news changes the story. Watch the headlines and stay informed. The big picture is still forming, but one thing is clear: U.S. politics is moving fast and many people want to know what will happen next.
