Penelope Putz, a 95‑year‑old Quaker, spent her life fighting for peace and the planet. She helped start the Conservation Society, joined Extinction Rebellion, and opposed fossil‑fuel pension investments. She wrote to Tony Blair against the Iraq war and attended vigils for Palestine and Gaza. She worked as a social worker, counsellor, and with the Cruse bereavement charity. After a 2024 accident in Greece, she kept campaigning. She is survived by her husband, three children, two grandchildren and a great‑granddaughter.
