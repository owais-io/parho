The Florida Bar said it had wrongly claimed it was investigating former U.S. attorney Lindsey Halligan, who was appointed by Donald Trump. Earlier letters suggested a probe, but the bar now says it is only monitoring the legal case filed against Halligan. Halligan had been sued for unethical conduct and her appointment was found unlawful. The confusion came as the Justice Department asked state bars to pause investigations during a review.
