The Oregon Supreme Court said that because the state can’t give defendants a lawyer in time, more than 1,400 criminal cases must be thrown out. Lawyers need 60 days for misdemeanors and 90 for felonies, but many people have waited years. Dismissals mean charges can be brought again later. The decision highlights a big lawyer shortage that hurts people’s jobs, homes and families.
