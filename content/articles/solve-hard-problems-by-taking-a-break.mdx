If you’re stuck on a problem, try doing something else—like folding laundry, taking a walk, or watching a movie. Those low‑pressure activities let your brain keep working in the background, often giving you the sudden insight you need. A cancer doctor had his breakthrough after a theatre break, and many writers and scientists swear by it. In busy jobs and governments, taking time off can spark ideas that help everyone. Even AI could give workers more creative time.
