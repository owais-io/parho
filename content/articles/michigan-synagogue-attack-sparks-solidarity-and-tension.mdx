A 41‑year‑old Lebanese‑American restaurant worker, Ayman Ghazali, drove a truck into Detroit’s Temple Israel synagogue, blaming Israeli strikes that killed his family. He was killed when security stopped him. Jewish and Arab leaders across Michigan condemned the attack and urged calm, warning against blaming all Jews for Israel’s actions. Some politicians fought over the incident, but many community figures see the tragedy as a chance to strengthen interfaith ties.
