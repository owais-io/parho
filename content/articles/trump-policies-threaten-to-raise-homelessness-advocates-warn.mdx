The Trump administration has changed the federal HUD homelessness program, moving most money from permanent housing to temporary shelters and cutting support for people who use harm‑reduction or identify as transgender. The shift has caused confusion, legal battles, and worries that 117,000 people could lose housing. Experts say the evidence shows permanent housing helps keep people stable and is cheaper than shelters. Local agencies are scrambling to keep programs running.
