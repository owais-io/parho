Trumps administration has cut the list of routine childhood vaccines from 17 to 11, making many shots optional. Health experts say this move could lower vaccine rates, let dangerous diseases spread, and confuse parents and doctors. The changes could lead to more infections like measles, flu, and meningitis, and may spark lawsuits and pressure on states to drop school vaccine rules. The shift also raises doubts about vaccine safety.
