The article looks at what makes ordinary people, like Darryn Frost in London and Ahmed al‑Ahmed in Sydney, rush toward a danger instead of running away. It shows that it isn’t pure instinct but a mix of quick thinking, past training, and how the brain focuses on one threat. Experts say the brain makes split‑second decisions before we’re aware, and anyone—from a mom to a soldier—can become a hero.
