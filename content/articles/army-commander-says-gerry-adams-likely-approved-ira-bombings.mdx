A former British army commander, Richard Kemp, testified that it is hard to believe Gerry Adams was not involved in approving IRA bombings. He said he had seen 20 years of intelligence linking Adams to the paramilitary group and that the IRA’s bomb attacks required approval from an army council, which Adams supposedly sat on. However, he admits no direct evidence and that some intelligence could be wrong. The case continues.
