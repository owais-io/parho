In February, UK house prices edged up by 0.3%, reaching an average of £273,176, slightly better than forecasts. Chancellor Rachel Reeves avoided the negative chatter that hit the market before last year’s budget, helping keep buyers and sellers confident. The rise comes amid a steady 1% yearly growth, improving affordability for first‑timers, and a Bank of England that may soon cut rates as inflation heads toward its 2% goal.
