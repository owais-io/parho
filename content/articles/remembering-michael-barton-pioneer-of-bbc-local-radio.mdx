Michael Barton, who died at 94, helped build BBC Local Radio. He launched Radio Sheffield in 1967, shaping local shows and inviting community voices, even adding Hindi, Urdu and Bengali programs for immigrants. In 1974 he became the first controller of BBC Local Radio, expanding the network to 20 more stations and protecting it from cuts. A passionate supporter of local voices, he left a lasting legacy for community broadcasting.
