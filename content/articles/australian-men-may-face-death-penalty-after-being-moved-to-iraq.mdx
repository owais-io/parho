Australia says some of its citizens are among 5,700 people held in Iraq after a mass transfer from Syria. The men could face charges that carry the death penalty, but the exact number of Australians is unclear. U.S. forces carried out the move, and human‑rights experts say the detainees were not given legal protection or a chance to be tried. Australia is looking into the situation and will not give legal help unless it can guarantee no death sentence.
