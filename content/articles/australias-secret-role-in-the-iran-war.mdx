Australia has quietly become part of the war against Iran. The country makes key parts of US F‑35 jets, shares intelligence through the Pine Gap base, and sent sailors on a US submarine that sank an Iranian warship. Australian troops aren’t fighting on the ground, but the government says its support is defensive. Critics argue Australia is a co‑belligerent and the country is pulled into a wider conflict.
