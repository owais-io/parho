After two patients died from mould in a Sydney hospital, the New South Wales government launched an urgent review of hospital maintenance. They found 112 problems, such as possums, asbestos, pigeons, flies and mold. About half are solved, some are being fixed, and six are still being examined. Health Minister Ryan Park says the system will act fast, while Premier Chris Minns announced a $10 billion investment to upgrade hospitals.
