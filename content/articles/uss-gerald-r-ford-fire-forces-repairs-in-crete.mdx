A fire broke out on the USS Gerald R. Ford, the world’s biggest aircraft carrier, while it was in the Red Sea. The blaze injured about 200 sailors, damaged 100 sleeping berths, and took hours to control. The ship also has a long‑running problem with its toilets, causing clogged lines and extra maintenance. After nine months at sea, the carrier will head to Crete for repairs, raising questions about crew morale and readiness.
