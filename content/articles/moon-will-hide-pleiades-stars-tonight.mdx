Tonight, the moon will pass in front of the Pleiades, making a few bright stars disappear for about an hour. The Pleiades are a famous star cluster in Taurus, about 440 light‑years away, and the event will happen around 9 pm in London. As the moon moves, the stars will pop back out when the moon’s other side turns toward them. Check your local time for the exact moments.
