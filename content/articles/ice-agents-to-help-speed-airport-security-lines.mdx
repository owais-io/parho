President Trump said ICE agents will start working at U.S. airports on Monday to cut the long security lines that have built up because TSA agents have been working without pay during the government shutdown. Border czar Tom Homan will lead the effort, but the exact duties of ICE are unclear. Lawmakers, unions, and civil‑rights groups warn that ICE agents aren’t trained for airport security and could create new safety problems.
