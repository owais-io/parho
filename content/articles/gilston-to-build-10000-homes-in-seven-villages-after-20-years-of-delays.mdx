After two decades of legal fights, the first bricks are being laid on Gilston, a £1bn project in Hertfordshire that will create 10,000 homes in seven connected villages. It’s designed as a future‑proof town, with schools, health centres and parks built before the houses. The plan focuses on walkable, car‑free living and aims to mix affordable, social and private homes. The project will bring jobs, new skills training and a fresh housing model.
