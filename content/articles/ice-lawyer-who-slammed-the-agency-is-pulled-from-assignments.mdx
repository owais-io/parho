ICE lawyer Julie Le publicly said the job was terrible and was removed from detail work for Minnesota’s U.S. attorney’s office. She testified that the department often ignores court orders, and she had handled 88 immigration cases in a month before deciding to stay. A judge ordered her to explain why ICE failed to release five detainees. The case highlights growing scrutiny of ICE’s Minnesota operations.
