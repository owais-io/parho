Jeremy Webb, a 16‑year‑old from New South Wales, died after a tick bite caused an allergic reaction to meat, leading to anaphylaxis. The incident happened while he was camping, when he ate sausages, collapsed and could not be revived. It’s the first recorded fatal case of alpha‑gal meat allergy in Australia. Experts say the allergy, linked to tick saliva, can trigger severe reactions hours after eating red meat.
