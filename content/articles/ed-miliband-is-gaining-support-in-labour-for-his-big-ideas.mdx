Ed Miliband, the energy secretary, is becoming more popular inside Labour because he brings deep thinking and big ideas. After the party lost a lot of its intellectual energy, Miliband’s focus on tough questions—energy policy, the future economy, and how to beat populism—fills a gap. Leaders like Keir Starmer are still learning to think big, while Labour looks for fresh ideas to win voters and stand out from rivals.
