China is now Germany’s largest trade partner, surpassing the United States, according to new data. Chancellor Friedrich Merz will visit Beijing this week, meeting Chinese leaders and touring sites like the Forbidden City and major German firms there. German exports to China are lower than imports, and U.S. tariffs may have hurt trade with the U.S. Germany’s auto makers rely on Chinese factories, so the country is balancing ties while the EU eyes limits on Chinese goods.
