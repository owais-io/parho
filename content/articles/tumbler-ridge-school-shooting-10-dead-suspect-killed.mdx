On Tuesday, a gunman opened fire at Tumbler Ridge Secondary School in British Columbia, killing 10 people, including himself, and injuring dozens. The town is only 2,500 people and the school had about 160 students. Police reached the scene in two minutes, found the suspect dead, and said there were no other threats. Prime Minister Mark Carney and provincial leaders expressed grief and called for community support.
