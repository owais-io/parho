Two British‑based tech founders run a company that made an Iranian messaging app called Gap Messenger. The app is popular in Iran and is part of a government‑controlled internet that censors and watches users. A watchdog says Gap has sent user data to Iranian authorities. The founders live in a quiet Sussex town, keep a low profile, and have not replied to questions about the allegations.
