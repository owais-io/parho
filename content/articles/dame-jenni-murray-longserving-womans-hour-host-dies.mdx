Jenni Murray, 75, died after a 33‑year run as the voice of BBC Radio 4’s Woman’s Hour. She turned the show from light‑hearted radio into a forum for tough topics like menopause, domestic violence and gender equality. Known for sharp interviews with politicians, celebrities and everyday people, she also sparked controversy over her views on trans rights and Brexit. Born in Barnsley, she was an award‑winning broadcaster who left a lasting mark on UK radio.
