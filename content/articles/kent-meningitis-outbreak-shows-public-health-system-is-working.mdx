A meningitis outbreak in Kent has hit 18 confirmed cases and 11 being investigated, with two young people dying. Public health officials used contact tracing, antibiotics and vaccinations – around 2,500 doses – to limit spread. The NHS and UK Health Security Agency have handled the crisis well, showing that established prevention and treatment work. Debate continues over wider MenB vaccination, but experts say decisions should be evidence‑based. The episode also highlights the need to strengthen infection‑control bodies after the COVID‑era.
