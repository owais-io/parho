The Rising Tide protest in Newcastle saw thousands of Australians paddle into the world’s biggest coal port to block ships. Organisers set up a beach festival with music, kitchens and legal advice, while police created a yellow‑buoy zone that could lead to arrest. Over 130 people have been jailed or charged so far. Protesters want no new fossil‑fuel projects, a 78% tax on coal profits and an end to coal exports by 2030.
