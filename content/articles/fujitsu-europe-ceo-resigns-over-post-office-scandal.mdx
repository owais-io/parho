Fujitsu’s European chief, Paul Patterson, will leave his executive role in March and become a non‑executive chair in the UK. He has led the company’s response to the Post Office Horizon software scandal, where faulty software hurt hundreds of sub‑postmasters. Patterson says Fujitsu must pay victims and will set the amount after the inquiry report. The UK government has already paid about £1.3 bn, and Fujitsu is planning its succession.
