The Metropolitan Police plans to fold its anti‑racism strategy into a wider anti‑discrimination scheme that covers gender, sexuality and race. Critics say this will dilute the focus on Black communities and let racism slide. Dr Shereen Daniels, who led a report that highlighted institutional racism, slammed the move as insulting. Police commissioner Mark Rowley insists the plan keeps race in focus, but many fear it merely rebrands old problems.
