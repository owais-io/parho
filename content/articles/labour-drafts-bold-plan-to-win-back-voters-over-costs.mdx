Labour ministers are working on a bold new plan to shake up the economy and win back voters upset over rising costs. The draft, called the “Beveridge Report for the Economy”, proposes giving local leaders more tax power, dropping national insurance, and big changes to property and land taxes. It also suggests new ways to run government and give tax breaks to start‑ups. The plan will be unveiled after the May elections, as the party faces a possible leadership challenge.
