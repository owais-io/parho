Upemba wildlife park in the Democratic Republic of Congo was raided early Tuesday morning by about 80 armed fighters, mostly from the Bakata Katanga militia. Within half an hour they overran the ranger base, killing eight people—including five civilians and several conservation staff—and looted weapons. The attackers targeted anyone they saw as a threat and even used a foreigner as a shield. The brutal attack highlights the growing danger faced by wildlife workers in the country.
