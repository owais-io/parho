UK Chancellor Rachel Reeves said she hasn't ruled out cancelling the planned 5‑pence rise in fuel duty that will end a tax cut in September. She said the government is looking at emergency help for households as oil prices spike because of the Iran‑Israel conflict. Reeves also stressed keeping fuel prices fair through competition and said renewable‑energy growth would help Britain avoid future shocks.
