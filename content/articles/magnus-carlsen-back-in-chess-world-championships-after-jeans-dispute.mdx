One year ago, Magnus Carlsen, the world’s top chess player, clashed with the chess federation (FIDE) over a rule that banned him from wearing jeans at a rapid‑play tournament. Now, FIDE has lifted that ban, clarified title rules, and Carlsen is ready to compete again in Doha. He will face a strong field, including world champions and rising stars. The event also marks the 99th Hastings chess congress, which is running without its usual council sponsor.
