Scientists in 2025 named 125 new plants and fungi, including a zombie fungus that pops out of a trapdoor spider’s burrow, an orchid whose red‑stained flowers lure male flies, and a bright orange‑red shrub from Peru named after a fire demon from a Ghibli movie. They also found a banana‑and‑guava‑flavored fruit tree in Papua New Guinea and a rare snowdrop in the Balkans. Researchers warn that many species are already threatened, stressing the need to document and protect biodiversity before it disappears.
