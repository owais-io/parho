In February, UK car makers produced 17% fewer vehicles than a year earlier, even before the Iran conflict hit markets. Exports dropped 12%, with big falls to the US (‑34%) and China (‑66%), though EU sales were up 5%. Electric‑vehicle output slipped 3%. The slump is the lowest output since 1952 and signals a crisis, with Nissan’s Sunderland plant and 50,000 VW jobs at risk. Industry leaders call for government support to keep the sector afloat.
