US forces have struck an eastern‑Pacific vessel they say was trafficking drugs, killing four people. The hit brings the overall death toll from US airstrikes in the region to 99 since the campaign started in September. Critics argue there is no proof the boat was involved in smuggling and call the strikes violations of international law, even war crimes. Human‑rights groups say the attacks amount to extrajudicial executions.
