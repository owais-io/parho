In France’s latest local elections, the radical‑left party La France Insoumise (LFI) won several cities, even though the media and politicians have attacked its leader, Jean‑Luc Mélenchon, for controversial jokes and alleged ties to extremist groups. The controversy grew after a far‑right activist was killed in Lyon, sparking accusations that LFI encourages violence and antisemitism. Despite calls for a “cordon sanitaire,” LFI’s supporters keep voting for it, showing that trying to isolate the party isn’t working.
