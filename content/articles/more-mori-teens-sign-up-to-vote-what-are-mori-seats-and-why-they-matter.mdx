New data shows 58% of 18‑to‑24‑year‑old Māori registered for the Māori roll, up from 50% last year. Māori seats are seven special parliamentary seats that only Māori can vote for, though anyone can run. They cover huge regions, usually lean left, and are often won by Labour. There’s still debate about keeping them, with some parties calling for abolition.
