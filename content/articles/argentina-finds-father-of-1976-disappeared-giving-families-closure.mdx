In 1976, Argentine soldiers seized and killed many people, including Mario Alberto Nívoli, who vanished while his wife was holding their baby. New DNA work at La Perla, a former detention center, uncovered his bones, giving families a long‑awaited closure. The find follows years of investigations into the “desaparecidos” and shows how the current government’s attempts to downplay the dictatorship are still contested by human‑rights groups.
