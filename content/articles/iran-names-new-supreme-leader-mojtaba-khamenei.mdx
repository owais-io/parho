After years of uncertainty, Iran elected Mojtaba Khamenei, son of former leader Ali Khamenei, as its new supreme ruler. He has spent most of his life in back‑room politics and has never given a public speech. The move was pushed by the Revolutionary Guard, which wants to keep its power. Many Iranians and outsiders question his experience, health and religious credentials, raising doubts about how he will steer the country.
