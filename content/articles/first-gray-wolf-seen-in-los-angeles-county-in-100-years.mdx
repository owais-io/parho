A three‑year‑old gray wolf crossed into Los Angeles County for the first time in over a century. Tracked by a GPS collar, she’s likely searching for a mate and may wander further north. The sighting marks a milestone in California’s wolf recovery program, which began after wolves were nearly wiped out in the early 20th century. Conservation groups welcome the move but still push for stronger nationwide protections.
