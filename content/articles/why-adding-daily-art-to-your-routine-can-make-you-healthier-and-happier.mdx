In January many people abandon New Year’s resolutions. A new book suggests instead of cutting back, we add creative habits—like choir, gallery trips, or dance—to our daily routine. Research shows regular art lowers death risk, makes us feel younger, and halves depression risk. Though arts funding is shrinking, authors offer daily prompts and advice to keep art alive. A few minutes a day with music, painting or museum visits can boost mood, control and community. Make art a habit.
