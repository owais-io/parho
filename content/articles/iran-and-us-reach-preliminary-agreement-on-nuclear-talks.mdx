Iran and the United States held a constructive meeting in Geneva, agreeing on broad guiding principles that could lead to further talks about Iran’s nuclear program. The discussion focused on allowing the UN agency IAEA to inspect sites, downblending highly enriched uranium, and suspending enrichment for a while. The U.S. is still building up its military presence in the region. Meanwhile, Iran is dealing with protests and arrests of opposition figures.
