Iran’s Revolutionary Guards blocked ships from passing the Strait of Hormuz, a key route for 20% of the world’s oil. The move is sparking fears of a big supply cut, so oil traders expect prices to jump—up to $80 a barrel, maybe more. OPEC+ has agreed to boost production by about 206,000 barrels a day to calm the market, but many oil‑rich countries can’t ship until traffic clears. Higher prices could hit gas pumps and stir stock markets.
