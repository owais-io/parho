The peaceful town of Tapalpa in Jalisco, Mexico, was rocked when drug king‑pin El Mencho was killed in a firefight that left more than 60 dead. Mexican forces, aided by the CIA and a U.S. drone, attacked his hide‑out, sparking a five‑hour battle that burned shops and streets. The violence spread to 20 states and halted travel. Despite his death, cartel activity remains strong, and the U.S. has been praised for the operation.
