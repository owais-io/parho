Fungi are being used to help pine seedlings grow faster and use less fertilizer. A Louisiana nursery sprayed young pine saplings with a liquid made from wild soil fungi. The fungi form a network with the tree roots, helping the plant get nutrients. The startup, Funga, says the treatment could give a 30% growth boost and may replace chemical fertilizers. It also could earn money through carbon‑offset contracts like the one with Netflix.
