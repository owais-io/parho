In Canberra, 100 survivors of Australia’s Stolen Generations gathered to mark the 18th anniversary of the national apology. Many share stories of being taken from their families, placed in children’s homes, and forced to work, with two siblings dying in care. The event highlighted ongoing needs—especially for ageing survivors—and the government announced new funding for support services. The apology was praised as an honest reckoning, but calls for reparations and improved care continue.
