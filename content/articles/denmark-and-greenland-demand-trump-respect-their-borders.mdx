President Trump named a U.S. envoy for Greenland, a Danish territory that many fear could be taken over. Danish Prime Minister Mette Frederiksen and Greenland leader Jens‑Frederik Nielsen said the move violates international law and threatens their sovereignty. The EU, Sweden, Norway and others backed Denmark, while most Greenlanders want independence, not U.S. control. Trump insists the move is about national security, sparking a sharp diplomatic backlash.
