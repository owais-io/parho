Oil majors like Shell, Exxon, and Chevron have jumped to all‑time highs after fighting in Iran sent prices sky‑high. In just two weeks, the combined value of the biggest Western oil companies rose more than $130 billion. Investors expect huge windfalls even though the war has hit some Middle Eastern sites. Environmental groups are urging governments to tax these gains so families can be helped instead of the companies.
