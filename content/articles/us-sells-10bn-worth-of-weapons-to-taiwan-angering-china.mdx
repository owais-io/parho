The U.S. announced a $10 billion arms package for Taiwan, including missiles, howitzers, drones and other gear. President Trump made the deal during a televised speech, sparking a sharp Chinese rebuke that the sale hurts regional stability and China’s sovereignty. Taiwan welcomed the weapons as a boost to its defense, which it plans to fund with increased military spending. The move heightens tensions between Washington, Taipei, and Beijing.
