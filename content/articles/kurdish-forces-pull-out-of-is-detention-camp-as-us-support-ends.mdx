Kurdish forces in northeastern Syria have pulled out of the al‑Hawl detention camp that holds about 24,000 people, most of them former ISIS fighters. The move comes after the U.S. said it would no longer back the Kurdish-led SDF. The Syrian army is now taking control of the camp, raising fears of chaos and possible escapes. Meanwhile, the SDF is losing territory to government troops and is preparing for more fighting.
