California Governor Gavin Newsom signed a bill changing Cesar Chavez Day to Farmworkers Day. The move follows a New York Times report that Chavez sexually abused women and girls who worked in the union he led. Lawmakers say the new name focuses on the workers, not a single leader. The change was rushed after the scandal, and several cities and universities are removing Chavez’s name from public sites.
