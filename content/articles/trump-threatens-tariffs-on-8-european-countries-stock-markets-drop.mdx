President Trump announced new 10‑percent tariffs on goods from Denmark, Norway, Sweden, France, Germany, the UK, the Netherlands and Finland until February, rising to 25% in June. The move aims to pressure those countries into supporting his Greenland plan. Investors are nervous, so major stock indices are expected to fall, while gold and silver prices climb as people seek safer assets. European leaders and business groups warn the tariffs will hurt trade and strain NATO ties.
