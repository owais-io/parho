Magnus Carlsen won the chess.com speed championship in London, beating France’s Alireza Firouzja 15‑12 for his fourth straight title. The event mixed blitz and bullet games and offered a $250,000 prize. While the crowd cheered, Carlsen noted they favored Firouzja. U.S. star Hikaru Nakamura, who usually finishes third, lost in the semi‑finals, the first time in the competition’s 10‑year history that he didn’t reach the final. The tournament ends as organisers prepare for a Freestyle world championship in Germany.
