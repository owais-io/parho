Brenton Tarrant, the man who killed 51 people in Christchurch’s mosques, has asked a New Zealand court to cancel his guilty pleas. He says harsh prison conditions harmed his mental health and forced him to admit guilt. The court will decide if the appeal can go ahead, and if the pleas are withdrawn, he may face a new trial. If not, his life‑sentence appeal will be heard later.
