Gold and silver fell sharply after a market “meltdown” triggered by news that former Fed governor Kevin Warsh may become the next Federal Reserve chair. Gold dropped about 8% to $4,465, then rose to $4,700 but was still 3.5% lower. Silver fell 7% after a 30% Friday slump, ending at $79.60. Despite the metals fall, the UK’s FTSE 100 reached a record 10,341 points. Other markets – oil, bitcoin, and the dollar – also moved on the back of the news.
