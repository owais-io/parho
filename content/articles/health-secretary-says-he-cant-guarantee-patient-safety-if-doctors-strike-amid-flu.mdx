Health Secretary Wes Streeting said he can’t guarantee patient safety if resident doctors strike, citing a bad flu season. He warned that a strike would add pressure to an already strained NHS. Streeting also criticised the British Medical Association and said he would extend the strike allowance if needed. He said that the government is trying to protect doctors’ training places and is ready to step in to keep patients safe.
