Paul Nurse, a Nobel‑winning scientist, was elected a second time as head of the Royal Society – the first scientist ever to serve twice. Some members think the job should go to a woman and say the society feels like a “boys’ club”. Nurse says the role is tough and unpaid, so many people refuse it. He also deals with tough issues like funding, visas, and the controversial inclusion of Elon Musk as a fellow.
