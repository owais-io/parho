Malaysia’s Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim said Malaysian oil tankers can now move through the Strait of Hormuz after talks with Iran. He thanked Iran’s president and said the country will free its ships and crew to continue home. Malaysia depends on the waterway for about half its oil. Other countries like Thailand and China also managed to cross, some without paying Iran. A UK plan to help reopen the strait is in discussion.
