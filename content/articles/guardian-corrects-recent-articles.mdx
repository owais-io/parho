The Guardian admits several mistakes in recent stories. It clarified that Codeberg is a nonprofit that hosts code, not one that builds software like the Librewolf browser; that Tuta’s office is in Hanover, not Berlin; and that Opera is still based in Europe. An interview with Leo Sayer had a typo in the song title. Other corrections covered misreports about a former Putin interpreter’s role in Hungary, economic worries from the Middle‑East war, a record‑breaking U.S. heatwave, and false claims linking ChatGPT to organized abuse.
