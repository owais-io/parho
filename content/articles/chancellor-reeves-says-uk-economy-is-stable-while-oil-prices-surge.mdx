Rachel Reeves told MPs that the UK economy is on a steady path, even as tensions in Iran push oil prices higher. She highlighted lower inflation and improved public finances, but warned that a long‑lasting conflict could upset the Office for Budget Responsibility’s forecasts. Rising energy costs, higher borrowing rates and potential migration and unemployment spikes add uncertainty to her claim of stability.
