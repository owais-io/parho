New Zealand is at risk of more deadly landslides as climate change makes storms stronger and more frequent. Two recent tragedies on the North Island killed 12 people. Experts say that rising temperatures, heavy rains, deforestation and building on slopes make landslides more likely. The government is being criticised for cutting climate‑resilience funding, while scientists urge faster carbon cuts and better planning to protect communities.
