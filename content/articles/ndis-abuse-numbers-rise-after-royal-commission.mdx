People with disabilities in Australia are being hurt more often in group homes than before. The National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) is meant to help them, but new data shows a jump in abuse, neglect and bad care. Last year the watchdog logged over 107,000 incidents, up from 75,000. Many complaints involve restraints, injuries and even sexual contact. Experts say the rise means we need better housing and more money for independent care.
