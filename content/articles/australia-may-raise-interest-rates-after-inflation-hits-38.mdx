Australian inflation climbed to 3.8% in the year to December, up from 3.4% the month before, driven by higher housing costs and a steep rise in electricity prices after subsidies ended. The Reserve Bank of Australia is now expected to hike rates next Tuesday, the first change since November. Most banks predict a single tightening, not a series of hikes, as the central bank responds to the sharper price growth.
