Drax, the UK’s largest power plant, plans to stop burning Canadian wood pellets within a year after a big financial loss and growing concerns about forest sustainability. The company wrote down almost £200 million and is ending Canadian imports because of new tariffs and worries over old‑growth tree use. Despite a £48 million impairment, Drax’s shares reached a 20‑year high after better‑than‑expected earnings and a dividend boost.
