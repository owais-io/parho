Police in Western Australia arrested a 20‑year‑old man for preparing a terrorist attack after finding a racist manifesto at his house. The document outlined plans to target mosques, police and Parliament. Police seized a butterfly knife, illegal ammunition and more than 1,000 rounds of gunpowder. He faces charges of possessing a prohibited weapon, firearms offences and using a carriage service to menace. Prime Minister Albanese called the allegation “deeply shocking,” while Home Affairs Minister Burke warned of rising Islamophobia. The man was denied bail and will return to court on March 23.
