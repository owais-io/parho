Mount Baldy, a popular 10,000‑foot peak near Los Angeles, has seen 23 deaths from 2016‑25, including a recent 500‑foot fall that killed a 19‑year‑old. Authorities closed trails for two weeks, then reopened them while urging hikers to respect the mountain’s dangers. Experts say the weather can change fast, and winter gear like crampons is needed. Officials want a permitting system and better safety rules to stop more tragedies.
