Brighton’s independent shops are struggling. Rising business rates, VAT, staff costs, and crime are cutting profits. The city’s council has introduced rate relief, but many shops still lose money. Small traders fear being replaced by big chains, losing local expertise and foot traffic. Council forums are forming to help, but the future of the North Laine’s quirky shops is uncertain.
