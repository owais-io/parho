Chancellor Rachel Reeves said her new budget will lower living costs by lifting the two‑child benefit limit and cutting energy bills, but to cover a £26 billion shortfall she will raise taxes on incomes, pensions and property. The budget was leaked early by the Office for Budget Responsibility, spooking the bond market. Reeves says the tax hike will create a £22 billion fiscal cushion and help bring inflation closer to the 2 % target, while keeping Britain’s public finances on a sustainable path.
