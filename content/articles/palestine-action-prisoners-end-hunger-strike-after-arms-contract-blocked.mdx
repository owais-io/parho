Three prisoners tied to the Palestine Action group in the UK have stopped their hunger strike after the government decided not to give a £2bn contract to the Israeli arms company Elbit Systems UK. The decision, which will stop the company from training 60,000 UK soldiers each year, met a key demand of the strikers. They are now starting to eat again under medical supervision, but some have paused or still refuse food.
