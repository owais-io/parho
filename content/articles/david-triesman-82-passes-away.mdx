David Triesman, 82, died on Jan. 30 after a long career that mixed politics, unions, football and business. He began as a university lecturer, then led major trade unions before becoming Labour Party secretary in 2003. In 2005 he joined the Lords as a government minister. He later chaired the Football Association, helping run the 2018 World Cup bid, but left after a scandal in 2010. He was known for honesty and a commitment to fairness.
