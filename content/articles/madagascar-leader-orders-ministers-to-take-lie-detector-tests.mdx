After a military coup, Madagascar’s new president, Michael Randrianirina, says any new minister must pass a polygraph test to weed out corruption. He fired the previous cabinet last month, citing youth protests that called for a clean government. The president plans a new cabinet soon and will only interview candidates who score over 60% on the lie‑detector. Critics say the test isn’t scientifically reliable.
