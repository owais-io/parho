A man convicted of child‑abuse, Timothy McDougall, has worked as a touch‑football referee in New South Wales for over a decade, even for junior teams. The sport’s governing body says a loophole in the state’s working‑with‑children check lets him stay on. New laws introduced in September aim to close gaps, but safety advocates argue more action is needed to stop such offenders from touching children at sport.
