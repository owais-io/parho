A recent survey of 23,000 people shows that men born between 1997 and 2012 are twice as likely as baby boomers to say a wife should obey her husband and that the husband should have the final say in important decisions. Only 13% of older men share this view, while 33% of Gen Z men do. The study finds younger men hold more traditional ideas about who should do household chores, be the breadwinner, and how men can show affection.
