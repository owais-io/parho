Dale Vince, a green entrepreneur who has given over £5 million to Labour, faced backlash after saying Israel’s government partly fuels rising antisemitism following a deadly Sydney attack. He quoted Netanyahu and blamed Israeli policy, sparking condemnation from Conservative leaders and prompting Labour to stress it would not excuse terrorism. Vince clarified he condemns violence, but the incident highlights how donor comments can stir political backlash.
