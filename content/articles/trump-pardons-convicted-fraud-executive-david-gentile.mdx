Donald Trump pardoned David Gentile, a private‑equity boss who was just starting a seven‑year prison term for a $1.6 billion fraud scheme. Gentile used money from investors to buy private jets, a Ferrari, and other luxuries. He was released from prison on Nov. 26. Prosecutors and New York’s attorney general said the fraud hurt thousands of investors, and the full details of the pardon haven’t been posted yet.
