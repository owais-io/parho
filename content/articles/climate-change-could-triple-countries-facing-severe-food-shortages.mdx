Scientists say a 2‑C rise in global temperature could almost triple the number of nations in critical food insecurity, from 9 to 24. Poorer countries would suffer the worst, losing food supplies faster than richer nations. The study urges stronger social safety nets, climate‑resilient farms, and better water and soil care to keep people fed. Even rich countries will feel the impact through higher food prices and market disruptions, but they can better absorb shocks.
