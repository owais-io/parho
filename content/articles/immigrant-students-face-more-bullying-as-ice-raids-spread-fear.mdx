Immigrant students are being bullied more and feel scared because of ICE raids, a survey of high‑school principals says. Over 600 principals reported that many students from immigrant families worry about themselves or their families, and that they miss school because of immigration politics. Attendance has fallen, parents sometimes leave the community, and more than a third of students say they’ve been harassed or called hateful names. Schools are trying to help, but feel limited by events outside.
