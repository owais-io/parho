The Inter‑American Court of Human Rights says Peru is internationally responsible for the death of Celia Ramos, who died after a forced sterilisation in 1997. The ruling covers a 1996‑2000 campaign that forced thousands of poor, rural, and Indigenous women to have surgeries without consent. Peru failed to give proper care, and the court demands reparations and measures to stop the practice. It marks a historic victory for women's reproductive rights in the region.
