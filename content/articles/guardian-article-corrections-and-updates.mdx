The Guardian corrects a few errors from recent stories. It says Philomena’s law, protecting benefits for Mother and Baby Home survivors, was scheduled for a second reading on March 28. It clarifies Crown prosecutors only prosecute, not investigate, and that domestic‑homicide research applies only to England and Wales. It also notes the FDA vaccine chief’s second departure, the ATO’s plan to bring work home, a livestream of a rare parrot, and how readers can send complaints.
