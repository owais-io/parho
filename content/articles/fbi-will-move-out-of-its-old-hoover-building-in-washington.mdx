The FBI is shutting down its old J. Edgar Hoover building and moving its staff to new offices in Washington, D.C. Director Kash Patel announced the change on social media. Employees will use space in the Ronald Reagan Building that was once home to the U.S. Agency for International Development. The move aims to give the agency modern, safer facilities and cut costs, helping it focus on national security and crime fighting.
