Social media is helping right‑wing groups spread their ideas faster than ever. Big names like Tucker Carlson let white‑supremacist Nick Fuentes on his show, boosting their followings. Platforms pay for engagement, so fringe messages about immigration, the “Great Reset” and other conspiracies quickly reach millions. These topics then slip into normal political talk, making extreme views feel ordinary. The result is a new, online‑driven way of shaping public opinion and elections.
