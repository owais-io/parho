Brad Lander, the former New York City comptroller, is running for Congress in a liberal district to challenge fellow Democrat Dan Goldman. He has the support of progressive mayor‑elect Zohran Mamdani and Senator Bernie Sanders. Lander, who was arrested while protesting at an immigration court, is seen as a left‑wing challenger. Goldman is a moderate two‑term congressman who has fought against Trump’s immigration policy. The race will decide the district’s next representative.
