After years of opposition, the UK government has officially cancelled the plan to dig a tunnel under Stonehenge. The project, which had cost £179.2 million in planning, was expected to reach £1.4 billion and had been approved only in 2023. Protesters worried the tunnel would damage the world‑heritage site, while some locals hoped it would ease traffic. The revocation means any future scheme would need a new planning process.
