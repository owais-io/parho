Alex Saab, who once ran the economy for former Venezuelan president Nicolás Maduro, was taken into custody by the country's intelligence agency and the FBI in Caracas. The arrest followed a US raid on January 3 that removed Saab from his ministerial role. Venezuelan officials have not confirmed the detention, but the US says Saab will be extradited. Saab’s lawyer disputes the claims, saying his client is fine. The case shows how the political fight around Maduro’s allies keeps flaring.
