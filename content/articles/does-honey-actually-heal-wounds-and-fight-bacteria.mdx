Honey has been used for centuries as a sweetener and a wound remedy. Modern science shows that its high sugar and natural compounds, like hydrogen peroxide, can kill some bacteria and help skin heal. But honey isn’t a cure‑all. It isn’t effective as an oral antibiotic, can contain dangerous spores for babies, and studies are inconsistent. Only medical‑grade honey is safe for wound dressings, and its benefits vary by type and source.
