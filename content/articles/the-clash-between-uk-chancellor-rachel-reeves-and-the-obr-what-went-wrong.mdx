After the 2024 budget, the Office for Budget Responsibility (OBR) lowered its growth forecast, showing a £20‑£40bn gap in the Treasury’s plans. Chancellor Rachel Reeves was blamed for exaggerating the damage. As the OBR revised its numbers, politicians debated whether to raise taxes. The OBR leaked early data, sparked market swings, and later a senior official resigned. The story shows how forecasting changes and political pressure led to a budget showdown.
