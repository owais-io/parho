Victorian arts bodies are hit hard after Creative Victoria slashed or ended its four‑year Creative Enterprises program. Key groups like Writers Victoria, the Australian Print Workshop and Freeplay risk closure, and many organisations lose their steady operational cash. Staff morale plummets, critics say the cuts lack transparency and fairness, and the state’s cultural scene may shift from a vibrant hub to a poorly funded city.
