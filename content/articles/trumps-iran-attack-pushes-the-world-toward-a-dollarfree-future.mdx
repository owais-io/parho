Trump’s sudden air strike on Iran has not only added tension to the Middle East but also sparked a bigger move away from the U.S. dollar as the world’s main currency. The dollar’s value has slipped, and countries are quietly building alternatives—like China’s digital yuan, Europe’s digital euro, and new central‑bank swap lines. As the dollar’s dominance wanes, the U.S. could face higher borrowing costs and a less stable global financial system.
