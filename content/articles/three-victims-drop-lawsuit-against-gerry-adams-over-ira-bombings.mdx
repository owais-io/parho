Three people who were hurt by IRA bombings in 1973 and 1996 have pulled their lawsuit against former Sinn Féin leader Gerry Adams. They had been suing him for symbolic £1 damages, claiming he was an IRA member. Adams says he was never part of the group. The case was dropped after the judge asked whether it was an abuse of court procedure. The claimants say the court was unfair, while Adams welcomed the end.
