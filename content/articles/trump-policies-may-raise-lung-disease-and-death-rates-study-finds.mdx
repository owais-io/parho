Researchers say that the Trump administration’s cuts to health care, loosening of pollution rules and reduced workplace safety could lead to more people getting lung illnesses and dying early. The study looks at 10 Trump policies that cut Medicaid, weaken air‑quality standards and slow clean‑energy projects. Those changes might cause more asthma, COPD and other breathing problems, especially in kids, the poor and people who work in mining or factories.
