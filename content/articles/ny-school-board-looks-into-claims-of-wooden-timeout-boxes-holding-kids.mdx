A school district in upstate New York is checking out accusations that teachers built wooden “timeout” boxes to isolate children, especially those with disabilities. The board has put three officials on leave, moved the superintendent home, and is working with the state education department. The superintendent says the boxes were removed and no child was ever locked inside, but many parents fear they might have been used. The governor said the claims are “highly disturbing” and called for a quick investigation.
