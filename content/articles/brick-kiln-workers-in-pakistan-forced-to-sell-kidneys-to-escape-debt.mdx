In Punjab, thousands of brick‑kiln laborers owe huge debts that grow as owners cheat them. Facing no way out, many workers sell a kidney to a stranger who promises money. They get far less than promised, stay in debt, and suffer health problems. The practice is illegal, but the scale is huge and is seen as a form of modern slavery.
