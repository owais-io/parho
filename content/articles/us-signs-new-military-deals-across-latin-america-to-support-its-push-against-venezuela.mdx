The US is signing new military deals with Latin‑American countries, giving it bases and air access to deploy troops. These agreements – made with Paraguay, Ecuador, Peru, Trinidad and Tobago, and others – come as Washington launches airstrikes and a blockade against Venezuela. While the U.S. says it’s part of a “war on drugs,” critics say the moves aim to pressure Maduro and seize Venezuela’s oil. The deal is sparking worry in the region about a US military buildup.
