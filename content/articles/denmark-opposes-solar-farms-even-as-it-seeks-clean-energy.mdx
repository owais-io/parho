Denmark, known for its aggressive climate plans, is facing a surprising backlash against solar farms. Farmers and right‑wing politicians argue that large solar fields spoil farmland, hurt property values and look ugly. The Denmark Democrats, a populist party, have used the issue to win votes in rural areas, even cancelling projects in several towns. While the country generates most of its power from renewables, the new opposition shows that local support can be hard to keep.
