Trinity Hall, a Cambridge college, plans to reach out to elite private schools for more applicants. Social‑mobility groups, alumni and charities say this move is unfair, hurts equality and could set a bad example. They want the policy scrapped and have even threatened to stop funding. The college insists it isn’t changing admissions rules and says it will still support disadvantaged students. Critics say more general outreach would be better.
