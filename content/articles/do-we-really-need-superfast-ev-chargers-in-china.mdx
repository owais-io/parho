The author argues that China’s push for superfast electric‑vehicle charging may be overkill. Most cars sit parked for hours, so widespread ultra‑fast chargers aren’t essential. They suggest high‑speed units near train stations could serve taxis, while regular drivers can charge at supermarkets or home outlets. Matching China’s charging speed isn’t necessary for everyday use.
