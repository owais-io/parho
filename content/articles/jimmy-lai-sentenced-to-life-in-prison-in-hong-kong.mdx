Jimmy Lai, the 78‑year‑old founder of the banned newspaper Apple Daily, was found guilty of national security and sedition charges and handed a life sentence in Hong Kong. Rights groups and the UK say the verdict is a political attack on press freedom. The ruling was welcomed by Hong Kong officials and China, while critics call it a sham conviction. Lai can appeal, but his health and future in solitary confinement raise further concerns.
