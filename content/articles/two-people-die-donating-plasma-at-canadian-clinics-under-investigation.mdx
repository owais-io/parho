In Canada, two donors died while giving plasma at facilities run by Spanish company Grifols, sparking a federal investigation. Health Canada says the deaths were rare but notes the clinics have had problems with record‑keeping, donor screening and equipment maintenance. Critics say the move to private companies for blood products puts safety at risk. The deaths add to concerns over the country’s blood‑safety record.
