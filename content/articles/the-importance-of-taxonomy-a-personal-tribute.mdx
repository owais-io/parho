After her husband, a professor who studied bacteria, died, the writer shares how his work in taxonomy—classifying species—was surprisingly useful. He helped research cold‑adapted Bacillus in Antarctica, advised on cleaning up old tannery sites to prevent anthrax, and helped the food industry fight spoilage. The letter reminds us that while taxonomy may not be flashy, it is vital to many fields. She hopes more students will follow in his footsteps.
