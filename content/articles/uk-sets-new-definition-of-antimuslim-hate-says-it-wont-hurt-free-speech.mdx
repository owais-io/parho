UK Communities Secretary Steve Reed told MPs that a non‑statutory definition of anti‑Muslim hate will help tackle rising hate crimes while keeping free speech safe. The wording, part of a wider social‑cohesion plan, lists unacceptable prejudice but stops short of naming race. It also gives police more power to curb extremism, lets the Charity Commission shut bad charities, and pushes for more online English classes. Critics warn the strategy may still miss the far‑right threat and could chill legitimate criticism.
