Robert Mueller, the former FBI director and special counsel who led the 2016 election interference investigation, died at 81. His family said he passed away quietly and asked for privacy. Mueller served as FBI chief from 2001 to 2013, reshaping the agency after 9/11. As special counsel, he built a case against 34 people, including six Trump aides. Trump called the probe a witch‑hunt, but Mueller stayed professional. The FBI and public remember him for integrity and leadership.
