Wildlife officials in Florida killed more than 5,000 non‑native green iguanas that had fallen from trees during a sudden cold snap. The Florida Fish and Wildlife Commission set up a two‑day drop‑off for people to hand over the stunned reptiles. The cull helps curb the iguanas’ damage to plants, sidewalks and seawalls. The freeze also threatens citrus crops and could hurt the state’s economy.
