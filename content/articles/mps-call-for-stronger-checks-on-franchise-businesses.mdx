Parliamentarians say the government must stop the gaps that let franchise businesses go unchecked after a series of scandals, including a tragic case involving a former Vodafone employee and claims of abuse at McDonald’s. They urge new laws that set a clear code of conduct and stronger enforcement, and want business rates that reflect a company’s real ability to pay. The report also highlights the daily loss of high‑street shops and unpaid invoices that hurt small firms.
