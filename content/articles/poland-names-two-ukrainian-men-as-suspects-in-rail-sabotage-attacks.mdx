Poland says two Ukrainian men linked to Russian intelligence sabotaged its rail network last weekend, blowing up a freight train in Mika with a C‑4 bomb. Prime Minister Donald Tusk warned that Russia uses “disposable” operatives to stir anti‑Ukrainian sentiment. The suspects crossed into Poland from Belarus, then fled back. Poland has raised the threat level for key rail lines but kept the overall alert unchanged, urging people to report suspicious activity.
