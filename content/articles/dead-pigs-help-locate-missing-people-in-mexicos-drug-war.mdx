In Mexico, over 100,000 people are missing, many thought taken by drug cartels. To aid families, volunteers bury dead pig bodies in fields and watch how plants grow on top of them. The plant growth signals where a real body might be. Scientists also use drones and ground‑scanning tools to spot graves. This new method has already helped find dozens of bodies, giving families some answers.
