Australia’s Reserve Bank governor Michele Bullock warned that a rate hike could happen at the March board meeting, not just the usual May session. She cited a sudden oil price rise after attacks on Iran that could keep inflation stubbornly high. The RBA has already raised rates to 3.85% and will consider how quickly to act to curb inflation while keeping jobs. Markets moved to a 25% chance of a March hike.
