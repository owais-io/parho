In January, the UK economy stopped growing, with GDP flatlining at 0%. The slowdown comes as rising oil and gas prices, driven by the Middle East conflict, hit households and businesses. Service and hospitality jobs fell, and unemployment hit a five‑year high. While construction grew a bit, manufacturing dipped. The result is higher inflation, tighter borrowing costs, and worries that a recession could follow. Chancellor Reeves said the government will cut costs and build a stronger economy.
