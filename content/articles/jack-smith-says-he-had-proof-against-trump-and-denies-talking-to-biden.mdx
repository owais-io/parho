Jack Smith, who led the failed federal case against Donald Trump, told a House committee he never spoke to President Biden about the cases. He said he had clear evidence that Trump tried to hide classified papers and interfere with the 2020 election, and that such actions would hurt democracy if left unchecked. Smith explained court rulings slowed the cases and that phone records he used were legal. He insists he had no political bias.
