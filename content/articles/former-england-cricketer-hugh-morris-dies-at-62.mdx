Hugh Morris, who captained Glamorgan and played for England, died at 62 after a long battle with bowel cancer. He was a respected player, scoring nearly 20,000 first‑class runs, and later led England’s cricket board, helping the team reach the top of the Test rankings and win the 2010 T20 World Cup. Morris also helped grow Glamorgan, turning Sophia Gardens into an international arena. He earned an MBE for his cricket and charity work.
