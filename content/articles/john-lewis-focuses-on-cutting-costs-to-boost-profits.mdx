After the pandemic, John Lewis decided not to raise outside money or build buy‑to‑rent flats. Instead, the partnership trimmed costs and focused on basic shopkeeping. A new chair, Jason Tarry, shut down the building plan and pushed a “keep it simple” turnaround. Profit rose 6% to £134 m and cash flow jumped 63%. The company cut its department‑store sites to 34, updated hours, IT and distribution, and is working on a loyalty card.
