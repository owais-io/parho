When Manchester City named Pep Guardiola manager in 2016, many expected a tough start. He did lose more games than before, but he also brought a new style that focused on passing, quick attacks, and playing from the back. Over the last decade, many Premier League teams adopted this style, making the league faster and more technical. Guardiola’s patience and willingness to learn— even from older managers like Neil Warnock—helped him win titles and shape modern football.
