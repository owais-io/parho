Because the war in Iran has pushed oil prices back up, European motorists could spend about €220 more on fuel each year, analysts say. In the UK the extra cost would be around £140. The surge could cost EU drivers €55 bn in total. Drivers who use their cars more will feel it hardest. Experts say this shows how fragile Europe’s dependence on imported oil is and urge a shift to electric cars, heat pumps and renewable power.
