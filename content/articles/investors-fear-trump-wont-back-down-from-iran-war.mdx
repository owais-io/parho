Since the US and Israel hit Iran with missile strikes, investors fear a long‑lasting war. The conflict could choke the Strait of Hormuz, pushing oil prices above $85 a barrel and hurting global markets. Australian stocks fell, but the dollar stayed strong because Australia exports energy. Analysts worry higher oil will raise inflation, forcing central banks to raise rates. Many investors think the war will end quickly, but that’s uncertain.
