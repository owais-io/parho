Caribbean athletes keep winning big, from cricket to sprinting, yet the region’s governments miss a chance to turn talent into lasting progress. Poor funding, weak schools and bad governance mean players train on broken tracks and leave the country to earn money. The article says the Caribbean needs real plans—build clubs, give scholarships, keep athletes in‑country and involve the diaspora. Only then can sport drive jobs, health, tourism and pride.
