Rijksmuseum, one of the world’s most famous art collections, is building a 3,500‑square‑metre branch in Eindhoven. The new centre, set on the banks of the River Dommel close to the main train station, will take six to eight years to finish and will be funded by the city and tech giant ASML. The move aims to share Dutch Golden‑Age masterpieces with people in the south, easing Amsterdam’s overtourism.
