Paul Taylor, a reporter in 1979, watched Iran’s monarchy fall and the new Islamic republic rise. He interviewed Ayatollah Khomeini, saw how clerics took control, and covered the hostage crisis and Iraq’s invasion. The article explains how foreign attacks actually strengthened the regime, leading to a long‑lasting clerical rule. Taylor notes that reforms in the 1990s were crushed, and today the country remains dominated by hard‑line leaders.
