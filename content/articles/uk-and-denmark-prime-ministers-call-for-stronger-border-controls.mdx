The leaders of the UK and Denmark say that when people don’t trust their governments, communities break apart. They argue that handling irregular migration is the key to keeping trust. Both countries are tightening asylum rules: Denmark lowered asylum grants, and the UK is speeding up deportations and linking settlement to integration. They also want Europe to act together, fight smuggling, and improve human‑rights rules, all while staying fair to real refugees.
