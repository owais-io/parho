People in Iran are still marching, demanding that the cleric‑run government stop. Some cheer the ex‑shah’s son, Reza Pahlavi, thinking a monarchy could help, but many are skeptical and want a new, secular system. The United States, led by President Trump, has been cautious and has not backed Pahlavi, fearing a civil war. No single leader or clear plan has emerged, leaving Iranians feeling stuck and frustrated.
