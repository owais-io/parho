Chile's new president, José Antonio Kast, is set to take office. The 60‑year‑old, ultra‑Catholic, has long opposed abortion and could roll back the law that allows it in limited cases. He appointed an anti‑abortion minister and his party wants to end the exceptions. Women’s rights groups, who fought for the 2017 law and 2025 bill, warn that access could be cut again. Activists continue to march and push for protection.
