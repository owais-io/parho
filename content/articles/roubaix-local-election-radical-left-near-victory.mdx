Roubaix, a northern French town that used to thrive from textile factories, is now the poorest town in mainland France. Local elections are happening, and the radical left party La France Insoumise (LFI) is close to winning. The mayoral candidate, David Guiraud, focuses on cleaning streets, housing, and police. He and the LFI have faced national controversies, but voters see them as a fresh choice. The victory would boost LFI leader Jean‑Luc Mélenchon's chances for next year’s presidential race.
