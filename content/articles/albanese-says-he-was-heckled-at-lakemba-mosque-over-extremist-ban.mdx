Prime Minister Anthony Albanese says he was heckled by some worshippers at a Lakemba mosque in Sydney when he visited after banning extremist groups like Hizb ut‑Tahrir. He denied evidence and said most of the crowd welcomed him. A few people shouted slogans and one was removed by police. The mosque’s leaders said the visit was a chance to speak directly with government and that it didn’t change their stance on Gaza and Lebanon.
