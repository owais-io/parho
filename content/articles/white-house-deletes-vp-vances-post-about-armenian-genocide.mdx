Vice‑president J.D. Vance posted a tweet saying he was visiting an Armenian genocide memorial and using the word ‘genocide’. The White House later deleted the post, saying it was an error made by staff. The move angered Armenians in the U.S. and U.S. lawmakers, who said it hurt the memory of the victims and showed weakness to Turkish pressure. The incident highlights the U.S. debate over how to name the massacre.
