Chile voters chose José Antonio Kast, a far‑right politician who promised to crack down on crime, after a rise in violent crime and fears of gangs. Kast won 58% of the vote in December, saying he would create an emergency government to restore safety. He is a supporter of the former dictator Pinochet and has long opposed abortion and the morning‑after pill. Many worry his hard‑line stance could threaten civil rights.
