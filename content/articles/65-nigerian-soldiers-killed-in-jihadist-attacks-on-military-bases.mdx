Two weeks ago, jihadist fighters from ISWAP attacked four military bases in Nigeria’s Borno state, killing at least 65 soldiers. The attacks also kidnapped around 300 civilians, including women and children. The Nigerian army said it beat back the attacks, but it has not confirmed the death toll. U.S. troops have arrived to train soldiers, and critics say the government is distracted by other events while the country faces a growing insurgency.
