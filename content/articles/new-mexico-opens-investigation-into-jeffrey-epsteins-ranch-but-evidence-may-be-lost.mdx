New Mexico officials have restarted an investigation into Jeffrey Epstein’s former ranch after the federal government said it would stop looking for evidence there. The state is hoping to find witnesses and documents that might help victims. But experts say it’s hard to get useful evidence after so many years and the property has changed hands. The state’s truth commission will also study the ranch’s past.
