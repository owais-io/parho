On Friday, the Justice Department released some documents about Jeffrey Epstein, but not all the files it was legally required to share. Congress passed the Epstein Files Transparency Act last month, giving the public a 30‑day deadline to see every unclassified record. Lawmakers on Capitol Hill slammed the partial release and said they could sue the DOJ. The agency said it would add more files soon while protecting victims’ privacy.
