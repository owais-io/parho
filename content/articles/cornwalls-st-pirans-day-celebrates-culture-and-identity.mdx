Cornwall’s annual St Piran’s Day, celebrated on March 5, is growing into a big festival that goes beyond beaches and pasties. Towns like Launceston, Bodmin, Truro, Falmouth and St Austell host parades, music, speeches, and even a two‑day festival linking Cornwall to Wales. The day reflects a renewed pride in Cornish culture, language and identity. It also boosts hopes for more local control and shows Cornwall’s growing role in renewable energy and global culture.
