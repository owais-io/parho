Uline, owned by big Trump donors, hired Mexican workers under a “shuttle program” that sent them to U.S. warehouses for weeks, paying them Mexican wages and giving them the hardest jobs. A former employee, Christian Valenzuela, says he was injured in 2023, denied benefits, and forced to sign resignation papers. Uline has not responded to questions. The story shows how corporate labor practices can leave workers in danger and unpaid.
