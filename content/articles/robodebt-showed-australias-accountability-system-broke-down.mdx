A whistleblower in 2016 revealed that Australia’s automated debt system, known as robodebt, was issuing wrong bills and the government lied about it. The scheme hurt poor people, even causing a child’s death. Over the years, several bodies – the Ombudsman, courts and the National Anti‑Corruption Commission – failed to hold anyone accountable. The latest NACC report names two senior officials for corruption but won’t charge them, leaving many feeling the system still lacks justice.
