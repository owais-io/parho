Keir Starmer is heading to Beijing to try and boost UK‑China trade, but experts say the chances of a big deal are slim. Analysts predict the visit will produce only a few memorandums of understanding and perhaps a small investment pledge, while bigger issues like human rights and the UK’s trade deficit remain unchanged. The trip is more about keeping dialogue open than clinching a major contract.
