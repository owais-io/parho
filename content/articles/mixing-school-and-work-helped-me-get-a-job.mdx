The author explains how a “sandwich” course – part apprenticeship, part university, part work‑experience – helped him land a job. In the 1960s he spent a year in industry, three years at university, and a final year back on the shop floor. The mix gave him clear direction and ready employment. He urges today’s apprenticeships to revive this model, blending work and study across all sectors.
