Scientists say one‑third of people on Earth now live in places where the heat makes everyday tasks like walking or housework impossible. Elderly folks face the toughest limits, spending over 900 hours a year trapped by heat. The hardest hit are poorer countries, even though rich nations drive most emissions. The Gulf, South Asia and parts of West Africa suffer the most. Researchers urge faster cuts in fossil‑fuel use and more cooling and warning systems for those most at risk.
