A 31‑year‑old man in Western Australia has been charged with terrorism after allegedly trying to bomb a protest on Invasion Day in Perth. This is the first time the state has used its terrorism laws in such a case. Premier Roger Cook said the charges showed the seriousness of the act and called the attack “what it is.” The case highlights new security measures for public events.
