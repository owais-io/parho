Starmer’s long‑time chief of staff, Morgan McSweeney, stepped down after a row over Peter Mandelson’s appointment as Washington ambassador. Some Labour MPs welcomed the move, seeing it as a sign of loyalty, while others think it shows Starmer’s weakness. McSweeney was a powerful figure behind the scenes, and his exit may leave Starmer exposed for upcoming by‑elections and local polls. The resignation fuels speculation that the prime minister could fall sooner.
