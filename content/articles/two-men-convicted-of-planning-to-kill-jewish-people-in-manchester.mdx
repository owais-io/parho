Two men who pledged allegiance to the Islamic State were found guilty of plotting a mass attack on Manchester’s Jewish community. They planned to use semi‑automatic rifles and had an undercover agent pose as a fellow extremist. Police seized the weapons before the attack could happen. The case was heard at Preston Crown Court, and the verdict highlighted how close the country came to a deadly terror attack.
