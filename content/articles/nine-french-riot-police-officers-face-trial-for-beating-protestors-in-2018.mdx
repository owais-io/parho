Nine members of France’s riot police are being tried in Paris for allegedly beating peaceful protestors who had taken shelter in a Burger‑King during the 2018 Yellow‑Vest protests. Video shows the officers striking people who raised their hands, and one victim received 27 blows. If convicted, the officers could face up to seven years in prison and a large fine. The case highlights the violent police response during the large anti‑tax demonstrations.
