A new, highly contagious flu strain called Super‑K has already infected more than 2,500 Australians. Health officials say it spreads quickly but isn't more dangerous than other strains, and the current vaccine still helps people, especially children. The outbreak began in August and has spread to 30 countries. In Australia, cases spike even in January, putting pressure on hospitals. Vaccination rates, especially among kids and seniors, are low, so doctors urge more people to get vaccinated.
