On Wednesday, the UN General Assembly voted to call trans‑Atlantic slavery the gravest crime against humanity. Ghana’s President John Mahama led the push and won support from most of Africa, Asia and the Americas. A few western states, including the US, opposed or abstained, fearing future reparations. The decision energises the African Union’s 2026‑2036 “decade of reparations” plan, with Mahama set to drive the effort and seek justice for descendants of enslaved people.
