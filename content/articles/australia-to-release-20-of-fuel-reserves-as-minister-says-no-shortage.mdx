Australia will let fuel companies pull about a fifth of their petrol and diesel stocks from the national reserve. Energy Minister Chris Bowen said the country isn’t close to running out, even though some remote towns are running low. The move is part of the IEA’s global oil release plan and should help curb panic buying and rising prices. The government won’t change taxes or ration fuel, and it will allow slightly lower‑quality petrol for a while.
