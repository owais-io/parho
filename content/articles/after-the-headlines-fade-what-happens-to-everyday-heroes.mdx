Everyday people who act bravely in violent incidents become instant heroes, but the spotlight fades quickly. The Guardian story follows survivors like Anneke Weemaes‑Sutcliffe after the 2016 Brussels bombings and other civilian saviors. Doctors find that 90% of awardees suffer PTSD or depression, and most receive no formal help. Media attention can both validate and destabilize them, leaving many isolated and financially strained. Researchers urge early support to prevent long‑term harm.
