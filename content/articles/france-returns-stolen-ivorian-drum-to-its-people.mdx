A 4‑metre, 430‑kg talking drum that had been taken by French colonial officials in 1916 is back in Côte d’Ivoire. The Djidji Ayôkwé, long a symbol of resistance and communication for the Ebrié people, arrived at Abidjan’s airport this Friday. After years of negotiations, France handed it over to Ivorian authorities. The return is a historic moment of justice, cultural pride, and a step toward further artifact restitution.
