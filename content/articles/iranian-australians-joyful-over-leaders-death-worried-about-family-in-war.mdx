After airstrikes hit Iran, many Iranian Australians felt relief that the country’s top leaders were killed. They celebrated online, but the fighting quickly turned into worry as internet blackouts cut them off from relatives. People like Ehsan Hakimi, who lives in Sydney, have not heard from parents or siblings since the strikes. The news that the regime might collapse has sparked hope for change, but the war’s cost looms large.
