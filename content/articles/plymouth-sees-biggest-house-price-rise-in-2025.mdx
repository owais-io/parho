Plymouth's house prices rose 12.6% in 2025, the biggest jump in the UK, thanks to new homes and better shops, sports and food. Other places like Stafford and Wigan also saw double‑digit rises. Across Britain prices were up 3.7%, but London actually fell a little. The country has mixed results – Scotland stayed flat, while Hull jumped 6.5%. Tax changes are slowly cooling the pricey London market.
