Prime Minister Anthony Albanese announced that Thursday will be a national day of mourning for the 15 people killed in the Bondi beach terrorist attack. Flags will fly at half‑mast, major buildings will be lit in a “light of unity” theme, and a minute of silence will be observed at 7:01 pm. The day isn’t a public holiday, but people are encouraged to take part, share a candle, and perform acts of kindness.
