The article argues that arts learning in UK state schools has fallen, leaving most talent in private schools while public students miss out. It highlights how the BBC has historically shared music and drama with everyone but is now under‑supported. Critics say the government must step up, giving schools more money, qualified teachers, and equal chances for all kids. Strengthening arts could boost education, wellbeing, and give every child a creative future.
