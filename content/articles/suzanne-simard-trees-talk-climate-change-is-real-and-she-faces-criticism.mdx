Ecologist Suzanne Simard argues that forests are social networks of trees connected by fungi, and that cutting forests and planting only fast‑growing conifers fuels deadly wildfires and turns Canada’s forests from carbon sinks into emitters. She calls for keeping “mother trees” to help forests recover, but her ideas have sparked scientific backlash. Despite criticism, Simard’s work is gaining public support as climate change hits Canada hard.
