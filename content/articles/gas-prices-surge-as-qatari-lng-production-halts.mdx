Qatar's largest LNG plant was hit by Iranian drone strikes, stopping production and cutting 20% of global LNG supply. This has pushed European wholesale gas prices up 50% and could hit 130% if shipping through the Strait of Hormuz stops for a month. Europe relies heavily on LNG, and low storage levels after a cold winter make the situation urgent. The U.S. can help as an LNG exporter, but UK households could see higher bills if the disruption lasts.
