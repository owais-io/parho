The film city of Los Angeles is losing its edge. Jobs in lighting, grips, camera crews are shrinking, and many productions travel to cheaper locations abroad. Big tech and streaming companies now produce and own most shows, cutting out the old studio model. Los Angeles is offering huge tax breaks to keep crews on its soil, but the industry is still fighting a mix of pandemic damage, strikes, and changing tech. Experts say Los Angeles will stay the hub, but it must adapt.
