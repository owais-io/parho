After Donald Trump’s return to the White House, a growing number of transgender Americans feel unsafe and have gone to the Netherlands to ask for asylum. The biggest Dutch refugee camp in Ter Apel holds about 2,000 people, including 35–50 trans U.S. seekers. The Dutch system is strict; many claims are rejected, and the U.S. is seen as a safe country. Those denied face deportation, and for trans people that could mean danger or forced detransition.
