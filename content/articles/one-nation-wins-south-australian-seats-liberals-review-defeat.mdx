South Australian voters gave the right‑wing One Nation Party a boost, winning at least one lower‑house seat and leading in several others. Labor kept control with 38% of the vote, while the Liberals suffered a sharp drop to 19%, winning only four seats. The result left Liberals calling for “sobering lessons” and a shift to the centre. Federal MP Barnaby Joyce defended One Nation’s hard‑line stance on immigration and culture.
