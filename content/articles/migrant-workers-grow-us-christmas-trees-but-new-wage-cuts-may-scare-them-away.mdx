In North Carolina, Mexican workers on H‑2A visas harvest and care for the state’s famous Fraser fir Christmas trees, spending months in the fields and away from their families. They work long, tiring hours and face health risks from chemicals and isolation. The Trump administration’s plan to lower H‑2A wages by $5‑$7 an hour could drive many workers home, hurting the tree industry and the local economy.
