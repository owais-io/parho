Sir Anthony Leggett, Nobel‑winning physicist who studied superfluids, died at 87. The 1957 launch of Sputnik pushed him from a classics degree to physics, after he got a scholarship. He later earned a first at Merton College and became a leading expert on superfluids and superconductors, especially helium‑3. His work earned him the 2003 Nobel Prize in Physics. Leggett was known for his energy, curiosity and willingness to help others.
