Japan’s first female prime minister, Sanae Takaichi, won a landslide vote, giving her party a supermajority. Her party can now push big spending plans and a two‑year food tax cut, but critics worry it could hurt Japan’s huge debt. The win also fuels her aggressive stance on China and Taiwan. Heavy snow made voting hard, yet turnout rose to 56 %.
