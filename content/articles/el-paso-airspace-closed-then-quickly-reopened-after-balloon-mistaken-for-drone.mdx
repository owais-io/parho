On Tuesday the FAA shut down 10 nautical miles around El Paso airport for 10 days, saying a possible cartel drone threat had entered U.S. airspace. Within hours the closure was lifted when officials realized a party balloon had been mistaken for a drone. The brief shutdown caused confusion for airlines, travelers and local officials. Trump’s drone‑busting order, Pentagon tests and mixed statements from Mexico added to the chaos, but the airspace is now open.
