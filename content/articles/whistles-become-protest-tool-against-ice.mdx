Activists across the U.S. now carry small whistles to warn people when ICE agents show up. The whistle is blown in short bursts when ICE is nearby and in long bursts when someone is being detained. Protesters have handed out hundreds of thousands of whistles, turning the device into a symbol of resistance. Some right‑wing voices claim whistles are weapons and say they may have contributed to violent incidents. The debate shows how even simple tools can spark controversy.
