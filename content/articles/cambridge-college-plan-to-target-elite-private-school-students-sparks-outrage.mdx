A Cambridge college, Trinity Hall, decided to focus on students from the country’s top private schools, claiming it would improve fairness. Critics say this is reverse discrimination and the decision sparked social media outrage and protests from alumni. The author argues that privilege can be mistaken for talent and urges universities to change admission rules to recognise true ability, not background. He highlights the ongoing gap between private and state school students at Oxbridge.
