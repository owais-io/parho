Police charged a 31‑year‑old man with terrorism after he tried to throw a homemade bomb at an Invasion Day protest in Perth. The device, aimed at a crowd of about 2,500 people, failed to explode, but authorities say the act was driven by a racist, pro‑white ideology. Premier Roger Cook called the incident a first for Western Australia and warned against hate. The case is being tried in court.
