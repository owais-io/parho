UK policymakers are urged to spend about £4 bn on a new social tariff that would give low‑income households a 21 % discount on gas and electricity. The think‑tank says the scheme would cut bills by about £300 for the poorest families and protect them before next winter. A blanket cut would waste money and leave some poor homes behind. The government is looking at targeted help, but data gaps mean it could take time.
