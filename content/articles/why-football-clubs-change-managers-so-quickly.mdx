Last week, Tottenham’s Thomas Frank and Nottingham Forest’s Sean Dyche were sacked after poor runs. The Premier League now sees a record eight managers leaving this season, a sharp rise from the early 1990s. Managers’ jobs have shrunk to about a season and a half, with clubs treating them as “ferrymen” to tide over crises. Owners, shareholders and social media fuel impatience, turning managers into scapegoats. This trend mirrors the fast turnover seen in politics and corporate CEOs.
