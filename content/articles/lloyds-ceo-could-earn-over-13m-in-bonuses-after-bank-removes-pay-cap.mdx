After the UK lifted a 2014 bonus cap, Lloyds Bank plans to raise its CEO Charlie Nunn’s maximum bonus to more than £13m, a 45% jump. That move matches rivals Barclays and HSBC, who have set ceilings of £14‑15m. The change follows shareholder approval of higher pay limits and a new pay policy that ties more to performance. Critics worry about inflated salaries, while supporters say it helps attract talent.
