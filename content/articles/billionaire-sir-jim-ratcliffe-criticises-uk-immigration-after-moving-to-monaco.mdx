In 2020, Sir Jim Ratcliffe, owner of Ineos and Manchester United, shifted his wealth to Monaco, sparing an estimated £4 bn in taxes. Later, he publicly warned that Britain was being “colonised” by migrants, using harsh language and wrong statistics. His comments sparked backlash from politicians, football fans and the public, who said they fueled racism and xenophobia. The incident highlights the tension over tax avoidance and immigration policy.
