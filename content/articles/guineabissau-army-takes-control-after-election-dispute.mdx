Three days after a presidential election that both leading candidates say they won, the army in Guinea‑Bissau says it has taken full control of the country. Soldiers stopped the election process, closed borders, and set up a military government. The move follows gunfire at key government sites and a long history of coups. The weak nation, known for drug trafficking, has struggled with political stability for decades.
