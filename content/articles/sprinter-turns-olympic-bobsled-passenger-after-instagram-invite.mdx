Ashleigh Nelson, once a top British sprinter, was invited to join the bobsleigh team after a photo‑post from pilot Adele Nicoll. A knee injury and the end of her sprinting career left her looking for a new challenge. She trained for 18 months, learned to hold on without a seatbelt or airbag, and now races at the Winter Olympics, often with her eyes tightly shut. Her journey shows how a sprinter’s power can help start a sled at speed.
