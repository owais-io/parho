Poland’s foreign minister said the weekend’s train sabotage was a state terrorism act ordered by Russia, and the country has shut its final Russian consulate in Gdańsk. The attack blew up rail tracks near Mika on the Warsaw‑eastern line, causing no injuries but damaging the tracks. Investigators say two Ukrainian men, who entered from Belarus, were involved and likely worked with Russian security services. Poland plans to deploy troops to protect key infrastructure.
