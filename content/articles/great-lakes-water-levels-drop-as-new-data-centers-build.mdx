Water in the Great Lakes is falling, and new data centers that need huge amounts of water for cooling are being built nearby. Farmers and towns worry that these facilities could strain local water supplies and harm the environment. While data‑center companies promise jobs and tax breaks, residents are concerned about pollution, power surges, and long‑term water use. Some communities are fighting back, but companies sometimes win legal battles to proceed.
