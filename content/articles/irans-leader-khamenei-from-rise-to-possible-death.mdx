Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, Iran’s long‑time supreme leader, has been a powerful but controversial figure. In 2024 he spoke to crowds in Tehran, calling Israel doomed. Later, Israel and the U.S. struck his compound, and officials say he may have been killed, though no confirmation has come. Khamenei’s rule began after the 1979 revolution; he built the Revolutionary Guard and backed groups like Hezbollah. The attacks expose Iran’s weakened position and spark speculation about who will take over next.
