Sarah Mullally has become the Church of England’s first woman archbishop, stepping into a role hit by past safeguarding failures, internal divisions over gender and sexuality, and questions about the church’s place in modern Britain. She has only a six‑year term and must steady the church, address abuse scandals, and decide how to deal with its unique political role.
