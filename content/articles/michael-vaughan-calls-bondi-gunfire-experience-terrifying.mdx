Former England captain Michael Vaughan was in a restaurant a few hundred yards from the Bondi Beach attack when he heard gunshots. He called the experience terrifying. The attack on a Hanukkah gathering killed 15 people, including a child, and one gunman was killed. Vaughan, now covering the Ashes, expressed support for the victims and urged people not to be cowed. Other cricketers and sports clubs also voiced condolences as the series continues.
