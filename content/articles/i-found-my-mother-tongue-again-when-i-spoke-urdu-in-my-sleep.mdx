She grew up speaking Urdu in Pakistan, but after moving to the UK and Australia she learned English and Urdu slipped from everyday life. When she returned to Pakistan, Urdu came back instantly, proving her first language was never truly gone. The experience shows how bilingualism can reconnect people with family, culture and a sense of belonging. This deeply personal journey underscores how language shapes identity.
