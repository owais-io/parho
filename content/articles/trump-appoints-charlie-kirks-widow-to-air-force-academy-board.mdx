President Trump added Erika Kirk, the widow of slain activist Charlie Kirk, to the 16‑member Board of Visitors at the U.S. Air Force Academy. Her husband had served on the board until his death in September. No formal announcement from the academy, but her name appeared on the roster, joining other Trump appointees like Senator Tommy Tuberville and Dina Powell. Kirk, a former Turning Point USA chief, will carry on her husband’s legacy in the academy’s oversight.
