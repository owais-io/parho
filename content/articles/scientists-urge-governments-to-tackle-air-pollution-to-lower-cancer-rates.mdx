Scientists say cutting air pollution should be a key part of government plans to lower cancer rates. The European Code Against Cancer now tells governments to meet WHO air‑quality limits, cut indoor pollution and stop using solid fuels for heating. It warns that 28,000 lung‑cancer cases each year in Europe come from pollution and that better air could reduce deaths by more than 10 %. The plan also calls for cleaner energy, safer school sites, and more walkable cities.
