Jane Hume, a Victorian senator, was named deputy leader of the Liberal Party after former leaders were replaced. Her appointment follows a rocky tenure that included criticism over a policy to end work‑from‑home for public servants and a controversial comment about Chinese Australians. Hume, a long‑time party member and former minister, is expected to help rebuild voter confidence and keep the moderate wing influential. The move comes as the party struggles to recover after last year’s election loss.
