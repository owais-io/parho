Lord Allen was hired in 2024 to overhaul the British Horseracing Authority, but he faced pushback from racecourses and racing groups. He tried to set up an independent board and keep low data fees for tracks, but the BHA’s media rights are controlled by racecourses, sparking disputes over revenue. Allen lost the support of owners, trainers and the Racecourse Association, and stepped down after a few months. His exit shows the sport’s stubborn factional politics.
