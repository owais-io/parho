California and much of the western US are in for a brutal early‑spring heatwave that could set new temperature records. Over the next week, the west will see 20‑30°F higher than normal, with temperatures that may hit 100°F in Los Angeles and even 110°F in desert areas. The heat will stress 26 million people, dry out snowpacks, and raise wildfire risks, making public‑health warnings a top priority.
