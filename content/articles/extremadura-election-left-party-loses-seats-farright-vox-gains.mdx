In Extremadura, Spain’s left‑wing ruling party, the PSOE, lost 10 of its 28 seats in a low‑turnout regional election, while the far‑right Vox doubled its seats to 11. The centre‑right People’s Party (PP) is still the largest but needs Vox to govern. Many voters who disliked the PSOE went to Vox or the smaller UxE group, hurting the PP’s chances. The results highlight corruption scandals hurting the left.
