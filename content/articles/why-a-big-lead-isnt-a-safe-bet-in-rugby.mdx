The piece explains why a large lead in rugby can disappear, using Glasgow Warriors’ 28‑21 win over Toulouse as a prime example. It notes that in recent years, teams have come back from big deficits thanks to changes in rules, better use of substitutes, and quicker momentum swings. The article highlights how a 21‑point advantage can be wiped out and that such reverse plays will become more common in the season.
