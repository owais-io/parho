Labour leader Keir Starmer is busy changing his plans, especially the plan for digital ID cards, to keep the public’s worries about the cost of living front‑and‑center. He says that sticking with unpopular policies only hurts the party. Inside the cabinet, people argue whether these quick reversals show lack of control or are necessary to win voters. The moves have also caused some MPs to question the party’s direction.
