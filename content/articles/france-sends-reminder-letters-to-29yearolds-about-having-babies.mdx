The French government has begun mailing letters to people who are 29, urging them to start a family. The writer of the letter says the move misses the real concerns of young people. He points out that money, mental health and parenting support are major hurdles. He compares France to Sweden, which offers longer maternity leave and free childcare. He calls for more concrete help, not just a polite reminder.
