In South Korea, people in their 20s and 30s are being teased by Gen Z and Alpha as “Young 40s.” The nickname, once a compliment, now feels humiliating. Interviews with those labeled this way show they feel confused, self‑conscious, and pressured to keep up with trends. The article notes that as they age, the criticism fades, and many in the next generation learn to accept themselves without trying too hard.
