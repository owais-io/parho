Australia has announced that it will restrict the sale of common rat poisons to protect wildlife. The government regulator said that chemicals called second‑generation anticoagulant rodenticides (SGARs) kill not just rats but also birds, owls, quolls and other native animals that eat poisoned rats. From March, only licensed professionals can buy them, and supermarkets like Bunnings, Coles and Woolworths must stop selling them. The move follows years of science showing the poisons are an unacceptable risk to wildlife.
