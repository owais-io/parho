France’s recent local elections showed that centre‑leaning parties surprised many by winning more seats than expected. The far‑right National Rally gained some council seats but missed big targets like Marseille. In Paris, Socialist‑Green candidate Emmanuel Grégoire became mayor, defeating the far right. The left’s radical LFI struggled, while the centre, led by former PM Edouard Philippe, held strong in places like Le Havre. Overall, centre forces look set to challenge the far right next year.
