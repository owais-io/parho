Crypto companies pumped more than $10m into Illinois Democratic primaries, trying to shape candidates who would favor lighter regulation of digital assets. But they spent a lot and didn’t win. The AI industry also threw money at opposing candidates, spending almost $20m in total. The money ended up backing many progressive candidates, but many still lost. The result shows tech firms are still learning how to influence politics.
