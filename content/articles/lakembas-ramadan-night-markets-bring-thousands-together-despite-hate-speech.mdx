In Lakemba, a suburb of Sydney, the first night of Ramadan brings more than a million visitors to its night markets. The markets buzz with food stalls, music, and friendly crowds from many cultures. The event shows a united, welcoming community, especially after a right‑wing politician, Pauline Hanson, made hateful remarks about Muslims. Her words sparked threats and fear, but the markets continued to celebrate together, offering hope and safety for everyone.
