Norfolk Wildlife Trust bought a 136‑hectare plot called Wood Norton to bring back an old woodland and wildlife after its 100‑year anniversary. The land, once farmed, will be turned into natural habitat, giving birds, deer, insects and clean water. The project also supports local farmers by providing pollinators and reducing flooding. It will help cut river pollution and even open the way for new homes. The trust hopes nature will guide the restoration.
