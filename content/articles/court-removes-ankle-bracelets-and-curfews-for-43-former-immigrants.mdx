Australia’s High Court ruled that the laws giving the government power to put former immigration detainees on ankle bracelets and curfews were unconstitutional. The decision means 43 people who had been monitored in the community will no longer wear bracelets or follow a nightly curfew; instead they just have to check in with officials. The ruling is part of a broader push to end extra‑monitoring rules that were created after the 2023 detention crisis.
