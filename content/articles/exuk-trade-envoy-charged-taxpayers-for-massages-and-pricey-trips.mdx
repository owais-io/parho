Andrew Mountbatten‑Windsor, the former UK trade envoy, is alleged to have billed taxpayers for massages, expensive flights, extra hotel rooms and a retinue of staff while he worked in the 2000s. Civil servants say a culture of deference let the claims go unchecked, with the money slipping into different budgets. He was briefly arrested and released, but no evidence of illegal conduct has emerged yet.
