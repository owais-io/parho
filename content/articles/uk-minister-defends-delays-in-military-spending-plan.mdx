A UK defence minister, Luke Pollard, says the government’s long‑delayed defence investment plan is a big job that will change the armed forces, focusing on more autonomous capabilities. He refuses to give a completion date, but stresses that the plan will show how to fund the Strategic Defence Review and address a £28 bn funding gap over four years. The delays are also holding up the Tempest fighter jet project, which needs the plan’s approval.
