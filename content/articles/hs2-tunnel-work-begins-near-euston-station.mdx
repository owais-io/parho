British ministers say high‑speed rail work into London is back on track. Two big tunnel‑boring machines have begun digging the final 4.5 miles of tunnel from Old Oak Common to a new Euston station. The new station will have more platforms than originally planned to let more trains run to the north and beyond. The project is expected to finish in about 12‑18 months and could bring huge economic benefits, including jobs and housing.
