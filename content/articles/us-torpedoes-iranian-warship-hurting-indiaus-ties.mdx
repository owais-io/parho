An U.S. submarine fired a torpedo that sank the Iranian warship IRIS Dena in the Indian Ocean, killing 84 people. The attack happened during a naval review in India while the ship was near Sri Lanka. The U.S. said it was a legitimate target, but India and others called it a betrayal and questioned the legality of striking a friendly ship. The incident has hurt India‑U.S. ties and raised worries about security in the Indian Ocean.
