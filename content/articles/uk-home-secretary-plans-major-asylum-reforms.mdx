Shabana Mahmood, the UK's Home Secretary, is set to unveil sweeping changes to the asylum system. The plan would make refugee status temporary, review it every 2½ years, and increase the number of people removed from the country. It also tightens support, cuts jobs and housing for those who can work, and adds digital ID checks. The government says the reforms aim to stop illegal boat crossings and restore control.
