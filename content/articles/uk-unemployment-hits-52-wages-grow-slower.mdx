Unemployment in the UK climbed to 5.2% in the last quarter, a five‑year high. At the same time, wages have slowed: basic pay rose just 4.2% and total earnings grew 4.2%, down from 4.6% earlier. Payroll numbers fell by 130,000 last year, and businesses say higher national insurance and minimum wage costs are tightening hiring. Fewer jobs and more people looking means more competition for openings.
