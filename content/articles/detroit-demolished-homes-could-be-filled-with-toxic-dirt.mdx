Many Detroit houses were torn down and the empty lots were filled with rubble that may contain dangerous chemicals like lead, mercury, and asbestos. The city’s rapid demolition program did not always test the fill material, and officials say the problem could be larger than reported. Residents worry about dust and health risks, especially for children, while the city plans to release test results and decide on clean‑up actions.
