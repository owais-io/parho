Britain’s diplomatic service is under threat from big cuts that could weaken the country’s ability to navigate global crises. Experts say the cuts, driven by budget worries and political shifts, would strip away crucial expertise, especially in Africa and the global south. The Foreign Office is losing staff and funding, sparking criticism from MPs and partners who argue that diplomacy is essential for early warning and cooperation.
