Hummus is now part of the UK cost‑of‑living basket, proving it’s become as common as ketchup. The dip, first sold in the 1980s, has grown from a niche snack to a daily staple, with supermarkets offering dozens of flavours. The rise reflects a wider shift toward Middle‑Eastern foods and healthier choices. Hummus now fills lunchboxes, spreads on toast and even replaces mayo in sandwiches.
