The AI boom has led to a surge in plans for new US data centers, but many projects are being canceled or delayed. Problems include shortages of steel and transformers, long wait times to connect to the power grid, and local community protests. Investors are also nervous about a possible AI bubble and tariffs that raise costs. Some companies are turning to battery storage and new transformer designs to speed up projects.
