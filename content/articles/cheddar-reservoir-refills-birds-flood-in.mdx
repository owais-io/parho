After weeks of heavy rain, Cheddar Reservoir in Somerset finally fills up, drawing thousands of birds. Coots, gulls, ducks, and even rare red‑necked grebes appear, each building energy for breeding. The author hunts for the elusive red‑necked grebe, spotting it near the shore, unsure if it’s the British or American race. The return of water has made the reservoir a vibrant, unexpected winter bird hotspot.
