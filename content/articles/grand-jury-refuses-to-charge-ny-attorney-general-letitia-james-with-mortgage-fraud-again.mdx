A federal grand jury said no to new mortgage‑fraud charges against New York Attorney General Letitia James for the second time this week. The Justice Department had tried to revive the case after a judge dismissed the original charges, saying the prosecutor was improperly appointed. James is accused of saying a home was a second residence when it was actually rented out. She denies any wrongdoing, and experts think the evidence is weak.
