NASA confirmed that a small meteor entered the sky over Ohio early Tuesday morning, making a loud sonic boom that people heard as far away as Pittsburgh and even New York. The space rock was about 2 metres wide, weighed roughly 6 tons, and flew at 45,000 miles an hour before burning up. No debris has been found, and the event was captured on video by the National Weather Service.
