US troops have taken Venezuelan president Nicolás Maduro from Caracas and sent him to New York. Many Venezuelans in the UK feel relief that a dictator is gone, but they also worry that foreign troops invading their country is wrong and could bring more instability. The move comes after years of poverty, hunger and repression that forced millions to flee. Some doctors and community leaders say the world must watch carefully for future violence and legal problems.
