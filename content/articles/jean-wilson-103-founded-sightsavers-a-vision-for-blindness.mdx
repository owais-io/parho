Jean Wilson (1922–2026) was a British charity founder who started Sightsavers with her husband in 1944. She coined the name “river blindness” to raise money for a treatable eye disease. The charity now gives millions of eye tests and half a million operations each year in 30 countries. Later she co‑founded Impact to tackle all disabilities and helped launch the world’s first hospital train. Her work changed how blind people are seen worldwide.
