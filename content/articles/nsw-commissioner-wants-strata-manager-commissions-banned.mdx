In New South Wales, a government report says that strata managers – the people who run apartment blocks – should stop earning big commissions from insurance and other services. The report says those commissions create conflicts of interest and can drive up costs for residents. The commissioner recommends a fee‑for‑service model and suggests a three‑year phase‑out or a ban. The change could save residents money and improve competition.
