Letters in The Guardian say the new John Lewis Christmas ad feels like a clever trick that pushes shoppers into buying pricey gifts when many are already under financial strain. They say the ad pretends to care but actually triggers the holiday shopping instinct. One writer notes it shows women only in background, doing chores, while men are the heroes, reinforcing old gender roles. Overall, the letters argue the ad manipulates emotions and keeps unfair expectations alive.
