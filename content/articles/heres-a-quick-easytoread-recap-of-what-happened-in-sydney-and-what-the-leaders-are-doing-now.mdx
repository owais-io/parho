- **What happened:**     On Sunday, a man fired guns at a Jewish Hanukah celebration on Bondi Beach.     15 people were killed, 13 others were hurt. - **Who was the gunman?**     He was a 25‑year‑old man who had a gun licence. He and his son had six guns that they used in the attack. - **The police response:**     Police shot and killed the gunman on the beach.     He is now being charged with 59 offences, including 15 counts of murder. - **What’s being done right now?**     * Prime Minister Anthony Albanese is calling a national security meeting again.     * Premier Chris Minns is pushing for a state‑based royal commission to find out why the attack happened and how to stop it in the future.     * Both the prime minister and the premier are encouraging Australians to show solidarity with the Jewish community—lights, flags at half‑mast, minute of silence, and candles. - **Health updates:**     13 people are still in hospitals. A few are in critical but stable condition; others are recovering. - **Next steps for the state:**     * Chris Minns says the NSW Parliament will debate laws that give police more power and ban hate symbols.     * He wants to work with Jewish leaders to make sure the royal commission is strong and has the right powers. - **A bigger picture?**     The attacks have shaken the community.     Many are asking what could have been done to stop it.     The leaders are saying they will investigate every angle—hate speech, extremist ideology, and how it spreads online. - **Why it matters:**     The attack showed that antisemitism can turn into violence.     The government wants to make sure this never happens again by tightening laws and putting a real investigation in place. **Bottom line:**   The attack has moved the whole country into a state of reflection. Leaders are promising to find answers, enforce stronger laws, and stand with the Jewish community. If you want to help, you can light a candle, hold a minute of silence, or do a good deed for someone you don’t know.
