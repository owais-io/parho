After an Israeli strike on Iran’s South Pars gas field, Iran launched missiles that hit Qatar’s Ras Laffan LNG complex, damaging the world’s biggest seaborne‑gas plant. The hit could take years to repair, pushing up gas prices and threatening the global energy market. Saudi, UAE, and Kuwaiti facilities also faced drone and missile attacks. Prices for LNG and crude oil spiked, heightening fears of a long‑term supply crisis.
