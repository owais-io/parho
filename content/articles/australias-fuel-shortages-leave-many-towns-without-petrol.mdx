In Australia, dozens of service stations are out of fuel because people are buying too much after price spikes linked to the Middle East war. The NSW government says it has enough petrol but struggles to deliver it to remote towns. State and federal officials are meeting with suppliers, but some farmers and workers warn that the lack of diesel could hurt farms and local communities. Prices are staying high, and some call for rationing or lower taxes.
