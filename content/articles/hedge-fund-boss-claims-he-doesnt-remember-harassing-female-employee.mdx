Crispin Odey, a former hedge‑fund boss, is on trial in London after the FCA banned him for sexual‑harassment allegations. He told the judge he can't remember a comment that hurt a female employee, even though the employee’s diary recorded it. Odey also admits to touching a colleague without consent in 2005. He is fighting the FCA’s ban, sued the Financial Times, and faces civil claims from five women.
