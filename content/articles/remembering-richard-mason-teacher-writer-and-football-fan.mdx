Richard Mason (82) was a beloved history and Latin teacher who also loved football. He coached school sports, led a local football club to a league title, and saw over 5,000 matches, including England’s 1966 World Cup. After teaching for decades, he moved to Italy, taught English, and later the Italian school system. The Covid‑19 lockdown in Bergamo left him in a wheelchair, but he kept writing “Letters from Italy” to his family. He is survived by his sister, brothers, and nieces.
