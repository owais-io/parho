The world economy did better than expected in 2025, but growth is slowing for 2026. Prices are falling, so central banks may stop cutting rates. Artificial‑intelligence spending could boost productivity, but investors fear a stock‑market bubble. Trade wars and tariff rules keep costs high, especially between the U.S., China and Europe. Jobs are falling in rich countries, and debt‑heavy governments could face bond‑market pressure. Policymakers must balance inflation, growth and job creation.
