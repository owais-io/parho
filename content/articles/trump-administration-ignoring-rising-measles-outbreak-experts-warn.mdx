Experts say the Trump administration has ignored the rising measles outbreak in the US. With over 1,000 cases and a few deaths, the CDC’s messaging and funding have been cut, slowing tracking and response. Local health departments are stepping up, but the national response feels weak. Experts call for more surveillance and better vaccine promotion to stop the spread.
