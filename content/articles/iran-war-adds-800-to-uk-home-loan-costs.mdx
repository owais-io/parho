The Iran war has pushed UK mortgage rates up, making the average two‑year fixed loan cost about £800 more per year than before. Lenders have pulled almost 700 deals, and the average fixed rate jumped from 4.83% to 5.28% in just two weeks. With 1.8 million mortgages ending in 2026, many borrowers will need new loans at higher rates. The Bank of England is likely to keep rates steady at 3.75%.
