Australia’s National Anti‑Corruption Commission (NACC) says two former public servants were involved in serious corruption while creating and running the 2017 ‘robodebt’ program, but found no evidence against former Prime Minister Scott Morrison. The report says the officials lied to the Department of Social Services and the ombudsman. Morrison was cleared because he relied on incorrect advice. The findings have drawn criticism from opposition politicians who say ministers must be held responsible.
