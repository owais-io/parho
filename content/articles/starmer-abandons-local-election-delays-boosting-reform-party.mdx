Prime Minister Keir Starmer has reversed his government’s earlier plan to postpone local elections in 30 councils, a move that has angered opposition parties and some Labour MPs. The reversal came after a legal challenge from Reform leader Nigel Farage, who sees the decision as a chance to win seats. The government had been trying to simplify local councils, but the U‑turn has sparked calls for a minister’s resignation and political backlash.
