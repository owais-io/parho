Nigel Farage, the Reform UK leader, said public Muslim prayers in the UK should be banned after an event in Trafalgar Square. Muslim groups and politicians across the spectrum slammed the remarks as bigoted. The call sparked a debate over how religious gatherings fit into British culture. While some Conservatives support the stance, others in Scotland and the UK call for tolerance and freedom of religion. The controversy comes as Reform UK pushes its manifesto for the Scottish election.
