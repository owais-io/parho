Sydney’s new fish market opened on Monday, a shiny 20,000‑square‑metre space that’s cleaner and more expensive than the old site. 40 vendors – from fresh salmon to pizza – took over after a year‑long delay. 836 million dollars were spent, and the government will add 70 million for transport. Visitors praised the tidy stalls but complained about the high price of king crab and limited public‑transport access. The mayor said it will attract food lovers from far and wide.
