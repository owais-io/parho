Australia’s plan to close the life‑expectancy gap for Indigenous people is still far off. Children born between 2020‑22 are expected to live 8–9 years less than their non‑Indigenous peers, and only four of 17 government targets are on track. A local family is pushing hard: the parents run a community organisation that supports education, health and jobs for young Aboriginal people. The government is urged to invest more in community‑controlled programs and set realistic goals.
