Australian bowler Mitchell Starc said the International Cricket Council (ICC) must step in and replace the shaky “Snicko” technology used in the Ashes. The system’s glitch cost England a key wicket and sparked further disputed calls on Day 2. Starc, frustrated, asked why the ICC isn’t paying for reliable, uniform tech across all series. He noted that inconsistent equipment leaves players and fans uncertain about the fairness of umpire decisions.
