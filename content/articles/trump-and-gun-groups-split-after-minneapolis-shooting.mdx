Alex Pretti was shot by U.S. border patrol in Minneapolis, sparking a tense debate. Trump and officials blamed him, claiming he threatened officers, while gun‑rights groups like the NRA and GOA condemned those statements, urging a full investigation. They argue Pretti had a legal right to carry a gun and that the government’s messaging is misleading. The incident shows a rare split between Trump and his usual gun‑rights allies, though the groups still support law‑enforcement.
