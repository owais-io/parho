Jewish leaders at Bondi’s one‑week‑after‑attack vigil urged a federal royal commission to look into the terror attack that killed 15 people. While the state already set up a commission, Prime Minister Albanese was booed by some attendees when he arrived. Speakers praised local politicians for their help, highlighted acts of bravery, and urged Australians to refuse to be silenced by hate. The event ended with a call for continued resilience and action.
