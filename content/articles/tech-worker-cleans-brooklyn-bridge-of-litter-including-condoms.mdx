Ellen Baum, a 37‑year‑old tech employee, noticed the Brooklyn Bridge littered with trash—tissues, hair ties, socks, condoms, and tampons. She decided to help and spent eight days cleaning the north side, taking 90‑minute shifts. Her effort drew local media attention and inspired other New Yorkers to join. Despite the city’s cleanup duties, Baum keeps fighting for a cleaner bridge, urging people to stop leaving waste behind.
