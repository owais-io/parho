Australia’s shadow cabinet has agreed to partner with Labor to pass hate‑speech laws after the Bondi beach massacre. In a special parliamentary session, shadow home‑affairs minister Sussan Ley met Prime Minister Albanese to find a compromise. The law aims to protect victims and their families. Critics warn that rushed laws can be ineffective, and the session began with a moment of silence for the Bondi victims.
