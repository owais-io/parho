Alolita, a mother from tiny Tuvalu, used a new Australian‑Tuvalu deal to escape rising seas and move with her family to Melbourne. The Falepili Union lets 280 Tuvaluans a year settle, work and study in Australia. After a lottery she arrived with her kids, but still shares a flat and worries about housing, schooling and jobs. The move shows how climate change forces people to leave home, while richer countries must act.
