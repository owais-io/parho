Bangladesh's new prime minister, Tarique Rahman, was sworn in, ending 18 months of caretaker rule and Sheikh Hasina’s 15‑year reign. The BNP won two‑thirds of seats, marking a comeback after two decades out of power. Rahman faces tough tasks—curbing inflation, fighting corruption, reviving the garment export economy—and plans closer ties with China while maintaining links with the West and India. Voters also saw new youth parties and a prime‑minister term limit.
