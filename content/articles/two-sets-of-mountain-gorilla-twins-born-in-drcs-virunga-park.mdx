Two sets of mountain gorillas were born as twins in Virunga National Park, the DRC, a rare event that shows conservation work is paying off. Rangers are closely watching the newborns to keep them safe from disease and predators. The twins remind scientists that healthy females can produce more kids, and that vets and protection have helped the population rise from 250 to over 1,000. Yet rangers still face danger from armed groups.
