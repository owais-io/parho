More people are being diagnosed with ADHD, sparking worry that normal feelings are being labelled as illness. The UK health secretary wants a review of the diagnoses, but the author says the problem isn’t just over‑diagnosis. Genetics play a small role, while modern stresses – poverty, isolation, and endless screens – damage family connections and children’s brains. He argues we need to give parents and kids more support and empathy, not blame, to help everyone thrive.
