After years of debate, TikTok’s Chinese parent ByteDance closed a deal to create a U.S. company that will be mostly owned by investors like Oracle, Silver Lake, and MGX. The new venture keeps 80% ownership and gives TikTok a board of security experts. The arrangement lets the app stay online in the U.S., avoiding the ban Trump and Congress had threatened. Trump signed an executive order approving the deal, and the U.S. side will manage the app’s data and algorithm.
