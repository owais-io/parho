An agreement that has kept Muslim prayer at Jerusalem’s Al‑Aqsa Mosque separate from Jewish prayer for six decades is now falling apart. Israeli far‑right politicians have let Jews visit the site, blocked Muslim caretakers, and even arrested an imam during Ramadan. The moves, led by the new police chief and national security minister, risk sparking wider violence in Jerusalem and beyond.
