Freddie Scappaticci was a 77‑year‑old spy who worked for MI5 inside the IRA during the Troubles. He ran a brutal unit that tortured and killed informants. In the 1990s MI5 let him escape to England and kept his identity secret. Families of his victims still don’t know he existed. A 9‑year investigation called Operation Kenova revealed MI5’s knowledge, the money he got, and why he was never prosecuted.
