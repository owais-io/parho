Florida’s new House Bill 945 would set up a state counter‑intelligence unit that could watch residents for their political views. The plan would give the state a CIA‑style office, blurring law‑enforcement and spying. Critics say it repeats past abuses, lacks oversight, and could spread to other states, threatening free speech and press. If approved, Florida could lead a wave of local spy agencies that undermine civil liberties.
