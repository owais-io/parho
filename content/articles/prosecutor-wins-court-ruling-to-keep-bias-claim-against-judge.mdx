Sally Dowling, the director of public prosecutions in New South Wales, won a legal ruling that lets her keep trying to have Judge Penelope Wass removed from a case because of suspected bias. The supreme court said the state’s Bill of Rights does not stop a prosecutor from asking a judge to step aside. It didn’t decide whether Wass should be recused, and the dispute stems from Wass’s criticism of Dowling in a parliamentary inquiry about a child‑defendant leak.
