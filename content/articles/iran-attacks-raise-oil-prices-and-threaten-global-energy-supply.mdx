Iran’s attacks on oil sites and shipping in the Middle East have scared ships from moving through the Strait of Hormuz, a key route for 20% of the world’s oil. Because of the danger, shipping slowed, oil prices jumped over 10% to above $80 a barrel, and European gas prices rose sharply after a drone strike on Qatar’s LNG plant. The fighting could keep prices high for weeks, hurting global economies and politics.
