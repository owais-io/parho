Scientists found a molecule in python blood that rises after a big meal and can cut appetite. When given to obese mice, the compound made them eat less and lose 9% of their body weight in a month. Unlike current drugs that slow stomach emptying, this molecule works in the brain’s appetite center and has fewer side‑effects. Researchers hope it could inspire a safer obesity treatment.
