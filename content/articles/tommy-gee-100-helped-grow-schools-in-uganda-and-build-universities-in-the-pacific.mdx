Tommy Gee lived to 100 and spent most of his life helping communities grow. In the 1950s he worked in Uganda, where he doubled secondary schools and supported local people. After independence he helped set up a new university in Fiji and later led universities in Papua New Guinea. Back in the UK he was a civil servant, a university director, and a local councillor. He loved change, travel, and sharing stories.
