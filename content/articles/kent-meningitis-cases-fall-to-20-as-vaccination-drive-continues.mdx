Kent's meningitis outbreak has seen confirmed cases drop from 23 to 20 after some tests reclassified earlier positives. Nine suspected cases remain under review, bringing the total to 29. Almost all confirmed cases are MenB and were hospitalized. A mass vaccination and antibiotic campaign is underway, with over 8,000 vaccines given. Two students died, but health officials urge people to watch for symptoms and seek help fast.
