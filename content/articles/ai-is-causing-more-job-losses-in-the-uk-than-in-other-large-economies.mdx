A Morgan Stanley study shows UK firms lost more jobs to AI than firms in the US, Japan, Germany or Australia, with an 8% net loss in the past year. While AI raised productivity by about 11.5%, it didn’t create enough new jobs, leaving many workers—especially younger ones—worried about their future. London’s mayor warned of mass unemployment, urging governments to create replacement roles.
