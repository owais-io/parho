The U.S. government has lowered the cost to formally give up citizenship from $2,350 to $450, even though it will lose money on the fee. Many Americans living abroad—especially those born in the U.S. but who never lived here—have struggled with tax reporting and bank rules, prompting a surge in renunciation requests. A backlog of over 30,000 appointments remains, but the new rule, effective 13 April, aims to ease the burden.
