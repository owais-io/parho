After a mass shooting in Australia, two Republican congressmen made shocking, anti‑Muslim remarks. They called for a U.S. ban on Muslims, deportations, and citizenship revocations. Democratic leaders and civil‑rights groups slammed the comments as hateful and called for accountability. The Council on American‑Islamic Relations labeled one senator a “bigot.” Officials also urged leaders to confront such rhetoric. This backlash shows how political speech can fuel hate and the need for swift condemnation.
