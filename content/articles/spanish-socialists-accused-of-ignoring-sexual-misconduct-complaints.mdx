Spain's ruling Socialist Workers' Party (PSOE) is under fire for not acting on sexual misconduct complaints from women. Several senior men, including a former Moncloa aide and a regional politician, faced accusations that were ignored for months. The party admitted a computer error and said it will investigate, but critics say this shows a pattern of cover‑ups. Opposition leaders used the story to accuse the government of hypocrisy and corruption.
