UK's chief medical adviser, Prof Chris Whitty, said that weight‑loss medicines like Mounjaro and Wegovy can’t solve the country’s growing obesity problem. He warned the drugs have side‑effects and people usually regain weight once they stop using them. Instead, he urged stronger rules on junk‑food advertising and healthier food reformulation. Whitty compared the fight to past public‑health successes such as smoking bans. He called for action to make food safer and reduce unhealthy options for children.
