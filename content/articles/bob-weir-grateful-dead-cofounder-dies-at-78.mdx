Bob Weir, 78, the rhythm guitarist and one of the co‑founders of the Grateful Dead, has died. He passed away surrounded by family after battling cancer and lung problems. Weir wrote and sang many famous Dead songs like “Truckin’” and “Sugar Magnolia,” and later played in his own band, RatDog. He was also a UN goodwill ambassador. The family shared the news on Instagram and asked for privacy while thanking fans for their support.
