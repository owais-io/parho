Sir James Munby, a High Court judge who pushed for better legal aid and child protection, died from a heart attack at 77. He was famous for telling governments off when they cut legal help or failed to care for patients. Munby also made family courts more open, supported no‑fault divorce, and fought for mental‑health beds for young people. He left a legacy of fairness and compassion for the vulnerable.
