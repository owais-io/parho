Al West, a local farmer, uses a small digger to rebuild Dartmoor’s damaged peatlands. He fills ditches, lifts the ground, and lets rainwater soak back into the soil. Keeping the peat wet stops carbon release and lets moss grow again. Over the last 17 years the South West Peatland Partnership has restored 5,000 hectares; this year they added 1,700 hectares. The work helps curb climate‑change damage and supports wildlife.
