Southern Australia is facing a record‑breaking heatwave, with temperatures near 50°C in parts of Victoria and South Australia. Such extreme heat is the biggest cause of weather‑related hospitalisations and can trigger heart attacks, kidney problems, and falls, especially for older people and those with existing health conditions. Doctors say even short periods in 49‑50°C heat can cause dehydration and heart strain. Staying hydrated, staying indoors, and keeping cool are key ways to protect yourself.
