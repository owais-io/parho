Wes Streeting, Labour’s health secretary, quietly plans to challenge Prime Minister Keir Starmer for the top job. After a quiet period, he used a speech at the Institute for Government to call out the party’s slow pace and say the government must change to deliver better public services. He didn’t name Starmer, but the move was a clear signal. Meanwhile, housing minister Matthew Pennycook stayed silent on a controversial Chinese embassy plan, drawing criticism.
