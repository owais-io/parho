Paulo Antonio Paranaguá’s new book, *History of Latin America in 100 Photographs*, uses pictures to tell a fuller story of the continent. It shows more than wars and dictatorships – it highlights art, culture, solidarity and everyday life. The author, a journalist who survived many regimes, pulls images from indigenous, colonial, slavery and Caribbean scenes, focusing on women, artists and ordinary people. The book links past violence to today’s far‑right movements, showing that Latin America is still fragile but vibrant.
