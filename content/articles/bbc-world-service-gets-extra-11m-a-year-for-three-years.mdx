The UK government has agreed to give the BBC World Service an extra £11 million a year for the next three years, an 8% bump over last year. The deal stops uncertainty over the broadcaster’s funding, but insiders say the rise won’t beat inflation, leaving budgets basically flat. The move aims to strengthen trusted journalism worldwide as disinformation grows, especially in places like Iran and Russia.
