Rachel Reeves, the UK Labour chancellor, used her recent speech to explain why the country needs stronger government help and better ties with the EU. She calls for a more active state, less regulation, and letting regions control more taxes. Reeves also argued the UK should re‑join the EU single market, but her plan faces limits set by “red lines” that keep the country from full integration. Her ideas need clearer political backing to work.
