Carrick, who once led Middlesbrough with a patient, possession‑based style, was fired because he refused to change tactics. His rigid approach cost the club after a key striker was sold and opponents used quick counter‑attacks. Now, as Manchester United’s interim coach, he’s praised for his integrity and calm leadership, but fans and critics say he needs to adapt his game plans to succeed.
