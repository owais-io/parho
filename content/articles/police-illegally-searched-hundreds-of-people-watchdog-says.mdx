Victoria police used special powers to search people for weapons in 23 events over the past eight years, but many of those searches broke the law. An audit and a Guardian investigation found 33 people were charged, but possibly hundreds more were searched without proper approval. Police say the problems were mainly paperwork errors and will inform those affected. The incident has raised worries about fairness, especially for communities of colour.
