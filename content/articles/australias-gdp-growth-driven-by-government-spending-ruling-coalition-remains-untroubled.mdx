Australia’s economy grew 2.6% last year, largely thanks to a rise in inventories, government spending and household purchases. State and local budgets, defence, and lower electricity bills helped push growth, while Black Friday sales and the Ashes tour added a boost. Still, growth is modest compared to past decades, so the ruling coalition isn’t alarmed and sees no immediate need for big policy changes.
