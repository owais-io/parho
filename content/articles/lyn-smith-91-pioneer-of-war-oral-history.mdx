Lyn Smith, who died at 91, was a celebrated oral historian who spent more than five decades recording the stories of people who lived through wars. Despite leaving school at 16, she earned degrees in the 1970s and worked for the Imperial War Museum, BBC and the Open University. She wrote seven books and collected thousands of hours of testimony from veterans, refugees and others. A lifelong traveler, she loved music, gardening and spending time with her family.
