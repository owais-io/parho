The UK’s financial watchdog, the FCA, has hired Palantir, a U.S. AI firm, to sift through its private data for three months to spot fraud and money‑laundering. The company will pay over £30,000 a week to analyse emails, calls and social media from 42,000 firms. The deal raises big privacy worries – Palantir is a ‘processor’ and must destroy data after use, but critics still worry about how the tech could be used.
