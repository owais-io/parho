Researchers discovered that during WWII, Nazi officials hired paper restorers and bookbinders to clean and repair old church and civil records. By making these documents readable, they could spot people of Jewish ancestry and create lists of those to target. The work was widespread across Europe, using harmful cleaning methods, and shows how everyday jobs were turned into tools of genocide.
