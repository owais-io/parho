Former UKIP leader Nigel Farage has attracted a wave of Conservative MPs who are leaving the party for his Reform UK. Simon Hart, a former chief whip, says defections are often driven by personal ambition, not principle, and can destabilise governments. He recalls past Tory switchers like Shaun Woodward and Dan Poulter, who left for Labour or Reform but later disappeared. Hart warns that each defector could cause political chaos and that loyalty should not be traded for promises.
