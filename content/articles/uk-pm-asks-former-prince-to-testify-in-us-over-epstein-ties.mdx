Prime Minister Keir Starmer wants Andrew Mountbatten‑Windsor, a former royal who lost his titles for his friendship with Jeffrey Epstein, to give evidence to a U.S. congressional committee. New U.S. documents show the prince invited Epstein to Buckingham Palace in 2010 and kept emails that raise questions about his knowledge of Epstein’s crimes. Starmer says the victims must come first and wants the former prince to share any information he has.
