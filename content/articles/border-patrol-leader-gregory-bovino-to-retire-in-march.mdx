Gregory Bovino, a former U.S. Border Patrol commander, announced he'll retire in March after leading a controversial Minnesota enforcement operation. He was moved back to California following two fatal shootings of U.S. citizens in January, which sparked investigations. Bovino has faced criticism for harsh comments and questionable remarks about a prosecutor’s faith. Despite his long career, the incidents and his public statements led to his demotion and now his retirement.
