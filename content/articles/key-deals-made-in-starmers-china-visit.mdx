Prime Minister Keir Starmer's trip to Beijing landed several important agreements. Britain now enjoys visa‑free entry for 30‑day visits, easing travel for holidaymakers and business people. The two sides agreed to study a services‑trade deal, which could give UK companies clearer rules when working in China. They also pledged to share intelligence on organised crime, helping curb illegal boat crossings and drug smuggling. Further memoranda will improve cooperation on standards, health, education, and more.
