Scientists have discovered that the way we walk can give clues about our emotions. In a study, researchers made people walk while thinking about angry, happy, fearful or sad memories. Others could guess the feelings from the videos, especially by noticing how much the arms and legs swing. Bigger swings were linked to anger, while smaller ones hinted at sadness or fear. This could help in future tech like CCTV or wearable mood trackers.
