Gregory Bovino, a former U.S. border patrol boss, was called a Confederate general in an email he got in 2018. The email and other pictures linked him to a white‑supremacy image. Two border agents sued, saying Bovino’s moves hurt Black and Latino workers. Bovino was later demoted, and a senior officer was reprimanded. The case shows ongoing problems in the agency’s hiring and conduct.
