Iran has been striking oil refineries and other infrastructure in Gulf countries, sparking outrage. The Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) – especially the UAE – is now looking at a military response rather than staying neutral. Talks have highlighted a shift from diplomatic talks to a possible ‘self‑defence’ stance, with some Gulf states warning of retaliation. The attacks have also hurt US‑Gulf ties and cost the UAE about $2 billion in missile interceptions.
