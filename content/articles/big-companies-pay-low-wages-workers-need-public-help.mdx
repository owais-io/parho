Eight hundred thousand workers at 20 of the biggest U.S. firms earn so little that most need Medicaid or SNAP. The study found median wages are below the amount needed for a family of three to qualify for these programs. CEOs earn tens of millions while workers earn under $30,000. The companies also spent billions on stock buybacks instead of higher pay.
