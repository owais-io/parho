A new study shows about one in five people in UK emergency rooms are being treated in hallways, offices, and cupboards – a practice called corridor care. Doctors say this is widespread, violates national rules, and puts patients at risk. The research looked at 165 emergency departments and found 17.7% of patients were in these unsafe spaces. Health officials are promising to end it by the next election.
