In the trial that convicted nurse Lucy Letby of murdering babies, a doctor named Prof Peter Hindmarsh testified that the infants were poisoned with insulin. While he was speaking, the medical regulator (GMC) was already probing him for possible patient harm. The jury never learned about the investigation. Hindmarsh later withdrew from the GMC, so no formal finding was made. Many experts now doubt his testimony and the convictions.
