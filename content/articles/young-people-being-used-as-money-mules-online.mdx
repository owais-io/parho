Scammers on social media are recruiting teens and young adults to act as “money mules,” moving stolen money through their bank accounts for a fee. Authorities say the problem has jumped 22% this year, with most people aged 22‑29. Those who help unknowingly can face up to 14 years in prison and lose access to banks. Experts warn parents, schools and banks need to spot signs like sudden cash gifts or secret phone use.
