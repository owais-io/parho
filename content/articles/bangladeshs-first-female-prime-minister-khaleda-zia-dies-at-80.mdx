Khaleda Zia, Bangladesh’s first female prime minister, passed away at 80 after a life of politics marked by a fierce rivalry with Sheikh Hasina. She led the BNP from 1991 to 1996, faced corruption trials, and spent years under house arrest. She was freed in 2024 after a mass uprising and hoped to run again in 2026. Her son returned to Bangladesh to lead the BNP in the upcoming election.
