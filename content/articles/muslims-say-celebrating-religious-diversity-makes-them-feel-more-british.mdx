At the huge Baitul Futuh mosque in south London, thousands celebrated the end of Ramadan. Politicians, however, have spoken sharply about Muslim prayers, calling them “un‑British” and “intimidating.” The comments have hurt many Muslims who feel singled out and scared of hate. The community says seeing other faiths peacefully practiced actually strengthens their British identity. They urge leaders to attend such events to see how calm and normal they are.
