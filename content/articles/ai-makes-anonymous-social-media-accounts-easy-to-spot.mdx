New research shows AI can match anonymous social‑media profiles with real identities by pulling clues from posts. The study tested the tech on fake and real accounts and found it could link a user’s anonymous handle to a known person with high confidence. This makes privacy attacks cheaper and faster, raising fears that governments or hackers could use AI to track dissidents or launch targeted scams. Researchers urge social‑media sites and users to limit data that’s freely shared online.
