Citizens across the U.S. are carrying passports because immigration raids make them feel unsafe. In places like Minneapolis, Louisiana, and Los Angeles, people who look or speak like immigrants—even though they are U.S. citizens—fear being stopped or detained by ICE agents. They keep passports in pockets or cars to prove identity quickly, but anxiety remains that they could be questioned or taken to a processing center. The trend shows how enforcement has turned everyday life into a source of fear.
