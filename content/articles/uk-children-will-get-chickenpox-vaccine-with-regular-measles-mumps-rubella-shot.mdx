From this Friday, babies in the UK will receive a single vaccine at 12 and 18 months that covers measles, mumps, rubella and chickenpox. The NHS will give the first dose for free, the same as other countries like the US and Australia. The new jab is expected to cut hospital visits and save the NHS and families money, while also keeping children from missing school. The change follows expert advice and existing shingles protection for older people.
