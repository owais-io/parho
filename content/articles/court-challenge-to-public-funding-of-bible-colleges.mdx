England’s university regulator, the Office for Students, is being sued by the National Secular Society because it has not checked that 12 theology colleges meet rules about academic freedom. The colleges have received more than £80 million from student loans and £1 million from the regulator. The Society says the schools’ faith‑based rules limit free speech and that they should not be registered. A court review will look at what the regulator has done and the colleges’ internal policies.
