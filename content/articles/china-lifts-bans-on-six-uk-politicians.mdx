China has removed sanctions from six British MPs and peers after Prime Minister Keir Starmer visited Beijing. The bans, first announced in 2021, targeted five Conservative MPs and two members of the House of Lords who had been active in the Inter‑Parliamentary Alliance on China or had raised concerns about Chinese human rights. The lifted bans include Tom Tugendhat, Iain Duncan Smith, Nus Ghani, Neil O’Brien, Tim Loughton, and Helena Kennedy. It is unclear whether all of them remain sanctioned.
