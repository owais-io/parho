Farage faces a fresh probe after a whistle‑blower claims Reform UK spent £9,000 more on leaflets, banners and a bar refurbishment than the £20,660 legal limit in the Clacton election. The Electoral Commission and Essex police are reviewing the claims, while Labour’s Anna Turley has called on Farage to explain the extra spending. Reform denies breaking the law, calling the whistle‑blower a disgruntled former councillor.
