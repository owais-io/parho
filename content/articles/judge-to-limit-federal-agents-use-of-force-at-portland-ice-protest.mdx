A federal judge is reviewing whether ICE agents can use tear gas, pepper balls, or rubber bullets at protests. The decision comes after agents fired these weapons at a crowd in Portland, including children and families, during a demonstration over the weekend. The case was filed by the ACLU to curb what protesters say is “retaliatory violence.” The judge will decide on a temporary restraining order tomorrow.
