A used‑car tycoon, Peter Waddell, is fighting a court case after being removed as chief executive of his £300m dealership, Big Motoring World. He says rivals and investors conspired to oust him, citing alleged racist and sexist remarks. Waddell, who still owns most of the company, denies the claims. The dispute centers on a private‑equity firm’s stake and a company investigation into his conduct.
