Odie, the family’s five‑year‑old cavoodle, shows a clear preference for certain people. He loves his mum the most because she gave him early care, but now he spends a lot of time with his older brother, who has a bigger bed and a more relaxed schedule. The narrator jokes about how Odie’s affection feels like a mystery, asking why the dog chooses who to snuggle with and how that changes over time.
