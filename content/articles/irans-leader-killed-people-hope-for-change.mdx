The death of Iran’s supreme leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, in an Israeli missile strike has sparked talk of a big change, like the fall of the Berlin Wall. Khamenei ruled for 37 years and decided everything from foreign policy to dress codes. With no clear successor, the powerful IRGC and political factions scramble to keep control, while protests and fear are fading, leaving an uncertain future.
