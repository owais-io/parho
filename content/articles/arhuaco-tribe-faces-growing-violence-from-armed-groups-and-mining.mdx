Arhuaco, an indigenous group in Colombia's Sierra Nevada, are battling violence from paramilitaries, guerrillas, and drug traffickers who want to turn the mountains into drug routes. They are also fighting forced child recruitment, sexual abuse, and attacks on their sacred sites. Mining companies and hydro projects threaten their land, while the UN warns the community could be pushed to extinction. The Arhuaco are demanding protection and a halt to the violence and exploitation.
