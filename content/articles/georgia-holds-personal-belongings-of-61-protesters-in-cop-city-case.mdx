Georgia’s federal court case tied to protests against a police training center has left 61 people in court trouble. The state keeps hundreds of phones, laptops, diaries and other personal things as evidence. A judge recently failed to write down a decision that would have ended the case, so the state still holds the items. The case shows how the government can hold onto private belongings when it is trying to punish protestors.
