The UK government has slapped a 50% tariff on imported steel and taken over the last blast furnaces, signalling the economic model is broken. Labour, the Greens and some Liberals say the issue is not just money but how the state is run. They call for more public investment, better budgeting tools, lower rents and energy costs, and policies that boost production. The debate points to a need for a new plan to grow jobs and cut living costs.
