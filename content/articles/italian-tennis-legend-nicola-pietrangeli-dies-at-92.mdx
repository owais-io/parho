Nicola Pietrangeli, Italy’s tennis icon, died at 92. He played more Davis Cup ties than any player, winning 120 of 164 matches over 18 years. Pietrangeli won the French Open twice, reached the Wimbledon semi‑final in 1960, and as captain helped Italy win the Davis Cup in 1976. Known for his elegant backhand and clay‑court skill, he also exposed corruption in amateur tennis. A beloved figure, courts and a stadium bear his name.
