During a fresh wave of fighting in southern Lebanon, many Lebanese Australians are worried that relatives they cannot see are being forced to flee villages and rush into Beirut. Families have had to travel over 25 hours, live in shared apartments, and face a lack of housing. The war has added stress, fear and guilt to those living in Australia, who feel ignored by their government and left with no help.
