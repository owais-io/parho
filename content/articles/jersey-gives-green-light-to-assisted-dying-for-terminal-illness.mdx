Jersey’s parliament voted 32‑to‑16 to legalise assisted dying for adults who are terminally ill and have lived on the island for at least a year. The bill now needs royal assent before it can become law, and the service will take 18‑24 months to set up. The vote follows similar moves in the UK, and about 61% of Jersey residents support the change, saying it offers compassion and choice at the end of life.
