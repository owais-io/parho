Some politicians want to limit student loans to people who have A‑levels, but many people succeed at university without those exams. The letter argues that colleges and universities decide who can finish a degree, not the government. If a student is admitted and can succeed, they should keep loan support. Cutting loans would shut doors for capable, non‑traditional learners.
