The new Women’s Champions League format is starting its playoffs this week. Eight teams, including Arsenal and Manchester United, are fighting to join the four top clubs that already qualified. The change from a group stage has made games tighter, with more one‑goal matches and more goals from both teams. Fans are watching 13.5 million people in total. The playoffs also give lower‑ranked teams a better chance to progress and let WSL clubs show how deep their squads really are.
