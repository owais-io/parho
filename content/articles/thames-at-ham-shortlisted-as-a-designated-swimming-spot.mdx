The River Thames in south‑west London, specifically the Ham stretch, has been chosen as one of 13 places in the UK that could become officially safe swimming spots. Local groups proved many people swim there all year. The move would push companies to improve water quality. Other rivers and coast areas are also being considered. A public consultation will let people give feedback, but water companies face challenges meeting the stricter standards.
