Tony Edwards, who died at 77, spent 50 years protecting Yorkshire’s rivers and wetlands. He worked for the Yorkshire Water Authority, the Environment Agency, and the Humber Nature Partnership, pushing for nature‑based flood defenses and better water quality. He taught at the University of Hull, wrote about eco‑engineering, and helped graduates find jobs. A lover of walks, birds and opera, he left a lasting impact on people and the land he served.
