Christine McCartney, a respected microbiologist, died at 79. She led the Health Protection Agency’s Regional Microbiology Network, linking lab science with public health. A pioneer of whole‑gene sequencing, she helped investigate the 2006 Litvinenko poisoning and advised the government’s crisis committee. Awarded an OBE in 2007, she worked long after retirement, sharing her expertise with Public Health England. She was survived by her husband Bill.
