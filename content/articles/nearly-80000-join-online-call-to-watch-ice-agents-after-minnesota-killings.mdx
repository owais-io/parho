After two Minnesota residents were killed by ICE agents, a video call drew almost 80,000 people who learned how to safely watch and record the agency’s activities. Participants from across the U.S. share tips, create safety plans, and organize local observer groups that can deter raids. Despite threats of legal action and accusations of “terrorism,” the movement has grown, showing community members are willing to document ICE work and hold officials accountable.
