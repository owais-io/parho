The UK government has removed the cap on how many days crown courts can sit, hoping to clear a backlog of over 80,000 criminal cases. Justice Secretary David Lammy said the change will give courts more time to hear cases next year, along with extra £2.8 bn in funding and a capital investment to fix building problems. Lawyers welcomed the move but said more reforms, like remote hearings, are needed.
