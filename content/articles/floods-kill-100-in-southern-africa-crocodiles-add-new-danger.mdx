Heavy rains across southern Africa have caused widespread flooding that has already killed more than 100 people and displaced almost 400,000. The floods also have pushed crocodiles into new waters, leading to several more deaths. In camps holding close to 100,000 refugees, experts warn of cholera and other water‑borne illnesses. Farmers lost 60,000 hectares of land and 58,000 animals, and road and bridge repairs could cost up to 700 million rand.
