A new study shows Australian teens and young adults (16‑25) are thinking about suicide, planning it, hurting themselves and trying to die more often than older age groups. The problem is happening at younger ages – even kids as young as six are calling for help. The rise is linked to online exposure, family violence, climate worries, and Covid stress. Experts say schools and parents need to act early and create safe spaces online and offline.
