Reform UK’s new Treasury spokesperson, Robert Jenrick, said the party will bring back the two‑child benefit limit and tighten other welfare rules. He plans to cut claims for mild mental health conditions, restrict benefits to British citizens, and curb Motability abuse. Jenrick also defended the Bank of England’s independence while promising fiscal discipline. The move was criticised by Labour and called a step toward child poverty, while the Reform leader explained the policy shift after a failed pro‑family pitch.
