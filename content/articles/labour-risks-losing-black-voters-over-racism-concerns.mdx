David Weaver, chair of the black‑rights group Operation Black Vote, says Labour is in serious trouble with Black voters because the party’s actions look like they are normalising racism. He argues that plans to cut jury trials and a slow pace on an equality bill show a lack of urgency. Black voters, who have historically backed Labour, feel the party is not defending their interests. Weaver urges the party to focus on local grassroots work to regain trust.
