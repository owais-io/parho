The war has left a maternity hospital close to the front line, but Ukrainian mothers are still giving birth. Births have dropped sharply—from 2,300 in 2020 to just under 1,000 this year—while 6 million people have fled and 4 million live in refugee camps. The government has raised maternity payments and added new subsidies for pregnant women, but many families feel unsafe and uncertain about the future. Despite the danger, parents say staying in Ukraine and raising kids is a proud act.
