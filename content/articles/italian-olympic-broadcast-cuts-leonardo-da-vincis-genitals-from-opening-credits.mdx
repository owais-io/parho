Rai, Italy’s state TV, sparked a backlash when its Winter Olympics opening credits showed Leonardo da Vinci’s Vitruvian Man without genitals. Critics claim the broadcaster censored the image and want an explanation. Rai says the footage was produced by Olympic Broadcasting Services, not altered by the network. The row comes after other Olympic controversies, including a “Penisgate” scandal involving athletes. Parliament and journalists are demanding answers.
