Two juries, one in California and one in New Mexico, just ruled that Meta and YouTube are responsible for hurting young people. The New Mexico case ordered Meta to pay $375 million for helping child sexual exploitation, while the California case gave YouTube and Meta a $6 million verdict for building addictive products for teens. These decisions signal a growing push to hold social‑media giants accountable and may force them to change their platforms.
