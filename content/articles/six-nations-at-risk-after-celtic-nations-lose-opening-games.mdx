After losing their first matches, Ireland, Scotland and Wales are putting the Six Nations in danger. France beat Ireland in Paris, Italy upset Scotland in Rome, and Wales had a poor start in London. The defeats show that the Celtic teams may need to change quickly, or the tournament could become a two‑tier affair. England and France are looking strong, while the Celtic nations are under pressure to improve.
