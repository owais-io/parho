Carolin Würfel talks about a Turkish friend who noticed she never answers the simple question “How are you?” She explains that in Germany people feel pressure to answer honestly, not just say “fine.” The question forces them to talk about politics, feelings, and even guilt from history. Older Germans keep their distance, but younger people are learning to speak openly. The piece shows why the question feels heavy.
