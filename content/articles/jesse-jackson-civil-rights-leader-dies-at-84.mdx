Jesse Jackson, the pioneering civil rights activist who ran for U.S. president twice and helped open the door for Barack Obama, has died at 84. He grew up in segregated South Carolina, became a Baptist minister, led the SCLC’s Operation Breadbasket, and founded the Rainbow Coalition. Though praised for his activism, he faced criticism over exaggerations and political clashes. Later in life he tackled Parkinson’s disease and remained a public speaker until his death.
