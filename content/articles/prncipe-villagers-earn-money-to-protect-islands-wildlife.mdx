In a West African island, villagers are being paid quarterly for protecting the unique rainforest. The Faya Foundation, backed by billionaire Mark Shuttleworth, pays up to €816 to anyone who follows an environmental code. So far 3,000 residents have signed up, covering over 60% of adults. The money helps improve homes, schools, and revives the old cacao business, giving the community a new way to earn while keeping the island’s rare species safe.
