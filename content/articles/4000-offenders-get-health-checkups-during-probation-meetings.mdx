A new pilot will give up to 4,000 offenders in England a health check during their probation meetings. NHS nurses and doctors will sit in on appointments to spot smoking, drug use, mental health problems and help patients register with a GP or book cancer tests. The scheme, run in Cambridge, Middlesbrough, Ilfracombe and Hammersmith, aims to stop untreated health issues that can lead to re‑offending and ease pressure on hospitals.
