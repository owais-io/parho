More than 500,000 Beirut residents were forced to flee when Israel began airstrikes on the city’s southern suburbs. The bombing, part of a new round of fighting with Hezbollah, turned neighborhoods into rubble and left many without shelters. Residents were told to head north, but many struggled to leave with just a few belongings. The violence also split communities, with some Christian areas welcoming refugees while others rejected them. At least 217 people were killed.
