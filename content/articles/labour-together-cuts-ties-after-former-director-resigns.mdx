Labour Together, a think‑tank that helped Keir Starmer rise, is taking a hard step away from a messy past after its ex‑director, Josh Simons, quit his minister role. Simons hired a firm that sent out a report linking journalists to a “pro‑Kremlin” network, a claim that was false and caused a scandal. The board has distanced itself, set up new oversight, and is under scrutiny, with MPs calling for a deeper probe. Starmer has promised to cut ties and return the think‑tank’s donations.
