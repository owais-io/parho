MPs, experts and activists are urging King Charles to apologise for Britain’s involvement in the trans‑Atlantic slave trade. Research shows the crown and Royal Navy benefited from and protected the slave trade for centuries. While the king has said “personal sorrow”, no formal apology has been issued. Groups such as the Runnymede Trust and UN experts say an apology should be paired with concrete actions to address slavery’s lasting legacy.
