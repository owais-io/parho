Neil Harvey, 97 and the world’s oldest living Test cricketer, sits at home watching games and reflecting on his time with Bradman’s Invincibles. He still loves cricket but is disappointed by modern batting tactics, calling England’s “Bazball” too reckless. Harvey worries about weak international teams and believes strong batting still wins fair games. He praises current stars like Kane Williamson and Mitchell Starc, shares memories of his 1950s career, and enjoys horse racing now.
