Art Manteris, who ran Caesars Palace’s sportsbook for 43 years, shares his inside view of the sports‑betting world in his new book. He talks about famous wagers—like betting on a Chicago Bears player in the first Super Bowl—high‑rollers, celebrities, and how the industry has shifted from mob‑connected casinos to legal, app‑based betting across the U.S. He warns about the rise of youth gambling and urges a clear split between sports and gambling.
