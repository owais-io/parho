Arsenal are close to winning the Premier League, but the team’s heavy use of data and constant online scrutiny is putting pressure on players. Coach Mikel Arteta relies on stats and AI, which can make the squad feel over‑monitored and limit spontaneous play. The article argues that being “terminally online” – always watched and analyzed – can hurt performance, leading to a “bottling” crisis. It shows how modern football mirrors a surveillance‑saturated society.
