Tens of thousands marched in Tehran for a pro‑government rally while protests spread across the country. State TV showed crowds cheering at Enqelab Square, where lawmakers warned of a “four‑front war” against the U.S. and Israel. At the same time, police and soldiers used force, reports say 600‑plus people died, and the internet was shut down. Critics say the crackdown is brutal, while Iran’s leaders claim it keeps order.
