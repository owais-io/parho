State and local leaders in Minnesota say the FBI’s investigation into Renee Good’s death is unfair. The Bureau of Criminal Apprehension (BCA) was told to step back, and the FBI now controls all evidence, leaving BCA unable to review key items like the car and the gun. Minneapolis mayor and prosecutors want a separate state probe, while federal officials claim the shooting was justified. Leaders urge the public to keep evidence safe and say the state can still investigate.
