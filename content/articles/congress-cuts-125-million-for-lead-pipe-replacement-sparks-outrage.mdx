U.S. Congress recently cut $125 million from the budget that was meant to replace lead water pipes in homes and businesses. The decision upset lawmakers and health advocates who say lead‑contaminated water harms children’s brains and adults’ hearts. The money was part of a $15 billion plan that could replace millions of unsafe pipes over five years. Critics say the cut hurts clean‑water efforts and sends a bad message about public‑health priorities.
