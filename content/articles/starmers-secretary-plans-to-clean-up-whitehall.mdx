Darren Jones, the government’s chief secretary, says he will rid Whitehall of inefficiency by setting up small, fast‑moving taskforces, putting clear performance targets on senior civil servants, and letting ministers fire those who fail to deliver. He will keep bonuses only for top performers and encourage risk‑taking. The plan also aims to cut red tape and improve services like childcare and driving tests, with support from civil‑service unions.
