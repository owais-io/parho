Canadian Prime Minister Justin Carney travelled to Beijing and secured a “preliminary” trade deal that eases tariffs on Canadian goods and lets Chinese electric cars enter Canada. The deal is part of Canada’s push to cut its heavy reliance on U.S. exports, a move welcomed by Beijing but criticized by some Canadian politicians who fear Chinese market dominance. The talks signal a fresh shift in Canada‑China relations amid a changing global order.
