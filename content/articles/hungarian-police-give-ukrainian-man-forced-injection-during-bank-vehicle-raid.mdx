A raid in Hungary on March 5 stopped a convoy carrying gold and cash from a Ukrainian bank. Seven Ukrainian workers were arrested, blind‑folded and handcuffed for over 24 hours. One man, a former security officer, was given a forced injection that caused a health crisis. The incident raises questions about Hungary’s motives and the treatment of the detainees, as political tensions between Budapest and Kyiv rise.
