The UK government’s homelessness minister, Alison McGovern, has pledged to stop using bed‑and‑breakfasts as temporary homes. She said B&Bs would be banned by the end of parliament even though homelessness figures have risen 8% in the last year. The new three‑year plan includes millions of pounds for better shelters, a law to keep people from ending up on the streets after prison or hospital, and a push for more houses and income support.
