Gavin Poynter, who died at 76, studied how east London changed after the dock industries collapsed. He joined the University of East London in 1988 and became head of social sciences in 2000. As chair of the London East Research Institute, he helped evaluate the 2012 Olympics. A longtime educator and left‑wing activist, he wrote books like *Building a Radical University* and critiqued neoliberalism. He loved swimming and wine, and left behind two children, four grandchildren, and a brother.
