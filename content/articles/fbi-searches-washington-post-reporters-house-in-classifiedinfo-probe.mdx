The FBI entered the Virginia home of Washington Post reporter Hannah Natanson early Wednesday, searching her house and taking her phone, laptop and other devices. The raid was part of an investigation into a government contractor who allegedly kept classified material. Natanson was not the focus of the probe, and press‑freedom groups said the search was over‑the‑top and threatens journalists’ safety. The case has raised questions about how the Trump‑era administration treats the press.
