Heavy rains this weekend caused the worst floods Hawaii has seen in over two decades. Thousands on Oahu’s North Shore had to evacuate, and more people left Maui for safety. The storms lifted houses, cars and farms, leaving mud everywhere and threatening a 120‑year‑old dam. About 200 people were rescued, and no deaths were reported. Damage could cost over $1 million, and a boil‑water notice remains in place as cleanup continues.
