Brazil’s Indigenous minister, Sonia Guajajara, said countries must treat Indigenous land boundaries as a climate‑action tool. She spoke at COP30, where Indigenous people briefly blocked the conference to protest mining plans that threaten forests and rivers. Guajajara wants the new Tropical Forest Forever Fund to protect Brazil’s Amazon and hopes other nations, especially the UK and China, will invest. The minister urges lawmakers to stop mining on Indigenous lands.
