Bondi beach has been Sydney’s Jewish community center for decades, with synagogues, schools, sports clubs and cafés that welcomed Holocaust survivors and later Russian refugees. It was a place where people met, prayed, and celebrated holidays. But after terrorists targeted the Chabad centre there, killing two rabbis, Bondi has turned into a place of mourning and remembrance. The community now seeks support and healing.
