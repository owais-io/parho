Labour's chancellor Rachel Reeves told a London event that the party can win voters by pushing for closer ties with the EU. She said the biggest economic prize is better trade with Europe, which makes up half of UK trade. Reeves highlighted recent moves, like re‑joining the Erasmus student exchange, and plans to negotiate sector‑by‑sector deals. She rejected a customs union but said more cooperation is needed for growth and security.
