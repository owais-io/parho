Til, a 1,000‑year‑old village in Nepal’s Limi valley, was hit by a sudden flood on 15 May, forcing 21 families to move to a nearby field and then back into damaged homes. With no help, residents decided to leave the village by year’s end. The area is prone to flash floods that destroy bridges and drain permafrost lakes, and young people are already moving away. The community is seeking government aid and a new home in Kathmandu.
