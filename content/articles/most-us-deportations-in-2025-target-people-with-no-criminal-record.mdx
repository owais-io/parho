A Guardian study shows that in 2025, 77% of people first sent to deportation courts had no criminal convictions. The data, taken from nearly 140,000 government forms, reveals that few were charged with violent crimes; most had traffic or immigration offenses. The findings contradict the Trump administration’s claim that they only remove the “worst of the worst.”
