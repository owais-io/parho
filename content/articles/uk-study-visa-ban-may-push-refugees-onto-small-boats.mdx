UK has banned student visas from Sudan, Afghanistan, Myanmar and Cameroon, saying they are abused for asylum claims. Critics say the ban will push people to risk dangerous small‑boat crossings across the Channel. The Home Secretary says the ban stops abuse but is an “unprecedented” move. The Home Office notes only a few hundred people from those countries have used study routes for asylum, but activists warn that no safe alternative exists, so smugglers will be used.
