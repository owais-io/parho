Victim‑advocacy groups have written to Justice Secretary David Lammy to stop plans that would cut jury trials in England and Wales. They say the changes would make it harder for abused women and girls to get fair hearings, especially because lone judges can be biased and many defendants are already wrongly criminalised. The bill, aimed at clearing an 80,000‑case backlog, passed but faces strong opposition.
