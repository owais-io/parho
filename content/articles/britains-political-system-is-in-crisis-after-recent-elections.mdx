After the recent Gorton and Denton election, many voters feel Britain’s political system no longer works for them. The article says the problem is deeper than a party’s leadership – it’s a legitimacy crisis. The old market‑driven model built after the 1980s still dominates, but people see it as unfair and unresponsive. To fix this, the writer argues for big changes: more local control, stricter limits on money in politics, a new voting system, and overall more democratic decision‑making.
