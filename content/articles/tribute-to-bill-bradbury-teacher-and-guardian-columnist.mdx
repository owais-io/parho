73‑year‑old former headteacher Bill Bradbury died from cancer. He wrote many left‑wing letters to The Guardian, known for his wit. Over 40 years he taught special‑needs pupils, improving two schools: Samuel Laycock in Tameside and Rumworth in Bolton, earning an Ofsted “outstanding” rating. In 2007 he became a national leader in education and helped other schools. Born in Bolton, he spent his life in education and music. He is survived by wife June, children Will and Lucy, and three grandchildren.
