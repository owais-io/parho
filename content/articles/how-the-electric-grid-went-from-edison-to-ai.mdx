Electricity started with a battle between Thomas Edison’s DC system and George Westinghouse and Tesla’s AC system. AC won because it can be sent over long distances at high voltage, so it became the backbone of the grid. Over time the grid grew, added coal, then nuclear and wind, and now is shifting back to small, local solar panels that feed power back into the network. Smart AI and batteries help manage this mix.
