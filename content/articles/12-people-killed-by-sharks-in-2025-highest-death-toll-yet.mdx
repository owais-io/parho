A new report from the International Shark Attack File shows a jump in unprovoked shark attacks worldwide in 2025, with 65 incidents and 12 deaths – almost double last year's figures. Most fatalities happened in Australia, where 21 attacks were recorded, and the US had 25 attacks but only one death. The report links the rise to more great white sharks at popular surf spots and notes that the overall risk remains low.
