During the holiday quarter, Sainsbury’s supermarkets grew 3.4% but Argos sales fell 1%, mainly in the final six weeks. The company says weak confidence, fierce online rivals and heavy discounts hurt the catalogue shop. CEO Simon Roberts wants earlier rules to curb cheap imports and said shoppers are hunting value. Despite lower merchandise sales, grocery and online sales rose, and the group plans to return £800 m to shareholders.
