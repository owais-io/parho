Shawn Harris, a retired brigadier general who now runs a cattle ranch, is ready to challenge the GOP in Georgia’s 14th district after Representative Marjorie Taylor‑Greene stepped aside. He says he’s a centrist and could win over Republican voters, but the party must choose between another extremist or a more moderate candidate. Harris talks about better Congress oversight, lower food prices, and the state’s farming economy. He’s collecting food for local pantries and wants to help everyday families.
