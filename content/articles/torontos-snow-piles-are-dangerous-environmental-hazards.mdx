Toronto’s massive snow piles – the city’s makeshift mountains – trap road salt, antifreeze, oil and other pollutants. These toxic heaps are stored at several sites, one of which can reach 100‑foot tall. When the snow melts, the chemicals rush into rivers, killing fish and raising salt levels in groundwater. City officials are concerned that the salt used on roads and driveways may exceed safe limits, while climate change is expected to bring more heavy storms and higher salt demand.
