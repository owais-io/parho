UK will increase child benefit for families with three or more children by about £440 a month, starting in April, after ending the two‑child limit that cut payments. The move comes as oil prices rise and inflation hits the poorest households. Economists say the extra cash will lift hundreds of thousands of children out of poverty and help parents meet basic needs. The policy is part of Labour’s social‑support strategy.
