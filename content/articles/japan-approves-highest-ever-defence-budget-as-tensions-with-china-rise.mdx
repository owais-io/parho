Japan’s cabinet has agreed to spend more than ¥9 trillion on defence – the biggest ever – raising the annual budget by 9.4%. The money will buy new missiles, unmanned drones and a new “Shield” surveillance system. The move comes as China complains about Tokyo’s space and military build‑up and after Japan’s prime minister said it could help Taiwan if China attacked. China has warned Japan, and the US is also backing Taiwan.
