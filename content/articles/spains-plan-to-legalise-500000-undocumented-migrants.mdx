Spain’s new law will give legal status to 500,000 people living without papers, helping them work, study and access services. Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez launched the move to show Spain as open and to boost the economy. Opponents say it will overload services and encourage more arrivals. The plan gives migrants one‑year permits that can be renewed. It marks a shift away from the hard‑line stance of some rivals and highlights the economic and human‑rights benefits of migration.
