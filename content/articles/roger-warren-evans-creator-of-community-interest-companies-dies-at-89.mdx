He was a lawyer who helped start community interest companies (CICs) in the UK, with more than 37,000 now. Born in 1935, he had a varied career as a barrister, housebuilder, government adviser, businessman and human‑rights activist. A conversation over wine in the early 2000s led him to create the CIC framework, aimed at keeping community organisations outside strict charity rules. Later, he founded a charity for asylum seekers. He died at 89.
