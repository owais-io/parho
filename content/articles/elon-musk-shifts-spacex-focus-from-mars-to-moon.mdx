Elon Musk has turned his SpaceX plans away from a 20‑year Mars city to a quicker moon project. He wants U.S. astronauts back on the lunar surface before 2028, helping NASA’s Artemis missions and keeping pace with President Trump’s moon‑landing goal. Musk also sees a faster business case and wants to stay ahead of Blue Origin. The shift could boost SpaceX’s contracts and future lunar travel.
