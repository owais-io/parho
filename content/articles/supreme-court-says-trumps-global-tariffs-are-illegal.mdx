The Supreme Court ruled that most of President Trump’s tariffs on foreign goods were unlawful, saying only Congress can set tariff rates. The court rejected the Trump administration’s use of the International Emergency Economic Powers Act as a loophole, calling it a peacetime power that belongs to Congress. The decision hits a key part of Trump’s trade strategy and may force refunds of billions in collected tariffs.
