David Triesman, a former Labour minister and the first independent chair of the Football Association, passed away at home on Friday night at age 82. The Labour Party and former Prime Minister Tony Blair praised his dedication to the party and to fighting racism in football. He also helped expose corruption in FIFA. Colleagues remembered him as kind, loyal and a passionate Spurs supporter. He is survived by his wife and daughter.
