The war in the Middle East has forced Iran to shut down the Strait of Hormuz, the shortcut that lets Gulf countries send about 15 % of the world's oil to the rest of the world. The closure could cost billions and spark further tension. Journalists on the ground note rising anger in the region and discuss possible ways to keep oil moving and avoid a bigger conflict.
