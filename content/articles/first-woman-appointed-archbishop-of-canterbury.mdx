Sarah Mullally, the Church of England’s first female archbishop, was installed in October. A former nurse and NHS chief, she is known for calm, practical leadership. Her main tasks are tackling abuse scandals, rebuilding trust, and handling divisive issues like LGBTQ services and racial justice. Critics worry she may be too cautious, but many see her as a steady hand to guide the church through a period of change.
