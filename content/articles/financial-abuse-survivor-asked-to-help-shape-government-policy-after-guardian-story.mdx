After the Guardian reported on her story, Francesca Onody, who survived a marriage where her husband killed her, destroyed their home and emptied their accounts, has been asked by the government to help shape policies that protect people from economic abuse. The Treasury minister wants to hear her advice and the charity Surviving Economic Abuse will help draft new rules for banks and insurers to stop others from losing homes and money.
