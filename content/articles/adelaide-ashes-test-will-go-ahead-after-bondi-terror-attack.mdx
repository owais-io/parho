Adelaide's third Ashes Test will still be played after the brutal Bondi beach terror attack. The government has added extra security, with armed officers patrolling the Oval and longer entry times. Politicians held a moment of reflection, and a million‑dollar donation was pledged to a local Holocaust museum. The match reminds us how sports can unite people even amid grief and rising antisemitism in Australia.
