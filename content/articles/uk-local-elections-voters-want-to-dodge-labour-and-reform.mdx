In May 2024, Britain’s local and devolved elections are turning into a negative voting spree. Many voters say they will choose whoever can stop Labour or the new Reform UK party, rather than a party they love. Polls show Reform is the most disliked, with 38% of voters saying they’d avoid it, up from 29% last year. The result could hurt Labour and set the stage for a big shift in the next general election.
