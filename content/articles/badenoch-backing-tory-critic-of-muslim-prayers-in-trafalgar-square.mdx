Badenoch backed her shadow justice secretary Nick Timothy after he said public Muslim prayers in Trafalgar Square were an “act of domination” and should be banned. Timothy posted photos of a Ramadan event and called it a challenge to British culture. Labour and the mayor criticised his comments, but Badenoch said the issue is how religion is shown in shared spaces, not freedom of faith. She added that prayers must be inclusive and respectful of British values.
