Oracle’s stock fell 15% on Thursday, wiping out about $80 billion from its market value after the company reported weaker-than‑expected revenue growth and a sharp rise in AI-related spending. Investors were alarmed that Oracle plans to boost capital expenditures by 40%, mainly to build data centres, while its debt has jumped 25% to nearly $100 billion. The news also sparked a sell‑off in Nvidia, adding to fears that the AI hype could be a bubble.
