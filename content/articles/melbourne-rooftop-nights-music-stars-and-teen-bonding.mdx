During hot Melbourne summers, teens from a public‑housing block used the roof as a meeting place. As the sun went down, up to fifty teenagers would climb up with portable radios, blasting pop hits and chatting under a star‑filled sky. The roof became a safe space where they could talk about feelings, family problems and hopes, away from parents' worries. Music and the night sky helped them feel connected and free.
