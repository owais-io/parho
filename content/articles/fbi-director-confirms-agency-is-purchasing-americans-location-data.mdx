During a Senate intelligence hearing, FBI Director Kash Patel admitted that the agency buys commercial location data on Americans. Senator Ron Wyden asked if the FBI still buys this data without warrants and urged Congress to close the loophole. Patel said the purchases are legal under the Electronic Communications Privacy Act and have helped intelligence work. Critics say it violates the Fourth Amendment and want new surveillance‑reform bills.
