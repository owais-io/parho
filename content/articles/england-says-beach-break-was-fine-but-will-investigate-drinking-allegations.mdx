England's team director Rob Key defended a mid‑tour beach break in Noosa, saying it helped players rest. However he said the ECB will investigate reports that players drank excessively after the second Ashes test. Key highlighted the tight schedule and media pressure as reasons for the break and said it wasn't meant to be a party. He also said the break’s impact on the Adelaide test will be reviewed, and the future of coaching staff is up in the air.
