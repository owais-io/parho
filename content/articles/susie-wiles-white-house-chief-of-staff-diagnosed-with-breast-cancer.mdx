Susie Wiles, the first woman to serve as White House chief of staff, was diagnosed with early‑stage breast cancer last week. She says she will keep working at the White House while receiving treatment and doctors expect a strong recovery. President Trump posted a supportive message on Truth Social and said Wiles would stay virtually full‑time. The diagnosis is early, which gives her the best chance, experts say.
