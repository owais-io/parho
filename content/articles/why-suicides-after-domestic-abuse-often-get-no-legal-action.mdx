Researchers found that in the UK more people are taking their own lives after domestic abuse than being killed by partners. Police often label these deaths as suicide without collecting evidence, and charges are frequently dropped. Experts say the system fails to treat them as crimes, leading to under‑reporting and missed prosecutions. A new law could force evidence gathering and make it easier to hold abusers accountable.
