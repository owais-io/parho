Kim Jong‑un’s 13‑year‑old daughter, Kim Ju‑ae, was seen in state media with her parents as they visited North Korea’s Kumsusan mausoleum. It’s her first big public outing, and the government’s portrayal of a united family has sparked speculation that she could be groomed to succeed her father. Analysts note she’s too young for party duties, and other siblings could also be contenders, so the future remains unclear.
