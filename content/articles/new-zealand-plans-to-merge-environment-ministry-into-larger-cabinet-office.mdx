New Zealand's ruling coalition wants to fold its separate environment ministry into a new big ministry that will also handle housing, transport and local government. The move, announced in February, aims to cut bureaucracy and make decision‑making faster. Critics worry the change will weaken environmental protection and accountability, especially as the country faces wildlife loss and water pollution. Opponents say it signals that protecting nature is no longer a priority for the government.
