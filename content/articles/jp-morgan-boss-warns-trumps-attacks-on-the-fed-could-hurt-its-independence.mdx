Jamie Dimon, chief executive of JP Morgan, said that Donald Trump’s criticism of Fed Chair Jerome Powell and a DOJ investigation into a $2.5 bn Fed renovation could threaten the central bank’s independence and raise borrowing costs. He added the bank will focus on serving clients amid political pressure, and that a possible 10 % cap on credit‑card interest could hurt consumers and the economy. JP Morgan’s profits fell 7 % in Q4.
