Viktor Orbán is refusing to approve the EU’s €90 billion loan to Ukraine because the Druzhba oil pipeline that brings Russian fuel to Hungary is still damaged. He says Hungary can’t get the oil it needs until the pipeline is fixed, and until that happens he will not support the loan. EU leaders in Brussels are pressuring him, warning that the delay hurts Ukraine’s war effort and could cost Hungary money. The dispute comes just before Hungary’s national election, adding political pressure.
