In Victoria, two new dairy farms are putting robots into the milking stalls. The first day was a bit chaotic – cows knocked the machines around – but the robots have since learned to milk the 600 and 450 cows on each farm. Robots cut labor time, let farmers monitor milk quality, and give people more free time. The tech is pricey, so only a few farms use it, but it could help solve a shortage of milk‑workers.
