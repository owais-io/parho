South Australia's new law stops direct money to parties and MPs, but allows third‑party groups to raise and spend money if they register. Many lobby groups, like Turning Point and GetUp, signed up before the vote, meaning they can spend hundreds of thousands of dollars after polling day. Experts warn that this could let powerful interests steer campaigns without full transparency, despite the government’s promise of public funding and caps.
