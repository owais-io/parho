Mattel has launched its first autistic Barbie, part of the Fashionistas line. Designed with the help of the Autistic Self Advocacy Network, the doll shows features like a fidget spinner, noise‑cancelling headphones and a communication tablet to reflect how some children on the spectrum feel and act. It also has slightly angled eyes and a loose dress to help with sensory comfort. The move highlights the brand’s push for inclusive toys and lets kids see themselves in Barbie.
