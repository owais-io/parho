In December, Chile’s national women’s football players’ association (ANJUF) became an official union, giving it legal power to negotiate contracts, benefits, and working conditions with clubs and the federation. The move builds on years of campaigning for medical care, anti‑harassment rules, and professional contracts. Leaders hope the new status will inspire other countries and help prepare for the 2027 Women’s World Cup in Brazil.
