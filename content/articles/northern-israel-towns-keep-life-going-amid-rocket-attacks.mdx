In Metula, a small town at Israel’s northern border, residents stay out in cafés and on streets while rockets and drones from Hezbollah and Iran keep coming. Local businesses stay open to give soldiers and civilians a break, and people talk about ending the war once and for all. The attacks have damaged property, hurt the economy and left many residents anxious, but they keep hoping for normal life again.
