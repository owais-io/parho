Contractors at U.S. bases in Kuwait say their shelters are inadequate, they’re getting less pay, and they’ve received almost no safety instructions after Iranian missile attacks. While U.S. soldiers were moved out, civilians stayed and now fear being treated as expendable. They also need company permission to leave, which can lead to legal trouble. They also worry about water shortages. The contractor, V2X Inc, has not responded to requests for comment.
