Heineken said it will remove up to 6,000 jobs worldwide over the next two years, about 7 % of its staff, as beer sales fall sharply in Europe and North America. The cuts will hit brewing and office roles and are part of a cost‑saving push after the CEO resigned in January. Shares rose after the news, and a new chief executive is still needed.
