In South Carolina, measles cases have jumped to 111, most people are unvaccinated. A church and several schools in Spartanburg County are at the center of the spread. About 254 people are in quarantine, with 16 isolated. The state’s vaccine coverage has slipped to about 93‑95%, lower than needed. Nationwide, almost 2,000 cases have been reported, and health officials warn that if the trend continues, the country could lose its measles‑elimination status. Encourage getting the MMR shot.
