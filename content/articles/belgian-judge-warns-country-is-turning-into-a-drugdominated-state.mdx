A senior judge in Antwerp says Belgium is turning into a drug‑controlled state. The country’s big port makes it a main route for cocaine from South America, and gangs are bribing workers, corrupting judges and even kidnapping politicians. Violence, theft and money‑laundering are on the rise. Judges and prosecutors are under threat, with many living in safe houses. The government has promised more money, but courts still lack security equipment and staff.
