After the Bondi terror attack, the Adelaide Writers’ Festival board said it was “culturally sensitive” to drop Palestinian scholar Randa Abdel‑Fattah from its program. Abdel‑Fattah says the move is racist and a form of censorship. The decision sparked protests from other writers and sponsors, who pulled out. The festival is now reviewing its policies and faces pressure to balance safety, sensitivity and free speech and defending open dialogue.
