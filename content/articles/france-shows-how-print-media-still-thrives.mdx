The author, learning French in Strasbourg, discovered a popular literary magazine called Revue21 that offers long, thoughtful stories. He sees how French bookstores, kiosks, and new print titles like Souvenir and Le Cri keep people reading, even when most news is online. Print books and magazines help people avoid screen fatigue, give stories time to grow, and let readers feel a sense of community and status. In France, paper is still alive and growing.
