Mike Tomlin, the Steelers’ head coach for 19 seasons, is leaving the team. President Art Rooney II praised his dedication, noting Tomlin led Pittsburgh to a Super Bowl win, 13 playoff appearances, and eight division titles, while never having a losing season. The decision follows a playoff loss to the Texans and the team’s last postseason win dating back to 2016. Tomlin finished with 193 regular‑season wins, tying him with legendary coach Chuck Noll.
