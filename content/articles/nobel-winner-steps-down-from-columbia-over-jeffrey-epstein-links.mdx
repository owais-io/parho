Dr Richard Axel, a 2004 Nobel laureate, has quit his role as co‑director of Columbia’s Zuckerman Mind Brain Behavior Institute after Epstein files show a nine‑year relationship, including a flight ticket to St Thomas. Axel apologized, said he would focus on his lab, and Columbia said it found no policy breaches. His departure joins a wave of academic resignations triggered by the Epstein scandal.
