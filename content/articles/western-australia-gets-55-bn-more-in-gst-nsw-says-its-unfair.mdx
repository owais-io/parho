Australia’s federal tax system will split $102.5 bn of GST revenue in 2026‑27. A 2018 deal gives Western Australia a guaranteed larger share, so New South Wales will receive less per capita than before, prompting the state premier to call the formula unfair. The Commonwealth Grants Commission says the cut is based on transparent data, but critics argue the extra money favours the richest state and hurts other regions.
