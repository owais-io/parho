Because of the war in the Middle East, shipping lanes through the Strait of Hormuz have shut down. This blocks not only oil and gas but also fertilizers that farmers need. Small‑scale growers in Tanzania, Kenya, and other African countries are losing markets for avocados and tea, and their prices are falling. Higher fuel and fertilizer costs could raise food prices worldwide, putting millions, especially in poorer countries, at risk of hunger.
