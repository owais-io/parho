The U.S. trade chief said that some imported goods could face tariffs of 15% or more, up from the 10% currently in place. The move comes after the Supreme Court struck down President Trump's earlier tariff plan. Trump had already announced a 10% global tariff for 150 days. The new higher rates could affect many countries, and some businesses, like FedEx, are already suing for refunds.
