A patient named Nathan Newby convinced a would‑be bomber to back off when he asked for a cuddle. Newby met Mohammad Farooq at St James’s Hospital in Leeds, where Farooq had a pressure‑cooker bomb aimed at nurses. Newby talked to him, moved him away, and kept him calm. The attack was stopped, Farooq got a 37‑year sentence, and Newby will receive the George Medal for bravery.
