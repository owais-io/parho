England’s Competition and Markets Authority has approved extra price hikes for four of the country’s biggest water companies. Customers served by Anglian, Southern, Wessex and South East Water will see average annual bill increases of 1.6% to 3.6% – an extra 2.8% on top of the 24% already allowed by Ofwat. The decision lets the firms spend an additional £463 million to upgrade pipes and reduce pollution, affecting 14.7 million households.
