Santa Monica once had a bright beach, a lively pier and good schools, but now it’s facing crime, empty storefronts, soaring housing costs and a budget hole. The city’s manager says the situation can’t last and has launched a “bold reset” that includes more police, faster permits, more trees and public art, and even new billboards. Yet restaurants and small businesses still worry about high rent and wages, and many projects have stalled.
