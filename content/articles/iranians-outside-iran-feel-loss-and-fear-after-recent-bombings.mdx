After the US‑Israel bombings of Iran, many Iranians living abroad feel a strange mix of life and horror. Dr Hosnieh Djafari‑Marbini, an Oxford doctor and councilor, says seeing pictures of bombed streets while she works feels like a parallel universe. People in the diaspora have split feelings: some see the strikes as a relief, others fear more violence and the loss of family. The war also threatens civilians, with schools and hospitals hit, and the internet blackout worsening the crisis.
