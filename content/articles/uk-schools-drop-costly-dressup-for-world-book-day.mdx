UK schools are scrapping the costume‑drama part of World Book Day to avoid extra costs for families, experts told MPs. They say spending on outfits can pull focus from encouraging kids to read for fun, a key driver of social mobility. Schools are looking for cheaper alternatives like swapping costumes or using art projects. The change comes as reading for pleasure hits a 20‑year low.
