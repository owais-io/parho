US and Israel think Iran will quickly collapse, but the fighting is turning into a long, guerrilla war that costs more money and time. Iranian attacks hit Gulf oil facilities and block the Strait of Hormuz, pushing crude prices up 50 %. Higher fuel and fertilizer costs raise inflation and could slow the economy, possibly sparking a recession. The war also shows how fragile global supply chains are and highlights the need for more renewable energy and self‑sufficiency.
