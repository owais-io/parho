Swiss prosecutors have opened a case against the two managers of the Le Constellation bar in Crans‑Montana after a fire that killed about 40 people and injured more than 100. The blaze started when sparklers were held too close to a foam‑covered ceiling, igniting the building. The managers face charges of manslaughter by negligence, bodily harm and arson. Investigators will also look at safety rules, fire exits and materials used. The presumption of innocence applies until a verdict is reached.
