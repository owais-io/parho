Record rain in Death Valley has sparked a wildflower bloom that could become a superbloom, a rare event that happens once a decade. Bright yellow, purple and other colors now blanket the valley’s lower plains, while higher elevations will see flowers from April to June. The park’s deputy says the spread isn’t as huge as the 2016 superbloom but still huge compared to normal years. Visitors and Instagram users are already snapping photos of the stunning scene.
