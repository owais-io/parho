Mexico’s police operation killed the feared cartel boss “El Mencho,” sparking a wave of retaliatory attacks. Six of his men died and the shoot‑out left dozens of National Guard soldiers dead. Roadblocks, car fires and school closures spread across western states, forcing tourists and locals to stay indoors. The raid was backed by US intel, but experts warn the power vacuum could lead to more bloodshed as rival gangs vie for control.
