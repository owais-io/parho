Workers at Perth‑based TSA, which runs a $90m contract for Centrelink, say managers are told to hide privacy breaches and fake performance numbers. Employees claim the company pushes staff to avoid sick leave, offers short breaks, and pays barely enough, leading to high stress and many quitting. The allegations highlight how private firms running government services can put workers’ health and customer privacy at risk.
