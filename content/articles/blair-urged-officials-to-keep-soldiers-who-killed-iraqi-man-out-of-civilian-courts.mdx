In 2005, former UK prime minister Tony Blair asked officials to keep a case involving British soldiers who beat an Iraqi man to death out of civilian courts. The soldiers were later tried by the army and one, Donald Payne, was convicted of a war crime and jailed. Documents show Blair wanted the soldiers to be dealt with by the military and not the International Criminal Court. The ICC later dropped its investigation.
