The Premier League says it will protect and support young footballers through its Elite Player Performance Plan (EPPP). Since 2012, clubs and partners must give full‑time education, medical, psychological and safeguarding staff at all academies. They also offer life‑skills courses, career advice and a 3‑year after‑care programme for players who leave. The League funds lower‑tier clubs and works with outside experts to keep players’ mental health and futures on track.
