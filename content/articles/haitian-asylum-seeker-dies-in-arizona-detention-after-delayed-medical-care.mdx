Emmanuel Damas, a Haitian man seeking asylum, died in March after complaining of severe tooth pain that was ignored at an Arizona detention center. He was moved between several hospitals, eventually developing septic shock from pneumonia, but still didn’t receive timely treatment. His death is part of a growing list of 32 ICE custody deaths last year and has prompted calls for investigation. The case highlights serious health‑care failures for detained migrants.
