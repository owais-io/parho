The UK has slapped sanctions on four top commanders of Sudan’s Rapid Support Forces, who are accused of killing civilians in the city of El Fasher. The sanctions include asset freezes and travel bans. Britain also said it will keep using diplomatic pressure on the UAE and the RSF boss, Hemedti, but is not sanctioning them yet. The move follows reports of mass killings, rape and a famine in Darfur.
