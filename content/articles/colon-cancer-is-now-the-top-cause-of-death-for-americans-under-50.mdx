A new study shows colon cancer has become the leading killer for U.S. people under 50. Many young adults ignore symptoms like frequent, painful bowel movements and bright‑red stool, thinking they’re just stress or hemorrhoids. Because they rarely get screened, most are diagnosed with advanced disease. Doctors now urge people with persistent bleeding to see a doctor, and experts suggest stool tests or early colonoscopies for early detection.
