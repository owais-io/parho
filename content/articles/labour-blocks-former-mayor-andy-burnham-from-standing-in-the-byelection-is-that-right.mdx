Manchester mayor Andy Burnham wants to win a seat in Parliament. Labour’s national executive stopped him from standing in the upcoming by‑election, saying it would be expensive and could give his rivals a boost. Some supporters say Burnham’s move is a smart power play, while critics say it shows the party is afraid of challenge. The debate shows a split in how Labour should handle internal competition.
