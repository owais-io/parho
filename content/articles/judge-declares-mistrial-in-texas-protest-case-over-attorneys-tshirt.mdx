A Texas judge declared a mistrial in a federal case against a group of protesters accused of terrorism after a defense lawyer wore a T‑shirt with civil‑rights images. The judge said the shirt could bias jurors and called the trial off, forcing a new jury and a fresh start. The case is part of the Trump administration’s push to charge antifa activists, and the mistrial has drawn criticism from both sides.
