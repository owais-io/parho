Criminals are using AI to hijack mobile, banking, and online shopping accounts. Last year, the UK anti‑fraud body Cifas logged a record 444,000 fraud reports, a 6% rise from 2024. The main tricks are account takeovers, sim‑swap attacks, and money‑muling schemes. Criminals sell “fraud‑as‑a‑service” kits and create synthetic identities that blur real and fake users. Consumers feel poorly equipped to spot AI‑driven scams, so cross‑sector cooperation is urged.
