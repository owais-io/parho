The UK Attorney General has asked Nigel Farage to apologise for bullying and racist remarks he allegedly made at school, according to a Guardian investigation. Over 20 former classmates say Farage, as a teenager, told anti‑Jewish jokes and targeted minority pupils. Farage denies the accusations and says his statements were “banter.” The Attorney General says Farage never condemned antisemitism and needs to speak up if he wants to be a serious political leader.
