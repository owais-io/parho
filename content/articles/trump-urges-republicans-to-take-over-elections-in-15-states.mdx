Donald Trump said on a conservative podcast that Republican state officials should step in and “nationalise” elections in 15 states to stop people he calls “illegal voters” from voting. He repeated false claims that non‑citizens are voting in big numbers. The remarks come after an FBI raid on Georgia ballots and raise concerns that the president might try to rig the November mid‑terms. Democrats say the comments threaten the integrity of U.S. elections.
