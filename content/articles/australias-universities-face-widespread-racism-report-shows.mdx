A new report from the Australian Human Rights Commission says racism is systemic in universities. It surveyed 76,000 students and staff and found that 70% had seen or heard racist remarks and 15% had faced it directly. Jewish and Palestinian students were hit hardest, followed by First Nations and Asian groups. Only 6% of those affected filed complaints, citing fear and distrust. The report urges a national anti‑racism framework and stronger accountability.
