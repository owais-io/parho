A flare‑up in the Middle East has pushed oil prices higher, with Brent crude climbing to around $79 a barrel. The conflict threatens the Strait of Hormuz, a key oil route, and could keep prices up for longer. Higher energy costs spread to everyday goods, potentially raising inflation and prompting central banks to rethink rate cuts. The situation shows how a regional crisis can hit the global economy fast.
