At the climate conference in Brazil, Amazon tribes blocked the entrance to protest forest destruction and lack of a say at the talks. They handed a baby to a diplomat in a moment that was photographed everywhere. The meeting was slow, with talks on ending fossil fuels and new roadmaps. A key moment was Brazil’s environment minister Marina Silva, who cried and then raised a fist, showing the fight will keep going.
