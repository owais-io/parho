Backlogs of potholes in England and Wales now cost £18.6bn, a record high. Even after councils fixed 1.9 million holes last year, only half of roads are in good shape, especially after a wet winter. The report says new holes appear faster than the money the government can add. The Department for Transport has pledged record £7.3bn of long‑term funding, but experts warn that more spending must be used wisely to keep roads safe.
