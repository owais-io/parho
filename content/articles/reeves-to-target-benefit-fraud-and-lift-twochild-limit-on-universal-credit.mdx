Chancellor Rachel Reeves will launch a new push to find and stop benefit fraud while also scrapping the two‑child limit on Universal Credit, which will cost about £3 billion. She says the changes will help people and save money by cutting wrong payments. The budget also keeps plans to freeze tax thresholds for a few years, raise taxes on very expensive homes, and reduce some tax breaks. The move faces criticism from both sides of politics.
