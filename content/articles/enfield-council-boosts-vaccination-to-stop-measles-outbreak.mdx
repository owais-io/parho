Enfield council is fighting a measles outbreak by finding kids who missed shots and setting up extra clinics. Many families are hesitant because of online myths and busy lives. The council is working with schools, faith leaders and health visitors to explain the benefits of vaccines. Doctors say that low income, moving homes and mistrust of health services make it hard to get shots. More community‑focused efforts are being tested.
