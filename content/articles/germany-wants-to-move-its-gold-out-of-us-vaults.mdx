Germany’s top economists say it’s too risky to leave billions of euros of gold in U.S. vaults amid Trump’s unpredictable policies and shifting alliances. The country holds about €164 bn in New York, making it the world’s second‑largest gold holder. Experts argue moving the bars home would boost strategic independence, while officials say Germany’s reserves are diversified and safe. The debate shows a growing push to bring gold back to Frankfurt.
