The Guardian’s letters column gathers readers’ stories of animals appearing at key moments of loss. One writer recalls a robin’s song the night his father died, and another describes a fox that lingered in her garden after her husband’s death. These shared memories show how wildlife can bring unexpected comfort in grief, offering a quiet presence that feels almost like a gentle hug. Readers applaud the writer, Amy‑Jane Beer, for capturing these tender moments.
