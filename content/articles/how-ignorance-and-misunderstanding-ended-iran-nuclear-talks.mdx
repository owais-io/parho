The article explains how U.S. and Israeli officials, especially Trump’s envoy Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner, ran Iran nuclear talks poorly. They brought little expertise, cut meeting time, and treated the talks as a pre‑war stunt. Iran’s side felt the talks were a trick to get the U.S. ready for war. Misunderstandings about Iran’s nuclear plans, lack of proper negotiation staff, and rushed talks helped push the conflict into a 12‑day war.
