In Rio’s Pedra Branca state park, Quilombola families grow bananas using an agro‑forestry system that blends trees, crops and livestock. Their banana groves help keep the city’s biggest forest healthy, protecting biodiversity, soil and water. After years of fighting, the state now sees the farmers as guardians of the park. But nearby condo developments threaten the forest, so the growers want official recognition and protection. They hope this will stop illegal land grabs and keep their way of life.
