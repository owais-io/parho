Royal Mail has lifted the price of a first‑class stamp to £1.80 (a 6% rise) and a second‑class stamp to 91p. The company says it must cover growing delivery costs, even though the number of letters has dropped by 70% over 20 years. Citizens Advice says the hike is unfair and that service quality has slipped, with many post deliveries late and only new, lower targets from Ofcom. The postal company faces a £21 million fine for missing targets.
