UK officials stopped issuing study visas to people from Sudan, Afghanistan, Myanmar and Cameroon. More than 200 Sudanese students had been offered places at 46 British universities, including Oxford and Cambridge. They say the ban throws their plans into chaos and cuts their chance to study abroad. The government says the move stops visa abuse that could lead to asylum claims. Students are campaigning for the policy to be reversed.
