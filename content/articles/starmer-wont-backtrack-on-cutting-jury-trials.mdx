UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer said he will keep his plan to cut some jury trials, arguing it’s needed to speed up justice for victims of violence against women and girls. The government wants more judge‑only cases to clear a backlog of about 80,000 criminal cases. Critics say the change weakens the justice system, but Starmer insists it will bring quicker outcomes for victims.
