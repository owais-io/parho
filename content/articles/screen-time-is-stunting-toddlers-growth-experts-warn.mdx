Kids now spend more than two hours a day looking at phones and tablets, and teachers say it hurts their language, coordination and teamwork skills. In schools, teachers see toddlers who can’t hold pencils or solve puzzles because they’re used to instant screen answers. New guidance from the government and WHO says little‑to‑no screen time for under‑two and no more than an hour for two‑to‑four. Some schools have switched to no‑screen classrooms and see better progress.
