Netflix agreed to buy Warner Bros Discovery for about $83 billion, adding franchises like Harry Potter, Batman, Game of Thrones and Friends to its catalogue. The merger will combine two of the biggest U.S. streaming services and could save Netflix $2‑$3 billion a year by its third year. Regulators will review the deal, and some industry voices warn it could hurt competition and long‑term value. Netflix says it will keep Warner’s movies in theaters and the catalog available.
