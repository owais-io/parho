Two US fighter jets, plus electronic warfare planes, circled the Gulf of Venezuela for about 40 minutes. The flights were seen as a step up in the Trump administration’s tense stance against Venezuela and its leader Nicolás Maduro. The US says the airspace is international and the operation is routine, while Venezuela claims the Gulf is part of its territory. The visit ties into the US’s drug‑war campaign, which critics say could be a war crime.
