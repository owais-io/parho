During the 2024 Olympics in Milan, the crowd booed Vice‑President J.D. Vance. Most international broadcasts recorded the sound, but NBC in the U.S. cut it out, sparking a debate about media honesty. The piece warns that when the U.S. hosts future events like the 2026 World Cup or the 2028 Los Angeles Olympics, viewers might expect the same silence. If broadcasters hide criticism, audiences will doubt their credibility.
