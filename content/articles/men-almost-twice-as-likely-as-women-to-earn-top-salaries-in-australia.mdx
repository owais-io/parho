A new report from Australia's Workplace Gender Equality Agency shows that men are almost twice as likely as women to earn high salaries. Men are 1.8 times more likely to be in the top salary quartile, while women are 1.4 times more likely to be in the lowest quartile. Half of companies have a pay gap favoring men, especially in finance, construction and mining. The report urges firms to analyse the gap, set targets and support flexible work.
