In Odesa, Ukraine, residents are living in fear as Russian drones and missiles hit the city almost every day. Since December, the attacks have knocked out power, water and heat, leaving many districts dark and cold. The strikes aim to break civilian morale and cut Ukraine’s sea trade, which is vital for its food exports. Ukrainian defenders set up anti‑tank barriers, but the long‑range Russian fire keeps the city in a state of emergency.
