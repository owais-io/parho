Luis Muñoz Pinto, a 27‑year‑old Venezuelan, was sent from the United States to a harsh prison in El Salvador after officials linked his two rose tattoos to a Venezuelan gang. He says the roses honor his twin sisters, not a gang. A U.S. court has now ordered his return, but the Trump administration ignored a ruling and sent him to El Salvador’s Cecot prison. He now lives in Bogotá, working to support his family.
