David Lammy, the justice secretary, called the court backlog of more than 100,000 cases an emergency and said victims are losing hope. He had wanted to cut jury trials so that only serious crimes like murder and rape would be heard by juries, but lawyers and MPs slammed the idea. After the backlash he withdrew the plan and promised £550 m over three years to help victims and witnesses in court.
