Spain’s rail system is under heavy scrutiny after two major accidents in three days. A high‑speed train crash near Barcelona killed 43 people and injured 152, and a later collision with a wall near Gelida killed a trainee driver and injured 41. Heavy rains are blamed, but the incidents have sparked calls for an indefinite strike by train workers and demands for better safety checks. The government and opposition are now debating the state of the country’s rail network.
