Peter Griffiths worked at the Inner London Education Authority’s English Centre from 1976 to 1978, organising programmes for secondary teachers. A young teacher at a difficult school recalls how visits to the centre gave fresh ideas and materials that boosted classroom work. Griffiths’s forward‑thinking approach inspired many teachers and left a lasting legacy in teaching practice. His initiatives helped shape modern teaching methods and encouraged teachers to experiment with new techniques.
