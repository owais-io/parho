UK officials signed a £330m NHS contract and a £240m defence deal with Palantir, a US surveillance company. The agreements give Palantir access to sensitive health and military data, sparking worries that citizens’ privacy is being sold to a private firm. Critics point out the deals lacked a competitive tender and involve political connections, showing a growing reliance on US tech that could erode UK sovereignty.
