In the town of Morón, Cuba, a small group of protesters broke into a provincial Communist Party office, tore down furniture, and set computers on fire. Five people were arrested for vandalism. The incident comes amid growing frustration over food, medicine shortages and power cuts, sparked by a U.S. oil embargo that has left Cuba short of fuel. President Miguel Díaz‑Canel says the country is in talks with Washington as protests spread beyond Havana.
