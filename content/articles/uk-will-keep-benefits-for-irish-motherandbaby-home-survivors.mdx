The UK government agreed to stop cutting benefits for Irish mother‑and‑baby home survivors who take compensation. Up to 13,000 survivors in Britain could lose universal credit etc. The decision came after UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer backed Philomena’s law to protect payments. Campaigners, actors, and survivors pushed for change. Irish scheme pays €5k‑€125k; survivors will now receive compensation without losing UK support. Both governments acknowledge suffering.
