A court in Perth lifted a suppression order, making Liam Hall, 32, public. Hall is accused of throwing a homemade “fragment bomb” at an Invasion Day rally, but the device failed to explode. He faces the state’s first terrorism charge, plus weapons and intent to endanger. The court said open reporting is key to justice, but Hall did not appear as scheduled, and his lawyer has requested a six‑week delay because of mental‑health concerns.
