A tropical low caused the Burnett River in Bundaberg, Queensland, to overflow, flooding more than 2,000 homes and businesses. About 197 people stayed overnight in an evacuation centre, and power was cut in roughly 2,000 households. The city’s bridges closed, isolating around 10,000 residents. Local officials said the full damage could take up to a week to assess, and the flood is part of a broader wet summer that has also affected the Northern Territory.
