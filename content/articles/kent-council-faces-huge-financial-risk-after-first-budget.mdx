Kent council officials just approved its first budget, raising council tax by 3.99% – the highest since a 2020 limit – but local opposition warns it leaves the council in extreme financial danger. Critics say reserves are too low and the risk register shows over £410 million in immediate threats, including cuts to fostering and stalled road repairs. The move has sparked a debate over whether the party can manage Kent’s growing costs.
