Heather Ellis caught HIV during a motorcycle trip in 1993 and only found out when she needed a visa test a decade later. Her case shows that Australian women are often missed by doctors and get diagnosed late, which harms their health. Experts say only 5% of women’s new HIV cases dropped in a decade, and 44% of women are diagnosed late. A new education video is being rolled out to teach doctors about women’s HIV and reduce stigma.
