In the first big election of the year, the Greens won in Baden‑Württemberg, Germany’s third‑largest state. Their victory, led by Cem Özdemir, shows voters are moving away from the traditional centre‑right parties, especially the Christian Democrats led by Friedrich Merz. The far‑right Alternative for Germany also gained support, riding on worries about jobs and immigration. Merz’s hard‑line stance on immigration and the economy is being challenged by a greener, more inclusive agenda.
