Roy Darke, 85, was a university teacher and local politician who believed everyone should have a voice in shaping their town. He taught planning in Sheffield during the 1960s and 70s, pushing for public input in city projects. Later he served as a councilor in Sheffield and Oxford, helping rebuild after the 1991 student games and protecting local heritage. A devoted husband, father and volunteer, he left a legacy of community care.
