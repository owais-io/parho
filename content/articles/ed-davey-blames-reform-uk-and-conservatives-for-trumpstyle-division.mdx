Ed Davey kicked off the Liberal Democrats’ local election push on May 7, blasting Reform UK and the Conservatives for spreading Trump‑style division. He said the party would fix rivers, boost local health, and cut energy bills for everyday families hurt by rising costs. The Lib Dems promise quick GP appointments, help for high streets, and stricter water rules. With more council seats than the Tories, the party looks set for record gains.
