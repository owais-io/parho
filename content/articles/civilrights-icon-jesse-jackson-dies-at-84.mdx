Civil‑rights activist Jesse Jackson, who spent more than five decades fighting for equality and ran twice for the Democratic presidential nomination, died at 84. He was a key figure in the 1960s movement, worked with Martin Luther King Jr., and founded groups that helped Black people gain jobs and college scholarships. Jackson also campaigned on health and voting rights during the COVID‑19 era. His legacy is one of justice, solidarity and community service.
