One Chilean diver died when a boat’s propeller hit him while he was at a salmon farm, and the company was fined for breaking safety rules. The incident highlights a broader problem: Chile’s salmon farms are the world’s second‑largest producers but have the highest number of work injuries and deaths. They also dump chemicals, antibiotics and waste into rivers, harming wildlife, fishers and local indigenous communities that rely on clean water.
