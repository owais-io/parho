In 1845 a British mining company in Brazil rented 385 enslaved Africans, even though Britain had outlawed buying them. The plan was legal under the 1843 Slave Trade Act because the people were ‘rented’ for up to 14 years, but many were never freed. It wasn’t until 1879 that 123 survivors were released, and most had died. The case shows how British businesses kept profiting from slavery in Brazil for decades after Britain abolished the trade.
