The New York Catholic archdiocese is trying to raise $300 million to settle lawsuits from about 1,300 people who say they were abused by priests. It cut staff costs, sold its Manhattan headquarters, and hired a judge to help negotiate. The goal is to pay victims and promise better safeguards. The move comes after similar large payouts in Los Angeles and New Orleans and while the church battles insurance and court cases.
