On Thursday the U.S. Justice Department released over 3 million pages of documents from its probe of Jeffrey Epstein, a former financier accused of sex‑crime. The new files show email exchanges between Epstein and several Trump‑era figures, including commerce secretary Howard Lutnick and former Trump aide Elon Musk. The release also raised concerns over redactions that hide victims’ names, prompting criticism from survivors and lawmakers who want full transparency.
