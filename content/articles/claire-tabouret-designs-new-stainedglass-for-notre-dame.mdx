Claire Tabouret, a contemporary artist chosen from over 100 competitors, will add six new stained‑glass windows to Notre Dame. Her designs, showing the Pentecost, cost about €4 million and have sparked protests and petitions from heritage groups. The French president and the archbishop support the project, but critics worry it vandalises the cathedral’s historic windows. Tabouret also has a solo exhibition in the Netherlands, showing her colourful, bold work.
