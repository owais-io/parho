The letter writer points out that Crisis charity is now acting as a landlord to help homeless people because it's hard to find housing. The writer asks if Shelter, which used to provide housing in the 60s and 70s, should also start offering direct accommodation. Shelter now focuses on advice and campaigning but has plenty of staff and money. The writer suggests Shelter rethink its role and use resources to fund housing again.
