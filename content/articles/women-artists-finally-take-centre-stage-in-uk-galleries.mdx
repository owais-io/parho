For decades women were underrepresented in major UK galleries. This year, a wave of solo shows featuring female artists is finally breaking that trend. Highlights include Dame Tracey Emin at Tate Modern, 91‑year‑old Rose Wylie at the Royal Academy, and 94‑year‑old Bridget Riley at Margate, among others. The move comes amid a broader cultural shift that celebrates women’s voices and challenges old biases in the art world.
