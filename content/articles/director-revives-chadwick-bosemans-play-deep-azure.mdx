A London theatre director, Tristan Fynn‑Aiduenu, has brought Chadwick Boseman’s rarely‑seen play “Deep Azure” back to life at the Sam Wanamaker Playhouse. The story, written in 2005, follows a Black woman grieving her fiancé’s death by police, mixing hip‑hop, verse and dance. Fynn‑Aiduenu’s production includes Black American actors and aims to honor Boseman’s legacy and highlight Black writing in mainstream venues. The show shows how grief, community and art intersect.
