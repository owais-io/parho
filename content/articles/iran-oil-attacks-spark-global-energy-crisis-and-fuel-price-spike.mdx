Iran’s recent attacks on Gulf oil and gas sites have rattled the global energy market, sparking fears of a prolonged crisis. Western governments, including the U.S. and EU, warn of “zero restraint” if more strikes hit key infrastructure, while fuel prices soar worldwide. In Australia, the competition watchdog has logged hundreds of petrol price‑gouging complaints, and Cyclone Narelle is set to hit Queensland with winds over 250 km/h.
