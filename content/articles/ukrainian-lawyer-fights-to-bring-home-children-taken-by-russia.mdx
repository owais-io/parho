Kateryna Rashevska, a 28‑year‑old human rights lawyer, leads Ukraine’s effort to return about 20,000 children forced into Russia. She has spoken to the UN, the US Senate and helped the ICC issue a warrant for Putin over deportations. Russia has returned only 20 kids, mostly from occupied zones, not from Russia itself. Despite threats, Rashevska keeps working, safeguarding evidence and hoping international law will bring justice. She sees her work as a calling and remains hopeful as children rebuild their lives.
