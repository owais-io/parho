Tony Bracegirdle, an 83‑year‑old amateur rose breeder from Lancashire, left a legacy of award‑winning roses and a love for the hobby that inspired others. He started growing roses in 1974, winning the Royal National Rose Society championship 18 times. His roses, like “Nikki” and “Tony’s Perfection,” were sold commercially and admired worldwide. He also served as a judge, columnist, and speaker, sharing his enthusiasm with the gardening community.
