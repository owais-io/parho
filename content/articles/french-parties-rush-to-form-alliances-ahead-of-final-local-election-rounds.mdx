French parties are scrambling to build coalitions before this weekend’s final local elections in big cities like Paris, Marseille and Lyon. The race is tight, with the far‑right National Rally and the left‑wing La France Insoumise making surprising gains. Candidates are negotiating deals—some centre‑left parties refuse alliances, others team up with the left or right—to try to win mayoral seats and keep the far‑right out. The results will shape France’s political map ahead of next year’s presidential race.
