Morgan McSweeney, Keir Starmer’s chief of staff, quit after Epstein‑file revelations, and the following day his communications chief also left. Scotland’s Labour leader, Anas Sarwar, publicly called for Starmer to step down, sparking a debate inside the party. The cabinet backed the prime minister, but the loss of his key aide shows growing doubts about his control and signals a potential leadership crisis.
