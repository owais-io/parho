The article shares how the everyday question “How are you?” becomes tough for people dealing with serious health problems. A man who has metastatic prostate cancer explains that the usual polite reply (“Oh, not bad”) doesn’t fit his reality. He now says things like “All right today” or “Pretty good today,” adding “today” to keep it honest about his changing situation. This simple trick helps him communicate his true state while keeping conversations respectful.
