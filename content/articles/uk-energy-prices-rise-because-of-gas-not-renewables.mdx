UK electricity bills are climbing mainly because the price of natural gas on global markets has shot up. Even though renewables now supply almost half of the power, the UK uses a “marginal cost” pricing rule that sets the price based on the most expensive source – usually gas. This rule makes the gas price drive almost all of the bill. Green levies and network charges add only a small share, and more domestic gas won’t change the cost.
