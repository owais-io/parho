A German publishing giant, Axel Springer, has offered £575 million to buy the Daily Telegraph and Sunday Telegraph, snapping Lord Rothermere’s three‑year attempt to take the papers. The deal could shift the papers’ right‑wing tone under new ownership, raising questions about media concentration and influence on the Conservative Party. The sale will need government approval, and the new owners promise to keep the papers’ editorial independence and invest in technology.
