Richard Walker, the chief executive of Iceland supermarket chain, will be made a Labour peer in the House of Lords by Prime Minister Keir Starmer. Walker, who used to back the Conservatives and was even a Tory candidate, switched sides after saying the party had drifted from business interests. As a peer, he’ll help push pro‑EU and business‑friendly policies. Starmer is adding 25 new peers, including Walker, to broaden Labour’s upper‑house voice.
