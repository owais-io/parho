A new change to the student‑loan repayment rules has left millions of UK graduates with debts that could top £100,000, sparking anger and calls for reform. Plan 2 borrowers now pay 9 % of earnings above £28,470, while interest can push balances up to £93k or more. Stories from a 24‑year‑old, a music teacher, and a data analyst show how the debt drags on salaries, hampers home‑buying and fuels resentment toward older generations. The government’s promise of fairness is being questioned.
