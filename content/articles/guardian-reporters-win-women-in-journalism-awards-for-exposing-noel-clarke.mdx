The Guardian’s Sirin Kale and Lucy Osborne won Women in Journalism’s “Women of the Year” award after their investigation into 20 women’s claims that actor Noel Clarke abused them. The pair faced legal threats and Clarke’s lawsuit, but a UK high‑court judge ruled the Guardian’s reporting was true and in the public interest. Osborne said the award celebrates the brave women who spoke out. The Guardian also gave its rising‑star prize to Malak Tantesh for fearless Gaza coverage.
