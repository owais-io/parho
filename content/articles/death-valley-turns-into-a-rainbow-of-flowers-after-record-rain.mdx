A rare superbloom has turned Death Valley into a rainbow of flowers after a winter of record rain. Visitors will see bright pink, purple, and yellow blooms covering the desert floor. The National Park Service says low‑elevation flowers will keep blooming until late March, with higher‑elevation blooms arriving in April and May. Popular flowers include desert gold, evening primrose, phacelia and Mojave poppy.
