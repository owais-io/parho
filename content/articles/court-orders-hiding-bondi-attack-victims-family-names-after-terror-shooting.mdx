Lawyers for Naveed Akram, who is accused of killing 15 people at Bondi beach, asked a court to keep his family’s names and addresses private because they’ve received death threats. A magistrate granted a temporary ban, but media groups say there’s no immediate danger. The court will decide on a permanent order on April 2. The family has faced harassment, vandalism and hateful messages since the attack.
