In 2025, Russian attacks on Ukraine killed 2,248 civilians and injured 12,493, a 26% jump from 2024, says the Action on Armed Violence (AOAV) group. Each strike left an average of almost five people dead or hurt, up 33% year‑on‑year. The worst hit was a June 24 strike on Dnipro that struck a train, apartments and schools, killing 21 and wounding 314, including children. AOAV’s Iain Overton warned that war’s proportionality has broken down.
