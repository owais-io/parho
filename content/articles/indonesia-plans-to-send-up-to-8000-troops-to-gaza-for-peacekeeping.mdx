Indonesia says it is ready to send 5‑8,000 troops to Gaza as part of a peacekeeping force in Donald Trump’s Middle East plan. The army chief says the move is still being negotiated, but the troops would mainly do engineering and health work. The plan could put Indonesia in the middle of the Gaza conflict, drawing criticism from Israeli hardliners and raising worries at home about getting dragged into a dangerous mission.
