The Noma scandal has sparked a look at kitchen abuse everywhere. Chefs say René Redzepi abused staff, and the fallout has reached a pop‑up in Los Angeles. The article argues the problem isn’t just one restaurant—it’s a culture that rewards high standards, unpaid interns, long hours and a lack of fair pay. It calls for awards like Michelin to include labour standards and for new fair‑accreditation schemes to protect workers.
