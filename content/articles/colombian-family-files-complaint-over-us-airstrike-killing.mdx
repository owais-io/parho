A family in Colombia has lodged a complaint with the Inter‑American Commission on Human Rights, saying a 42‑year‑old fisherman named Alejandro Carranza Medina was unlawfully killed in a U.S. airstrike on a suspected drug boat on 15 September. The family argues it was an extrajudicial killing, part of a campaign that has killed at least 83 people on over 20 vessels in the Caribbean and Pacific. U.S. officials deny the allegations and have not responded directly to the complaint.
