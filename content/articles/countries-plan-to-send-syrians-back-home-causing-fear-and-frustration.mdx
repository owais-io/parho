After the fall of Assad, Austria, Germany, and other European states are tightening asylum rules and planning to bring Syrians back to Syria. Many Syrians have lived in Europe for years, built careers and families, and feel betrayed by sudden policy shifts. The move sparks worry about deportation, job shortages, and mental health. While some Syrians want to return, the damaged infrastructure in Syria makes it difficult. Many refugees hope to stay, but governments insist on a “return” option.
