A U.S. military airstrike on a suspected drug‑smuggling boat in the Caribbean killed nine people in the first attack. Two men survived and stayed clinging to the wreckage for about an hour before a second strike killed them. Senators saw a video of the event, sparking a debate over its legality. The Pentagon says the strike was lawful, but many experts argue it could be a war crime because the men were incapacitated. The campaign has now killed at least 87 people.
