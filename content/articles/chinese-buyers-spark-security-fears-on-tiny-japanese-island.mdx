Kasasa, a tiny island in Japan’s Seto Inland Sea with only a handful of residents, has become a security flashpoint after two plots were bought by Chinese developers. Local fishermen, like 80‑year‑old Hideya Yagi, fear the land could turn into a surveillance or drone base because it sits near US and Japanese military bases. The Japanese prime minister has promised tighter rules on foreign land sales, while China has responded with trade restrictions.
