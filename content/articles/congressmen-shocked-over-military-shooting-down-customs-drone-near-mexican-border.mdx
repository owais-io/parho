Democratic lawmakers are furious after the U.S. military used a laser to shoot down a drone that turned out to belong to Customs and Border Protection, not a threat. The incident happened near the Texas‑Mexico border and closed airspace around Fort Hancock. It’s the second drone shoot‑down in two weeks, raising concerns about coordination between the Pentagon, CBP, FAA, and Congress. Senators are calling for an independent probe and a review of the laser system.
