The Australian Open marked Evonne Goolagong Cawley Day with ceremonies, workshops and Indigenous ball kids to celebrate First Nations culture. The event shows how sport can welcome Aboriginal people, but racism remains a problem. Surveys reveal many athletes and community players avoid reporting abuse. Experts say clubs need real action plans, more diverse leaders and government conditions to change the culture, not just symbols.
