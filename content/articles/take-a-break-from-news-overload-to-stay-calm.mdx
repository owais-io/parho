The article explains how constant news and social media can make people feel anxious, like living in a war of nerves. It compares today’s panic to the 1940s when Britain feared war and notes that we now have similar fears with politics, pandemics and global tensions. The writer suggests simple ways to stay calm: set a daily routine, limit news, take breaks, stay connected with friends, and practice small pleasures. If anxiety gets too big, call a helpline.
