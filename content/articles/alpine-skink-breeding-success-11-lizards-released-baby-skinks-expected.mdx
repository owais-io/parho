Eleven endangered skinks were released into a fenced area in Victoria’s Alpine National Park. Scientists hope a female called Omeo will give birth to two babies in March, boosting the tiny population. The skinks live only on high mountain “sky islands” and are losing habitat as the climate warms. The project is part of a breeding program that aims to protect the species from extinction.
