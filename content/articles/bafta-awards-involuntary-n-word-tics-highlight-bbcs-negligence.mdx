At the Bafta awards, a man with Tourette’s named John Davidson had involuntary tics, including the N-word, while two black actors were on stage. The BBC didn’t edit the slur from the broadcast, even though they had a delay. The author argues that Davidson’s tics are not intentional and should not be blamed, but the BBC’s failure to manage the situation caused hurt. He calls for accountability and empathy.
