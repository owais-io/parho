The International Energy Agency’s chief, Fatih Birol, says the energy crisis caused by the war in Iran is as serious as the 1970s oil shocks and the fallout from Russia’s invasion of Ukraine. He warns shortages of petrochemicals, fertilizers, sulfur and helium could damage the global economy. Markets fell sharply after Donald Trump threatened to destroy Iran’s power plants, adding pressure to an already tense situation.
