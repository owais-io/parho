Donald Trump said NATO troops stayed off the front lines in Afghanistan, a claim that shocked British MPs, veterans and the public. The comment was met with widespread condemnation. Soldiers’ groups highlighted the 457 British deaths and 3,486 total NATO casualties. Trump’s remark was seen as disrespectful, especially given his own draft‑dodging history. The backlash shows how sensitive the war’s legacy remains.
