The FBI has started buying phone location data from private companies without court warrants, raising concerns about privacy and the Fourth Amendment. Senators and privacy groups say this lets the agency track people’s movements in bulk, using data that apps and advertisers sell. The data reveals where you go, who you meet, and even who you sleep with. Congress is pushing new rules to stop warrantless purchases.
