Britain’s Keely Hodgkinson set a new world indoor 800m record in Liévin, running 1:54.87, nearly a second faster than the 2002 mark by Slovenia’s Jolanda Ceplak. The 23‑year‑old had been training hard after hamstring injuries and a delayed MBE ceremony. She beat a strong field that included Olympic silver medallist Tsige Duguma and U23 champion Audrey Werro. Hodgkinson says it was a dream come true and she plans to aim for a medal at next year’s world indoor championships.
