At Adelaide Oval, three old stories stand out. In 1888 the ground hosted the world’s first wooden rollercoaster, which wowed 10,000 riders in its first 30 hours. A century‑old cricket record still shines: in 1908 debutant Roger Hartigan and Clem Hill batted together for 243 runs, rescuing Australia from a tough spot. And a famous spat between Don Bradman and spin bowler Clarrie Grimmett ended when Grimmett coached India’s Vijay Hazare, forcing Bradman to respect his former rival.
