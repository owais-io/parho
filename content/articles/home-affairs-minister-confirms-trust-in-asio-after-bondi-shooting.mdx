Home affairs minister Tony Burke says he still trusts Australia’s spy agency, ASIO, even after the deadly Bondi shooting. He reviewed ASIO’s handling of Naveed Akram, who faces 59 charges including murder, and says the agency made the right decisions. Questions remain about how Akram and his father travelled to the Philippines last month, but Burke says the agency has enough resources and works closely with police.
