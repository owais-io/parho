David Winnick, a Labour MP who served Walsall North for 38 years, died at 92. He was known for standing up for civil liberties, especially when he stopped the Blair and Brown governments from holding terror suspects for months without charge, limiting detention to 28 days. He also fought against identity cards and campaigned for free TV licences for older people and a national minimum wage. His long career spanned 42 years in Parliament.
