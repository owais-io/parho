Lloyds is being asked by the UK’s Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) to explain why it used data from 30,000 staff bank accounts during pay talks with unions. The bank says it only looked at aggregated salary and spending figures, not individual details. The ICO could fine Lloyds up to 4% of its turnover—about £1.36 bn—if a breach is found. Meanwhile, Lloyds has agreed a £1.2 k pay rise for junior staff in 2026–27.
