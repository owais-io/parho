UK mortgage rates have slipped above 5% as banks like HSBC, Nationwide, Halifax and Barclays pulled almost 500 loan products in the last two days. The move follows a surge in global swap rates triggered by the war in the Middle East. With about 1.8 million fixed‑rate mortgages set to end in 2026, borrowers will face higher costs, and the chance of a future interest‑rate cut has dropped sharply.
