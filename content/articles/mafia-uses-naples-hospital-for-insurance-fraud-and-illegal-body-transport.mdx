Police arrested four people linked to the Contini clan of the Camorra for exploiting San Giovanni Bosco hospital in Naples. The mafia hijacked the hospital’s food services, bribed staff, staged fake road accidents to claim insurance money, and ran a private ambulance crew that carried bodies home with oxygen masks to avoid the mortuary, charging families €700‑€1,200. Doctors, a psychiatrist, and a lawyer were also involved in issuing false medical documents.
