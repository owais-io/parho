Last year a tailings dam at Sino‑Metals’ copper mine in Zambia broke, dumping 50 million litres of heavy‑metal‑laden water into the Kafue River. The spill killed fish, poisoned wildlife, shut off drinking water for Kitwe’s residents and harmed crops. The incident highlights a global pattern: thousands of tailings dams sit in sensitive ecosystems, and extreme weather is making them more likely to fail. Investors and regulators are pushing for stricter safety rules, but many mines still lack proper oversight.
