During a high‑court hearing in London, Judge Raquel Agnello ruled that claimant Laimonas Jakštys was secretly receiving answers through smart glasses linked to his phone. He paused before replying, and an interpreter heard a voice coming from the glasses. The judge said the evidence was unreliable and dismissed it entirely, finding the claimant untruthful and likely coached by a Lithuanian lawyer. The case highlights new tech risks in court.
