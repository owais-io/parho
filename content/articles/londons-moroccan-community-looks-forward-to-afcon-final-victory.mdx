In London’s Little Morocco, people are buzzing about the Morocco vs. Senegal final. Football gives the community a break from worries over Gaza and brings everyone together, from café‑owners to bakers to retirees. People are planning to watch the match in cafés, at home, and in Trafalgar Square, hoping for Morocco’s first AFCON trophy since 1976. The game’s excitement unites fans across ages, jobs, and backgrounds.
