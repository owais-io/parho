Young Muslim student Mayasa Mandia says Denmark’s far‑right is slipping into everyday politics while the Social Democrats keep tightening immigration limits. Though the anti‑immigration Danish People’s Party won only about 7.5% of the vote, its ideas have spread to mainstream leaders, pushing the whole party spectrum to the right. The election may keep Prime Minister Mette Frederiksen in office, but neither major bloc can win alone, leaving the Moderates as kingmakers.
