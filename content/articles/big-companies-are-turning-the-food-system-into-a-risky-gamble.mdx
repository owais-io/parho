George Monbiot warns that the world’s food supply is dangerously fragile because a few huge corporations now control most of it. He says that if a chokepoint like the Strait of Hormuz is blocked or a big company collapses, food could quickly disappear from shelves. He calls for breaking up these firms, stronger regulation, more diverse diets and a shift to plant‑based eating to make food safer for everyone.
