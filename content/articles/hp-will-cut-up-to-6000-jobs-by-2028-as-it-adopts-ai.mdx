HP plans to cut 4,000‑6,000 jobs worldwide by the end of 2028 as it uses AI to speed product development and save money. The company, which has 56,000 workers, expects the cuts to save about $1 billion a year but will cost $650 million. Jobs in product design, operations and support will be affected. The move follows earlier cuts and mirrors other firms replacing roles with AI.
