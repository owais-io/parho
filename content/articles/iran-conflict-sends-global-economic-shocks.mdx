Since the US‑Israel war in Iran, oil prices have spiked and the Hormuz waterway is blocked, affecting gas, food and construction goods. Countries are cutting power, freezing rents, and restaurants in India are trimming menus. Sri Lanka and South Africa face fuel queues and higher airfares. Japan even paused crisp production, and Bangladesh’s bus fares jumped. While some oil exporters benefit, the war is hurting families and businesses worldwide.
