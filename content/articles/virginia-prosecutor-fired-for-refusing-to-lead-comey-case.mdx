Robert McBride, the second highest prosecutor in Virginia’s Eastern District, was fired after he declined to head the prosecution of former FBI director James Comey. The dismissal follows a judge’s ruling that the current chief prosecutor, Lindsey Halligan, was appointed illegally. Several other prosecutors have been let go over their reluctance to charge former officials. The Justice Department is appealing the judge’s decision, and Halligan’s future role remains in question.
