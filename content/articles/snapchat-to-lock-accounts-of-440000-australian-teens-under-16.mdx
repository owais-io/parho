Snapchat will shut down accounts for kids under 16 in Australia when the ban starts on Dec. 10. The app will flag users who look like they’re 13‑15, using clues from how they use it and any ages they’ve entered. If a flagged user isn’t under 16, they can prove their age with a bank, ID or selfie. Those under 16 can save chats and videos before the lock‑down. Accounts locked for over three years without verification will be deleted.
