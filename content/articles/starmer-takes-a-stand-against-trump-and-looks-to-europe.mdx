Prime Minister Keir Starmer has finally taken a stand against US President Donald Trump after the American leader criticised Britain over the Chagos Islands and its role in Afghanistan. Starmer demanded a public apology and warned that the UK would no longer tolerate such attacks. While the move risks souring UK‑US ties, it boosts Starmer’s standing at home and opens the door to stronger links with the EU, signalling a shift away from close dependence on America.
