On February 28, six planets—Jupiter, Saturn, Venus, Mercury, Neptune, and Uranus—will line up in the night sky, a rare event that can be seen from many parts of the world. The bright planets are visible to the naked eye, while Neptune and Uranus need binoculars. The best time is early evening, and stargazing apps can help locate them. It’s a memorable celestial display that won’t repeat until 2040.
