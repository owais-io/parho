Scientists have discovered a way to repair a common age‑related egg defect that hurts IVF chances. The protein Shugoshin‑1 keeps chromosomes together; older eggs lose it, leading to faulty embryos. In tests, adding the protein cut chromosome problems in half. If proven safe, this could let more women succeed in one IVF cycle, but the technique still needs years of research. Still, it offers a hopeful path.
