South Korea marks the first anniversary of last year’s failed attempt to impose martial law. President Lee Jae‑Myung says those who tried to topple democracy must face justice and that the country still needs to heal. Despite his call for unity, supporters of former President Yoon Suk‑Yeol, who declared the martial law, are still gathering, while Yoon and other officials are on trial. Political divisions and a rise in right‑wing protests keep the wounds raw.
