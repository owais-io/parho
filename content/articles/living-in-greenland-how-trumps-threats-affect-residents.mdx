Trump says the U.S. must take control of Greenland, a move that has turned the largely Danish‑controlled island into a hotspot for international rivalry. As Arctic ice melts, new shipping lanes open and the island’s rich rare‑earth deposits become valuable. Greenland’s leaders are scrambling to protect their sovereignty, while residents worry about possible U.S. military presence. Europe is debating tariffs and diplomatic responses, highlighting how a small, remote country can suddenly find itself in the centre of world politics.
