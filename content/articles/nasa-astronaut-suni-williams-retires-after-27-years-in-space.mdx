Suni Williams, a veteran NASA astronaut who spent 608 days in space and set a record for women’s spacewalk time, is retiring after 27 years with the agency. She is best remembered for the 2024 Starliner mission that turned a planned 10‑day test into a 286‑day stay, sparking political debate. Williams’s career highlights her leadership and her role in shaping future lunar and Mars plans.
