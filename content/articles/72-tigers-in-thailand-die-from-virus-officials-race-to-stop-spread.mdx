A deadly virus has killed 72 captive tigers in northern Thailand. Officials say the animals had canine distemper and a secondary bacterial infection. Some experts point to feline panleukopenia. The outbreak hit Chiang Mai’s Mae Rim and Mae Taeng districts and shut down the popular Tiger Kingdom zoo, where visitors can touch tigers. Staff are cleaning cages and preparing vaccines, while vets study the virus. Similar tiger deaths have happened in Vietnam and a Thai zoo in 2004.
