Nine Entertainment sold its talk‑back radio stations – including 2GB and 3AW – to Sydney pub baron Arthur Laundy for $56 m. At the same time, Nine bought outdoor‑ad firm QMS Media for $850 m. The radio stations were losing money and attracting an older audience, while Laundy’s family owns 40 pubs. Nine plans to use QMS to boost its newspapers and streaming service, and to keep the radio brands alive under the Laundys.
