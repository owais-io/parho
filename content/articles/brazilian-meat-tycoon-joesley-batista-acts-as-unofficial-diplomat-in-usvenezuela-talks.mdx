Joesley Batista, a Brazilian meat magnate with a corruption record, flew to Caracas to meet President Maduro and try to push him out of power. While this trip didn’t change Maduro’s rule, Batista used his connections to influence U.S. policy, helping Brazil get tariffs on its beef lifted and easing U.S. sanctions on Venezuela. His move shows how business leaders can now play key diplomatic roles.
