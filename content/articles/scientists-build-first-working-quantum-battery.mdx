Australian scientists built a prototype quantum battery that charges in a few femtoseconds and stores energy for nanoseconds. The tiny device shows quantum batteries can charge faster as they get bigger, using a laser for wireless charging. But the current capacity is far too small to power phones or cars. Researchers aim to keep the charge for longer periods, hoping the technology will eventually power quantum computers, drones, or cars on the go.
