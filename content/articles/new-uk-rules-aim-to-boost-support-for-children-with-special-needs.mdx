England’s government plans big changes to how children with special educational needs get help. The new rules move support from local councils to schools, giving kids quicker access to therapy and other services. The move comes after the number of children needing help has doubled, putting pressure on budgets and causing legal battles for parents. While the aim is to make schools more inclusive, critics worry the changes could weaken legal protections.
