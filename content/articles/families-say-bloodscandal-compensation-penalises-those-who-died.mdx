Victims of the UK’s contaminated blood scandal say the government’s payout plan is unfair. The scheme gives money to people still alive and to families of those who died, but the payment for dead victims is based only on the short time they lived after infection. Families say this creates a “penalty for dying” and leaves them far short of what would be fair for them.
