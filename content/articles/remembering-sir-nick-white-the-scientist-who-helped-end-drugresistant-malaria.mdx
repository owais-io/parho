Sir Nick White was a British doctor who spent most of his career in Thailand, leading research that turned a Chinese herbal plant into the world's main malaria medicine. He pushed for artemisinin‑based combination drugs, which now cure 98 % of uncomplicated malaria and saved millions of children worldwide. White also built a research network across Asia and Africa, fought drug resistance, and trained local scientists to keep the fight strong. His legacy lives on in the fight against malaria.
