Australia’s Bureau of Meteorology warns that over half the country will go through the hottest heatwave since the 2019–20 bushfire season. Temperatures could top 45°C in many places, putting big fire risks in South Australia, Victoria, NSW and WA. Big cities like Sydney, Adelaide and Melbourne could see highs in the low‑to‑mid 40s, and power lines may struggle to keep up with air‑conditioner use. Residents are urged to stay cool and be ready for extreme heat.
