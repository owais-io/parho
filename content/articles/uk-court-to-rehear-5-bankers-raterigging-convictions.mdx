The Criminal Cases Review Commission has sent five former bankers’ convictions for manipulating Libor and Euribor to the UK Court of Appeal. The move follows a Supreme Court decision that cleared Tom Hayes and Carlo Palombo, saying trial judges gave wrong jury instructions. The five men, jailed between 2 and 8 years, argue the same mistakes ruined their cases. The appeal court will decide if their sentences should be overturned.
