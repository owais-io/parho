In Oxford, Mississippi, Wendy Pfrenger found her teens’ school sex‑ed only taught abstinence and was not medically accurate. She felt kids needed real answers about bodies, pregnancy and LGBTQ issues. In January she launched a free, inclusive program from the church called Our Whole Lives. Parents and kids who aren’t church members joined, showing how faith groups can fill gaps left by restrictive state laws.
