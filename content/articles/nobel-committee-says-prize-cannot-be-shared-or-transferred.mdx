In October, Venezuelan opposition leader María Corina Machado was chosen for the Nobel Peace Prize. She said she would give it to U.S. President Donald Trump, who has long wanted the award. The Nobel Committee replied that a prize cannot be revoked, shared or transferred once awarded. So the plan to give the prize to Trump will not happen. The decision is final and stands for all time.
