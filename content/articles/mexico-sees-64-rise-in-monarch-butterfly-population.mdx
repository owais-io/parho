Scientists reported that Mexico’s monarch butterflies increased by 64 % this winter compared with last year, covering 2.93 ha of forest – the largest area seen since 2018. The jump is a hopeful sign for the species, which has lost habitat to deforestation, herbicides, and illegal logging tied to avocado farming and cartel activity. While Mexico’s protected areas have curbed illegal logging, U.S. policy on endangered‑species status remains stalled.
