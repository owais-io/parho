President Trump has temporarily relaxed the Jones Act, letting foreign‑flagged oil and gas ships travel between U.S. ports for 60 days. The move is meant to ease rising fuel costs while the U.S. supports Israel against Iran. Meanwhile, Iran continues to export barrels through the Strait of Hormuz, and U.S. strikes on Kharg island target Iranian military sites. The decision has drawn criticism from European allies who refuse to help reopen the waterway.
