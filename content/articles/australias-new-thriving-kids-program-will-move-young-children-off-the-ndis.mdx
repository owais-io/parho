Australia’s new Thriving Kids program will move children under nine with mild developmental delays and autism off the NDIS, giving them early‑intervention services in schools and child‑care centres. It cuts the need for a formal diagnosis and focuses on four pillars—awareness, info, parent skills, and quick access to allied‑health help. The government and states will share a $4 billion budget. The plan starts on 1 October 2024 and aims to ease NDIS cost growth.
