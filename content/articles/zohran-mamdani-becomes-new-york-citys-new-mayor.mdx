Zohran Mamdani, a 34‑year‑old Democrat, was sworn in as New York City’s new mayor in a midnight ceremony inside an old subway station. He’s the city’s first Muslim mayor, using a Qur’an for the oath. He promised to improve public transit, freeze rents and support working people. He beat former governor Andrew Cuomo in the primary and won the election by focusing on affordable housing and city‑run services. The day will continue with a public inauguration.
