The US Treasury will print money with President Donald Trump’s signature, a first for a sitting president. The move replaces Treasurer Brandon Beach’s name on bills and is tied to the nation’s 250th‑anniversary celebrations. Treasury officials say the change marks economic growth and dollar strength. Trump has also pushed coins with his likeness, even though federal law usually forbids living presidents on money.
