Kay Mason Billig, the Conservative leader of Norfolk County Council, said the government had bullied her into postponing local elections for extra money and powers. When the government reversed that plan, she withdrew from the county’s devolution deal and said Norfolk would not take part in local‑government reorganisation. The move could delay the biggest council reshuffle in 50 years and may prompt other leaders to follow suit.
