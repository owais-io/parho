Reform UK’s new London mayoral candidate, Laila Cunningham, said the capital is no longer safe and would launch a hard‑talk crime campaign if elected. She called London “pity” and said crime is up, even though police data shows it’s falling. The remarks angered city leaders and the Labour party, who say she’s unfairly painting London and spreading division. Cunningham, a former prosecutor, has also promised to scrap the ULEZ if she wins.
