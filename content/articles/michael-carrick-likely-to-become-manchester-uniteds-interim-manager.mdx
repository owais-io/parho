Manchester United officials are leaning toward former midfielder Michael Carrick to take over as interim manager for the rest of the season, instead of the club’s long‑time coach Ole Gunnar Solskjær. Executive Omar Berrada and director Jason Wilcox met Carrick at the training ground and plan to decide by Monday. The club wants its new boss to have time to prepare for the upcoming match against City. Meanwhile, caretaker Darren Fletcher remains uncertain about his future after United’s recent defeats.
