Matt Brittin, a 57‑year‑old former Google Europe boss, is close to being appointed as the BBC’s next director general. Brittin, who once rowed for Britain in the 1988 Olympics, is also a non‑executive director of the Guardian Media Group. His tech background fits the BBC’s push to grow iPlayer and tackle competition from YouTube, a Google‑owned platform. Critics worry he may lack the public‑life experience needed for the role.
