Evgeny Lebedev and former cricketer Ian Botham are the two members of the UK’s House of Lords who have attended the fewest meetings. Between 2022 and 2025 each only showed up at seven of the 625 sessions, a 1.12% attendance rate. Both barely meet the minimum rule of one appearance a year, and they have rarely voted or spoken. Their low attendance has drawn criticism of the Lords’ efficiency.
