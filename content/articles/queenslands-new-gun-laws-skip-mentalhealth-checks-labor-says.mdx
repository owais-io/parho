Queensland’s state government introduced a gun‑control bill after the Bondi terror attack, but it did not adopt a coronial recommendation for mandatory mental‑health checks for licence applicants. Labor says the government ignored the Wieambilla inquest, which found mentally ill gun owners killed two police officers. The bill also adds reporting rules for health workers and a new hate‑speech law giving the attorney‑general power to ban slogans.
