Bank of Scotland was fined £160,000 after the UK’s sanctions watchdog found it opened a bank account for Dmitrii Ovsiannikov, a former Russian governor linked to Vladimir Putin. Ovsiannikov made 24 payments totalling £77,383 in February 2023. A spelling mistake on his passport let the account slip past the bank’s screening. Once discovered, the bank reported the breach, and the fine was reduced by half.
