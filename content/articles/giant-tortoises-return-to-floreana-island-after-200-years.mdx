Scientists and conservation groups have brought giant tortoises back to Floreana Island in the Galápagos after almost two centuries of absence. The tortoises were saved on a nearby volcano and bred in captivity. After a program that removed rats and cats, the hatchlings were released in 2025. Their return is boosting plants, birds and local farmers, and signals a big step toward restoring the island’s wildlife.
