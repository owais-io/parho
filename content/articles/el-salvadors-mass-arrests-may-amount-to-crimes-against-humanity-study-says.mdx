A new report by human‑rights lawyers says El Salvador’s president, Nayib Bukele, has put the country in danger of crimes against humanity. Since 2022 the government has jailed about 90,000 people – many without a fair trial – in harsh conditions, killing hundreds and torturing others. While the policy has lowered murder rates, it has trampled rights, shut out judges and critics, and many fear the country is moving away from democracy.
