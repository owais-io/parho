A council in rural New South Wales has scrapped a plan to ban Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander flags after 700 community letters said the idea was wrong. The flags were removed from council rooms for procedural reasons, and the motion to keep them was overturned by a close vote. Councillors and residents argue about the flags’ symbolism, with some saying removal would encourage racism. The council will now focus on a reconciliation action plan with local Indigenous groups.
