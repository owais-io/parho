Shahira Sadat, an Afghan software engineer, was stunned when the UK announced it would stop accepting student visas from Afghanistan, Myanmar, Sudan and Cameroon. She had been awarded a Chevening scholarship and an interview, but the visa brake meant her offer was withdrawn. Similar stories hit other women from the four countries, who were planning to study in the UK to gain skills to rebuild their war‑torn homes. The move has left many hopeful students without a path forward.
