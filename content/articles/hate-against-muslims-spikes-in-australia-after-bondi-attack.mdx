After the Bondi beach shooting, hate against Muslims in Australia jumped sharply. In the week after the attack, an organization logged 126 hate incidents – ten times more than before. Mosques received threatening phone calls, vandalism, and some worshippers were verbally abused. Muslim leaders say fear is growing, especially for women in hijabs. Despite this, communities are standing together, holding vigils, and leaders urge police to protect everyone.
