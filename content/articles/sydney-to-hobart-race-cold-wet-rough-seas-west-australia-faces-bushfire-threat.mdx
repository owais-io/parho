The Bureau of Meteorology says the Sydney to Hobart race will be cold, wet and rough, with up to 25‑knot southerly winds after Boxing Day, but conditions are expected to be safer than last year’s storm. Meanwhile, Western Australia is battling a 40‑C heatwave and high bushfire risk, with evacuation routes blocked at Boddington Goldmine. A cyclone has moved away, and the rest of the country faces a mix of rain, storms and chilly temperatures.
