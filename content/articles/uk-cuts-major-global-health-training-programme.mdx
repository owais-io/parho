UK is ending its Global Health Workforce Programme, which trained doctors and nurses in Africa, because of aid cuts. The scheme, running in six countries, helped build pandemic preparedness. Cancelling it could leave gaps in health security, even for the UK. The government says it will try to keep projects running, but the cuts reflect a shift to more military spending. Experts warn the loss could cost more in the long run.
