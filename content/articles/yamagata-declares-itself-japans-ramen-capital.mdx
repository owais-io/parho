Yamagata, a small city of 240,000 in northern Japan, has won the title of Japan’s ramen capital after a campaign to beat rival Niigata. The city hosts about 230 ramen shops, and locals buy an average of 22,000 yen (≈£106) per household for noodles each month. While ramen is a daily comfort food, studies link frequent consumption to higher health risks because of salt. The title is a source of pride and boosts local businesses.
