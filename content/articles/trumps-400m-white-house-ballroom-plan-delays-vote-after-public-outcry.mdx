A federal panel that reviews changes to the White House pushed back its vote on Donald Trump’s $400 million ballroom plan to April 2, after receiving thousands of public comments—mostly against the project. The NCPC chair said the delay was to allow more deliberation. Trump wants the ballroom built on the former East Wing, financed by private donations, but critics say it would be gaudy and unnecessary. White House officials insist the vote will still happen.
