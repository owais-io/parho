The SNP, led by John Swinney, says Scottish independence could cut household energy costs and will centre its campaign on renewable power. He points to Norway’s model of keeping oil profits in public hands and criticises the UK tax that drains money from the sector. Swinney stresses Scotland already has cheap renewable resources and calls independence the way forward. Labour leaders accuse the SNP of failing the NHS and other services.
