The BBC has lost its top boss, Tim Davie, amid a string of scandals, from a Bafta awards blunder to political pressure. The search for a replacement has stalled because several high‑profile candidates, like Jay Hunt and Alex Mahon, have turned down the job. The most talked‑about contender now is Matt Brittin, a former Google executive and 1988 Olympic rower. Critics worry he lacks broadcasting experience, but he could bring new tech skills to the broadcaster.
