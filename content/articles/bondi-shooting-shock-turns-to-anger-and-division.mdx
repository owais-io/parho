On Sunday night a gunman opened fire at a Jewish Hanukkah event in Bondi, Sydney. The country was stunned at first, but anger and division are growing. Some politicians blamed immigration, while community leaders praised acts of courage and called for unity. People wonder why security was weak and how the gunman could get weapons. The attack has left many Australians feeling scared and in need of support and solidarity.
