Farzana, a woman in northern Afghanistan, asked a Taliban court for a divorce after her husband beat her with a phone charger cable. The judge dismissed her claim, telling her that a few beatings were allowed under new Taliban rules and that she should stay. Human‑rights groups say the new laws give husbands a “license” for violence, while UN experts warn this sets a dangerous precedent for women’s safety.
