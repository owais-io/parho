England's Labour government plans to overhaul the special needs system, promising expert support for each child. The move will add about £1.5bn a year, but many schools face teacher shortages and budget limits. Local authorities have been borrowing extra money, and the government has cleared some historic debt, yet the source of new funding remains unclear. Critics worry it may hit schools hard and still cost taxpayers more.
