News said a giant AI supercomputer would rise in Loughton, London, but the site is still a pile of scrap and metal for scaffolding. The government promised £2.5bn investment and 750 jobs, yet the company behind it, Nscale, has no formal contract with the state and may not own the land. The story highlights how AI promises can turn into empty promises, with delays and unclear funding.
