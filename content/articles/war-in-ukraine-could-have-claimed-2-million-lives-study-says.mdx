According to a Washington-based think‑tank, Russia and Ukraine together could have lost up to 2 million soldiers in the nearly four‑year war, with Russia bearing the brunt—about 1.2 million casualties and 325,000 deaths. Ukraine has suffered roughly 600,000 losses. The figures, gathered from officials and media, show Russia losing far more troops, even as it slowly gains little territory. Moscow offers big enlistment bonuses and hires foreign soldiers, while Ukraine struggles to replace its soldiers.
