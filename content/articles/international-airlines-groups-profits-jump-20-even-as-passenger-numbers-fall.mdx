IAG, owner of British Airways and Iberia, posted pre‑tax profits of almost £4 bn – a 20% rise – despite a 0.4% dip in total passengers. The lift comes from strong transatlantic traffic, especially premium leisure seats. IAG CEO Luis Gallego praised the airline’s performance but warned that Heathrow’s planned third runway could push costs too high, urging tighter charges. Share prices fell after the report.
