Diane Munday was a leading voice for women’s rights in the UK. She fought hard for the 1967 Abortion Act, then helped start BPAS to give safe, legal abortion care. A magistrate and diabetes supporter, she also stood up against unfair treatment. Munday faced threats and abuse but never stopped campaigning. She died at 94, remembered for her courage and dedication to justice.
