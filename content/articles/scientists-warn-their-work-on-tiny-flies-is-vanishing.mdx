Art Borkent, a 72‑year‑old specialist who has described over 300 species of biting midges, says that as he ages, the whole field of taxonomy—the science of naming and studying species—is dying. With few young scientists, limited funding, and universities dropping courses, many insect groups risk becoming “orphaned” and unknown. DNA barcoding helps identify species but can’t replace the detailed, hands‑on knowledge that taxonomists provide.
