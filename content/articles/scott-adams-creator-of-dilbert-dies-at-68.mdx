Scott Adams, the cartoonist who made Dilbert and later became a conservative commentator, died at 68 after a battle with aggressive prostate cancer. His ex‑wife Shelly Miles announced his death on a livestream and read his farewell note. Adams started Dilbert in 1989, selling the strip to hundreds of papers, and later wrote books. In his later years he made headlines for anti‑vaccine remarks and a controversial statement about Black people, which led many newspapers to drop the comic.
