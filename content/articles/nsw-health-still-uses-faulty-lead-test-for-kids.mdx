NSW Health is still using a point‑of‑care machine called LeadCare II to test children’s blood for lead in Broken Hill. The device has a history of giving falsely low readings and was removed from the Australian medical register in 2020. Despite this, the department says it helps screen more kids, but families say they get little help after a high result. The state plans to replace the machine when a new model is ready.
