Therapists at Kaiser Permanente in California are striking because a new patient screening system uses clerical workers and online questionnaires instead of licensed clinicians. The change, introduced in 2024, has made it harder for people in crisis to get timely care, and some patients are waiting weeks for appointments. Therapists worry AI and non‑licensed staff may miss urgent risks, and that the system could replace human judgment. The union says the system is illegal and risks patients’ safety.
