Aaron Horsey, a father who lost his wife while his baby was born, helped push a new law that lets parents who lose a partner get up to 52 weeks of paid leave before their child turns one. The change, part of the Labour‑led Employment Rights Act, will start in April and gives fathers and other caregivers a clear right to take time off right away. It aims to ease the burden on grieving families and keep them from having to keep working while dealing with loss.
