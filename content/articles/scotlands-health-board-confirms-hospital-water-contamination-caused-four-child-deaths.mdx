Scotland’s largest health board, NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde, finally admitted that contaminated water at a Glasgow hospital caused infections that led to four child deaths, including 10‑year‑old Milly Main. The admission came in closing papers of a 2020 inquiry into the hospital’s design and construction. Families welcomed the truth, while politicians called for broader investigations. The board said the water system was “likely the cause” of the infections. The inquiry will keep probing the failures.
