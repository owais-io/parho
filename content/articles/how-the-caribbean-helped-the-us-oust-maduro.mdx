In early 2026 the US, under Trump, took Venezuelan president Nicolás Maduro to court and jailed him. Trinidad and Tobago, a Caribbean island, openly backed the move, giving the US radar gear, letting jets land, and even helping with drug‑strike operations. The island’s prime minister pushed for US help over Venezuela’s gas, while other Caribbean nations were more cautious. The move split the region, raised legal worries, and put local leaders on edge about their own safety.
