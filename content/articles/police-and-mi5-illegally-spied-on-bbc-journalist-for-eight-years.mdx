Police and MI5 illegally monitored BBC journalist Vincent Kearney for eight years, collecting his phone data to find his sources. The case was heard by a special court that looks at spy‑agency complaints. Both the Police Service of Northern Ireland and the Metropolitan Police admitted they got his communications data without permission, plus MI5 did the same. The spying caused a chilling effect on Kearney’s work and hurt his relationships with sources.
