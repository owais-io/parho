Jimmy Lai, a 78‑year‑old media boss who opposed Beijing, was sentenced to 20 years in prison for sedition under Hong Kong’s national security law. The case sparked a global outcry, but local press groups were silent or even praised the sentence. This shows how the city’s free‑press environment has narrowed, with journalists fearing retaliation for criticizing the authorities.
