Many young people born between 1997 and 2012 are not putting money into pensions because they think retirement will never be an option, says a Guardian piece. With wages stretched by rent, bills and care costs, a student teacher and a 28‑year‑old couple in Cumbria say they can’t afford to save. They prefer low‑interest savings that can be taken out if something unexpected happens. Researchers say distrust of banks and worries about the state pension also keep Gen Z away from retirement plans.
