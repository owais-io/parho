Local elections in France show how parties are reacting to the far‑right. After Lionel Jospin’s death, Paris and Marseille were won by left‑wing candidates who teamed with Greens and Communists. In other towns the Rassemblement National didn’t capture the big wins it wanted, though its support grew. The results suggest the 2027 presidential race may still be a contest between the centre‑right and the far‑right, but left‑wing unity could help them win.
