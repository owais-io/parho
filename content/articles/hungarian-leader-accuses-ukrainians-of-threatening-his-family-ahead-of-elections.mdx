Prime Minister Viktor Orbán says Ukrainians threatened his family, using the claim to rally voters ahead of next month’s elections. The claim follows a Ukrainian accusation that Orbán’s anti‑Ukraine stance hurts Hungary’s relations with Kyiv. Meanwhile, Hungary seized a convoy carrying millions of euros and gold from a Ukrainian bank, sparking accusations of unlawful detention. The tense exchange shows how the election is turning into a fight over Ukraine ties.
