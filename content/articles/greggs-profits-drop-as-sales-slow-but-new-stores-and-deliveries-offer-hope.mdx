Greggs, the UK bakery chain, saw its pre‑tax profit fall 17.9% to £167.4m for the year ending Dec 27, even though sales grew 6.8% to £2.15bn. The slowdown is blamed on high living costs, inflation and hot weather that hurt footfall. CEO says easing inflation should help. The company keeps opening new shops—121 in 2025—aiming for 3,000 stores, and is boosting delivery and evening trading to grow.
