The House of Lords voted to pardon women who were convicted or jailed for getting abortions outside the legal system. The move follows a Commons vote that stopped criminalising these women but left doctors who provide illegal abortions still at risk. The new law will clear police records, helping women avoid long‑term problems like job checks. It is seen as a major relief for those who faced arrest and stigma.
