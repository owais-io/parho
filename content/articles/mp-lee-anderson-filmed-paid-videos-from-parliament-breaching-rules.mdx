Lee Anderson, a Reform UK MP, sold two Valentine’s Day videos filmed from his Westminster office through the Cameo app, violating rules that ban commercial use of Parliament. The clips, sold for £45 and £56, were recorded in his taxpayer‑funded office and offered to private customers. Anderson has previously been warned for similar breaches and has apologized, but the new videos raise fresh concerns about MPs exploiting parliamentary space for profit.
