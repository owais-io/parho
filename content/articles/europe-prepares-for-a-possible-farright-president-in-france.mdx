European leaders are scrambling to prepare for the chance that France could elect a far‑right president next year. The possibility of Marine Le Pen or Jordan Bardella worries Germany, Brussels and Kyiv because France’s nuclear weapons could change Europe’s security and budget. Partners are negotiating joint nuclear drills and early‑warning systems, and some are even talking about their own nuclear plans. Meanwhile, French president Macron is moving loyalists into key posts to keep influence after he leaves office.
