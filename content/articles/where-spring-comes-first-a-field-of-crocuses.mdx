In a small English village, a muddy valley called Inkpen crocus field is a Wildlife Trust reserve that blooms early each spring with 400,000 wild crocuses. The meadow has never been ploughed, so it’s a rare wildflower spot full of orchids, sage, and butterflies. Some say the crocuses were brought back by Crusader knights, others think they came from old gardens. The field’s beauty keeps birds and mammals happy, drawing many visitors.
