In the quiet town of Lake Cargelligo, a woman and her partner were killed by a former boyfriend who then fled. The gunman, Julian Ingram, was on bail for domestic‑violence charges when the shots were fired. Police are hunting him across the bush after reports of sightings, while the community mourns and questions how a known abuser could escape a court order. The killings highlight the risk Indigenous women face in family‑violence cases.
