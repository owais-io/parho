After a spike in fuel prices caused by Middle East tensions, farmers in regional Australia started buying extra diesel, saying it was a smart move, not panic buying. Independent fuel distributors say they can’t get enough petrol from major companies because the suppliers are allocating stock mainly to branded stations. The shortage is hurting rural businesses, and the government has called a cabinet meeting to boost fuel supply and stop price gouging.
