Tunbridge Wells residents are racing to buy their town’s 106‑hectare commons before developers can turn the land into housing. The Commons, sold to a private company 18 years ago, is now up for sale. Local groups have until March 26 to raise money and keep the area protected by law. The land is home to rare wildlife and beloved to joggers, dog‑walkers, and music fans. If sold, the park could disappear.
