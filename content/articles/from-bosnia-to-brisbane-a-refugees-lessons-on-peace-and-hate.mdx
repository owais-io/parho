Jasmina Joldić was nine when the Bosnian war started. Her dad was taken by armed men, and she learned she was Muslim. She fled to Germany, then to Brisbane, Australia. Over the years she built a career in public service and now works in Canberra. Joldić says the war taught her how quickly hate can destroy society. She wants Australians to remember that migrants help keep peace and that we must guard against rising anti‑immigrant sentiment.
