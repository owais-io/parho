A federal judge halted the Trump administration’s practice of arresting people in Washington, DC without a warrant or proof they would flee. The decision came after a lawsuit from the ACLU and other groups that said officers were stopping Latino residents indiscriminately. The judge said arrests must show probable cause and an escape risk. The ruling protects residents from fear of random stops and lets the court monitor future compliance.
