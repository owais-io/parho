Scientists say the number of new antibiotics in development has dropped 35% over five years, from 92 to 60 projects, and only a few big companies are still investing. They warn that drug‑resistant infections could kill 8 million people a year by 2050. While some new drugs like GSK’s gepotidacin and Innoviva’s zoliflodacin have been approved, the overall research pipeline remains thin, especially for low‑and middle‑income countries.
