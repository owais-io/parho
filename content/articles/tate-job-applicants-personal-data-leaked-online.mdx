Personal details from 111 people who applied for a website developer job at Tate galleries were exposed online, showing addresses, salaries, and referee contacts. The data dump appeared on an unrelated site, and some referees’ phone numbers and emails were publicly visible. Applicants, like programmer Max Kohler, learned their info had leaked after a stranger contacted a referee. Tate says it’s investigating, and the UK regulator says organisations must report breaches within 72 hours.
