Germany has reversed a plan to bring 640 Afghans from Pakistan to safety. The new conservative government says it won’t accept them, even though they helped German troops during the Afghanistan war. Rights groups warn they could face danger if returned. The move follows a shift in policy to limit new arrivals and offer money to those who give up their right to move to Germany. The decision has sparked criticism from NGOs and human‑rights groups.
