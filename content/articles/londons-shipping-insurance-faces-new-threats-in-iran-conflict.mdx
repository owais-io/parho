Lloyd’s of London has insured ships for over 330 years, but the Iran war has introduced a new threat: drones and missiles in the Hormuz Strait. More than 100 vessels are trapped, with some attacked and crew killed. Insurance costs have jumped from 0.25% to as much as 7.5% of a ship’s value. Shipping firms fear the danger, so many have left their ships anchored. The UN wants a safe corridor, and some governments are thinking about naval escorts.
