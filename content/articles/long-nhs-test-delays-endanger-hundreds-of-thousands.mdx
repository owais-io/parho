Doctors warn that more than 380,000 people in England waited over six weeks for cancer, heart, and other scans in September. Almost half of hospitals miss the six‑week target, delaying treatment and raising the risk of serious harm or death. NHS England has set a new 20 % limit, but the problem persists, and critics doubt the government can meet its promised turnaround times.
