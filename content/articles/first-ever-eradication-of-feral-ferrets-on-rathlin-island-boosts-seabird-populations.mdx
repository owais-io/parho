Rathlin Island, off Northern Ireland’s coast, became the first place in the world to have all feral ferrets removed. The effort, led by the RSPB and local volunteers and funded through EU and lottery money, used cameras, drones and a specially‑trained dog to locate and shoot the predators. With the ferrets gone, puffins, corncrakes and other birds are breeding again, and the island is now working to keep rats out.
