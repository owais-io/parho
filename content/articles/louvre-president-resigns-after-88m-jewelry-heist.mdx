The Louvre’s president, Laurence des Cars, stepped down after the museum was hit by a €88 million jewellery robbery. The thieves broke in in ten minutes, stealing priceless Napoleonic pieces. A parliamentary inquiry said the museum’s security was badly under‑funded, with only 39 % of rooms fitted with cameras. Des Cars admitted a “terrible failure” and the museum faces strikes and calls for new security upgrades.
