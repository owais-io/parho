On Wednesday afternoon a fire broke out at Wang Fuk Court, a complex of eight 31‑storey towers in Tai Po, Hong Kong. It has killed 44 people, left 45 in critical condition, and left hundreds still missing. Firefighters fought a five‑alarm blaze with 128 engines. Three men were arrested on manslaughter charges. Officials blame the blaze on possible scaffolding sparks, but the exact cause is still under investigation. Shelters have been opened and the incident has shocked the city.
