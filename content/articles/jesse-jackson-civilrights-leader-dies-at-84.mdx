Jesse Jackson, the American civil‑rights icon who helped end segregation, died at 84. UK politicians like Diane Abbott and Bell Ribeiro‑Addy praised him for fighting racism, economic inequality and for inspiring Black communities. He toured the UK for his Equanomics campaign, spoke about the role of Black people in Britain’s history, and supported voter registration. His death prompted reflections on how his messages of equality still matter today.
