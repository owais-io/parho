In 2024, Ireland’s sole alumina producer, Aughinish Alumina, exported a growing amount of alumina to Russia, mainly to the Russian aluminium group Rusal. The shipments are legally allowed because alumina itself is not a restricted item, but the material can be turned into aluminium that is used in weapons. Leaked trade data shows that the alumina ends up in Russian smelters and then to companies that build missiles and explosives, raising doubts about whether EU sanctions are being fully enforced.
