Football clubs across the UK have faced a wave of online racist abuse targeting players like Chelsea’s Wesley Fofana and Burnley’s Hannibal Mejbri. While clubs promise to work with social‑media platforms and police, no perpetrators have been named or punished yet. Players and anti‑racism groups say the focus should be on victims, not on apologies or leniency. The government has called for stricter enforcement, but the rise of abuse is still going up by 45% year‑on‑year.
