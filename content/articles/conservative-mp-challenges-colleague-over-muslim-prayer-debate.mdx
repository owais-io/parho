James Cleverly, a Conservative MP, said he disagrees with Nick Timothy’s claim that a public Muslim prayer in Trafalgar Square was an act of domination. Timothy’s comments sparked backlash, with Kemi Badenoch defending him and others like Emma Best arguing that prayer is a fundamental right. The dispute centers on gender segregation in the event and whether Muslim prayers should be treated differently than other faiths. Cleverly called for debate but did not support the domination claim.
