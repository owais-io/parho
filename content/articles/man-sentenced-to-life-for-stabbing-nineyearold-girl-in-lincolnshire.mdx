A 26‑year‑old Lithuanian man, Deividas Skebas, stabbed a 9‑year‑old girl to death while she played in a Lincolnshire town. He had schizophrenia and told police he was being controlled by “Nasa”. He pleaded guilty to manslaughter, but a jury convicted him of murder. He was given a life sentence with a minimum of 25 years. He was in the UK only three weeks before the crime.
