After a 10‑day war with Israel, Iran’s clerical council picked Mojtaba Khamenei, son of former Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei, as the country’s new head of state. The move comes amid missile and drone attacks across the Middle East. Iran, US and Israel all reacted, with the US calling the new leader “unacceptable” and Israel promising further strikes. Inside Iran, the choice sparks debate over a possible dynastic rule.
