Palantir, the U.S. data‑analytics firm, has secured a new contract with the UK’s Financial Conduct Authority, adding to deals worth over £500 m across the NHS, police, military and now finance. The company promises AI tools to spot money‑laundering and other crime, but critics say it risks privacy and could be manipulated by bad actors. Campaign groups protest Palantir’s ties to US immigration and Israel, yet the contracts keep coming.
