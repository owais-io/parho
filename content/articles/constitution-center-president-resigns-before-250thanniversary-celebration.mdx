The National Constitution Center in Philadelphia announced that long‑time president Jeffrey Rosen will step down after 12 years, with Vince Stango stepping in as interim CEO. The move just before the 250th‑anniversary celebrations has sparked drama: some board members say Rosen left because he lacked operational focus, while former judge J Michael Luttig claims the chairs forced his resignation to please the Trump administration. The center’s trustees deny any political motive, and Rosen will become CEO emeritus.
