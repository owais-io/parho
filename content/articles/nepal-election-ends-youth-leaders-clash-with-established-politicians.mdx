Nearly six months after Gen Z protests forced the prime minister to resign, Nepali voters went to the polls for an election that pits the old guard against a young, reform‑oriented movement. Turnout was only about 60 %, the lowest in two decades, and the vote is unlikely to give any party an outright majority, meaning coalition talks will follow. Key rivals include former PM KP Sharma Oli and rapper‑turned‑mayor Balendra Shah, who represents the youth’s push for corruption‑free politics.
