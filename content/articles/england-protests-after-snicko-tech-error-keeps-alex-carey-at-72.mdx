England’s coaching staff, led by Brendon McCullum, filed a complaint after a faulty Snicko system let Australia’s Alex Carey stay not out at 72 on day one of the third Ashes Test. The third‑umpire couldn’t confirm the dismissal because the audio spike came too early. BBG, who owns Snicko, said the wrong microphone was used and accepted responsibility. The ICC will investigate, and England said the tech was out of calibration.
