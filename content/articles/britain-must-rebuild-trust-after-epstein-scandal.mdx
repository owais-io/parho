Britain is grappling with the fallout from Jeffrey Epstein’s abuse network. The scandal exposed how powerful politicians, like Peter Mandelson, may have used inside information and lobbyists for personal gain. Gordon Brown calls for sweeping changes – an independent anti‑corruption commission, stricter ethics rules, public vetting of appointments, and tighter lobbying rules – to restore public trust and protect victims. He urges the government to act now in the near future.
