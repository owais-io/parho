The Justice Department opened up about 3.5 million documents linked to Jeffrey Epstein, but says there are still around 6 million pages that could be made public. Critics say the redactions hide too much, especially names of victims and powerful people. The release was announced by Deputy Attorney General Todd Blanche, who said Trump wasn’t directing the review. Congress wants the rest of the files out by the end of 2025.
