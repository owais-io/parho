Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth led a Pentagon prayer that called for violent action against enemies. He also announced reforms to the military chaplain corps, cutting the number of faith codes from 200 to 31 and removing officer insignia. Veterans and civil‑rights groups argue the moves mix politics and religion in the armed forces. Lawsuits now seek records of these prayer sessions to determine if the Pentagon keeps a neutral stance on faith.
