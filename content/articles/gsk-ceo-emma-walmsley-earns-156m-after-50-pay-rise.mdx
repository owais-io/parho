Emma Walmsley, who stepped down as GSK chief executive, got a 50% jump in pay, taking her total earnings to £15.6 million. Her 2025 salary was £1.4 million, and her bonus rose to £3.5 million, mainly from a share‑price boost that lifted her long‑term incentive award. GSK’s profits grew sharply, with sales up 7 % to £32.7 billion, and the company added five new product approvals. Walmsley’s move shows how big pharma leaders can earn huge bonuses when the company performs well.
