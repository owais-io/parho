UK and Germany’s top military leaders have urged citizens to support bigger defence spending, saying it’s a moral duty to protect people from Russia. They warned that Europe’s security has weakened and called for joint weapons purchases and stronger cooperation. Polls show many voters are uneasy about higher taxes or cuts to get more money for the army. Both countries are already building new factories and moving troops closer to Russia’s borders.
