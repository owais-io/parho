The Guardian article argues that UK politicians like Kemi Badenoch and Nigel Farage praise the U.S. for its free‑market system, even as the country faces poverty, health problems and financial instability. It says the U.S. exports economic crises abroad and lets the poor suffer while the wealthy prosper. The piece notes that Europe’s higher taxes and stronger regulations keep most citizens safe, and that older voters tend to favor a less‑regulated approach. It urges governments to adopt protective reforms.
