In Minas Gerais, Brazil, record rain has killed dozens and flooded homes in the coffee‑producing heartland. Scientists say the worst storms happen when the planet heats up from burning oil, gas and coal. They warn that cutting fossil‑fuel use, improving urban planning and building shelters for poor hillside communities can save lives. The floods are also pushing up global coffee prices and hurting food security.
