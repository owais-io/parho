People in the Central African Republic will vote on Sunday as President Faustin‑Archange Touadéra runs for a third term. About 2.3 million voters will choose a president, parliament and local officials. Seven candidates are on the ballot, including former prime ministers who were allowed to run after a court decision. Critics worry that the election may be unfair because of missing paper lists and limited internet access. The country has a million displaced people and a peace force keeping order.
