A new U.S. indictment of Venezuelan president Nicolás Maduro claims he worked with a Venezuelan street gang called Tren de Aragua, a story that echoes former President Trump’s allegations. Trump used the gang’s name to push deportations and label it a terrorist threat, but U.S. intelligence says the Venezuelan government has no link to the gang. The indictment’s claims are widely criticized as political and lacking evidence.
