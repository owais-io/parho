A Victorian judge slammed the police for waking people in custody every hour, calling the practice “idiotic” and “cruel.” He said the policy, introduced after deaths in custody, was unnecessary for low‑risk detainees and left them too tired for trial. The judge moved a murder case to Melbourne after the accused claimed the nightly checks left them exhausted. He warned the policy could end long trials in regional courts.
