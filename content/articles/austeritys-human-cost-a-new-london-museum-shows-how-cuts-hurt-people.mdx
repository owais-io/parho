The Guardian column explains a new Museum of Austerity exhibit in London that uses VR to show people who died because of government cuts. Researchers say 330,000 extra deaths happened between 2012‑2019. The show tells stories of two women who took their lives, two men who died from hunger, and a cancer patient who faced cruel bureaucracy. The piece criticises how the state talks about tightening budgets while people suffer, and argues that a “better or die” mindset is wrong.
