Labour’s chancellor, Rachel Reeves, gave her spring statement on Thursday, cutting the 2026 GDP growth forecast to 1.1% as energy prices hit record highs. The Office for Budget Responsibility said the war in the Middle East could push costs even higher, raising inflation worries. Reeves said the government had built a buffer of £23.6 bn and would keep borrowing costs low. She promised to protect families while markets brace for slower interest‑rate cuts.
