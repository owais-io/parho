The Trump administration launched a military campaign against Iran without a clear plan or goal. Early strikes killed Iranian leaders but left a power vacuum, and the US used old targeting data that caused civilian deaths. The war cost over $11bn in the first six days, and the Pentagon has no strategy for what happens after the fight. Experts say the lack of direction could drag the U.S. into a long‑term conflict and hurt its ability to project power worldwide.
