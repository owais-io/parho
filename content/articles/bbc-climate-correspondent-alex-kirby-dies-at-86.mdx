Alex Kirby, a BBC journalist who covered climate change and agriculture for 30 years, died of cancer at 86. He began as a priest, became editor of the Race Today journal, then joined the BBC in 1978. He travelled worldwide reporting on the environment, later worked as a religious affairs correspondent and covered Princess Diana’s funeral. After leaving the BBC he co‑founded the Climate News Network and wrote for the Guardian. He is survived by his wife, sons and grandchildren.
