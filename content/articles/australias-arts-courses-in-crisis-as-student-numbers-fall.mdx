Australia is losing its creative talent. A new study shows that student numbers in arts, media, drama and design have fallen sharply since the 2021 job‑ready graduate scheme, which made these courses more expensive. As a result, 48 art degrees have been cut and enrolments dropped 21% for high‑school arts subjects. Universities call for the scheme to be re‑worked, warning that without change the country could become “artless.”
