An 18‑inch Rembrandt lion drawing sold for a record $18 million at Sotheby’s in New York. The buyers, billionaire Thomas Kaplan and Jon Ayers, are giving all the money to Panthera, a charity that protects wild cats. The tiny chalk sketch shows a resting lion with a leash, a rare image by Rembrandt. The sale tops the previous record for a Rembrandt drawing, showing how art can raise huge funds for conservation.
