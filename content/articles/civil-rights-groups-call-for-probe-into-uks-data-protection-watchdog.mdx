Civil rights groups, lawyers and academics have asked Parliament to investigate the UK’s Information Commissioner's Office (ICO). They say the ICO stopped enforcing rules after the Afghan data leak that exposed former Afghan helpers to danger. The letter criticises the ICO’s weak punishments, its choice not to probe the Ministry of Defence and its overall lack of enforcement. The groups want a formal inquiry to address these problems. The ICO said it will explain its actions in upcoming meetings.
