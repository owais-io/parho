The Louvre, France’s most famous museum, is struggling. Water leaks, a cracked ceiling and damaged books are making the building unsafe, and staff have gone on strike for months. The government plans a €1.15 billion makeover with a new entrance, better security and new galleries, set to start in 2027, but it will cost visitors more. The museum’s past heists and old security warnings show the urgency to fix the place before it collapses.
