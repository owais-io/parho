The Geminids meteor shower will peak on the night of 13‑14 December, offering more than 100 shooting stars per hour from dark rural spots. They come from the asteroid 3200 Phaethon, not a comet. The radiant is near the star Castor in Gemini. The best view is from the northern hemisphere, but southern viewers can watch after midnight. Jupiter will also shine brightly that night.
