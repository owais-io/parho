Ian Russell, whose 15‑year‑old daughter Molly died by suicide after being exposed to hateful and harmful content online, has spent five years uncovering the role of social media in her death. He now speaks out against a blanket ban on social media for under‑16s. Instead, he says the Online Safety Act, with age checks and fines, is a better way to protect children. Russell urges sensible regulation and holds tech companies accountable.
