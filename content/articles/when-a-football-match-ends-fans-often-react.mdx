Why?   - **Team loyalty** – They love their club, so a win feels great, a loss hurts.   - **The game’s stakes** – If it decides a title, a promotion, or a rivalry, emotions run higher.   - **Surprises** – A last‑minute goal or a big comeback can make fans jump or sigh.   - **Personal memories** – A team’s history, famous players, or past glories add extra feelings.   How to keep the reaction simple:   1. **Short sentences** – “The team lost, so fans booed.”   2. **Plain words** – Use “happy,” “sad,” “angry,” not jargon.   3. **Speak like a friend** – “It was a tough loss, and people were disappointed.”   That’s why a match can spark strong emotions in fans.
