Barnet’s manager, Dean Brennan, was charged by the FA after he allegedly made sexist remarks to referee Kirsty Dowle during a League Two match against Shrewsbury. The comments were flagged in Dowle’s post‑match report, leading to an investigation that could result in a six‑match touch‑line ban. It’s the first time an EFL manager has been charged for this. Similar bans have hit other clubs for offensive language toward women. The FA has not yet issued a statement.
