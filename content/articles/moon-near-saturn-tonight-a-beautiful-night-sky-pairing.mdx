On November 29, the Moon will line up close to Saturn in the evening sky. The moon, still in its waxing gibbous phase, will shine silver, while Saturn glows faint yellow. They will be only 3.75° apart, visible to the naked eye. The best view is from dark skies, but even city lights let you see them. Just use binoculars to enjoy the pair. It's located in the Pisces and Aquarius constellations.
