Glasgow’s charity Refuweegee has been sending welcome packs with handwritten letters to refugees for ten years, showing the city’s openness. But last year, the rise of Reform UK and far‑right comments made many feel unsafe. Housing pressure, lack of flats and rising tensions between locals and newcomers add to the problem. Despite the fear, many residents rally to help, proving that Glasgow still values its tradition of welcoming strangers.
