At the UN climate summit in Brazil, Tuvalu’s climate minister Maina Vakafua Talia made the first public jab at former U.S. President Donald Trump, saying the U.S. had “shamefully” ignored the Paris climate deal and was harming tiny island nations. Tuvalu, a group of low‑lying atolls threatened by rising seas, calls on the U.S. to step up. Other countries are nervous to criticize Trump, who has cut climate funding and called the science a “hoax.”
