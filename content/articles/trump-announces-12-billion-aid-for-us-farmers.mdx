Trump announced a $12 billion aid package for U.S. farmers, using money collected from tariffs. He said the cash would give farmers certainty after tough trade battles, especially with China, and help lower food prices. About $11 billion will be paid right away to row‑crop growers, with the rest earmarked for other crops later. Funds should reach farms by the end of February. He also warned that other nations' protectionist rules hurt U.S. farmers.
