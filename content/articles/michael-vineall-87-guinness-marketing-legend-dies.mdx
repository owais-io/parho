Michael Vineall, 87, passed away. He worked for Guinness for 20 years, helping to grow the bottled Guinness brand and later joining the board that created the Guinness Book of Records. After leaving, he became a freelance marketing consultant, for drinks, education and agrifood. He studied psychology, philosophy and physiology at Oxford, loved jazz, cricket, and Leeds United, and was active in his church. He is survived by his wife, two children, six grandchildren and a brother.
