Israel has not brought anyone to court for killing Palestinian civilians in the West Bank since 2019, according to Guardian data. The killings—mostly by settlers and Israeli soldiers—number in the thousands, with a quarter being children. Yet less than 2 % of police investigations lead to charges, and just eight cases out of 368 resulted in convictions. Former officials now ask the International Criminal Court to step in, saying domestic courts shield perpetrators.
