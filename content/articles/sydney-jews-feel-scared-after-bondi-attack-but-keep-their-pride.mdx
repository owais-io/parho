After a terror attack on a Hanukkah event at Bondi beach, Sydney’s Jewish community is in a tough spot. Some feel unsafe and shut down places, while others still show their faith openly. At a funeral, leaders urged people to stay proud. Young Jews, like a 13‑year‑old, are fighting hate but also singing and keeping traditions. The community balances safety worries with a stubborn sense of identity and hope for solidarity.
