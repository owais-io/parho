Former Australian foreign ministers Bob Carr and Gareth Evans warn that Australia should rethink its close ties to the United States. They cite President Donald Trump’s sudden actions in Venezuela and his threat to take over Greenland as evidence that the US is unpredictable and ignores international law. They also criticize the Aukus submarine pact and other defense deals, saying they may not serve Australia’s interests. The government is monitoring the situation but has not publicly condemned the US.
