Israel’s top military lawyer said there isn’t enough evidence to prosecute five soldiers who allegedly abused and raped a Palestinian detainee in Gaza. After the victim was sent back to Gaza, prosecutors lost key evidence, and officials said senior staff had interfered with a fair trial. Rights groups say the decision shows a lack of accountability for abuse in Israeli detention centres. The Prime Minister welcomed the move, calling the soldiers heroes.
