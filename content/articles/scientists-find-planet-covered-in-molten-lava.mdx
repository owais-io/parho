Astronomers using the James Webb Space Telescope have found that exoplanet L98-59d, about 1.6 times Earth's size, is likely a molten world. The planet, orbiting a tiny red dwarf 35 light‑years away, shows a sulfur‑rich atmosphere and surface temperatures around 1,900 °C. Computer models suggest a global magma ocean and possibly a molten core, making the planet inhospitable for life. The discovery hints that many “habitable‑zone” planets might instead be hot, liquid worlds.
