Emma Brockes reviews how recent Hollywood movies treat the rise of Trump‑style authoritarianism. She notes that thrillers such as Netflix’s “Anniversary” focus on flashy conflict but miss the quiet ways democracy erodes through voter manipulation and political language. Comparing this to shows like “The Handmaid’s Tale” that depict the bureaucratic steps to dictatorship, she argues Hollywood often glosses over subtle political threats, making the real danger feel distant.
