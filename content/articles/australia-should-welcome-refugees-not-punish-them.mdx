Australia hosts big U.S. bases and has become a target, raising concerns about safety. The government’s actions show a split: one minister helped Iranian women flee war, while another canceled visas for thousands from Israel, Lebanon and Iran. The piece argues Australia should welcome people escaping conflict, not punish them, and notes past mistakes in treating refugees as enemies. This highlights a need for fairer immigration rules and better protection for those in danger.
