Argentina is about to pass a new labour law that cuts union power and reduces employee rights. The bill would let companies extend work days to 12 hours, lower severance, and change sick‑leave pay. Pro‑business supporters say it will create jobs, but unions and lawyers warn it will hurt workers and increase informal jobs. The law has sparked protests and police clashes, raising fears of more exploitation.
