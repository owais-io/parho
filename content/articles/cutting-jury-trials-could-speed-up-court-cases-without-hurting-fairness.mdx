The article argues that the long debate over whether to keep jury trials is wasting time while Britain’s courts face a huge backlog of 80,000 cases. It suggests that for less serious offences, trials could be handled by a judge and two magistrates instead of a jury, keeping juries only for the most serious crimes. The author also proposes that retired judges sift through old cases, dismissing the unimportant ones to clear the docket quickly.
