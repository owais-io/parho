Eid, the festival that marks the end of Ramadan, and Nowruz, the Persian New Year, were celebrated by Australian Muslims and Iranian Australians this weekend. But both groups felt grief as they watched the war in the Middle East. Mosques added security because of fears of Islamophobia, while families wanted to keep faith alive. Meanwhile, the Iranian community held a cultural event at Federation Square, calling Nowruz a form of resistance and hope.
