Two years after a government report warned of a needed payment scheme, the UK government has made no commitment to compensate women harmed by transvaginal pelvic mesh implants. Campaigners say the delay worsens pain, financial hardship, and mental health for thousands of victims. They call the inaction “morally unacceptable” and are pushing the matter straight to the prime minister.
