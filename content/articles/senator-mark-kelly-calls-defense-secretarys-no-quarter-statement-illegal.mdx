Arizona Senator Mark Kelly condemned Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth for saying the U.S. would give “no quarter” to enemies, a phrase that could mean no prisoners and is illegal under the Hague Convention. Kelly said the remark could put troops at risk and violate law. The comment came amid criticism of the Pentagon’s meme‑style war posts and Hegseth’s claims about Iran’s leader. The clash shows a growing row between the two veterans over how the U.S. talks about war.
