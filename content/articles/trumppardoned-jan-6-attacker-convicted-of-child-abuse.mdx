Andrew Paul Johnson, who helped storm the U.S. Capitol on Jan. 6 and was later pardoned by Donald Trump, was found guilty in Florida of several child‑abuse charges. He was arrested in Tennessee, extradited to Florida, and faced five counts of molesting minors and lewd conduct. A jury acquitted him of one electronic‑device charge. He could face life in prison, with sentencing still pending.
