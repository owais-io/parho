After years of campaigning, former mineworkers and their families will see a £100 a week increase to their pensions. The boost is part of a 41% uplift that follows the government returning a £2.3 bn reserve fund. Around 40,000 people, including more than 5,000 women, will get extra monthly payments and an average £5,500 lump sum. The change corrects a long‑standing injustice and gives many retirees the financial relief they need.
