Martyn Webster, 86, was a key figure in microsurgery, teaching and leading teams in the UK and abroad. He worked at Glasgow Royal Infirmary, helped start microsurgery societies, and wrote an early textbook on free tissue transfer. He also ran training courses, led European plastic surgeons’ groups, and spent years teaching doctors in Ghana and Sierra Leone, building burn units. Outside medicine he loved sailing, owned classic boats, and had a large family.
