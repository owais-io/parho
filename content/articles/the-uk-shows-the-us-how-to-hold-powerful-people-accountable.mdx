The UK shows the US how to keep powerful people honest. In Britain, the press and parties can pressure leaders, so scandals can lead to resignations or court cases. In the US, a strong media bubble, party loyalty, and a court that protects presidents let elite misconduct slip past. The article compares Prince Andrew and Lord Mandelson to Trump and the problems that keep American politicians untouchable. It says the UK’s system of shame and justice can teach America.
