Michael Ma, a new Conservative MP, left his party to join the Liberals, boosting them to 171 seats—just one short of a majority. The move hurts the Tories, who have already lost three members. Liberal leader Mark Carney praised Ma’s decision. Inside the Liberal side, a cabinet minister quit over a controversial oil pipeline plan. The shift shows growing frustration in both parties.
