Suzanne travels back to her birth country every year to see family, but each return feels more painful than the last. Missing everyday moments—birthdays, tea, casual visits—leaves her with a deep, unresolved grief called “ambiguous loss.” Therapists say this can last a lifetime, but by acknowledging both sadness and gratitude, people can create space for both worlds. Suzanne is learning to accept her heart in two places.
