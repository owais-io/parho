New Zealand’s coalition government has passed a bill that would give English the same official status as Māori and New Zealand Sign Language. The move, supported by the National and New Zealand First parties, has sparked criticism from opposition parties and language experts, who say it’s unnecessary and could hurt minority languages. The bill now moves to a public consultation stage before it can become law.
