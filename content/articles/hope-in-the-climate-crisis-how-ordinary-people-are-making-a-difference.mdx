The article explains how, despite climate disasters and slow politics, many ordinary people—activists, Indigenous groups, scientists, and communities—are pushing for real change. They use protests, court cases, and new international rules like the just‑transition mechanism to pressure governments and companies. Countries such as Colombia and the Netherlands are drafting their own fossil‑fuel phase‑out plans, while courts worldwide are holding polluters accountable. The piece stresses that only people’s action and Indigenous wisdom can truly move the climate fight forward.
