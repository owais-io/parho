Keir Starmer wants to bring forward a bill that would let the UK follow EU rules on food, drink and electricity. The law would give ministers the power to change standards before any final deal is signed. MPs could vote on it before the agreement is finished, sparking debate. The Liberal Democrats want a binding vote on a customs union, while critics warn the bill could give ministers too much control. The aim is to boost the UK economy.
