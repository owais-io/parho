France’s main warplane maker, Dassault, warns that the €100bn Future Combat Air System project could collapse if its partner Airbus, backed by Germany and Spain, refuses to cooperate. Dassault wants to lead the program, while Airbus wants a smaller role. The dispute could end the joint effort, which also includes drones and a combat‑communications cloud, forcing Europe to choose between keeping the joint plan or splitting it into separate projects.
