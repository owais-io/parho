Venezuela’s former president Hugo Chávez warned for years that the United States would invade the country. Now, U.S. forces have bombed Caracas and captured President Nicolás Maduro and his wife. The attack was carried out without a United Nations mandate or U.S. congressional approval. Chávez used the threat of a U.S. invasion to keep power and silence opposition. The new U.S. operation is seen as a continuation of past U.S. meddling in Latin America.
