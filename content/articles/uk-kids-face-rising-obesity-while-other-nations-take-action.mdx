Child obesity in the UK is climbing fast, with more than a third of 10‑year‑olds having a high BMI. Other countries are tackling the problem with clever rules: South Korea bans junk food near schools, Denmark sends kids to obesity clinics, and Wales partners with farmers to put cheaper veggies in lunches. The UK is trying similar measures, but schools still struggle to give healthy meals because of tight budgets and a focus on basic food provision.
