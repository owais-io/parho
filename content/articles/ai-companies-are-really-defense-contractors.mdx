AI companies have become part of the military’s kill chain, creating lists of people to hit in conflicts like Gaza and Iran. Their algorithms rank who might be a fighter, but they rely on old data and give commanders quick “yes” approvals. This removes clear responsibility, lets children be hit, and breaks international law. The article calls for treating these firms like defense contractors and regulating them.
