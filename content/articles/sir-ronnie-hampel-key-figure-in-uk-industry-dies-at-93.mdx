Sir Ronnie Hampel, who helped turn Britain’s biggest chemical firm ICI into the pharmaceutical giant AstraZeneca, died at 93. He joined ICI in 1955 and rose to chair the company, steering it through major changes and a split that created Zeneca. Hampel also led a government committee on corporate governance and chaired United News. Known for his golf links and sharp business sense, he left a lasting mark on UK industry.
