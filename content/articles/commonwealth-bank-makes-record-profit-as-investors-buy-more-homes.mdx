The Commonwealth Bank made a record $5.45 bn cash profit in the first half of the year. Investors now own 43 % of new home loans, up from 37 % two years ago, while loans for people buying homes for themselves are falling. The bank’s shares jumped over 7 %, and it announced a $2.35 bn interim dividend. Workers are worried about jobs and automation, and the Reserve Bank said the loan boom was bigger than expected.
