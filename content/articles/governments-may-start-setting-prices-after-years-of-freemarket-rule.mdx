After decades of free‑market rules, governments are now looking at setting price caps on essentials like energy, food and rent to curb inflation. Mexico’s president and Spain’s government already cap prices, keeping voters happy. In Britain, politicians argue that the privatised system lets firms hike prices too high. The debate is heating up, with calls for nationalisation and tighter state control to protect everyday living costs.
