Thousands of Australians plan to march against Israeli President Isaac Herzog’s visit, sparking questions about protest rights. New South Wales has a law that limits marches in certain areas, a rule now being challenged in court after the Bondi terror attack. Protesters normally don't need a permit, but the law requires notice and can let police give a move‑on order if safety or traffic is threatened. Courts can strike down laws that infringe the implied right to political communication.
