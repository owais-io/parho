Donald Trump’s US forces recently abducted Venezuelan president Nicolás Maduro, sparking global debate. The operation shows a shift away from a rules‑based world order toward U.S. action in its own hemisphere. Analysts say the real motive may be Venezuela’s oil. The move forces allies and opponents to rethink strategy and could reshape regional politics for years. International bodies warn it risks undermining diplomatic norms. The fallout will likely influence elections and trade deals.
