Nick Clegg, the former deputy prime minister, has become a general partner at London-based venture capital firm Hiro Capital. The firm, which focuses on tech such as gaming, AI, robotics and space, plans to raise more than €500 million for its new fund. Clegg says Europe should rely on its own ideas, not just U.S. tech. The firm also hired AI expert Yann LeCun as an adviser. This move follows a trend of ex‑politicians moving into big business roles.
