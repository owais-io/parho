After a deadly attack on Bondi beach that killed 16 people, thousands of Sydneysiders rushed to blood‑donation centers. Over 20,000 Australians booked appointments, and lines stretched for up to four hours at Lifeblood’s Sydney hub. Donors shared why they came: to help victims and show solidarity. The agency welcomed the surge, plans to broaden eligibility, and the state premier praised the community’s self‑less response.
