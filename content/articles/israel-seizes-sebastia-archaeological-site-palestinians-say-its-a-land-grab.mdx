Israel announced it will take 182 hectares of an archaeological hill near Sebastia in the West Bank for a new museum and visitor centre. Palestinians, who earn money from the site and their olive groves, say the move will cut them off and is part of a wider push to annex land. Critics claim the project is political, not historical, and that it violates international rules and UNESCO heritage plans.
