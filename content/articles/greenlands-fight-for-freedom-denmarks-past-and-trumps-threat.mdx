Greenland tells two stories. The Danish view says Denmark helped it build schools, health care and a parliament, giving more control. The Greenlandic view says Denmark still treats Greenlanders like a colony, citing past abuses and racism. Greenland gets €600 m a year from Denmark, but some argue it can’t solve its own problems. Trump has used anti‑colonial rhetoric to push Greenland between staying with Denmark or risking American takeover, which many fear could harm its rights and security.
