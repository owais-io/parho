South Korea has turned gaming into a serious industry, after moving from calling it an addiction to treating it as a job. Pro gamer Lehends grew up in PC‑bangs and now earns six‑figure salaries. The country’s market is worth over $11 bn and exports almost $6 bn. Schools, corporate sponsors and government support help players, but only about 1–2 % become pros. The story shows how hard training and policy made gaming a national pride.
