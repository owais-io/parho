New York's gaming board has approved three new casinos in Queens and the Bronx, saying they will bring jobs and billions in tax money. But many locals and researchers worry the money won’t be new, the jobs are temporary, and the venues could push more people into gambling addiction. Studies from other cities show casinos rarely boost tax revenue or lasting jobs. The decision has sparked heated debate over whether the promised benefits will actually appear.
