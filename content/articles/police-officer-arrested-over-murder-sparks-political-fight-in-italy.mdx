An Italian police officer, Carmelo Cinturrino, was arrested after killing a 28‑year‑old Moroccan man, Abderrahim Mansouri, during a drugs patrol in Milan. The officer claimed self‑defence, but witnesses and video show Mansouri was unarmed and trying to escape. The case has turned into a political firestorm: the ruling Meloni government wants a law protecting officers in self‑defence, while opposition parties accuse the government of politicising the murder to boost support, adding to debates about judicial reforms.
