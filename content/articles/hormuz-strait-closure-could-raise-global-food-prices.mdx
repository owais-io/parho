The shutdown of the Strait of Hormuz, where 25‑30% of global fertilizer trade passes, could block ammonia and nitrogen shipments. That would make synthetic fertilizer more scarce, driving up prices for staples like bread and pasta. Farmers already feel the squeeze, with lower yields possible if fertilizer is limited. The risk could hit food security and push household costs higher worldwide.
