At the Smithsonian National Zoo in Washington, DC, a female Asian elephant calf was born – the first baby in almost 25 years. The 12‑year‑old mother, Nhi Linh, and 44‑year‑old father, Spike, produced a 308‑lb baby at 1.15 a.m. The new calf boosts genetic diversity for Asian elephants in zoos. Visitors can vote on a name by donating $5. The baby will bond with her mother for a month before debuting to the public.
