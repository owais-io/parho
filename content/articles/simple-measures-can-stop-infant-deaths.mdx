UNICEF’s latest child‑mortality report shows that most deaths of children under five happen in sub‑Saharan Africa and South Asia, and most are preventable. The main gaps are in basic care – delays in deciding to seek help, getting to a health facility, and receiving timely treatment. Pakistan’s focus on reliable facilities, enough staff and medicines cut infant deaths by 35% in five years. The message is clear: no miracle is needed – just proper, everyday care.
