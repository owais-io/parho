After a gunman killed two U.S. National Guard soldiers and a civilian interpreter in Palmyra, the U.S. military carried out a big airstrike campaign in central Syria, hitting more than 70 Islamic State targets. Jordanian jets helped. The U.S. and the new Syrian government say the strikes were coordinated and will keep going. President Trump and defense officials said the U.S. will not let the attackers get away. The attacks are part of a broader effort to root out ISIS.
