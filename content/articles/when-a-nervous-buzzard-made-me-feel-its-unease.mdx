The piece begins with the author watching a tiny long‑tailed tit hop among hawthorn branches, then shifts to a large buzzard trying to land on a tree. The buzzard struggles, awkwardly positioning its wings, eventually giving up and flying away. Watching the bird's nervousness, the narrator feels a surge of empathy, realizing that even simple creatures experience unease and the need to find balance.
