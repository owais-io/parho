UK science officials have cut funding for theoretical particle physics by almost 70% for 2026‑2030, leaving less than 20 postdocs per year. Professor Brian Cox and other scientists warn this will cripple the country’s ability to study the Higgs boson, dark matter and black holes, and could close departments. The cuts came even though the overall science budget has risen, and scientists worldwide have urged the government to reverse the decision.
