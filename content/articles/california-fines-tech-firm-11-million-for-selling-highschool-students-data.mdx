California’s privacy agency hit GoFan, a ticket‑selling arm of PlayOn, with a $1.1 million fine for collecting and selling data from students who signed up for school events. The company forced users to click “agree” without giving an opt‑out option, violating the state law that requires at least two ways to refuse data sales. GoFan also misled users in its privacy policy, saying it didn’t sell data.
