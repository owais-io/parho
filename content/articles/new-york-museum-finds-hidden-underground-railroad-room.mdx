Archaeologists at the Merchant’s House Museum in Manhattan uncovered a 2‑by‑2‑foot hidden room with a ladder that likely served as a secret escape route for enslaved people during the 1800s. The space suggests the house was a safe house on the Underground Railroad, built by an abolitionist. The find highlights a rare piece of New York’s anti‑slavery history and underscores the museum’s need for preservation funding.
