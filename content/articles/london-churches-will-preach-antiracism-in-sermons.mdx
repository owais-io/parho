Church Commissioners are giving £730,000 to London’s 400‑plus parishes to push anti‑racism in sermons and classrooms. The money is part of a three‑year Racial Justice Priority, separate from a £100m fund for the church’s slave‑era history. Bishops and leaders say the plan will boost diversity in leadership, help refugees, and teach about the church’s links to slavery. It aims to make all feel welcome.
