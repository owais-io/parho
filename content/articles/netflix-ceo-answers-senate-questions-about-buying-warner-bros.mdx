Netflix's co‑CEO, Ted Sarandos, was grilled by U.S. senators about the company’s $82.7 billion purchase of Warner Bros Discovery. Senators asked if the deal would hurt jobs, raise streaming prices, limit content, or give Netflix too much power, especially over HBO Max. Sarandos said the merger would create jobs, keep Warner Bros running as it is, and offer more content for less money. He also defended Netflix’s programming as diverse and non‑political.
