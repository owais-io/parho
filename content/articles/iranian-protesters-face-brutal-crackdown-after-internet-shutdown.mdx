After a sudden currency drop, crowds marched across Iran demanding better living conditions and a return to the old monarchy. The government shut down the internet, cut phone lines, and then sent police and soldiers into the streets, shooting at unarmed protesters. Dozens of people were killed, thousands arrested, and hospitals raided. The opposition, including the Shah’s son, urged united protests, while the U.S. threatened military action, further hardening the regime’s response. The protests kept going, but the people were cut off from the outside world.
