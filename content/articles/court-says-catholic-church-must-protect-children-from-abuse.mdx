An Australian high court ruled that the Catholic Church’s Newcastle and Maitland diocese had a duty to protect a 13‑year‑old from a priest who abused him in 1969. The court said the church couldn’t shift the responsibility away and that its failure to stop the abuse was a breach of duty. The decision is a big win for abuse survivors and may let more people sue the church for similar harm.
