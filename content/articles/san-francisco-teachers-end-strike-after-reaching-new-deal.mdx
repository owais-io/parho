San Francisco teachers walked out last week over pay and benefits, the city’s first teachers strike in almost 50 years. After two days of school closures, the district and the United Educators of San Francisco reached a tentative contract. The deal gives teachers a 6 % wage increase over three years, better health‑care coverage options and new protections for special‑needs and immigrant students. Schools will reopen next week.
