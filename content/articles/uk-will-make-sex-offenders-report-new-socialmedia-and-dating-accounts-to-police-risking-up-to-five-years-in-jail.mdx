The UK government will require convicted sex offenders to tell the police about any new dating or social‑media accounts, and also about job changes. If they don’t, they could face up to five years in jail. The move aims to let police track offenders online and stop repeat attacks. Police will get more power to impose internet restrictions and use new online‑offence officers. The policy is part of a wider plan to cut violence against women and give better support to survivors.
