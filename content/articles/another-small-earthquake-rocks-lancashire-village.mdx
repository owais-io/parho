A small village in Lancashire, Silverdale, felt another earthquake earlier this week, just a few weeks after a larger quake that shook the area. The new tremor was a 2.5‑magnitude quake that rattled houses and radiators. Nobody was hurt and there was no damage. Scientists say it was an aftershock of the first quake. Residents described the shaking as a quick jolt, a bang, or a rumbling, but it was short and gentle.
