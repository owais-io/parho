Traveling to Cartagena, I saw groups of women from San Basilio de Palenque, the first free Black town in the Americas. They sell fruit and sweets to tourists, keeping their language and traditions alive. The city’s past as a slave port clashes with its tourist vibe, and the women’s stories remind us how the legacy of slavery still shapes lives today. Their culture feels like a living “small Africa” in Colombia.
