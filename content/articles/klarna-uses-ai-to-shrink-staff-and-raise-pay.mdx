Swedish buy‑now‑pay‑later firm Klarna says its AI tools helped it cut its staff in half and raise salaries. In the last three years the workforce fell from 5,527 to 2,907, with many roles now done by software instead of people. Revenues doubled, costs stayed flat, and average pay jumped 60% to about $203,000. The CEO plans more cuts to keep revenue per employee high, while warning about costly data‑centre spend.
