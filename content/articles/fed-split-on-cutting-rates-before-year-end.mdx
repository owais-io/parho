The U.S. Federal Reserve is holding a key meeting with a split opinion on whether to cut rates again before the year ends. Data gaps from a recent government shutdown mean officials have less information to decide. While some members want a third cut this year, others are cautious, noting rising inflation and unemployment. Markets expect a cut, but it’s unclear if enough Fed members will agree.
