South Australia’s election shows a big shift: One Nation grabbed over 20% of the vote, beating the Liberals and threatening their survival. The Liberals may end up with only 4‑7 seats, while One Nation could win a few. The result also shows a record 42% for non‑major parties and a loss of confidence in both major parties. Voters feel frustrated, pushing for more political disruptors and raising questions about the Liberals’ future.
