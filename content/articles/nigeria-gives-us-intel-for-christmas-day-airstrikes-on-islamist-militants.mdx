Nigeria told the U.S. that it shared information about suspected Islamist fighters before the U.S. launched airstrikes on Christmas Day in Sokoto state. Nigerian officials said the strikes were part of a joint effort to fight terrorism and were not aimed at any particular religion. U.S. lawmakers praised the action, while some locals heard loud explosions but reported no major casualties. The moves follow months of U.S. pressure on Nigeria over violence that hurts Christians and Muslims alike.
