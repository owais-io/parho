The Treasury has chosen March 3 for its spring statement, hoping to give businesses and families certainty after a year of budget rumours that dented spending and investment. Treasury officials want the Office for Budget Responsibility to deliver clear forecasts, while chancellor Rachel Reeves has doubled the safety cushion to £22 bn to guard against future shocks. The move comes as the economy shrank, bond prices rose, and firms are holding back on hiring and new projects.
