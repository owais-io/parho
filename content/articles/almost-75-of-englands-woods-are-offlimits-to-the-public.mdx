Study by Forest Research found that 73 % of English woodland is off‑limits, with over a third of ancient trees hidden from visitors. Most closed lands are used for timber, pheasant shooting, or private estates. Campaigners want a right‑to‑roam bill and plan mass trespasses to pressure the government. The new policy aims to create more accessible green spaces, but critics warn it may cut habitats and worsen health gaps.
