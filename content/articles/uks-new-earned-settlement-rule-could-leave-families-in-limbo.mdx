The UK government is tightening its immigration rules, requiring anyone living with a British citizen to prove a set income before gaining permanent residency. Felix, an IT worker, and his American wife, Tessa, must show £12,570 a year for 3‑5 years to qualify for settled status. The rule could delay or deny them residency, make adoption harder, and add steep visa costs. Same‑sex couples feel the impact most, fearing they can’t start a family in Britain.
