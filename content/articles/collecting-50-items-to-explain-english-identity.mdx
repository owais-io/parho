A new project called A Very English Chat asks people to send in objects, foods, music, or memories that they feel capture what it means to be English. The goal is to gather 50 examples that show the country’s diverse culture, instead of debating things like the St. George’s flag. Supported by politicians, musicians and a law professor, the collection will become cards, posters and other items for St. George’s Day, hoping to spark inclusive conversations about identity.
