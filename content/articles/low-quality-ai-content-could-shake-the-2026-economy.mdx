AI is spreading fast, but much of it is low‑quality “slop,” a term Merriam‑Webster chose for 2025. Companies are spending huge amounts—$400 bn in 2025 alone—to build data centres and run models, yet they’re not yet profitable. Critics warn the debt and hype could lead to a market correction. If big‑tech prices fall, the hit will ripple worldwide, even hurting UK growth and public finances.
