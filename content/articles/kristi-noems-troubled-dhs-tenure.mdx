Kristi Noem was chosen by Donald Trump as the U.S. homeland security secretary in 2024. Her hard‑line approach to immigration sparked raids, mass deportations and a string of scandals. She rescinded temporary protections for hundreds of thousands of migrants, faced lawsuits over secret detentions, and was criticized for using taxpayer money to promote herself. In 2026, ICE officers killed two U.S. citizens in Minneapolis; Noem’s defense led to protests and investigations. Trump fired her in March 2026.
