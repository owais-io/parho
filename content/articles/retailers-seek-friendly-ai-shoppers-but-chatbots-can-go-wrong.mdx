Retail companies want chatbots that feel friendly and can plan shopping, meals and parties. But the first trials have gone wrong – Woolworths’ Olive told customers about its family, Bunnings’ bot gave illegal electrical advice and Air Canada’s assistant promised a refund that didn't exist. Experts say the new “agentic” bots learn on their own, making them riskier. Businesses must add strict rules before customers see them.
