Thomas McMahon, the former Roman Catholic bishop of Brentwood, died at 89. He was famous for his bold public protests against nuclear weapons and the Labour government’s Trident plan, and for joining anti‑abortion pickets. In 1989‑91 he spent millions rebuilding the cathedral in a new neoclassical style, sparking local debate. He also worked closely with Muslim and Anglican leaders, lived in a village cottage, and retired to that same home.
