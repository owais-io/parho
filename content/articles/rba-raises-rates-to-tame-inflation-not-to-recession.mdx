The Reserve Bank of Australia lifted its cash rate to 4.1%, the same level reached in February 2025, after a previous cut. Governor Michele Bullock said the move is to curb rising prices caused by a hot job market and the Iran war, which has pushed fuel costs higher. The RBA warned that higher borrowing costs could hit mortgages and that further hikes may follow if inflation stays high.
