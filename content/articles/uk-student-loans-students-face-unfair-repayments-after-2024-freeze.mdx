UK students are upset after the government froze the student‑loan repayment threshold for eight years, meaning people with lower earnings will start repaying earlier. More than 5 million borrowers on “Plan 2” are already struggling, and critics say the system mis‑sold the promise that thresholds would rise with wages. The Treasury plans a review, and many say the UK should adopt kinder rules similar to the US, where interest stops growing while you pay.
