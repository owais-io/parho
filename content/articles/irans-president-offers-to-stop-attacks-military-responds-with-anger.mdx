Iran's president Masoud Pezeshkian said the country would stop firing at neighboring states as long as their airspace and U.S. bases aren’t used against Iran. The offer sparked angry reactions inside Iran, with the armed forces and some politicians saying they had already attacked Gulf countries and would keep striking U.S. bases. The move has also raised calls for a new Supreme Leader, as power struggles deepen amid the ongoing conflict.
