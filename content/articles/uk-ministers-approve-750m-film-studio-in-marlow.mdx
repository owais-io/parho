UK ministers have green‑lit a £750m film and TV studio plan in Marlow, west of London, after a national review. The complex, backed by famous directors like James Cameron and Sam Mendes, will have 18 sound stages, a skills academy and create about 4,000 jobs. Local residents and a green‑belt campaigner opposed the project, saying it would damage the countryside, but the government said the economic benefits outweigh the concerns.
