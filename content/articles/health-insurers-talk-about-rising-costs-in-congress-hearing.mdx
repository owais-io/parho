Five big health‑insurance companies sat before a House committee to explain why healthcare costs are climbing. UnitedHealth, CVS Health, Elevance, Cigna and Anthem said the market is dominated by a few giants that lack transparency. UnitedHealth pledged to give back profits from its ACA plans to customers. Lawmakers asked how insurance sets drug prices and whether the industry should break up its corporate ties. The hearing is the start of a series to find ways to cut costs.
