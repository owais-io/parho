Keir Starmer, the UK Labour leader, is said to keep shrinking his promises and policy plans after becoming prime minister. The article says many of his big pledges – a green deal, ending leasehold, childcare reforms – are being watered down or dropped. It also criticises his team for being full of lobbyists and for silencing left‑wing voices. The writer argues that Starmer’s approach is turning the party into a narrow right‑leaning faction that may hurt its future.
