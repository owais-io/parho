AA, Britain’s biggest roadside help company, might sell for about £5 billion or put itself on the London stock market. Its owners—TowerBrook, Warburg Pincus and Stonepeak—are exploring options after improving its finances. The rival RAC, owned by CVC, GIC and Silver Lake, is also eyeing a London listing at a similar valuation. Both companies have turned profits after debt cuts and have millions of customers.
