American politics feels broken and out of touch with ordinary people. A new study shows that candidates who grew up in the union movement connect better with working‑class voters, push pro‑worker bills, and get results. But less than 5% of congressional hopefuls have union ties. Funding and job protection are the main hurdles. Programs in New Jersey and Alaska already win most of the seats they run, proving that a small investment could change the political game.
