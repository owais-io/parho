Prosecutors have charged Budapest mayor Gergely Karácsony for organising a large Pride protest after the government banned the event. He told the police he was running a city‑run rally, but the ruling party said it was illegal. He could face a fine and no trial. The case comes before Hungary’s next election, raising concerns about free speech and LGBTQ+ rights.
