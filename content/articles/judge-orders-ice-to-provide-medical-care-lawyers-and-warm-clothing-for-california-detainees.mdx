A federal judge told the U.S. to give people held at a California ICE detention center better medical care, medication, lawyers, and warm clothes. The order also requires blankets, outdoor time, and a third‑party monitor to check the conditions. The decision follows a lawsuit by seven detainees who said they were denied medicines, food, and clean housing. The center is run by CoreCivic and has been called a torture chamber.
