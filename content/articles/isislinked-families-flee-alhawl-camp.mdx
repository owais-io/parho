After the Syrian government took control of al‑Hawl, thousands of foreign women and children—many suspected of ties to ISIS—escaped the camp. Most went to Idlib, but no clear records show whether they were released or ran away. Humanitarian groups warn that the chaos could let former ISIS members regroup and that the families face danger, trafficking, or new recruitment.
