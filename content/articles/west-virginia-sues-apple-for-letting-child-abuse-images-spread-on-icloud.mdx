West Virginia’s attorney general has sued Apple, saying the company let child sexual‑abuse pictures be stored and shared on iCloud because it didn’t scan uploads. The state wants Apple to add better detection tools and face damages. Apple says it protects privacy and is already working on safety features. The suit is the first of its kind from a state, joining other complaints that Apple shares far fewer abuse reports than rivals.
