In Bodega Bay, California, a UC Davis lab is working to bring the endangered white abalone back to the ocean. Scientists mix the snails in special tanks, add a little “love potion” to help them release eggs and sperm, and then raise the babies until they’re ready for release. After a decade of overfishing, only 2,000 snails survived, but the lab has already released more than 20,000. Funding was nearly cut last year, but private donors and a new federal grant have kept the program going. The work shows that science and community support can rescue a species.
