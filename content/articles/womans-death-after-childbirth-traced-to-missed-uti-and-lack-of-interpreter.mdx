Gia Lam, a 32‑year‑old Vietnamese‑Australian, died from sepsis three days after giving birth in Sydney. Doctors missed a urinary tract infection that could have been treated. She also had trouble speaking with nurses because no Vietnamese interpreter was provided. The coroner said better language support and quicker treatment could have saved her life. Hospital policies have since changed to ensure interpreters are used.
