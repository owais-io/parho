Protests across Iran have exploded over a crashing currency and high inflation, turning into nationwide anti‑government rallies. The government has shut down the internet, used force and arrested thousands, with estimates of 6,000+ deaths. Despite the crackdown, people keep protesting, chanting for change. Leaders blame foreign enemies and plan to stay in power. The regime may survive, but it will become more repressive and less responsive. The protests show Iran’s people won’t stay silent.
