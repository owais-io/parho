UK government has promised big AI data centers to boost the economy, but projects are falling behind. Deals with tech giants like OpenAI and Oracle hit funding and schedule snags, and the planned Essex centre is still just a scaffolding site. Politicians are pushing for ‘sovereign AI’ while the real work—building powerful chip‑powered facilities—lags. This may slow Britain’s AI growth and add to worries that the AI boom could collapse.
