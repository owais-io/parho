The war over Iran has hit Gulf‑state sports hard. Qatar’s airspace is closed, forcing its biggest club president to miss PSG games and cancel events like the Bahrain and Saudi Formula One races and a match between Argentina and Spain. More than 100 events across the Gulf have been cancelled. The region’s push to grow sport as a new business is now fragile because many projects rely on foreign workers and outside investors, and the war threatens future funding.
