Councilmember Nithya Raman announced she’s running for Los Angeles mayor on Saturday, just before the filing deadline. She said the city has lost its chance for opportunity, blaming rising housing costs, a broken homelessness system, unsafe streets, and slow new housing construction. Raman, known for rent‑stabilization work, wants a mayor who will hold departments accountable and boost affordable homes. Her entry could challenge incumbent Karen Bass, who faces criticism over wildfire response and other city problems.
