President Trump warned that nations not supporting his plan to take over Greenland could face a 25‑percent tariff on imports. He made the threat after NATO troops moved to the Danish‑owned island and a U.S. congressional delegation visited Copenhagen. Denmark and Greenland leaders said they would not hand over the territory, and many Greenlanders are anxious about U.S. intentions. The move raises tensions between the U.S. and its European allies.
