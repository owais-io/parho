Omer Al Tijani’s new book, The Sudanese Kitchen, gathers recipes from across Sudan, a country torn by war and mass displacement. The cookbook shows how food ties to identity, especially during Ramadan, when families gather to break fast. For Al Tijani, the book is a form of resistance, keeping Sudan’s culture alive while the country faces conflict and famine. Readers see how ordinary dishes become powerful symbols of hope.
