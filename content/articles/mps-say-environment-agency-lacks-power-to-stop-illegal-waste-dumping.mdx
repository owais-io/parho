MPs have warned that the Environment Agency lacks the power and intelligence to stop a growing wave of illegal waste sites—there are about 8,000 dumps across the UK, costing taxpayers £1 billion a year. The public‑accounts committee found the agency can’t act fast enough, doesn’t share data with police or local councils, and has limited enforcement tools. It calls for tighter cooperation, more staff, better tech and quicker responses. Defra says reforms and a £100 million investment are underway.
