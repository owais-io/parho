UK researchers using the country’s biggest health study, UK Biobank, accidentally posted huge data sets on the internet. The files contain millions of hospital diagnoses, dates and basic birth details for hundreds of thousands of volunteers. Even without names, the data can be cross‑checked to reveal private medical history, sparking worries about privacy. Biobank has issued takedown notices and added training, but many files still circulate online.
