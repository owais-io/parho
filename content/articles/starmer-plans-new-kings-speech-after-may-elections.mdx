After local and national elections in May, Labour leader Keir Starmer is preparing a new King’s Speech to reset the government’s agenda. He hopes the speech will keep MPs in line and push new laws through Parliament. The speech will cover bills on assisted dying, AI, immigration and more. It comes amid possible losses for Labour and rising criticism of Starmer’s leadership, making the speech a high‑stakes confidence vote.
