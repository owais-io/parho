Aston Martin is laying off about 20% of its workforce, roughly 170 jobs, to cut costs and save around £40 million. The luxury carmaker reported larger losses in 2025, with a pre‑tax loss of £363.9 million, up from £289.1 million the previous year. The cuts follow earlier redundancies and come as the company struggles with weak demand, U.S. tariffs and a tough Chinese market.
