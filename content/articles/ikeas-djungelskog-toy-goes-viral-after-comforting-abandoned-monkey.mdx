Ikea’s Djungelskog plush orangutan has become a viral sensation after zookeepers gave it to Punch, an abandoned monkey at a Japanese zoo who clings to the toy for comfort. The story sparked a surge in sales in Australia, with nearly 1,000 units sold online and in stores in a week. A reader raced from Sydney to get one, and despite the hype, the author reflects on the monkey’s loneliness and animal welfare concerns.
