U.S. farmers are struggling as fertilizer prices have doubled after Iran shut the Hormuz Strait, cutting a key shipping lane. The spike makes growing corn and soybeans much more expensive, wiping out profits that have already been thin for years. Farmers may shift to cheaper crops or cut fertilizer, but that could hurt yields and food prices. The crisis could also influence politics and farmers’ future.
