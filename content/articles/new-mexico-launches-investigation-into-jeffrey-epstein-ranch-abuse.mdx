New Mexico has created a truth‑commission to look into claims that Jeffrey Epstein’s Zorro Ranch was used for sexual abuse of girls. The panel will use subpoenas, witness testimony and records to uncover what happened and who knew about it. The effort comes after federal prosecutors said they wouldn’t investigate the ranch, and after a 2023 settlement tied the state to Deutsche Bank over failures to spot abuse. The commission, funded at $2 million, will run until 2026.
