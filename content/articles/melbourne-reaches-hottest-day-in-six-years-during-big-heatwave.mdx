Melbourne hit 41 °C – the hottest it’s been in six years – while Adelaide climbed to 43–47 °C. The heatwave stretched from Western Australia to Tasmania, sparking bushfire warnings and evacuations in Victoria and South Australia. Trains slowed, parks closed and workers were told to stay cool. Health officials warned people to stay hydrated, avoid midday sun and never leave kids in cars. It’s a record‑warm day across the south‑eastern states.
