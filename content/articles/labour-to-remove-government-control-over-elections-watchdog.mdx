The Labour government says it will scrap powers that let ministers set the strategy for the Electoral Commission, the body that keeps elections fair. Critics say those powers could let future governments sway the regulator. The move is part of a new elections bill that also adds votes for 16‑ and 17‑year‑olds and eases ID rules. MPs from several parties welcome the change, seeing it as a step toward a truly independent watchdog.
