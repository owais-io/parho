In a quiet gorge beneath an Essex housing estate, a Victorian tramway pit hides a treasure trove of Ice Age fossils. Scientists have found giant bison, bear, mammoth, and even Neanderthal tools in the old chalk layers. The same place now is littered with a mattress, beer cans and other modern junk. The story shows how ancient history and everyday life can share the same ground.
