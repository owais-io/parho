After two pupils were injured when a student stabbed classmates at Kingsbury High School, Deputy Prime Minister David Lammy announced that every child in England and Wales caught with a knife will receive a mandatory support plan. Local youth justice teams, combining health, schools and community services, will tailor mentoring and skills training to help children move away from crime. The government will spend £320 million over three years to run these programs, warning that refusal leads to criminal charges.
