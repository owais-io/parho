Kemi Badenoch, a UK Conservative minister, faced backlash after saying the main cause of violence against women is foreign cultures and immigrants. She criticized the government’s new strategy, calling it unnecessary and blaming immigration. Human‑rights groups argued her words could make migrant women unsafe and spread racism. The government’s plan still aims to cut such violence in half over ten years, focusing on prevention and support for victims.
