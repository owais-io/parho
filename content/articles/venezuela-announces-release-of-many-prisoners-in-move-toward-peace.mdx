Venezuela says it will release an “important number” of detainees as a gesture for peace, five days after the U.S. seized former president Nicolás Maduro. Human rights groups estimate the country holds 800‑1,000 political prisoners jailed for protesting the 2024 election. The government confirmed the release of five Spanish nationals and plans more. NGOs want all releases to be immediate, unconditioned and fully transparent, and the move is still being closely watched.
