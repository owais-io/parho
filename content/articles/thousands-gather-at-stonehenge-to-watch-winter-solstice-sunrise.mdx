On a cold Sunday morning, thousands of people gathered at Stonehenge to watch the winter solstice sunrise. Visitors came from around the world, wearing costumes and celebrating in different ways – pagans, druids, and even a Zoroastrian family from the U.S. The event drew a mix of ages and cultures, all drawn by the ancient site's power. People touched the stones, felt the energy, and enjoyed the first light of day together.
