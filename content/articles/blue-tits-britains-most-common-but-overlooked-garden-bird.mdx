Blue tits are one of the most common birds in Britain, with about 3 million breeding pairs found in gardens across cities and countryside. They’re always in the top five of UK garden bird surveys and love visiting feeders, competing with larger great tits. Despite their bright plumage, they’re tiny—only 11 g, like a handful of paperclips. They sing simple trills, nest in tree holes or boxes, lay 8‑10 eggs, and parents bring 1,000 tiny meals a day.
