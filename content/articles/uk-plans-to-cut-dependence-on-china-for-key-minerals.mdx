The UK has announced a new plan to cut reliance on China for critical minerals like lithium, tungsten, and rare earths that power batteries, phones, and AI. Prime Minister Keir Starmer said Britain depends too much on a few overseas suppliers, hurting the economy and security. The plan includes a £50m fund to boost mines in Cornwall and limits any single country to 60% of supply by 2035. It also partners with Saudi Arabia to secure more materials.
