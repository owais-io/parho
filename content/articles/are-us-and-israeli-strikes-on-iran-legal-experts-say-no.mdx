The Guardian asks if the United States and Israel’s recent airstrikes on Iran were legal. Legal scholars agree the strikes were probably unlawful because there was no proven imminent threat from Iran. Britain, not part of the attacks, said it would help with defensive action, but experts say its legal footing is shaky. Iran says it can strike back in self‑defence, yet attacks on non‑combatants and neutral countries are illegal.
