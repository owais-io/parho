A senior civil servant at France’s culture ministry, Christian Nègre, is accused of giving hundreds of women coffee or tea laced with a powerful diuretic during job interviews, forcing them to urinate in public. More than 200 women say they suffered humiliation and health problems. A judge is investigating the case, which started after a coworker noticed odd behaviour. The trial is still pending, and victims feel justice is delayed.
