A tiny, critically endangered moth called Arctesthes avatar – named after the movie Avatar to raise awareness of a mining threat – has been chosen as New Zealand’s bug of the year. It beat the giant wētā and other insects with over 5,000 votes. The moth lives only on the Denniston Plateau, which a coal‑mine expansion plans to disturb. The win, organized by conservation group Forest & Bird, spotlights the danger of fast‑tracked mining projects and urges protection.
