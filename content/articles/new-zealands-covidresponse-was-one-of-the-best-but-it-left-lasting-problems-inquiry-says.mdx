A royal commission report says New Zealand handled the pandemic better than most countries, keeping deaths low and using lockdowns, mandates, and testing wisely. However, the review notes the shift from a strict elimination plan to a more relaxed strategy was slow, and vaccine rules caused frustration and economic harm. It also found the government didn’t always react quickly to new variants and missed chances to keep people more engaged. The report offers 24 lessons for any future outbreak.
