A 53‑year‑old woman from south London, Aisha Jung, was arrested at a Trafalgar Square protest for holding a sign calling Israel an apartheid state. She was taken to a police station, searched, and held overnight. After months of anxiety, the Metropolitan Police agreed to pay her £7,500 for false imprisonment, assault, and misconduct. The case highlights concerns about heavy‑handed policing of Gaza protests and the need for clearer rules.
