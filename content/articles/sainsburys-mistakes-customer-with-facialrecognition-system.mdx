At a Sainsbury’s in Elephant and Castle, a customer named Warren Rajah was asked to leave after staff mistakenly flagged him as a suspect using Facewatch’s facial‑recognition software. He had to prove his innocence by sending a photo and passport, even though Facewatch confirmed he wasn’t in their database. Sainsbury’s blamed the staff, Facewatch blamed the retailer, and the man felt the system was unfair and invasive.
