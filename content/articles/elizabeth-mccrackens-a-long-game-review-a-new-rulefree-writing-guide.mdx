Elizabeth McCracken’s new book “A Long Game” shakes up writing advice. She scoffs at rule‑based craft manuals and workshop clichés like “show, don’t tell.” Instead, she champions a playful, rule‑free approach that lets writers embrace oddity and self‑critical humor. The book mixes quick quips, memoir moments, and a no‑pretense style that aims to free writers from fear and let them write with a mischievous attitude.
