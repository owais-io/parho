Australia’s government says there’s no need to panic‑buy petrol because the Iran war hasn’t threatened the country’s fuel supplies. Australia imports most of its fuel and relies on a small domestic refining industry, but the government keeps a safety stock of petrol, diesel and jet fuel—about 36, 34, and 32 days of supply, respectively. Experts say the reserves could cover several weeks even if supply were cut, but debates remain about how much insurance we need.
