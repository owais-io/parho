Starting March 31, a $286 m federal program that gave free birth‑control, condoms and STD tests to low‑income people may stop working. The Department of Health and Human Services hasn’t finished the paperwork to keep the money flowing, so clinics could lose funding, cut hours, and lay off staff. Congress is asking for a one‑year extension, but the chaos could leave millions without essential care.
