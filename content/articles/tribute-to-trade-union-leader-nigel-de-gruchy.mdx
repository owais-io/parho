Nigel de Gruchy was a prominent British trade unionist who championed workers' rights and fought for public education. He backed the Campaign for State Education in the 1990s, helping raise funds to oppose the 11‑plus exam. Though he personally supported school selection, he followed union policy to oppose it publicly. Colleagues remembered his sharp wit, his insistence on keeping members informed, and his lively presence at union events. His legacy is a mix of fierce advocacy and firm leadership.
