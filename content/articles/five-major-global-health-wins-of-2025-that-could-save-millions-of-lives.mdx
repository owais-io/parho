In 2025, global health saw big wins. HPV shots hit a target of 86 million girls vaccinated ahead of schedule, cutting cervical‑cancer deaths. A new malaria medicine, GanLum, could cure 99% of cases and beat drug resistance. Three African islands and many Pacific nations officially ended measles, and a twice‑annual HIV shot called Lenacapavir could stop most new infections. Tuberculosis research made progress with new vaccines, simple tests, and a stronger antibiotic, sorfequiline.
