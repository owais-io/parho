Sunak, the UK chancellor in March 2020, said he feared investors were worried the country couldn’t pay its debts. He watched bond rates spike and the market wobble, so the government set up a backup line of credit with the Bank of England – a plan they never used. QE helped keep rates low. He also pushed the furlough scheme to stop mass job losses, admitting the choices were hard and no science was perfect.
