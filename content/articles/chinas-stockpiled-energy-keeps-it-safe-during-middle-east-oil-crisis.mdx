China has built huge oil and gas reserves to keep its economy running when Middle East supplies drop. Even as U.S.–Israel tensions shut down the Strait of Hormuz and cut 61% of Middle‑East oil exports, China still gets most of its crude from Iran and has a strategic petroleum reserve of about 1.4 billion barrels. The country’s mix of wind, solar and LNG also helps, but long‑term shortages could still hurt its refineries.
