Indiana lawmakers voted 19-31 against a new congressional map that would have given Republicans two extra seats. The map would split Indianapolis into four districts and divide the north‑west district, giving GOP a clean win in all nine seats. Republican Senator Greg Goode was a key opponent, saying he was following his constituents. President Trump and VP J.D. Vance pushed hard for the change, while Democrats warned that refusing the map could cost the state federal money.
