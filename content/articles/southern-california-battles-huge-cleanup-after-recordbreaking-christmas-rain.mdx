Southern California got its heaviest Christmas rain ever, with some places receiving over 17 inches. The wet weather caused flooding, mudslides, 200+ car crashes, power outages and even a 10‑minute tornado in Boyle Heights. Gov. Newsom and the mayor declared emergencies. Firefighters rescued more than 100 people. While the worst is over, flash floods and mudslides still pose a danger as cleanup continues.
