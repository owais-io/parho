While UK ministers push a “Brexit reset” to strengthen ties with Brussels, a new fight has erupted over the cost of university tuition for EU students. Brussels wants all EU students to pay lower fees, a change that could cost UK universities £140 m a year. UK officials call the demand a “non‑starter,” and the dispute could jeopardise the wider reset talks, including food‑agriculture and emissions agreements.
