The Guardian’s Hope appeal has raised over £350,000 in just two weeks, supporting five charities that unite communities, fight racism and build tolerance. The telethon brought in more than £30,000, with journalists taking calls. The charities run projects like a ‘Walk of Hope’, neighbourhood rebuilding after far‑right riots, and school programs for kids of different faiths. Readers praised the effort and felt empowered to help stop hate. The Guardian’s editor says hope can beat division.
