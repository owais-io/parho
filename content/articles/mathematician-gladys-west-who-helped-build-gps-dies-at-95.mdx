Gladys West, a mathematician who spent decades at the U.S. Naval Weapons Laboratory, helped build the math behind GPS. Born in segregated Virginia, she taught high school before joining the lab in 1956, becoming the second black woman there. She ran complex calculations on early computers, and her 1986 paper supplied the models that let GPS work. Only after her death did her name become public, earning her hall‑of‑fame honors and a BBC 100 Women spot.
