A court in London and Dubai froze up to £1.3 billion in assets and put a travel ban on Paresh Raja, the boss of Market Financial Solutions (MFS). MFS, a mortgage lender that collapsed in February, allegedly used two bridging companies owned by Raja to borrow money from banks and private lenders and then lend it to customers. The case shows how private‑credit deals can slip through regulators and leave banks and hedge funds in debt.
