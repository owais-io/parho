The Nationals plan to triple Australia’s paid parental leave to 18 months and cut HECS debt for mothers. They’ll test ideas from a Page Research Centre report that also suggests income‑splitting, extra childcare payments, and tax cuts for families. The party will talk to regional communities before sending proposals to Parliament. Experts say longer leave boosts child development and national prosperity, and current leave is only 26 weeks, far below the OECD average.
