Many young men dream of becoming online influencers, thinking they can make money while doing nothing. A new Netflix documentary by Louis Theroux shows the dark side of this world, especially the misogynistic manosphere. Creators like Harrison Sullivan flaunt lavish lifestyles, but their lives are repetitive, pressured to keep audiences hooked, and built on provocative content that sells products or scams. The article shows that influencer fame is a trap, not a free‑handed career, and highlights ethical and economic risks.
