After the U.S. launched attacks on Iran, two exile figures—MEK leader Maryam Rajavi and former Shah’s son Reza Pahlavi—are seeking U.S. approval to head a new government. Both have high‑profile American backers, but they lack real support inside Iran. The MEK has a history of violence and was once labeled a terrorist group, while Pahlavi’s influence is limited. U.S. officials remain skeptical about either becoming a realistic choice.
