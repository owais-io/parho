China has asked all sides to keep ships safe in the Strait of Hormuz after US and Israeli missile attacks on Iran shut the waterway. The strait, which carries about 20 % of the world's oil, has seen traffic fall by 80 % and freight rates jump, pushing tanker charters to record highs. The blockage is hurting energy exporters and forcing container ships to detour around the Cape of Good Hope, raising costs worldwide.
