At the Munich Security Conference, US Democrats like Newsom, Ocasio‑Cortez, Gallego, and Whitmer will pressure European leaders to confront President Trump. They argue that trying to appease him hurts Europe’s reputation. European heads are split: Macron wants a tougher stance, while NATO leaders think keeping Trump’s goodwill is key. The debate reflects a wider shift away from the old rules‑based order toward a new, deal‑based global game.
