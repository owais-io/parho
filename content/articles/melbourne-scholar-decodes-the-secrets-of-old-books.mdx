Professor Wallace Kirsop, a 92‑year‑old scholar from Melbourne, runs a massive home library of about 20,000 books. He studies books as physical objects—paper, bindings, markings—to learn history. He has taught for decades, helped start Australian rare‑book societies, and has donated many volumes to libraries. He warns that digital books can’t replace old copies, especially for studying early printing. He even helped uncover a rare Giorgione sketch in a 1497 Dante edition, which now sits online.
