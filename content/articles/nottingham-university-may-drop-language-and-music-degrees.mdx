Nottingham University is planning to close 48 degree courses, including Spanish, French, and music, if its council approves the cuts next week. Staff and students are worried, with more than 15,000 people signing a petition to stop the closures. Critics say the move would turn the East Midlands into a “linguistic desert” and hurt the university’s reputation. The university says financial pressures and lower student demand are the main reasons.
