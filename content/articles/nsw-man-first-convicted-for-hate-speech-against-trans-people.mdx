Thomas Fordham, 27, was sentenced in Sydney to a 12‑month community order after he posted hateful comments on YouTube calling for violence against transgender people. He became the first person in New South Wales to be convicted under the state’s gender‑identity vilification law. The judge noted his multiple mental‑health problems, yet said the comments were an “invitation to genocide.” Fordham pleaded guilty to two charges, and the case highlights the reach of social media hate and new anti‑Nazi measures.
