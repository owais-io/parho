A sudden avalanche on Castle Peak in California’s Sierra Nevada killed eight people, including six women who were backcountry skiers and friends. The 46‑person rescue team battled heavy snow, wind and a risk of more avalanches to reach survivors. Survivors had built tarps and found bodies. The disaster is the fourth deadliest in U.S. history. Investigators are looking into why the group took a risky route.
