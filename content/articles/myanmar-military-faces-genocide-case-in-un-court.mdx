On Monday, the International Court of Justice in The Hague began a case that could hold Myanmar’s military accountable for the 2016‑17 attacks on Rohingya people. The Gambia filed the case, saying the army used violence, rape and burning villages to try to destroy the Rohingya. Survivors will speak, and Myanmar will argue its side from January 16. The case is the first major genocide trial in over ten years and could set a global rule for future accusations.
