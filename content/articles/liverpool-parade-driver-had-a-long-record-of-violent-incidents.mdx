Paul Doyle, a former Royal Marine, crashed into 134 Liverpool FC fans during the May 26 victory parade, killing many and injuring others, including a 7‑month‑old baby. He was sentenced to 21½ years in prison. Court videos show Doyle shouting angrily as he drove. He had a long history of violent behavior, including earlier convictions in the 1990s, and was discharged from the Marines after repeated offences. The incident left many families deeply traumatized.
