A sudden 24‑hour rainstorm in Nairobi dumped 112 mm of water, more than the city’s March average, and triggered severe flooding. Poor drainage and thunderstorms killed at least ten people, while two more died when they were electrocuted. Over 70 cars were trapped. The event follows a moderate‑to‑heavy rainfall warning and shows how Kenya’s two rainy seasons, driven by the ITCZ, can produce extreme weather.
