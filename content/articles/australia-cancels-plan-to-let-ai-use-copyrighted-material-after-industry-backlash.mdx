Australia’s top productivity body has scrapped a plan to let AI companies use copyrighted content for training after a strong backlash from creators and the music industry. The government will wait three years before re‑examining copyright rules. The commission had suggested a “fair dealing” exemption, but the Attorney General stopped it. The decision follows uncertainty over how AI impacts incentives and overseas practices.
