A federal judge has denied Minnesota’s request to shut down the ICE operation in Minneapolis, which has caused two deaths and sparked protests. The state, along with Minneapolis and St. Paul, sued after Renee Good and Alex Pretti were shot by ICE agents. Judge Menendez said the plaintiffs didn’t prove a 10th‑amendment violation, so the 3,000 agents stay. The ruling has angered local leaders and will be followed by nationwide protests.
