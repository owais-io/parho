A Tehran surgeon explains how Iran’s security forces fired live bullets at protesters, turning a hospital into a battlefield. In just a few nights, he estimates more than 5,000 people died. The crackdown cut off phones and the internet, leaving doctors with a flood of gunshot injuries that overwhelmed operating rooms and staff. Patients were scared to come in because the government could seize their names, and many never made it to the hospital.
