The government says it wants to bring more music into schools, citing big benefits for students and the economy. But it also plans to stop paying a £10,000 bursary to people training to be music teachers from the 2026‑27 school year. The letter points out that music is good for students’ wellbeing, teamwork, creativity, and future jobs, yet the funding cut shows mixed priorities.
