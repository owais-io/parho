Carolin Würfel, a writer who lives in Berlin and Istanbul, found a 1965 German language book in an Istanbul flea market. The book was made for Turkish workers who came to Germany in the 1960s to work in factories. She explains that the book’s lessons reveal how Germans treated these workers as temporary “guests” rather than people. The article also looks back at the older ties between Germany and Turkey and how the relationship has changed over time.
