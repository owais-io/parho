In Glasgow this week, 2,000 space leaders gathered at Space‑Comm to discuss the end of the International Space Station and new alliances forming. Russia and China are teaming up for lunar work, while the US, Europe, Canada and Japan plan their own moon programs under Artemis. The focus is on returning to the moon, building bases, and maybe mining resources, but scientists worry about damaging untouched spots. Some see space tech as a climate win, while others question the cost.
