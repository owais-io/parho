Unite's top boss, Sharon Graham, says Chancellor Rachel Reeves must approve a huge defence spending plan or she will be fired. Graham warns that the delay could cost tens of thousands of jobs, including the Leonardo helicopter factory in Yeovil that employs 3,300 people. The government promised to raise military spending to 2.5% of GDP by 2027, but the plan is still delayed and the Treasury questions its cost.
