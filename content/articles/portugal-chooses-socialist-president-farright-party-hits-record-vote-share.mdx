Portugal’s presidential election ended with centre‑left candidate António Seguro winning 66.8% of the vote, beating far‑right challenger André Ventura who secured a record 33.2%. The result came despite storm‑damaged polling, with voters showing confidence in democracy. Cross‑party support helped block a Chega win, while European leaders praised Portugal’s resilience. Ventura’s strong performance highlights the growing influence of the far‑right and politics.
