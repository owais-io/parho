UK government bonds saw higher yields for a second day as worries about the Iran conflict pushed investors to fear rising oil and gas prices, higher inflation, and a slower economic rebound. The market’s confidence in the Bank of England cutting rates next month fell sharply. While the government’s spring forecast hoped to steady borrowing costs, the surge in energy prices and inflation expectations pushed yields up, delaying any interest‑rate relief for the UK.
