Scientists finally spotted the rare Braun’s wrasse in a Western Australian kelp forest after a 17‑year absence. Marine biologist Océane Attlan and colleague Albert Pessarrodona captured two photos during a final dive, confirming the species is still alive. The fish, only 6‑7 cm long, lives in a tiny area near Albany in the Great Southern Reef, a hotspot for unique marine life. The find reduces fears it might have gone extinct amid heatwaves.
