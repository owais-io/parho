Scientists have found that being alone can hurt your brain and body, while hanging out with friends helps you stay healthier and live longer. Ben Rein, a brain researcher, explains how loneliness triggers stress and inflammation, and how social contact releases feel‑good chemicals that fight illness. He warns that online chats lack the real‑life signals our brains crave, and suggests more face‑to‑face meetings, pets, or emojis to boost connection.
