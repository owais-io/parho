Quebec lawmakers have voted to ask Air Canada chief Michael Rousseau to quit after he posted a four‑minute video of condolences in English for families of a fatal New York crash. The video included only two French words and no full French apology, sparking criticism that he didn’t respect Quebec’s language. The motion, backed by all parties, demands a future CEO speak French and calls Rousseau’s apology “too little, too late.”
