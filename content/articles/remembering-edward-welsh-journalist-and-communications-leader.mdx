Edward Welsh, 63, died after a battle with bowel cancer. He began as a reporter for the Sunday Times and the Daily Telegraph before moving into public‑sector communications. After studying history at Bristol and a master's at LSE, he worked at the London Assembly, the Local Government Association, the rail industry and the Nursing and Midwifery Council. He shared his life with partner Tony McLeod, adopted two sons, and loved gardening, music and books. He is survived by family.
