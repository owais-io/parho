In 1976, 18‑year‑old Ingrid LeFebour went surfing on Nias, Indonesia. She fell very ill with malaria, was carried to a clinic, and doctors put her in a morgue because she looked dead. She actually woke up, escaped, and was taken by a ferry back to Sumatra. She survived, returned to Perth, and later appeared at a film screening about Nias. Her story shows how a missing person turned out to be alive, not dead or a headhunter.
