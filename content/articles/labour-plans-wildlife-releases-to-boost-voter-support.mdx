Labour is speeding up plans to release animals like eagles, beavers and pine martens across England before the May election. The move is meant to appeal to nature‑loving voters who feel the government has been bad to the environment. The release has rushed approval and has faced opposition from landowners and some MPs. Critics say the policy is more about political optics than real environmental progress.
