Donald MacIntyre, 76, was a botanist and farmer who made Britain greener by growing and selling native wildflower seeds. He founded Emorsgate Seeds, earned a royal warrant, and helped farmers and parks plant thousands of acres of meadow and grass. He invented the first brush harvester for meadow seeds, which wildlife trusts now use all over the country. Donald also co‑founded Flora Locale and wrote a guide to restoring wild vegetation.
