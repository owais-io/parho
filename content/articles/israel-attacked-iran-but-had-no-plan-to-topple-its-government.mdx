Israel’s air campaign against Iran has hit missiles, factories and security forces, but officials say there was no realistic plan to topple the government. The strikes hoped a popular uprising would follow – a wishful idea that proved unlikely. The war also leaves a stock of enriched uranium in Iran, raising fears it could be used for a nuclear weapon. Both US and Israel are weighing ways to secure or remove the material.
