A big winter storm across the east and mid‑west knocked out thousands of flights. Airports like JFK, Chicago, Atlanta and Charlotte saw delays and cancellations, with FAA grounding planes for safety. Meanwhile a partial government shutdown left TSA workers unpaid, causing longer security lines and extra stress for travelers. Many had to stay overnight at airports, and some flights didn’t leave until Tuesday. The storm hit with heavy snow, high winds and possible tornadoes.
