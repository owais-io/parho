The UK government’s report on biodiversity loss was released three months late and already contains outdated data. Scientists warn that coral reefs are the first tipping point reached, with ice sheets, ocean currents and the Amazon next. The letter criticises the government for not halting investments that drive deforestation or banning harmful imports, and urges urgent action to protect nature and national security.
