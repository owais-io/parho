Danone, the French food giant, has agreed to buy the UK protein‑shake maker Huel for roughly €1 billion (£870 million). Huel, founded in 2015 by Julian Hearn and James Collier, sells plant‑based powders, bars and meals that are fortified with vitamins. The deal will let Huel use Danone’s global distribution and research facilities to grow its “functional nutrition” business. The co‑founder will receive about £400 million, and the sale is expected to give Idris Elba and other investors a big payout.
