At least 14 reporters were taken into custody in Caracas, but 13 were let go and one was sent back to the U.S. Their phones were searched and many of their messages and photos were taken. The arrests followed a U.S. raid that removed President Nicolás Maduro. Venezuelan authorities keep tight limits on foreign reporters and often charge journalists with crimes like terrorism, conspiracy, and spreading false information.
