After Russia invaded Ukraine, people believed the world would stand firmly behind Ukraine. But years later, the international response has been slow, fragmented and often weak. The writer shares her own experiences in Lviv, stories from soldiers and civilians, and the harsh reality of Russia’s attacks on Ukrainian infrastructure and civilians. She urges that Ukraine must remember its early moral clarity and push for full Russian accountability.
