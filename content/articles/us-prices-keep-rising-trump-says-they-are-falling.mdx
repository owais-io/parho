US inflation rose 2.7% in the year to November, a little lower than the 3% seen in September but still above the 3% target. President Trump said prices are falling quickly, but the data shows they are still climbing. Unemployment hit 4.6%, and the Fed has stopped cutting rates. Many Americans blame Trump’s tariffs and the former Biden administration for the high prices. The Federal Reserve is watching the numbers carefully after a government shutdown.
