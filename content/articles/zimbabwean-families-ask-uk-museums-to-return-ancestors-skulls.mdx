Descendants of Zimbabwe freedom fighters are writing to the Natural History Museum and the University of Cambridge to find and return the skulls of their ancestors, who were killed by forces in the 1890s. They believe the institutions hold about 11,800 African human remains, including those of the first chimurenga heroes. The families want a joint taskforce to confirm the skulls’ identity and repatriate them. The museums say they haven’t found any matching remains yet, but are open to research.
