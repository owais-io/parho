Australia began its Winter Olympics journey in 1936 with just one lone athlete. Over the years it has turned a small effort into a medal‑winning powerhouse, especially in freestyle skiing and snowboarding. Strong investment in training sites and government support has helped the country win four golds in 2022, its best Games yet. Still, athletes worry that summer‑sport funding gains may cut winter budgets, threatening future growth.
