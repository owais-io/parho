The US Justice Department has released more than 3 million pages of documents about Jeffrey Epstein’s case. The batch contains videos, photos, emails and other records that were heavily redacted. It shows Epstein’s links to well‑known people, like Elon Musk and former US officials, and includes some new financial details. Survivors say the release puts their names at risk, while the department says it protects victims. The move follows a long wait after the 2024 law.
