A 24‑year‑old California Highway Patrol officer, Angelo Rodriguez, was charged with second‑degree murder after he drove his patrol car at over 130 mph on duty and crashed into a civilian car, causing four people to die. Prosecutors say he didn't turn on lights or sirens, left the scene, and later returned only to find the car on fire. He was fired and faces serious legal consequences.
