A hidden love letter by John Cairncross, a Cambridge spy, was discovered by Tom Brass. It shows Cairncross was gentle and romantic, contrary to his usual prickly reputation. Brass also found the letter tucked in his mother’s copy of Cairncross’s biography, revealing more about her private life. The letter is part of the Love Letters exhibition at the National Archives, which displays historic private correspondence from Tudor times to today.
