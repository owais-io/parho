Keir Starmer has said Labour’s big economic plan won’t be fully finished for years. He backed Chancellor Rachel Reeves’ decision to raise £26bn in taxes and promised a long‑term strategy that includes cutting red tape, reforming welfare and closer ties with Europe. Starmer also defends Reeves against Conservative accusations that she misled voters about why taxes rose, saying the budget was needed to protect the economy.
