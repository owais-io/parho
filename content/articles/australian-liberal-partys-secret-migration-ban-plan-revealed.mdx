A hidden plan from former Liberal leader Sussan Ley’s office would have barred migrants from 13 countries, including parts of the Philippines, after Australia’s worst terror attack. The policy never reached the shadow cabinet and was leaked to the press, sparking criticism from senior Liberals who said they never approved it. New opposition leader Angus Taylor quickly distanced himself, while lawmakers debated the impact on humanitarian and skilled visas.
