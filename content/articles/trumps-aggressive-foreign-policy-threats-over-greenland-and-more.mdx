Donald Trump used a very aggressive tone at the World Economic Forum in Davos, claiming the U.S. should take Greenland from Denmark and criticizing NATO for not helping America enough. He also threatened economic retaliation against the EU, saying he could put high tariffs on wine and champagne, and warned the EU it might hit American whiskey. Trump’s speech highlighted his unpredictable approach to international relations and strained ties with allies.
