The US military stormed into Venezuela, seized President Nicolás Maduro, and Trump said the US would “run” the country and take its oil. This first large‑scale US attack in South America sets a dangerous precedent, echoing past US interventions. The move has shocked the world, but many governments stay quiet out of fear. Trump’s actions show he’s willing to break international rules for oil and power, raising concerns for future U.S. aggression.
