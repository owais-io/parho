Zohran Mamdani, a young Muslim candidate, won the New York mayoral race by beating former governor Andrew Cuomo. He relied on volunteers, donors and a message about housing affordability and economic justice that resonated with voters. His win shows how grassroots campaigning can beat big names, giving Democrats hope for future elections. Some fear Republicans may use him, but many see his victory as a positive sign for progressive politics.
