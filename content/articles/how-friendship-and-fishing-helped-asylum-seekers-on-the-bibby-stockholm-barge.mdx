An exhibition in London shows how a small community in Portland, Dorset, turned the negative story of the Bibby Stockholm barge into one of kindness. The barge, used to house asylum seekers, sparked protests and scary headlines. But local volunteers formed the Portland Global Friendship Group, gave welcome packs, organized gardening, fishing and clean‑ups, and helped men feel at home. Many former barge residents now live in the UK and thank the group for saving them.
