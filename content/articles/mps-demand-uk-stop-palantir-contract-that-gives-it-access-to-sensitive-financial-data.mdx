MPs, led by the Liberal Democrats and the Greens, have called on the UK government to cancel a new contract with US spy‑tech firm Palantir. The deal would let Palantir analyse two years of the Financial Conduct Authority’s internal data to fight crime. Critics worry about security, data misuse, and the UK’s reliance on a Trump‑linked company. They fear the contract could lock the UK into Palantir’s systems.
