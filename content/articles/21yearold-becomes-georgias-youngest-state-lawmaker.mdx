Muhammad Akbar Ali, a 21‑year‑old college graduate, just won a runoff for a Georgia state house seat. He’s the state’s youngest lawmaker, and he says he will use his experience and relationships to work with Republicans. Ali grew up fighting for a mosque in his hometown, then led pro‑choice marches and joined the Young Democrats. He focused on small businesses in a diverse, immigrant‑heavy district, promising Medicaid expansion, lower housing costs and school safety.
