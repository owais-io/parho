Catherine Howard, the Treasury adviser to Rachel Reeves, has decided to leave her role at the end of the year, even though the government wanted her to stay. Her departure has stalled the government’s plan to cut planning rules for big projects. The move comes amid disagreements about how far to deregulate and worries that the changes could hurt EU trade. Some Labour MPs have also urged the prime minister to back off from the most radical reforms.
