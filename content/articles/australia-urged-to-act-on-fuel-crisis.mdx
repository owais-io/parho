Australia’s government is racing to stop fuel shortages and high prices after an oil shock. The IEA urged cuts to car use, lower speed limits and more remote work to curb demand. Parliament is debating odd ideas, from drilling offshore to a gas‑export tax. Labor plans new laws to double the ACCC’s penalties for petrol price‑gouging, while the government looks at using gas exports to secure future oil supplies.
