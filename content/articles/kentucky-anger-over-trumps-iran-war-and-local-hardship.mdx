People in Appalachian Kentucky feel the impact of Trump’s Iran war. Two soldiers from the state were killed, and the war cost the U.S. about $16.5 bn in its first 12 days. Meanwhile, many families are already poor, have seen health and Medicare help cut, and face rising utility prices. Some residents say the money could buy better schools or jobs instead of a costly war. Politicians on both sides criticize Trump, and local churches and politics mix to shape views on the conflict.
