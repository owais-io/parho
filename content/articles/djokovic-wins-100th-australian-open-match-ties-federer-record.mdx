Novak Djokovic opened his Australian Open campaign with a comfortable win over Spain’s Pedro Martínez, marking his 100th victory at the tournament. At 38, he’s chasing a record as the oldest champion and has just tied Roger Federer’s appearance record. Djokovic played with sharp serves and powerful forehands, showing that age hasn’t slowed him. Meanwhile, 40‑year‑old Stan Wawrinka also won, confirming his place as the second‑oldest to beat a top‑ten player at the event.
