On Martin Luther King Jr. Day, parades and speeches across the U.S. celebrated Dr. King’s legacy while drawing sharp focus on today’s racial and social struggles. Activists in Harlem and Washington warned about police and ICE actions, and city leaders spoke about deep inequality. Some events were cancelled because of political pressure, and the National Civil Rights Museum opened for free admission. The day reminded people that the fight for equality is still very much alive.
