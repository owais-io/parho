In Madrid, a building owned by an investment fund began loud construction to pressure 50 tenants into leaving. Noise, power cuts, leaks and even falling ceilings made living impossible. The tenants sued, claiming the work was a deliberate tactic to force them out—known in Spain as ‘acoso inmobiliario’ or housing harassment. A Madrid court is now investigating the case, making it the country’s first legal challenge against such tactics. The fight has drawn national attention and similar cases are emerging across Spain.
