The Dublin Racing Festival at Leopardstown attracted a sold‑out crowd, including many British fans who were drawn by low ticket prices and cheap Ryanair flights. Michael O’Leary of Ryanair praised the event’s value. The festival’s €75 weekend tickets offer two days of top‑grade races in a capital city, making it a cheaper and more convenient alternative to Cheltenham, whose crowds and costs have fallen in recent years. The event’s success shows the festival is quickly rivaling the Arc in popularity.
