Stocks fell the biggest since the Israel‑Iran war started. The Dow slid 450 points, the S&P 500 dropped 1.7%, and Nasdaq fell 2.3%, entering correction territory. Oil prices are high—Brent at about $107 a barrel and U.S. crude near $93—yet President Trump said they haven’t risen as badly as expected and will drop once the conflict ends. He also said talks with Iran are improving and the U.S. will pause further strikes for 10 days.
