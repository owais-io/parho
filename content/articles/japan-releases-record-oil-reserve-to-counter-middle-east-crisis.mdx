Japan is releasing its largest oil reserve dump ever, moving about 80 million barrels (45 days of demand) from state stockpiles after worries that a Middle East conflict could close the Strait of Hormuz and cut oil supplies. The move follows the release of private reserves and aims to keep fuel prices stable. The government is also subsidising gasoline and warning people not to panic‑buy toilet paper, as no real shortage exists.
