Nigel Farage said people can’t remember school bullying from more than 40 years ago. Several former pupils replied that the pain stays for life. They shared stories of hurtful words and racist abuse from teachers and classmates in the 1970s and 60s, showing that memories of bullying do not fade. The letters also remind us that time does not erase the impact of bullying or racism.
