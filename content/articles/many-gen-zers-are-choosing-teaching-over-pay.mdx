A growing number of Gen Z graduates are opting to become teachers, even though pay and conditions are low. Many say they want real connection and purpose after the isolation of the pandemic. Organizations like Teach For America have seen a 43% rise in applicants. New teachers bring fresh ideas, such as social‑emotional learning and tech use, but face burnout, low wages and safety worries. Schools need better pay and support to keep them.
