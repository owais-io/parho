Prince Andrew was appointed in 2001 as the UK’s special representative for international trade and investment, a role that involved lobbying overseas, arranging meetings, and promoting British industry. The job had no salary but covered travel and expenses, costing taxpayers about £4 million over ten years. His work faced scrutiny after links to Kazakhstan and Libya sparked calls for him to step down. He denied wrongdoing amid a recent arrest on misconduct charges.
