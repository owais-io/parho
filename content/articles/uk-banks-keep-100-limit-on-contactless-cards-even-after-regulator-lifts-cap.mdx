Even though the Financial Conduct Authority has removed the £100 cap on contactless card payments, most UK banks still keep that limit in place. Barclays, HSBC, Lloyds, NatWest, Santander, and Nationwide say they'll stay at £100 unless customers ask for a lower amount. Digital banks Monzo, Starling, Revolut haven’t decided yet. Banks can change the limit when they want, but they must warn customers first. The change mainly affects card payments, not phone‑wallet payments like Apple Pay.
