Dozens of people died in a severe winter storm that hit the southern US, and a new cold blast is moving up the East Coast. At least 85 people have died, with many deaths from car crashes, hypothermia and carbon‑molecule poisoning. States in North and South Carolina have declared emergencies, and power outages hit up to 190,000 homes. Tennessee, Louisiana and New York City also reported multiple deaths.
