The FDA has rejected Moderna's new mRNA flu vaccine, saying the trial didn’t give older people the higher‑dose shots that experts recommend. A senior FDA official said the study design was a “brazen failure” and raised ethical concerns. Critics argue the decision is part of an anti‑vaccine bias that could scare companies from developing new vaccines. The move could slow future mRNA flu vaccine progress.
