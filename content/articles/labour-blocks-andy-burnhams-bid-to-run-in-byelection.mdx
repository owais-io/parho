Labour's national executive committee has stopped Andy Burnham, the former mayor of Greater Manchester, from standing in the Gorton and Denton byelection. The decision, made by a committee that includes PM Keir Starmer, sparked angry headlines and accusations of a "stitch‑up". Burnham called the move a setback and slammed the party’s leadership. Media outlets argue the veto could spark a fight inside Labour and threaten Starmer’s hold on the party.
