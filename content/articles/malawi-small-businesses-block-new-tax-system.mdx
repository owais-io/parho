In Malawi, thousands of small shop owners shut their doors in Blantyre, Lilongwe, Zomba and Mzuzu to stop the rollout of a new electronic invoicing tax system. The protests, backed by signed petitions, forced the government to postpone the change until April. Business owners say the new rules will raise costs, especially when the country struggles with foreign‑currency shortages and high import prices. The strike highlights a wider economic crisis that also sees rising fuel and electricity prices.
