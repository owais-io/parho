On Friday, a massive winter storm slammed across the U.S., prompting 16 states and Washington D.C. to declare emergencies. The cold will affect about 230 million people, bringing heavy snow, ice, and power outages, especially in southern areas that rarely experience such low temperatures. Residents are stockpiling supplies, flights are being canceled, and officials warn of dangerous roads and power disruptions. Climate experts say the storm reflects rising weather extremes.
