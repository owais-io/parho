In August, a team of scientists injected 65,000 litres of sodium hydroxide—an alkaline chemical—into the Gulf of Maine. Over five days they measured about 10 tonnes of CO₂ taken up and a rise in pH from 7.95 to 8.3, showing the ocean could absorb more carbon. While the experiment was small and found no obvious harm to fish or plankton, experts warn about possible side effects and the risk of companies racing to sell carbon credits.
