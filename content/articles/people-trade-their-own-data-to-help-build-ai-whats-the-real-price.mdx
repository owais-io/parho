Across the globe, folks from Cape Town to Chicago are filming their walks, recording street sounds, or sharing phone chats to earn a few dollars. These “AI trainers” sell their personal videos, audio, and even faces to companies that need human‑grade data to improve chatbots and image tools. While the money can help with bills, the gigs pay low, offer no job security, and leave users vulnerable to misuse like deepfakes, identity theft, and lack of legal protection.
