Police have kept four men in custody until 13 March after they were arrested for spying on Jewish community locations and people on behalf of Iran. The suspects, one Iranian and three dual British‑Iranian, were taken at three different addresses in the north‑west of London. Six other men were released on bail while detectives and MI5 look into whether the surveillance aimed at possible attacks on Britain. Home Secretary Shabana Mahmood said the moves keep the country safe.
