A U.S. operation that captured Venezuelan leader Nicolás Maduro and his wife was condemned at a UN Security Council meeting. Countries from Brazil to Russia, China and Spain said the raid was a “crime of aggression” that broke Venezuela’s sovereignty. The U.S. said it was a law‑enforcement move to enforce long‑standing charges against Maduro. UN leaders warned the raid could worsen regional instability and called for peaceful dialogue instead.
