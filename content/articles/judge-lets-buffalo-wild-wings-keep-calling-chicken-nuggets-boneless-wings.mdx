A customer sued Buffalo Wild Wings, claiming their “boneless wings” were not real wings. A U.S. judge dismissed the case, saying the name is fine and customers won’t be misled. The judge explained that “boneless wings” are simply fried chicken breast pieces and the term has been used for years. The company can keep using the name, and the lawsuit sought about $10 million.
