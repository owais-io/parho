MPs have asked the government to stop the £2m a day subsidy that Britain’s biggest power plant, Drax, receives while an investigation into its green claims is underway. Court documents suggest the plant may be burning wood from unsustainable forests, raising doubts about its renewable status. The FCA is looking into the claims, and MPs want any future payments paused until the truth is clear. Drax denies wrongdoing and says its biomass meets standards.
