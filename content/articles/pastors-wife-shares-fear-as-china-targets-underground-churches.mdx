Two months ago, plain‑clothed officers raided a Beijing suburb at 2 a.m. and took senior pastor Gao Yingjia, who runs the underground Zion Church. His wife, Geng Pengpeng, fled to Thailand with their son, fearing she would be arrested too. Since then, China has detained more than a dozen church leaders and even banned unlicensed online sermons. The move has alarmed the U.S. and human‑rights groups, who warn that the crackdown could end unofficial Christian worship in China.
