Jonathan Seidler, a Jewish writer from Sydney, explains how the recent antisemitic attack in Bondi has turned his family's Hanukkah celebrations from a joyful holiday into a time of fear and grief. He describes the menorah he keeps at home and how the attack made it feel like a mourning ritual. Seidler talks about his anxiety, therapy sessions, and the effort to keep his children safe. He laments that Jewish life in Australia now feels risky and wants normality back.
