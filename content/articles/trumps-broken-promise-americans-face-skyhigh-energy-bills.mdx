Trump promised to cut energy bills in half, but prices are higher than ever. A Guardian report shows U.S. households paid about $116 more for electricity in 2025, with gas up too. People like Baltimore’s Sims‑El and Texas’s Lott are cutting groceries, skipping doctor visits, or even shutting off heating to survive. Big tech data centres and more LNG exports strain the grid, while aid programs have been cut, leaving many stuck.
