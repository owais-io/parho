Since the war began, Iran’s government has organized more than 850 pro‑regime rallies while arresting about 1,400 people. The protests show how stubborn the regime is, even as U.S. and Israeli attacks kill civilians. Most demonstrations mourn the leader, criticize Israel, and support the new successor. Security forces use checkpoints, internet blocks, and force to silence opposition, killing several protesters. The regime also arrests people for filming or speaking out, labeling them spies.
