Cluster headaches are a rare, very painful condition that hits one side of the head in short, intense attacks. The author, a teacher, first felt the pain in 2016 and was only diagnosed in 2019 after years of misdiagnosis. Symptoms include eye redness, tearing and facial sweating. Treatments that work are oxygen therapy, triptans and preventive drugs like verapamil. Support groups and apps help people track and manage attacks.
