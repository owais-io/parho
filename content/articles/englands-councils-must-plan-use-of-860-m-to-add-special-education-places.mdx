England’s local councils will need to show how they will spend an £860 million fund to put more children with special educational needs and disabilities (SEND) into mainstream schools. The money is part of a £3 billion push to create 50,000 new SEND spots. Councils must submit plans by June 2026, outlining extra classrooms, sensory rooms and “inclusion bases” so pupils stay in their local schools. If the plans are approved, payments start in autumn 2026.
