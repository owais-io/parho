After a police officer allegedly shoved a 69‑year‑old woman to the ground during a protest against Israeli President Isaac Herzog, several demonstrators are planning to sue the New South Wales government. Lawyers say the case could be blocked by a 2009 law that lets the state shield police during “major events.” The protest, which used pepper spray and kettling, has led to several injuries and ongoing investigations. Police and government officials have denied wrongdoing.
