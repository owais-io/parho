A Utah prosecutor told a judge that his office had no conflict of interest in the trial of Tyler Robinson, the man accused of killing right‑wing activist Charlie Kirk. Robinson’s lawyers argue that the prosecutor’s daughter attended the rally where Kirk was shot and that the death‑penalty push shows bias. They also want to block graphic videos and cameras in court, while the prosecution and media insist on open proceedings.
