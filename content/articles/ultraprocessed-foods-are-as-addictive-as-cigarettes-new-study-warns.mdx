Ultra‑processed foods are engineered to be as addictive as cigarettes, a new study shows. The paper points out that the way manufacturers design and market snack drinks and chips mirrors how tobacco companies created cravings. It warns that misleading labels like “low‑fat” or “sugar‑free” hide real health risks. The authors urge stricter rules, similar to tobacco control, and say governments should hold food firms accountable, not just blame consumers.
