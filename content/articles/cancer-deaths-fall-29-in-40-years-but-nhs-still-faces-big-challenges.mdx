New data shows cancer deaths in the UK fell 29% over the last 40 years, giving hope amid worries that the NHS is missing its goals. Despite this progress, long waits for treatment, unequal care and rising deaths from some cancers still hurt patients. The government’s fresh cancer plan aims to speed diagnosis, cut inequalities and bring new tests, but critics say more action on prevention and funding is needed.
