ICE acting director Todd Lyons told a House committee that the agency will keep operating during the 2026 World Cup in the United States. He said ICE is part of the event’s security, but critics worry arrests could target visitors. Representative Pou asked for a pause, warning wrongful detentions could hurt the tournament. Lyons insisted ICE will protect attendees yet declined to stop enforcement. The decision may worry fans and city CEOs.
