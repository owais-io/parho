Since the Ukraine war began, Russia’s top officials and intellectuals have stayed quiet about their true feelings and instead played it safe, using culture to show loyalty to Putin. Major awards in books and films went to close allies and old Soviet families, with no dissent. The article follows the career of culture minister Lyubimova, who climbed the ranks by aligning with the Kremlin, even while hoping to keep some western ties.
