After her husband died, 76‑year‑old Lani Ritter Hall felt lost until she joined a volunteer group called Third Act. She helped set up Zoom chats for new members, and suddenly she had purpose and friends. Across the U.S., people of all ages are doing the same—joining activism, mutual aid, or community events—to stop loneliness and stand up for justice. The article shows the benefits and some risks of getting involved.
