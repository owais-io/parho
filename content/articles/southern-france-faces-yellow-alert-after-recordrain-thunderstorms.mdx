On March 9, southern France went under a yellow alert after powerful thunderstorms dumped record rain. The Var region saw over 100 mm in a few hours, breaking a 1988 daily record at Hyères. The Gapeau River flooded, reaching 2.34 m. In the U.S., Illinois may have hit a new hail record with a 6‑inch stone. Meanwhile, a heat dome kept South Africa hot, with Cape Town setting a new March temperature record of 42.1 °C.
