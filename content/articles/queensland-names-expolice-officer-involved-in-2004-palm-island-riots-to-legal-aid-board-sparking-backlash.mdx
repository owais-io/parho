Queensland’s government has named ex‑police detective Darren Robinson—who was a key figure in the 2004 Palm Island riots—to its Legal Aid board. Indigenous leaders and lawyers say the move hurts the community, citing Robinson’s role in the death of Mulrunji Doomadgee and the heavy police response that followed. The appointment ignores an inquest that found Robinson’s actions were “superficial and biased” and no discipline was taken. Many fear it will undermine trust in legal aid for First Nations people.
