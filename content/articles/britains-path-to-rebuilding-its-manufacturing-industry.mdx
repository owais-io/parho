Britain has become a service‑dominated country, but that makes it vulnerable when global supply chains break. Seeing how China turned from a poor, peasant society into a manufacturing giant shows the power of rebuilding industry. The article argues Britain must accept that it needs a new industrial strategy, invest in factories, support steel and sectors, and even use protective measures like “buy‑British” policies to help its makers grow. With effort, manufacturing could rise from 8 % to 10 % of the economy.
