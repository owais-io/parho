US special forces seized Venezuelan leader Nicolás Maduro in Caracas on Saturday. Republicans say it stops drug trafficking and restores oil control. Democrats argue it violates Venezuela’s sovereignty and could start a war. The move sparked cheers among Venezuelan communities in Miami, while lawmakers called for a trial and warned against unilateral military action. Critics fear the operation sets a dangerous precedent for other countries.
