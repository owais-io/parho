Fighting at the Cambodia‑Thailand border has worsened, forcing more than 500,000 people into shelters. At least 20 people died and nearly 200 were hurt. Both sides blame each other for heavy weapon attacks and for violating a peace deal. The U.S. and others have tried to mediate, but political changes in Thailand and new accusations over landmines have stalled talks. The conflict has also led to Cambodia pulling out of a sports event and raised concerns from leaders worldwide.
