First Minister John Swinney says Scotland needs more immigrants to keep its workforce strong. He defends diversity while a far‑right group, Reform UK, is gaining votes. Swinney calls the protests against asylum camps a small minority and says worries about local services are not only because of migrants. He says the economy would benefit from newcomers and that Scotland should not blame migrants for austerity. He predicts Reform will win a few seats but will not support them.
