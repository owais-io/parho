UK officials say they’re ready for price jumps, but the market says otherwise. Britain gets about 75% of its energy from fossil fuels and 44% of its gas from overseas. The government hasn’t set up a real safety net for consumers, hasn’t cut the link between gas and electric prices, and hasn’t built enough gas storage. Even the North Sea isn’t being explored more. The result: a shaky energy supply that made bonds rise and prices soar.
