Belgium’s prime minister, Bart De Wever, sparked a storm when he said Europe should fix ties with Russia to get cheaper energy. Critics say it would weaken the EU’s stand against Russia and help Putin. De Wever argued the West is too weak and needs U.S. backing to push Russia back. The foreign minister and other EU leaders rejected his idea, stressing the bloc’s plan to phase out Russian gas and oil by 2027.
