An FBI supervisor in Minneapolis stepped down after his attempt to investigate the ICE agent who shot 37‑year‑old Renee Good was stopped by the bureau’s Washington office. Good’s death sparked protests and calls for accountability. The supervisor had been pushing for an investigation, but the agency said it had no basis for a civil‑rights probe. His resignation comes as several federal prosecutors quit over related inquiries.
