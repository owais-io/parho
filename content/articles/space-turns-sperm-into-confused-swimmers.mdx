Researchers in Adelaide found that sperm act like untethered astronauts when exposed to microgravity, tumbling and losing direction. In a lab maze that mimics the female tract, only about 60% of space‑treated sperm reached the end, compared with normal conditions. Adding progesterone helped guide them, showing that hormones could counter disorientation. The findings matter for future space colonies and also give insights into Earth‑based fertility science.
