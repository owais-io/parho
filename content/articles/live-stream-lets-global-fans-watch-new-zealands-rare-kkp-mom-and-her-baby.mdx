New Zealand’s Department of Conservation has set up a live stream so people all over the world can watch a rare, flightless parrot called a kākāpō caring for her baby. The mother, Rakiura, is on a predator‑free island and has already had two chicks. More than 100,000 viewers tune in each week, chatting and cheering as scientists hope this is the best breeding season ever. The stream helps people learn about the bird’s recovery and keeps the public involved.
