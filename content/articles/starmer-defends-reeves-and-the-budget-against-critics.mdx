Starmer is stepping up to protect his chancellor, Rachel Reeves, after the budget caused a fight. He says the budget was right for Britain, cutting energy bills, keeping the NHS safe and easing child poverty. He will also highlight his long‑term plan for welfare and deregulation. Critics say Reeves lied about tax rises, but OBR data shows she kept the budget balanced. Starmer wants voters to see the plan’s benefits before Parliament ends.
