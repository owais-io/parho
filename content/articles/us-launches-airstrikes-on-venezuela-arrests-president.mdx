The United States carried out airstrikes on Caracas and seized President Nicolás Maduro and his wife. The move was slammed by many countries—France, Spain, Brazil, Russia, China, the EU—and the UN, who say it violates international law and threatens peace. Some Latin American leaders backed the action, but most urged restraint and a peaceful transition. The incident raises fears of ongoing U.S. involvement and possible conflict.
