Wesley Plaisier, a Dutch player ranked 92, shocked the darts world by beating world No.9 Gerwyn Price in a quick three‑set match at Alexandra Palace. Plaisier hit nine of 16 doubles and stayed calm while Price missed many of his favourite shots. Price, a former champion and favourite to win, looked stunned but respectful. The win shows how a lower‑ranked player can rise on the big stage, and it opens up the tournament for other contenders.
