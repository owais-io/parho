The Charity Commission has opened a serious inquiry into William Blake House, a care home for adults with learning disabilities. The investigation follows revelations that the chair received £1 million in fees, the charity faces a £1.6 million unpaid tax bill, and its finances have collapsed. Families fear residents could be evicted. The inquiry will examine financial mismanagement, tax debts, and possible conflicts of interest. The charity hopes to stay open by selling land.
