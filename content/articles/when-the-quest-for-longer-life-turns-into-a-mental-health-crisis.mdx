Jason Wood, a 40‑year‑old from Michigan, spent thousands on diets, workouts and expensive ‘longevity’ treatments to try to live forever. The pressure pushed him to the brink, causing a breakdown. He now says he suffered from ‘longevity fixation syndrome’, an anxiety disorder where people obsess over extending life at the cost of mental health. Doctors are noticing more cases, especially after the pandemic, and are offering therapy to help people stop tracking every number.
