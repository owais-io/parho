Venezuela's opposition was excited when President Maduro was taken into U.S. custody, but U.S. President Trump chose not to back their leader María Corina Machado after the raid. Trump instead recognized Vice‑President Delcy Rodríguez as interim president and warned she must align with U.S. interests. Opposition leaders felt abandoned and frustrated, while some U.S. officials say they still support a democratic transition. The future of elections and who will lead Venezuela remains unclear.
