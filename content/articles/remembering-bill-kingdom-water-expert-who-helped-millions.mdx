Bill Kingdom (69) was a water specialist who spent 20 years with the World Bank, bringing clean water to poor communities in Asia, Africa and the Middle East. He began his career with Mott MacDonald, worked in Indonesia, Oman and the UK, and later taught in Oxford. A generous mentor, he loved traveling and puzzles. He died after fighting a brain tumour. He is survived by his wife, children and grandchildren.
