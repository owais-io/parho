After the U.S. and Israel bombed Iran, markets thought the crisis would be brief. But the fighting is dragging on, sending oil above $100 a barrel and gas prices up, causing inflation and slowing growth. Shipping through the Strait of Hormuz is threatened, hurting fuel, fertilizer, and even food prices. Central banks warn of higher interest rates, and businesses fear a global slowdown.
