England’s women’s football is booming. Attendance at WSL games has jumped from about 1,000 a year to nearly 7,000, led by big clubs like Arsenal. Participation has risen from 2.5 million players in 2015 to over 6.5 million today, thanks to school programmes that now reach 90 % of kids. The FA is pushing for more coaching, better pitches and a 2035 World Cup hosted by the UK. The goal is to give every girl a chance to play.
