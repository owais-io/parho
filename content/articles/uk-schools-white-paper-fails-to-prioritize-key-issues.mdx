The latest UK schools white paper is heavily focused on special educational needs (SEND) but misses clearer priorities elsewhere. It pushes all schools into multi‑academy trusts, plans to cut the attainment gap with £8 bn for disadvantaged pupils, and offers incentives for heads of struggling schools. It also highlights community service rebuilding, attendance worries and new AI‑related teaching changes. Critics say the paper is too broad and its reforms may cause disruption rather than improvement.
