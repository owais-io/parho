Glasgow artist Nnena Kalu, who has learning disabilities, won the Turner Prize for sculptures made from recycled VHS tape and cling film. She’s the first artist with a learning disability to win. The prize follows shows that highlight work by blind, deaf and neuro‑divergent creators, and groups have pushed museums to address disability. Some critics questioned the panel’s motives, but many see the award as a step toward mainstream recognition and better support for disabled artists.
