Halligan, a Trump‑appointed U.S. attorney in Virginia, resigned after judges said she was unlawfully appointed and her indictments of Letitia James and former FBI director James Comey were politically motivated. Federal judges stripped her of the title “U.S. attorney” and warned of disciplinary action. The Justice Department’s attempt to keep her in the post failed, and she has now stepped down, ending a controversial tenure.
