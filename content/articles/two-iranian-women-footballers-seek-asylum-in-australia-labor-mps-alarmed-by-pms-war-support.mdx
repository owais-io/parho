Two members of Iran’s women’s football team are now asking for asylum in Australia after the team left the Asian Cup. The move comes after the players declined to sing the Iranian anthem, raising fears they might stay in Australia. Labor MPs are worried that Prime Minister Anthony Albanese has backed the U.S. and Israel’s attack on Iran, saying it could hurt the Labor Party. The situation highlights tensions for athletes abroad and the political pressure on the government. Labor officials fear international criticism and domestic backlash.
