A cold wave from the Arctic has left much of Europe, especially central and northern parts, feeling twice‑the‑usual cold and causing heavy snow on New Year’s Eve in places like Poland, Ukraine and the Alps. The chill is set to stay this week, with temperatures up to 10 °C below normal. In South Korea, Jeju island saw big snow and strong winds that shut flights and ferries. Corsica faced record‑breaking gusts up to 100 mph, blowing trees and damaging property.
