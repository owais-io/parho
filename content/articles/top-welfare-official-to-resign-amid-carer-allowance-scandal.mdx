Sir Peter Schofield, the permanent secretary of the Department for Work and Pensions, will leave his post in July after months of criticism over how the department handled the carer’s allowance scandal. The scandal left thousands of unpaid carers with huge debts and, in some cases, fraud charges. An independent review blamed leadership failures, and MPs are still unsure the department can fix the problem. Schofield says he is stepping down to spend more time with family.
