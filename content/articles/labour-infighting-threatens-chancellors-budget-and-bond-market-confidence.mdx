Rachel Reeves, the chancellor, is trying to keep the UK’s bond market calm by planning tax hikes in her November budget. But fights inside Labour, with backbenchers pushing back on cuts, could spoil that plan. If investors see the party in chaos, they may demand higher interest rates on UK government bonds. This would raise the country’s borrowing costs and hurt the economy. The situation is tense.
