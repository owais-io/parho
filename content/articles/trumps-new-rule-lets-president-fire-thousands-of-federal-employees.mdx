The Trump administration’s Office of Personnel Management has issued a rule that reclassifies about 50,000 career federal workers as “policy” employees, giving the president the power to fire them at will. The rule also shifts whistleblower protections from an independent office to individual agencies, a move critics say could lead to politically motivated purges. Unions and advocacy groups plan to challenge the rule in court.
