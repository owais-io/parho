The Grand Emperor hotel in Macau has taken down the gold‑covered tiles that line its entrance and sold them for about $13 million. The move comes as gold prices soar amid global uncertainty, and the hotel’s owner says the money will help the group invest in new projects after it shut its casino in October. The gold bricks, weighing 79 kg, were sold to a Hong Kong refiner. Macau is a major gambling hub that is now diversifying its economy.
