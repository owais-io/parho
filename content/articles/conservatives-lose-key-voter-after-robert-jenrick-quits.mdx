Robert Jenrick, a popular Conservative MP, has left the party, creating a gap in the core voter base the party relies on. His exit gives Kemi Badenoch an opportunity to strengthen her leadership, but it also exposes divisions inside the party. Left‑wing Conservatives hope it will trigger a policy shift, while right‑wing members fear losing influence. Critics argue that the party’s past decisions on immigration, taxes and prisons show incompetence and that Jenrick’s departure could deepen internal rifts.
