The UK government has decided to raise duties on steel imports from China and other countries to 50%, cutting quotas by 60%. This move is aimed at keeping the country's remaining steel mills, like Tata Steel in Wales and the Scunthorpe plant, from shutting down. The policy wants half of the steel used in the UK to be made domestically, with 30% more local production. It also ties in with plans for greener, electric‑arc furnaces.
