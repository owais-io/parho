Australia’s federal government has decided to build a high‑speed rail line between Sydney and Newcastle after 50 years of stalled plans. Construction will start in two years, with trains running up to 320 km/h and cutting travel time to about an hour. The first leg will cost $61 billion, rising to $93 billion when the line reaches a new Western Sydney airport. The project aims to create jobs, housing and help the country hit net‑zero emissions, and the government seeks private investors.
