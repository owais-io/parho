On Nov. 8, 2019, a Chilean protester was shot with a rubber bullet by Lt. Col Claudio Crespo, leaving him blind. A Santiago court later ruled Crespo acted in legitimate defense and cleared him of wrongdoing. The decision has alarmed human‑rights groups, who fear it sets a precedent for police abuse. With a far‑right president due to take office, concerns about rising impunity grow.
