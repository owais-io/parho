Ardan Fisher, 79, was a BBC film editor who helped shape award‑winning documentaries and dramas, including “Edge of Darkness.” Born in Cambridge, he grew up surrounded by art students and loved books, which led him to improve his school library. After starting at the BBC as an assistant editor, he became a celebrated editor, later freelancing on films like “Nature Boy” and the series “Foyle’s War.” He left the industry in the 2000s and is survived by his wife and two children.
