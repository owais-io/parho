Prices for groceries and meals are going up all over the country. In Atlanta, low‑income families are turning to food pantries that now serve twice as many people, while food costs at the pantry have risen 30%. At the same time, some folks still spend more than $500 a month on food, and restaurants—both fast‑food and high‑end—have hiked prices. Even luxury items like caviar have become more affordable, showing that food trends are shifting while costs keep climbing.
