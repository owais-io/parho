Maya Rani, a 36‑year‑old Delhi mother, spends days queuing for a gas cylinder that keeps running out. The shortage comes after Iran closed the Strait of Hormuz, cutting most LPG shipments to South Asia. India, which imports 60% of its gas, has barely enough stock to cover two weeks. Restaurants, factories and poor households are scrambling; many are forced to eat less or switch to electric cooking. The crisis shows the region’s weak backup plans.
