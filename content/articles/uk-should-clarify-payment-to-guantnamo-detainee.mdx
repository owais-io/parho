UK ministers must explain why they paid a large sum to Abu Zubaydah, a Palestinian man who was tortured by the CIA and is still in Guantánamo. Former attorney general Dominic Grieve says the government should answer questions about the money and admit the role of MI5 and MI6 in his mistreatment. He also calls for a public apology and clearer accountability.
