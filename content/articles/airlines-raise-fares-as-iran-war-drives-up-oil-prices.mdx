The conflict between the US, Israel, and Iran has pushed oil prices higher, hurting airlines that still rely on expensive jet fuel. Big carriers like Cathay Pacific, AirAsia, Thai Airways, Qantas and Air New Zealand have lifted fares and fuel surcharges, especially on long routes that used to cross the Middle East. Travelers are advised to book early or wait a few weeks, as ticket costs could rise up to 30% while flight options shrink.
