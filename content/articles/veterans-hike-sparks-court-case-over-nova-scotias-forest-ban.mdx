A Nova Scotia veteran is taking his case to the province’s supreme court after he defied a seasonal ban on walking in the woods to manage PTSD. The ban, aimed at preventing wildfires, forbids hiking, camping and fishing in most of the province from August to September. He was fined almost C$29,000 and is challenging the ban as unconstitutional, arguing it violates his rights.
