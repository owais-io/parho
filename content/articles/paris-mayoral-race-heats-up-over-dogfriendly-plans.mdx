Paris voters are eyeing the city’s future mayor with dog‑friendly policies in the spotlight. The two finalists, left‑wing Emmanuel Grégoire and former culture minister Rachida Dati, have each pledged more dog parks, free‑leash areas, and even subsidised vet visits. With more than 100,000 dogs in the city, pet owners’ votes could tip the tiny margin. Other candidates offered similar perks, but the race looks too close to call.
