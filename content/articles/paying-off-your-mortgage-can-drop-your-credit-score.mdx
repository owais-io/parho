When you finish paying off a mortgage, your credit score can actually fall because lenders look for a mix of debt types and a long credit history. A mortgage is often the oldest account, so closing it shortens your record and reduces your score. The dip is usually temporary, and lenders still consider you creditworthy because you have more free money without a monthly mortgage payment.
