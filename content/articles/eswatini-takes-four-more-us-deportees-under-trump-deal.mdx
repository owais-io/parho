Eswatini, a small African nation, says it has taken four more people the U.S. sent to it as part of a Trump‑era deal. These ‘third‑country’ deportees were from Somalia, Sudan and Tanzania and arrived after a flight from Phoenix. The U.S. has paid Eswatini more than $5 million to take in people who are not its citizens, but the deal has drawn criticism over human‑rights concerns and legal challenges.
