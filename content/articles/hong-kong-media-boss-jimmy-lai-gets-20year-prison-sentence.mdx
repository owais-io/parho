Jimmy Lai, the founder of the defunct Apple Daily newspaper, was sentenced to 20 years in Hong Kong for national‑security crimes. His family and human‑rights groups say the punishment is cruel and shows a crackdown on free speech. The case has sparked criticism from the UK, US, Taiwan and press‑freedom organisations, who call for his release on humanitarian grounds.
