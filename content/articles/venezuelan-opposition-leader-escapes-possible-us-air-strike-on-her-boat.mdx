María Corina Machado, a Venezuelan opposition leader and Nobel laureate, narrowly avoided a US air strike while fleeing the country. Disguised, she slipped out of Caracas, crossed roadblocks, and boarded a small fishing boat. During a 5,500‑mile sea journey she feared US planes might mistake her for a drug smuggler and bomb her. The rescue, run by a US NGO, finally brought her to Curaçao and then Oslo for the Nobel Peace Prize ceremony.
