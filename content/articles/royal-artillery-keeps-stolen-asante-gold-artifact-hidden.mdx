The Royal Artillery refuses to let the public see a golden ram’s head taken from Ghana’s Asante people during 19th‑century wars. It sits in a private mess room at Larkhill and is said to be kept for security and insurance. Scholars and the Asante king want it returned, while the regiment says only invited guests can view it. Ghana already returned many pieces to its museum, but this one remains locked away.
