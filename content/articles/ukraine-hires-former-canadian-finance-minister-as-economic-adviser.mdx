Ukraine’s president Volodymyr Zelenskyy has appointed former Canadian finance minister Chrystia Freeland as an adviser on economic development. The move aims to boost the country’s “internal resilience” as it fights the war with Russia and rebuilds its economy. Freeland’s track record in attracting investment and transforming economies will help Ukraine recover and strengthen its defence. This comes amid a broader shake‑up of Kyiv’s top advisers and security leaders.
