James Watt, the co‑founder of beer maker BrewDog, admitted on LinkedIn that the company’s sale for just £33 million left small investors – who put in £75 million through crowdfunding – with nothing. He apologised for the loss and for letting a private‑equity deal pay out £100 million while the “equity punks” got nothing. The sale also saw many staff jobs cut and sparked backlash from investors and a trade union.
