Vince Zampella, the man who helped create the popular Call of Duty series, died in a car crash in Los Angeles at 55. He began in the early game industry, then co‑founded Infinity Ward and made Call of Duty famous for its cinematic battles and teamwork. After leaving the company he started Respawn Entertainment, creating hits like Titanfall, Apex Legends and the Star Wars Jedi games. He also helped reboot Battlefield and left a lasting mark on shooters.
