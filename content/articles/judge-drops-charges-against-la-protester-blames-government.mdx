A federal judge in Los Angeles tossed the case against a protester who was accused of hitting an immigration officer with a hat. The judge said the government had acted in bad faith and that the evidence was shaky. The protester, who had spent six months in jail waiting for trial, was released after the dismissal. The ruling also warned that prosecutors can’t keep reopening cases to silence dissent.
