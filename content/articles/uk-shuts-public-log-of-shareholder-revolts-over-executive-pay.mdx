The UK Treasury has ordered the Investment Association to close the public register that listed shareholder protests about company pay and bonuses. A thinktank says the move cuts transparency and lets firms ignore investor concerns. About a quarter of FTSE 100 firms have faced such protests in the last three years. Critics worry that retail investors will have less information, while the government says it’s cutting red‑tape to boost growth.
