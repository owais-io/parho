Robert Kraft, the owner of the New England Patriots, first presented himself as a calm voice during the NFL player protest controversy, encouraging his players to work on civil‑rights issues. He even helped criminal‑justice projects with artists like Jay‑Z. After Donald Trump’s 2016 campaign and the January 6 attack, Kraft publicly cut ties with Trump, only to later re‑align with the former president, accepting money and showing support. His flip‑flop shows how business owners can shift their political stances.
