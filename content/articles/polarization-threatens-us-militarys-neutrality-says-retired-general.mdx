Former top U.S. commander retired admiral Mike Mullen says the country’s growing political divide is making it harder for the armed forces to stay neutral. He points to Trump‑era moves that fired senior leaders, put the National Guard in city policing roles and pushed the military into political fights. Junior officers now worry about getting caught in these debates. Mullen stresses that soldiers are professional, but the orders they receive might be politically tainted.
