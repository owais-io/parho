UK rural households that use heating oil are suddenly facing a massive rise in bills. Prices have jumped from about 62p to £1.73 per litre, almost tripling since the start of the Iran war. With 1.7 million households dependent on it, suppliers are canceling orders and limiting deliveries. The government is looking into help, while customers scramble to buy before prices rise further.
