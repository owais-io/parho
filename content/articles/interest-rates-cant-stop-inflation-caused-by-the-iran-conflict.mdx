The Bank of England is ending its rate‑cut cycle after a long run of lowering rates to tame inflation. But the real cause of rising prices is the Iranian blockade of the Hormuz Strait, which has pushed oil, gas and fertilizer costs up sharply. The UK is heavily exposed, with high gas‑fuel prices hurting households. Analysts say rate hikes won’t fix supply shocks; instead, price caps or government action are needed.
