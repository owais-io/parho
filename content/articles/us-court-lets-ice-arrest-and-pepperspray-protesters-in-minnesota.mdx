An appeals court temporarily removed a court order that had stopped ICE agents from arresting and pepper‑spraying peaceful protesters in Minnesota. The ruling lets the Justice Department back its earlier request to lift the injunction. ICE officials say they are acting lawfully, while Minnesota officials and activists say the agents are intimidating protesters. The decision comes amid protests after a federal agent killed a woman and ahead of a visit by a top US official.
