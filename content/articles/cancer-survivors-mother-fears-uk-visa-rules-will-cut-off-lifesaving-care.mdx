A mother in London says she is terrified her six‑year‑old daughter, who survived neuroblastoma, will lose life‑saving treatment if her visa is denied. New UK rules will double the wait for permanent status from five to ten years, meaning the family could be forced to leave. The mother, who now relies on disability benefits, worries the change could strip her daughter’s free NHS care. Politicians and charities are opposing the reforms.
