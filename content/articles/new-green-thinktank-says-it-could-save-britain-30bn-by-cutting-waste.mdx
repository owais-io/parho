Verdant, a new green think‑tank, says it could free the UK up to £30 billion a year by rooting out waste, fraud and tax avoidance. It wants a “Doge of the left” – a chief savings officer who would audit spending, stop runaway projects and open procurement to more competition. The plan also calls for ending oil‑and‑gas subsidies and giving the National Audit Office power to halt over‑spending. The ideas could shape the Green Party’s next manifesto.
