England’s Covid inquiry revealed that the NHS was already fragile before the pandemic, and that a chronically under‑funded social‑care system made the crisis worse. Hospitals rushed patients home without proper testing, and many people had to rely on inadequate support. The report says a stronger, well‑funded social‑care network is essential for patients to stay independent and to keep the NHS from collapsing again. Reform plans now aim to make the two sectors work together and be sustainably financed.
