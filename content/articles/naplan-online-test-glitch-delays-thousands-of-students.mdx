Naplan tests were halted on Wednesday morning when a technical glitch stopped students from logging in. The Australian Curriculum authority paused the online assessment for more than 1 million pupils, fixing the problem by 11:15 am. Some schools resumed the test while others postponed it. Officials warned the glitch could skew the data and hurt fairness, and the agency promised a full investigation.
