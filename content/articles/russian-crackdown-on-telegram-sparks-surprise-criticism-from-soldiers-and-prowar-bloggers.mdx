Moscow has begun slowing the popular Telegram app, claiming it violated Russian rules. The move, part of a drive for a “sovereign internet,” has shocked many – even frontline troops and pro‑war bloggers who rely on the service for orders, coordination and quick chats. Soldiers complained that the slowdown hurts battlefield communication, while Telegram founder Pavel Durov publicly defended freedom of speech and privacy.
