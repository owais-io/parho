Germany’s new chancellor, Friedrich Merz, is known for his loud, confident speaking style. He often talks before thinking, which has earned him jokes and lowered his approval, especially among women. While his brashness might scare domestic voters, it could help push Europe toward stronger defense ties and less US dependence. However, he has also made factual errors and mixed messages about nuclear weapons, raising doubts about his grasp of policy.
