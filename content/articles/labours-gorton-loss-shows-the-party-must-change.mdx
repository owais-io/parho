Labour did badly in the Gorton and Denton by‑election, and leader Keir Starmer responded with a harsh letter to MPs that blamed the Green win and accused them of division. The article argues that Labour’s old way of running – focusing on traditional left‑wing base and ignoring new city voters – is failing. It calls for the party to adopt new ideas, like proportional representation, and to team up with other progressive groups to stay relevant.
