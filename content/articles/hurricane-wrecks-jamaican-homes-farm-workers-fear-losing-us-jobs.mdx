Hurricane Melissa ripped through Jamaica, destroying about 90,000 homes and leaving 360,000 people in trouble. Many of those hit are Jamaican farm workers on H‑2A visas who work in New York each year. With their houses gone, they’re worried they can’t return and that their U.S. jobs will be lost. The United Farm Workers has raised money to help rebuild homes, and farms like Wafler Farms are donating to relief efforts while working with the union.
