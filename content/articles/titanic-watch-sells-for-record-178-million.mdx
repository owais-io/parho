A gold pocket watch that once belonged to Isidor Straus, a New York department‑store partner who died on the Titanic, fetched a record £1.78 million at auction. The watch, an 18‑carat Jules Jurgensen piece, broke the previous Titanic‑memorabilia record. The same auction also sold a letter from Straus’s wife and other items for a total of £3 million, underscoring the enduring fascination with the ship’s tragic story.
