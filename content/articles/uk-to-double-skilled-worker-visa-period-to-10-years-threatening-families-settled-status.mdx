The UK government plans to raise the time needed for skilled workers to qualify for settled status from five to ten years. Many workers are already near that milestone, but the change would keep them on temporary visas and could delay or prevent them from getting permanent rights. Families say the rule change causes real pain—like missing a funeral or losing fertility treatment—because they have built lives in the UK on the promise of a five‑year path.
