Sudanese asylum seeker Deng Chol Majek was sentenced to life in prison after he stabbed 27‑year‑old hotel worker Rhiannon Whyte 23 times, killing her in Walsall. The attack followed his stay at a Wolverhampton hotel. Majek was found guilty of murder and using a screwdriver as a weapon, receiving a minimum of 29 years with no possibility of early release. The case sparked anger from local groups.
