Romania’s roads are among the deadliest in Europe, with 78 people dying per million each year and half of those deaths involving pedestrians or cyclists. The government has introduced new laws defining aggressive driving, higher fines, speed cameras and automated detection to curb speeding and reckless behaviour. Deaths fell 13% in 2025, and the country is holding a road‑safety week and facing an EU notice to improve safety. Old imported cars and a growing SUV market add extra danger.
