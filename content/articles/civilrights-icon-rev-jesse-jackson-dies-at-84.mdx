Civil‑rights leader Rev. Jesse Jackson, 84, has passed away after a career of fighting for equality. He was a key figure in the civil‑rights movement, worked closely with Martin Luther King Jr., and ran for the Democratic presidential nomination in 1988. His family calls him a servant‑leader who championed justice, equality and love, and urges people to keep his legacy alive.
