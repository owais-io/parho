Trump ordered U.S. air strikes in Iran, saying an attack was coming, but he did not get Congress or courts to agree first. Critics say this breaks the U.S. system of checks and balances and pushes the limits of the war‑powers resolution. The article also notes a Supreme Court decision that makes it harder to hold presidents accountable after they leave office. The result is a weaker system that lets presidents act with fewer consequences.
