Mexico’s navy ships arrived in Havana with 800 tonnes of food and medicine, helping Cuba face a crisis caused by a U.S. embargo that halted oil deliveries. President Claudia Sheinbaum says Mexico wants to keep talking with the U.S. so Cuba can get fuel, but she also keeps sending humanitarian aid. The move shows Mexico’s long‑standing friendship with Cuba, even while it follows Washington’s rules.
