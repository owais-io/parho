UK MPs will see their basic salary jump to £110,000 by the end of the current Parliament, the Independent Parliamentary Standards Authority (Ipsa) said. The rise – from £98,599 in April – is based on higher workloads, rising abuse of MPs and a need to keep pay in line with similar roles abroad. Critics say it clashes with the public’s own economic struggles and could push MPs into a tax band that loses childcare benefits.
