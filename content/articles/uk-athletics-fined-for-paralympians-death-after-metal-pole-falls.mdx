In July 2017, a metal pole at Newham Leisure Centre fell on Paralympian Abdullah Hayayei, killing him while he trained for the London World Para Athletics Championships. UK Athletics admitted corporate manslaughter, facing a £1.2‑£5 million fine. The head of sport, Keith Davies, pleaded guilty to a health‑safety breach. Investigations revealed missing metal connectors that made the cage unstable, showing long‑standing safety failures.
