In Texas's midterm primaries, Democrats chose James Talarico, a 36‑year‑old Presbyterian seminarian, to run for the U.S. Senate. He beat progressive congresswoman Jasmine Crockett, aiming to win over moderates and independents. On the Republican side, attorney general Ken Paxton and senior senator John Cornyn split the vote, forcing a runoff. Texas voters showed a preference for less extreme candidates, giving both parties a chance to focus on the general election.
