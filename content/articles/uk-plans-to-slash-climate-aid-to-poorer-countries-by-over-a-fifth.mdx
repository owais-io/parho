The UK is set to slash its climate aid for poorer nations by more than a fifth, cutting the next five‑year budget from £11.6bn to about £9bn. The move comes despite promises to triple global climate finance and warnings that the cuts could cost lives and damage the UK’s security by raising food prices and sparking conflict. Critics say the cuts hurt vulnerable countries and undermine Britain’s climate leadership.
