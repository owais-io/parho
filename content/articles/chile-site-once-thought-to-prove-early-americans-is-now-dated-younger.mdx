A new study says Monte Verde in Chile, once thought to show people were in South America 14,000 years ago, is actually only 6,000‑8,000 years old. Researchers found that soil erosion had pushed newer finds into older layers, making the site older than it really is. This undermines the earlier “pre‑Clovis” idea and supports the theory that early humans moved north‑to‑south across the Americas. The study calls for re‑examining other sites.
