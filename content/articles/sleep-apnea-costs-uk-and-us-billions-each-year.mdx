Sleep apnea costs the UK and US more than £137 bn ($185 bn) a year, a new study says. One in five adults show the breathing‑pause symptoms that mean a diagnosis. The research shows the disease causes huge lost workdays and lower productivity, costing the US $180 bn and the UK £4 bn annually. The authors urge workplace screening and early treatment, but many patients stop therapy, and some workers fear job loss.
