UK museums hold thousands of human remains, many from colonial times. A recent study shows museum staff care deeply but lack resources. The debate isn’t about keeping the remains, but how to treat them ethically. Repatriation is possible for some, but many cannot be returned. MPs say the government should fund ethical care, not just museum managers. Better support could improve trust between institutions and communities.
