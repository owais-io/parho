A London court ruled that Saudi Arabia must pay more than £3 million to Ghanem al‑Masarir, a former Saudi critic whose phones were hacked with Pegasus spyware and who was physically attacked in London. The judge found the hacking and attack were carried out by Saudi officials. This rare legal defeat for the kingdom gives the dissident compensation for psychiatric harm and loss of life.
