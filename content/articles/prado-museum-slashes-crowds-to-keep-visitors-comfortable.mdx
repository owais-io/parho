Prado Museum is taking steps to stop crowds from making visits feel like a rush‑hour train. After drawing a record 3.5 million visitors last year, the museum wants to keep the experience pleasant by tightening entrance flow, limiting group sizes and enforcing a strict no‑photo rule in galleries. Visitors who arrived early found short lines and quieter rooms, while popular works still drew small crowds.
