A 40‑year‑old translator, Martina Karos, who cared for her blind eight‑year‑old daughter, was found dead in their Salford home after she took her own life. The coroner said Karos felt isolated and lonely after her breakup and struggled to cope with the heavy caregiving duties, despite support from social workers and respite care. She allegedly used carbon monoxide to end both her and her daughter’s lives.
