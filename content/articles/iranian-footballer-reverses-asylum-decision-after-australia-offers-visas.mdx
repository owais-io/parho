An Iranian women's football team player who had been granted an asylum visa in Australia decided to return home, reversing her earlier decision. Home Affairs Minister Tony Burke confirmed that Australia had offered humanitarian visas to seven players, but one woman changed her mind after talking with teammates. Iran accused Australia of holding the players hostage, but the government moved the other women to safety and will help them settle and learn English. Some players faced pressure from Iran.
