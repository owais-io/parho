The US removed a potash export ban, leading Belarus to free 123 prisoners, including Nobel laureate Ales Bialiatski and opposition figure Maria Kalesnikava. The move followed talks with a US envoy and is part of a thaw aimed at pulling Belarus away from Russia. The released prisoners were jailed for protesting and voting; many had suffered harsh treatment. Opposition leaders thanked the US, but some still urge EU sanctions to stay.
