Andy Whitelaw was a leading newborn doctor who helped save countless babies. He invented ways to clear blood clots from tiny babies’ brains, cooled babies after birth to protect their brains, and promoted “kangaroo care,” where parents hold their babies against their chests. He worked in London, Norway, and Bristol, taught worldwide, and led trials that are now standard practice. He died on 9 November 2025, leaving a legacy of better care for premature and sick babies.
