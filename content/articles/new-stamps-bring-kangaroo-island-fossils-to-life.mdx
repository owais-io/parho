Scientists turned fossils from Kangaroo Island into colourful drawings that Australia Post turned into a stamp set called *Creatures of the Palaeozoic*. Dr Peter Trusler used recent research to paint detailed pictures of 512‑million‑year‑old animals, like giant trilobites and early arthropods. The stamps show four species, including a 25‑cm trilobite and a 50‑cm predatory creature. The artwork will also hang in the museum, letting visitors see these ancient animals up close.
