A 20‑year‑old woman who began using YouTube at six and Instagram at nine is giving her first testimony in a high‑profile lawsuit that says social media companies make their apps addictive. She says the use made her depressed, anxious and caused self‑harm. The case could force YouTube and Meta to pay huge fines and change how the platforms work. Other firms, TikTok and Snap, already settled. The trial could set a precedent for all social media.
