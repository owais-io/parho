After U.S. and Israel fired missiles, Iran launched an economic war that hit global prices. Britain, which buys most of its food, gas, and other goods from overseas, feels the pinch hard. Blocked shipping routes and rising fuel costs raise living costs, and the country’s high debt and dependence on foreign money make it vulnerable. The story says Britain needs to move to renewables, cut imports, and protect households with new rules.
