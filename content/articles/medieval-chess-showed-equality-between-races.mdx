A Cambridge historian studied medieval books, paintings and chess sets and found that players from Africa, the Middle East and Asia were shown as equals on the board. The images show a Black player sitting on a fancy bench, a Mongol warrior relaxed, and a Muslim and Jewish pair playing peacefully. The study argues chess was a space where people could meet as equals, not as racial or social hierarchies. The work won an award for its insights on race.
