Tāme Iti, once called a terrorist by New Zealand police, is now seen by many as a national treasure. His new book, Mana, mixes personal photos and stories of his early life, his protests like the 1975 Māori Land March, the 2007 police raids, and his famous “Māori embassy” outside Parliament. Iti talks about his fight for Māori rights, the 2024 protest marches, and urges the country to rebuild its relationship with Indigenous people.
