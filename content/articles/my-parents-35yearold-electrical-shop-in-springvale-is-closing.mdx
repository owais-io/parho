On the last day of her family’s 35‑year‑old Betta electrical shop in Springvale, Alice Pung helps pack everything from air fryers to old Game‑Boy cassette cases. Her mother, a top salesman who can’t read English, poured decades of hard work into the store. The shop was the family’s heartbeat and the neighbourhood’s gathering spot, mixing gadgets with quirky items that drew regular customers. Though the shop closes, her parents plan to keep trading in a nearby Footscray location.
