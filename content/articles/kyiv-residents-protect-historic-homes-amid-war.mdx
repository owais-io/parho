Kyiv residents are restoring old art‑new‑style houses even as Russian missiles hit the city. Lesia Danylenko proudly opened a new front door on her 1906 mansion, a symbol of everyday life and resistance. Volunteers and activists fight developers and corrupt officials who tear down historic buildings for new projects. With limited political support and a tough winter, the city’s heritage is at risk. People are working to keep these rare homes alive for future generations.
