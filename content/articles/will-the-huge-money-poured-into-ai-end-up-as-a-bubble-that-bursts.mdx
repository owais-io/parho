A trillion‑plus dollars are being spent on AI data centres, chips, and talent, all hoping to reach “general intelligence” that can do many human jobs. Investors expect huge profits, but if AI stalls, stock and bond markets could drop sharply, hurting pensions and credit markets. Big tech firms have cash to survive a hiccup, but the risk is still there, and experts warn of a possible crash if the AI boom turns out to be a bubble.
