England has pushed back the start of the Future Homes Standard, a rule that would require new houses to use solar panels and heat pumps. The delay lets builders keep cheaper gas boilers and even wood‑burning stoves, which will keep many homes from being truly low‑carbon. Experts say this means higher energy bills for buyers and missed chances to cut emissions. The government claims the change will still save households about £1,000 a year.
