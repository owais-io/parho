When World War II broke out, the BBC stopped its daily weather reports because the information could help German forces plan air raids. Farmers still needed weather advice, so the Met Office sent coded messages that used animal names and phrases like “Buy” for good weather and “Sell” for bad. Even train announcements were tightened to avoid giving away useful clues.
