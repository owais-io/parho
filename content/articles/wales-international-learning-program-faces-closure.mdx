After Brexit, Wales launched a student exchange program called Taïth to replace the EU’s Erasmus+. It has let about 12,000 people travel to nearly 100 countries and helped many from under‑represented groups. The Welsh government stopped funding it, so Taïth will shut down by 2028 unless the Senedd or UK Parliament give it new money. Supporters worry the scheme’s future depends on whether the UK re‑joins Erasmus+ in 2027.
