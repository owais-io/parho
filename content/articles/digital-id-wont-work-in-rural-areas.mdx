The government’s plan for a national digital ID depends on everyone having reliable phone service, but many rural areas like Devon still have spotty coverage. Without a signal, residents can’t use the digital ID for things like paying car tax or accessing medical records, and would need to travel far to find service. The government is holding a citizens’ assembly to get public input, but it must be designed to genuinely involve people and rebuild trust.
