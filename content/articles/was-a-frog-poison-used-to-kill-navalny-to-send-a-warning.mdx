Experts say the deadly poison found in the body of jailed Russian opposition leader Alexei Navalny was a rare frog toxin called epibatidine, which only Russia could have supplied. The toxin is extremely powerful, causes paralysis and has no known antidote. Some officials think the choice was meant to send a message about Russia’s secret weapons, while others argue it was simply a cold‑blooded murder. The debate highlights Russia’s long history of using poisons against political foes.
