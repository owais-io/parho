Shipping through the Hormuz strait has slowed, pushing oil prices above $100 a barrel. A recent study shows US oil firms made $281 bn of profit in 2022, with the richest 0.1% of people capturing most of the windfall. The rest of the world, especially poorer households, feel higher energy costs and little compensation. The authors suggest a permanent tax on excess oil profits or price caps to curb profiteering.
