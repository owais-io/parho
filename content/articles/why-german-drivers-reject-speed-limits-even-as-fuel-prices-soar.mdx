German drivers love the freedom of the open Autobahn and many refuse to slow down, even as fuel prices rise. Politicians have proposed a blanket 130‑km/h limit, but most parties and the ruling party say no. Studies say a limit could save money, cut pollution and reduce crashes, yet car makers and some voters worry about freedom and jobs. Public opinion is slowly shifting toward limits, but the debate continues.
