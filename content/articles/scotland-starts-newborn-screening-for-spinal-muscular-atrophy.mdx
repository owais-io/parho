Scotland has begun testing all newborns for spinal muscular atrophy (SMA), a rare genetic disease that can cause muscle weakness and breathing problems. The test is done with a heel‑prick blood sample a few days after birth. Early diagnosis is key because treatments work best when started early. The pilot will last two years, and if it shows good results, other parts of the UK might follow. The program is funded by the Scottish government and Novartis.
