The UN is planning to merge UN Women and the UN’s reproductive‑health agency, UNFPA, to cut costs and boost efficiency. Many activists and some governments fear the move could cut funding, weaken programs that fight gender‑based violence, and leave women’s rights vulnerable. Critics say the merger happens while U.S. aid is shrinking and rights are under attack worldwide. The proposal could be voted on at this week’s women’s rights conference.
