Melbourne’s West Gate Tunnel will finally open for drivers on Sunday after years of setbacks, including a costly legal fight over toxic soil. The $10.2 billion project adds 6.8 km of tunnels and 9.2 km of upgraded roads, giving motorists a route across the river and a backup to the West Gate Bridge. It also brings 14 km of bike paths and a ban on truck traffic. Drivers will pay tolls, but the government says it gives people more options.
