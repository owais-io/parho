NS&I, the UK’s government‑backed savings bank, fired its chief executive after a 2024 audit found that it had failed to locate accounts for thousands of deceased customers, leaving about £470 m owed to bereaved families. The Treasury hired a new interim boss, Jim Harra, and promised quick payments and interest. An independent review will uncover how the tracing errors happened, while the bank works to rebuild trust and speed up claims.
