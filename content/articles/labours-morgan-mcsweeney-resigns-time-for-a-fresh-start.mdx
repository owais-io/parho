Labour’s campaign chief, Morgan McSweeney, has quit the party, a move that leaves Leader Keir Starmer scrambling. McSweeney was credited with turning Labour’s fortunes around after the 2019 election, but his resignation highlights how badly the party’s cabinet choices have faltered. Starmer’s team has seen several senior figures leave, and voters are worried about a lack of direction. The party may need a new leader before the next election, but no one has stepped forward yet.
