A former Royal Marine named Paul Doyle admitted to driving his car into a Liverpool FC victory parade on May 26, injuring 134 people, including two babies. He pleaded guilty at the start of his trial and faces more than 10 years in prison. Doyle had been tailgating an ambulance on a closed road and deliberately crashed into the crowd. Police said his actions were reckless, not a terrorist attack.
