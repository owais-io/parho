Charlotte Mortlock, a former Liberal staffer and founder of Hilma’s Network, quit the Liberal Party and stepped down from her network after the party removed its first female leader, Sussan Ley. Mortlock said she fears a backlash from female voters and wants to support women in other ways. Her exit follows calls for gender quotas and concerns that the party has ignored women’s priorities.
