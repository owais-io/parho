The cyber‑attack that shut Jaguar Land Rover’s factories in September forced the company to lose £485 million, turning last year’s £398 million profit into a huge quarterly loss. Production has since resumed, but the hit cost the UK economy up to £1.9 billion and knocked GDP growth to 0.1 %. JLR spent £196 million on IT fixes, faced tariff pressures and ended some older models, while its new electric cars await launch.
