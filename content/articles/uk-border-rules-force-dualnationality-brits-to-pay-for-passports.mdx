Two women who have lived almost all their lives in Britain are now scrambling to prove their British citizenship after new border rules require a passport or £589 certificate for dual nationals. They were born to unmarried British fathers and French mothers, a situation that only gained automatic citizenship rights in 2005. Now they face extra fees, paperwork, and even crowdfunding to keep their travel rights and benefits.
