In Australia, the Liberal Party is on the brink of collapse after a heated debate over a new hate‑speech bill led to a split with the National Party. Senior Liberal figures say leader Sussan Ley’s role is almost finished, and Nationals leader David Little‑proud confirmed the parties will sit separately in parliament. The fallout has left the coalition in disarray, and the long‑running partnership that former PM Malcolm Turnbull once led is now in trouble.
