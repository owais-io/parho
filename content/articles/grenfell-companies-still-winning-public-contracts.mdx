Survivors of the Grenfell Tower fire say the government should stop companies linked to the blaze from winning public contracts. A study by MP Joe Powell found at least 87 contracts in the government’s database involve firms criticised in the 2024 inquiry, including Saint‑Gobain, Rydon and Celotex. Powell wants an audit of all contracts and urges use of the 2023 Procurement Act to bar these companies. The call comes after 72 people died in the 2017 fire.
