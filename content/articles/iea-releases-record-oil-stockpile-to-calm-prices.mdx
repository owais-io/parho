The IEA, with 32 member countries, is releasing a record 400 million barrels from their emergency oil reserves – about a third of all available stock. The move aims to soothe the sharp price rise after US‑Israel attacks on Iran. Key contributors include the UK, Japan, South Korea and Germany, each putting up hundreds of millions of barrels. The release may ease price spikes, but market volatility still lingers.
