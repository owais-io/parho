A flood of raw sewage has been flowing into Wellington’s south coast for more than two weeks after the city’s wastewater plant collapsed. Millions of litres of untreated waste have hit reefs, a marine reserve and popular beaches, causing health warnings and upsetting locals. The plant was badly damaged by an electrical fault, and the city’s water system is under investigation. Officials say a new operator will take over soon, but the clean‑up could take months.
