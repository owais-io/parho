Phil Ochs, who died 50 years ago, wrote the protest song “Cops of the World” in 1966. With sharp sarcasm, he mocked the United States’ global power and its disregard for international law. The Guardian article points out that the song’s critique of empire is still timely, especially as leaders now discuss the G8, G7 and other global politics. Readers shared how the song echoes modern events like Trump’s foreign‑policy moves.
