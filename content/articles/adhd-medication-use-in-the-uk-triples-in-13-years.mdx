The UK’s use of ADHD medicines has jumped three times in 13 years, a study shows. Adult users, especially women over 25, have surged—over 20 times higher than in 2010. The trend is seen across Europe, but the UK has the biggest rise. Many people with ADHD aren’t on meds, and NHS spending on ADHD is now £164 m too high, adding pressure on supplies.
