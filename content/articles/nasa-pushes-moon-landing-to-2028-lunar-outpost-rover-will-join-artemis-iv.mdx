NASA has moved its first crewed moon landing to 2028, changing the Artemis schedule. The agency said astronauts will go on Artemis IV, and the small, commercial rover built by Colorado startup Lunar Outpost will travel with them. The rover, Mapp, survived a bad landing in 2023 but was trapped on the surface. Now it’s ready for a new trip and shows how private firms help NASA reach the moon again.
