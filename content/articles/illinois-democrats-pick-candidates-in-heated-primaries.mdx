Illinois voters picked new Democratic House candidates in five open districts, after fiery primaries that highlighted party divisions, especially over Israel. Outside groups, notably Aipac, spent heavily to influence races, backing more pro‑Israel candidates. Winners include Evanston mayor Daniel Biss, Cook County Commissioner Donna Miller, former city treasurer La Shawn Ford, and others. The contests suggest voters are skeptical of corporate‑funded messaging and want candidates who reflect local priorities.
