The piece celebrates the passing of philosopher Jürgen Habermas and explains why his work on “communicative action” remains relevant today. It traces his journey from a radical left‑wing critic to a thinker who believed that true public dialogue can keep power structures in check. In an age of misinformation and algorithmic control, the author argues that Habermas’s insistence on shared understanding and non‑coercive consensus offers a hopeful path forward.
