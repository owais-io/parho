A toxic algae called Karenia cristata has been spreading along South Australia’s coast for a year, covering 20,000 sq km and killing millions of sea creatures. The bloom has destroyed habitats, wiping out species such as leafy sea dragons and sharks. Fishermen have had to stop catching fish, and many people report headaches, coughs, and sadness after seeing dead marine life on beaches. Scientists worry the algae may keep thriving and could hit other states.
