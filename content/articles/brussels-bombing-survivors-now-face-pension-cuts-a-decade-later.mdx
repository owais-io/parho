Ten years after the 2016 Brussels airport and metro attacks, survivors are being hit with sharp cuts to their victim pensions. Walter Benjamin’s monthly pension was slashed 70%, leaving him worried about a future debt for his daughter. The Belgian pensions service says the reductions are due to a 2017 law that stops double compensation, but many victims say the cuts are wrong and unfair. The state apologized, will reimburse those wrongly docked, but no retroactive adjustment is offered.
