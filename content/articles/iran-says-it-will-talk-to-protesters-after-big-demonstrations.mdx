Iran’s president has said the government will listen to protest demands after big demonstrations over a falling currency and high costs. The protests began when the rial hit a record low, hurting traders and shoppers. Tehran’s leaders plan talks with protest groups and promise changes to the economy. Meanwhile, food and medicine prices have jumped, and new tax hikes are coming. The US has warned of more strikes, but Iran says it wants dialogue.
