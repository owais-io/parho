Prime Minister Keir Starmer said sorry for putting former minister Peter Mandelson in charge of the US embassy, after people said Mandelson was close to the convicted sex‑offender Jeffrey Epstein. He apologised to Epstein’s victims, explained why he made the decision, and asked Labour MPs to stand together. Starmer also wants documents about Mandelson released by a committee, not a civil servant. He’s trying to refocus the party on new investment plans for deprived areas.
