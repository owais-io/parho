A powerful storm sent a year’s worth of rain to parts of South Australia, Victoria and New South Wales, breaking records in many towns. The Bureau of Meteorology warned of heavy downpours, with some spots getting up to 100 mm in a day. While the rain helps water tables, it flooded homes, damaged crops—especially grapes—and caused thousands of emergency calls and forced tractor work to halt.
