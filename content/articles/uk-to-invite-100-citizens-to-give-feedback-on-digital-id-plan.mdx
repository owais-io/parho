British ministers plan to pick 100 random citizens from across the UK to join a consultation on the government's digital ID project. The aim is to gather everyday people's views and address worries about how the IDs might be used. The group will meet over several weekends, hear experts, and propose recommendations. The plan came after scepticism from the public and some ministers, who fear the scheme could spread conspiracy theories.
