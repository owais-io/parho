Three U.S. states—California, New York and Hawaii—are drafting bills that would let state attorneys general sue major fossil‑fuel companies for the rising cost of home insurance caused by climate disasters. The measures target firms with at least $500 million in revenue that operate in the state. If successful, the money would help pay higher premiums and fund fire‑proofing programs. Oil lobbyists oppose the bills, arguing they hurt jobs.
