A new study shows that firms with more women in senior roles are far more likely to fire men who are accused of physically or sexually abusing colleagues. The research, using data from around the world and the UK, also found that women who suffer workplace abuse face lasting pay cuts and higher risk of leaving work. It highlights that female‑led companies handle abuse better and that stronger policing can reduce repeat violence.
