The German army is launching a big recruitment push, aiming to add 80,000 regular soldiers and 140,000 reserve soldiers by 2034. To attract volunteers, it is raising pay, offering free driving licences, language courses and other perks. The army is also reopening conscription‑style paperwork for new 18‑year‑olds. Events like the Essen Motor Show let recruiters talk to anyone, from kids to adults, about a career in uniform and the army’s defensive role.
