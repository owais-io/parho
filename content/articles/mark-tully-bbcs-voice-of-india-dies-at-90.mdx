Mark Tully, the BBC’s long‑time voice in India, died at 90. He covered India’s most turbulent moments – from the 1984 Bhopal disaster to the Golden Temple siege – and became a national icon. After a 20‑year career with the BBC, he hosted the Radio 4 show “Something Understood” and wrote many books about India. His love of the country’s spiritual diversity earned him Britain’s knighthood and India’s Padma awards.
