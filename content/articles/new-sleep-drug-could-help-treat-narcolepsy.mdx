A new medicine might one day cure narcolepsy, a rare condition that makes people fall asleep suddenly. Scientists are studying a drug that could also help with insomnia. To diagnose narcolepsy, doctors put patients in a quiet room and see how quickly they fall asleep—less than eight minutes means narcolepsy, while normal people usually take about 22 minutes. This breakthrough could change how we treat sleep disorders.
