Trump announced the U.S. would ditch the EPA’s 2009 finding that climate change harms public health. The move removes a legal basis for the Clean Air Act to regulate greenhouse‑gas emissions. It weakens protections for vulnerable communities and lets polluters thrive. The article argues the decision is another example of Trump denying facts and putting American lives at greater risk from heat, floods and wildfires.
