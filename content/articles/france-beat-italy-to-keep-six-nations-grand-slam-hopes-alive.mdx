France beat Italy 44-15 in Lille, keeping their hopes of a Six Nations grand slam alive. Louis Bielle‑Biarrey and others scored early, putting France ahead. Italy tried to come back in the second half, but France’s defence held and the French added more tries, including a debut try from Gaël Dréan. The win means France stay at the top of the table and only need to beat England to secure the grand slam.
