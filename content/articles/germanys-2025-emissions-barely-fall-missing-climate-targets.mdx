Germany’s greenhouse‑gas output slipped by just 0.1% in 2025, falling short of the Climate Protection Act’s goal and the 1.5% cut experts expected. The country released 649 million tonnes of CO₂, 48% below 1990 levels but still a step behind. Environment Minister Carsten Schneider urged faster adoption of electric cars, heat pumps and wind power. He remains hopeful Germany can still hit the 2030 65% reduction target, despite a conservative government easing rules.
