For the 10th day of Iran’s nationwide protests, police fired tear gas and removed demonstrators from the historic Grand Bazaar in Tehran. More than 35 people have died and over 1,200 have been arrested since the unrest began over soaring prices and a weak economy. The president said the government can’t solve the crisis, while the central bank cut a dollar‑subsidy program that could worsen shortages. A video of officers raiding a hospital sparked U.S. condemnation, and Trump warned the Iranian regime could face intervention.
