An 80‑year‑old retired ballet dancer, Roy Morton, was mistakenly targeted by the Met during a dawn raid for a missing person. Police officers entered his Cricklewood home, fired a Taser at close range, and held him for 11 hours before releasing him. He sued for assault and false imprisonment, and the police settled out of court without admitting fault, after an internal review found no misconduct.
