Sam Groth, a former tennis star and Victorian deputy leader, resigned early, triggering a Nepean byelection. The Liberal state executive chose Mornington Peninsula mayor Anthony Marsh as the replacement, even though he joined the party weeks ago. Local branch members were left out of the decision, sparking anger and accusations that the party is ignoring long‑time members. The move could cost voters up to $2 million and hurt the Liberals' standing.
