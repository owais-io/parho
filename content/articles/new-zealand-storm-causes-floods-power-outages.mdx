A powerful low‑pressure system hit New Zealand’s North Island on Sunday, dumping over 100 mm of rain in some places, whipping gale‑force winds, and flooding rivers. The storm caused power outages, forced evacuations, and damaged buildings. The system stuck in place, keeping heavy rain on the same areas and raising the risk of more floods. In Europe, a similar storm brought high winds, flooding, and a collapsed bridge and many homes were affected.
