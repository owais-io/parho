Space storms can smear radio signals from far‑away worlds, a new study by SETI shows. The research explains why searches for alien broadcasts have been quiet: solar flares and stellar winds spread a narrow signal across many frequencies, lowering its peak strength. As a result, existing detection methods might miss the messages. The team suggests tweaking searches to look at wider bands.
