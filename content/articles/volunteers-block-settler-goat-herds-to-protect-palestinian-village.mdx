In the West Bank village of Ras Ein al‑Auja, Israeli settlers drive goats and camels toward homes every morning. A mix of Israeli, Hungarian, American and other volunteers step forward to block the animals, putting their bodies between settlers and villagers. The settlers aim to force Palestinians out; the volunteers say they protect the village from raids and police arrests. Their stand is a small but courageous push against the wider occupation.
