A Yellowstone hot spring called Black Diamond Pool blew up on Saturday, sending muddy water 40 feet into the air. Video from a new camera shows the splash against a snowy backdrop. The eruption was the first clear, visible one in over a year, after earlier blasts were only heard or filmed at night. Scientists put a new camera and sensors there in the summer to better track such events. The incident also helped close a boardwalk for safety.
