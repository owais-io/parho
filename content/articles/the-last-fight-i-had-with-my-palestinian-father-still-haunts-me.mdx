In 2021, the author and her Palestinian father fought over a Gaza comment while he was about to start chemo for stage‑four prostate cancer. The father, who had lived in the US and used the Italian name Frank Leone, was deeply upset by what he saw as a casual dismissal of Palestinian suffering. The argument pushed her to leave their desert home and return to New York, leaving the dying father behind. She now feels guilt over that last fight.
