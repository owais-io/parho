India has confirmed two cases of the deadly Nipah virus in West Bengal, the first since 2007. The outbreak has prompted high‑alert checks at airports in Thailand, Vietnam, Nepal and other Asian countries. Nipah spreads from animals like fruit bats and pigs, can cause fever, vomiting, pneumonia and brain swelling, and kills 40‑75% of patients. No vaccine exists, so authorities are testing contacts and keeping the situation under tight surveillance.
