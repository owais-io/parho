In late winter, a series of 16 powerful storms hit Spain, Portugal and Morocco, dumping record rain and unleashing winds that ripped roofs and trees. Whole towns like Grazalema in Spain and Leiria in Portugal were flooded, and 43 people were killed across the region. Scientists say climate change may have made the rains stronger, but the exact link is still unclear. Emergency warnings were weak, leaving many unprepared.
