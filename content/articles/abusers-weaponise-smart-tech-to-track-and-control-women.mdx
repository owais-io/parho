Domestic abusers are increasingly using smart watches, Fitbits and other tech to stalk, track and manipulate women. Refuge saw a 62 % jump in tech‑based abuse cases in late 2025, affecting 829 women and a rise in younger victims. Perpetrators exploited wearable data, smart‑home controls and AI‑spoofed videos to gaslight and intimidate. Experts urge safer device design and stronger digital‑crime laws.
