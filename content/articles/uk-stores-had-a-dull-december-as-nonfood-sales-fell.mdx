December sales rose just 1.2% in the UK, far below the 2.3% average, and non‑food items like clothes and gadgets slipped. Shoppers stayed on the sidelines, waiting for discounts, and credit‑card use fell 1.7%. Grocery prices kept climbing, so 64% of shoppers plan to spend less on food. Discount chains Aldi and Lidl saw record Christmas sales, but big names like Tesco and Sainsbury’s missed expectations and lost shares. Online rivals hit many retailers hard, forcing some to seek help.
