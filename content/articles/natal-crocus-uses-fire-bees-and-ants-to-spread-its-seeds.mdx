The Natal crocus, a South African plant, has learned to use fire, bees, and ants to help it grow. After a natural fire, its bright white flower blooms and attracts bees with a sweet scent, which pollinate it. The fruit stays buried for six months, and when ready the seeds trick ants into thinking they are carrying their own larvae, so the ants move them to their nests. This clever teamwork lets the plant spread and survive.
