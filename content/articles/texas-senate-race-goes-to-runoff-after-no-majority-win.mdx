In Texas, the Republican Senate race between long‑time Senator John Cornyn and Attorney General Ken Paxton ended with no candidate getting the required 50% of the vote, so the contest will move to a May 26 runoff. Cornyn leads by a few points, while third‑place congressman Wesley Hunt captured about 13% of the vote. Republicans fear Paxton’s scandals could cost the party the seat, so national GOP groups poured millions into Cornyn’s defense. Trump remains silent, but the fight will be costly and could affect Texas’s Senate balance.
