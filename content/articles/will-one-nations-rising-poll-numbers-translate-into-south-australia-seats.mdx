One Nation’s polling spike has fans hoping for a shock in South Australia’s March election. Pauline Hanson and former Liberal Cory Bernardi, now front‑liners, are pushing for seats in the lower house and an upper‑house presence. Despite a big Labor lead, polls show One Nation could win one or two seats, possibly becoming the main opposition. Critics point out the party’s past chaos and questions over its policies. The election will test whether the surge is real or just hype.
