Massive rains hit Oahu and the Big Island, flooding thousands of homes and covering streets in thick red volcanic mud. The deluge tore off roofs, washed cars away, and left kitchens, floors and furniture stained. Farmers suffered $9.4m in crop losses, while the state expects disaster costs to top $1bn. Gov. Josh Green is seeking federal aid and urges donations to help residents recover.
