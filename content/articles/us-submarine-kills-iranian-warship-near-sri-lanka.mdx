A US submarine fired a torpedo that sank the Iranian frigate Iris Dena off Sri Lanka’s coast, killing 87 sailors. The ship was returning from an Indian naval exercise in international waters. The strike raised legal questions and drew criticism from former US officials. Sri Lankan coastguards rescued 32 survivors and recovered 87 bodies. The event added to a wider US‑Iran conflict that has seen air strikes and missile exchanges across the Middle East.
