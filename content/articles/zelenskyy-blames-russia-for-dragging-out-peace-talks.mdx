The Geneva peace talks between Russia and Ukraine ended without a breakthrough. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy said Russia was dragging out negotiations, and key issues like eastern territories and the Zaporizhzhia nuclear plant remain unresolved. Both sides held military and political sessions, but progress was slow. Russia’s negotiator called the talks business‑like but difficult, and more rounds are planned. Zelenskyy wants security guarantees first before any territorial compromise.
