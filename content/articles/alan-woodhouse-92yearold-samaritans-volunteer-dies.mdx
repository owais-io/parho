Alan Woodhouse, a 92‑year‑old who had called the Samaritans in Liverpool for 60 years, has died. He was the charity’s longest‑serving volunteer, answering calls from the day the branch opened in 1960 until the Covid lockdowns stopped him. In 2017 he received an MBE for his work. He also helped renovate the branch and later taught French at Liverpool University. He is survived by three children.
