China’s population shrank for the fourth straight year in 2025, dropping to 1.405 billion as the number of babies born fell 17 % to 7.92 million. Deaths rose to 11.31 million. Beijing’s new childcare subsidies, IVF coverage and tax changes haven’t stopped families from saying children are too expensive, especially with high living costs and a shrinking pool of young parents. The country’s ageing population is adding extra pressure.
