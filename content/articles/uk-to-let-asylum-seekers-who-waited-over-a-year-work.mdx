The Home Office will let up to 21,000 asylum seekers who have waited more than a year to have their claims processed start working, so they can support themselves. The move is part of a plan to empty hotels and cut payments to people who break the law, work illegally or can live without aid. Refugee groups worry the cuts could push people into rough sleeping. The government says it will create a safe route for student refugees in autumn.
