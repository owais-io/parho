Three café owners in north London are temporarily out of danger after the City of London Corporation agreed not to force them out next week. Patrick Matthews and Emma Fernandez ran cafés at Parliament Hill Lido, Queen’s Park and Highgate Wood. The corporation had chosen new operators after a retendering process, but the owners say the procedure was unfair. With legal help and public support, they’re fighting to keep their businesses.
