In England, the number of patients who walk out of emergency rooms without getting treatment has tripled in six years, from 100,000 to 320,000. Waiting more than 12 hours has jumped over 100 times. Doctors blame overworked hospitals and weak community care. Fewer people can afford private care, so they stay in the NHS. The government says it will spend nearly £450 million this winter to improve urgent care.
