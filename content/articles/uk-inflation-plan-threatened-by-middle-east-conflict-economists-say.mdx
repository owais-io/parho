The latest rise in oil and gas prices, caused by the Middle East crisis, could derail Chancellor Rachel Reeves’s strategy to lower inflation and boost growth. Economists warn that higher energy costs will push up living costs and hurt the UK's economic outlook. The government may need to pause a planned fuel duty increase and will rely on more interest‑rate cuts to support spending and investment.
