The Bank of England keeps its main borrowing cost at 3.75% and said it could raise rates again within a few months. The rise would be driven by the war in the Middle East, which is pushing up energy prices. Higher costs are already pushing inflation over 3%, so the bank wants to bring it back to the 2% target. This move means households may see higher bills soon.
