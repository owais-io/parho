An 80‑year‑old woman in San Francisco was found responsible for a crash that killed four people. She was given two years of probation, a driver‑license suspension, and must do 200 hours of community service. There was no jail time. She pleaded no contest. The judge said she was remorseful and that the sentence keeps her accountable while acknowledging her personal loss. Victims’ families still want harsher penalties.
