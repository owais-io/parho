Bruce Haigh, an Australian diplomat who worked in South Africa in the late 1970s, was added to the country’s Wall of Names at Freedom Park, a list of people who helped end apartheid. Haigh helped anti‑apartheid activists like Steve Biko and Donald Woods, smuggling them out of the country. He was praised for his courage and empathy. His name was added alongside former Australian prime ministers. South African officials said Haigh’s actions helped end racial discrimination.
