The Brazilian movie “The Blue Trail” imagines a future where an authoritarian government honors seniors with golden medals but then forces them into isolated colonies, a satire on ageism. The story follows 77‑year‑old Tereza, who refuses to be left behind and instead explores the Amazon to chase her dreams. The film has struck a chord with women, showing that age should not stop adventure or dignity. With Brazil’s population growing fast, the movie sparks debate on how society treats its elders.
