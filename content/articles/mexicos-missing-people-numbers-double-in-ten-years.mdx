In Mexico, the number of people who have vanished has jumped about twice as many as a decade ago, a rise blamed on drug gangs that now kill and hide bodies to avoid police. Families like Ángel Montenegro’s mother keep searching for missing relatives because the state’s investigations are slow and often ineffective. A weak national registry was trimmed before elections, sparking outrage, and most crimes stay unsolved.
