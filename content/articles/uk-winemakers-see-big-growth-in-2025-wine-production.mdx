In 2025, winemakers in England and Wales produced 16.5 million bottles – the second‑largest UK harvest ever. The hot, dry summer and more vineyard plantings boosted yields by 55% over 2024, when rain had cut production in half. White wine sales shot up 131%. While still below the 2023 peak, the sector now employs over 10,000 people and is worth £14 bn.
