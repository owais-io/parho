Tarique Rahman, who spent 17 years in exile, is back in Bangladesh to lead the BNP in the first free election since former leader Sheikh Hasina was overthrown. He pledges to fight corruption and run the country from the top down. The vote could return the BNP to power after 20 years, while the Islamist Jamaat party also gains seats. The election marks a crucial step toward restoring democracy and improving ties with India.
