South Korea is waiting for a verdict on former President Yoon Suk‑yeol, who is charged with leading an insurrection. Prosecutors want a death penalty, but the country has not executed anyone since 1997. The trial echoes a 1980s military coup and has divided the nation. Public fatigue is high, and Yoon’s supporters have dwindled. The outcome will test the country’s democratic safeguards and shape the political future.
