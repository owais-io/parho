A 31‑year‑old man from Bankstown was arrested after he sent emails threatening to kill NSW communications minister Anika Wells and her family. Police traced the messages to him and took his devices during a raid. He faces up to ten years in jail. The case comes amid other controversies, including Wells’ recent travel‑expense scandal, but the threats were made before those were public. The Australian Federal Police say they will not tolerate threats against elected officials.
