U.S. officials are moving unaccompanied pregnant minors to a single Texas facility to keep them from getting abortions. Many of these girls are pregnant after rape. Texas has almost no abortion law exceptions. Critics argue the move violates international human‑rights rules and deprives vulnerable children of needed medical care. The government says it is protecting their safety, but human‑rights groups say the policy forces children into a state that can’t provide abortion services.
