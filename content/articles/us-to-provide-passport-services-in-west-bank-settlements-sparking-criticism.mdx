The United States will give passport and consular services to people in two Israeli settlements in the occupied West Bank. The first time the U.S. has done this, it sparked anger from Palestinian officials who say it breaks international law and supports Israel’s illegal occupation. Israel’s foreign minister welcomed the move. The U.S. plans more services in another settlement, a Palestinian city, and several towns inside Israel.
