Australia is reviewing its animal‑research rules after a decade. The debate centers on whether crabs count as animals, how much we should test on monkeys, and the need for better data and stricter oversight. Experts, animal‑rights groups and universities want clear rules on animal sentience, limits on high‑harm studies, and stronger enforcement. The review invites comments until Feb. 16 and aims to draft new rules by late 2026.
