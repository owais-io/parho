Congressional Progressive Caucus chair Greg Casar is urging Democrats to insist on real changes to Immigration and Customs Enforcement before voting on a $10 billion DHS spending bill. He lists five non‑negotiable items: no masks for agents, independent investigations of killings, ICE withdrawal from Minneapolis, no citizen arrests or deportations, and end to arrest quotas. Republicans want the DHS bill bundled with other funding, risking a partial shutdown. The debate follows the killing of Alex Pretti by federal agents.
