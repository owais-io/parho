Major League Rugby and its players’ union have signed a new agreement that gives players better pay, health protection, and a $2,000 upfront payment. The deal comes after last year’s turmoil, when four teams quit and two merged, leaving only six teams. The contract sets a new standard for player safety, stabilises the league and lets the union reopen talks next season.
