Donald Trump said Venezuela will ship about $2 billion worth of oil to the United States, a deal that could reduce the country’s exports to China and help avoid deeper production cuts. The move follows U.S. pressure and the removal of President Maduro. Venezuelan officials are vague, and interim president Delcy Rodríguez warned the U.S. that it does not control Venezuela. The oil would be sold at market price and shipped to U.S. ports.
