Trump has been warning that the U.S. will bomb Iran’s civilian infrastructure, then quickly pulling back those threats. Markets jumped when the threat was made, but fell again after he said talks were going well. This back‑and‑forth has lost weight—investors see Iran still in control and the U.S. has little leverage. With the midterm elections near and oil prices rising, Trump’s threat‑and‑back‑down approach is proving ineffective.
