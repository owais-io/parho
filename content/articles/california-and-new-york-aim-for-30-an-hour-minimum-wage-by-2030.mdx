Workers in Oakland and other parts of California are fighting to raise the city and county minimum wage to $30 an hour by 2030, while New York City is also pushing a similar bill. The plan targets big employers first and is backed by local labor groups who say the current $17‑plus wage can’t cover living costs. Businesses argue the hike will hurt them, but studies show it doesn’t cut jobs.
