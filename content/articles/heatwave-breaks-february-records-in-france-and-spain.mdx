In February, Spain and France saw unusually hot days, breaking long‑term February records. Cities like Bilbao and San Sebastián hit 27°C, while Saint Gladie reached 29.6°C, well above the normal 13–16°C for the month. The warmth came from a big high‑pressure area pushing warm southerly winds and a Föhn effect that heats air as it slides down mountains. The high pressure should keep temperatures higher than normal for the rest of the week.
