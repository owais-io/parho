A federal advisory group, which helps the CDC decide on vaccines, held a heated meeting about whether to let babies wait longer than 24 hours to get the hepatitis B shot. The panel, now full of people who question vaccines, has pushed back the vote and said it needs more time to look over the wording. Changing the rule could affect how many babies get protected against a serious liver disease.
