At Jesse Jackson’s memorial in Chicago, former presidents Barack Obama, Bill Clinton and Joe Biden, along with Kamala Harris, spoke about the civil‑rights leader’s legacy. They called him a champion for the poor and a powerful organizer, saying his 1988 presidential run helped pave the way for Barack Obama’s own election. Jackson, who died last month after a long illness, is remembered for fighting for voting rights, health care and equal opportunity.
