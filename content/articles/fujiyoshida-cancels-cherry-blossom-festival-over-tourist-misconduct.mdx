Fujiyoshida city in Japan has cancelled its annual cherry blossom festival because of repeated disruptive tourist behavior, including trespassing, littering and even defecating on private property. The event, which usually draws about 200,000 visitors, has been halted to protect local residents and their quiet way of life. Officials will still host visitors at the park, adding more security, parking and portable toilets to manage crowds.
