In Gaza, people hear the word “peace” more often than the sound of rockets. Since a ceasefire was announced in October, Israeli forces have killed hundreds, especially children, and many homes are destroyed. Families live in cramped, unsafe apartments or tents, with no electricity, limited water, and no reliable money. The author shares her daily fear, the lack of safety, and the painful wait to reunite with loved ones, hoping the world’s talks will bring real relief.
