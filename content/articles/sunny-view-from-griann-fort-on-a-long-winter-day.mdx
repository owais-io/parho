The writer stands near the Grianán of Aileach ringfort in northern Ireland on a long winter day. Late‑afternoon sun highlights the high walls and the nearby Inch Levels reserve, where winter swans and geese glide across flooded fields. He reflects on the fort’s history as the seat of the Owen dynasty and its 19th‑century rebuild, and on the peaceful view that feels like a palace of sunlight.
