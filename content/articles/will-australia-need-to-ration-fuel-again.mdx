Australia’s fuel supply is under pressure after a global energy shock caused by the US-Israel war on Iran pushed oil prices up. Petrol stations are running low and the government is considering measures like fuel rationing, which the country last used in 1979. While officials say reserves and imports should keep most people supplied, experts warn that supply disruptions and low spare capacity could last months, so steps to use less fuel and new policies are being discussed.
