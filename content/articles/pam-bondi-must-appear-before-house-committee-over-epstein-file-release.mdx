The U.S. Attorney General, Pam Bondi, has been subpoenaed by a House committee to explain how the Justice Department handled the release of Jeffrey Epstein documents. Lawmakers from both sides are upset that some files were hidden or redacted, and they worry that survivors’ private information might have been exposed. Bondi will give a sworn deposition on April 14, after the committee asked her to explain the department’s actions and compliance with the new transparency law.
