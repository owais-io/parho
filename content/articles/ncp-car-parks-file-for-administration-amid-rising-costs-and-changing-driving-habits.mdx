NCP, a historic London car‑park chain owned by Japan’s Park24, has filed for administration after it can’t pay £305 million in debt and rising rents. A fall in driving post‑pandemic, long leases that can’t be lowered, and inflation‑linked rent have made the business unprofitable. The move threatens 682 jobs and the future of 340 UK car parks, some of which may be sold or redeveloped, but the NCP brand is likely to disappear.
