Sam Dumitriu says we can stop protecting nature by easing laws, but the writer argues that legal rules are the only thing that keeps the few habitats left. In the last century England lost almost all fens, most grasslands, many woodlands and saltmarshes. Developers cut habitats and the rules that stop this are weak and poorly enforced. The article calls for stronger protection and more money for conservation, so Britain can still reach net‑zero and keep wildlife.
