The daughter of Reuven Morrison, a victim of the Bondi terror attack, says her father was the man seen in video throwing bricks at gunman Sajid Akram. She says he jumped up when the shooting began, shouted, and tried to protect the crowd, but was shot and killed. The footage also shows Ahmed al‑Ahmed disarming the gunman. Morrison was a generous businessman who moved from the former Soviet Union to Australia. The daughter blames the government for ignoring warning signs.
