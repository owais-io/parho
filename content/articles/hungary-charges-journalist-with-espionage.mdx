Police in Hungary have charged popular investigative journalist Szabolcs Panyi with spying for Ukraine after claims that the foreign minister, Péter Szijjártó, regularly talked to Russia’s Sergei Lavrov and may have shared EU secrets. President Viktor Orbán’s government says Panyi worked with foreign intelligence. The case comes as Hungary’s ruling party faces an election, low poll numbers, and growing criticism over media freedom and rule‑of‑law abuses.
