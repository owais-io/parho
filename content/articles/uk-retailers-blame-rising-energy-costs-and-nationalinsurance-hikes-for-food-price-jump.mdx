Retailers say higher energy bills and a rise in employers’ national insurance charges pushed food prices up. In January, food inflation hit 3.9% year‑on‑year, up from 3.3% in December. The British Retail Consortium says the new 15% NIC rate and lower threshold force suppliers to raise prices. The Treasury says the changes help cut debt, but inflation remains sticky. Shoppers may still find some discounts.
