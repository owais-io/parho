Taylor Wimpey’s chief says buyers, especially first‑time ones, are still cautious even as house prices ease a bit. The company’s shares dropped 4.7% after the announcement. They built 6% more homes in 2025, but expect profit margins to shrink and order books to stay thin. Other builders and estate agents, like Foxtons and Savills, echo the same concerns about a sluggish market and economic uncertainty.
