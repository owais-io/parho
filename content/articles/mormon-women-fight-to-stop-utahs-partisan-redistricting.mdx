In Utah, a group of Mormon women called Mormon Women for Ethical Government is working to stop partisan gerrymandering. They sued the state over new congressional maps that split Democratic voters in Salt Lake City. Their fight won a court ruling that forces Utah to redraw the lines, giving Democrats a better chance to win a seat in 2026. The group also talks about how faith can guide civic action.
