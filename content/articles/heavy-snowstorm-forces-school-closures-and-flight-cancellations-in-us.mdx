A fast‑moving winter storm called Hernando dumped record snow across the Northeast, causing over 10,000 flight cancellations and school shutdowns in places like New York, Boston, and Portland. The blizzard brought wind gusts over 70 mph and left millions without power. Some spots saw up to 38 cm of snow, while Rhode Island recorded 96 cm. The storm, dubbed a “bomb cyclone” because its pressure dropped rapidly, killed two people.
