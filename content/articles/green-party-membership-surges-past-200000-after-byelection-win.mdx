After winning a surprise victory in the Gorton and Denton byelection, the UK Green Party’s membership jumped to over 200,000, three times the size it had a year earlier. The win gave the Greens five MPs and pushed Labour into third place. Leader Zack Polanski says the surge shows progressive voters want a new direction. The rise worries Labour, which may need to shift left to keep those voters.
