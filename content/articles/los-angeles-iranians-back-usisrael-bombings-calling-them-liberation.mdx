In Los Angeles, a city with the world’s largest Iranian community, many Iranians have flipped their view after Israel and the U.S. bombed Iranian targets. Once skeptical, now they see the strikes as a form of liberation and want the Iranian regime gone. Some even back the ex‑crown prince Reza Pahlavi as a temporary leader. The community is split, and some experts warn that such unity may be a dangerous “group‑think” that could backfire.
