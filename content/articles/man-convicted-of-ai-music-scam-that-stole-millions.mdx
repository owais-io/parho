A 52‑year‑old North Carolina man, Michael Smith, pleaded guilty in New York to a scheme that used AI to create fake songs and bots to generate billions of fake streams. The fraud netted him more than $10 million in royalty payments that should have gone to real artists. He faces up to five years in prison and must forfeit about $8 million. The case shows how AI can undermine music‑streaming revenue.
