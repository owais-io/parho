Royal Mail said bad weather and more staff being sick caused many missed delivery rounds and letters that were left undelivered for weeks. Storms in January and a rise in sickness meant letters and parcels were delayed. Workers say parcels get priority over letters, a claim the company denies. The new owner’s takeover and a £21 m fine from Ofcom added to the problem.
