Data stolen from the Department of Homeland Security shows the agency is paying companies to build AI tools that could watch people at airports, scan phones for fingerprints and eyes, and turn 911 calls into heat maps that predict crime. The leak lists over 6,800 companies and 1,400 contracts worth $845 million. Critics warn the projects risk privacy violations, racial bias and a move toward predictive policing.
