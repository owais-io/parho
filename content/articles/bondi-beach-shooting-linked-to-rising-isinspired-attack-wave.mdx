Australian experts say the 14‑December shooting at a Hanukah festival on Bondi Beach fits a global spike in attacks inspired by the Islamic State. The study links the attack to a 2024 IS message urging lone‑wolf attacks against Jewish and holiday events. The two suspects, Naveed and Sajid Akram, trained with guns and planned the hit while other plots were foiled in Europe, the US and Turkey. Police say there is no evidence they were part of a larger cell.
