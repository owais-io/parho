Owen Jones writes that Labour’s right wing is blaming Keir Starmer for the party’s woes. He claims Starmer’s lack of vision and charisma hurt Labour, and that older Blair‑era figures like Wes Streeting are pulling the strings. Starmer’s policies on the welfare state, immigration and the economy have made the party unpopular, and the future may hinge on a new leader.
