Will Lewis, the Washington Post’s publisher, stepped down just days after the paper cut almost a third of its staff. The move comes after 300 reporters were let go in one of the biggest media layoffs ever. Lewis said the cuts were needed to keep the Post financially healthy. Jeff D’Onofrio is now acting publisher, while owner Jeff Bezos praised the new team. The staff and critics blame Lewis for the heavy cuts.
