The US Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP) recently changed its hepatitis‑B vaccine recommendation, sparking worry among health experts. The new panel is led by former vaccine critics appointed by health secretary RFK. Many fear the move could undermine the whole childhood schedule, lower vaccination rates, and cause confusion for parents and insurers. Experts say there is no evidence of new safety risks, but misinformation is spreading and measles outbreaks are rising.
