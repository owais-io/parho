Minneapolis tenants are trying to use a rent strike to fight rising rents. They formed a new union called Twin Cities Tenants and wanted 10,000 people to withhold rent in March. The plan would hit landlords with about $15 million in lost rent. But the organizers couldn’t get enough people to join, so the strike was called off. The city also denied an eviction‑policy extension. Still, tenants hope to keep using rent as a tool.
