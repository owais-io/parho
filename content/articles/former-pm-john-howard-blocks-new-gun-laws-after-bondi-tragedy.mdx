After a tragic antisemitic attack on Bondi beach, Australians want stronger gun laws. Prime Minister Albanese said the government would talk about tightening gun rules, but the changes are vague and slow. Former Prime Minister John Howard publicly attacked Albanese’s plans, saying they were weak. Howard’s criticism turns the issue into a cultural debate, making it harder for the government to pass real gun‑law changes.
