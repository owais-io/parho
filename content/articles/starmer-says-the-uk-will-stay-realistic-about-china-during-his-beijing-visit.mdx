UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer flew to Beijing to meet President Xi and Premier Li Qiang. He said the UK will stay realistic about China, balancing economic benefits with security concerns. Starmer wants a steady, clear approach after the Tories’ inconsistent stance. He will meet China’s leaders, bring a 60‑person business delegation, and discuss trade while warning against human‑rights abuses. Critics say he’s too soft on China, but he argues engagement keeps Britain safe and prosperous.
