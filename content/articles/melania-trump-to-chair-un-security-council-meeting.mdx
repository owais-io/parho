Melania Trump will chair a United Nations Security Council meeting on March 2, the White House said. She will focus on how education can build tolerance and help end conflicts. This is the first time a U.S. first lady has led a Security Council session, and it happens while the U.S. holds the council’s monthly presidency. The session will also discuss children, technology and peace, and highlight efforts to return Ukrainian children taken during Russia’s invasion.
