Meg O’Neill, the former boss of Australian gas giant Woodside, will step down after being tapped to run BP. She’s known for calling young climate critics “ideological”. BP has recently dropped its net‑zero pledge and is pumping more oil and gas. O’Neill guided Woodside through big projects and earned $11 bn in dividends, but her move sparks debate about fossil‑fuel leaders.
