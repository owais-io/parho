Instagram announced it will stop using end‑to‑end encryption on direct messages from May 8, 2026. That means the company can read all private chats, not just those that are currently unencrypted. The move follows criticism from law‑enforcement and child‑safety groups that said encryption made it harder to spot abuse. Instagram says only a small number of users used the feature, so it was dropped. Users can still use WhatsApp for encrypted chat.
