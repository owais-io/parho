Atlassian has fired 1,600 employees—about 10% of its staff—after a rapid move toward AI tools. Workers from engineering, design and product teams, many of whom had long tenures or were on leave, were let go with little notice and unclear severance details. The layoffs followed a sharp fall in the company’s value, from $77 bn to about $13 bn, and a new performance system that stressed AI use. Employees feel blindsided and say the company must stay agile and profit‑focused.
