Gary Walters spent 37 years as the White House chief usher, watching every president and first family. He remembers Nancy Reagan’s dress‑rehearsal dinners, a jogging track built for President Clinton, and the tense moment when Gorbachev signed the nuclear treaty. Walters also recalls pulling up the 9/11 picnic tables so Marine One could land after the attacks. His book offers a rare, personal look at life behind the scenes in the West Wing.
