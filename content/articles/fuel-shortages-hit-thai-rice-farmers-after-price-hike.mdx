Thailand’s rice farmers are struggling because diesel is scarce and more expensive. The war in the Middle East has pushed fuel prices up, and many gas stations run out of stock. Farmers like Thanadet have to save fuel for watering crops and harvesting, while fishing crews and even taxis are affected. The government is offering support, but the shortages could hurt food production and raise hunger risks across the region.
