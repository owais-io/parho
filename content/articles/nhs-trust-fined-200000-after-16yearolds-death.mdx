Ellame Ford‑Dunn, 16, left a Worthing hospital ward under 24‑hour supervision and died moments later. The supervising nurse didn’t chase her, because the trust’s rules said not to follow a patient who absconds. The NHS trust was fined £200,000 for criminal health and safety offences. Parents say the system failed them, while the trust admits its policies were unclear and pledges improvements to protect vulnerable young people.
