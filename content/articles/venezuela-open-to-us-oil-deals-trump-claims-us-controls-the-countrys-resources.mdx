Venezuela’s interim leader, Delcy Rodríguez, said her country is ready to sell oil to the United States, even after critics say the U.S. wants to run Venezuela’s oil industry. U.S. officials, including Vice‑President JD Vance, insist the U.S. will manage all Venezuelan oil sales and only allow sales that serve U.S. interests. President Trump announced a $2 billion oil deal and said Venezuela would buy only U.S. products from the proceeds. Oil companies want guarantees before investing.
