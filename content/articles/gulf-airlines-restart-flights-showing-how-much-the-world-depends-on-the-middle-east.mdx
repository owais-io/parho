Emirates and Etihad have begun flying again after a week of Gulf airports being closed because of a US‑Israel‑Iran conflict. The pause shows how much the world relies on Dubai, Abu Dhabi and Doha as travel hubs. The break pushed airlines and governments to scramble for other routes, and fuel prices have shot up. Some carriers warn of higher fares, while others say they’ll still try to offer cheaper tickets once traffic returns.
