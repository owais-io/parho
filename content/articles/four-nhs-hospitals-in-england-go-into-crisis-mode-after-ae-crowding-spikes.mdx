Four NHS hospitals in England have declared a crisis after a sharp rise in A&E visits, mostly from flu, norovirus and other cold‑weather illnesses. The three Surrey trusts – Royal Surrey, Epsom and St Helier, and Surrey and Sussex – and one in Kent – Queen Elizabeth the Queen Mother – say their beds are full and staff are sick. All hospitals stay open for urgent care and staff are urging patients to call 999 or 111 for help.
