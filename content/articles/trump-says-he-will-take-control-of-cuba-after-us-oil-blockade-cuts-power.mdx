After a U.S. oil blockade left Cuba in a total power blackout, President Donald Trump said he would “take Cuba” and could do whatever he wanted with the island. He hinted at removing President Miguel Díaz‑Canel and toppling the communist regime, though Cuba says it will not accept outside interference. The move follows U.S. pressure on Cuba and the U.S. remains ready to impose sanctions.
