Two trains crashed in Andalusia on Sunday night, killing 39 people and injuring 12. The wreckage was near the town of Adamuz, with about 4,000 residents. Police, firefighters and volunteers rushed to the site and then to the town hall, which became an emergency centre. Residents brought blankets, food and medical supplies, while the Guardia Civil set up offices for families to find information and leave DNA samples. The tragedy shocked the community, but everyone pulled together to help survivors.
