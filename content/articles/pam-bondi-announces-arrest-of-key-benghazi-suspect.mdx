US attorney general Pam Bondi announced Friday that a key suspect in the 2012 Benghazi attack, Zubayar al‑Bakoush, has been taken into U.S. custody. He faces charges for murder, terrorism and arson. The arrest comes after years of investigations into the attack that killed four Americans, including ambassador Christopher Stevens. Other suspects have been tried before, and the case highlights past security gaps.
