Six women who were drugged and raped by a Denver cardiologist filed a lawsuit against Match Group, the company that owns Tinder and Hinge. They say the apps let the known abuser stay active and even promoted him, creating a dangerous environment. A report found Match Group kept the killer active and cut safety teams. The suit argues the apps ignored reports, violating users’ safety, and faces legal hurdles under Section 230.
