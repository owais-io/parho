Pat McFadden, Britain’s work and pensions secretary, said Labour MPs should support new welfare changes and revealed a £1 billion plan to create 200,000 jobs for 18‑24‑year‑olds on benefits. Companies get £3,000 for each hire and £2,000 for apprenticeships, while the minimum wage rise for young workers will be slower. He also said the government will keep looking for ways to overhaul the welfare system after last year's backbench revolt.
