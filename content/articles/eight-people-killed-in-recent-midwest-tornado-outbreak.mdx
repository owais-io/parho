Over a weekend, 18 tornadoes hit central US, mainly Michigan and Oklahoma, killing eight people and injuring dozens. The strongest were EF2‑EF3 storms with winds up to 165 mph, devastating towns like Union City, Michigan, where homes were ripped from foundations and many cars were crushed. Governor Gretchen Whitmer declared a state of emergency, sending help and resources to the hardest‑hit communities.
