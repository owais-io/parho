Lemi Limbu, a woman with severe intellectual disability, had her death sentence cancelled after more than a decade on death row in Tanzania. A court ruled she was not fit to be tried and allowed her to appeal, but a retrial is still pending. Human‑rights groups say she should never have been imprisoned. Limbu’s case highlights problems in the country’s justice system and the treatment of vulnerable people.
