After the May 2025 local elections, Reform UK won over 600 seats and took control of 10 councils, including Kent and County Durham. They promised to cut waste, lower council tax and overhaul council operations. While they claim savings, critics question their promises and accuse them of raising taxes, with Worcestershire expected to issue England’s biggest council‑tax rise this April. The article asks residents in Reform‑led areas how their expectations have held up.
