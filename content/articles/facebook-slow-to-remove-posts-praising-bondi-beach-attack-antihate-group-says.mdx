Facebook was slow to take down posts that praised the Bondi beach attack, an anti‑hate group said. The posts celebrated the murder of 15 Jews in Sydney and praised the Islamic State. Some were still online a few days after the attack, and were liked and shared. Facebook said it was removing some of the content, but critics say the platform failed to act quickly enough. Authorities urge faster action.
