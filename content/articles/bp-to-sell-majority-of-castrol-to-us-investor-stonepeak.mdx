BP has agreed to sell 65% of its lubricants arm, Castrol, to U.S. investment firm Stonepeak. The deal values Castrol at about $10 billion and is expected to finish by the end of next year. BP will keep 35% of the business through a joint venture, giving it a chance to earn from Castrol’s future growth. The sale will help BP pay down debt and focus on core oil and gas operations.
