A bushfire started in the Fleurieu Peninsula, south of Adelaide, and has scorched over 650 hectares, damaging three buildings, including a family's home. Around 120 firefighters, plus volunteers, are battling the blaze in difficult terrain. Residents in places like Tooperang and Nangkita were warned to leave, and a motorhome was destroyed shortly after an evacuation alert. No injuries have been reported, but the fire danger remains high as hotter, drier weather continues.
