A truck will leave CERN’s campus on a 20‑minute test drive carrying a one‑tonne container that holds about 1,000 antimatter particles—tiny, powerful anti‑matter that annihilates when it touches regular matter. If the container stays intact, CERN will be able to ship antimatter to other labs for very precise experiments that could explain why the universe is made of matter, not antimatter.
