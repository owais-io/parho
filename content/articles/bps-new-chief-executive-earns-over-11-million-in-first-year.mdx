Meg O’Neill, BP’s first woman CEO and the company’s first external hire, will earn at least £11.7 million this year. Her package includes a £1.6 million salary and large share awards from Woodside Energy that will vest over the next few years. That’s more than double the £5.3 million paid to former CEO Murray Auchincloss. BP still faces pressure from shareholders to lift performance after a shaky year. If she earns bonuses, total could rise further.
