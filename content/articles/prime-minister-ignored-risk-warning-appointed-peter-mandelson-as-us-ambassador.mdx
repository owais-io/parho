New documents show UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer overruled officials’ warnings about a “reputational risk” when appointing former minister Peter Mandelson as U.S. ambassador. The papers say senior advisers were told Mandelson kept close ties with Jeffrey Epstein after Epstein’s conviction, but Starmer said he was satisfied with Mandelson’s explanations. The case raises questions about how the government checks diplomatic appointments and whether security procedures were sidestepped.
