In eastern Slovakia, a student wrote anti‑Prime Minister Fico slogans in chalk on a school pavement. The simple act sparked a nationwide wave of chalk messages, called the “Chalk Revolution,” as people protest his pro‑Russia stance and accusations of slipping back into authoritarian rule. The protests grew, drawing police, canceling Fico’s lecture, and gathering crowds. The movement continues ahead of the 2027 election, with more chalk art and rallies.
