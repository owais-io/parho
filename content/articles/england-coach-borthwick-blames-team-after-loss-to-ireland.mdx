After England lost 53‑10 to Ireland in the Six Nations, coach Steve Borthwick said the team had to blame itself for the steep decline. He blamed poor starts, too many turnovers and a weak first‑half performance. He also defended fly‑half George Ford, while players like Ellis Genge and Maro Itoje apologized and promised to improve. Ireland’s defence and kicking were praised as key to the win.
