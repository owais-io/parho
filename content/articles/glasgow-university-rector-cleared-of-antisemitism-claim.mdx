Dr Ghassan Abu‑Sittah, the student‑elected rector of the University of Glasgow, was cleared by a medical regulator after complaints from a pro‑Jewish group. The tribunal found no proof that his comments about Palestinian militants were antisemitic or encouraging violence. He had been questioned over tweets praising Hamas and other groups. The panel said ordinary readers would see them as political support, not terrorism. He remains free to continue his duties.
