Because too few new antibiotics are being made, experts say the G7 must invest together. The problem is a market failure: if antibiotics are saved for only the most serious cases, they sell less and companies lose money, so they drop research. Britain’s NHS–Nice subscription pays firms a set fee for each new drug, regardless of use. Coordinated G7 funding could bring huge health returns and save millions of lives.
