In the first round of France’s municipal elections, far‑right National Rally and left‑wing LFI surprised many by gaining a lot of votes. The National Rally hit 35% in Marseille and kept the mayor’s seat in Perpignan, while LFI won in towns like Roubaix, Toulouse, and Lille. Traditionally, the Socialists and Les Républicains have dominated city councils, but the new results show a shift. A second‑round runoff on 22 March will decide who keeps the power.
