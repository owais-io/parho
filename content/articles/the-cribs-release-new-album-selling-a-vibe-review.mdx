The Cribs’ latest album, Selling a Vibe, shows the trio’s steady, no‑fuss indie rock after 22 years. The songs mix distorted guitars and tight pop beats, with a hint of ’80s sheen. Lyrics look back at past legal fights, fading youth, and the band’s longevity. The review notes the album’s confident, balanced sound and the brothers’ bond, proving the Cribs are still a solid, mature force rather than chasing headlines.
