Sunderland’s National Glass Centre, a landmark built in 1996 to showcase the city’s long glass‑making history, may close in July because the university says repairs could cost as much as £45 m. Many locals, politicians and heritage groups think the figures are exaggerated and are campaigning to keep the building open. The dispute has become a political flash‑point, with parties from the Liberal Democrats to the far right involved.
