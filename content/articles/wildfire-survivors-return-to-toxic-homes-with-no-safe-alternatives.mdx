One year after the Eaton wildfire in Altadena, many families are moving back into houses that still carry ash, lead and asbestos. The cleanup often left toxic dust in walls, floors and mattresses. Residents suffer headaches, asthma and fight insurance companies that refuse extra tests. A local group, Eaton Fire Residents United, is pushing for thorough checks before people return. Survivors feel trapped, fearing unseen dangers in what used to be their safe homes.
