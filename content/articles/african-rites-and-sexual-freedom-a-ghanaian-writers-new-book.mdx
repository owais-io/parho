Ghanaian writer Nana Darkoa Sekyiamah launches a new book that looks at African puberty rites like Dipo and Uganda’s “sex auntie” role, the ssenga. She argues that useful traditional practices can help teach girls about bodies, pleasure and healthy sexuality, offering a gentle alternative to conservative attitudes. By reviving these rituals, she hopes to create new models of sexual freedom and better sex education across Africa.
