Kemi Badenoch is in hot water after it emerged her shadow attorney‑general, David Wolfson, is working for Russian billionaire Roman Abramovich, who is under UK sanctions. Wolfson’s legal team is trying to unlock billions of frozen assets in the Channel Islands, delaying £2.5 bn from Chelsea’s sale that the government wants to give to Ukraine. Labour says the relationship is a conflict of interest and wants Wolfson to quit the shadow cabinet. The Conservatives defend the lawyer, citing the cab‑rank rule.
