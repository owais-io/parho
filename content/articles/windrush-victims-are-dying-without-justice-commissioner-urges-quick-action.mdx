The Windrush commissioner, Rev Clive Foster, says that many victims are dying without justice and urges the government to act quickly. He called for a public inquiry and new laws to fix the messy, slow compensation process that still hurts survivors like Deborah, whose brother cannot return from Barbados. Labour has adopted some of his suggestions, but critics fear a new government could stall reforms. The case shows the urgent need for real change, not just apologies.
