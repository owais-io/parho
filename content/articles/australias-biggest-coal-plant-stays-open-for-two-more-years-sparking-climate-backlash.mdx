New South Wales has agreed to keep the Eraring coal power station running until 2029, extending its life by two years. Origin Energy says the extra time will help keep prices stable and give renewables more time to catch up. Climate groups call the move a disaster, saying it hurts the state’s emissions targets and pushes back clean‑energy progress.
