After Afghan border troops were hit, Pakistan launched airstrikes on Kabul, Kandahar and Paktia, saying it was an “open war”. The strikes killed at least 12 civilians and hit Taliban offices. Both sides blame each other for the violence. The fighting is part of a long‑standing dispute over militants and border security. It shows how peace talks are stalled and civilians keep paying the price.
