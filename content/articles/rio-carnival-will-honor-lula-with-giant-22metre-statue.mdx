Rio’s biggest carnival parade will feature a giant metal figure of former President Lula. The 22‑metre statue, taller than the Sphinx, is part of a 2026 show that tells Lula’s story—from poor childhood to three terms in office. The parade, organized by the Acadêmicos de Niterói samba school, also includes dancers representing his work on education and democracy. The event comes just before Brazil’s presidential election, raising concerns about campaigning, but organisers say it is a celebration of history.
