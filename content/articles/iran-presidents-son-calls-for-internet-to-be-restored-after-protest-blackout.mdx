The son of Iran’s new president, Yousef Pezeshkian, says the country’s internet blackout is worsening public anger and should be lifted. He argues that keeping the network down only delays the truth about the violent crackdown on protests, where thousands of people – including children – may have been killed. The shutdown also hurts the economy, costing the country about $20 million a day. Inside Iran, officials are split over whether to reopen the internet.
