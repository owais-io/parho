The U.S. Department of Health and Human Services has stopped all federal money for childcare programs until states can prove the funds are being used properly. The pause covers every state and demands documents like attendance logs, licensing, inspections, and complaints. The move follows a viral video that alleged fraud at Somali‑run daycares in Minnesota, sparking accusations of targeting Somali Americans. The freeze is part of a broader Trump effort to tighten scrutiny of social‑service programs.
