The BBC has struck a deal with YouTube to produce brand‑new shows just for the video platform. The content will cover entertainment, news and sport, starting with the Winter Olympics in February. BBC viewers will also see these programmes on iPlayer and BBC Sounds, but the move lets people without a TV licence watch BBC material online. Advertised outside the UK, the partnership is part of the broadcaster’s push to reach new audiences and keep up with tech‑led rivals.
