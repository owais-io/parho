Animal Aid, a UK charity, is suing over a new law that treats animal‑testing sites as “key national infrastructure,” which would let police stop peaceful protests. The charity says the change is too broad, includes many labs and universities that aren’t vital to the nation, and was made without consulting animal‑rights groups. The amendment, which started on 12 Feb, could criminalise non‑violent protest and has already sparked concerns about civil‑rights restrictions.
