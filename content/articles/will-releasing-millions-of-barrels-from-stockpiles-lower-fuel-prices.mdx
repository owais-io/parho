Facing a sharp rise in oil prices after tensions between the US and Israel hit Iran, the International Energy Agency decided to release 400 million barrels of emergency crude. It’s the biggest coordinated drop ever, with the UK sending 13.5 million barrels. The move aims to ease supply shortages, but experts warn it may not fully stop prices if the conflict drags on and pipelines can’t handle all the oil.
