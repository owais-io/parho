Oil prices jumped above $100 a barrel after Iran struck energy sites in the Middle East, raising fears of a supply cut. The IEA released a record 400 million barrels from reserves to calm markets, while the U.S. plans to tap its strategic reserve. The attacks disrupted shipping in the Strait of Hormuz and shut Iraq’s oil ports, tightening global supply and pushing prices higher.
