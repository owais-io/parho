Rhoda Roberts, a Bundjalung cultural leader who helped bring Indigenous voices into mainstream media, died at 66 after a battle with ovarian cancer. She invented the phrase “welcome to country,” co‑founded the National Aboriginal Theatre Trust, and was the first Aboriginal host of a prime‑time news show. Her family, friends and leaders praised her kindness and lasting influence on Australian culture.
