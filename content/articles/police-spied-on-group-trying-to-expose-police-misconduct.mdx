Police officers secretly watched the Hackney Community Defence Association for ten years to track people who tried to expose police abuse and corruption. The spy files even recorded Graham Smith’s family details. The Metropolitan Police said it was wrong for senior officers to order the spying, and the inquiry found that the reports were often inaccurate. The Met admits it misled the public and has faced criticism for how it handled the undercover operations.
