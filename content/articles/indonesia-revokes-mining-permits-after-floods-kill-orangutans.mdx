In November 2024, heavy floods and landslides in Indonesia’s Batang Toru region killed about 11% of the world’s rare Tapanuli orangutans and more than 1,100 people. The disaster blamed the work of several mining and hydropower companies. In response, President Prabowo Subianto ordered the revocation of permits for 28 firms, including a gold mine and a hydro‑electric project, and sued six others for damage. The move aims to protect the endangered orangutans and prevent further deforestation.
