Gulf countries like Qatar, the UAE, and Saudi Arabia are investing billions to develop their own AI, aiming to keep technology in local hands and reduce reliance on the US. The plans cover data centres, chips, and local talent, but face hurdles such as limited chip supply, a shortage of Arabic data, and few home‑grown engineers. Meanwhile, US tech giants will spend over $600 bn on AI this year, showing the race is far from over.
