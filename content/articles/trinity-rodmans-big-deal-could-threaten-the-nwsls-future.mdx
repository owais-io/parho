Trinity Rodman, a top U.S. soccer star, signed a £1.5 million‑per‑year contract with the Washington Spirit. To make the deal work, the NWSL added the “Rodman Rule,” letting clubs pay above the salary cap for highly marketable players. The players’ union says the rule breaks the collective‑bargaining agreement and could create a two‑tier system, risking the league’s competitive balance and community feel.
