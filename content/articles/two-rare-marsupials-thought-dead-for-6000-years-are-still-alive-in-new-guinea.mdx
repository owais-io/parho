Researchers led by Australian scientist Tim Flannery found two marsupials, a striped possum and a ring‑tailed glider, still living in West Papua’s rainforest after being thought extinct for 6,000 years. These Lazarus species were known only from fossils and old records. The discovery shows the forest’s unique biodiversity, stresses the need to protect it from logging, and highlights local communities’ role in conservation. It also suggests the area may hold more hidden species linked to ancient Australia.
