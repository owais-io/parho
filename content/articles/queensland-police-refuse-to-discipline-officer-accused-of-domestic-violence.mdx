Queensland police said they would not investigate an officer who allegedly beat his pregnant partner and tried to undermine her protection order. A tribunal later ruled the police missed a duty, ordering a disciplinary probe that the officer, who had already been put on paid leave, had to face. The case shows the police may avoid reviewing misconduct after an officer leaves, raising concerns about accountability and public trust.
