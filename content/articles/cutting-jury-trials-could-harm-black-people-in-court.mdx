The government wants to cut jury trials to speed up court backlogs, but experts say this change won’t actually clear the backlog. It will, however, hurt Black defendants, witnesses and complainants because jurors are chosen from local communities, giving Black people a better chance of a relatable jury. Removing juries could widen the already‑existing mistrust of the justice system.
