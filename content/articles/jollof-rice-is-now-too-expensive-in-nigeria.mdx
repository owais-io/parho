In Nigeria, the once‑every‑day dish jollof rice has become too pricey for many families. A pot for five costs about 26,656 naira, up from 21,300 naira last year, while wages barely keep up. People are cutting out expensive ingredients or swapping them for cheaper ones, and some are making lighter “concoction rice” instead. The same trend hits Ghana, where families also find the dish hard to afford.
