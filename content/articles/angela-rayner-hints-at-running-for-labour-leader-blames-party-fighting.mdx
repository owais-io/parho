Angela Rayner, former deputy PM, said she might run for Labour leader and didn’t rule out getting back into front‑line politics. She slammed the party’s recent fights and said she wasn’t pushing anyone to quit. She’s still shaken by how the drama affected her family and plans to return after leaving her housing role. Rayner also attacked a briefing against health secretary Wes Streeting and urged the party to focus on work.
