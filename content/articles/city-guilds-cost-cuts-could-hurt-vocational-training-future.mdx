City & Guilds, a key provider of practical skills training in the UK, is slashing its budget by £22 million. The move signals a shift from a charity‑style focus to a profit‑driven approach, raising worries that training costs will rise and support will shrink. Outsourcing jobs overseas and past regulatory fines add to the concern that quality could drop. The cuts could make it harder for people who rely on vocational courses to find good jobs.
