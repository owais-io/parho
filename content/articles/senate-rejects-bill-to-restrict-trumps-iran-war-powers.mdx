On Wednesday, the U.S. Senate rejected a resolution that would have required Congress to approve any military action against Iran. The vote split almost strictly along party lines, with only Republican Rand Paul and Democrat John Fetterman voting in favor of the measure. The bill followed an earlier effort to curb President Trump’s unilateral war powers after U.S.–Israeli strikes on Iran. Democrats say the president can’t decide to go to war alone.
