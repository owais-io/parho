Cyclone Narelle, now a weak low after dumping heavy rain in northern Australia, is set to travel west across the continent and could become a severe storm again. Forecasts say it may regain strength over the Indian Ocean, reach Perth by Wednesday, and bring gale‑force winds and heavy rain that could flood rivers. Residents in the Northern Territory were warned of flooding after 100 mm of rain, while scientists note the unusual westward journey is rare.
