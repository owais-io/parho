Peter Wright, 79, was a pioneering Formula One engineer who helped Lotus win the 1978 world championship with the ground‑effect chassis and later designed active suspension. He kept Lotus racing until 1994 and then joined the FIA to improve driver safety, developing better helmets, cockpit materials and barriers. His work cut fatal crashes and influenced everyday car safety. He wrote books, loved classic cars, and was survived by his wife Dorothy and three children.
