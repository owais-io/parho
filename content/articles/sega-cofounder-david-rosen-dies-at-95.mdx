David Rosen, who helped start Sega and led it to become a video‑game giant, died at 95. A Korean‑War pilot, he stayed in Japan after the war, began importing photo booths and later pinball machines. In 1965 he merged his company with Nihon Goraku Bussan, creating Sega. Rosen guided the shift from arcade machines to games, launched the Master System and the Mega Drive (Genesis in the US), and shaped the industry until he retired in 1996.
