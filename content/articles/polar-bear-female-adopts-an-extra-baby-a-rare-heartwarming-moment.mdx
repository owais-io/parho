In Canada, scientists spotted a female polar bear with two babies that weren’t hers. Using a GPS collar, researchers saw she was caring for the extra cub, a rare event – the 13th adoption in 45 years. The mother, known as bear X33991, will help the 10‑month‑old cub learn to hunt seals and survive on sea ice. Adoptions are uncommon, but this gives the orphan a better chance to live.
