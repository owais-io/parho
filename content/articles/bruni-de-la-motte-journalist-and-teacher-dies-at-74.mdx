Bruni de la Motte, 74, passed away after a life as a journalist and teacher. She freelanced for German papers, interviewed Tony Benn and Harold Pinter, and wrote for the Guardian and New Statesman about life in the former East Germany. With a PhD in English, she taught at Potsdam, researched feminism, and later worked for the trade union Unison in the UK. She loved gardening, bright clothing, and left a legacy of writing, teaching and activism.
