The UK government is considering new legislation to remove former prince Andrew from the line of succession once a police investigation into alleged misconduct ends. Andrew, who is currently eighth in line after Prince William and Harry, has already given up his royal titles but remains a counsellor of state. Politicians from all parties say Parliament should act after the probe, even though Andrew hasn’t been charged.
