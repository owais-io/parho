UK museums keep more than 260,000 human bodies and parts that were taken from former colonies. The items include whole skeletons, mummies, skulls, teeth and other bones. MPs and scientists say the collections are disrespectful and show a colonial legacy. Many institutions don't know who each remains belong to or where they came from. Calls for a national register and for the remains to be returned are growing.
