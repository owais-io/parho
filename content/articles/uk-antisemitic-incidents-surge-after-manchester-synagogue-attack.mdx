Following the fatal attack on a Manchester synagogue on 2 October, anti‑Jewish incidents in the UK jumped sharply. The Community Security Trust logged 40 attacks on the day of the bombing and another 40 the next day, with over half mentioning the attack. Last year, 3,700 incidents were recorded—about 4 % more than 2024—and October was the worst month for hate. Most incidents cited Israel or Gaza, though school‑related incidents fell by 23 %. The government is pledging more security funding.
