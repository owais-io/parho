New UK records show Tony Blair’s government worked hard to persuade Australian Prime Minister John Howard to commit soldiers to Iraq and Afghanistan. The files reveal friendly phone calls, behind‑the‑scenes notes, and personal pressure on Howard’s defence minister to convince the Australian leader that sending troops was essential. Although Howard was hesitant, the UK used diplomacy and friendly ties to try and secure Australian support for the Iraq mission.
