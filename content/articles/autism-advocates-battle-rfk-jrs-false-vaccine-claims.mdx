After the CDC updated its site to support Robert F. Kennedy Jr.’s claim that vaccines cause autism, autism advocates rushed to correct the story. Groups like ASAN, Autism in Black, and the Autistic People of Color Fund urged people to use trusted health information and launched social‑media campaigns. They also pushed for better support, faster diagnoses, and more vaccine resources for autistic families. The activists say Kennedy’s words could hurt people who already face barriers.
