Australian leaders want to lower the petrol and diesel tax, hoping to ease fuel costs. Economists argue that cutting the excise could make gas shortages worse and push inflation higher. Fuel prices have jumped to around 2.50 dollars a litre, and the tax is about 0.53 dollars per litre. A brief cut would only help wealthier users, while poorer families still face high fuel costs. Experts say it's a quick fix that doesn't solve the root problem.
