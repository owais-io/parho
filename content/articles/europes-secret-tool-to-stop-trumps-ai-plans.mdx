Europe has two powerful ways to challenge President Trump’s AI push: limiting exports of key chip‑making machines from Dutch firm ASML and tightening data‑use rules on U.S. tech giants. If Brussels tightens controls, American AI growth could stall, hurting Trump’s economy and popularity. The article says European leaders, especially Ursula von der Leyen, must act decisively, learning from Brazil’s stand‑up to Trump. This could end Trump’s AI bubble in the near future.
