A new UK study shows that weight‑loss medicines such as Ozempic and Wegovy might help heart‑attack survivors avoid a dangerous blood‑flow problem called ‘no‑reflow’. In animal experiments the drugs improved tiny heart vessels, lowering the risk of death or readmission. If the findings hold in people, paramedics could give the medicines on the way to the hospital. More human trials are still needed.
