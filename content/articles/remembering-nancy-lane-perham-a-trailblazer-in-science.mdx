Dr. Nancy Lane Perham, a Canadian‑born cell biologist who died at 89, was a pioneering scientist and champion for women in STEM. She taught at Cambridge’s zoology department and led the Women in Science, Engineering and Technology Initiative and the UK Athena Swan Project. She published over 100 papers and was honored with an OBE in 1994. Beyond science, she painted cell structures for journal covers. She is survived by her husband’s legacy, children, grandchildren, and sisters.
