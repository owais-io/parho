Because the U.S. government hasn’t passed a funding bill, TSA employees at many airports are working without pay. Airports across the country are turning to the public for help, setting up food pantries and asking for small grocery and gas card donations to keep staff fed and moving. While some airports still see normal security lines, others have longer waits. The effort shows how a stalled budget can affect everyday workers and travelers.
