**  
On budget‑day, Keir Starmer defended his Labour budget, insisting Treasury chief Rachel Reeves didn’t mislead the public about the country’s finances. He said the productivity downgrade cost the government £16 billion more than expected and that the budget still helps people with higher wages, lower energy bills and cuts to child poverty. Starmer also pledged more deregulation and a tougher welfare system to boost growth, while critics accused Reeves of downplaying fiscal problems.
