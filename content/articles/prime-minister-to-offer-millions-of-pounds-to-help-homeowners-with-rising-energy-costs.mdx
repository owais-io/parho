Prime Minister Keir Starmer will announce a package worth tens of millions of pounds to help households hit by a sharp rise in energy costs caused by the Iran war. The aid mainly targets people who use heating oil, especially in rural parts of Northern Ireland. Starmer will also call out suppliers for price‑gouging and say the government will investigate. He may cancel a planned fuel‑duty increase to ease the burden.
