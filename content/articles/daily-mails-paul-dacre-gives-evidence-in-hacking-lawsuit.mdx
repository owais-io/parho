Paul Dacre, the former Daily Mail editor, stood in court to defend the newspaper’s publisher in a lawsuit that accuses the paper of bugging, hacking and phone‑tapping celebrities like Prince Harry and Elton John. The judge cut the lawyer’s questions short, and Dacre gave short, quiet answers, saying he was too busy to remember details. He occasionally showed anger but kept his composure.
