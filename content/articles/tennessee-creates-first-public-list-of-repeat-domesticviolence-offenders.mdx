Tennessee has launched the country’s first public list of repeat domestic‑violence offenders. Called Savanna’s Law, it names people who have been convicted of more than one domestic‑violence crime after Jan. 1. The registry is run by the state Bureau of Investigation and lets anyone search the database. While it can raise awareness and help victims, experts warn it could miss many offenders, contain errors, or even wrongly flag survivors.
