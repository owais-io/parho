Jürgen Habermas, a leading German philosopher and sociologist who shaped postwar thought, has died at 96. He was famous for his ideas on democratic dialogue, the rule of law, and European integration, and he wrote for seven decades, critiquing leaders and politics. Habermas survived the war, struggled with a speech impediment, and remained active in public debates until his final book. He left a wife and children.
