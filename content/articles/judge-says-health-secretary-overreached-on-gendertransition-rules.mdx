A federal judge in Oregon found that Health Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. exceeded his authority when he said gender‑affirming treatments were unsafe. The judge said Kennedy did not follow proper procedures before issuing a 12‑page statement that could have barred doctors from Medicare and Medicaid. The ruling protects doctors who treat transgender youth and keeps gender‑transition care legal. It also rejects a lawsuit that aimed to overturn the declaration.
