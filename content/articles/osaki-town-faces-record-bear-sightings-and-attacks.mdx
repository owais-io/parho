Osaki, a town of 128,000 in Japan’s northeast, has had a record 400 bear sightings and many attacks, drawing national attention. Nationwide, 13 people died and 197 attacks were recorded from April to November. Bears are moving into villages because acorn and beech‑nut crops are poor, and they’re delaying hibernation. Local officials declared an emergency, urging people to stay indoors and remove fruit from trees, while some authorities are trapping and culling bears for safety.
