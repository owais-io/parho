The UK has relied on hidden taxes such as stamp duty and capital gains tax to raise money without bumping up major taxes. Labour’s finance minister Rachel Reeves has avoided an income‑tax hike and instead plans more stealth taxes. Leader Keir Starmer says the country should come first, but voters want clear, fair tax rules. The article reviews past UK leaders who used stealth taxes and warns that this approach may leave Britain in debt and confuse voters.
