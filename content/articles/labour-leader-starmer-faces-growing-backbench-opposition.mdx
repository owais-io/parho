Shabana Mahmood, Labour's home secretary, announced tough new rules ending permanent refugee status and cutting support for some asylum seekers. The move hit backbench MPs hard, sparking letters from 100 colleagues who say the plan hurts integration and social cohesion. Leader Keir Starmer sent a letter attacking the Green Party, but did not address cost‑of‑living worries. With local elections in May and Green challenges looming, Starmer’s leadership faces a growing revolt from party members.
