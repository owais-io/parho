Trump told oil giants at the White House that they can work safely in Venezuela and should invest $100 billion to rebuild the country’s oil infrastructure after the U.S. helped oust Nicolás Maduro. He said the money would come from the companies, not the government, and that the U.S. would provide protection. Many executives, including leaders from Chevron, Exxon and ConocoPhillips, showed interest but warned that more political changes are needed for them to invest.
