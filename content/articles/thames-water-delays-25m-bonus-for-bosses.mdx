Thames Water has pushed back a £2.5 million bonus package for 21 senior managers until the new year. The payment was due in December, but the company paused it after the chair mistakenly said creditors wanted the bonuses. The water firm is already deep in debt, borrowing £3 bn from banks and hedge funds, and is negotiating a rescue deal that could let the lenders buy the company. The move is aimed at calming critics and focusing on saving the company.
