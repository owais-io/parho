Financial markets now say the Bank of England will probably keep its main rate at 3.75% for the rest of 2026 and may lift it to 4% next June. The shift comes after tensions with Iran pushed oil prices up, raising bond yields and making investors nervous. Mortgage rates have already ticked higher, while the FTSE and other European shares fell. The ECB is also expected to hike rates soon.
