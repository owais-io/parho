A former NYPD officer who ran the city’s school‑security unit is accused of taking bribes to push a private company’s panic‑alert app into New York schools. Prosecutors say he used his influence, sent emails, and even enjoyed fancy trips and dinners while steering a contract worth millions. The officer has pleaded not guilty. The investigation is ongoing, and officials are reviewing other procurement deals.
