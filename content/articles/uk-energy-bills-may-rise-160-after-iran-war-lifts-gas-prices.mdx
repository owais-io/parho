After the recent war in Iran, gas prices in the UK have jumped, causing a forecast that average households may see their yearly bills rise by about £160. The spike comes from the U.S. attack on Iran, which triggered Tehran to stop gas and oil shipments through the Hormuz Strait. The government’s price cap still limits costs, but higher wholesale prices could push bills closer to £1,800 a year.
