Abu Zafar, 88, was a pioneering engineer and inventor who helped start Bradford’s first Asian‑owned printing press with his wife Hisako. The couple left East Pakistan in 1971, got a UN fellowship, and were stuck in the UK when the Bangladesh war broke out. Zafar studied at Bradford, taught maths, and in 1974 opened Orient Press. He introduced new tech, earned a British Empire Medal, and helped build community ties for over 50 years.
