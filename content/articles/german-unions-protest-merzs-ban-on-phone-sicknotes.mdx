German Chancellor Friedrich Merz wants to end the option of getting a short‑term sick‑note over the phone, saying it lets people abuse sick leave and hurts the economy. Unions, doctors and the health‑insurance sector argue the move would unfairly target workers and could create more waiting‑room traffic. While Merz claims Germany has too many sick days, studies show the country is similar to other European nations. The debate has sparked strong criticism across the political spectrum.
