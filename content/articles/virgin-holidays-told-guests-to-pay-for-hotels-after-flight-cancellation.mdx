When a war in the Gulf forced Virgin Holidays flights to cancel, many travelers in Mauritius were told by Virgin staff to book their own hotel stays and pay for them, hoping to recover the costs through insurance. Only after complaints did Virgin agree to cover some costs, while others were still left paying out‑of‑pocket. The story highlights how tour operators must follow package‑travel rules and protect customers’ rights during crises.
