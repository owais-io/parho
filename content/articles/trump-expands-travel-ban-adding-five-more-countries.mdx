Trump announced a new travel ban on Tuesday, adding five more countries—Burkina Faso, Mali, Niger, South Sudan, and Syria—to the list of nations whose citizens are almost barred from entering the U.S. He also tightened rules on Palestinian travel documents and added 15 countries to a partial ban. The move follows a shooting that killed two National Guard members by an Afghan asylum seeker and aims to curb people the U.S. says it can’t safely screen.
