A Los Angeles jury ruled that Meta and YouTube intentionally built features like infinite scroll and autoplay to hook users, and awarded a 20‑year‑old plaintiff $6 million. Human rights groups applauded the decision, calling it a turning point for child safety online. The verdict urges tech companies to change their design, though some worry new laws might hurt free speech and privacy.
