New South Wales is becoming the first place in Australia to stop courts from considering a person’s good character when deciding sentences. The change was driven by survivors of sexual abuse who say hearing a “good person” label can re‑traumatise them. While some legal experts worry it limits defendants’ rights, judges will still look at risk of reoffending and rehabilitation prospects.
