Antoine Semenyo, once turned down by several big clubs, worked his way up from lower‑league football to the Premier League. After a solid spell at Bournemouth, City paid £62.5 m to bring him in. The Ghana international is a powerful, skillful winger who can also throw long kicks, giving City a new attacking option. His journey shows that even if a player is rejected early, clubs still can find talent in lower divisions.
