Wes Streeting, Britain's health secretary, warned that resident doctors’ planned Christmas strikes, combined with a record‑high flu outbreak, could push the NHS to the brink. Flu cases have jumped 55% last week, putting about 2,660 patients in hospital each day. Streeting urged doctors to accept a new deal that adds training posts and funding for exams. The BMA will vote online, but many worry the strikes could still happen.
