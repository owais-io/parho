In Ramsgate, Kent, the last youth centre was saved after a charity and a £535,000 grant from Labour’s Pride in Place programme. Pie Factory Music bought the building from Kent County Council, which wanted to auction it. The centre provides counselling, job advice, music projects and help for young refugees. The move follows a report saying the centre saves the council over £500,000 a year and that youth services nationwide are underfunded.
