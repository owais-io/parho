A decade after Jamie Oliver spotlighted junk lunches, England’s schools still struggle to serve healthy meals. Past cuts in funding, competitive bidding and academy conversions have cut nutrition standards. Covid cut breaks and rising food costs worsen the issue, with many kids grabbing cheap, low‑quality take‑away. Labour ministers are reviewing the rules, but enforcement remains weak. No clear body enforces standards, so kids often choose junk food instead.
