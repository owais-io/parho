A winter storm is moving across the northeastern U.S. and southeastern Canada, dropping heavy snow and freezing rain. It could create blizzard conditions with up to 80 cm of snow near the Great Lakes and winds up to 70 mph. Meteorologists warn of a 15% tornado risk for Monday in three states. A cold front will bring temperatures 10‑25 °C below average across much of the eastern U.S., with sub‑zero temps as far south as Alabama and Georgia.
