Heathrow’s chief executive says the slow pace of the £33 billion third‑runway project is a national problem. The airport’s profits fell a third in 2025, and the new runway could add 276,000 flights by 2035. For it to start in 2029, the government must finalize funding and legal rules this year. Airlines fear higher landing fees, while opponents worry the plan will burden taxpayers.
