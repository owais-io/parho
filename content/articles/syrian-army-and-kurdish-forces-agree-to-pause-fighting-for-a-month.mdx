The Syrian government and Kurdish SDF have agreed to stop fighting for up to a month. The pause will help move suspected ISIS fighters from Syria to Iraq. Both sides had already paused earlier this week, and the new deal keeps the front calm while diplomats work on a longer plan. If the Kurds don’t accept the terms, the army could resume attacks on their strongholds.
