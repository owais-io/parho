Record‑breaking rains in Washington turned rivers into flood waters that swelled neighborhoods, forced evacuations and saved dozens of people.  No deaths were reported, but about 10,000 residents in Burlington were told to leave and trucks carried them to shelters.  The flood‑water level is dropping, but the river stays high and more rain is expected.  The state asked for an emergency, which President Trump approved.  Climate scientists warn that storms like this will happen more often.
