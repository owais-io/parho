Qantas is lifting fares on its overseas flights because jet‑fuel costs spiked after the war in the Middle East. The airline’s hedges couldn’t fully cover the jump, so the price hikes will differ by route. Demand is high, with many seats filling as passengers from affected airlines rebook with Qantas. The rival Virgin Australia has not yet announced any changes, while fuel price swings hit airlines worldwide.
