The CDC’s vaccine advisory group, ACIP, was scheduled to meet at the end of February but has been pushed to March because of legal questions about its authority. The delay comes as vaccine critic Robert F. Kennedy Jr. pushes big changes – dropping six childhood shots, easing state exemption rules, and cutting money for mRNA research. A pediatrician association is suing to challenge the committee’s legitimacy. The ACIP’s advice shapes insurance coverage, school rules and doctor recommendations across the U.S.
