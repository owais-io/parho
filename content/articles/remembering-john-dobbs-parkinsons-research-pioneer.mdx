John Dobbs, 81, was a leading researcher who changed how we think about Parkinson’s disease. He showed that the disease is driven by inflammation in the whole body, especially gut bacteria and infections. He set up a research group at King’s College London that studied the gut‑brain connection and worked on ways to treat the inflammation. Dobbs was also a warm, thoughtful scientist who loved travel and culture.
