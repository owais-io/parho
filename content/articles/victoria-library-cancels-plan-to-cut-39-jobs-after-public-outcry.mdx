Victoria's oldest public library has pulled back a plan that would have cut 39 jobs and focused more on digital tours. The move came after writers, scholars and thousands of citizens protested the changes, saying the library should stay a place for people to read and learn. Library leaders say they will keep improving services and will listen to public feedback before making new plans.
