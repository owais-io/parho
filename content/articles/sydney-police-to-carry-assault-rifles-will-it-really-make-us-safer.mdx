After the Bondi tragedy, NSW premier Chris Minns announced police would carry assault rifles for New Year's Eve and is even discussing using the army on the streets. He thinks armed officers will calm people. But research shows militarised policing can raise fear, hurt civil liberties, and doesn’t lower crime rates. Experts argue that community policing and mental‑health support are better ways to keep residents safe.
