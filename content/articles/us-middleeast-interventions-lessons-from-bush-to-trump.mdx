The U.S. has repeatedly stepped into Middle‑East conflicts, from the 1990–91 Gulf War to the 2003 Iraq invasion, hoping to topple dictators and spread stability. Those efforts instead sparked chaos, new terrorist groups, and huge costs. Now President Trump is eyeing a possible strike on Iran, citing nuclear threats and unstable allies. The article warns that another intervention could again fracture the country, fuel extremism, and backfire on America.
