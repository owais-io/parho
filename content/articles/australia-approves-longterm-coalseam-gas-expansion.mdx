Australia’s federal government has given a 20‑year go‑ahead to a new coal‑seam gas project in Queensland, allowing up to 1,700 wells, thousands of kilometres of pipeline, and three processing plants. The project will emit roughly 120 million tonnes of CO₂ over its life, more than the country’s yearly transport emissions. Critics say it undermines climate goals, arguing that approving another fossil‑fuel scheme is like lighting a cigarette while trying to quit. The developer says it will meet strict environmental rules and help supply gas to domestic and export markets.
