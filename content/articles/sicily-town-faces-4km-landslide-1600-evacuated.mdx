A heavy rainstorm from Cyclone Harry tore a 4‑km landslide out of the town of Niscemi in Sicily, swallowing roads and homes. More than 1,600 residents have been moved out, and 17‑century churches and other historic buildings are in danger of sliding too. Residents feel angry and scared because many homes were built in risky areas. Experts blame decades of poor planning and climate‑driven weather.
