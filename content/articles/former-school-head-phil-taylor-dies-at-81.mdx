Phil Taylor, who ran several schools in Greater Manchester, died at 81. He was known for banning school uniforms, dropping the daily bell, and even having a farm with peacocks in the building. He often appeared on BBC programs to criticize education ministers. A lifelong socialist, he moved from the Labour Party to the Green Party. After teaching and headship jobs, he later suffered dementia. He is survived by his children, stepchildren and grandchildren.
