After a five‑day strike that ended on Monday, resident doctors say they will keep trying to talk with Health Secretary Wes Streeting. They want to avoid more NHS strikes in 2026. Streeting said he doesn’t want any industrial action and keeps his door open. The government and the BMA had talks, but they broke down. Doctors want pay back to 2008 levels and more training places. Both sides now aim to keep the conversation going.
