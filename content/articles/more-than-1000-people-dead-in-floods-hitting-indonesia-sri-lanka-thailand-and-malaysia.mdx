Floods triggered by Cyclone Ditwah and other storms have killed over 1,000 people in Indonesia, Sri Lanka, Thailand and Malaysia. Heavy rains caused landslides, road damage and stuck many families in shelters. Governments are rushing to give food, water and medical help, but some areas are still cut off by broken bridges. The death toll is rising as rescue teams struggle to reach people, and leaders are calling for emergency aid and faster relief.
