The UK government has stopped issuing study visas to students from Afghanistan, Cameroon, Myanmar and Sudan, citing asylum‑claim worries. Many argue this will cut off talented young people who could help rebuild their own countries. The letter features examples of alumni from the blocked countries—leaders in education, health and journalism—who have gone on to make global impacts. The writers urge the UK to lift the ban or offer limited scholarships so knowledge sharing can continue.
