Greece’s worst train crash trial began in chaos as grieving families shouted “shame on you” at judges. The hearing was postponed to April after the court felt overcrowded and “like a chicken coop.” The crash killed 57, and investigators blame a failed signalling system. Families want justice, even threatening the European court, while rail workers strike and the EU prosecutor’s office adds charges. The incident is still hurting trust in Greek politics.
