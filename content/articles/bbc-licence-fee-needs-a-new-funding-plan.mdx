The BBC is losing money because fewer households pay its licence fee, and its funding has fallen 30%. The article suggests that the BBC should be funded through a council‑tax levy, like police and fire services, so every household pays a small share. This could make the licence fairer, keep the BBC independent and ensure it continues to offer free‑ad‑vertising TV and radio. It also notes that some argue the BBC should become a charity.
