Axel Springer has outbid other suitors to buy the Telegraph, ending a long run of speculation that the Daily Mail group would take over. The German owner promises editorial freedom but also pushes a heavy focus on artificial intelligence and a digital expansion. Staff worry about changes to the paper’s right‑wing, Eurosceptic tone, and whether Springer’s “Essentials” will alter its editorial stance. The deal leaves many staff anxious for stability.
