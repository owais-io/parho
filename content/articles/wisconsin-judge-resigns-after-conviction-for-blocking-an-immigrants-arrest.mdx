Judge Hannah Dugan, who served in Milwaukee, was found guilty of obstructing federal officers when she helped an undocumented immigrant avoid arrest during a hearing. After the conviction, state Republicans urged her to quit, and she stepped down. She said she was fighting for the judiciary’s independence but that people deserve a working judge. The move ended a tense political debate and cleared the court of her presence.
