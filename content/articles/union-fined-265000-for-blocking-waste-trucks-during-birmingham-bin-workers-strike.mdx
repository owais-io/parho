Unite, the union of Birmingham bin workers, was fined £265,000 after it repeatedly blocked waste trucks during a strike. The court said the protests caused 22,000 tonnes of rubbish to pile up, forcing the city to declare a major incident. Unite admitted breaking the court order and apologised, but the judge said it was deliberate. The council welcomed the ruling, and the union will cover the fine through a cut to its Labour affiliation fee.
