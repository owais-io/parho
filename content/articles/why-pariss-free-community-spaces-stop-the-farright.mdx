Paris is putting people back in the streets, not just by banning cars but by creating ‘third places’ – free cafés, galleries, and parks where anyone can hang out. These spots, like the Print pop‑up, let residents meet, chat, and feel connected without spending money. The city’s effort keeps people from feeling isolated, a key reason far‑right politics rise when community spaces disappear. Paris’s focus on public living rooms is a political win against alienation.
