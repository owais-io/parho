Labour's top leaders are in conflict. The article shows tension between Prime Minister Keir Starmer, his chief of staff Morgan McSweeney, and Health Secretary Wes Streeting. Starmer and McSweeney's relationship has weakened, raising questions about McSweeney's job. McSweeney publicly accused Streeting of plotting to replace Starmer, but Streeting defended himself and appears stronger. Some inside the party think Streeting could become the next Labour leader.
