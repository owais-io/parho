Germany’s new chancellor, Friedrich Merz, and Italy’s prime minister, Giorgia Meloni, are pushing for looser environmental rules. They want to relax the emissions‑trading system, delay the ban on new petrol and diesel cars, and cut back 2040 climate targets. The Guardian warns this could undo the EU’s 2019 green deal, hurting the planet and Europe’s competitiveness. Defenders of the green deal are urged to step up.
