Energy watchdog Ofgem has approved a £28 billion plan to upgrade Britain’s gas and electric grids. About £17.8 billion will go to gas and £10.3 billion to high‑voltage power lines. The upgrade covers over 80 projects and aims to make the network safer and greener. Customers will see about £108 extra in network charges by 2031, roughly £30 more per year, but the upgrade could save about £80 on bills in the long run.
