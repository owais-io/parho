Scientists exploring limestone caves in western Cambodia found three new gecko species, plus other animals. The team, which included researchers from Cambodia, Thailand and the US, spent nights mapping over 60 caves. The geckos are about 20 cm long, striped, and named for their night‑time habits. The discoveries highlight the fragile karst ecosystems that are at risk from cement mining and need protection.
