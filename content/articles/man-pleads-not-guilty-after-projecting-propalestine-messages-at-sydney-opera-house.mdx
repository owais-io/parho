Fouad Masri, 50, projected pro‑Palestine slogans onto the Sydney Opera House forecourt on Oct 11 and was later charged with breaking the building’s bylaws. He pleaded not guilty, and his lawyers say the case could challenge Australia’s limits on political protest. The court will consider a constitutional argument that the bylaws unfairly restrict free speech. The incident follows other protests around the Opera House tied to the Gaza conflict.
