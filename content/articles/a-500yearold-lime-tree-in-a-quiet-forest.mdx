On a bright winter day I stand in a ring of small‑leaved lime trees, one of which is probably over 500 years old. The writer describes its big canopy and the quiet forest around it. He talks about how people used to cut these trees for wood, leather and rope. Now the craft is being revived and new lime seedlings are appearing, hinting at hope for the species.
