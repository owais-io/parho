A single‑tusked elephant in Jharkhand, India, has killed 22 people since the start of January, mostly at night while it roams villages. Forest officers have tried to calm it with tranquiliser but the attempts failed. About 100 staff and wildlife experts from three states are chasing it, covering almost 30 km a day, but its erratic route makes tracking hard. The killings highlight growing human‑elephant conflict caused by habitat loss and crowding.
