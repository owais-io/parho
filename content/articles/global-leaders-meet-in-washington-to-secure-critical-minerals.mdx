Ministers from the US, EU, UK, Japan, Australia and New Zealand gathered in Washington to plan an alliance that will secure critical minerals and reduce dependence on China. Australia will set up a 1.2 billion‑dollar reserve of key elements. The US may not guarantee minimum prices, but the summit aims to repair trade ties after Trump’s tariff war. The EU also pushes the US to drop its steel tariffs.
