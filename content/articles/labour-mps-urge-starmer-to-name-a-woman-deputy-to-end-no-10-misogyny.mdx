Female Labour MPs are demanding that Keir Starmer appoint a senior woman as his de‑facto deputy, arguing it will help transform the “boys’ club” culture at Downing Street. They want the role of first secretary of state to be filled by a woman to oversee gender‑related reforms after scandals involving Peter Mandelson and Matthew Doyle. Starmer’s leadership is under pressure as he deals with fallout and calls for action against misogyny.
