Tom Homan, Trump’s new immigration czar, has taken over the Minneapolis crackdown, pulling 700 federal officers but leaving 2,000 in the state. He says the focus is on public safety, but critics argue the same aggressive tactics continue. The city still mourns the deaths of Alex Pretti and Renee Good, and protests persist as local leaders want more police cooperation with ICE. Homan’s arrival may change style, not goals.
