Melbourne will let people tap their card or phone to pay for train fares on four suburban lines – Craigieburn, Upfield, Ballarat and Seymour – starting Monday. The trial runs through March and April and only works on adult fares. Anyone with a Myki card for discounts, or who needs to travel on other lines, will still have to use a Myki. The move is part of a $1.7bn plan to upgrade the state’s old ticketing system.
