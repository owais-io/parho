When Trump cut USAID, conservation workers worldwide lost funding. In Liberia, eco‑guards who protect forests and rely on USAID salaries suddenly had to consider poaching. Similar cuts hit rangers in Africa, Asia and the Americas, hurting efforts against poaching, trafficking and habitat loss. Some groups are finding new support from private donors, European governments and the new US Foundation for International Conservation. While gaps remain, locals say they can still make a difference.
