A federal judge has temporarily allowed the Trump administration to keep lawmakers from inspecting immigration detention centers without giving a week's notice. The ruling also backs the department’s plan to keep ICE enforcement active in Minnesota after a lawsuit from the state and cities. Meanwhile, the justice department is appealing a court order that limited ICE’s aggressive tactics toward protesters. The decision keeps the administration’s control over ICE facilities in place for now.
