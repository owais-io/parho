Kherson, a Ukrainian city next to the Russian border, is still fighting everyday. Soldiers fire rockets and drones, so residents live in basements, underground schools, and hospitals. Kids and adults get help from psychologists, and anti‑drone nets stretch 100 km across the streets. New plans aim to double those nets and build more underground classrooms and clinics, so people can keep living safely even as the threat stays high.
