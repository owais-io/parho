UK and South Korea have signed a fresh trade agreement that will help British companies export more cars, salmon and Guinness. The deal replaces the 2019 pact, adds £400 million in extra trade for the UK, and lowers the rule for cars to qualify for zero tariffs—from 55% to 25% UK/EU parts. It also opens South Korean public‑procurement bids to UK firms and gives them access to digital contracts.
