Senate will vote to stop President Trump’s attacks on Iran. Experts say the war breaks U.S. law and international rules. Trump says Iran’s missiles threaten the U.S., but there is no clear proof. Lawyers argue the president needs congressional approval before sending troops. The upcoming vote could force Trump to rethink or limit the attacks. This move has sparked debate among lawmakers and activists about how the U.S. should handle foreign threats and the limits of presidential power.
