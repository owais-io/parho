A group of 34 Australian women and children, many of whom are relatives of former ISIS fighters, were escorted out of a Syrian detention camp by Kurdish forces. They were headed toward Damascus to catch a flight back to Australia. A few kilometres from the camp, the Syrian government warned the convoy that it would be fired upon if they entered Syrian territory. The families were forced to turn back, leaving them shocked and upset.
