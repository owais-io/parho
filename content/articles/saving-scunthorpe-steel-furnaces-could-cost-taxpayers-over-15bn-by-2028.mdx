The UK government’s rescue of British Steel’s Scunthorpe blast furnaces, taken over in April 2023 after the Chinese owner threatened to shut it down, could cost taxpayers more than £1.5bn by 2028. The National Audit Office warns that daily operating costs are high and that additional money may be needed for a future sale or to replace the furnaces with greener technology.
