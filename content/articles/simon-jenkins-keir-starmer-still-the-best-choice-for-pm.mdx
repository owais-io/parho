Simon Jenkins argues that, even though Keir Starmer isn’t very popular and polls are unreliable, he remains the best person to lead Britain. He notes Starmer has kept public borrowing low, helped the economy grow, and restarted the Erasmus student exchange. Despite mistakes and a shaky Downing Street team, Starmer’s steady leadership could keep him in the job longer than his rivals.
