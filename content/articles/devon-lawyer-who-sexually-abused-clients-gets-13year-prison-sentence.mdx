Alan Harris, a 72‑year‑old criminal‑defence lawyer from Plymouth, was jailed for 13 years after courts found he sexually abused vulnerable clients, some as young as 14, over nearly 30 years. He was convicted of 10 offences against seven victims, mainly in police stations and court rooms. Survivors spoke about lasting trauma, and a judge warned of the betrayal of trust. Harris retired in 2016.
