Donald Trump and Robert F. Kennedy Jr. promoted leucovorin, a vitamin B form, as a cure for autism at a press event. Parents rushed to doctors, and prescriptions rose 71%. But the FDA only approved the drug for a rare folate deficiency, not autism, after studies showed weak evidence and a retracted trial. Experts warn that hype can mislead families and erode trust in health regulators.
