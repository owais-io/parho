A small group of older feminists, called Howl, is gathering stories, photos, and other materials from the 1960s Women’s Liberation Movement in London. They want to keep those voices alive before they’re forgotten. The article also mentions WWII female pilots who fought for equal pay and how their experiences inspired later civil‑rights work. Howl invites anyone with memories or documents to share and help build a richer history.
