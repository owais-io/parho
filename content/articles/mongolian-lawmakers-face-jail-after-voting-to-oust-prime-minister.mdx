Mongolia’s younger parliamentarians, many of them women, are being charged with trying to seize state power and could spend up to 20 years in prison after a parliamentary vote called for Prime Minister Zandanshatar Gombojav to resign. The move is part of a clash over mining tax reforms, with the state intelligence agency backing the prosecution. Critics warn this threatens the country’s democracy and free debate.
