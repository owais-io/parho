Trump told the New York Times he regrets not having the National Guard seize voting machines after losing the 2020 election. He still insists he won, saying the Guard isn’t “sophisticated enough” to check for fraud. The plan was floated in late 2020 with advisers, but top officials, including Attorney General Barr, blocked it. Trump’s story is backed by no evidence, and election experts say the 2020 vote was secure.
