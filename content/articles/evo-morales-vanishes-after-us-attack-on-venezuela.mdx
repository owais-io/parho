Former Bolivian president Evo Morales disappeared from public view after the US attacked Venezuela. For a year he was seen at rallies and even voted in 2025, but now he’s gone missing. Some say he fled, others say he’s sick with dengue. The government says he’s ill, while critics think the arrest warrant hasn’t been enforced. The mystery adds tension to Bolivia’s political and drug‑policy drama.
