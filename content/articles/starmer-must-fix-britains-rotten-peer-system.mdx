Keir Starmer is under pressure because Britain’s upper house, the Lords, keeps giving life‑long seats to people with weak credentials. Recent scandals involving peers have made the system look corrupt. Labour promised in its manifesto to scrap hereditary peers and tighten appointments, but the plans are stalled. Critics say the government is avoiding the tough work, which hurts public trust.
