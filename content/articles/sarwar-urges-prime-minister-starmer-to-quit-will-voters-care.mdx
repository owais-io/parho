Anas Sarwar, Scotland’s Labour leader, has flipped on former ally Keir Starmer and is calling for his resignation. With Labour’s UK support falling and a scandal involving Peter Mandelson, Sarwar believes a new leader could help the party win Scotland’s May election. The move risks further division and may turn voters away, but he frames it as putting Scotland’s interests first. Whether voters will back it remains uncertain.
