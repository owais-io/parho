Alcoa’s bauxite mines in Western Australia have been blocking water inspectors from checking the 12 public catchments that supply Perth’s drinking water. New documents show fences and paperwork keep rangers out, while the state government stopped giving the Water Corporation details of Alcoa’s plans. Conservation groups say this limits safety checks that protect 2.3 million residents. Alcoa insists it has never harmed the water, but critics warn the risk is high.
