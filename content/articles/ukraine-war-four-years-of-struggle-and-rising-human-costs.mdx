Four years after Russia’s full‑scale invasion, Ukraine is still fighting a slow, bloody war. Moscow pushes with drones and heavy fire, while Kyiv endures constant attacks on homes, power and water. Western aid helps, but many feel the war is endless and hope for peace is fading. Ukraine’s president stays popular, and U.S. support feels shaky after Trump’s comments. The battle’s human toll keeps climbing.
