A group of top politicians and tech CEOs wants to build a $7 billion, 1.4‑gigawatt data center in rural Michigan. They say it will supply AI tech and create jobs, but locals fear it will raise electricity bills, pollute groundwater, and ruin the area’s rural feel. The town’s board voted against it, but the powerful backers sued and reversed the decision. Residents have staged protests, and regulators are debating how much power the center will need and its environmental impact.
