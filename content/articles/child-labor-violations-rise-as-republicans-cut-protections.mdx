Child labor violations have jumped five‑fold in a decade, with 5,272 kids illegally working in 2025 compared to just 1,012 in 2015. While the Biden team stepped up enforcement, Republican lawmakers in states like Nebraska, Indiana and West Virginia are cutting protections—lowering teen minimum wages, ending work‑permit tracking and removing hazardous‑job limits. Seventeen states have passed or are pushing roll‑back bills, threatening safer jobs for minors.
