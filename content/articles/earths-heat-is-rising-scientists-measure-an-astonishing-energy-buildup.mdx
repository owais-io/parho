A new climate report says the planet is trapping a record‑high 23 zettajoules (that's 23 × 10²¹ joules) of extra heat each year. That’s more than 18 times all the energy humans use and is warming oceans, land and air. If this keeps going, we’ll face hotter heatwaves, stronger storms and more floods—like having millions of Hiroshima‑size explosions happening every second.
