Trump warned he would send Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agents to U.S. airports if Democrats refuse to sign a deal that would strengthen security and give ICE more power. The threat comes as the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) faces a funding stalemate, leaving TSA workers unpaid and prompting calls for reforms. Democrats want changes to ICE’s tactics and better protections for law‑enforcement officers, while Republicans push for full DHS funding. Senators are holding talks to break the deadlock.
