Berlin’s Berlinale is holding an emergency meeting after a pro‑Palestinian speech at its closing gala sparked backlash. U.S. director Tricia Tuttle, who has led the festival since 2024, may be fired because her team appeared in photos with Palestinian symbols and because speeches called Germany a “partner in genocide.” The event has split the German government, with officials walking out and condemning the remarks, while the festival insists it should stay out of politics.
