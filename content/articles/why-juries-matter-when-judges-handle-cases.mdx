The article argues that removing juries would hurt justice. People who sit as jurors bring a human, conscience‑based view that balances strict law. Critics, like Simon Jenkins, blame juries for court problems, but experts say backlogs and prison rates stem from underfunding, not juries. Juries decide guilt; judges set sentences. Losing juries would strip a safeguard against harsh decisions. The solution is more funding, not abolishing jury trials.
