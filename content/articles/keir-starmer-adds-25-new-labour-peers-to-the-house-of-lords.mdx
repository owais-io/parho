Labour leader Keir Starmer has named 25 new peers, many former government and party aides, to strengthen his influence in the House of Lords. The appointments bring his total to 62, aiming to balance the chamber against the Conservatives, who have 282 peers. Critics say the process is undemocratic, but Starmer says it will help pass reforms like better workers' rights and a new second chamber.
