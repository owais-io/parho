In Australia’s growing outer suburbs, more parents are sending their kids to private schools instead of public ones. The move is driven by uneven funding, with private schools getting far more money for new buildings and facilities. Families also favour schools that share their values, like Catholic schools, and hear positive reviews from other parents. The trend is especially sharp in places like Melbourne’s Caroline Springs, where new private schools are opening fast.
