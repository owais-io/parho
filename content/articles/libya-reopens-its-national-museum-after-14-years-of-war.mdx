After almost 14 years closed because of war, Libya’s National Museum in Tripoli finally opened with fireworks, music and an Italian orchestra. Inside are Greek, Roman, Ottoman and ancient Libyan artifacts, including old mosaics and 5,000‑year‑old mummies. The reopening is seen as a chance to unite the country’s eastern and western parts, teach history to children, and highlight Libya’s Mediterranean heritage. The event also underscored ongoing political tensions and economic challenges.
