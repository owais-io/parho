Public schools in Australia now have the lowest student numbers in years, with more families choosing private or faith‑based schools instead. Statistics show a 0.4% drop in government enrolments and a 21% rise in private schools over the last four years. Experts blame cuts in funding, saying public schools lack staff and supplies. The shift could widen social gaps and hurt community cohesion, experts warn.
