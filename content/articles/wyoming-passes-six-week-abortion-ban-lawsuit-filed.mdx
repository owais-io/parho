Wyoming’s Republican‑led legislature approved a law banning abortions after six weeks of pregnancy, a time many women don’t yet know they’re pregnant. The governor signed it while warning it could be struck down. An identical lawsuit was filed right away, making the ban illegal in court. The bill would carry up to five years in prison for anyone violating it. The move has drawn sharp criticism from Democrats and will halt services at the state’s only abortion clinic.
