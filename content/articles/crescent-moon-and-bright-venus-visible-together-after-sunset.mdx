On March 20, look west after sunset to see a tiny crescent moon next to bright Venus in the evening sky. Venus has been shining since early March. The moon will be almost new, only 3.7% lit. Start watching around 6:30 p.m.; the moon’s faint earth‑shine will become visible as the sky darkens. This view is easier in the northern hemisphere.
