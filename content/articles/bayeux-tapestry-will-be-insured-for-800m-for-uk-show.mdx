Bayeux Tapestry will be insured for about £800 million while it travels from France to the British Museum in 2026. The Treasury uses a government indemnity scheme that protects cultural items during borrowing, saving museums huge insurance costs. The 70‑metre cloth, which shows the 1066 Norman invasion, will be on loan until 2027 while the French museum repairs. The swap also includes other treasures loaned to France.
