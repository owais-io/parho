Harry hates the holiday season because loud carols, gift lists and questions about plans make him feel lonely. He grew up with tense family Christmases and now spends most holidays alone, feeling left out. A therapist helped him see that the holiday isn’t a rule book and taught him simple ways to stay connected—like joining a neighborhood lunch or a volunteer event. By changing his thoughts and building new habits, he can enjoy a quieter, kinder Christmas next year.
