Since 2015, the Salaam Shalom kitchen in Nottingham has been a joint Muslim‑Jewish project that prepares and serves free vegetarian meals to anyone in need. Volunteers from Muslim, Jewish, Catholic and other backgrounds work together, chopping veggies and chatting with guests. The kitchen aims to fight food poverty, social isolation and rising hate, showing that people of different faiths can share common values and help each other.
