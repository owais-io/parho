Eight current and former Toronto police officers have been arrested after a big investigation uncovered organised crime ties inside the city’s police service. Officers are accused of taking bribes, helping drug traffickers, leaking private information to criminals, and even aiding a murder plot against a corrections officer. The probe, called Project South, began after a shooting attempt on a jail staff member and has linked police misconduct to drug operations and the tow‑truck industry.
