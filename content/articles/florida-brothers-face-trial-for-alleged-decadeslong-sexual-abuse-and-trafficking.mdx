Three brothers from a wealthy Florida family are going on trial in Manhattan for supposedly raping, drugging, and trafficking dozens of women over several decades. Prosecutors say the men lured victims through bars, dating apps and lavish trips, spiking their drinks and using force. The brothers plead not guilty, and up to 60 women may testify. The case could shake up the real‑estate world and spark broader scrutiny of similar schemes.
