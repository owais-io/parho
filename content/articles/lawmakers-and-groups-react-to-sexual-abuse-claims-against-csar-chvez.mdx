The New York Times released a report saying the late labor leader César Chávez sexually abused several women, including prominent activist Dolores Huerta. Politicians and unions across the U.S. are responding: California’s governor said the farm‑workers movement is bigger than one man and will consider renaming the holiday; Arizona has stopped observing it as a state holiday. Many organizations cancel or rebrand events and call for accountability and support for survivors.
