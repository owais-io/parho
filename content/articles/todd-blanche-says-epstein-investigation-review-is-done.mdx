Todd Blanche, the deputy attorney general who led the Trump administration’s review of the Jeffrey Epstein files, told ABC News that the investigation into Epstein’s sex‑trafficking scheme is finished. He said prosecutors can’t build a case if there’s no evidence, even if there are disturbing photos. Blanche said the Justice Department will fix any missing redactions, but lawmakers say the release is incomplete and want all documents, including those linked to high‑profile figures, to be made public.
