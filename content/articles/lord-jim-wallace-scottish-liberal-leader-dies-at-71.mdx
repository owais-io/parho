Jim Wallace, a well‑known Scottish Liberal politician, died at 71 after complications from surgery. He served four decades in politics, helped bring the SDP and Liberals together, and was Scotland’s deputy first minister from 1999 to 2005, acting as first minister twice. He championed free university tuition, freedom of information and land reform. Outside politics he worked on child‑protection reforms and the Braer oil‑spill inquiry, and was a respected Christian community leader.
