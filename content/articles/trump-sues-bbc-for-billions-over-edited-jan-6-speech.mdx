Former U.S. President Donald Trump has sued the BBC, claiming its edit of a speech he gave in Washington before the Jan. 6 attack made him say things he didn’t. He wants up to $10 billion in damages, citing defamation and unfair trade practices. The BBC apologised for the editing mistake and says it was an error. Trump’s legal team says the edit could have harmed his reputation and tried to influence the 2024 election.
