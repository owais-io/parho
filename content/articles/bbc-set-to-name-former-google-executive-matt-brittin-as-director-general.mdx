BBC’s board will announce that Google chief Matt Brittin will become the network’s director general. Brittin, who ran Google’s Europe, Middle East and Africa unit, is a tech choice to guide the broadcaster through the shift to digital platforms. He will face questions about the BBC’s funding, charter renewal and whether the service should move to subscription or advertising models amid political pressure. Brittin has stressed partnerships with tech companies and the potential of AI for creative content.
