Los Angeles County logged 2,208 deaths of homeless people in 2024, the first drop in a decade and a 10% fall from 2023. Most deaths were from drug overdoses, which fell 21% thanks to harm‑reduction programs. Still, traffic crashes and suicides rose, with the latter hitting a 10‑year high. The report urges more addiction treatment, primary care and violence prevention, and warns that funding gaps could undo progress.
