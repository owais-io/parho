A tiny Arizona community just outside Martinez Lake hit 110°F (43°C) on Thursday, the hottest March temperature ever recorded in the United States. Nearby towns in California and Texas also reached or topped 108°F, and Phoenix broke its own March record with 105°F. The heat wave left many places in the Southwest unusually hot for early March. Scientists say climate change made this extreme weather four times more likely.
