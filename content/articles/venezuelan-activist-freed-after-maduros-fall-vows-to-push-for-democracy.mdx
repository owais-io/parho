After Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro was seized by a U.S.-backed operation, Jesús Armas, a 39‑year‑old political prisoner, was freed from the country’s notorious El Helicoide jail. He now speaks out about the shaky start of a new democracy, urges the release of all political prisoners, and warns that Venezuela’s former top officials still hold power. Armas joins protests demanding real elections and an end to authoritarian rule.
