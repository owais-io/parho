Dame Marie Bashir, NSW’s first female governor, died at 95. She served from 2001 to 2014 and was a respected psychiatrist who pushed for better mental‑health care. Bashir was the first person of Lebanese background to hold the role and a champion for LGBTIQ+ rights. Friends and politicians praised her compassion and humility. A state funeral will honour her life of public service.
