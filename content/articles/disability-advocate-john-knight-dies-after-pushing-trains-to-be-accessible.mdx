John Knight, a disability campaigner who fought for accessible trains, died aged 67 from sepsis. While at Leonard Cheshire, he led the “All Aboard” campaign that pushed the UK government to set a 2020 deadline for trains to be fully accessible, instead of 2035. He used pranks, like sending postcards in a horse‑drawn hearse, to draw attention. Knight also worked on bus training, disability benefits and later supported assisted‑dying laws.
