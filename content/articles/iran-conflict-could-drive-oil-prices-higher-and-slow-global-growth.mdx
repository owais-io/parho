An attack on Iran by the US and Israel could raise oil prices as the Strait of Hormuz, a key oil route, faces threats. If prices rise 10%, the IMF says global inflation could climb 0.4% and growth dip a few tenths of a percent. Britain, the eurozone and the US already feel higher fuel costs, while central banks debate rate moves amid the crisis.
