Oil prices have spiked to $119 a barrel after the US‑Israel‑Iran conflict, with analysts saying a closed Hormuz Strait could push them close to $150. The jump would raise fuel and energy costs, hurt households, and could trigger higher interest rates and inflation. The shock may slow growth and risk a recession. Governments are eyeing emergency oil stocks and faster renewable‑energy moves to protect economies.
