A former Syrian army officer, Salem Al‑Salem, appeared in a London court to face charges of murder, torture and other war‑crime offences linked to the 2011 crackdown on protests in Damascus. The case is the first time Britain has charged someone with crimes against humanity under the International Criminal Court Act. Al‑Salem, who is seriously ill and uses a breathing tube, is accused of killing four civilians and torturing others while leading an air‑force intelligence unit.
