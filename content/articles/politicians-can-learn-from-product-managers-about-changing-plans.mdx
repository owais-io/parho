In the Guardian, a letter argues that politicians should treat policy changes like product managers treat updates, seeing them as normal iterations rather than failures. Critics like Kemi Badenoch called a shift in the digital ID plan a U‑turn, but in tech flexibility is key. The writer says politics has become obsessed with consistency, ignoring the benefits of agile, lean methods that adapt to feedback. He urges lawmakers to welcome change as a sign of smart governance, not weakness.
