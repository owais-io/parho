In January 2025, a 37‑year‑old chef named Thomas P was on Austria’s highest mountain, the Großglockner, with his girlfriend Kerstin G. When the weather got bad, he left her on a ridge and went to get help, but never called for rescue. Kerstin later died of hypothermia. A Innsbruck court found him guilty of gross‑negligence manslaughter, giving him a five‑month suspended sentence and a €9,400 fine. The case may change how climbers are held liable.
