New York City’s mayor has put the city on a snow emergency, banning all non‑essential travel from 9 p.m. Sunday to noon Monday. Schools get a full snow day and public services prepare for a blizzard that could bring up to a foot of snow, high winds, and flooding. Flights were already cancelled, and similar emergency orders were issued in New Jersey and Boston to keep people safe.
