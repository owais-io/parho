Unemployment in the UK climbed to 5.2% in December, the highest in nearly five years, while wages are growing more slowly. Young workers are hit hardest, with 18‑ to 24‑year‑olds at 14% jobless. Rising business costs and a shift toward AI are squeezing entry‑level hiring. The Bank of England may cut rates again as inflation eases, and the government plans a £1.5bn push to create 50,000 new apprenticeships.
