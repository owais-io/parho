UK petrol station owners say the government’s harsh language about rising fuel prices may have stirred the public to abuse forecourt staff. The Petrol Retailers Association (PRA) first called ministers’ words “inflammatory” and withdrew from a meeting, then reversed and joined it. While prices hit 18‑month highs after the Iran conflict, the government and CMA warn against profiteering, demanding retailers disclose margins to stop unjustified price hikes.
