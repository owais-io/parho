Rains and thunderstorms battered Karachi, Pakistan and parts of India this week. In Karachi, more than 50 mm fell and winds up to 60 mph tore down walls, a bridge and caused power cuts. At least 18 people died, many from collapsed buildings and a lightning strike. Neighboring Gujarat and Rajasthan saw lighter showers but still had outages and some tree damage. Farmers fear crop loss, as March rains are normally dry. The piece notes a hot spell in the western U.S.
