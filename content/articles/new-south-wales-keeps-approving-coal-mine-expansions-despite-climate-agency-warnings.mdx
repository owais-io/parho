New South Wales has decided to keep approving extensions and expansions of existing coal mines even though its own climate agency warned that such projects would breach the state’s emissions targets. The government will no longer consider new, undeveloped coal mines but will continue to allow larger mines to grow. Supporters say it protects jobs and energy security, while critics argue it keeps emissions high and offers no plan for workers when coal demand falls.
