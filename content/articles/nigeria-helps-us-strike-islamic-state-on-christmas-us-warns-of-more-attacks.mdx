On Christmas Day, the U.S. carried out airstrikes against Islamic State camps in north‑west Nigeria, using intelligence supplied by the Nigerian government. President Trump said the attacks were aimed at stopping the group’s killings of Christians. Nigerian Foreign Minister Yusuf Tuggar confirmed the strikes had the Nigerian president’s approval and stressed they weren’t about religion. U.S. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth warned that more strikes would follow, while Trump supporters celebrated the move as a “Christmas gift.”
