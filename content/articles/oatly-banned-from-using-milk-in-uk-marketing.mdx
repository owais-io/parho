The UK’s highest court has ruled that Oatly, the oat‑drink maker, cannot use the word “milk” in its marketing or trademark in the UK. The decision stops Oatly from calling its oat drinks “milk” or using slogans like “Post Milk Generation”. The ruling follows a long legal fight with Dairy UK and means plant‑based brands must use more descriptive terms. The case could affect other European trademarks.
