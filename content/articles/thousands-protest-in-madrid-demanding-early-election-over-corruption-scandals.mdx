Thousands of people marched in Madrid on Sunday, calling for a snap election as Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez faces corruption accusations. The protest was led by the conservative People’s Party, which accused Sánchez of a “mafia” style of politics and said the government was rotten. Attendees were drawn by the recent arrest of former transport minister José Luis Ábalos, linked to kick‑back schemes. Sánchez says the charges are politically motivated, but the demonstrations show deep public anger.
