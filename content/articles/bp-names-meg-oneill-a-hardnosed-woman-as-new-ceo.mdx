BP has named Meg O’Neill, a 55‑year‑old former Woodside CEO, as its new chief executive. She is the first woman to run a big oil company and the first outsider to get the job. O’Neill has led Woodside’s growth and worked at Exxon for 23 years. She wants BP to focus more on fossil fuels and less on green projects, a move that has drawn protests from climate activists. O’Neill is also a visible gay woman in the industry.
