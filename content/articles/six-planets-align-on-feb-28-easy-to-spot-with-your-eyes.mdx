On Feb. 28, six planets – Mercury, Venus, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune – will appear close together in the night sky. It’s a rare visual event that happens when planets line up from Earth’s point of view. You can see Mercury, Venus, Mars, and Jupiter without a telescope, while Uranus and Neptune need binoculars or a telescope. The best time is about 30 minutes after sunset.
