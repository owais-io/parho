Bulgaria’s Prime Minister, Rosen Zhelyazkov, stepped down after weeks of huge street protests over high taxes, bad budgets, and corruption. Thousands of people in Sofia and other towns shouted “Resign” and carried signs calling for an end to graft. The president and opposition asked the government to quit, and Parliament will try to form a new cabinet or hold fresh elections. The country is already facing economic worries as it prepares to join the euro zone next year.
