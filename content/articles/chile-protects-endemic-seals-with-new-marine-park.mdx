Scientists discovered a baby seal off Chile’s Juan Fernández islands, proving the species isn’t extinct. The fur seal population grew from a few dozen to about 200,000 thanks to community efforts and protection. Chile’s president signed a deal creating a huge ‘no‑take’ marine park, covering 337,000 km², protecting over half the country’s waters. The park will keep fishing away except near the coast, helping seals and the local lobster‑fishing community.
