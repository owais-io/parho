Western Australia’s EPA has cleared the Valhalla fracking project, which would drill up to 20 gas wells in the Kimberley region. Experts say it could add 1.8‑2.6% to the country’s greenhouse‑gas emissions. Environmental groups and many locals call the decision “outrageous” and are appealing the approval. The state premier says the project is not a green light, and the final say rests with the environment minister. The decision is under review.
