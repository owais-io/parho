Lobbying firms in the U.S. are helping both the chemical industry that makes PFAS chemicals and health groups that want to ban them. A study of six states found that firms like Holland & Knight and KP Public Affairs lobbied for and against PFAS‑related bills at the same time. This double‑dipping slows the push for stronger rules and lets firms earn money from both sides. The report says groups should cut ties with firms that play both sides.
