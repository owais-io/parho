Britain’s competition watchdog, the CMA, is looking into eight firms for hidden online fees and trick sales tactics. The companies include ticket sites StubHub and Viagogo, the AA driving schools, Gold’s Gym, and retailers Wayfair, Appliances Direct and Marks Electrical. The regulator wants to stop “drip pricing” and misleading timers, and can fine firms up to 10% of their worldwide turnover. It’s a move to help shoppers see the true price before buying.
