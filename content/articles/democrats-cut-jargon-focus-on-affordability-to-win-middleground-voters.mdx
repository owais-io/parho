At a Charleston conference, Democrats discussed how to appeal to swing voters. Critics said the party's language is too academic and ‘woke’, pushing voters away. The group decided to drop fancy jargon and focus on everyday issues like rising costs. They also talked about framing the child‑tax credit as a tool that lets parents get back to work, not as a handout. The goal is to keep voters who aren’t college‑educated and to make Democrats sound relatable.
