Labour adviser Richard Walker urges the government to set a short‑term cap on the profits of energy and petrol firms so they can’t take big windfall gains from the Middle‑East war. The proposal comes amid rising fuel costs that are hurting UK households. Economists warn the conflict could slow growth and raise bills, while unions push for a quick taskforce to protect jobs and help families.
