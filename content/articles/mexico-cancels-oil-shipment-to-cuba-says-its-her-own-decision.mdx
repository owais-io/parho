Mexico's president, Claudia Sheinbaum, confirmed that Mexico has cancelled an oil shipment to Cuba, saying the move was her own choice and not because of U.S. pressure. Cuba is running out of fuel, causing blackouts, and Mexico has been its main oil supplier after the U.S. stopped shipments from Venezuela. The decision comes while the U.S. is still watching Mexico's aid to Cuba. Sheinbaum wants to keep good ties with the U.S. while supporting Cuba against the blockade.
