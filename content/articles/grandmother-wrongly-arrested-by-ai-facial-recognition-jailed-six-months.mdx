Angela Lipps, a 50‑year‑old Tennessee grandmother, spent almost six months in jail after Fargo police used AI facial‑recognition software to mistakenly link her to a North Dakota bank‑fraud case. She was arrested at home, held without bail, then extradited 108 days later. After her bank records proved she was in Tennessee at the crime time, she was released. Lipps lost her home, car, and dog, and no apology came.
