Rev Jesse Jackson, the long‑time civil rights champion who ran for the Democratic nomination in 1988, died at 84. He built the “rainbow coalition” to fight for Black, poor and working‑class people. The Guardian invites anyone who knew him or admired his work to share tributes and memories. A simple form lets you submit your story, and the paper thanks readers for their contributions.
