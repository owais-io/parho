Canadian premier David Eby called meetings between Alberta separatists and U.S. officials “treason.” The Alberta group, which wants a $500 bn U.S. credit line to fund a new country, met with members of the former Trump administration. Alberta’s premier Danielle Smith opposes secession, while other provinces warn that U.S. involvement could destabilise Canada. Indigenous leaders and analysts say foreign help could make a referendum impossible and spark wider conflict.
