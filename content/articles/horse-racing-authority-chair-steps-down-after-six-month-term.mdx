Lord Allen of Kensington resigned as chair of the British Horseracing Authority (BHA) after just six months. He said he couldn’t change the BHA’s governance because all member organisations needed to agree. The board thanked him and will seek interim leadership. Several racecourses have called for an urgent review of the Racecourse Association’s governance to drive industry change.
