El Paso’s airspace was briefly shut down after reports that drones used by Mexican drug gangs had entered U.S. airspace. Experts say the cartels now own advanced drones that can drop bombs, carry drugs, and attack rivals. They can also cross the border for surveillance and smuggling. U.S. officials have detected thousands of such drones near the border, and Mexico denies new incidents. The situation worries lawmakers about safety and cross‑border retaliation.
