M23 rebels, who are believed to be supported by Rwanda, say they have taken the lakeside city of Uvira in eastern Congo. The claim is disputed by officials from Burundi and the Congolese government. The fighting has pushed about 200,000 people from their homes and left at least 74 dead. Uvira is strategically important and could let the rebels move further into the country. The war has sparked calls for sanctions on Rwanda and more international pressure.
