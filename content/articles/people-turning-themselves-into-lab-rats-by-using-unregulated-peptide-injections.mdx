A new trend is making people buy cheap, unapproved peptide injections from overseas and mix them in their own bodies. These peptides, sold online as “research chemicals”, promise faster healing, better skin, weight loss, and athletic boosts. But doctors warn they can cause serious side‑effects like allergic reactions, cancer growth, or infections, and most show no proven benefit in humans. The craze has spread through social media and tech‑savvy communities, turning the market into a risky experiment.
