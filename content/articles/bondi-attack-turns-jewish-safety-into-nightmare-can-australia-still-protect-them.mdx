The Bondi beach shooting has turned a rare safety for Jewish Australians into a nightmare. Over the past two years, hate crimes against Jews—defaced shops, firebombed synagogues, online doxing—have risen. The article’s author, a former Labor adviser, says the attack shows antisemitism is real and growing. He calls on the government and all Australians to speak out, act, and rebuild trust. He worries many Jews may leave, fearing they cannot live safely in a changing country.
