After the deadly Palisades fire, the Los Angeles Fire Department Foundation gave $65,000 to a top public‑relations firm, the Lede Company, to help the department answer media questions. The fire department had no public‑information director at the time, so the firm was hired to manage the heavy media workload. Critics say the deal shows the department is trying to cover up mistakes and raise doubts about its after‑action report.
