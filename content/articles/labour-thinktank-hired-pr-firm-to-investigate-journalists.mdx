A Labour thinktank led by Morgan McSweeney hired the PR firm APCO Worldwide to investigate journalists covering its money. APCO’s briefings name reporters from the Guardian, Sunday Times and others, and even look at possible hacks of the Electoral Commission for sources. The papers say the firm cost at least £30,000 and that it tried to find leverage over the journalists. Labour MP John McDonnell has demanded an inquiry, adding pressure on McSweeney.
