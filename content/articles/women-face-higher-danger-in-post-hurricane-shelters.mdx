After Hurricane Beryl hit St Vincent, women like 61‑year‑old Tedica Alexander had to run shelters, often taking on extra jobs as caretakers. Studies show that women bear more risk in post‑disaster shelters because they’re expected to care for others and shelters lack locks, private rooms and safety checks. In the Caribbean, UN and Red Cross groups push for better protection, more shelters, and gender‑responsive training, but many facilities still fall short.
