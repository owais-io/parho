An angel on a fresco in a Rome church that looked like Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni was taken down overnight. The painting had been restored by an amateur artist, Bruno Valentinetti, who later admitted the face resembled Meloni. The Vatican asked for the image to be removed, and the angel now appears headless. The incident drew criticism from the church, politicians and the public, sparking a debate about art and politics.
