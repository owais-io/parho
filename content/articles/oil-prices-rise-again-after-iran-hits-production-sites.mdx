Iran has fired drones and missiles at oil and gas production sites since the war with the US and Israel, sending Brent crude up about 2.3% to $103 a barrel. The attacks hit UAE’s Shah gasfield, Majnoon in Iraq, and the Fujairah oil port, where loading of a million barrels a day has stopped. Supply worries have pushed Asian countries to cut energy use, while analysts warn that fuel prices like jet and diesel could rise further.
