England and Wales will stop asking rape victims about past sexual experiences that could make them look dishonest. New rules will keep such questions out of court unless there’s evidence a victim has lied. The government also gives £550 million to help victims and adds new ways to keep them calm, like using screens or a courtroom helper. The changes aim to make courts fairer and stop many victims from leaving the system.
