Bunnings can use facial‑recognition cameras in its shops to spot banned people and cut down on shoplifting and violence. An Australian tribunal overturned a privacy commissioner’s ruling that said the company had breached privacy. The decision says the tech is allowed if customers are warned and if the system doesn’t unfairly target anyone. Bunnings will fix signage and keep watching the privacy impact.
