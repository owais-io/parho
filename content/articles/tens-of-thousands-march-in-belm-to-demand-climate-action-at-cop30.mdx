Tens of thousands marched in Belém, Brazil, on Saturday to pressure negotiators at COP30 to take stronger climate action. Indigenous groups, activists and artists used chants, songs and creative displays like a giant coffin and a 30‑metre snake to demand the end of fossil fuels, protection of the Amazon and more climate money for local communities. It’s the first major protest at a climate summit since 2018, with activists calling for real implementation.
