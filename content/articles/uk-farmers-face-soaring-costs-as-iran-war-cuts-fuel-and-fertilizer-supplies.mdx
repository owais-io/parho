The war in Iran has shut down the Hormuz Strait, stopping the flow of oil and key fertilizer ingredients. Prices for diesel, fuel and nitrogen fertilizers have jumped, squeezing UK farmers’ budgets. With subsidies cut after Brexit and unpredictable weather, many are rethinking crop choices or buying less fertilizer. The government and farmer groups warn that the cost rise could push food prices higher.
