Labour has announced a child poverty plan that scrapes the two‑child limit on universal credit, costing £3 bn but expected to lift 450,000 children out of poverty by 2031. The package also adds cheaper baby formula, faster exit from temporary homes, breakfast clubs and more free school lunches. Work and Pensions Secretary Pat McFadden said the plan is an investment in children’s future and that the welfare system still needs reforms to get people into work, while reviews are underway.
