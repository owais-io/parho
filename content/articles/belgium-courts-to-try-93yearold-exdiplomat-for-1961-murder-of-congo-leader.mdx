A 93‑year‑old former Belgian diplomat, Étienne Davignon, has been cleared for trial in Brussels over alleged involvement in the 1961 murder of Congo’s first prime minister, Patrice Lumumba. The court says he helped move Lumumba and two other leaders to a dangerous region, where they were killed by separatists with Belgian support. Lumumba’s family says the case is a historic step toward justice for colonial crimes. The trial could start in 2027 and may be appealed.
