France’s rail company SNCF launched a ‘child‑free’ carriage called ‘Optimum’ to give adults a quiet ride. The move drew angry reactions from the public, children’s rights groups and even the government. SNCF later softened the rule, saying it only bans those under 12. The article argues that shunning kids from public spaces is wrong and that adults should welcome children, especially as birth rates dip.
