After Cuba’s power grid collapsed, President Trump said he would “take” the island. His comments came as 10 million Cubans lost electricity, food spoiled, and the nation faced heat, mosquitoes, and economic fallout from U.S. sanctions. Cuban officials claim they’re open to U.S. business, but Trump’s threat signals a new level of pressure. The country is struggling to restore power and manage protests amid the crisis.
