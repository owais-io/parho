The letter argues Britain should learn from the Netherlands, which has kept flooding at bay for decades. It points out that the UK’s repeated floods are costing huge amounts in insurance and property damage. Dutch experts have built the Delta Works and manage two major rivers with little flooding. The writer suggests Britain could adopt raised homes, flood‑risk certificates and other Dutch techniques to protect residents and cut costs.
