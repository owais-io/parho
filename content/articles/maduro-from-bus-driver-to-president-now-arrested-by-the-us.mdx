Nicolás Maduro, once a bus driver and union leader, became Venezuela’s president after Hugo Chávez’s death. His rule saw a collapsing economy, widespread shortages, and violent crackdowns on protests, earning him international criticism. In 2025 he was taken into U.S. custody and faces drug‑related charges. Venezuelans and the world will judge him for his years in power, while his family, especially wife Cilia Flores, also faces scrutiny. The country remains tense and divided.
