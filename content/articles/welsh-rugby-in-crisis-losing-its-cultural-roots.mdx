Welsh rugby fans are facing a painful downturn after the national team won only two of their last 22 matches and suffered a 73‑0 loss to South Africa. The sport’s decline is tied to financial struggles, possible cuts to regional clubs, and a loss of its historic community ties. To revive rugby, Wales needs to bring the game back into schools, protect playing fields, and rethink how the sport fits into modern Welsh culture.
