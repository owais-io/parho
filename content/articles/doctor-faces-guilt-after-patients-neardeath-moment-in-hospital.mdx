A doctor in an Australian hospital watches an elderly patient with kidney disease collapse, but the patient had chosen to avoid resuscitation. A mix‑up over her wishes leads to a chaotic code blue, and the patient surprisingly survives. The doctor is left feeling guilty, anxious, and unsure. He learns about vicarious trauma—how intense work can haunt caregivers—and sees that many hospitals offer short, ineffective counseling instead of long‑term support.
