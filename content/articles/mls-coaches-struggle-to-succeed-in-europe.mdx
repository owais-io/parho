Many American soccer managers find Europe hard to beat. Former MLS coach Bob Bradley only lasted 11 games at Swansea City, and Wilfried Nancy was sacked after 33 days at Celtic. They say European clubs demand instant results, give little patience, and the business side can change a team’s fortunes overnight. Coaches like Chris Armas and Jesse Marsch keep moving back and forth, proving the climb is tough but possible.
