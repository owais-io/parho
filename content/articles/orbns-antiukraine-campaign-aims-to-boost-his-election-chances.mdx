Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orbán is using scary ads that blame Ukraine for everything—from rising prices to security threats—to rally voters before the national election. Huge billboards and AI‑made videos show bleak scenes, while the government has rejected EU aid and sanctions for Ukraine. Opponents say this fear‑mongering isolates Hungary, but Orbán’s party still leads in many polls.
