Shares in WPP jumped 11% after the Sunday Times reported that its rival Havas, owned by Vincent Bolloré, and private‑equity firms might want to buy it. Apollo said it won’t bid, while KKR stayed silent. WPP’s value has dropped 80% in eight years and it’s losing clients. The new CEO has started a review, and the company’s media arm, worth more than the rest of the group, is under pressure.
