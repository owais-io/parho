Scientists have shown that hedgehogs can hear high‑frequency ultrasound, up to 85 kHz. Researchers at Oxford and Denmark used tiny electrodes on rescued hedgehogs and 3‑D ear scans to find that their ears are built like those of bats, letting them detect very high pitches. Because dogs don’t hear as high, the team hopes to build car‑ and lawn‑mower‑mounted ultrasound emitters that keep hedgehogs away from roads, cutting traffic deaths.
