The parents of two Queensland police officers killed in a 2022 ambush are urging the federal government to go ahead with a national gun buyback program, after Queensland’s state government said it would not participate. They say better gun laws could stop illegal guns and prevent future attacks. Queensland’s premier says the buyback won’t tackle the real problem of antisemitism and hate, so the state is staying out. The debate shows how divided the country is over gun reform.
