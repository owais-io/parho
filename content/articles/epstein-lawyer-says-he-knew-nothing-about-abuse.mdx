Epstein's longtime lawyer, Darren Indyke, testified before a House committee that he had no knowledge of the financier’s sexual abuse. He said he only provided legal services and would have quit if he had known. The committee remains skeptical, noting that survivors and other witnesses question his ignorance. Indyke, who helped manage Epstein’s estate, denies any role in the crimes, but his testimony highlights gaps in the investigation.
