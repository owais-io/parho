Record coffee prices have hit Colombian farms, but a shortage of pickers means many beans go unharvested and spoil. Small farms in the Andes are hit by climate change, making growing conditions harder and drawing young people to cities. Big countries like Brazil use machines, but the rugged Colombian terrain and low farm sizes make that difficult. Farmers are turning to specialty coffee and other crops, hoping to keep their families afloat.
