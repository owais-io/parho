Almost half of people using job‑placement services were told their payments could be stopped. 618,000 suspension notices were issued – up 23% from the previous quarter – and 290,000 actually paused a payment. Many affected were First Nations. An Ombudsman report says the system may be unlawful and many decisions are overturned after the cut. The government says it is fixing the problem, but critics say it still hurts those it should help.
