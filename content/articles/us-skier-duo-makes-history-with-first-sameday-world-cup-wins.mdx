At the Tour de Ski in Italy, American skiers Gus Schumacher and Jessie Diggins became the first U.S. men and women to win World Cup races on the same day. Both won a new 5‑km mass‑start event that splits skiers into timed heats. Schumacher finished in 9:35.4, moving to sixth overall, while Diggins topped the women’s podium and widened her lead in the overall standings. Their wins show U.S. skiing is catching up to Europe.
