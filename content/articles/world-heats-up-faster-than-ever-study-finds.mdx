A new study shows the planet is warming faster than ever, with the rate climbing from under 0.2°C per decade (1970‑2015) to about 0.35°C per decade over the last ten years. Scientists say this acceleration started around 2013‑2014 and could push the Earth over the 1.5°C Paris‑agreement target before 2030 if emissions stay the same. While some variation may be natural, the trend stresses the need to cut CO₂ fast to avoid tipping points.
