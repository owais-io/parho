Three merchant vessels were struck in the Hormuz Strait last week, the hottest shipping lane in the world. A Thai bulk carrier caught fire and its crew was evacuated, while a Japanese container ship and a Marshall Islands‑flagged bulk carrier took minor damage. The attacks come after U.S. and Israeli strikes on Iran, which has threatened to block the strait and may have laid mines. Oil prices rose briefly but have since steadied.
