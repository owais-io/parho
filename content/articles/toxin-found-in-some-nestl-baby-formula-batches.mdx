The Food Standards Agency says a toxin called cereulide has been found in several Nestlé baby formula batches. The toxin can make babies feel sick. The problem comes from a shared ingredient supplier used by Nestlé and Danone. Both companies have recalled the affected products. Parents should stop using the recalled tins or cartons and switch to another brand. If a baby has already eaten the formula, call a GP or NHS 111.
