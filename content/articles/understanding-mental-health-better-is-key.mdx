A child‑psychotherapist writes that Wes Streeting’s new review of ADHD and autism diagnoses is a welcome step. She says the rise in reported cases shows real distress, especially after Covid, and that a diagnosis can help people get help and reduce shame. But she warns against quick, unqualified tests that waste money and can mislabel kids. She calls for evidence‑based, quality assessments and more support after diagnosis.
