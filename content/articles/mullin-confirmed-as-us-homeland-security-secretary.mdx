The Senate approved Markwayne Mullin as the new Secretary of Homeland Security, voting 54‑45 largely along party lines. Mullin, a Republican from Oklahoma, will lead a 260,000‑person department that runs ICE and the Border Patrol. He’ll focus on reopening agency operations shut down by funding disputes, keep a lower public profile than his predecessor, and maintain existing enforcement rules, including a requirement for judicial warrants before entering homes or businesses.
