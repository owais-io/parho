Residents of Bentham, a small North Yorkshire town, have been found to have dangerously high levels of PFAS, or forever chemicals, in their blood. Testing linked the pollution to a former firefighting foam factory, Angus Fire. The highest level measured was 405 ng/ml, more than 200 times the US safety threshold. About a quarter of those tested are at high‑risk levels, raising concerns over cancer and infertility.
