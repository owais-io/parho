Rupert Murdoch moved his major London newspapers to a new Wapping printing plant in 1985, using computers instead of the old metal press. His decision broke ties with powerful print unions, sparking a strike that lasted 13 months. Workers were fired, pickets set up, and some journalists chose to stay, while others resisted. The clash highlighted tensions between new technology, management, and workers’ rights, reshaping the British media landscape.
