Labour’s new plan would slash the number of jury‑based criminal trials by half and move many cases to judge‑only proceedings. Human‑rights lawyers say this cuts a key safeguard against unfair punishment, especially for serious crimes that still carry sentences under three years. Critics argue the move won’t speed up the backlog of 80,000 cases and could raise wrongful convictions. The government claims underfunding is the real problem, but experts point to decades of neglect.
