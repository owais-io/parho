Donald Trump is sending U.S. troops on missions in more countries than any previous president, but he’s doing it without Congress’s approval. The Constitution says only Congress can declare war or control money for war. History shows presidents stop fighting when lawmakers refuse to fund them. Democrats are hesitant to block Trump’s Iran campaign, even though many voters oppose it. Some lawmakers, like Senator Murphy and Rep. Khanna, argue that no extra money should be given, while others still support spending.
