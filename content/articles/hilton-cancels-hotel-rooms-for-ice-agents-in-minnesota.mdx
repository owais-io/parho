The U.S. Department of Homeland Security said a Hilton hotel in Minnesota shut down bookings for Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) officers. The hotel, run by Everpeak Hospitality, explained it was refusing to host ICE staff. Hilton apologized, said the action didn’t reflect its values, and promised to help affected guests. The move sparked protests, drew criticism from immigrant‑rights groups, and caused a small drop in Hilton’s stock.
