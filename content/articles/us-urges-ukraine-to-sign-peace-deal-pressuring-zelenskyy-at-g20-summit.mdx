At the G20 in Johannesburg, U.S. officials pushed Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy to accept a 28‑point peace plan that would give up territory to Russia and limit Kyiv’s military. EU leaders like Macron and Starmer met with Zelenskyy, urging the deal to include Ukraine’s input and fair terms. The U.S. threatened to cut aid if the deal isn’t signed soon, while Belarus pardoned 31 Ukrainian prisoners as part of the talks.
