The U.S. Supreme Court is weighing a case that could cut how many late‑arriving mail ballots are counted. Mississippi’s law lets ballots be accepted up to five business days after Election Day if they’re postmarked on time, but the court may force states to stop counting ballots that arrive after the polls close. The decision could affect over a dozen states and many voters, especially military and overseas ones.
