Kim Ju‑ae, Kim Jong‑un’s daughter, is widely discussed as a possible successor to the North Korean throne, but experts are split. Some view her public appearances as symbolic, while others believe she could become leader if the regime breaks its gender traditions. The country’s recent congress did not name her, and no official confirmation exists. Observers point to her frequent public appearances and symbolic outfits, indicating she remains close to the center of power.
