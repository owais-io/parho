Two Indonesian boys, 15 years old, were wrongly treated as adult smugglers after a faulty wrist‑X‑ray test was used to judge their age. They spent years in maximum‑security jails before being quietly released. Now, Attorney‑General Michelle Rowland has allowed their cases to be heard again, letting them appeal their convictions. The case follows a $27.5 million court award to 220 wrongfully detained children, after a previous AG rejected the request.
