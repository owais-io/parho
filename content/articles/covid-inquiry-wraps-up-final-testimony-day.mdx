Bereaved families marked the last day of the UK Covid inquiry’s public hearings, calling the government’s handling “incompetent and chaotic.” The inquiry, the costliest in British history, heard 381 witnesses and produced over 5 million pages of evidence. Two early reports blamed poor planning and the government’s chaos, and families welcomed the findings but say more action is needed. Five more reports will finish this year.
