The House of Commons Standards Committee proposed taking MPs’ staff names off the public register, a change critics say would lower transparency and erode public trust. The current register lists about 2,000 staff; the plan would double its size but then delete names if no interests are declared. Opponents argue knowing who works for MPs, especially if they have other paid jobs, lets voters spot conflicts. The move sparks debate over safety versus openness.
