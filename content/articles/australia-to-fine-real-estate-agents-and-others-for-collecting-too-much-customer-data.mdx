Australia’s privacy watchdog will check 60 companies in six high‑risk industries, like real estate, car dealers and bars, to make sure they only collect and keep customer data that is needed. Businesses could face fines up to $66,000 if they fail to explain why and how long they store information or if they send it overseas. The review comes after concerns that customers are pressured into giving personal details, creating security and privacy risks.
