The UK government has passed a bill that will end the seats of all hereditary peers in the House of Lords. The law now allows the government to give life peerages to some of those who will lose seats, and the Conservative party has dropped its opposition. Until now, 92 hereditary peers sat in the upper chamber, but the new bill removes that number entirely. The change will take effect when the current Parliament ends in spring.
