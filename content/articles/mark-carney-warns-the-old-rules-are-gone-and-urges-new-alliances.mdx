At Davos, Canada’s new prime minister Mark Carney said the world’s old, rules‑based system is breaking. He called out U.S. President Donald Trump for undermining international cooperation and urged “middle powers” to move away from building walls and instead form flexible coalitions, a strategy he calls “variable geometry.” Carney’s speech stresses that Canada and like‑minded countries must stay in the talks if they want to keep a say in global affairs.
