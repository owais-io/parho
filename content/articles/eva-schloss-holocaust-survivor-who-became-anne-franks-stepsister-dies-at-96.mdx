Eva Schloss (née Geiringer) was an Austrian‑born Jewish girl who survived Auschwitz and later taught the world about the Holocaust. Born in 1929, she fled Vienna, lived in hiding with the Franks in Amsterdam, and became Anne Frank’s post‑war stepsister when her mother married Otto Frank. After the war she married Zvi Schloss, ran an antiques shop, and became a passionate speaker and author, writing books for children and adults. She died at 96.
