More than 5 billion people now use social media, and the internet has replaced newspapers and TV for most news. Influencers, not politicians, drive conversation. Traditional political messages get drowned out or mocked. Right‑wing and disinformation figures thrive by riding the attention economy, while liberals struggle to adapt their messaging. To stay relevant, politicians must learn how to speak directly to online audiences.
