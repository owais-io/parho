Scott Morrison, speaking at an antisemitism conference, said Australia should register and accredit imams to curb radical Islam. Muslim leaders and community groups called the move a dangerous attack on religious freedom, saying it would divide Muslims and unfairly target a whole faith. Defence minister Pat Conroy and others condemned the idea, while Morrison denied he meant to police faith. Critics warn that singling out one religion can fuel division and radicalism.
