Germany’s parliament has passed a bill that lets farmers hunt wolves in parts of the country from July to October. The law aims to protect livestock after a rise in wolf attacks. The decision was made by the centre‑right coalition and the far‑right AfD, while the Greens and left‑wing parties voted against it. Environmental groups say culling isn’t the best solution and call for better fences and protection.
