UK rural households are facing a sharp rise in heating‑oil bills. The author argues the price jump is driven more by profiteering than by the Iran war, pointing out that suppliers raised costs after the conflict started. When the author complained, the supplier briefly lowered the price but many neighbours were not so lucky. The piece calls for regulators to enforce fair pricing under the energy cap.
