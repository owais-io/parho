Norway’s Crown Princess Mette‑Marit's son, Marius Høiby, was tried for 40 alleged crimes, including rapes, assaults and breaking restraining orders. Prosecutors say he is guilty of 39 offenses and want a sentence of more than seven years, plus a two‑year ban from contacting a victim. The case has drawn heavy media focus and added strain to the royal family, already dealing with criticism over the princess’s links to Jeffrey Epstein.
