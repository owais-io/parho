In Minneapolis, eight people were arrested outside a federal building after a day of protests against ICE officers. Federal agents used tear gas, pepper balls, and flash‑bangs on demonstrators. Trump aides warned that any obstruction of ICE would be punished, even saying officers had “federal immunity.” The city, state, and Chicago sued the Trump administration for what they call a “federal invasion.” Despite threats and arrests, crowds stayed on the streets, demanding justice for the killing of a mother by an immigration agent.
