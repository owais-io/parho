UK energy minister Michael Shanks said the government will examine every pound added to household energy bills after suppliers warned the Iran war could raise costs by about £250 a year. The government will not rush to cut prices but will target help for those who need it most. Past universal support was criticised for giving money to people who didn’t need it. A new price cap may still push bills higher, leaving many households with rising debt.
