Because families are having fewer children and many are choosing private schools or homeschooling, public schools in Memphis are shrinking. With fewer students, the district gets less money and old buildings need expensive repairs. To stay afloat, the board is closing under‑enrolled schools like Ida B Wells Academy. While some parents worry about losing community schools, officials say the money saved will improve newer, better‑equipped schools for the remaining students.
