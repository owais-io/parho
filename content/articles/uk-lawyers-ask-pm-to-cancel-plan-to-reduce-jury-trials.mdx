A letter from 3,200 lawyers, including 300 senior barristers, has been sent to Prime Minister Keir Starmer asking him to stop a plan that would cut the number of jury trials in England and Wales. The move is part of a government effort to tackle a backlog of court cases, but many lawyers say the evidence for the change is weak. The proposal has sparked a potential rebellion among Labour MPs, with more than 65 members possibly voting against it.
