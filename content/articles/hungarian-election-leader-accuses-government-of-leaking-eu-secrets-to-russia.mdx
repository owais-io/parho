Péter Magyar, the top opposition candidate in Hungary, says the ruling party is betraying Europe by sending confidential EU talks to Russia. He claims the foreign minister has been telling Moscow what happens at EU meetings and calls the act treason. The European Commission wants answers, and Hungary’s leader Viktor Orbán has started an inquiry. Meanwhile, Orbán has blocked a €90 billion EU loan for Ukraine, heightening tensions in the country’s election race.
