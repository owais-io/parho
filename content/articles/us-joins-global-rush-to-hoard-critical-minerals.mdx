The U.S. has joined other countries in a scramble to secure vital metals like copper, lithium, and cobalt needed for electric cars, batteries, and electronics. Rising global electricity demand, climate‑driven heat waves, and China’s dominance in refining are driving this push. The U.S. is buying stakes in mining companies and building a national strategic reserve, while other nations are also stockpiling metals to protect against supply shocks.
