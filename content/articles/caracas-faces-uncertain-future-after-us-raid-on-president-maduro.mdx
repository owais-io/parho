The night after a US airstrike and the capture of Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro, people in Caracas were left anxious and confused. Residents hurried to supermarkets to buy basic supplies, worried about shortages and security. Many feared a new crackdown and were unsure if Maduro’s removal would bring change. The army now recognizes Vice‑President Delcy Rodríguez as acting president. Neighboring countries have increased border patrols, and the future remains uncertain.
