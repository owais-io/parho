Spanish prosecutors decided not to pursue a sexual‑assault complaint against singer Julio Iglesias, saying Spain has no jurisdiction because the alleged incidents happened in the Dominican Republic and the Bahamas. Two women who worked at Iglesias’s Caribbean homes accused him of abuse. After a three‑year investigation, the complaints were filed with Spain’s top court. The case may still be pursued in the victims’ countries, but Spain will not act.
