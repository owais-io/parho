The Washoe Tribe has bought more than 10,000 acres of land near Lake Tahoe, turning the former Loyalton Ranch into the Wélmelti Preserve. The purchase, costing $6 million, was helped by a $5.5 million grant and private donations. The tribe plans to protect wildlife, clean up past trash, and use parts of the land to house members and revive cultural practices. It’s a big step in California’s Land‑Back movement.
