Daniel Suhr, 40, heads a small Chicago conservative law group. In just over a year he’s filed dozens of complaints against networks like CBS, ABC and NBC. The FCC’s new chair, Brendan Carr, has reopened many of them, forcing CBS to give out a 60 Minutes transcript and adding conditions to a big merger. Critics question Suhr’s sudden influence and whether he’s too close to the FCC.
