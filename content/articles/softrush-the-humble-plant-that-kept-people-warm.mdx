Soft‑rush is a spiky, green plant that spreads quickly across wet pastures. For centuries, its pith was used to make rushlights—cheap candles made from plant pith wrapped in animal fat. When governments taxed candles, poor people used rushlights to light homes. The article describes how the plant’s hidden silver cells give it a lattice structure and recounts old recipes for making rushlights, linking nature to history.
