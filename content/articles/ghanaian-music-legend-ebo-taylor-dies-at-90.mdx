Ebo Taylor, a Ghanaian guitarist and songwriter, died at 90. He blended highlife, jazz, funk and Afro‑beat, creating a unique sound that inspired Western artists and earned him a cult following. His first international album came out in 2010, and he toured Europe and the US, even after a stroke cut off his guitar playing. He left behind a family of 15 and a lasting musical legacy.
