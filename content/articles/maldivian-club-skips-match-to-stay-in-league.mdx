Green Streets, a top Maldivian club, avoided relegation by not playing its last game against New Radiant. The rules award a 2‑0 win to teams that fail to show up, so Green Streets stayed up while Club Valencia, the team that would have been relegated, protested and accused them of fixing. The football authority fined Green Streets 50,000 rufiyaa, put them on a transfer ban, but kept the result. Valencia says the decision was unfair and may appeal to AFC and FIFA.
