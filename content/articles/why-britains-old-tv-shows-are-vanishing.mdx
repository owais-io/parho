In the 1950s and 60s, BBC tapes were often wiped or junked, so many classic programmes are now lost. At a Quatermass fan event, actor‑writer Toby Hadoke explained why shows like Doctor Who and The Quatermass Experiment are still sought after – their rarity makes them feel like treasures. Volunteer groups now hunt old film reels before collectors die and their collections get dumped, but digital archiving has its own problems. The story warns that the last chance to save these shows is slipping away.
