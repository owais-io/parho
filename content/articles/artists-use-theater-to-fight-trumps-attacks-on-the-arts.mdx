In Los Angeles, artists used poetry and improv to protest Trump’s attacks on the arts and immigration raids. The show, part of a national movement called Fall of Freedom, highlighted the $27 million arts funding cut. Playwright Lynn Nottage, activist Azo Safo, and others have turned theater into a form of resistance, drawing on the Theater of the Oppressed tradition to let audiences participate and speak out.
