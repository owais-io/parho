On March 29, the Moon will briefly block Regulus, the brightest star in Leo. The occultation will happen just after sunset in the western sky. In London, Regulus disappears behind the Moon at 7:14 pm and re‑emerges at 9:21 pm. The event can be seen from towns and cities without special equipment – simply look west after dusk. The Moon moves fast, so the star disappears almost instantly, giving sky‑watchers a brief but striking moment.
