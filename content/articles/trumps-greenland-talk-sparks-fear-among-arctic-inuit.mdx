70 people marched in Nunavut, waving signs to show Inuit worry about U.S. interest in Greenland for minerals and defense. Trump said he wouldn’t force‑fully take Greenland, but the U.S. still wants to control it. Inuit leaders insist Greenland is self‑governing and not for sale, and they fear their rights are ignored in big‑power talks. Arctic leaders warn that indigenous voices are often sidelined when geopolitics heat up.
