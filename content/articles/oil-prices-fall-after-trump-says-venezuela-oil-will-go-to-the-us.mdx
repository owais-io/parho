Trump said the U.S. would take 30‑50 million barrels of Venezuelan oil, sending Brent below $60 and U.S. crude to about $56. Energy stocks like BP and Shell fell, while Asian markets slipped and Europe stayed mixed. Investors worry about added supply, the U.S‑China rivalry and the climate impact of boosting Venezuelan production. The move shows a new U.S.‑First approach and keeps markets on edge.
