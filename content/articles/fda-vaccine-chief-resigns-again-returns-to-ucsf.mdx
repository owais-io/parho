Dr Vinay Prasad, the FDA’s chief vaccine officer, steps down again for the second time in less than a year, heading back to his academic post at UCSF. His departures have come amid disputes over vaccine and drug approvals, with critics saying he oscillated between speeding reviews and adding new safety tests. He was briefly fired in July after backlash from biotech leaders and right‑wing activists, but was reinstated after pressure from health officials. The FDA will name a replacement soon.
