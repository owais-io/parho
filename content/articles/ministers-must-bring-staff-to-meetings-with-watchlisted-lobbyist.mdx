New South Wales ministers have been told not to meet alone with lobbyist Eamonn Fitzpatrick or his firm, EJF Advisory, after the firm was put on a watchlist for violating lobbying rules. Ministers must now bring at least two government officials to any meeting, take notes, and send them to the relevant public service agency. Fitzpatrick said he fixed the mistakes, apologized, and is improving compliance. The move highlights the need for clearer rules on lobbying.
