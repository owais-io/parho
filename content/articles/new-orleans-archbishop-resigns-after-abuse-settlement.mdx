The Vatican has accepted the resignation of Gregory Aymond, the archbishop of New Orleans, after he reached the mandatory retirement age and spent the last year meeting with abuse survivors. The archdiocese settled a bankruptcy case by paying about $305 million to 600 victims, a deal that required Aymond to hold daily talks with survivors. Pope Francis named former Bishop James Checchio as his successor for the community.
