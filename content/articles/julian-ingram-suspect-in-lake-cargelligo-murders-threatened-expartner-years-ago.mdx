Julian Ingram, who is accused of killing his pregnant partner Sophie Quinn, her boyfriend and her aunt in Lake Cargelligo, had a long history of domestic violence. Court papers show that in 2016 he threatened his former partner, breaching an AVO and calling her “sick” and “burning her house”. Despite this, police granted him bail and the case is now under investigation as part of a larger review of how he was handled.
