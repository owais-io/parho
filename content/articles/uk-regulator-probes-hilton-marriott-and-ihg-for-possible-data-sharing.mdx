The UK Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) has opened a probe into Hilton, Marriott and IHG. The watchdog says the chains might be exchanging sensitive pricing and occupancy data through the STR analytics platform, which could let them coordinate and hurt competition. CoStar, the company that owns STR, is also being examined. Shares of IHG fell 5% after the news, and the CMA will gather more evidence over the next six months before deciding if the law was broken.
