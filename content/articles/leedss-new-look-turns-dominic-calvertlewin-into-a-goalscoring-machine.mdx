Leeds United’s coach Daniel Farke switched to a 5‑3‑2 setup, giving striker Dominic Calvert‑Lewin a new role. The forward, who joined the club in August after a slow spell at Everton, has scored seven goals in seven games and is on track for an England call‑up. Calvert‑Lewin says a break with his family helped him reset mentally, and the team’s fresh tactics let him use his strength and quick runs to finish chances. The club is now unbeaten in six games, boosting confidence for the next match.
