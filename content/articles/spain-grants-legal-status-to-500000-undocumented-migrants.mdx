Spain's government has approved a decree that will give legal status to about 500,000 people who are living in the country without proper paperwork. To qualify, people must have no criminal record and have lived in Spain for at least five months, or have applied for asylum before Dec. 31, 2025. The move is seen as a human‑rights approach that could boost integration and the economy. Critics from right‑wing parties say it will strain public services and increase immigration.
