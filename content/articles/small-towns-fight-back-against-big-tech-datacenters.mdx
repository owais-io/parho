In many small U.S. towns, plans to build huge data‑center farms are sparking protests, resignations and legal fights. Companies like Amazon, EdgeConneX and Related Digital promise jobs and tax breaks, but locals say the deals favour the firms and hurt the community. Residents complain about secret meetings, low pay for officials and the risk of noise, power strain and lost farmland. The tension shows how tech growth can strain rural governance.
