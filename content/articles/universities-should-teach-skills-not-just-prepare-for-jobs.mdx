The letter argues that university courses should be judged by how well they prepare students for the economy, not just by student interest. The reply points out that higher education is valuable beyond immediate job skills. It notes that humanities teach critical thinking, analysis, and communication, which employers also need. It also mentions that past polytechnics offered industry‑linked sandwich courses that combined study with work experience.
