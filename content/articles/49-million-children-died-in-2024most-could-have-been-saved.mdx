A new UN report says almost 5 million children died in 2024, and most of those deaths could have been avoided. Progress to end preventable deaths of kids under five has slowed 60 % since 2015, and cutting aid is hurting the effort. The worst rates are in sub‑Saharan Africa and South Asia, where prematurity, pneumonia, birth injuries, malaria and malnutrition kill many. Closing hospitals and losing workers threatens to reverse decades of gains.
