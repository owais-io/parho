A UK investment firm called Oxygen Conservation is buying huge estates to rewild land and make money. CEO Rich Stockdale says the plan is profitable and good for nature, using data like in Moneyball to manage forests, peatlands, solar farms and wind turbines. The firm plans to own 250,000 acres and sell premium carbon credits. Critics worry the strategy relies on borrowing, may ignore local communities and treats nature as a commodity.
