An Australian halal certification company, AHAA, falsely claimed that its rival ICCV was linked to Islamic extremism. Midfield Meats, a big meat supplier, stopped its contract with ICCV because of those rumors. A Victorian court ruled the allegations were false, found AHAA's representative made the claim, and said the accusations caused the contract loss. ICCV earned about $35,000 a month under the contract before it was cancelled.
