The Environment Agency in England has downgraded almost all of the 2,778 serious water‑pollution reports it received in 2024 to minor incidents without sending inspectors. Only 496 sites were visited. A whistleblower says the agency is too cozy with water companies, which pay for its budget, creating a conflict of interest. While the agency claims it will do 10,000 inspections, the data shows a sharp rise in unvisited downgrades.
