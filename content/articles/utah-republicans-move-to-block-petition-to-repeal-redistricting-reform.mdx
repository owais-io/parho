Utah’s Republican lawmakers have passed a new rule that makes it harder for voters to withdraw their signatures from a petition that would repeal Proposition 4, the 2018 law that set up an independent commission to draw congressional maps. The move follows a court ruling that struck down the GOP‑drawn 2021 maps and restored the commission. The new law, added to a court‑packing bill, is part of a larger battle over redistricting that could affect the 2026 mid‑term election.
