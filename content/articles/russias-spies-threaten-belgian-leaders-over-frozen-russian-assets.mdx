Russia’s military intelligence has been sending intimidation to Belgian politicians and executives at Euroclear, the company that holds most of the frozen Russian assets in Brussels. EU leaders are debating whether to let the EU use those assets to loan money for Ukraine, a move that could help Kyiv for the next few years. Belgium worries about legal risks and security threats, while Russia says it will sue and warns of long‑term retaliation.
