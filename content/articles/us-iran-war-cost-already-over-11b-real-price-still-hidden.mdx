Pentagon told lawmakers that the first six days of the war against Iran cost more than $11.3 billion, mainly for munitions. However, the real cost may be far higher once you add troops, medical care, and aircraft repairs. Daily spending fell from $2 billion to $1 billion after the first day, and it could drop even more if the war eases. Congress is wary of giving more money without clear limits.
