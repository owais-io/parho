After Denmark’s election left and right sides failed to win a majority, Prime Minister Mette Frederiksen handed in her resignation and the king will now appoint a “royal investigator” to try to form a new government. The Social Democrats are the biggest party, but neither the red nor blue blocs have enough seats. The Moderates, with 14 seats, could become kingmakers as the parties start talks that could take weeks.
