Octopus Energy is selling a share of its software arm Kraken for $1 billion, valuing the business at about $8.65 billion. New investors, including Fidelity International and the Ontario Teachers’ Pension Plan, will own the majority, while Octopus keeps a 13.7% stake. Kraken makes it easier for energy companies to bill customers, run smart meters and manage electric‑car charging. The deal could help Kraken go public later. Octopus is still adding jobs and making plans for future growth.
