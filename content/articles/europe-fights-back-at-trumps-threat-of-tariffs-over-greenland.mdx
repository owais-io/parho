President Donald Trump warned he would impose 10% and later 25% tariffs on European countries—like Denmark, Germany, France, the UK—unless the U.S. buys Greenland. European leaders slammed the threat, saying it hurts transatlantic ties and is unacceptable. France’s Emmanuel Macron and Britain’s Keir Starmer called the tariffs “wrong.” Greenlanders marched in protest, chanting that Greenland isn’t for sale. The move could damage long‑standing U.S.–European relations.
