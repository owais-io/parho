Volvo and Polestar leaders are telling Brussels not to postpone the 2035 deadline that stops new petrol and diesel cars. They say a delay would slow electric‑vehicle adoption and let China get an advantage. German officials want to allow hybrids past 2035 because many buyers still hesitate. The car makers argue that keeping the ban will speed electrification, improve range, reduce charging time, and keep the EU competitive.
