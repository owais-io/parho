A Guardian columnist satirises Donald Trump's practice of giving his aides huge shoes to wear, suggesting it shows how he keeps them under control. The article jokes that Rubio, Vance and Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth all receive oversized footwear, implying a power play and a subtle test of loyalty. It also lampoons Trump's personal quirks, his use of big shoes as a metaphor, and how his staff might feel stuck in the president’s shadow.
