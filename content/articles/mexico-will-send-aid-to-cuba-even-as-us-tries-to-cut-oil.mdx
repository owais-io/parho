Mexico’s president, Claudia Sheinbaum, said her country will keep sending humanitarian aid and fuel to Cuba, even though the United States, led by President Trump, has ordered tariffs on any oil sent to the island. Mexico is now Cuba’s biggest oil supplier after Venezuela stopped deliveries. Sheinbaum denied talking to Trump about stopping oil and says she is looking for diplomatic ways to help Cuban people. The move strains Mexico‑US ties and could lead to more trade talks.
