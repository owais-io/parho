Rio Tinto and Glencore decided to end talks for a $260bn deal that would have made them the world’s biggest mining company. Both sides said the terms didn’t give enough value to shareholders, especially Glencore’s copper business. The collapse means Glencore shares fell over 10% and Rio’s slid slightly. This is the third time talks have fallen apart after attempts in 2014 and early 2024.
