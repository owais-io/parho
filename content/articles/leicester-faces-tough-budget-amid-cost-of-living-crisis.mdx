Leicester’s residents are feeling the pinch. Prices are still 20% higher than three years ago and many low‑income families are living on a handful of pounds a day. The city’s charity workers say cramped homes and energy bills are turning people into each other. The council is hoping the new national budget will lower energy costs, raise the child‑benefit cap and give more money to schools, but cuts could still leave the city stuck in a budget crisis.
