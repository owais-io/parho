In Melbourne’s Hopetoun Gardens, I spent months watching tawny frogmouths, a common night‑hunting bird. The study followed a breeding pair from August to December, noting how the adults fly silently, bring food, and care for their chicks. I saw the nestlings grow, survive a fierce windstorm, and finally fledge. Their quiet swoops, “oom” calls, and surprisingly friendly nature make them an unexpected star of the local wildlife scene.
