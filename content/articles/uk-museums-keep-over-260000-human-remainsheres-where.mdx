A Guardian investigation found that 241 UK museums, universities and local authorities hold more than 263,000 human remains—mostly skeletons, mummies and bone fragments. Only 100 sites gave exact numbers, totalling about 79,000 people. The Natural History Museum has the biggest collection, with around 27,500 individuals, while Cambridge holds roughly 18,000. Most remains are from the UK, but many are overseas, acquired through purchases, exchanges, or sometimes unethical means.
