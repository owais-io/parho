After fighting bushfires, Victorian farmer Neil Tubb turned his Longwood farm into a food hub for surviving livestock. Neighbors and volunteers bring piles of hay, water and supplies, while Tubb’s daughter coordinates donations. More than 15,000 animals have died, and many injured animals need euthanasia. Road closures slow aid, and local MP Annabelle Cleeland’s family has lost most of their property. The community’s teamwork shows their resilience and love for one another.
