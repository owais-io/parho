Social Circle, a 5,000‑resident town in Georgia, is fighting federal plans to convert a vacant warehouse into a massive ICE detention center. The government bought the 1‑million‑square‑foot building for $128 million, but town officials, led by city manager Eric Taylor, refuse to supply water or other services until talks begin. Residents worry about strained water, sewage, and emergency services, and the loss of the town’s small‑town feel. Despite community push, no ICE officials have responded yet.
