In Daulatdia, Bangladesh, a campaign has finally issued birth certificates to 400 children born in brothels who had long been invisible to the state. Thanks to a law change allowing registrations without parent details, NGOs and local groups collected information and convinced officials to approve the certificates. Now the kids can attend school, get passports and vote, and are less vulnerable to trafficking and abuse. The move gives them legal recognition and hope for a safer future.
