Brazil’s new president, Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva, said he would veto a congress bill that cuts former President Jair Bolsonaro’s prison sentence for the 2022 coup attempt. The law would lower Bolsonaro’s minimum time from about six years to just over two years and also help his aides. Lula warned the vote could be overridden, but he is ready to veto. The move is praised by Bolsonaro’s family and opposed by many Brazilians, who fear a return to impunity.
