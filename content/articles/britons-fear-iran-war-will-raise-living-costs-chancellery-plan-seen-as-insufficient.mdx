Rachel Reeves’ spring budget left many UK citizens uneasy. While the chancellor announced no big tax changes, people worried about student‑loan debt and the possibility that the Iran war could push inflation higher. Interviewees spoke about high mortgage rates, rising energy bills, and stagnant wages. They want cheaper borrowing, wage increases and clearer plans to curb food and fuel prices. Many feel the budget gives no real relief.
