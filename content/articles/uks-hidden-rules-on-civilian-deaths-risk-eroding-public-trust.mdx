UK courts say the government keeps its rules for tracking civilians killed in military attacks secret, which could make people doubt the system. Airwars, a group that watches for civilian harm, asked for details about a single death in Syria. The judge found no published procedure exists and that this secrecy could damage confidence. He called for clearer, open rules so the public knows what happens.
