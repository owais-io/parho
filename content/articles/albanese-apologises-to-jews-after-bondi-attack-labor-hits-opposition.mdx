Prime Minister Anthony Albanese said he feels the “weight of responsibility” after the Bondi beach terror attack and apologized to Australia’s Jewish community. Labor has taken aim at the opposition for using the tragedy for politics, calling out leader Sussan Ley’s comments about the foreign minister. The government has introduced tougher hate‑speech laws and will review intelligence agencies, but has not yet set up a federal commission on the attack.
