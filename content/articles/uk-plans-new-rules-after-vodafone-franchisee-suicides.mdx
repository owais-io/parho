UK ministers said they’ll create new rules to protect small shop owners who run Vodafone stores after suicide reports surfaced. The Guardian found former franchisees claimed the company cut commissions in 2020, causing debt and mental‑health problems. A group of 62 ex‑owners sued Vodafone for unfair commission cuts, and the firm has offered settlements to some. Vodafone says the dispute is commercial and denies putting pressure on partners. Officials hope a statutory code or arbitration could fix the imbalance.
