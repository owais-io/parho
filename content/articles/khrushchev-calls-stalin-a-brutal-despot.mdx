Khrushchev delivered a secret speech in February 1956 that attacked Stalin’s rule, calling him a brutal despot. He accused Stalin of causing fear, terror, mass killings, and weakening the USSR before World War II. The speech exposed the cult of personality and pushed for collective leadership and a return to Lenin’s ideas. Though leaked to the West, it was not widely published in Soviet media, but it marked the start of de‑Stalinization.
