Law enforcement charged a Pentagon contractor with illegally sharing top‑secret defense documents with a Washington Post reporter. The documents were used in at least five stories. Following the indictment, the FBI raided the reporter’s Virginia home, seizing laptops, a hard drive, and other gear. A judge has temporarily stopped the police from reviewing the seized items while a court review considers returning them. The case has sparked worries about press freedom and government overreach.
