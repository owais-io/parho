The Guardian invites readers to try three geometry puzzles from famous maths writer Ian Stewart. First, can a 33‑cell square grid be covered with 11 straight‑tiled tri‑cell pieces? Second, can a shape be cut into four equal parts that can re‑assemble into a square? Third, what’s the least number of pizza slices needed so five people can each get identical slices? Stewart also promotes his new book, “Reaching for the Extreme.”
