In Brisbane’s Gabba, Australia beat England in the second Ashes Test, thanks to star bowler Mitchell Starc who took nine wickets. The match was played under lights with a pink ball, a format that has become popular because it keeps crowds and TV audiences high. Australia’s experience with the pink ball helped them win 14 of 15 night Tests, while England struggled to adapt. The game proved night Tests can keep Test cricket exciting and profitable.
