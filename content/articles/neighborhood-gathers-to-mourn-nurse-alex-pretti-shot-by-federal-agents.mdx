In a Minneapolis neighborhood, the block where nurse Alex Pretti was shot by federal agents turned into a memorial. Local restaurants, bars, and residents offered free food, handwarmers, and candlelight vigils. Community members sang, danced, and shared stories while federal agents used tear gas and projectiles against protesters. The area also became a makeshift medical center, with local businesses helping those injured. The incident sparked anger over immigration enforcement and highlighted how neighbors rallied to support one another.
