Israel’s latest airstrikes hit two Iranian oil depots near Tehran. Scientists say the blasts released soot, oil, sulfur and heavy metals that fell with the rain, turning it into “black rain” that can damage lungs, skin and even contaminate water and food. Health officials warn that breathing the polluted air can cause headaches, breathing problems and long‑term cancer risk. Experts fear the war will keep spreading environmental harm as more oil sites are hit.
