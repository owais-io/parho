Iran’s top officials are accused of sending their adult children to live in the West on state money while their country suffers economic hardship and repression. Critics point to figures like Ali Larijani and others whose kids study or work in the U.S., U.K., and Canada. The move fuels anger over hypocrisy and raises questions about how foreign governments might handle these well‑connected residents.
