Georgia is appealing a judge’s decision that threw out criminal conspiracy charges against 61 people who protested the new Cop City police training center in Atlanta. The appeal keeps the case alive, raising questions about the use of a federal‑style RICO law on social movements. If the state’s domestic‑terrorism law is struck down, prosecutors across the U.S. could be more cautious. The case also highlights worries about vague laws targeting political activists.
