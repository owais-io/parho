A New York judge has ruled that Luigi Mangione will not face the death penalty in the case for killing UnitedHealthcare CEO Brian Thompson. The judge dismissed two capital‑eligible counts, leaving only two stalking‑related charges that could carry life in prison. Mangione remains accused of stalking, and the trial will start in September. Prosecutors have not yet said whether they will appeal the decision.
