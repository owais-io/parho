An inquiry found that Gibraltar's chief minister, Fabian Picardo, tried to stop police from acting on a search warrant that targeted his friend and business partner, James Levy. Picardo summoned the police commissioner, berated him, and shared confidential information. Although the police did go ahead with the search, the investigation shows Picardo's actions conflict with his duties. Levy was never charged, but the commissioner felt pressured and retired soon after.
