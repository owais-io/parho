Denmark’s intelligence agency says the United States is using its economic clout and even the threat of military force to push allies, marking the first time Denmark lists the U.S. as a threat. The report warns that U.S. power plays—especially in the Arctic and over Greenland—raise espionage risks. While Denmark still sees America as a key ally, it worries the U.S. stance could embolden Russia’s hybrid attacks and undermine European security.
