Hurricane Melissa hit Jamaica with 185mph winds, wrecking homes, hospitals and water supplies. While money is being paid to rebuild, experts warn that many survivors are still haunted by the storm. Simple sounds or sights can trigger flashbacks and anxiety. Women and people who lost homes or jobs feel especially vulnerable. Communities and doctors are stepping up to offer mental‑health help, but the emotional scars may linger for years.
