The Louvre, the world’s biggest museum, has had a tough year. Its new director quit after saying the building was in danger, with water damage, old tech and huge crowds. The €1bn “New Renaissance” renovation plan faced criticism for being too big and costly. Staff protests, a ticket‑scam, and a €88m jewellery heist added to the problems. The new director must fix security, repairs, and staff morale while deciding how far to go with the big makeover.
