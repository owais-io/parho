In 2019, Alexander Villa was convicted of killing off‑duty officer Clifton Lewis, but Guardian’s new podcast, *Off Duty*, argues the case was built on shaky evidence and possible misconduct. Investigative reporter Melissa Segura shows how coerced confessions, missing files and flawed digital evidence might have led to a wrongful conviction. The series highlights systemic failures in the Chicago justice system and raises questions about many similar cases.
