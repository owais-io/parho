Canada’s coach, Jesse Marsch, was nervous about the 2026 World Cup draw. The pairings look good on paper – they’ll face Switzerland in the group and Qatar in the third match. But the opening game is against whoever wins a European play‑in, most likely Italy, which could be a tough test. Marsch wants early info to prepare. He’s hopeful about Switzerland and Qatar but remembers Canada’s past struggles.
