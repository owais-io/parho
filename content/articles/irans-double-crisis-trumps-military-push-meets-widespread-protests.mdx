Forty‑seven years after the revolution, Iran is facing an unprecedented mix of domestic protests and a looming U.S. military threat. Trump has sent a large naval task force to the region, signaling he wants Tehran to accept a U.S.‑led deal or risk war. Inside Iran, protests over economic collapse and corruption are growing, while Israel’s attacks and regional pressure add to the danger. Experts warn that the only options are a forced compromise, a limited war, or a chaotic collapse, all of which threaten stability.
