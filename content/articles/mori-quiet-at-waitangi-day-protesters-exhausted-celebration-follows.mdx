On Waitangi Day, fewer than 100 people gathered to protest New Zealand’s leaders at the treaty grounds, showing how tired many Māori are of fighting over the Treaty. Politicians delivered speeches that left many disappointed, while the real focus shifted to a lively celebration the next day. Families, music, food and traditional canoe races turned the site into a space for community and culture, not confrontation.
