Massive fires in Brazil's Pantanal have burned a quarter of the wetlands and killed millions of animals, including jaguars. Climate change has made the region drier, raising fire risk. Researchers, volunteers, and local communities fight fires with limited gear, using GoFundMe money to buy masks and fire‑resistant clothes. The river remains the last safe spot for wildlife, keeping jaguars and other species alive.
