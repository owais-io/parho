France beat Ireland 22‑10 in the Six Nations opener at the Stade de France. Winger Louis Bielle‑Biarrey scored two tries, driving the French attack. France built a 22‑0 lead before Ireland scored a late try. The hosts’ ball‑handling, kicking and forward work left Ireland unable to win a tackle. Ireland, hampered by injuries, struggled to counter French pressure. Head coach Andy Farrell said the team must start stronger. Antoine Dupont returned after injury, but slippery conditions made the game hard.
