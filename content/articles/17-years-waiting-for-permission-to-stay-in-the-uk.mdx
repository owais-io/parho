After being tortured in his homeland, the man escaped to the UK in 2008 with a fake passport. He was jailed, wrongly convicted, and faced years of failed asylum claims because of poor legal aid and lack of experts on his country. He survived homelessness, worked in a bike‑repair charity, and finally found a strong law firm. In 2024, after 17 years, the court approved his leave to remain. He now hopes to work, study and reunite with his children.
