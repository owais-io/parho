Trump plans to replicate the way the U.S. helped remove Venezuela’s president by removing Iran’s Supreme Leader and installing a friendly head of state. Analysts warn the idea is unlikely to succeed because Iran has no U.S. troops on the ground, no diplomatic ties, and strong anti‑American sentiment. The Revolutionary Guard would probably block any foreign influence, and the move could spark backlash among Iranians.
