Rachel Cohen, a Chicago lawyer, says she won’t pay her $8,800 federal tax bill. She keeps the money in a high‑yield account and claims she doesn’t want her tax dollars to fund wars in Iran and Gaza or support ICE. Her move echoes a growing trend of Americans refusing to pay taxes on war spending, a trend that has exploded since Trump’s re‑election. A group says hundreds attend training sessions and risk penalties like wage garnishment or bank levies.
