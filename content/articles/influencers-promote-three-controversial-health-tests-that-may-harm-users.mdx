Three popular tests—full‑body MRI scans, egg‑count (AMH) tests and testosterone checks—are being sold on social media by celebrities and wellness brands. Doctors warn the scans can find harmless spots that cause anxiety and needless surgery, the AMH test gives false fertility data, and testosterone boosters can trigger health problems. A University of Sydney campaign is fighting the misleading ads and urging people to talk to doctors instead of self‑testing.
