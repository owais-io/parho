Matt Brittin, former Google boss, has been appointed BBC’s new director general, stepping into a role that must navigate a new funding deal with the UK government. Though he brings big‑tech expertise, he lacks editorial experience, raising questions about handling BBC crises. The board will also create a deputy director role with editorial expertise and appoint a new BBC News chief. The BBC aims to cut costs, reform its licence fee and partner with YouTube amid political scrutiny.
