Because of the war over the Strait of Hormuz, a key oil route has been closed for almost two weeks. Asian nations, which depend on Middle‑East oil, are running low on supplies. China, India and Japan are using their stockpiles and loosening price controls, while countries like Bangladesh, Thailand and Pakistan have started fuel rationing or price caps. The U.S. says the crisis will end soon, but the future remains uncertain.
