Rachel Reeves says she didn’t lie about the budget, insisting the tax rises were needed to protect the economy and build a safety buffer. She pointed out that the Office for Budget Responsibility cut its growth outlook, but that strong wages and tax receipts should cover it. Critics, including the opposition and Kemi Badenoch, are calling for her resignation. Reeves will defend her decisions and the government’s plans in a speech by Prime Minister Keir Starmer.
