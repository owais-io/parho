Michelle Mone’s husband ran a PPE company that won big contracts from the government during COVID‑19. The firm supplied faulty, non‑sterile gowns and owes the NHS almost £150 m. After a court found the gear was unfit, the Department of Health asked the court to liquidate the company. The firm also faces tax claims. Mone and her husband denied involvement for years, but now admit they profited from the deals.
