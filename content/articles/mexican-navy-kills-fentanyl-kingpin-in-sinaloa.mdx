Mexican navy soldiers killed Pedro Inzunza Coronel, known as “El Pichón,” a top fentanyl trafficker in Sinaloa. He and his father were charged by the U.S. with moving huge amounts of fentanyl, cocaine, meth and heroin into the U.S. and with money‑laundering. Mexican police seized 1.65 tons of the drug in earlier raids. The operation also found weapons, vehicles and drug labs. U.S. officials praised the joint effort.
