US‑Israel attacks on Iran release huge amounts of greenhouse gases. A new study shows the first two weeks of bombing produced about 5 million tonnes of CO2‑equivalent—more than 84 low‑emission countries combined. Most of it came from destroyed buildings, jets flying long distances, and oil facilities set on fire. The climate damage is already as big as a small country’s yearly emissions and could grow as the war continues.
