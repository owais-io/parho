Paul Phillips, 49, said his Labrador Stanley dug up a blue glass bottle marked “Not to be taken” in his garden. He thinks it might be linked to the 1865 murder of William Ashford by his wife, Mary Ann, who was publicly hanged. Stanley keeps digging the same spot, and Paul wonders if the bottle was buried by the Ashford family. The find reminds people of the old case and its effect on public executions.
