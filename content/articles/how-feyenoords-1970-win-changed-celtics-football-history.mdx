Celtic’s 1970 European Cup final loss to Feyenoord was a turning point. They had won the Lisbon final the previous year but the defeat shook the club, leading to the break‑up of the famous Lisbon Lions and a long absence from Europe’s biggest stage. The loss showed managers like Jock Stein underestimated a Dutch side, hurting confidence. It forced Celtic to rethink tactics and rebuild, but they never reached another final again.
