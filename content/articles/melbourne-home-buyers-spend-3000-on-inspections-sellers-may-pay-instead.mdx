A first‑time buyer in Melbourne named Toshi paid more than $3,000 for six building and pest inspections, discovering serious damage before buying. The Victorian government is proposing that sellers must pay and provide these reports to buyers, hoping to protect buyers from costly surprises. Some buyers worry the plan could give a false sense of safety. The change is part of broader reforms tackling hidden costs and under‑priced homes.
