Farmers in Kentucky’s Mason County are turning down offers of more than $30 million to turn their fields into data centres that will power AI. While tech firms promise jobs and tax money, farmers say the land is part of their family history and their way of life. Similar rejections have happened across the U.S., with concerns about power use, water use and loss of farmland. The clash shows how the tech boom is reshaping rural America.
