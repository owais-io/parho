In 1946 Churchill spoke in Missouri about the danger of Soviet expansion. He warned that many European capitals were now under Soviet influence and called this a new "iron curtain." Churchill urged a UN air force, tighter ties with the U.S., and more contact between the peoples of Europe and America. The speech sparked criticism from the Soviet press, which accused Churchill of being hostile to the USSR after the war.
