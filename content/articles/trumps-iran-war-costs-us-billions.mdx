Trump’s administration launched a joint US‑Israel attack on Iran in late February. In the first week, it spent about $11.3 billion on bombs that killed hundreds. That amount exceeds the annual budgets for agencies like the EPA, CDC, and the National Cancer Institute. The spending highlights a shift toward military actions over funding public health and science, sparking concerns about national priorities.
