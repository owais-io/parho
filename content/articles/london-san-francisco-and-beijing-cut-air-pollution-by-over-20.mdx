A study of almost 100 cities found that 19 have cut two major air‑pollutant levels by 20‑45% since 2010. London, San Francisco and Beijing are among the top performers, thanks to cycle lanes, more electric cars and bans on dirty vehicles. Beijing and Warsaw led the way for fine dust, while Amsterdam and Rotterdam cut nitrogen dioxide. The report shows cities can solve the crisis.
