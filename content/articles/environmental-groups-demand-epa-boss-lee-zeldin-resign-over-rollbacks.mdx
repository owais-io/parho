Over 160 environmental and public‑health groups have asked EPA head Lee Zeldin to step down, saying his cuts and staff reductions betray the agency’s mission to protect people and the planet. They blame his weakening of climate, air and water rules and say he favors polluters. The letter, signed by groups like Sierra Club, Public Citizen and Physicians for Social Responsibility, calls for a leader who will tackle pollution and climate science, not corporate interests.
