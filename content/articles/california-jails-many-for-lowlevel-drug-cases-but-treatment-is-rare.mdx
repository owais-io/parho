California has used a 2024 law to file almost 20,000 drug‑possession felonies, but less than 1% of those charged end up in treatment. The new measure, called Prop 36, was meant to give people help instead of jail. Instead, thousands are sent to prison, especially in some counties, and racial disparities are high. The state needs more money and better programs.
