An inquiry says a former UK special forces chief and senior officers tried to hide reports that SAS troops killed civilians in Afghanistan. A whistle‑blower said the chain of command stopped short in 2011 and that killings, including shooting two toddlers asleep in their beds, continued until 2013. The case is part of a larger investigation into about 80 alleged unlawful killings by three SAS units. The government says it supports the inquiry and will wait for its findings.
