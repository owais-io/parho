Donald Trump praised FCC chief Brendan Carr at a White‑House event, calling him a powerful figure. Carr has warned that broadcasters airing “hoaxes” could lose their licences, sparking criticism from Democrats and even some Republicans. He has opened investigations into ABC shows, pushed a big Nexstar‑TEGNA merger, and frequently speaks with Trump. The move has raised fears of media censorship and the FCC acting like a “paper tiger” rather than an independent regulator.
