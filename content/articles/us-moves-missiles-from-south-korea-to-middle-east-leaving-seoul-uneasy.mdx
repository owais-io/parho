South Korea is worried after the US moved parts of its THAAD missile‑defence system and other weapons out of the country for use in the Middle East. The move raises doubts about the US’s commitment to South Korea and could give North Korea a chance to push back. Korean leaders say the shift won’t hurt their security, but critics warn that the US is abandoning its key ally and could leave Seoul and Japan more vulnerable.
