Slovenia’s recent election pits the centre‑left leader Robert Golob against right‑wing populist Janez Janša. Both have stirred anti‑Roma sentiment, with new laws allowing police to raid Roma neighbourhoods. The vote, seen as a test of how the country treats its marginalized Roma, could decide whether democratic norms survive or if stricter, discriminatory measures spread. The campaign also involved corruption scandals and fears that the Roma community’s rights will be further eroded.
