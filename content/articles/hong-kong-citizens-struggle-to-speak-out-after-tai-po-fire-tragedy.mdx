Hong Kong has lost 159 people in a massive Tai Po fire, and people want to know why it happened. But the city’s government, under pressure from Beijing, has shut down protest groups, media, and lawyers who could ask questions. The crackdown means there’s no public debate, and even small acts of criticism are met with arrests. People are left feeling voiceless as officials keep their jobs.
