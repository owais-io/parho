Scientists say the record heatwave that hit the US west this week—temperatures up to 30 °F (17 °C) higher than normal—would almost never happen without climate change. Their study, covering March 18‑22, found the event is four times more likely now than a decade ago. The heat dome broke temperature records in 140 cities, raised health risks, and forced ski resorts to close. Action on climate policy is urgent.
