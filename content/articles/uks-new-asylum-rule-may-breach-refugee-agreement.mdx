UK Home Secretary Shabana Mahmood announced a new rule that will make a person’s refugee status temporary. After 30 months, refugees must apply again or pay a fee to stay, similar to Denmark’s system. Lawyers, refugees’ groups and unions say the move clashes with the 1951 Refugee Convention and could re‑traumatise people who fled war and persecution. They warn it creates long‑term uncertainty and hurts people’s chances to settle.
