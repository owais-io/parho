UK officials recommend limiting money from British voters abroad to £100,000–£300,000 per year and temporarily banning political donations made in cryptocurrency. The aim is to reduce foreign interference and make donations easier to track. The rules would hit Reform UK, which has received millions from overseas donors, and could shape new election‑law proposals. Other suggestions include tighter donor checks and a ban on foreign‑funded adverts.
