In 2026, eight people have died in incidents involving the U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement agency. Some were shot by agents—like Minneapolis nurse Alex Pretti and mother Renee Good—while others died in custody from medical problems or suicide. Cases include a Honduran man who died of heart failure at a Texas hospital, a Cuban who was allegedly choked, and a Mexican found hanging. The deaths have sparked protests and calls for better care.
