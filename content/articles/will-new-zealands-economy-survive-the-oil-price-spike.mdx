New Zealand’s economy is showing early signs of recovery, with growth expected to rise to about 1.6 % this year and 2.8 % next year. But the war in the Middle East is pushing oil prices up and could shake the country’s fragile rebound. The small, export‑and‑tourism‑based economy is vulnerable to supply‑chain hits, higher fuel costs and global market swings. Analysts warn the recovery may stall for a quarter.
