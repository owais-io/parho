Schroders, a British asset‑management firm that has been run by the same family for 200 years, will be bought for £9.9 billion by Chicago‑based Nuveen. The deal will keep the company’s London headquarters and its name but remove it from the London Stock Exchange. It will become part of one of the world’s biggest fund managers, adding to a trend of UK firms leaving the market for U.S. or European rivals.
