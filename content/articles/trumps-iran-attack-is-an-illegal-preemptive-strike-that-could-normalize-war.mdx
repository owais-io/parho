The U.S. and Israel’s strike that killed Iran’s top leader, Ayatollah Khamenei, was a targeted assassination, not a defensive move. It broke international law because there was no imminent attack from Iran and Congress didn’t approve it. The attack has sparked Iranian retaliation, raised fears of higher oil prices, and could set a dangerous precedent where powerful countries feel free to strike pre‑emptively. This threatens the global rules that keep war in check.
