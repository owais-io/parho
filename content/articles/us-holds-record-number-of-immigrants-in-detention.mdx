The U.S. immigration agency ICE says it now holds a record 68,400 people in detention, the most ever recorded. Since the start of the year, the department has arrested more than 328,000 and deported nearly 327,000 people. Despite a push to target serious criminals, most detainees have no criminal record—showing the policy mainly targets ordinary undocumented migrants. Communities are organizing to block raids and provide support, and the report notes growing opposition to the administration’s approach.
