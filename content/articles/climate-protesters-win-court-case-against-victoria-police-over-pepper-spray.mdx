Climate activists won a class action against Victoria Police after police used capsicum (pepper) spray on them during an anti‑mining protest in Melbourne. Jordan Brown, who was sprayed twice in 2019, was found to have been unlawfully bathed and received $54,000 in damages. The case may set a new standard for police crowd‑control tactics, and activists are calling for a ban on pepper spray at demonstrations.
