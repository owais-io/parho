FDA has changed its plan to ban artificial food dyes. Instead of a hard ban, it will allow companies to label products as “no artificial colors” as long as the dyes aren’t petroleum‑based, even if they still contain chemicals like titanium dioxide. Health experts say this move could mislead shoppers and leave dangerous substances in food. The change has drawn criticism from consumer‑health groups and state lawmakers.
