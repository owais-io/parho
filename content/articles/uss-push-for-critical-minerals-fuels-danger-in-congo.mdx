The United States has rolled out a $12 bn plan to secure minerals like cobalt, lithium and tantalum, while other powers race to outpace China. Deals promise wealth and peace for the Democratic Republic of Congo, but they ignore local safety and processing needs. A recent mine collapse in Rubaya killed more than 200 miners, showing that the hunt for clean‑energy materials often brings violence, smuggling and human‑rights abuses.
