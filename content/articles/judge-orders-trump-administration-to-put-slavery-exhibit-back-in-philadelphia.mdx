A federal judge in Pennsylvania told the National Park Service to put back a slavery exhibit at George Washington’s home in Philadelphia. The exhibit was taken down after President Trump said it was “anti‑American.” Philadelphia sued, arguing the removal was illegal. The judge said the government can’t erase history and ordered the exhibit be restored while the case continues. The judge highlighted nine enslaved people who lived there and quoted George Orwell to stress the danger of rewriting history.
