In North Finchley’s Little Tehran, Iranian families in London are split after the death of Iran’s Supreme Leader Khamenei. Some cheer, while others like Sara and her father feel uneasy about the war’s fallout. They fear that celebrating the regime’s fall could invite violence or U.S. retaliation, and that the UK’s friendly stance toward Iran might hurt the community. The story shows the diaspora grappling with relief and fear.
