Over the weekend, Chinese military planes were said to have pointed their radar at Japanese fighter jets near Okinawa, raising tensions. Japan’s prime minister said the country would respond calmly but firmly, promising to boost surveillance and monitor Chinese activity. Japan summoned China’s ambassador, while China denied the claims and protested. The incident adds to rising disputes between the two nations over air and sea security.
