Scientists at CERN discovered a new, heavier version of the proton, thanks to an upgraded detector. The particle, four times heavier, contains two charm quarks instead of up quarks and could help explain the strong nuclear force. It appeared in LHCb data and was spotted in just a year of improved data. Meanwhile, UK scientists worry that funding cuts to the LHCb upgrade may hinder future discoveries, and are pushing for full support.
