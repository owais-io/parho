In a Melbourne suburb, a brief meeting at a business park turned into a hot‑button dispute over the pre‑selection of MP Moira Deeming. Her backers say they were locked out and the vote was predetermined, while party leaders claim the voters were simply late and Deeming had no realistic chance. The clash highlights deep divisions inside the Victorian Liberals, with rival candidates, concerns about branch‑vote integrity, and the threat of One Nation defections.
