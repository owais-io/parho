Persimmon, one of the UK's biggest housebuilders, warned that fighting with Iran could scare buyers, raise inflation and keep mortgage rates high. The company expects to finish about 12,000 homes this year but says the war could shift customer confidence. Banks are already raising fixed‑rate mortgages, and the Bank of England might keep rates near 3.75 %. A Barclays survey shows most Britons fear higher fuel, food and energy costs.
