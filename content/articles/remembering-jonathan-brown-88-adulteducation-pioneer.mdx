Jonathan Brown, 88, was a leading adult‑education pioneer. He began as a lecturer at a police college, then taught at Newcastle University where he set up courses for women. For over two decades he worked at the Open University, helped launch its European Scheme, founded the National Association of Educational Guidance for Adults, and co‑authored a 1986 government report on adult guidance. He studied PPE at Oxford, married Jean in 1963, and is survived by family.
