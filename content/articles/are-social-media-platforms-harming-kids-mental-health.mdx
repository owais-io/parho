Meta, YouTube, Snap and TikTok are facing lawsuits that say their platforms hurt kids’ mental health. The case claims the sites are addictive, leading to depression, eating disorders and even suicide. Scientists say proving addiction is hard and that the evidence shows only a small average harm. Experts warn that while social media can be harmful, calling it “addictive” may overstate the issue. The trials could push new rules on how the platforms are run.
