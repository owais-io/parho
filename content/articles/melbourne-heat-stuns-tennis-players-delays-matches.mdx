The Australian Open hit 39 °C, forcing players to rely on ice towels, hydration, and higher‑tension rackets. A heat‑stress scale rose to 5, shutting roofs on Rod Laver, Margaret Court, and John Cain arenas and suspending play for hours. Jannik Sinner collapsed from cramps, while others like Madison Keys and Jessica Pegula adjusted gear and prep. Fans huddled in air‑conditioned halls, and some players joked that they’d never feel cold again.
