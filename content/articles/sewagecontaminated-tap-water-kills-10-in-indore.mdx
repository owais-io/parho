Sewage mixed into the tap water in Indore, India’s city often called the cleanest in the country, caused at least ten deaths, including a baby, and sickened more than 270 people. Residents had complained about foul‑smelling water for months, but the problem was ignored. Tests found bacteria from sewage in the supply. Municipal officials are suspended while investigations continue, and new rules are being promised to prevent future incidents.
