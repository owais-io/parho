At Low Park, an abandoned farm near the Lune Gorge, I’m planting fruit trees every 200 metres to create diverse hedgerows. I’ve also started an apple and damson orchard. The trees are growing and early primroses are already blooming. Inside the old farmhouse, moss, ferns, and a temperate rainforest are taking over. I remember children once crossing the River Lune in a wooden box to get to school. Now nature is quietly reclaiming the place.
