Britain’s rail companies are launching a big timetable overhaul this weekend. The new schedule will add extra trains, cut journey times to Edinburgh and Leeds, and give people more seats on the East Coast Main Line. After the chaos of last year’s change, railway officials say they’ve learned from the mistakes, but passengers still worry about delays. The government hopes the £4bn investment will pay off and improve travel across the country.
