French President Emmanuel Macron and German President Frank‑Walter Steinmeier both said the U.S., especially under Donald Trump, is moving away from international rules and alliances. They cited the U.S. raid in Caracas, its interest in Greenland, and a general weakening of multilateral institutions. The two leaders warned that this could turn the world into a “robber’s den,” putting weaker states at risk. The EU is now debating how to respond while still relying on the U.S. for security and trade.
