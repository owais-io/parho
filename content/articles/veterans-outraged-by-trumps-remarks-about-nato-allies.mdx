Former soldiers from Canada, the UK, and Poland slammed Donald Trump after he said NATO troops were “a little off the frontlines” in Afghanistan. The comments angered troops who fought side‑by‑side with Americans, including a Canadian reservist who survived a friendly‑fire attack. The president later praised UK forces but never apologized, leaving veterans feeling disrespected and hurt.
