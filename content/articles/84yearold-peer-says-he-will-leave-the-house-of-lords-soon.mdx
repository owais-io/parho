David Trefgarne, an 84‑year‑old hereditary peer who has sat in the House of Lords since 1962, will leave when the bill to remove hereditary peers becomes law next spring. He says it’s a natural end to an era. Though he hasn’t been a minister in 35 years, he’s stayed active in committees and debates. He’s sad about leaving but feels it’s time for change.
