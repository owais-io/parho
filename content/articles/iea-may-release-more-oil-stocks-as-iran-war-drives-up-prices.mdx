Energy watchdog IEA says it may put more emergency oil supplies on sale to lower prices that have jumped because of the Iran‑Saudi conflict blocking the Strait of Hormuz. They already released 100 million barrels to Asia this week, but have enough for more releases. The IEA hopes the strait opens soon so Gulf oil can get back to markets. U.S. and other nations push to unblock the route.
