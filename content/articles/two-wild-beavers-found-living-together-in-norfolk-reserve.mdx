A nature reserve near Norwich has found not one but two wild beavers living together. Camera footage shows the pair swimming and grooming, the first wild beaver couple in Norfolk since the 1500s. The origin of the animals is unclear – the reserve suspects they may have been released illegally. The beavers are helping clean the riverbank and encourage new plants, and officials are investigating the sighting. If more beavers are allowed, the river could become healthier.
