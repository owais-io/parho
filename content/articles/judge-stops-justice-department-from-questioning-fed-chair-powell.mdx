A federal judge stopped the Justice Department from sending subpoenas to Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell. The investigation, tied to the Fed’s expensive renovation, is seen by many as a Trump‑era attempt to pressure the Fed to lower rates. The judge said there was no evidence of wrongdoing, calling the case a pretext. The move is part of a broader clash between the Treasury, the Justice Department, and the Fed’s independence.
