ICE raids across Minnesota’s suburbs have turned quiet neighborhoods into zones of fear. Federal agents have taken people away, schools now patrol their gates, and churches are quietly supplying food and shelter to families. Communities are divided politically, making help hard to give openly. While the surge is winding down, the trauma and disruption linger, leaving many families uncertain about returning to normal life.
