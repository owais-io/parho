Since taking office, the new Labour government has flipped several key policies – from making digital ID cards mandatory to cutting inheritance tax on farms and changing pub business rates. These repeated U‑turns have left many voters and MPs puzzled and frustrated. Critics, like Tory leader Kemi Badenoch, poke fun at the lack of direction. The constant reversal is hurting Starmer’s credibility and makes it hard for people to trust the government’s plans.
