Herbert Munk, a 94‑year‑old electrical engineer, died after a long career at GEC in Coventry. He helped build Britain’s first computer‑controlled telephone exchange and held three patents, including one for speech transmission. Born in Vienna, his family escaped to Yugoslavia, then Kenya, before moving to the UK in 1949. He loved bridge, theatre, and learning languages, supported the EU, and is survived by his daughters, grandchildren and great‑grandchildren.
