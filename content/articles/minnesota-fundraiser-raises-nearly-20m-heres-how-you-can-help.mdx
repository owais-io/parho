After ICE raids in Minneapolis, a Minneapolis‑born woman named Ashley Fairbanks created an online hub called Stand With Minnesota. She quickly grew it into a tool that connects donors with people who need help—flights home, groceries, laundry, and rent relief. Since opening, the site has pulled in 2.4 million visits and raised almost $20 million. Fairbanks wants other cities to copy her model so neighbors can help each other directly.
