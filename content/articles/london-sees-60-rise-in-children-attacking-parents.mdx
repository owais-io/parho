Over the last decade, London police recorded a 60‑percent jump in violent attacks by children on their parents, rising from 1,886 incidents in 2015 to 3,091 in the first ten months of 2025. Experts say the pandemic, rising child poverty and stronger reporting help may be driving the trend. While the numbers are still small, the rise shows a growing family‑violence problem.
