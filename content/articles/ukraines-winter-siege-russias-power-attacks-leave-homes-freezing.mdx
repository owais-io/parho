Since late 2025, Russian missile strikes have hit Ukraine’s power grid, leaving electricity out for hours on end. In cities like Kyiv, thousands of homes lose heating during the coldest months, forcing families to use gas stoves, tents or generators to stay warm. The attacks cost about $1 billion to repair, and many civilians are freezing, even as the Kremlin uses winter itself as a weapon.
