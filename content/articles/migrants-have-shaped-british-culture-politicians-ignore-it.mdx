Migrants are often shown as both eager to join society and dangerous, but that’s a false picture. The article shows how migrants have added to art, music, architecture and even religious texts. It shares stories of refugee artists and the everyday objects they carry, proving that migrant culture enriches Britain. The author urges us to listen to migrants’ voices instead of relying on fear‑based politics, reminding us that new ideas grow when people come together.
