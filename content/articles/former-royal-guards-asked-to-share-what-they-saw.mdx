Police officers who protected Prince Andrew are being asked to tell detectives what they saw while on duty. The request follows new evidence linking former police guards to Jeffrey Epstein, who was believed to use UK airports for human trafficking. The Met is reviewing records from UK airports and looking at whether Andrew’s visits might have hidden sexual abuse. Some former guards claim they were told to stay quiet, raising questions about how the investigation is handled.
