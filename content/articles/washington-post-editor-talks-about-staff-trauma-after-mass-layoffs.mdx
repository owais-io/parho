Washington Post editor Matt Murray said that the recent layoffs, which cut almost a third of staff, have left employees feeling deeply upset. He explained that the cuts were based on readership data and targeted sections like sports and international news. Although the company’s leaders say the mission remains unchanged, workers are skeptical and concerned about mistakes and the loss of leadership. Murray expressed confidence that the Post can recover.
