A Scottish auctioneer is selling 18th‑century neck shackles used in the East African slave trade. He says the items are historical artefacts meant to confront people with the past. Critics argue the sale profits from slavery and call the event vile, urging the shackles be donated to a museum. Although no law stops the sale, the debate over ethics and restitution is heating up.
