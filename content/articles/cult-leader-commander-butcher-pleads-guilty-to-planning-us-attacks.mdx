A 23‑year‑old Georgian man named Michail Chkhikvishvili, who called himself “Commander Butcher,” was found guilty of planning bombings and school shootings in the United States. He promised to help others make poison, set fires, and even had a plan for Santa‑claus‑like figures to hand out toxic candy. In court he apologized, said he was working on his life, and faces up to 18 years in prison.
