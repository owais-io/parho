The Trump administration has warned it will stop sending SNAP money to states that refuse to hand over food‑stamp data. Agriculture Secretary Brooke Rollins says the data is needed to fight fraud, but 21 Democratic states, including California, New York and Minnesota, have said no. Democrats call the move a privacy violation and a political attack, noting that SNAP fraud rates are actually low.
