ITV has set up a New York studio to cover the World Cup live, while the BBC stays in Salford, England. ITV’s team, hosted by Mark Pougatch and Laura Woods, will film in Brooklyn and show 51 games, including England’s matches. The BBC, aiming to cut travel and save money, will broadcast 54 games from Salford. ITV will bring pundits like Gary Neville and Roy Keane to the US. Historically, the BBC has higher ratings at key matches.
