Joseph Tirrell, the top ethics lawyer at the Department of Justice, was abruptly fired in July, the agency gave no reason. Tirrell had just taught Attorney General Pam Bondi about the department’s ethics rules. His dismissal comes amid a trend of political firings of career federal workers. Tirrell is suing, arguing the cuts are a warning to non‑political staff that political pressure is growing. He also handled gifts from high‑profile donors that exceeded limits.
