Mayors around the world are stepping up to tackle big city problems, from housing and climate to politics. In Budapest, a mayor defied a government ban on Pride, while Barcelona’s leader plans to shut down tourist flats. Paris’s mayor is reshaping the Seine for pedestrians. These leaders are forming alliances, getting new training, and facing pressure from far‑right governments, showing cities now lead on global challenges today.
