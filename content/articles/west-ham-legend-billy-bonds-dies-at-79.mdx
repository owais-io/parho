Billy Bonds, who played for West Ham for 21 years and made 799 appearances, died on Sunday at 79. He was a key player and captain, winning the FA Cup twice and leading the club for a decade. After retiring as a player, he managed West Ham, helping them win promotion and reach a cup final. Bonds is remembered for his strength, leadership, and loyalty to the club.
