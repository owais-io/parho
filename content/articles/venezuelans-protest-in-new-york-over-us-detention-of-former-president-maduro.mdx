In cold New York, Venezuelan immigrants gathered outside the federal courthouse to cheer on the U.S. holding former President Nicolás Maduro on drug‑trafficking charges. Some celebrated his capture, while others warned that U.S. intervention could harm Venezuela’s future. Protesters shared stories of family struggles, safety worries, and economic hardship back home, reminding observers that true freedom still feels distant for many Venezuelans.
