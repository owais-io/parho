Three men who the U.S. deported to Eswatini, instead of to their home countries, have filed a complaint with the African Union’s human‑rights commission. Two are still in prison, while the third was sent back to Jamaica. Their lawyers say the men already served U.S. sentences and are being held without legal visits, one even went on a hunger strike. Eswatini says the men are safely housed while repatriation is arranged.
