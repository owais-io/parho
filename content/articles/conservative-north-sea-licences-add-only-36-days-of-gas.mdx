Hundreds of new North Sea oil licences issued by the Conservatives since 2010 have only produced enough gas for 36 days of extra supply. Analysts say the 20 fields that could have delivered six months of gas have barely yielded any output so far. Experts argue the basin is almost depleted and extra licences won’t lower bills, which are set by global markets. The Labour government says the focus should be on renewables and home energy upgrades.
