The President announced a federal emergency to help clean up a huge sewage spill in the Potomac River that started after a big sewer pipe broke near Washington, D.C. Millions of gallons of raw sewage have flowed into the river, raising health worries and blocking recreational use. The declaration lets FEMA coordinate relief and work with local officials in D.C., Maryland, and Virginia. Local leaders, including the mayor, have also called for a major disaster declaration.
