Amazon wants all workers to use AI tools, but the software often gives buggy code that employees must fix. Developers, engineers and other staff say the push slows them down and adds extra chores. The company even tracks how often employees use AI and ties it to promotions. Meanwhile, Amazon has cut 30,000 jobs in the past four months and says AI won’t drive those cuts, even though many think otherwise.
