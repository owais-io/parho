Asda's new chair, Allan Leighton, told the government it must step up to help farmers and lower fuel taxes. He said the Middle East war is driving up food and petrol prices, which will hurt shoppers and Asda's profits. The chain saw sales fall and debt grow, but is working on IT fixes and cost cuts. The government says it is already cutting energy bills.
