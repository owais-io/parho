Germany’s leading women’s teams have left the German Football Federation (DFB) to create an independent league, aiming to grow the sport faster and attract more money. The move mirrors England’s Women’s Super League. The new association, headed by Katharina Kiel, will manage the league from 2027‑28. Clubs want more control over sponsorship, salaries and rules, hoping to catch up with England and Spain and boost Germany’s national team prospects.
