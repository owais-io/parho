Satellites have mapped dozens of huge methane leaks that are as hot as coal plants. Most come from oil and gas sites in Turkmenistan, Texas, Venezuela and Iran, and some are at landfills. The gas is easy to capture and sell, but most sites are poorly maintained. Cutting these leaks would quickly slow warming, because methane breaks down faster than CO₂. The study urges governments and companies to fix them fast.
