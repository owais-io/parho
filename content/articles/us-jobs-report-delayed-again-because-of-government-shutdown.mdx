The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics has pushed back the January 2026 jobs report until federal funding comes back on line. The data is ready, but a 43‑day government shutdown tied to a dispute over Homeland Security spending is holding up the release. The report will show job growth for a year that added only 584,000 jobs, the weakest increase since 2020.
