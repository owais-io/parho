For months, an Ohio police officer tried to keep his name secret after he shot and killed a woman in a parking lot. He used Marsy’s Law, a privacy law meant to protect crime victims, to argue he was a victim himself. The Supreme Court eventually said this was wrong. Similar cases in Florida, North Dakota and other states have sparked protests and court fights over police anonymity.
