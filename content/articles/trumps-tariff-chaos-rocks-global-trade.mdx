After Trump announced big Japanese investment deals, the U.S. Supreme Court struck down most of his tariffs, saying they’re illegal. Now almost every country has a 15% tariff on U.S. goods, wiping out earlier deals. Markets wobble, and partners like Japan, the UK, and India are scrambling for clarity. While some exporters feel less hit, consumers and U.S. firms now bear most of the cost.
