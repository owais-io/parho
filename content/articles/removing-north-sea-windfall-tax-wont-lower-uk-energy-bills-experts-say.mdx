Experts say cutting the North Sea windfall tax won’t lower UK energy bills. The tax is on producer profits, not on prices consumers pay, so it can’t be passed on. Companies already earn big profits from high oil prices, so they don’t need tax breaks to invest. Removing the tax would give companies more money, not help households. Experts urge spending on renewables, electric vehicles, heat pumps and insulation instead.
