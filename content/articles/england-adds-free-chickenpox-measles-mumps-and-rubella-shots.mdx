England is adding a free combined vaccine for chickenpox, measles, mumps and rubella (MMRV) to the NHS schedule. Kids will get it at 12 and 18 months. The new shot will cut chickenpox cases, lower hospital visits and save the NHS about £15 million a year. Chickenpox can cause serious problems like pneumonia and meningitis. The vaccine has worked safely in other countries for decades, and experts say it is a key way to protect children.
