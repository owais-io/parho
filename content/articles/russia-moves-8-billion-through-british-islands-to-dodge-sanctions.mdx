Since Russia invaded Ukraine, companies tied to Moscow have used Britain’s overseas territories – the British Virgin Islands, Bermuda, the Cayman Islands and Gibraltar – to route about £5.9 billion ($8 billion) of goods, from luxury yachts to drilling equipment. Most of the trade happened after 2022 sanctions were imposed, but continued through 2025, raising questions about how well the UK’s sanctions are enforced.
