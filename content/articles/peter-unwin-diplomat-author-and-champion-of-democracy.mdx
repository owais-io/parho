Peter Unwin, who died at 93, was a British diplomat who served in places like Budapest, Tokyo, New York, Bonn, and Copenhagen. He helped open Hungary to the West and wrote books about European politics. Unwin was a strong supporter of democracy, international law and was critical of Brexit. He married Monica Steven, a refugee family’s daughter, and they raised children together. He left a legacy of fair, thoughtful commentary on world affairs.
