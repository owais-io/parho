Britain’s gatekeeper butterflies are dropping 38% since 1976, and scientists say mowing, chemicals and lost grass patches are to blame. The fix? Leave a strip of long grass, especially by hedges, and plant nectar‑rich flowers such as oregano, thyme or buddleia. Use free apps to count sightings and push for long grass in parks. Small changes can keep these “gentle” butterflies alive.
