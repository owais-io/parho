Nine left‑wing protesters in Texas were found guilty of providing material support to terrorism, along with charges of rioting, attempted murder, and weapons offenses. The case, which linked the defendants to an alleged antifa cell, centered on a July 4 protest outside a Fort Worth ICE detention center where some participants vandalized property and attacked police. Convictions range from 10 to life in prison, sparking debate over the use of anti‑terrorism laws against political protesters.
