At a two‑day summit in Nara, Japan, President Lee Jae‑Myung and Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi spent the final hour playing a simple drum set, a light‑hearted way to show cooperation. Their jamming came after Lee’s trip to Beijing, where he had met Chinese President Xi. Beyond the music, the leaders signed agreements on historical justice, crime‑fighting, economic security, and North Korea. They also toured the ancient Horyu‑ji temple together. The drum session was a symbolic step toward better ties.
