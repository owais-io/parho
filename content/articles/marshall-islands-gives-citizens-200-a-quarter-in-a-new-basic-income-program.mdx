The Marshall Islands has launched the world’s first national basic‑income plan, paying every citizen about $200 each quarter. People can receive the money by bank transfer, cheque or a digital crypto wallet. Most choose banks, with only a handful using crypto. The program, funded by a U.S. trust for past nuclear testing, aims to ease living costs and test new tech in a small island nation.
