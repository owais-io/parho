New York is facing its coldest winter in decades, and the city’s 800 homeless residents are at risk. Seventeen people have died, most from hypothermia, as the mayor declares a “Code Blue” emergency that opens shelters and sends outreach teams out. Volunteers hand out blankets, food, and try to build trust, while city uses charter buses to keep people warm. The debate rages over whether to keep or tear down encampments, but the mayor keeps asking residents to call for help.
