The Bank of England is cutting jobs after a critical review by former Fed chair Ben Bernanke that said it failed to predict rising inflation. It launched a voluntary exit scheme that runs until January, with staff who choose to leave in March. The move is part of a multi‑year overhaul of its forecasting tools and operations to be more efficient and resilient. The bank also plans to cut interest rates soon.
