After US and Israel bombed Iran, Gulf countries have been hit by thousands of Iranian drones and missiles that attacked airports, hotels, oil refineries and gas plants. Their air defences have shot down about 90% of the strikes, but attacks on gas facilities in Qatar, UAE, Kuwait and Saudi Arabia have still hit the region’s energy output. Gulf leaders are weighing whether to stay defensive or to retaliate, while the US pushes them to join a larger war.
