Former BBC deputy director Mark Damazer says the broadcaster's top bosses quit because of a toxic culture of mistrust and over‑assertiveness. He says some board members put politics ahead of the BBC’s job, creating a hostile atmosphere that led to the resignations of Director General Tim Davie and News chief Deborah Turness. Damazer also criticises the board’s handling of bias claims and says it should improve its self‑reflection. The BBC’s chair is under pressure to fix the situation.
