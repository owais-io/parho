Two U.S. juries just handed Meta—owner of Facebook and Instagram—big legal pain. In New Mexico, the company was fined $375 million for letting child abuse happen on its sites and for lying about safety. A day later, California jurors awarded $6 million to a young woman who said Meta’s design hooked her from childhood and caused deep harm. These wins could open the door to huge payouts and force the tech giant to change its addictive systems.
