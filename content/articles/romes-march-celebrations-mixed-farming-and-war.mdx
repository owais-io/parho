Rome’s month of March is named after the god Mars, who ruled both war and farming. Every spring, Romans held ceremonies to bless crops and also reviewed and trained troops, ready for campaigns once the weather cleared. The dual focus shows how the empire balanced agriculture with the need for soldiers, timing battles with the seasons for efficiency. This practice highlighted Rome’s efficient bureaucracy and the belief that orderly planning, not sudden rage, led to victory.
