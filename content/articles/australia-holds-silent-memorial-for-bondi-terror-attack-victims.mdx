On January 22, Australians gathered in silence at the Sydney Opera House to honour the 15 people who were shot dead in the Bondi beach terror attack. 15 candles were lit, survivors sang, and leaders spoke of unity and healing. The prime minister apologized and said the attack was an attack on all Australians. Police and helicopters stayed on alert, but the event showed the nation coming together to remember and support each other.
