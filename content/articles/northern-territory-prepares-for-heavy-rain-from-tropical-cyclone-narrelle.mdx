The Northern Territory is ready for Tropical Cyclone Narrelle, which will bring up to 300 mm of rain to an already water‑logged region. After months of flooding, many towns have been evacuated, and local leaders say the government’s help is slow and uneven. Residents in remote Aboriginal communities have faced extra hardships, while the state keeps spending on projects like fracking. Officials say the area needs better emergency planning as climate‑related storms rise.
