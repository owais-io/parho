Scott Davies, who runs Slough Town and once played in the Football League, fell into a gambling addiction that wrecked his career and almost his life. Now a coach and school lecturer, he shares his story to protect young footballers from the same trap. He tells players and students that the sport’s competitive nature and easy access to betting make them “perfect customers” for bookmakers. His team, low‑ranked yet in the FA Cup, benefits from a supportive, honest club culture.
