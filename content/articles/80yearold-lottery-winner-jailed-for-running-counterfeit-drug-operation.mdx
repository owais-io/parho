An 80‑year‑old man from Wigan, who won £2.4 million in the lottery in 2010, helped run a huge counterfeit drug business that produced fake Valium tablets. He was found guilty of conspiracy to make and supply drugs, weapons possession and tampering with justice, and was jailed for 16½ years. The court said the operation was the biggest ever uncovered, centred on his quiet rural home and run with his son and others.
