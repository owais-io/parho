Young people buying second‑hand clothes on sites like Vinted and Depop are giving UK charity shops a lift. Sales at Save the Children rose 3% last year, and big charities such as the British Heart Foundation saw their shop sales up. But rising rent, wages and utility costs squeeze profits, and the number of charity shops fell almost 80% last year. Still, more space, online sales and young volunteers are keeping the sector alive.
