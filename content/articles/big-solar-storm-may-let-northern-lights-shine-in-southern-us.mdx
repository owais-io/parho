On Monday night, a powerful solar storm could let the northern lights be seen as far south as Kansas, Colorado, Texas, and even further. NOAA’s space‑weather team warned that the geomagnetic disturbance is the strongest in over 20 years and will last into Tuesday. While the auroras would be a spectacular sight, the same storm may jostle satellites, GPS and other tech.
