In the quiet Lady Chapel at St Albans, a 19th‑century restoration replaced missing medieval stone carvings. Sculptor John Baker asked local women to bring plant samples, so the new capitals, bosses and corbels show real flowers, ferns, berries and even orchids from Hertfordshire orchards. The carvings celebrate the city’s plants and the women who collected them, making the chapel a living record of local nature.
