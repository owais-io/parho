If the Strait of Hormuz stays closed, the U.S. could face a huge supply crisis for sulfur, copper and other minerals that power everything from jets to drones. Prices have jumped 165 % and the U.S. military might have to pay twice as much to replace damaged radar, communication gear and batteries. The problem is worsened by secretive supply chains that hide where the materials come from.
