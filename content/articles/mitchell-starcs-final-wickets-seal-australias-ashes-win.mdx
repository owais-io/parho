Australia won the Ashes thanks to Mitchell Starc, who took the last two English wickets and reached 31 wickets in the series with a 19‑run average. The left‑hander has been a key bowler for eight years, often leading the attack when other fast bowlers are absent. Starc also chipped in with useful runs, and his accuracy and speed made him a decisive factor in each of the five Tests. His performance could make him the series' best bowler.
