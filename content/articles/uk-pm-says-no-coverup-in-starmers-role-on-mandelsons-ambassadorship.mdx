Downing Street says the prime minister didn’t hide his input in appointing Peter Mandelson as UK ambassador to Washington. A 147‑page file shows the PM’s section was left blank, meaning no formal comment. Starmer has apologised for the choice, which led to Mandelson’s dismissal after ties to Jeffrey Epstein surfaced. The government says normal vetting was followed, but the case highlights the need for clearer appointment procedures.
