Miami chose a new direction when Democrat Eileen Higgins won the mayor’s office with about 60% of the vote. She beat the Trump‑backed Republican Emilio González in a run‑off that snapped 28 years of GOP control. Higgins is the city’s first woman, first non‑Hispanic mayor since the 1990s, and her victory reflects voters’ anger over immigration policies. The win signals a shift that could affect Florida’s politics before the next mid‑term elections.
