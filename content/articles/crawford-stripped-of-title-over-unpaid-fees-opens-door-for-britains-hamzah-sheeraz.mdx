The World Boxing Council removed American boxer Terence Crawford from his super‑middleweight world title because he didn’t pay the mandatory fees. With the belt vacant, the WBC has set up a fight between Britain’s Hamzah Sheeraz and interim champion Christian Mbilli to decide the new champion. Crawford still holds three other titles and might drop to middleweight next. The decision highlights the cost and politics that can keep champions out of the ring.
