Microsoft announced its second‑quarter results, beating analysts with $81.3 billion in revenue and $4.14 earnings per share. Its Azure cloud unit grew 39% and powers much of the company’s AI services, showing strong demand. Shares fell 4 % after the news, but the results confirm that Microsoft’s AI business is already larger than some older products. Investors remain wary of the huge AI spending, yet the company keeps outperforming forecasts.
