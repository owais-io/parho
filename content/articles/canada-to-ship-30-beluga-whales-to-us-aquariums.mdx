Canada plans to move 30 beluga whales and four dolphins from Marineland to U.S. aquariums. The plan follows a federal deal that will ship the animals in transport boxes on trucks and planes. Before departure, each whale gets a health check and a dose of Valium to calm it. Trainers use fabric slings and cranes to lift the whales into steel containers. The goal is to end the last captive belugas in Canada.
