The UK government has pulled a proposed amendment to the Hillsborough law that would let security services decide whether to share information. Campaigners and MPs argued the change would weaken accountability after the 1989 stadium disaster. The bill will still go to the Commons for its next stages, and the government says it will work with families and supporters to keep the law strong while protecting national security.
