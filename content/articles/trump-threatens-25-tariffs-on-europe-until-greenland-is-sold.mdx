Donald Trump has announced that he will raise tariffs on a number of European countries—Denmark, Germany, France, the UK, Norway, Sweden, Finland, and the Netherlands—to 25% if the United States doesn’t buy Greenland. He’s already warned of a 10% tariff starting February. European leaders, the EU, and NATO members have strongly rejected the threat, saying the move would damage ties and hurt their economies. Trump’s tariff push is part of a wider strategy to force deals with allies.
