A federal court is hearing a dispute between Coles and the Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC) over the supermarket’s ‘Down Down’ sale. Coles argues that the price drops are real and that shoppers would see them as honest discounts, while the ACCC says the store inflated prices before the promotion to trick buyers. The case could bring big fines and will also affect a similar Woolworths lawsuit. The hearing runs for ten days.
