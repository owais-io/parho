In Orlando, volunteers planted 120 fruit trees with the Arbor Day Foundation and local groups to help people in a food desert get fresh food without high grocery prices. The trees, which grow citrus, mango, banana, avocado, and pecans, will produce fruit for community members and teach kids where food comes from. The project shows how trees can give food, shade, and hope to low‑income neighborhoods.
