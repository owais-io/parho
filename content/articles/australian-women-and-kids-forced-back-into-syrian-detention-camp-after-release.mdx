34 Australian women and children, who are wives and kids of former IS fighters, were supposed to leave Syria for home but were turned back into a Kurdish‑run camp. They had no travel papers and the Australian government says it won’t bring them home. The camp suffers from disease, fires and lack of food, and human‑rights groups say keeping them there is illegal. International pressure is mounting for their safe return.
