The Charity Commission has started a formal investigation after the Guardian revealed that City & Guilds sold its training arm to PeopleCert and handed out huge bonuses to top bosses. The chief executive got a £1.7 m award and a salary rise, while the finance director received £1.2 m and a pay bump. The inquiry will check how the trustees decided on the sale, the bonuses and any other regulatory concerns. City & Guilds is also cutting costs and staff.
