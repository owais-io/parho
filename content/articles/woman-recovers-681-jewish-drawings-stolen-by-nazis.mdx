Woman and her mother fought for 681 drawings, letters and poems created by Jewish artist Peter Kien during the Theresienstadt ghetto. After WWII, the suitcase was seized by communists and kept in a Czech museum for decades. Using her mother's legal papers and help from officials, the items finally flew from Prague to London in 2023. They now belong to the Wiener Holocaust Library, honoring the artist’s legacy and the mother’s promise to return the lost works.
