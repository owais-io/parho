The UK has signed a deal that will make it pay 25% more for new medicines from the United States. Health experts say this could set a bad example for other countries, including Australia, which might feel pressure to raise its own drug prices. Australian Health Minister Mark Butler said the PBS will keep offering affordable medicine and that Australia will not compromise that. He is still talking to the U.S. and global drug companies to understand the change.
