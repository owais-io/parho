Scientists in Italy found a new, bigger, darker wall lizard that could outcompete three long‑existing color types. The common wall lizard has been a model of intraspecies diversity for millions of years, but the aggressive new morph may push the others toward extinction. Researchers stress the fragility of genetic diversity inside a species and the need to protect variations in other lizards, like the endangered Aeolian wall lizard.
