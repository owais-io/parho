Pacific Grove, California—nicknamed “Butterfly Town”—used to see tens of thousands of monarch butterflies each winter, but recent counts have dropped to just a few hundred. Scientists warn that western monarchs could be extinct by 2080 because of pesticide use, habitat loss and climate change. Volunteers now count the butterflies, share tips to cut chemicals, and plant native flowers to give them a safe place to rest.
