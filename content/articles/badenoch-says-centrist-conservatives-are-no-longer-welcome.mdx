Kemi Badenoch, the new Conservative leader, told MPs that the party is moving further right and that centrist voices like Andy Street and Ruth Davidson are no longer needed. She criticised politicians who left for Reform UK and said they should “get out of the way.” Her speech focused on cutting immigration, stopping net‑zero goals, and reducing taxes and welfare. The move may alienate moderate voters and hurt the party’s chances in upcoming elections.
