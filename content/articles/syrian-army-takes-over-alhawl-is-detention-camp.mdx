After Kurdish fighters withdrew, the Syrian army entered the al‑Hawl camp, which holds about 24,000 people linked to ISIS. Many are relatives of suspected militants. The camp is overcrowded and critics say conditions are cruel. If prisoners escape, ISIS could regroup. The US has started sending some detainees to Iraq for better security. The SDF still runs other camps, and talks continue about handing them over to Damascus.
