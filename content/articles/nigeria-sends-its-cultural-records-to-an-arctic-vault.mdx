Nigeria has placed more than a million cultural records in the Arctic World Archive, a secure vault under a mountain in Svalbard, Norway. The archive keeps data on special film that can last 2,000 years, protecting Nigerian books, films, music and history that are at risk in poorly funded libraries. The move follows a global effort to keep human knowledge safe in harsh, dry conditions, and marks the first time an African country uses the facility.
