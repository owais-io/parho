The brother of Israel’s Shin Bet chief has been charged with helping the enemy by smuggling cigarettes and other goods into Gaza. Prosecutors say he moved about 14 cartons of cigarettes, worth about $120,000, into the blockaded strip over three trips. He allegedly used the cover of the right‑wing Uriah Force, a reserve unit that worked outside the army. His lawyers deny the charges, calling them a “cigarette‑only” case. The case highlights how smuggling fuels Gaza’s black market.
