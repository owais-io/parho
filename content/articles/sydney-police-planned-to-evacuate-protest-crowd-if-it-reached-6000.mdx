Police in Sydney had a hidden plan to break up a protest against Israeli President Isaac Herzog if the crowd grew to more than 6,000 people. Inside messages show officials were watching numbers and ready to use containment lines and pepper spray. The protest turned violent, leading to arrests and police‑watchdog investigations. The police claim they acted because demonstrators tried to march, while organisers say the plan was unfair and the crowd was largely peaceful.
