Angela Rayner told a Labour supporters’ event that the party is in danger of falling apart if it keeps playing it safe. She slammed the government’s plan to raise the time needed for permanent residency and called the move “un‑British”. Rayner urged the party to show real change, not just go through the motions, and some colleagues have hinted she might run against Keir Starmer. Her comments fuel a growing leadership debate.
