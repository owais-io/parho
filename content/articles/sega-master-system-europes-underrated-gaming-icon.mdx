In the 1980s, Nintendo’s NES dominated the US and Japan, but in Europe the Sega Master System stole the spotlight. Built on a fast Z80 chip, it offered more colours and sprites than the NES and shipped popular arcade titles like OutRun and Golden Axe. European marketers ran quirky road‑shows, targeting teens and making the Master System a local favourite that outshone Nintendo in many markets. Its classic games still sell cheaply for collectors today.
