A Georgia carpenter was arrested for allegedly burning police motorcycles during protests against Cop City, a new police training centre in Atlanta. After spending time in jail, under house arrest, and wearing an ankle monitor, he pleaded to a reduced charge of property damage. A judge then gave him ten years of probation. The case highlights the intense surveillance and legal pressure used to curb protest activity.
