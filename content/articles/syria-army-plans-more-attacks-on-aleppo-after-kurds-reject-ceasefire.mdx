The Syrian army said it would start new attacks on the Kurdish‑controlled Sheikh Maqsoud area of Aleppo after Kurdish groups turned down a ceasefire that would force them to leave. A brief humanitarian corridor was set up, but fighting has been intense, killing about 21 people and forcing many to flee. Both sides blame each other, and a stalled deal to merge the Kurdish SDF into the national army keeps tensions high.
