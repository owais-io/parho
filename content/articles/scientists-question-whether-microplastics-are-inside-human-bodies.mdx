Recent reports that tiny plastic particles are found in brains, blood, and other organs are now under scrutiny. Researchers say the studies may have been contaminated or misread, leading to false positives. Because the methods are still being refined, the real amount of plastic in us is unclear, and the health risks are unproven. Scientists warn that shaky evidence could mislead policy and create unnecessary fear, so better testing is needed.
