Common Wealth think‑tank says that if the UK stops letting gas prices set the cost of electricity, households could save up to £200 a year. The report calls for a “single buyer” model that would pay renewable generators a fixed price and keep gas in a strategic reserve for emergencies. This change would stop volatile gas costs from inflating bills and keep renewable profits fair.
