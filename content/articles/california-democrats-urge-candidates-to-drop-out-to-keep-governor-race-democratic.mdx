California Democrats are worried that their nine hopefuls for governor could split the vote and let two Republicans win the November election. Party leaders are urging candidates who lack a clear path to the general election to drop out. Top contenders include Katie Porter, Tom Steyer, Eric Swalwell and new entrant Matt Mahan. The party fears a Republican win would hurt turnout and threaten blue seats in Congress.
