In South Africa, a young boy’s brother has cystic fibrosis, a disease that once killed people early. The usual treatment, sold by Vertex for $370,000 a year, now has a generic version that could cost only about $2,000. Parents worldwide have pushed for this lower price, forming a buyers’ club and working with companies in countries like Bangladesh and Argentina. The cheaper drug lets families hope for better health and a normal life for their children.
