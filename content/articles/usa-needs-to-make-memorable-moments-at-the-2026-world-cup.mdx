The 2026 World Cup will have 48 teams, giving the US men’s national team more games and chances to create unforgettable moments. Coach Mauricio Pochettino says the goal isn’t to win the cup but to leave a legacy that sticks in a nation full of instant sports. The U.S. faces Australia, Paraguay and possibly Turkey in Group D and must push as far as possible. Players like Pulisic and Adams want to set new benchmarks to inspire the next generation.
