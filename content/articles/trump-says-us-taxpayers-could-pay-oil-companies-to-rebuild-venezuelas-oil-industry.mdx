Trump said the U.S. could have taxpayers cover the costs of oil firms fixing Venezuela’s damaged oil plants after the regime change. He promised a big payout once the companies invest. The White House plans meetings with Chevron, Exxon, and Conoco at a Miami energy conference to discuss how to boost production. However, the companies haven’t yet talked to the administration, and analysts warn that Venezuela’s future and needed investment are uncertain.
