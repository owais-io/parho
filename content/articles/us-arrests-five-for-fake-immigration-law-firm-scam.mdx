Five people in New Jersey were charged for posing as immigration lawyers and running a fake law firm. They used Facebook to find clients, charged hundreds or thousands of dollars, and staged fake court hearings with bogus judge papers. The scheme made over $100,000 and sent money to a Colombian accomplice. One victim missed a real court date and was deported in absentia. Authorities say the fraud was purely greed‑driven and harmed vulnerable people.
