After the US captured Venezuelan President Maduro, Cuba is worried because it relies on Venezuelan oil for electricity. President Miguel Díaz‑Canel slammed the raid as terrorism and said Cuba would stand by Venezuela. Blackouts and fuel shortages are already hurting everyday life. Some Cubans wonder if the US could next target them, but most have no strong opposition. The move shows how closely Cuba and Venezuela are tied.
