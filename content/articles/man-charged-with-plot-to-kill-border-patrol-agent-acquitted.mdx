Juan Espinoza Martinez, a 37‑year‑old man in Chicago, was acquitted after prosecutors claimed he offered a $10,000 bounty over Snapchat to kill border‑patrol agent Gregory Bovino. A jury found the evidence insufficient, agreeing the messages were a joke. The verdict, reached in less than four hours, is part of a trend of immigration‑enforcement cases falling apart in court, putting federal prosecutors in a difficult spot.
