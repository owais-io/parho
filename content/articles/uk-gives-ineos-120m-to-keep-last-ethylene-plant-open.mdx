The UK government has agreed to give Ineos £120m to keep the country’s only ethylene plant in Grangemouth running. The money will protect over 500 jobs and keep the plant’s supply of chemicals – used for medical plastics, water treatment and the auto industry – flowing. The move comes after a recent plant closure that hurt jobs, and it’s also a political statement ahead of upcoming elections.
