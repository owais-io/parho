José Jerí, Peru’s interim president, was removed after a scandal called “Chifagate” revealed secret meetings with Chinese businessmen. Lawmakers voted 75‑24 to impeach him. The move comes as Peru faces political chaos, with upcoming elections and investigations into alleged influence‑peddling and unqualified appointments. Jerí denied wrongdoing, saying the hires were legal. Critics want to distance themselves before the April presidential vote. The drama also ties into U.S.‑China tensions over a Chinese‑owned port in Peru.
