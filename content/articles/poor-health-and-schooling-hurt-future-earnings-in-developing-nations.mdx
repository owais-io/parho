World Bank says poor health, schooling and training in many developing countries are getting worse. The report finds 86 of 129 low‑ and middle‑income nations have seen declines from 2010 to 2025. If they matched the best performers, children born now could earn 51% more in their lifetimes. Problems include child malnutrition, crime, low workplace training and many women out of work. The bank recommends better home care, clean water, apprenticeships and learning in small firms.
