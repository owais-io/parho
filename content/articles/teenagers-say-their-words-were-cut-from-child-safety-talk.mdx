Teen voices about social media addiction were cut from a child safety event backed by tech giants, sparking accusations that the charity censored its own speakers. Two teens, 17 and 14, were asked to speak at Safer Internet Day, but their warnings about addiction, isolation and the influence of TikTok and Snapchat were edited out. The charity said the cuts were for tone and time limits, not funder influence.
