During President Trump’s State of the Union, a woman named Aliya Rahman—who was invited by Congresswoman Ilhan Omar to protest immigration policies—was arrested by Capitol police for standing in the gallery. Rahman had earlier been injured when ICE agents pulled her from a car in Minneapolis. The police said she refused to sit when ordered, but Omar says she was treated violently and wants an investigation. The incident raised questions about crowd‑control and First‑Amendment rights.
