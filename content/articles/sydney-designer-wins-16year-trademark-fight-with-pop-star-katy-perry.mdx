A Sydney fashion designer, Katie Taylor, who runs a label called Katie Perry, won a 16‑year trademark battle against pop star Katy Perry. Australia’s High Court said the designer’s use of the name for clothes didn’t conflict with the singer’s rights because the singer’s trademark only covers music and merchandise, not clothing. The judge ruled the singer’s earlier claims were wrong and that the designer can keep her trademark. It shows even small businesses can fight big stars.
