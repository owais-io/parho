Retailers report 5.5 million shop‑lifting incidents last year, costing the industry about £400m. Gangs target stores systematically, and shop workers face an average of 36 weapon‑related assaults a day. The British Retail Consortium urges police to boost resources and prioritise retail crime, while the government introduces new laws to increase penalties for assaulting workers and lower the theft threshold. Despite some progress, violence and theft remain high.
