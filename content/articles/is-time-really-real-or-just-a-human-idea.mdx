Time feels like a ticking clock we chase, but scientists say it’s just a math tool for ordering events, not a physical flow. The article explains how “clock time” can make people feel short, causing stress. It also shows how our brain’s sense of time changes with mood, drugs, or rare conditions. Indigenous cultures have no word for time, focusing on lived experience. By noticing moments instead of numbers, we can ease time‑famine and feel more in control.
