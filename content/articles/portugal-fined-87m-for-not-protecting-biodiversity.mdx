Portugal has been hit with a €10 million fine (about £8.7 million) by the EU court for not protecting 55 key wildlife sites. The court also wants Portugal to pay a daily penalty of €41,250 until it fixes the problem. The fine is meant to push Portugal to meet EU rules that protect habitats and species. Portugal says it has already made progress and only a few areas remain.
