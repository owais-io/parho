HS2 contractor Mace says the government’s plan to double tariffs on imported steel will make the high‑speed rail project and other UK construction projects more expensive. The move is meant to protect domestic steel makers, but it will raise steel prices at a time when energy costs are already up. Mace and other builders say the tariffs could push the £100bn HS2 budget even higher and delay the line’s opening. The government may review the policy after a year.
