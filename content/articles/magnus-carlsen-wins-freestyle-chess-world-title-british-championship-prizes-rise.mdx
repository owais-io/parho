Norwegian grandmaster Magnus Carlsen defended his world title in a new freestyle chess tournament, where the back‑row pieces start in random positions to level the playing field. He beat rivals in a tight final and won a $100,000 prize. Meanwhile, the English Chess Federation announced a record prize fund for the 2026 British championships, raising first‑prize money to over £34,000 and boosting support for men, women and junior events.
