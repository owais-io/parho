After a long career helping people in Kingston upon Thames, Giles Darvill spent decades organising volunteers across the UK. He later ran his own consultancy, advising social‑service agencies, and taught at the National Institute for Social Work until 2004. In his spare time he loved making stained‑glass windows, writing poetry, and gardening a wildlife garden in Milford on Sea. He left a legacy of community support and artistic creativity.
