Five hikers died in a Chilean storm that blew 190 km/h and dropped temperatures to –5 °C. Australian hikers were caught on a difficult pass, but no park rangers were present because of a voting day. Poor communication let the trail stay open. Survivors had to rescue each other and later call for stronger safety rules and better emergency help more quickly.
