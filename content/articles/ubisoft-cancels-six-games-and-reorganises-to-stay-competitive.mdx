Ubisoft has shut down six game projects, including a remake of Prince of Persia, and will delay another seven. The company is also closing its Halifax and Stockholm studios and will split its operations into five creative divisions to cut costs. The moves caused shares to tumble to a decade‑low, wiping out billions in value. Ubisoft says the changes are needed to survive a tough, high‑budget AAA market dominated by shooters and online multiplayer.
