A recent survey shows that one in four rural doctors feel their single-handed practice is hurting their well‑being, and 71 % think the government doesn’t understand rural health. Doctors complain about centralised services, cuts, long distances to hospitals and a lack of policies that fit their isolation. Even though the job remains rewarding, the article argues that recruiting more doctors alone won’t fix the problem.
