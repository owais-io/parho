A letter criticises Parliament's proposal to drop jury trials in the Courts and Tribunals Bill, arguing the real problem is long delays caused by poor court management and staff shortages. The writer lists issues such as overbooked hearings, missing interpreters, prisoners not being produced, closed rooms and a lack of judges. He says adding staff, better planning and more judges would solve these delays, while keeping juries preserves fairness and guards against political influence.
