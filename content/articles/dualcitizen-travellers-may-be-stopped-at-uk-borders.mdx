From 25 February, the UK Home Office will check all passengers for the right to enter, even if they are British citizens by birth or descent. Airlines, ferries and trains must verify a valid British passport or a foreign passport that includes a certificate of entitlement. If neither is present, the carrier may refuse boarding. Dual nationals are encouraged to share their experiences and help the government improve the rule.
