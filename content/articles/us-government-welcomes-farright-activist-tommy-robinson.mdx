US government officials hosted Tommy Robinson, a controversial UK far‑right activist, at the State Department in Washington. The visit, led by senior adviser Joe Rittenhouse, was announced on social media but left questions about who he met and what was discussed. Robinson, convicted of assault and fraud, has long promoted anti‑immigrant and Islamophobic rhetoric. The trip sparked criticism from UK politicians, who warned that it legitimised extremist views.
