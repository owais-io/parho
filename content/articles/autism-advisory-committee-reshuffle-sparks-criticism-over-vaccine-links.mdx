Robert F. Kennedy Jr. replaced the U.S. autism advisory committee’s members, cutting autistic voices and adding anti‑vaccine advocates. The first public meeting was cancelled, raising concerns about the committee’s focus. A new independent group, also called the autism coordinating committee, was created but has only one autistic member and similar controversies. Advocates fear the new bodies could fund research linking vaccines to autism and push unscientific ideas like a “profound autism” label.
