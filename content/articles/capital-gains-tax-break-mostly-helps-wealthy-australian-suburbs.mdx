A new study shows that the 50 % capital gains tax discount in Australia mostly goes to a handful of rich city suburbs, especially in Sydney and Melbourne. In the wealthy Wentworth electorate alone, people get an average $13,450 break, while poorer areas like Blaxland receive only $333. The Australian Council of Social Services says the tax loophole widens inequality and wants the discount cut. Politicians are debating whether to reduce the break as part of tax reform.
