Professors in the humanities are worried that AI tools like ChatGPT replace deep thinking. They are using offline activities, like memorising poems and museum visits, to keep students engaged. Many say AI makes grading hard and turns essays into machine‑made work. Some universities ban AI in the classroom, while others let students use it for research but insist on live discussion. The debate shows AI could change what a university education is.
