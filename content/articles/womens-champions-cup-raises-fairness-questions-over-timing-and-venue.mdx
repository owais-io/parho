The new FIFA Women’s Champions Cup has sparked complaints because it was held in London and the final at Arsenal’s Emirates Stadium, giving the English club a home‑field edge. The schedule also clashes with other leagues, so teams like Gotham FC (NWSL) and Corinthians face travel and training problems. FIFA said it chose London for exposure and facilities, but clubs say they’re paying extra costs and missing recovery support. The tournament’s fairness and cost remain hot topics.
