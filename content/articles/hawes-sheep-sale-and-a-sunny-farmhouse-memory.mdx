The author drives to the Hawes auction for pregnant ewes, taking a different route that leads to a farmhouse lit by a single sun ray. The farmhouse ties to family history and local legend of Pendragon Castle. At the auction, a champion Herdwick ewe sells for £2,200 and a Bluefaced Leicester lamb tops the day at £22,000, boosting local farmers.
