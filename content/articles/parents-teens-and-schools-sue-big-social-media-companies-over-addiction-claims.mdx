A huge group of parents, teens and school districts has taken the world’s biggest social media companies—Meta, Snap, TikTok and YouTube—into court in Los Angeles. They say the platforms are built to be addictive and that this harms kids, leading to depression, eating problems and self‑harm. The trial could force the companies to change how their apps work and could open the door to many more lawsuits.
