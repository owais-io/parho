Australia’s daylight saving ends on Sunday, April 5. On that morning at 3 a.m., clocks in New South Wales, Victoria, South Australia, Tasmania and the Australian Capital Territory will fall back one hour to 2 a.m., giving back an hour of evening light. People with clocks that don’t auto‑adjust will need to set them manually. Workers may find they work an extra hour unless their contracts say otherwise. The change will be reversed on October 4.
