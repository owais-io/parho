Researchers used satellite images to spot more than 3,000 mines in nature‑rich areas, making up about 5% of all mining sites worldwide. Countries like China, Brazil, Argentina and Mexico lead the list. The push for green‑tech metals such as nickel and gold is pushing mines into forests, rivers and protected lands. This destroys habitat, floods rivers, and pollutes water, threatening thousands of species. Experts say we need clear limits on mining in the world’s most valuable nature spots.
