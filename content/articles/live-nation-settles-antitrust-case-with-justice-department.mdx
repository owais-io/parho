Live Nation, which runs Ticketmaster, agreed with the U.S. Justice Department to end its antitrust case. The deal creates a $280 million fund for states, forces Ticketmaster to let rival sellers use parts of its site, cuts its service fees to 15 percent, and requires it to give up exclusive contracts at 13 major venues. Some state attorneys general say the settlement still lets Live Nation dominate the market and may reopen the fight.
