Scientists thought the Moon had a very strong magnetic field early on, but a new study shows that belief was wrong. By looking at titanium in Apollo rocks, researchers found that rocks rich in titanium were linked to brief, strong magnetic moments. Apollo’s landing sites happened to collect more of these rocks, creating a sampling bias. Most of the Moon’s early history had a weak magnetic field.
