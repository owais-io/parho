Candy makers have started replacing real cocoa with sugar and oil coatings in their chocolate treats, outrage from a Reese's heir. The move comes after cocoa prices surged to $12,000 a ton amid droughts in West Africa, forcing companies like Hershey to cut costs. Brad Reese, a grandson of the peanut‑butter‑cup inventor, blasted the change and vowed to stop buying Reese's. This shift raises questions about chocolate quality and consumer trust, reflecting wider industry moves to reformulate amid supply.
