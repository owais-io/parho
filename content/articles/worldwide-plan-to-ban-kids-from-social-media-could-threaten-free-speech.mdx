Many countries want to stop kids from using social media, saying it will keep them safe. The rules would force companies to prove users’ ages, creating huge surveillance systems and giving governments new powers to watch and censor. Experts say the bans are not proven to help kids and could shut down small sites, hurt privacy, and limit free expression. The push is part of a wider crackdown on online anonymity.
