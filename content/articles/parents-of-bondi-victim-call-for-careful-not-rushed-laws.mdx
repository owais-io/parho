Matilda Poltavchenko and her husband are urging a slow, careful approach to new laws after her death in the Bondi Beach shooting. They want a footbridge where the gunmen used to stay painted yellow to honor their daughter, and to name a park after her. They argue that rushed laws don’t stop crime and that the bridge should remain as a reminder, not be torn down.
