Seventeen Venezuelan men, sent by the U.S. to El Salvador’s big prison, say they were beaten, sexually assaulted and left without food or clean water. They filed a petition with the Inter‑American Commission on Human Rights asking for justice, reparations and a public apology. The men say the abuse left lasting scars and mental trauma. The case is part of a larger fight against U.S. deportations to countries with poor human‑rights records.
