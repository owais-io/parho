The UK Office for Budget Responsibility now expects unemployment to reach 5.3% this year, up from the 4.9% it forecasted last month. The rise is mainly driven by a sharp jump in jobs lost for young people – 16% of those aged 16‑24 are now unemployed, the highest level in almost a decade. Employers are hiring less, partly because higher minimum wages and tax costs make young workers more expensive. The forecast also warns that a weak job market and higher taxes could slow GDP growth.
