After the October 7 attacks in Gaza, antisemitic killings and hate have spread worldwide. Jews have been murdered on five continents, from the UAE to Egypt, and attacks hit places that once felt safe, such as Manchester’s Yom Kippur service and Sydney’s Hanukkah party. The article links these attacks to growing pro‑Palestinian rhetoric that sometimes encourages violence. It calls for stronger action against hate and warns that Jewish communities can’t stay safe anywhere.
