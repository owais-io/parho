A new study of 375 Sydney homes shows that bigger driveways are taking over front gardens. Between 2018 and 2023, average front yard size fell 46%, and tree cover dropped 62%, while driveways grew 57%. The changes come from knock‑down‑rebuild projects and more cars, with councils not enforcing minimum green space. The loss raises heat, biodiversity, and neighbour‑connection problems. The findings warn that without stronger rules, Sydney’s suburbs could lose their natural character in just a few years.
