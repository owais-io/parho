Britain has flown back 100,000 of its nationals from the Middle East since fighting with Iran started, Yvette Cooper said. That’s one‑third of the 300,000 Brits who were there before hostilities. While many were stuck after airspace shut, the UK is working with Europe, the US and Gulf states to keep the Strait of Hormuz open for shipping. Ministers also warned about rising tensions in Lebanon and pledged more humanitarian aid.
