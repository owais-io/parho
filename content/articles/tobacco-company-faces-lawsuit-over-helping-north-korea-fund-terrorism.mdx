Hundreds of U.S. military members, civilians, and families have sued British American Tobacco (BAT) for damages. The lawsuit says BAT secretly ran a cigarette factory in North Korea from 2001 to 2007, even after U.S. sanctions warned against it. The profits were used to fund weapons that hit U.S. soldiers and civilians in Iraq and Kurdistan. BAT has already paid $629 million in fines for breaking sanctions and bank‑fraud rules.
