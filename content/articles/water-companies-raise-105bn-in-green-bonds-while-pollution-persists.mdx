Water companies in England have issued £10.5 billion in green bonds, yet their record on sewage pollution remains poor. Anglian Water raised the most, followed by Thames Water, which has yet to publish impact reports for its bonds. Investors view green bonds as cheaper borrowing, but critics claim the money isn’t effectively cleaning rivers. The sector faces pressure for real environmental improvement, not just marketing.
