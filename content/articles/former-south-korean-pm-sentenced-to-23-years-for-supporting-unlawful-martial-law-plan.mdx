South Korea’s former prime minister, Han Duck‑soo, was given a 23‑year prison term for helping a failed martial‑law plan that the court calls an insurrection. The judge said Han, who should have stopped the decree, instead helped make a fake cabinet meeting and lied in court. He showed no remorse, and evidence from a phone call and CCTV proved his role. Prosecutors wanted 15 years, but the judge gave a harsher sentence. Han can appeal.
