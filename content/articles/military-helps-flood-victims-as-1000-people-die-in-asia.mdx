Floods in Sri Lanka, Indonesia, Thailand and Malaysia have killed over 1,000 people and left many missing. The governments sent the military, ships and helicopters to help, but blocked roads and collapsed bridges slow rescue. Sri Lanka declared a state of emergency and promised to rebuild, while Indonesia and Thailand are giving compensation and loans to victims. The storms show how climate change is making big floods more common.
