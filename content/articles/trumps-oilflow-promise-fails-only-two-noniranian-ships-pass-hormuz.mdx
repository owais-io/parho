After President Trump announced a $20 billion insurance plan to keep oil moving through the Strait of Hormuz, only two vessels not linked to Iran or Russia actually crossed the narrow waterway. Most traffic still consists of Iranian or Russian‑backed ships, and insurers warn that missile and drone attacks keep tanker crews cautious. Oil prices swing wildly, and G7 leaders are ready to act but haven’t released any strategic reserves yet.
