Researchers at Cornell tested how corporate buzzwords affect workers. They made a fake buzzword generator that spits out phrases like “actualize a renewed level of cradle‑to‑grave credentialing.” 1,000 office workers rated how smart each phrase seemed and chose actions for workplace scenarios. Those who liked the buzzwords scored lower on reasoning and picked worse decisions. The study warns that love for corporate jargon can make leaders less effective, even when it feels motivating.
