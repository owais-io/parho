Oil prices climbed to more than $100 a barrel after a week of fighting between the U.S‑Israel alliance and Iran, hitting a four‑year high before easing to $85 by market close. The rise rattled markets worldwide, but U.S. shares bounced back, with the Dow gaining 230 points and the S&P and Nasdaq up over 0.8% and 1.3%. President Trump said the price hike was a short‑term cost of the conflict, which helped lift the market.
