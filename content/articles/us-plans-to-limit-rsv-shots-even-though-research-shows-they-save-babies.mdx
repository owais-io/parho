US officials are tightening rules on RSV shots, which protect babies from serious lung infections. Studies show the vaccine cuts hospital stays by up to 80 %. In the US only about a third of pregnant people get it, while other countries give every newborn the shot. Doctors warn that limiting the vaccine could bring more babies into hospitals, create confusion, and hurt access for families.
