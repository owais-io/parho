In western Iran’s Kurdish areas, people have taken to the streets to protest rising prices and corruption. Authorities have responded with tear‑gas, pellet guns and, according to witnesses, live rounds. At least 36 people, including children, have died and more than 2,100 have been arrested. While the government promises talks and food subsidies, it also warns it will use force if protests turn violent.
