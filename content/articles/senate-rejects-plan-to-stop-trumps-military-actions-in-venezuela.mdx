The Senate voted 50‑50 to kill a resolution that would have required President Trump to get Congress’s approval before any further military moves in Venezuela. Two Republicans changed their minds after talks with the administration, while others stayed in the fight. Democrats see the defeat as a warning that Trump can act abroad without checks. The vote shows growing alarm about the president’s foreign‑policy ambitions.
