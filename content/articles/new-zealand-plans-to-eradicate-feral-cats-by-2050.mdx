New Zealand’s conservation minister announced that feral cats will be added to the country’s Predator‑Free 2050 plan, aiming to eliminate them by 2050. Over 2.5 million feral cats threaten native wildlife, including birds and bats. While the move faces public debate, 90 % of people surveyed support cat control. The government will release detailed plans in March 2026, and animal welfare groups are calling for better rules for domestic cats, like mandatory microchipping and neutering.
