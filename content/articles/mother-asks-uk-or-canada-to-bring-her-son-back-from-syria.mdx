Jack Letts, a 30‑year‑old British‑born man, has been held in a Kurdish‑run Syrian prison since 2017 without a trial. His mother, Sally Lane, wants him repatriated to the UK or Canada after the US plans to airlift 7,000 IS‑linked prisoners to Iraq. She says governments haven’t updated her, and she fears he could face a death sentence or unfair trial if left in Syria. The situation is unclear as fighting continues.
