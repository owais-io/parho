Spanish figure skater Tomàs‑Llorenç Guarino Sabaté must redo his Olympic short program because the Minions music he used couldn’t be cleared for competition. He learned about the problem just days before the Games, forcing him to change choreography he had practiced all season. The issue highlights growing complications in music licensing for figure skating, with other athletes facing similar late‑stage disputes. The ISU is working on a better clearance system, but the skater remains determined to compete.
