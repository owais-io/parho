Afghan Australians in Melbourne, once grateful for the Liberal government’s evacuation aid, now feel hurt by a new policy that could blacklist certain countries. The article follows a cultural festival with camel rides, showing community pride and integration. It highlights how Afghans, many fleeing the Taliban, contribute as doctors, business owners, and volunteers. The policy threatens to lump diverse people together, undermining the nation’s inclusive values.
