Survivors of Jeffrey Epstein’s sex‑trafficking scheme are furious about the recent release of about three million government files. They say the Justice Department made mistakes that exposed victims’ names and gave the public only a small, edited portion of the documents. Attorneys want all files unredacted so Congress can investigate and to hold powerful people accountable and make sure no more survivors are harmed.
