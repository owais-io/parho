Donald Trump sparked anger when he said the U.S. might not need to be part of the fight to protect the Hormuz Strait, while urging other nations to step in. He told reporters that the U.S. has enough oil to stay out, but that allies should defend the area. Critics called the remark reckless, citing U.S. casualties and the ongoing war with Iran. Countries like the UK and France said they would not join the conflict.
