Kiwi Chow, a Hong Kong filmmaker famous for Ten Years and the protest documentary Revolution of Our Times, has had his latest movie Deadline rejected by the city’s censorship board over national‑security concerns. He says he won’t appeal because it would be pointless. Chow, who has criticised the government and the Wang Fuk Court fire, feels the move is a warning aimed at shutting his career. Despite the pressure, he plans to stay and keep speaking out.
