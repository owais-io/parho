Australia’s 2023 law says anyone who has extremist videos on a phone or computer can be charged, even if they’re just curious. The new rules have already led to arrests of kids as young as 13, many of whom have autism or language challenges. Critics argue the law is too vague and unfairly targets vulnerable teens who may not understand the rules. The government says it helps stop radicalisation early, but many say it hurts kids.
