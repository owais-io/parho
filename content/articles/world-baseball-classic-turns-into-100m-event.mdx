The World Baseball Classic (WBC) started in 2006 as a 16‑nation tournament that finally let professional stars compete for a national team. Over two decades it has become a $100 million event, drawing big names like Ohtani, Soto and Trout, and earning strong U.S. TV ratings. Fans worldwide cheer in places from Japan to the Netherlands. Still, the WBC faces uneven team strength, timing problems, injuries and limited reach in Africa and other new markets.
