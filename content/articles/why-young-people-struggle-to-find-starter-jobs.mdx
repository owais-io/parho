In this letter, the author says the decline of starter jobs is partly due to higher minimum wage, employer national insurance costs, and automation replacing workers. He recalls a 1971 school interview that showed personal connection mattered more than tests. He cites the Milgram experiment to warn companies about hiring practices that could harm their reputation. The piece shows how rising costs and technology make it harder for young people to get their first jobs.
