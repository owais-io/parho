Dan Driscoll, a former investment banker now the U.S. Army secretary, has been picked by Donald Trump to deliver a 28‑point peace plan to Ukrainian President Zelenskyy and discuss it with Russia’s Vladimir Putin. Though he has little diplomatic background, Driscoll’s tech‑drone interests and close ties to Trump aide JD Vance earned him the job. The plan has been criticized as a “capitulation” to Moscow, and Driscoll’s role replaces Trump’s sidelined Ukraine envoy.
