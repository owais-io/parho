In several U.S. states, voters have put abortion rights on the ballot and won, but lawmakers are still pushing restrictions. Missouri, Arizona, Michigan, Montana, Nevada and Ohio are fighting court cases over new rules that could limit access. Even after a state constitution says abortion is protected, lawmakers can add laws that make it hard to get care. The fight shows that voter wins don’t automatically remove barriers.
