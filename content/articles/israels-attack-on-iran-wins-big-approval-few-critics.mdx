After a 12‑day strike on Iran, Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu called the battle a historic win. Most Israeli politicians and many voters cheered, even as Iranian attacks killed civilians and damaged homes. Few people questioned the move, and the war helped boost Netanyahu’s image ahead of upcoming elections. The article argues Israel’s constant war focus, driven by economic success and a powerful defence industry, may keep the country in a cycle of militarism.
