Scotland’s life‑science industry is booming, worth about £10.5 bn, thanks in part to the famous Dolly the Sheep cloning breakthrough. RoslinCT, a cell‑therapy company spun out of the Roslin Institute, produces treatments for diseases like sickle cell. The country’s strong universities, government support, and a growing talent pool keep it a global hub for biotech. With a new £25 bn strategy, Scotland aims to lead the next decade of medical innovation.
