In a long‑running case, about 4,500 Greenlandic women who had IUDs inserted without their knowledge between 1960 and 1991 are now able to claim about 300,000 DKK (≈£35,000) from Denmark. After a three‑year lawsuit, Denmark apologized, declared the practice a human‑rights violation, and set up a fund. Women must prove they were unaware of the procedure, but the decision marks a major step toward justice.
