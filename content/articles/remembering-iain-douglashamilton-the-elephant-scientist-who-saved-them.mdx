Iain Douglas‑Hamilton was a British scientist who spent his life studying African elephants. From 1965 he lived in Kenya’s Lake Manyara park, learning to identify each elephant and track their behavior. He warned the world about the rising ivory trade and helped enforce a global ban in 1990. After that he founded Save the Elephants, using satellite‑tracking to protect herds and work with local communities. He died at 83, leaving a lasting legacy of elephant conservation.
