In the 1980s, Reagan’s tax cuts hurt working‑class cities and left many African Americans feeling abandoned by the Democratic Party. Rev. Jesse Jackson organized huge voter‑registration drives that added 2 million Black voters. He used this momentum to push for a “People’s Platform” demanding higher taxes on the rich, lower defense spending, and universal health care. In 1984 he ran for president, turning Black voting power into a political force that reshaped the party and helped elect Black mayors.
