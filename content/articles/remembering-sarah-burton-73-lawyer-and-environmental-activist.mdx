Sarah Burton, 73, was a powerful lawyer and activist who fought for the environment, human rights, and nuclear disarmament. She worked at Greenpeace and Amnesty International and helped miners during strikes. She won a court order to protect a coroner’s jury and persuaded foreign activists to block nuclear waste dumping. After retiring, she lived in Dorset, painted, and mentored young women. She is survived by her daughter, Hannah, and brother, Milton.
