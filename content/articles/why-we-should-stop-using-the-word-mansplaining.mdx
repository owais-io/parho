The Guardian columnist argues that words like “mansplaining” have become overused and misused, turning useful criticism into a punchline that divides people. The piece notes that Oxford’s word of the year, “rage bait,” was chosen over less useful terms, and that the word “mansplaining” was once a hot choice but later saw backlash and political misuse. The article urges us to stop using buzzwords that create division and lose meaning.
