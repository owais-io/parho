Johannes Klæbo of Norway finished the Winter Olympics with an unprecedented sixth gold medal in the 50km cross‑country mass start. His win made him the first athlete ever to win six golds at a single Games, putting him ahead of legendary skier Marit Bjørgen. Norway finished with 40 medals, the most of any country. Other highlights included Dutch speed skater Jorrit Bergsma winning the men’s mass start, and Canada’s curling team taking the men’s final.
