Polly Toynbee argues Labour should pick a new leader before May. She says Andy Burnham would bring fresh energy, but Keir Starmer is too cautious and weak for the job. Toynbee warns that letting the current situation drag on could cost Labour seats and make the party less able to fight right‑wing politics. She calls for a swift, decisive leadership change to keep Labour relevant.
