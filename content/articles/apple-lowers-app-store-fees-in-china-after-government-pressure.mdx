Apple is cutting the commission it takes from app sales in mainland China from 30% to 25% starting March 15, 2026, after talks with regulators. The cut will save Chinese developers over 6 billion yuan ($873 million) a year and could reduce app prices for Chinese users by up to 1 billion yuan. Smaller “mini‑app” developers will see rates drop from 15% to 12%. The move follows global scrutiny of Apple’s fees and EU regulation.
