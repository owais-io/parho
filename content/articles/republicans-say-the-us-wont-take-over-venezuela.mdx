After Donald Trump said the U.S. would run Venezuela after pulling President Maduro into the country, top Republicans—including Secretary of State Marco Rubio—quickly denied that claim. Rubio and others said the U.S. has “leverage” but is not stepping in to run the nation. They noted the operation was for security reasons, not for regime change, and that any future leadership in Venezuela would still be Venezuelan, not U.S. officials.
