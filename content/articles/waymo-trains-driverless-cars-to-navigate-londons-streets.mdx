Waymo, the U.S. self‑driving company, has 24 cars parked in London that are being driven around by people to learn the city’s quirks, like how to handle zebra crossings. The company hopes to start taking paying customers by the end of 2024, once UK laws allow it. Other firms such as Wayve and Uber also want a share of London’s market. The government will run a pilot program in spring, while black‑cab drivers voice concerns about safety and job loss.
