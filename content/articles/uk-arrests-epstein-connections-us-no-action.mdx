After the US Justice Department released millions of pages about Jeffrey Epstein, no US arrests have followed. Meanwhile Britain arrested former royal Prince Andrew and ex‑ambassador Peter Mandelson on unrelated charges, showing a more aggressive approach. Lawyers say the US already looked at the documents and decided no charges were possible, citing evidence gaps and statutes of limitations. Some hope a new administration could change that, but for now victims still face no prosecutions in the US.
