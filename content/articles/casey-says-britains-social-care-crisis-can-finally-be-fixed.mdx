Polly Toynbee reports on Louise Casey, a troubleshooter appointed by Labour to overhaul England’s adult social care. Casey gave a blunt speech saying the current system is collapsing, with councils and the NHS fighting over who pays. She proposes a national care service, better pay for workers, and a new way to fund care that would involve those with enough assets. The plan aims to stop the crisis and get public support.
