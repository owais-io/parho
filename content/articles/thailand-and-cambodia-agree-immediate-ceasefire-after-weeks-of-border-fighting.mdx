Thailand and Cambodia have agreed to stop fighting at the border after more than 100 people died and half a million were displaced. The ceasefire starts Saturday noon, banning all weapons, even attacks on civilians. Both sides will keep troops in place and work together to clear mines and fight cybercrime. The deal ends 20 days of jets, rockets, and artillery, following recent air strikes on Cambodia, and seeks to calm tensions that began in July.
