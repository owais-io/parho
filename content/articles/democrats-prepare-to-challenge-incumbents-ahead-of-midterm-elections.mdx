A new wave of Democratic primary challengers is taking on party incumbents before the November midterms. Inspired by last year’s shock victories and fed by anger over Trump, the Gaza war, and rising living costs, these fighters—mostly from the party’s progressive wing—are pushing back against a complacent establishment. Incumbents fear being unseated, while PACs and grassroots groups back aggressive candidates who promise to stand up for voters.
