A recent survey of 1,200 young men in New South Wales found that more than a quarter say violence is acceptable to earn respect, and over a third admitted to sexually harassing a woman in the past month. In response, the government has earmarked $3.5 million to campaigns aimed at men and boys, tackling harmful stereotypes, relationship issues, isolation and porn use. Experts say the money will help keep the decline in abusive attitudes going.
