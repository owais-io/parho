Attorney General Richard Hermer challenged Conservative minister Kemi Badenoch, asking whether she would object to a Jewish man praying in a public space. Badenoch said all religious expressions should fit British norms and had criticised a recent Muslim prayer event in Trafalgar Square, citing gender segregation. Hermer compared her stance to far‑right views and urged her to clarify if she dislikes only Muslim prayers. The government defends freedom of religion as a core value.
