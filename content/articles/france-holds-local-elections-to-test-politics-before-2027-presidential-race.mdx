France is voting in its first round of municipal elections, a key test of the country’s political mood before the 2027 presidential contest. About 35,000 towns and cities choose mayors and councillors on issues like safety, housing and waste. Voter turnout is 49%, up from 2020 but down from 2014. The results, especially in big cities, will show how the far‑right National Rally is doing locally, and what alliances the left and right might form ahead of the presidential race.
