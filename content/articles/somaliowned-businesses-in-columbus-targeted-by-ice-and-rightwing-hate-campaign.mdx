ICE agents and right‑wing influencers have been driving around Somali‑owned businesses in Columbus, Ohio, threatening the community after President Trump’s anti‑immigrant comments. The intimidation began with cars parked outside a Somali‑run daycare, followed by a break‑in on New Year’s Day. Many Somali owners feel unsafe, see customers leave, and worry about losing their temporary protected status. Local politicians are divided, with some urging cooperation with ICE while the city council has banned local police from enforcing federal immigration law.
