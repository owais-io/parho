Emma Beddington read about a “90‑second rule” that says feelings like anger fade after a minute and a half. She spent a whole day timing herself, letting anger, anxiety and other emotions play out for 90 seconds. She found the idea hard to follow – the time adds up, and the emotions still linger. The experiment showed the rule didn’t help her stay calmer or save time.
