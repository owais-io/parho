The UK plans to reduce the amount of steel it can bring in without a tariff, aiming to shield local steel makers from a global oversupply, mainly from China. New quotas would be announced soon, taking effect July 1, after current safeguards expire. Some steel users worry the limits will raise costs, while manufacturers call for stricter rules. The government says it will decide soon to keep UK steel jobs safe.
