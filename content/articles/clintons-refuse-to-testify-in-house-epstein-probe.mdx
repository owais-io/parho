Bill and Hillary Clinton have declined to testify in a congressional inquiry about their ties to Jeffrey Epstein. They argue the subpoenas are illegal and politically motivated, calling them an abuse of power. The House committee, led by Republican James Comer, is investigating how the government handled Epstein’s case and wants the Clintons to answer questions. The Clintons’ refusal could lead to contempt charges, while critics say the probe is partisan.
