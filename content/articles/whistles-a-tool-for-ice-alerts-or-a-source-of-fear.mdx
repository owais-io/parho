Whistles were handed out by grassroots groups to warn neighbors when ICE agents were nearby. Thousands have been made and shipped to 25 states. Some activists say whistles provide needed alerts, while others worry they scare children, the elderly and those with disabilities, or draw extra attention. The debate shows how anti‑immigration‑enforcement movements change tactics to fit local needs today.
