The writer admits to holding many old keepsakes—birthday cards, toys, handmade gifts—that fill her apartment and make cleaning hard. She calls herself a sentimental maximalist and wonders if she’s hoarding. These items keep memories alive, but some also bring hurt. The trend of keeping mementos has grown as people avoid the pressures of adulthood. She questions whether her attachment is healthy or just a deeper attachment to the past.
