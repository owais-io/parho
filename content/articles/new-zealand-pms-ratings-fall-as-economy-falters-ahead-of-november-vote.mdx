A recent poll shows New Zealand’s new prime minister, Christopher Luxon, is losing favour, with his rating falling to 17.3 %—the lowest he’s had since becoming leader. His National party is now five points behind Labour, the main opposition. Voters say the country’s heading in the wrong direction, and the economy’s weak growth and rising energy costs are blamed. If the election goes ahead now, the left and right could end up with a hung parliament.
