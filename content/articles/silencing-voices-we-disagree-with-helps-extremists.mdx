Peter Greste, a journalist, says stopping voices we disagree with lets extremists win. He left Adelaide Writers’ Week after the festival board removed writer Randa Abdel‑Fattah because of past comments, citing sensitivity after the Bondi gun attack. Greste argues that silencing people based on fear destroys the “grey zone” where different ideas coexist. He warns that shrinking public debate helps extremist goals, and he withdrew to protect open discussion.
