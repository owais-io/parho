Roland Littlewood was a UCL professor who studied how people heal. He worked in psychiatry and anthropology, travelling to Trinidad, Haiti, and Lebanon to learn about local healing rituals. He wrote books on mental health and culture. He taught and led the Royal Anthropological Institute. Roland died at 78 from pancreatic cancer. He left a wife, a daughter, three grandchildren and a legacy of cross‑cultural research.
