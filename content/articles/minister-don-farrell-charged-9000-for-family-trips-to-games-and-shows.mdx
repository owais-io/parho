Don Farrell, a senior minister, used the parliamentary family‑travel allowance to bring relatives to events like football matches, the Australian Open, the opera and theatre premieres. Over three years he claimed a little over $9,000, a sum that has drawn media attention and calls for a review. The rules say such trips are permitted, and the Independent Parliamentary Expenses Authority says no rules were broken.
