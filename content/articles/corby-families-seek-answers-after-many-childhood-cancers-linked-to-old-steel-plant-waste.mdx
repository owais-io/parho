Fraser Gaffney’s 17‑month‑old son was diagnosed with leukemia in 2018. After his recovery, Gaffney and her husband wondered if the cancer was caused by toxic waste dumped when Corby’s steel plant closed in 1979. They joined 130 other families who say children in the town keep getting cancer. The local council is now testing sites and a public health report is due. Families want full answers and clean‑up of the area.
