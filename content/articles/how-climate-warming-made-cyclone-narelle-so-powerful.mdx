Severe Tropical Cyclone Narelle hit Queensland’s coast in early 2024, becoming almost a category‑five storm. Scientists say the heat in the Coral Sea made the ocean 1–2 °C warmer than normal, giving the cyclone extra fuel. Warm seas also raise sea level, making storm surges worse, and a warmer atmosphere can drop more rain. Although fewer cyclones form overall, the most intense ones are likely to grow stronger as the planet warms.
