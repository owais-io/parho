Sharon Bond, a pioneering black social worker and family therapist, died at 77. She began as an education worker in Tower Hamlets and later joined Hackney’s Child and Family Consultation Service. In 1997 she founded Chiron, a therapy training center that welcomed those overlooked by mainstream schools. A first‑born black woman supervisor, she also ran a Saturday school for black children and was active in the British Black Panthers. She was survived by her family.
