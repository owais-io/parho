The former chief of collapsed construction firm Carillion, Richard Howson, was fined almost £238,000 by the UK financial regulator FCA. Howson helped hide serious money problems, so investors were not warned. He stopped his appeal after the FCA’s ruling. The penalty follows similar fines on other ex‑executives. Carillion’s collapse left 3,000 jobs, ruined public projects and caused huge cost overruns.
