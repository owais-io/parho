Crispin Odey, a wealthy hedge‑fund founder, is suing the UK’s Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) after the regulator fined him £1.8 million and banned him from the industry over allegations that he blocked an internal investigation into sexual harassment claims. Odey says the FCA acted with hostility and pre‑judged the case. The suit comes as he also faces civil claims from several women and a libel action against the Financial Times.
