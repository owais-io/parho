England appointed Brendon McCullum as a temporary coach for the Ashes. He is known for a relaxed, inspiring style but lacks detailed planning. Critics argue he shouldn’t lead the whole team or be held responsible for failures. The article stresses that even if England wins, McCullum must still be held accountable for poor preparation and management. It also highlights deeper structural problems in English cricket that need fixing.
