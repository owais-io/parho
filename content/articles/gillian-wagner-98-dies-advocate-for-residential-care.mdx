Gillian Wagner was a leading voice in Britain’s residential care sector. In 1986 she chaired a committee that pushed for better standards and a national inspectorate, leading to many changes even without laws. She also wrote books exposing the history of charities, such as Thomas Barnardo, and helped set up groups that still promote high‑quality care today. Born in 1927, she earned an OBE and later a DBE, and left a legacy of improved support for elderly and disabled people.
