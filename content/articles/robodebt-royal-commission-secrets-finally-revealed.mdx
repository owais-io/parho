The Australian government has finally opened a 56‑page chapter of the robodebt royal commission that had been hidden for almost three years. The sealed section names six public officials, including former PM Scott Morrison, who were referred for investigation over the unlawful debt‑collection scheme. The National Anti‑Corruption Commission (NACC) later found that only two of those officials had serious corruption. The release closes the last chapter of the robodebt inquiry and lets the public see why the commissioner wanted further probes.
