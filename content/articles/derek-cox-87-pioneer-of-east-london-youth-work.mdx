Derek Cox, 87, died after a life of helping young people in East London. He started Avenues Unlimited in 1965, a group that worked with tough neighbourhoods and new Bangladeshi families. He believed in whole‑community action, organising football, camps, and lobbying for better housing. He earned an OBE in 1995. Later he became a foster carer and converted to Islam. His funeral drew a diverse crowd that celebrated his compassion.
