After months of threats from Donald Trump, Cuban President Miguel Díaz‑Canel announced that his country is ready to talk to the United States, but only if the talks are “without pressure” or pre‑conditions. He called the U.S. media campaign a “psychological war” and warned that Cuba is suffering from an economic slump, power cuts and food shortages. The president also said China, Russia and other allies support Cuba.
