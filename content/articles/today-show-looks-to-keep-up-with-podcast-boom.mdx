BBC’s long‑running Today programme, heard by 5‑6 million people every week, is in crisis. After veteran presenter Amol Rajan left for his own podcast, other key staff are quitting or considering other opportunities. Staff worry the show’s tight, news‑focused style feels old‑school, while others want a more relaxed, personality‑led format. The BBC is hunting new editor and presenter, but the future of the flagship radio show is uncertain.
