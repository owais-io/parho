Immigration enforcement agents are detaining kids and parents in New Haven, Connecticut, making students scared to go to school. Teachers and school leaders have built trust with immigrant families and set up policies to protect children from ICE. Despite these efforts, enrollment in English‑language classes has fallen, and many students skip classes or drop out. The fear of deportation is turning classrooms into quiet, anxious spaces.
