The West is experiencing a record‑breaking heatwave in March, with temperatures up to 112°F, melting snow that feeds rivers and reservoirs. The dry, hot air fuels wildfires—over 1.4 million acres have already burned this year, double the 10‑year average. Experts warn the heat will keep rising, hurting water supplies, power generation and ecosystems. Climate scientists say these extremes are driven by climate change and will keep getting worse.
