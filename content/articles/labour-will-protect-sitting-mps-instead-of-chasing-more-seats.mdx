Lucy Powell, Labour’s deputy leader, told MPs that the party will change its focus from winning extra seats to keeping current MPs in office. Labour will provide training so MPs can showcase how government actions—like frozen rail fares and NHS support—benefit their local communities. Powell urged unity and backing for Keir Starmer, saying a strong Labour government can tackle the cost‑of‑living, NHS and other key issues.
