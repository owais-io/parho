The United States has paused a £31 billion tech deal it had promised to Britain, citing the UK’s slow progress on trade issues. The deal, which would have brought money from Microsoft, Google and others and created a new AI hub in the north‑east of England, is on hold because of disagreements over the UK’s digital services tax, food safety rules and other trade barriers. The move hurts UK‑US relations and delays potential jobs and investment.
