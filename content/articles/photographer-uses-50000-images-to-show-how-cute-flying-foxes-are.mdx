Photographer Doug Gimesy has spent more than 160 days in the field, taking around 50,000 photos of four Australian flying‑fox species. He shows their playful, mother‑and‑baby moments and their useful role in pollination. Gimesy believes that friendly pictures can change the public’s fear and misunderstanding of bats, helping protect them from harm. His book, *Life Upside Down*, is now in many school libraries to spread this message.
