NASA has delayed its Artemis II crewed launch to the moon until March because engineers found leaking hydrogen and a valve problem during a dry‑run test. The 10‑day mission, which will carry the first woman and the first person of colour to go beyond low‑Earth orbit, is now on hold as teams review the data and fix the issues. The crew will re‑enter quarantine before the next launch window.
