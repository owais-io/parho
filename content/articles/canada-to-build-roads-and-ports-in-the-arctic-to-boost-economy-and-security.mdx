Canada is putting new money into building roads and ports in its Arctic. The government wants to make the region more connected and boost jobs, especially after the U.S. raised tariffs. Two main projects are a road to the deep‑water port at Grays Bay for mining and an expanded road to Inuvik that will help local towns survive the changing climate. Experts worry about cost and who will benefit.
