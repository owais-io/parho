The war in Iran has pushed global oil prices past $90 a barrel, its highest since the pandemic, sparking fears of higher inflation worldwide. Reports say Kuwait and other Gulf producers may cut output because storage is running out, which could force more shutdowns. The conflict also threatens LNG supplies from Qatar, putting gas prices and bond yields higher. Stock markets, especially in Asia, have fallen sharply.
