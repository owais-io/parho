An Indigenous man from Cape York was wrongly found guilty of a traffic offence. A magistrate repeatedly cut off the defence lawyer, made rude comments and used the man's past crimes to justify the conviction. An appeal judge said the trial was unfair and the evidence didn't support the guilty verdict. The conviction was overturned, and the man was cleared of the charge.
