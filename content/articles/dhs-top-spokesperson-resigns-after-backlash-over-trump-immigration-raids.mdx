Tricia McLaughlin, the main voice of the Department of Homeland Security, will leave her job next week. She has been a tough supporter of Trump’s immigration raids, which have drawn heavy criticism after violent clashes and the deaths of two American citizens in Minneapolis. Her departure comes while the department faces calls for accountability and lawsuits over the shootings. A deputy will take her place as the agency’s press chief.
