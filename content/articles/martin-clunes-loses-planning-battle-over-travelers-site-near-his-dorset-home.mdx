Martin Clunes and his wife were at a Dorset council meeting to hear a decision about two people, Theo Langton and Ruth McGill, who want to stay permanently on a plot of land near Clunes' home. The couple, who call themselves new travellers, own the site and sell their handmade goods. The council voted to let them stay, a decision that Clunes' lawyer said would set a precedent for other travellers.
