NASA has pulled back its plans for a human moon landing under the Artemis program. The agency will now add a low‑Earth‑orbit test flight before sending astronauts to the moon, pushing the first landing to 2028. The change comes after rocket problems and safety panel warnings. The new schedule also delays Artemis II to early April. The goal is to lower risk by building up experience step by step.
