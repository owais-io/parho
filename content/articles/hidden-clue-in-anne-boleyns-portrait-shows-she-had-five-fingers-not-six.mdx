Scientists examined a 16th‑century painting of Anne Boleyn at Hever Castle and found a hidden sketch that reveals the artist deliberately kept her hands clear of a rumored extra finger. The discovery proves the portrait was meant to fight witch‑craft gossip and support Anne’s daughter Elizabeth I’s legitimacy. The evidence will be on display in a new Hever exhibition about the queen’s image.
