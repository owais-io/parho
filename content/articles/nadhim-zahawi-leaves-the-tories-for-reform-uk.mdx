Marina Hyde writes that former Conservative chancellor Nadhim Zahawi has left the Tory party to join Reform UK. She jokes that politicians are “doing us a favour” by switching parties, but notes Zahawi could have retired instead. The piece criticises politicians who think they are “pre‑loved” and can avoid responsibility. It also mentions other Reform members, like Nigel Farage and Richard Tice, and comments on how wealth can shield people from the consequences of their actions.
