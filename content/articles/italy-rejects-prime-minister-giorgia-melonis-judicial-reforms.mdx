Italian voters turned down Meloni’s plan to change the judiciary in a referendum. Nearly 54 % said no, with a record 58.5 % turnout. Young voters were especially opposed, making the outcome tough for Meloni’s far‑right government. The defeat weakens her chances of passing new election laws and a direct vote for the prime minister, giving opposition parties a chance to unite.
