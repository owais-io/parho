Two recent U.S. court cases hit big tech. In New Mexico, a jury fined Meta $375 million for letting kids be sexually exploited on its sites, citing weak safety rules. In Los Angeles, a woman sued Instagram and YouTube for addiction‑related mental‑health harm, winning $3 million in damages (Meta 70 %, Google 30 %). The rulings say the platforms were deliberately built to hook users, and could trigger more lawsuits.
