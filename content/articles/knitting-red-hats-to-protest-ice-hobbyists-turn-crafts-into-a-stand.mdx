A Minneapolis yarn shop owner launched a “Melt the ICE” red hat pattern that blew up online, selling 70,000 copies in two weeks and raising more than $250,000 for immigrant‑aid groups. The red hat, inspired by Norwegian resistance caps, became a symbol of defiance against ICE raids. The movement spread to nail art, dolls, and other crafts, showing how everyday hobbies can become powerful political statements.
