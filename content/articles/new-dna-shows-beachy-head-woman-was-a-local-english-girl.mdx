Scientists have finally solved the mystery of the Beachy Head Woman, a Roman‑era skeleton once thought to be the first black Briton or even from Cyprus. Using improved DNA techniques, they found she came from southern England and lived around AD 130‑310. The skeleton was about 18‑25 years old, ate lots of seafood, and had a healed leg wound. A plaque in Eastbourne was taken down after earlier theories were wrong.
