Julia Letlow, a Louisiana Republican who won a special seat after her husband died, has officially launched a bid for the U.S. Senate. Trump posted a full endorsement on Truth Social, urging her to run. Letlow will face two‑term incumbent Bill Cassidy, who has taken controversial votes on health policy. The new closed‑party primary in Louisiana could mean Cassidy has to win the Republican vote outright to stay in the race.
