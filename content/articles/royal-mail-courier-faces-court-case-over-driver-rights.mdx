A group of 46 Royal Mail‑owned eCourier drivers are suing, claiming they should be treated as workers instead of self‑employed contractors. The drivers deliver blood and tissue samples for the NHS, work long shifts, and pay for their own vans. If the tribunal agrees, they could gain minimum wage and holiday pay. The case follows a Supreme Court ruling that Uber drivers are workers. The claim is backed by the Independent Workers Union.
