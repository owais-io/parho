Investors are moving money into companies that own big, useful physical assets—called Halo firms—because they’re less likely to be displaced by AI. This shift has helped the UK’s FTSE 100 and the European Stoxx 600 hit record highs in early 2026. Goldman's research shows capital‑heavy stocks beat light‑weight tech by 35% since 2025. Energy, utilities, shipping and mining names are the biggest winners, while software firms face pressure.
