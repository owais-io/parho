The article talks about how right‑wing politics now attack empathy, calling it weak or evil. It then shows a real moment in Minneapolis where a man chose care over fear. The man used his right to carry a gun but stepped back, protecting a protester instead of fighting the police. The piece compares this act to old ideas of chivalry, arguing that true manhood is about empathy, not violence. It urges society to teach young men the power of caring.
