The letter argues that Adam Smith used the phrase "invisible hand" only once to explain how personal investment decisions benefit the domestic economy. It warns that heavy taxes can discourage home investment. It also clears up the myth that Marx supported an "iron law of wages," noting he saw wages rising with growth and that workers should not be trapped. The writers urge careful reading of these ideas.
