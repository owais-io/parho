Three senators—Fatima Payman, Lidia Thorpe and Mehreen Faruqi—have written to Senate President Sue Lines asking Labor to change the Senate’s rules. They want a new inquiry into racism and sexism, mandatory anti‑racism training for all senators, and standing‑order changes that stop double standards. They say incidents like Pauline Hanson’s burqa display and other snubs have made the chamber hostile, hurting democracy and discouraging future leaders.
