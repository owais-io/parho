The UK charity watchdog closed a compliance case after staff raised concerns that the Alan Turing Institute’s trustees were not meeting their legal duties. The Charity Commission gave the board advice on finance, governance and change management, but warned it could reopen the case if the trustees ignore the guidance. The complaint listed eight points, including a failed letter of no confidence. The institute’s CEO resigned after the government pushed it to focus on national security.
