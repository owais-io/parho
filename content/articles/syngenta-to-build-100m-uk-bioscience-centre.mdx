Syngenta, a Chinese‑owned agribusiness, will set up a £100 million research centre in Berkshire. The hub, called BioSTaR, will bring together scientists working on biological pesticides and AI tools to make farming greener. The move shows the UK government’s confidence in science, especially after a pharma dispute that hurt other companies’ investment. No new jobs are planned at first, but the centre is part of a larger £345 million grant scheme for UK farmers.
