Prime Minister Keir Starmer has made a point of avoiding hard choices, especially on the international front. His first visit to China in over five years shows he prefers to keep talks open and friendly, even while the UK faces tension with leaders like Xi Jinping. Inside the Labour Party, Starmer has sidelined left‑wing rivals and kept his focus on pragmatic solutions rather than ideological battles. Critics say this style may leave Britain stuck when tougher decisions become unavoidable.
