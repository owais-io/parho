A California judge, Charles Breyer, has expressed doubt about President Trump’s right to keep federal troops in the state’s National Guard after they were sent to Los Angeles to guard federal immigration offices during protests. Breyer said the crisis has changed, asked the government for proof that California can’t handle the situation, and is waiting to decide if the troops should be returned to the state. The move was the first time the Guard was federalized without the governor’s request, sparking a lawsuit and an appeals‑court pause.
