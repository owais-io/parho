War in the Middle East could shut the Hormuz Strait, driving UK fuel prices up to 2022 levels – over 50p a litre and extra £900‑£2,500 a year in bills. Britain’s current plan to drill more at home won’t protect households; they would still import refined fuels. Instead, expanding wind, nuclear and electric transport can give a steady, cheaper supply and cut dependence on volatile markets. Clean power is the real insurance.
