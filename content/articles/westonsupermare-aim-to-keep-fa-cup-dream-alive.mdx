Weston‑super‑Mare, a part‑time club in England’s sixth tier, have made the FA Cup third round for the first time after beating Chelmsford. They now face Grimsby Town, a fourth‑tier side that famously knocked out Manchester United. Manager Scott Rogers and his mixed‑career squad—builders, teachers and former pros—hope the cup run continues, dreaming of an upset and a boost to the club’s finances. The journey has brought pride, money and a chance for a historic win.
