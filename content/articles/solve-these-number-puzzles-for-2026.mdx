The author posts a fun set of number puzzles celebrating 2026. They give one example using six 1s to make 26, then challenge readers to create 26 with five 9s, six 8s, six 7s, six 6s, four 5s, six 4s, four 3s, four 2s, and a “partridge in a pair tree.” Allowed operations are +, – × ÷, brackets, exponentiation, and digit concatenation. Solutions are listed for each case.
