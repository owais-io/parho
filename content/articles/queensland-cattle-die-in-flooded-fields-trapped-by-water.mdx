After months of heavy rain, Queensland farmers face a crisis: thousands of cattle are dying on flooded plains. The animals are stuck on small dry patches surrounded by water and fresh grass, but they won’t move to drink because they seem scared or confused. Since Christmas, estimates suggest as many as 100,000 cattle may have perished, echoing a 2019 flood that killed half a million animals. Farmers are scrambling to rescue the rest while dealing with stress and rain forecasts.
