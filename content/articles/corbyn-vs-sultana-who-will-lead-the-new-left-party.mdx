A new left‑wing party founded by former Labour leader Jeremy Corbyn and Zarah Sultana is heading into a showdown over who will steer it. After months of heated quarrels about money, policy and sexism, the leadership election will decide who controls the 24‑seat executive. The split threatens the party’s future, with members saying the fight is about ego and could force key figures to quit.
