Rabbi Yossi Friedman holds daily vigils at Bondi Pavilion to remember 15 victims of the 14 December attack. He recites prayers, shares each victim’s story, and invites people to lay flowers and reflect. Thousands gather, and even after the flowers were removed, visitors keep coming. Friedman’s goal is to keep the victims’ names alive, unite diverse groups, and show that community support can help heal and counter antisemitism.
