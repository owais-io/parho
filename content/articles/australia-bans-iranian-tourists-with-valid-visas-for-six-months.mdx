Australia has put a six‑month ban on about 7,200 Iranian tourists who hold valid visa‑subclass 600 travel permits. Home Affairs Minister Tony Burke said the move is needed because many tourists might not be able to return home after the Iranian conflict. The ban is temporary, but it leaves many people stuck in limbo. Critics say the decision is unfair and undermines trust in Australia’s immigration system.
