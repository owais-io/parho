A charity worker, Debs Newman, 60, was hit on the head when police tried to take her from a protest against cutting three lime trees in Falmouth, Cornwall. She fell to the ground, was treated by paramedics, and said the police action felt like an assault. The council said the trees were removed because they were damaging the road and property, but Newman said there was no proper consultation. The police are investigating the incident, and no arrests were made.
