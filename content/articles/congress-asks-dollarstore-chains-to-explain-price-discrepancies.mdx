Thirty U.S. lawmakers have written to the CEOs of Dollar General and Family Dollar, demanding documents that explain why their prices on shelves often differ from what customers pay at the register. A Guardian investigation revealed thousands of price‑accuracy failures in the chains’ 28,000 stores. The letter also questions the companies’ mobile‑app policies that block class‑action lawsuits and asks for steps to fix the problem and compensate shoppers.
