An intense storm called a bomb cyclone slammed the eastern US with record‑low temperatures and heavy snow. In North Carolina, the storm caused two deaths, more than 1,000 car crashes, and over a foot of snow in Charlotte. Florida saw its coldest spell since 1989, ice on streets, and even iguanas falling from trees. The blizzard shut down power for thousands, grounded thousands of flights, and left at least 110 people dead across the country.
