Republican senators are debating a deal that would reopen most of the Department of Homeland Security but leave the Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) deportation budget out. They plan to use a budget‑reconciliation trick to fund ICE later, sidestepping a Democratic vote. The idea grew after a meeting with President Trump. The partial DHS shutdown has caused long TSA lines and unpaid staff. Democrats want stricter rules for immigration agents, like warrants and ID.
