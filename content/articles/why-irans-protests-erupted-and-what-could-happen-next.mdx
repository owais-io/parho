Iran’s streets erupted after people were fed up with high prices, bad water, air pollution and a government that limits freedoms. Over 600 people have been killed and more than 10,000 arrested, showing the unrest is the biggest challenge to the Islamic Republic. The government has cracked down hard, cut the internet, and threatened death sentences. Trump warned the U.S. could use force, and sanctions are tightening. The regime’s future looks uncertain as anger grows and experts say the protests could change the political balance.
