During the German premiere of Tiago Rodrigues’s play *Catarina, or the Beauty of Killing Fascists* in Bochum, an actor’s far‑right monologue sparked chaos. Audience members heckled, hurled fruit, and two people climbed onto the stage trying to pull the actor off. Security stopped the attempt, and the theatre kept the performance going. The director said the play is meant to unsettle, while critics praised its boldness, and the play has won several awards.
