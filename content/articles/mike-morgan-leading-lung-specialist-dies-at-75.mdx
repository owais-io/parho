Mike Morgan, a pioneering lung doctor, died at 75 after a 30‑year career in Leicester that grew a small team into a national hub for respiratory care. As NHS England’s respiratory director, he championed early diagnosis and exercise‑based treatment for COPD, changing how the condition is managed across the UK and abroad. He also founded the Leicester Institute for Lung Health, now a major research center with extensive funding and publications.
