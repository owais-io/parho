A new Barclays study shows that people with student loans save about £2,000 less a year toward a home deposit than those without debt. They put away £310 a month instead of £474, meaning a £164 monthly gap. Almost half say repayments hurt their financial stability, and many feel they can't buy a house because of the debt. The report comes amid talks to freeze the loan‑repayment start point and a parliamentary inquiry.
