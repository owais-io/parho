Trump said he was happy with FCC chair Brendan Carr’s plan to threaten the broadcast licenses of news outlets that criticize U.S. coverage of the war in Iran. Carr said stations could lose licences for spreading “fake news” and that early renewals could be pulled. Trump praised Carr’s stance, calling the media “corrupt.” Democrats, Republicans, and free‑speech groups criticized the move, saying the First Amendment protects journalists.
