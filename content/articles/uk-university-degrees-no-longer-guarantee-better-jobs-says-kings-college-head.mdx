King’s College London vice‑chancellor Prof Shitij Kapur says a UK degree is no longer a ticket to good jobs. He points to too many graduates, a shrinking pay advantage, AI competition and a slow economy. The old promise of a degree guaranteeing social mobility has changed to a “visa” that offers chances but not certainty. He warns of rising debt, government fee cuts and the need for universities to drive future tech.
