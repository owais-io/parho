Colossal Biosciences, a Dallas‑based company, claims it has revived extinct animals with gene‑editing. Last year they produced pups that look like the long‑gone dire wolf, and they plan to create woolly mammoths and dodos next. Scientists see the technology as a tool for endangered species, but critics say the animals are only hybrids, not true resurrected species, and warn about ecological risks. The debate centers on science, ethics and conservation priorities.
