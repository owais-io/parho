On Monday, Scottish Labour leader Anas Sarwar announced a surprise press conference calling for UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer to quit. In the span of a few hours, Downing Street officials, including the new chief‑staff team, scrambled to shore up the PM’s position. They called ministers, reassured key figures like Wes Streeting and Angela Rayner, and filled vacant roles after the chief‑staff resignation. The coordinated effort stopped a leadership challenge and kept Starmer in power.
