A top U.S. vaccine adviser has said the country might rethink routine childhood shots, raising fears that polio could come back. Experts warn that doctors who know how to treat polio are disappearing and health systems aren’t ready for an outbreak. Survivors, like Grace Rossow and Art Caplan, say polio still hurts and that only the vaccine can protect people. Families who refuse shots risk a dangerous return of the disease.
