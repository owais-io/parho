The Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) raised interest rates even as the Middle East war risks higher fuel costs and inflation. The decision split the board 5‑4; some members wanted to wait for more data. Governor Michele Bullock said timing was key, aiming to curb rising inflation already above the 2.5% target. She warned that a longer conflict could hurt growth and asked the board to watch closely for changes.
