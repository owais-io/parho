Lelia Duley was an obstetric epidemiologist who studied pregnancy and childbirth outcomes. She led the 1995 eclampsia trial that proved magnesium sulphate beats diazepam, instantly changing care worldwide. After training in Oxford and Aberdeen, she held senior roles at the UK Cochrane Centre, Leeds and Nottingham, designing clinical trials. She retired in 2016 after a leukemia diagnosis but stayed active in patient charities. Lelia died aged 67, survived by her husband, two daughters and siblings.
