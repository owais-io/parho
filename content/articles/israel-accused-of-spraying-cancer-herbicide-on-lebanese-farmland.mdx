Lebanon says Israel sprayed a cancer‑linked herbicide, glyphosate, on farms in the south, calling it a health crime that threatens food security and farmers. President Joseph Aoun slammed the move as a violation of sovereignty and said Lebanon would take legal and diplomatic action. The spraying happened Sunday morning, with videos showing light aircraft and lab tests finding 20‑30 times normal glyphosate levels. Lebanon says this adds to earlier Israeli attacks that already damaged the region’s soil and wildlife.
