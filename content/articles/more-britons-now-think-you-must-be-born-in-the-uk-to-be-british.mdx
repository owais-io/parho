A recent poll shows that almost a third of Britons now believe you have to be born in the UK to be truly British – a big jump from last year. Most people still see Britishness as based on shared values, not race. Supporters of Nigel Farage are the most extreme, saying ethnicity matters. The study warns that right‑wing ideas are growing, but most voters still value law, kindness and hard work.
