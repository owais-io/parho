Trump wants the US to land a flag on the moon before he leaves office, so NASA’s Artemis program is in a hurry. The agency’s new boss, billionaire Jared Isaacman, is steering the effort with help from SpaceX, Blue Origin and other private firms. Artemis 2 is set to launch in early 2026, followed by a crewed landing in 2027. The plan also aims to beat China’s lunar ambitions, but costs and delays are still a challenge.
