Lanark’s Whuppity Scoorie is a yearly spring ritual that brings kids and families together. On 1 March (or the next day if it falls on a Sunday), a bell in St Nicholas’s church tower rings, and about a hundred children run around the church swinging paper balls on strings. The tradition, which dates back over 150 years, is part of the town’s community spirit and helps new residents feel welcome. Local schools hold poster contests to keep the custom alive.
