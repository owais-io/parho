Janine di Giovanni, a war reporter, explains how powerful governments kill journalists who reveal the truth, using her friend’s death and the killing of Marie Colvin as examples. She notes that when reporters are blocked from war zones, fake stories spread, making it hard for people to know what really happened. Her project, The Reckoning Project, trains local reporters to gather evidence that can be used in courts to fight lies.
