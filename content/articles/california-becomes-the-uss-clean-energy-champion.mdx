At the Cop30 climate summit in Brazil, California’s governor, Gavin Newsom, highlighted the state’s clean‑energy surge, saying it runs mostly on solar, wind and batteries. California has added 30,800 MW of renewable power and batteries, cutting natural gas use. While the Trump‑era federal government rolled back climate rules, California is suing to protect its projects and is on track to reach an all‑clean grid by 2045. The state is becoming a global clean‑energy leader.
