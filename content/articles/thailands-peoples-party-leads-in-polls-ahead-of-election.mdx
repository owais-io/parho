Thailand’s People’s Party, a young pro‑democracy group that has been dissolved twice, tops the polls for the upcoming election. Leader Natthaphong Ruengpanyawut urges voters to back a chance for political change, focusing on fighting corruption and ending patronage politics. The party faces legal challenges, competition from the ruling Bhumjaithai and the strong Pheu Thai, and doubts about whether voters will support a reform‑oriented agenda. The election will decide if the People’s Party can bring new governance.
