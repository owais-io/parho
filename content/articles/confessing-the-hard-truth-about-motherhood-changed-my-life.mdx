The Guardian piece blends praise for Rose Byrne’s Oscar‑nominated role in “If I Had Legs I’d Kick You” with a personal essay by Polly Hudson about the hidden realities of motherhood. Hudson admits she once felt she was a terrible mom and that the pressure to be happy made her miserable. After opening up to a friend, she found relief and acceptance that motherhood can be joyful, tough, and scary. That’s the takeaway.
