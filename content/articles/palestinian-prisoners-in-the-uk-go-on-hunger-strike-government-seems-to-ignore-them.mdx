In the UK, a group called Palestine Action has been banned, and prisoners linked to it are stuck on long pre‑trial detentions. Several inmates are now on hunger strike, with two reaching 48 days. The government is seen as ignoring their plight, while the public points to harsher treatment of Gaza civilians. Critics urge ministers to lift the ban, grant bail, end prison communication cuts, stop prolonged detentions, and review the government’s tough stance on protest.
