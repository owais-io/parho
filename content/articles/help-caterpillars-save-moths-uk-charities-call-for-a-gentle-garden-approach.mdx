UK gardening charities the RHS and Wildlife Trusts are asking people to stop treating caterpillars as pests and instead let them eat plants. They say the moths that grow from them are in danger, with numbers falling 33 % since the 1960s. The charities suggest pesticide‑free methods like hand‑pick, barriers or pruning, and plant native flowers such as foxglove, hawthorn and mint that feed caterpillars. A healthy garden gives food to moths, birds and other wildlife.
