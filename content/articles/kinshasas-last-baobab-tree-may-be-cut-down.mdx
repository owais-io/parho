In Kinshasa, the capital of the Democratic Republic of Congo, there is only one baobab tree left in the city centre. Baobabs are huge, water‑storing trees that have helped people survive droughts for thousands of years. The last tree is at risk of being cut to make room for new buildings and roads. Local activists and community members are fighting to protect it, because the baobab is a living symbol of resilience and culture.
