The Danish election left the left‑leaning Social Democrats and allies short of a majority, winning 22% of votes and 84 seats out of 179. The right‑leaning blue bloc also fell short with 77 seats, while the centre‑right Moderates hold 14 seats and could decide the coalition. No clear majority means weeks of talks, with the centre‑left likely to form a government. Greenland and wealth‑tax issues were less decisive.
