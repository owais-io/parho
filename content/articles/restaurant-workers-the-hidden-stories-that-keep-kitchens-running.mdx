The book *Voices from the Kitchen* shares 27 stories of immigrant cooks, dishwashers, and servers who keep U.S. restaurants alive. Chef Marc Meyer records their struggles—long journeys, family separation, and even violence—while showing how many start in low‑paid jobs even when they had better careers back home. New U.S. immigration raids threaten these workers and the restaurants that rely on them. The book reminds us that behind every plate are people who deserve respect.
