After my wife died, I turned to philosopher Jürgen Habermas’s ideas about coffee‑house conversations and public spaces. Reading an obituary reminded me that my wife had lived her life in a similar way, talking to strangers on the street and creating her own small public forum. Understanding this helped me see her life differently and made it easier to cope with her loss.
