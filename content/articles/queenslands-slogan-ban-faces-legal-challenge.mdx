Queensland’s parliament made a law that bars the use of two political slogans – “from the river to the sea” and “globalise the intifada.” The bill was originally drafted to protect the public while staying legal, but last‑minute changes stripped it of safeguards. Now the law is a straight ban, which could be struck down by courts because it targets specific ideas. The move has raised concerns about free speech and how easy it is to change the law overnight.
