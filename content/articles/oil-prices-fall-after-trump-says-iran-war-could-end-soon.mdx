Oil prices dropped from record highs after former President Donald Trump said the Iran war could end soon, easing fears of higher fuel costs. The slump helped stocks climb across Europe and Asia, with major indices gaining 1–6%. Trump also warned the conflict might continue and hinted at easing sanctions on oil supplies. Markets remain wary as the situation in the Middle East unfolds, signaling investors hope for a quick resolution but caution about lingering risks.
