The Liberal Democratic Party, led by Japan’s first female prime minister Sanae Takaichi, won a sweeping victory in Sunday’s lower‑house election, grabbing 316 of 465 seats. The win gave Takaichi a supermajority, letting her push a big stimulus package. Japanese stocks jumped to a record high, bonds fell, and the yen steadied after the election, boosting investor confidence.
