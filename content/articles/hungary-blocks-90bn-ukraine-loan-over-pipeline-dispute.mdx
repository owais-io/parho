Hungary’s prime minister Viktor Orbán is refusing to approve a €90 billion loan the EU wants to give Ukraine. He says the loan can’t go out until the damaged Druzhba oil pipeline, which brings Russian oil to Hungary and Slovakia, is fixed. EU leaders are pressuring him to back down, especially since the loan was agreed before the dispute. The situation could delay money Ukraine needs for defence and budgets.
