Dr Susan Gilby, a former chief executive at the Countess of Chester NHS hospital, says senior NHS bosses launch fake investigations to silence whistleblowers. After she raised concerns about bullying by the board chair, she was unfairly dismissed and later awarded a £1.4 million settlement. The tribunal found that board members hid documents and built a sham case against her. Gilby warns that such practices risk patient safety and calls for stronger rules for senior managers.
