A Hong Kong court found media boss Jimmy Lai guilty of conspiracy to publish seditious material and foreign collusion, a verdict that could lead to a life sentence. Lai, who built a billion‑dollar media empire and used his platform to support Hong Kong’s pro‑democracy protests, has spent most of his life fighting Beijing’s influence. The case, seen as part of a wider crackdown, has drawn global criticism and worried his family, who fear he may never see freedom again.
