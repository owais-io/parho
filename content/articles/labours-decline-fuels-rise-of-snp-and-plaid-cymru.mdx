After the July 2024 election, Labour’s shaky performance let the SNP win big in Scotland and Plaid Cymru overtake Labour in Wales for the first time since devolution. Both parties now eye a push for independence, but only the SNP is ready to campaign for a second referendum. Labour’s missteps and the collapse of its Scottish coalition have widened the gap between Westminster and the devolved nations, raising fears of a more fragmented UK.
