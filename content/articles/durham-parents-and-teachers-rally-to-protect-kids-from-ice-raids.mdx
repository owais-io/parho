In Durham, North Carolina, parents and teachers have organized to keep kids safe from ICE raids. After federal agents started sweeping the city, parents set up welcoming committees at schools, offered rideshares, and shared food. Teachers and students helped find friends who were detained, and local politicians passed laws to protect schools from surprise arrests. The community’s quick teamwork shows how neighbors can look out for one another.
