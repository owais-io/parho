Minneapolis is in the eye of an ICE storm. Over a week, federal agents raided neighborhoods, used pepper spray and tear gas, and shot residents. Two women—a nurse and a mother—were killed by agents. The city’s residents organized watches, mutual aid, and protests, demanding accountability. Community groups video‑document incidents and accuse agents of racial profiling. The violence has hurt local businesses, deepened distrust of law‑enforcement, and fueled a fierce political backlash.
