Rome has introduced a €2 fee to enter the Trevi Fountain, a move aimed at controlling crowds and funding upkeep. The charge applies weekdays 11:30 am‑10 pm, weekends 9 am‑10 pm, and only those stepping onto the basin pay. Residents, children under six and disabled visitors are exempt. The €6.5 m annual target has sparked praise and protests, with many visitors welcoming the modest cost while others argue it unfairly monetises history.
