Archbishop Stephen Cottrell was found not guilty of misconduct after a tribunal reviewed how he handled former priest David Tudor, who had abused teenagers. Tudor was allowed to stay in ministry while Cottrell was in charge, even after learning of his past abuse. The tribunal said mistakes were made, but the rules didn’t show misconduct, and Cottrell apologized for the harm caused. The case highlights ongoing abuse scandals in the Church of England.
