A new show at Ghent’s Museum of Fine Arts puts more than 40 female artists from the 1600‑1700 Baroque period front‑and‑center. It follows a long history of women like Judith Leyster, Maria van Oosterwijck and Catrina Tieling being forgotten after their deaths. The exhibition highlights their paintings, still‑life work and even paper‑cutting art, and asks why they were left out of art history.
