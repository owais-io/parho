The U.S. and Israel first tried to hit Iran’s top leaders, but that plan failed. Now they are switching to a “Plan B” that relies on bombings, using ethnic groups like Kurds and Baluchis to stir trouble, and on a heavy‑airstrike strategy called the Dahiya doctrine that targets civilians. The move could hurt the region’s oil supplies and spark economic fallout, while both sides question if the war can end.
