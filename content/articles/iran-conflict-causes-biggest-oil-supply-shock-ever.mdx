An ongoing fight in Iran has stopped thousands of tanker trips through the Hormuz Strait, cutting global oil supplies by an estimated 10 million barrels a day. The IEA and the U.S. released record amounts of stored crude to calm markets, but oil prices still jumped above $100 a barrel. The spike could hurt global growth, while the conflict keeps shaking oil prices and trading routes.
