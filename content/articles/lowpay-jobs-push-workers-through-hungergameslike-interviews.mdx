In the UK, many people chasing jobs that pay just above minimum wage are stuck in exhausting, expensive hiring stages that feel like a Hunger Games. Candidates must go through long group tests, unpaid trials and travel for interviews, only to be ghosted or offered low pay. Employers say these hard screens protect them from costly hiring mistakes, but the process burdens job‑seekers, wastes time and often mismatches the tasks with real work.
