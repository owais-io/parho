Many nations in Africa, Asia and Latin America say the U.S. and Israel’s bombing of Iran is illegal and a misuse of military power. They argue the attack happened before talks on Iran’s nuclear program could finish and called for negotiations instead of war. Critics point to colonial‑style motives and warn that the move undermines international law. The backlash shows the South’s growing opposition to Western military interventions.
