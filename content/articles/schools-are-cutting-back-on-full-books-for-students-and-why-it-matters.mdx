Schools are replacing full novels with short excerpts on laptops, citing shorter attention spans, test prep and Common Core standards. Fewer teens finish whole books, though some teachers still push complete novels. The writer stresses how printed books bring joy, improve vocabulary, academic success, and job prospects. Programs like Reading Is Fundamental give children physical copies, urging families and readers to help create home libraries.
