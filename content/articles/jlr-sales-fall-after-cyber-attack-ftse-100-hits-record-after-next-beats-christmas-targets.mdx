JLR’s sales dropped sharply after a cyber attack that halted production and U.S. tariffs cut its exports. The hit shows up in a 25‑percent drop in retail sales last quarter. Meanwhile, the FTSE 100 climbed to a record 10,065 points after Next beat Christmas sales expectations, lifting the index. UK grocery sales hit a record £13.8bn for the holidays, and Nestlé recalled infant formula over a toxin. Big tech and India’s steel industry also made headlines.
