Anas Sarwar, leader of Scottish Labour, reversed his earlier call for Prime Minister Keir Starmer to quit. He now says Starmer can visit and campaign in Scotland if he shows how the UK government is helping Scots. The move comes after critics said Sarwar’s demand hurt Labour’s chances. Some MPs are upset, but Starmer is still expected to support Sarwar’s bid for first minister. The change could keep the party’s overall hopes alive.
