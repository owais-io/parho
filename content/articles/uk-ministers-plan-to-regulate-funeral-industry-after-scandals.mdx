UK ministers are planning to regulate the country's funeral industry after a string of scandals involving mishandling of bodies. An inquiry into a double‑killer who abused over 100 remains and other cases revealed the sector has no licence or oversight. Proposed rules would require a licence, regular inspections, and enforcement powers. The move follows convictions of funeral‑home owners and calls from bereaved families for safer, more accountable practices.
