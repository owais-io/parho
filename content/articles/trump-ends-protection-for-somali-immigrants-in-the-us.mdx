Trump ends the U.S. protection program for Somali immigrants, giving them only two months to leave or face deportation. About 705 people with temporary status must decide by March 17. Officials say Somalia is safer, but critics say the decision ignores ongoing violence. The move sparked lawsuits from Minnesota and protests after a federal agent shot a citizen. The program, which first started in 1991, had been renewed by previous presidents.
