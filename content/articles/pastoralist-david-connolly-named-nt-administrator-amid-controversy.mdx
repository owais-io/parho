David Connolly, a well‑known cattle rancher, has been chosen as the Northern Territory’s next administrator, a role similar to a state governor. He is under fire for past tweets that mocked Indigenous people, the Greens and the prime minister. Indigenous leaders and Labor MPs want him to apologize or be removed. The federal government says NT can choose its own administrator, but many see his appointment as a symbol of pastoral power over native rights.
