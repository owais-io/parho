Former children’s commissioner Anne Longfield has been named chair of a £65 million, three‑year inquiry into grooming gangs in England and Wales. The investigation will look at how police and other agencies failed to protect children and will consider cultural and ethnic factors in the crimes. Victims want a fair process, and some panel members have left in protest over the chair’s selection. Longfield will work with two other experts to uncover the truth.
