The Guardian looks at whether the south of the Philippines is a terrorism hotspot after two gunmen who attacked Bondi Beach were found to have been in Mindanao a month earlier. For years the area has hosted fighting Islamist groups such as Abu Sayyaf, the Maute group and ISIS‑linked fighters. Though attacks have dropped, there have still been bombings in churches and a university, and the government has kept tightening security.
