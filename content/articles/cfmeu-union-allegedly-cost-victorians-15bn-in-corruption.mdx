Geoffrey Watson, a lawyer who ran the CFMEU union’s Victorian branch, said the union’s actions cost Victorians $15 billion. He says the union turned into a crime syndicate, worked with biker gangs, and took jobs from construction workers. The Victorian government was accused of ignoring the problem. Premier Jacinta Allan called the claim “sickening” and denied the figure. Opponents want a royal commission and stronger anti‑corruption powers. The report’s key chapter was redacted, prompting more calls for investigation.
