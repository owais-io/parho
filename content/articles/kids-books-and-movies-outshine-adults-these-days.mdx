Kids’ books and films today are hitting a new high, says film editor Catherine Shoard. She points to Harry Potter, Louis Sachar, and Malorie Blackman as examples of writing that outshines the stories we read as kids. On screen, animated movies like *The Mitchells vs. the Machines*, *Hoppers* and Pixar’s latest tackle real‑world problems with humor and heart, while most adult films feel flat. The gap is clear and widening.
