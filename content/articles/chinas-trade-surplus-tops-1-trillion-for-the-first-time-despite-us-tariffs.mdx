China’s trade surplus reached $1 trillion for the first time, proving the country can keep growing exports even while the U.S. imposes tariffs. EU shipments rose 14.8% last month, raising fears of dumping, and French President Emmanuel Macron warned Europe could impose tariffs if China doesn’t curb its surplus. China has shifted sales from the U.S. to Europe, with strong growth in ships, semiconductors and autos, and a 26% jump in rare‑earth exports.
