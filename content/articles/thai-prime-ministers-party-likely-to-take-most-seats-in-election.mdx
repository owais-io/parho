Bhumjaithai, the party of Thai prime minister Anutin Charnvirakul, is set to win the most seats in Sunday’s vote, putting a hit on the young, pro‑democracy People’s party. With half of votes counted, Bhumjaithai is already in the lead, while the People’s party trails behind and the Pheu Thai party comes in third. The result weakens the pro‑democracy camp that had won a majority in the last election.
