Hungarian authorities seized a truck carrying about $40 million in cash, €35 million in notes, and 9 kg of gold from Ukraine. Prime Minister Viktor Orbán said the shipment will be kept for up to 60 days while the tax office investigates possible money‑laundering. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy slammed the move as “banditry”, accusing Budapest of acting illegally and calling on other European leaders to condemn the act. The seizure follows a gas‑pipeline dispute and comes amid ongoing fighting in eastern Ukraine.
