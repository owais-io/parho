When President Trump expanded U.S. travel bans to include Mali, Burkina Faso and Niger, the two West African countries responded by prohibiting U.S. citizens from entering. The bans are framed as a “reciprocal” move to protect against security threats and armed groups that have plagued the region. The U.S. said the travel limits help keep people who might pose a risk from entering the country.
