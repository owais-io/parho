Up to 3.2 million people have been forced out of Iranian cities since the US‑Israeli war began, and the conflict has made everyday life in Tehran a series of air‑strike alerts. Many residents stay home, turning apartments into safe havens, sharing food with friends and hoping the regime will change – but not through this war. The strikes hurt businesses, shut schools, and leave people scared, yet they cling to hope for a new, peaceful future.
