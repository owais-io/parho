Nigel Farage, the Reform Party leader, remains popular even after allegations that he was racist as a teenager. About 28 former classmates from Dulwich College say he used racial slurs and encouraged other students to do the same. Farage denies the claims and has attacked the BBC. Despite the accusations, he is still second in polls for prime minister, while right‑wing media praise him and calls for an apology are ignored.
