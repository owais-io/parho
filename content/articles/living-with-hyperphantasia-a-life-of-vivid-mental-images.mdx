Hyperphantasia is a trait where people can picture scenes so vividly they feel like a movie. About 5–6% of people have it, the opposite being aphantasia where no mental images form. The article shares a first‑person story, research on measuring imagery, how it helps with memory and creativity, and the emotional challenges when vivid memories become overwhelming. It highlights new scientific studies and online communities for those who experience hyper‑imagery.
