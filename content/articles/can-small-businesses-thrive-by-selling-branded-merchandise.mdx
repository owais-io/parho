Small shops are turning to branded items—from tote bags to condoms—to build loyalty and boost sales. In London, bakeries and cafés sell hats, socks and even novelty condoms, while chains like Greggs offer themed accessories. The trend started with free tote bags but has expanded as customers see merch as a status symbol and a way to show support. Production is cheap, so it’s a low‑risk way for businesses to create buzz and extra income.
