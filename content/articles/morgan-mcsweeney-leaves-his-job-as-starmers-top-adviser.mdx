Morgan McSweeney, Keir Starmer’s chief of staff, quit after the UK party’s decision to name Peter Mandelson, a former minister linked to Jeffrey Epstein, as U.S. ambassador. McSweeney said he took full responsibility for advising the appointment and that it damaged trust in politics. Starmer praised him but said the scandal will keep pressuring him. Labour MPs split, some blaming McSweeney, others defending his role. The move weakens Starmer’s inner circle.
