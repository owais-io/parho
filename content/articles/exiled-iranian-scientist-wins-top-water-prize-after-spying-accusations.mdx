Kaveh Madani, a civil‑engineering expert from Iran who fled the country after the Revolutionary Guards accused him of spying, was honoured with the Stockholm water prize. His research shows that water shortages are often caused by bad management, not just climate change. He has warned that wars, like the one now raging in the Middle East, make water crises worse and that politics can’t solve a lack of water. The award celebrates his risky work and global outreach.
