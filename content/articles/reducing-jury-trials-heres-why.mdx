Most jury trials are slow, expensive, and only a few countries still use them. A jury gives a verdict but no explanation, making it hard to review the decision. The author argues for panels that include judges, lawyers, or experts so there’s better reasoning behind verdicts. He also points out how a single juror’s sickness can waste time, highlighting the system’s inefficiency.
