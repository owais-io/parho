Quebec Premier François Legault surprised everyone by announcing his resignation. He will stay on until the Coalition Avenir Québec picks a new leader, but the party is already struggling and could lose all seats in the upcoming November election. Legault’s departure follows months of turmoil, including resignations over a doctors’ pay law, a costly online portal scandal, and controversial secularism rules. The move leaves Quebec facing an uncertain political future.
