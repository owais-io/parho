The U.S. operation that ousted Venezuela’s President Maduro has threatened Cuba’s financial lifeline, as the two countries have traded oil and security help for decades. Cuba, already in debt and hit by frequent blackouts, now faces a sharp drop in Venezuelan aid. While Cuban leaders resist U.S. pressure and plan to keep the regime, the island’s future looks uncertain amid growing economic hardship and political tension.
