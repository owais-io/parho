British manufacturers saw a strong start to 2026 after January’s PMI hit 51.8, the best since August 2024. New export orders rose for the first time in four years, with Europe, the US and China driving demand. The data suggests the economy is picking up, making the Bank of England likely to hold rates at 3.75%. Rising costs still weigh on firms.
