A federal judge has ordered that prosecutors cannot look at any documents or devices seized from Washington Post journalist Hannah Natanson after a raid on her home. The ruling will stay in place until early February, when a court hearing will decide whether to return the material. The Post says the seizure harms her ability to protect sources and threatens press freedom. Lawyers argue the government has no legal basis to hold the data.
