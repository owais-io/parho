The Trump team just released new eating rules that say people should focus on real, whole foods and cut back on sugary, processed snacks. They call for more protein, healthy fats, fruits, veggies and whole grains, and less refined carbs and added sugar. The brief at the White House was led by the health and agriculture secretaries. The new guide is shorter than before and uses a new upside‑down pyramid picture. The goal is to help stop chronic illnesses.
