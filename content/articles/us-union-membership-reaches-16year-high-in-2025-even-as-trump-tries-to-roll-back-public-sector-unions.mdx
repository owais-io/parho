Union contracts covering workers hit a 16‑year high in 2025, with 16.5 million people in contracts—up from 16 million the year before. That’s about 11.2 % of all U.S. workers, and 10 % of workers are union members. Public approval of unions is rising, while the Trump administration is pushing to cancel deals for about 1 million federal staff. Still, the numbers show growth. Workers say they feel more secure and can bargain for better conditions.
