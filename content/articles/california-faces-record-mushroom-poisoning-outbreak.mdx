California’s wet winter has unleashed a surge of deadly mushrooms, especially the death cap, leading to four deaths and 40 hospitalizations – far more than usual. The outbreak mainly hit the Salinas area, where many mushroom pickers are from Mexico. Health officials have issued a multilingual warning, urging people to be cautious, avoid foraging alone, and only buy mushrooms from trusted stores. Climate change is helping rare toxic species appear in new places.
