María Corina Machado, a key Venezuelan opposition figure, was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in Oslo but could not attend the ceremony because she is still hiding from the Maduro regime. She said she will keep fighting for a free Venezuela. Supporters, including Latin American presidents and a famous pianist, attended the event to cheer her on. The award sparked debate because some critics say Machado has supported military action against Maduro, which conflicts with the idea of a “peace” prize.
