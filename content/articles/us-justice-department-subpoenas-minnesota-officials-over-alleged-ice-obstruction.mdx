The Justice Department has sent subpoenas to several top Minnesota leaders—including the governor, the mayor of Minneapolis, the state attorney general, and county attorneys—to investigate claims that they tried to block federal Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agents. Officials say the subpoenas are political pressure and refuse to be intimidated. Legal experts say the accusations are weak. The case comes amid a recent lawsuit by the state’s attorney general and a court order limiting ICE tactics.
