Nurses in New York have been on strike for the biggest nurses walkout in the city’s history, demanding more staff, safer hospitals, and better benefits. They’ve been picketing in sub‑zero temperatures while leaders like Mayor Eric Adams and Senator Bernie Sanders show support. Hospitals are hiring temporary nurses and complain the union’s demands are too high, especially given hefty CEO salaries. Negotiations are set to resume soon.
