For a decade, Liverpool and Manchester City were the faces of English football. Their battles drew fans and defined new tactics. But now the match no longer feels like a big showdown. Both teams are changing squads and styles, moving away from the high‑pressing, possession game that once set them apart. With new signings and a crowded calendar, they’re struggling to find a fresh identity, and the rivalry has faded.
