Tricolor Holdings, a bankrupt auto‑lender that helped people buy cars with low credit, is now facing criminal charges. CEO Daniel Chu and several executives are accused of a long‑running fraud that tricked banks and investors. They allegedly lied about loan data, double‑pledged collateral, and stole more than $6 million from the company. The scheme caused the firm to owe nearly $900 million, leading to its Chapter 7 bankruptcy. The case hurts banks, investors, employees and customers.
