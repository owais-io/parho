Tennessee’s Rep. Andy Ogles says Muslims don’t belong and keeps winning the 5th‑district seat, even though the area has a large Muslim community. He’s faced fraud accusations and inflammatory remarks. The district was reshaped by gerrymandering into a safe Republican seat, so the real contest is a low‑turnout primary where a few votes decide the outcome. Ogles’s case shows how gerrymandering and plurality primaries let unrepresentative politicians stay in office, and how ranked‑choice voting could help.
