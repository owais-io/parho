Trump has moved to take control of money, agencies, and foreign policy that Congress normally manages. He fired heads of independent agencies, cut the Department of Education, stopped spending bills, and used emergency powers to impose tariffs. He also raided Venezuela’s former president without telling Congress. Republicans are split, and many of these actions face legal challenges and may reach the Supreme Court.
