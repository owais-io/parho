Ian Balding, a celebrated British racehorse trainer who trained the champion Mill Reef, died at 87. Over four decades he won 1,755 flat races, including 123 top‑class Group wins. His biggest moment was guiding Mill Reef to win the 1971 Derby and the Paris Arc, a rare double that tested his skill. Born in New Jersey, he moved to England as a child, ran the Kingsclere yard until 2002, and passed it to his son Andrew.
