China said it would cancel a high‑level UK‑China trade meeting after a UK trade minister visited Taiwan in June. Beijing sees any contact with Taiwan as a violation of its claim over the island. UK diplomats rushed to keep the talks going, and the meeting took place in September, where both sides hoped to lock in about £1 billion of trade deals. The incident highlighted the tense diplomatic dance around Taiwan and China’s strong reaction to outside engagement.
