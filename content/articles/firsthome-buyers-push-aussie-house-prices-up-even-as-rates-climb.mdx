Australian first‑home buyers are driving up prices for affordable homes, even as the Reserve Bank hikes interest rates. In Brisbane, one‑bedroom units can now climb $20,000 a week, and two‑bedroom apartments are exceeding budgets. Investors are also bidding, taking loans despite warnings about tax cuts. Banks have cut mortgage rates, keeping borrowing cheap, so demand stays high. Economists say prices will keep rising unless rates jump sharply.
