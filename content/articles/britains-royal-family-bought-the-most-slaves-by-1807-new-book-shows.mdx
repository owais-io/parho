New book The Crown’s Silence shows the British monarchy was the biggest buyer of enslaved Africans by 1807, buying 13,000 men for £900,000. It reveals the crown owned slaves in the Caribbean until 1831 and used them as naval workers. Even after Britain banned the trade, freed Africans were forced into apprenticeships or the army. The navy also protected slavers and helped the trade for Crown profit.
