Cancer, the crab, is high in the southern sky after sunset. Its star cluster, M44, the beehive cluster, is hidden among faint stars. To find it, look between Gemini’s bright twins Castor and Pollux and Leo’s sickle shape. The cluster sits in the middle of Cancer, covering an area three times the Moon’s size. A dark, light‑free sky and 20 minutes of eye‑adjustment help you see it.
