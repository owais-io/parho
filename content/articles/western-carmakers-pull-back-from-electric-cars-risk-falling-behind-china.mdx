Western carmakers are pulling back from electric cars and focusing on gasoline engines. After China’s cheap EVs like BYD and Leapmotor take market share in Europe and the U.S., these automakers worry about profit, but experts warn that the retreat could make them irrelevant by 2035. Governments are easing rules that would ban diesel and petrol cars, adding uncertainty. The result: the U.S., U.K., and Europe risk losing the race to China.
