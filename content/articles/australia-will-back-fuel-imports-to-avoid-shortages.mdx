Australia will use public money to back extra fuel shipments so people don’t run out. After a month of high prices and empty pumps, Prime Minister Albanese said the government can now help private importers buy more petrol, diesel and fertiliser. The plan is aimed at rural stations that struggle for stock. The move is a precaution while the Middle East war keeps prices high. The government will use Export Finance Australia to offer loans and insurance for these purchases.
