British Columbia will stop changing clocks and keep daylight saving time all year. The province will adopt a new Pacific time zone, matching the Yukon, while still letting local towns pick their own times. Most residents want this permanent change, but business groups worry it could hurt cross‑border trade. The move reflects frustration with U.S. time practices and aims to simplify schedules for the province.
