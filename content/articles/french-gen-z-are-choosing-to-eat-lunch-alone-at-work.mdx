Recent research shows almost a third of French workers under 25 now eat lunch alone at work, a sharp rise from older groups. In France, sharing a meal at lunch has long been a workplace tradition, but many young people say group lunches feel forced and even “patriarchal.” Some faced dismissal for refusing. The writer applauds the move toward solo dining, linking it to better mental health and independence.
