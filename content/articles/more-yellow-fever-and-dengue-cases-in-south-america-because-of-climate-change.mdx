More yellow fever and dengue cases are popping up in South America because the planet is getting hotter. Mosquitoes that carry these diseases thrive in warm, wet conditions, and they’re spreading into new places like Europe. Brazil is hit hardest, with millions of dengue cases and thousands of deaths. Health leaders at the COP30 meeting in Belém launched a plan, backed by $300 million, to help countries track and fight the diseases. Experts say we need stronger climate and health action now.
