Australia is facing a crisis in its cultural scene after the cancellation of the 2026 Adelaide writers’ week and other controversies. The author argues that cultural leaders—editors, directors, and board members—must actively protect free speech, even when it means hosting uncomfortable voices. He cites examples like Randa Abdel‑Fattah and Thomas Friedman, showing the tension between cancel culture and democratic dialogue. Governments should appoint capable, courageous custodians who can manage these tough decisions.
