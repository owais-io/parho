A new study of 11,000 babies in New Hampshire found that drinking water contaminated with Pfas chemicals—called “forever chemicals”—could almost double infant deaths and raise the rates of pre‑term and low‑birth‑weight babies. The research shows that cleaning up Pfas is cheaper than the health and productivity costs caused by the pollution. It highlights the need for stricter water‑quality rules.
