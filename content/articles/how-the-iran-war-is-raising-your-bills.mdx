The war in Iran has sent shockwaves through the UK economy, making mortgages, energy, fuel and groceries cost more. Mortgage rates have jumped, cutting a lot of new‑home deals out of reach, and the Bank of England may raise rates instead of cutting them. Energy bills are climbing, heating‑oil prices have doubled, and petrol and diesel are expected to hit record highs. Food prices and holiday fares could rise as well, squeezing household budgets.
