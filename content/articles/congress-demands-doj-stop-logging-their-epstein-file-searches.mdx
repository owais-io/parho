Members of Congress are demanding that the Department of Justice stop logging every file they open while reviewing the newly released Jeffrey Epstein documents. The Justice Department says the logs protect victim privacy, but lawmakers call the practice spying and a breach of the separation of powers. They want an investigation and a clean review process free from monitoring. The issue highlights concerns over privacy, oversight and the role of the DOJ in congressional investigations.
