Highway 1 in Big Sur, a scenic coastal road, finally reopened after a years‑long closure caused by landslides. State crews spent $82.6 million fixing a 75‑mile stretch that had been shut since 2023, using remote‑controlled machines to move huge amounts of rock. The opening means tourists can again drive the famous drive, helping local businesses and families who have waited for the road to return. Officials say the repair work was tough but needed.
