A vape‑shop blaze next to Glasgow Central station revealed how quickly historic buildings can catch fire. The incident echoes earlier fires at the Glasgow School of Art and other heritage sites. City leaders say protecting heritage and keeping people safe can be done together, but many owners still neglect maintenance. Plans are underway to turn empty old buildings into homes and restaurants, though costs and safety rules remain tough.
