Sarah Mullally has become the Church of England’s first woman archbishop, but her job is huge. She must end the abuse‑scandal cycle and establish independent safeguarding. She also has to reassure LGBTQ+ members about same‑sex marriage while keeping the worldwide church united. In a secular society she will tackle social‑care failures and counter rising Christian nationalism, using her NHS background to guide reforms.
