A new report shows the NHS still makes costly medical mistakes, costing about £3.6 billion a year. The public accounts committee blasted the Department of Health and NHS England for ignoring warnings for over two decades, especially in maternity care. Legal claims have quadrupled to £60 billion, and complaints are seen as confusing and slow. The report urges quicker apologies, clearer complaints, and more open communication to cut costs and keep patients safe.
