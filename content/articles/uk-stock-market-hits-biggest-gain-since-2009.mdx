The FTSE 100 rose 21.5% in 2025, its biggest annual jump since 2009, thanks mainly to mining, defence and banking stocks. Companies like Fresnillo and Rolls‑Royce saw huge gains, while the wider market was up nearly 20%. London outperformed Wall Street, which finished the year up 17%. Other highlights include Eurostar delays, new US ETFs, and a weaker dollar. Overall, 2025 was a strong year for UK shares.
