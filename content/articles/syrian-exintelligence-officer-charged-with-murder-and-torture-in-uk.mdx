A 58‑year‑old former Syrian air‑force intelligence officer who fled to the UK has been charged with murder and torture as crimes against humanity, the first such case in England and Wales. The charges stem from the brutal 2011 crackdown on protests that sparked the civil war. He will appear in Westminster magistrates court on Tuesday, with lawyers asking to keep his name hidden. The case shows the UK’s “no safe haven” policy.
