EU regulators raided the Chinese e‑commerce company Temu’s office in Dublin to investigate whether it received unfair government help. The raid came after the company was already under scrutiny for selling illegal items on its platform. Temu, with 116 million EU customers, says it has systems to vet sellers and remove unsafe products. The probe is part of the EU’s effort to stop companies that benefit from government subsidies.
