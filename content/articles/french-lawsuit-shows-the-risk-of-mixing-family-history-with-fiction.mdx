French historian Cécile Desprairies is facing a defamation suit from her brother and cousin over her 2024 novel *La Propagandiste*, which portrays her mother as a Nazi collaborator. The family says the book has no evidence and defames their memory, demanding it be pulled from shelves. Desprairies, who blends autobiography and fiction—a style called autofiction—argues that the law protects only living people, so the case may fail. Verdict is due 17 March.
