Jesús, a 40‑year‑old DACA recipient, remembers his Mexican father’s 1999 deportation. As a teen, he suddenly became the family’s main provider, facing money worries, grief, and confusion. The article follows others—Yara and Molina—who lost parents to deportation and now battle anxiety, depression, and trauma. A child psychiatrist explains how family separation can cause lasting psychological and physical harm.
