The UK government dropped a plan to postpone 30 local elections in England, leaving councils with just 11 weeks to get ready. Local leaders say the decision is chaotic, unfair, and undermines democracy. They warn the scramble adds pressure on staff and voters amid ongoing council reorganisations. Some leaders blame the government’s back‑and‑forth for eroding public trust.
