A speech by Irish MP Thomas Gould in parliament sounded oddly Jamaican, and videos of it went viral. Linguists say the similarity is not due to Irish influence, but to settlers from southwestern England who also came to Jamaica in the 1600s. The episode highlights how colonial history still shapes accents and shows why people sometimes prefer European links over African ones.
