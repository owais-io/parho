Sir Patrick Duffy, a Labour MP who served in the Royal Navy during WWII, died at 105. He spent most of his 29 years in Parliament on the opposition side but was a respected defence and NATO expert. Born in Wigan to Irish parents, he studied economics, taught at Leeds and in the US, and later served as NATO assembly president.
