Researchers followed 958 healthy people, mostly around 70 years old, for two years. Those who took a daily multivitamin showed a small slowdown in biological ageing – about four months over the study – measured by two DNA‑methylation clocks. The effect was modest and seen mainly in people who aged faster at the start. Experts say the benefits are unclear and a balanced diet is still key. More research is needed to see if the small change translates into real health benefits.
