Western Australia’s north coast faced Cyclone Narelle, a strong tropical storm that tore roofs off houses, flooded roofs, and left hundreds of homes without power. Heavy rain hit Perth and many schools shut, while roads were closed. Residents in towns like Exmouth and Denham were moved to shelters and now cope with damaged evacuation centres. The storm weakened but still produced gusts up to 220 km/h and brought a flood warning from north to south.
