Finance minister Rachel Reeves must convince MPs that the £6bn a year spent on special educational needs (Send) won’t wreck the country’s budget. MPs worry the cost is hidden from the official budget and could eat into the government’s savings. Reeves plans to delay a decision until next year, while analysts say the money could be taken from future budgets or add to borrowing.
