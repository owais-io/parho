Harry Roberts, who shot three police officers in 1966, has died at 89. He spent 48 years in prison after the killings, which shocked Britain and led to a massive 96‑day manhunt. The case sparked debate over sentencing, parole and police safety. He was released on licence in 2014, but his death has reignited criticism of the justice system.
