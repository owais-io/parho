A loophole lets big land buyers keep the price they pay out of Scotland's public land register, hiding more than £300m of Highland estate deals. Companies like Discovery Land, Oxygen Conservation, and the John Muir Trust have used it to avoid listing prices. Land‑reform groups want the government to close the gap and force prices to be shown. The Scottish government says it is looking into the issue.
