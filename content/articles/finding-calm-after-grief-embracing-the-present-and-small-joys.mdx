After my mom died a year ago, I was overwhelmed by grief and the hectic holiday rush. The author talks about a Buddhist idea called “growing down,” which means slowing down and being present. She says she no longer rushes to buy gifts or plan events, and instead enjoys simple moments like coffee, music, and family smiles. The piece invites readers to let go of stress and appreciate everyday joys.
