Indian railways minister wants to remove the bandhgala jacket from staff uniforms, calling it a colonial reminder. The jacket, a high‑collared piece worn by grooms and used by rail workers since the 1800s, has Mughal and Rajput roots. A designer says it’s an Indian garment that evolved over centuries, even though it borrowed some British tailoring. The debate shows how India is reshaping its cultural symbols.
