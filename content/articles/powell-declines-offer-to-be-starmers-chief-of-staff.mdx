Jonathan Powell, who advises UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer on national security, has turned down an offer to become the new chief of staff after Morgan McSweeney quit. He said he prefers to return to his private mediation firm, Inter‑Medium, and may leave Downing Street by year‑end. Downing Street insists he will stay on as national security adviser. Powell’s history includes a role under Tony Blair and influence over Starmer’s foreign policy.
