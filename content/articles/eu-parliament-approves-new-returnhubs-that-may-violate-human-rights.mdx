EU Parliament members voted Thursday to create new return‑hubs that could hold deported migrants outside the EU for up to two years. The law lets authorities detain people longer, charge them for blocking returns, and ban them from entering the bloc permanently. The move has sparked anger from human‑rights groups, who warn it could create “black holes” where detainees’ rights are ignored. The vote reflects a rightward shift in Brussels.
