In the wake of Iran’s January protests, Dr. Ahmadi and a network of over 80 doctors gathered data from hospitals, morgues and graveyards across 12 provinces. They find that the true death toll could be more than 30,000—far above the government’s 3,000 and the HRANA’s 6,000 figures. Witnesses report bodies being moved in ice‑cream vans, buried in mass graves, and many corpses never reaching official forensic facilities.
