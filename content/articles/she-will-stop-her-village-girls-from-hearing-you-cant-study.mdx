Naushaba Roonjho was the first girl in her Sindh village to pass Pakistan’s high‑school exam, but her family told her to stay home. She married at 17 and then left the household to become a community health worker. Though her parents cut her off, she built a home with her husband, started a girls’ school, leads local projects, mentors young women, and plans to run for council to bring better services.
