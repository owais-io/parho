U.S. and Israeli airstrikes on Iran have killed at least 742 civilians, including 176 children, according to the US‑based NGO Human Rights Activists. Iran’s Red Crescent reports 787 deaths, and Norway‑based Hengaw says the toll is over 1,500. A near‑total internet blackout makes it hard to confirm numbers. Legal experts say the strikes were unlawful, and the war could last weeks or more. Iran’s control of the Strait of Hormuz has shut the oil route, driving prices up.
