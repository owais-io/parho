The Pittsburgh Post‑Gazette will shut down, with its last edition coming out on May 3. Block Communications, the owner, said the paper has lost more than $350 million over 20 years and can no longer keep operating. A recent court decision that strengthened the paper’s union contract added to the costs. Block also recently closed the Pittsburgh City Paper, citing weak finances. The company apologized for the impact on the community.
