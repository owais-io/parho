Two friends from different backgrounds – Peter, a 34‑year‑old London student who used to be a civil servant, and Akshat, a 43‑year‑old risk manager from India – met at a London restaurant. They ate spring rolls and dumplings and chatted about the UK’s immigration rules, how the government treats newcomers, and the country’s colonial past. Though they come from different political views, they found common ground in their desire for fairness and open conversation.
