In São Paulo, a new law lets people bury their dogs and cats in family graves, inspired by a mixed‑breed dog named Bob who stayed beside his owner’s grave for ten years. Governor Tarcísio de Freitas signed the bill, calling it the “Bob Coveiro” or “Gravedigger” law. The law acknowledges the emotional bond between owners and pets and offers a cheaper alternative to cremation. Similar laws already exist in Rio and Santa Catarina.
