The Indiana Republican lawmakers voted no on a new congressional map that would have added two GOP seats, a blow to President Trump who had been pressuring them with threats that the state would lose federal money if the map failed. The backlash showed that Trump’s influence isn’t absolute, as lawmakers cited intimidation. The loss hurts the Republican plan to boost seats nationwide, complicating redistricting fights in other states and raising questions about the future of the Voting Rights Act.
