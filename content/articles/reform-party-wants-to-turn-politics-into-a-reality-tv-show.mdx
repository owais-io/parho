Reform UK’s leaders, Nige Farage and Honest Bob, are turning politics into a reality‑TV style campaign. They run YouTube shows that offer things like “cheapest petrol” prizes and fake checks to win energy bills, and they promise to cut VAT and frack for gas. The article satirises their personality‑driven stunts, the small audience at their press event, and their get‑rich‑quick schemes, suggesting the party is more about marketing than real policy.
