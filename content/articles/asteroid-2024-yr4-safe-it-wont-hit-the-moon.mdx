Scientists have found that asteroid 2024 YR4, a 100‑metre rock spotted last year, will safely miss the Moon in 2032. Early estimates had suggested up to a 4.3% chance of a lunar impact, raising fears that debris could damage satellites. New observations with the James Webb Space Telescope and other telescopes sharpened its orbit, showing it will pass more than 20,000 km away. The asteroid poses no threat to Earth or space infrastructure.
