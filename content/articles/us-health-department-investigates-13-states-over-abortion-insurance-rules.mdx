The Health Department is investigating 13 states that require health insurance plans to cover abortion services, saying they may violate the Weldon amendment, which bars federal funds for programs that discriminate against providers who refuse to cover or refer abortions. The states are California, Colorado, Delaware, Illinois, Maine, Maryland, Massachusetts, Minnesota, New Jersey, New York, Oregon, Vermont and Washington. Leaders and advocacy groups have sharply criticized the probe, calling it a waste of money and an attack on abortion rights.
