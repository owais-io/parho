Supreme Court judges allow a Republican congressman from Illinois to sue the state over a law that lets mail‑in ballots be counted up to two weeks after Election Day if they were postmarked before the deadline. The 7‑2 decision means the candidate has a legitimate interest in voting rules. The ruling is part of a broader fight over mail‑in voting that Trump and other Republicans keep pushing.
