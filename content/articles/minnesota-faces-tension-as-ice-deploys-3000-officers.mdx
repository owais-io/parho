More than 3,000 ICE officers have been sent to Minnesota, causing fear and protests. People keep passports, avoid streets, and worry about their kids. Schools see fewer students, some run online. Hospitals are empty as families fear police violence. Residents feel unsafe but also proud of community support. They record encounters, use whistles, and volunteer. The surge shows how the state is being tested for tolerance.
