My parents had dementia, but when I read aloud to them, they still understood stories. Despite being silent, they could follow a plot, remember words, and respond to reading. The author’s experience shows that reading can spark communication and reduce symptoms. A charity’s study confirms that reading aloud helps people with dementia feel better and stay connected. He warns that we shouldn't assume a silent patient is lost, and that care workers and family should keep trying to engage.
