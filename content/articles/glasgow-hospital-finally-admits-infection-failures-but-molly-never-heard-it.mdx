The NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde admitted that a contaminated water system at Queen Elizabeth University Hospital caused serious infections in 84 child cancer patients, including the 19‑year‑old Molly Cuddihy who later died. Families say the board denied problems for years, dismissing whistleblowers and opening the hospital before it was ready. The public inquiry ends with a partial apology, but parents demand full accountability and real change.
