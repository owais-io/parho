Immigration agents started a sweep in Charlotte, North Carolina, last Saturday. Masked officials raided an east‑Charlotte church where a man was arrested while church members were gardening. Residents felt scared and local leaders said the actions were unnecessary. Other people were stopped in yards, and a citizen had his car broken into but was released after proving he was American. The mayor and some state officials warned of fear and called the moves a violation of rights.
