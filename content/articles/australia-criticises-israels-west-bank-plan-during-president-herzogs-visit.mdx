When Israeli President Isaac Herzog visited Australia, the country joined a global backlash against Israel's plan to tighten control over the West Bank. Australian politicians, including Prime Minister Anthony Albanese, raised the death of aid worker Zomi Frankcom and criticised the move. Senator David Pocock said inviting Herzog was wrong, sparking protests and police action. The story highlights Australia’s firm stance on Israeli policies.
