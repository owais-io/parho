Angela Rayner, Labour leader, says the party must act fast to fix Britain’s cost‑of‑living crisis and defend workers. She highlights the party’s recent wins on employment rights, rent protection and leasehold reforms, but warns that people still see Labour as part of the status quo. Rayner urges stronger battles against entrenched interests, more local power, fair migration rules and a state that protects ordinary people. The party’s future depends on delivering real, quick change.
