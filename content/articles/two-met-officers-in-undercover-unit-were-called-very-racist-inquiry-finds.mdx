A whistle‑blower named Peter Francis said two senior officers who ran a Met undercover unit called "spycops" were extremely racist. He told a public inquiry that the men used black slurs and talked about the Stephen Lawrence family as if they were controlled by others. Francis also says the unit spied on thousands of left‑wing activists from 1968 to 2010. The Metropolitan Police have apologized, and the inquiry is still looking into why the unit targeted the Lawrence family.
