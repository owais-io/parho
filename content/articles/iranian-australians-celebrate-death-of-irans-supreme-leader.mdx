People of the Iranian community in Australia, many refugees, are celebrating the death of Iran's supreme leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. They gather in Melbourne, Sydney, and Canberra, shouting praise for Israel and US. They remember victims of the regime and hope for a freer Iran. Some worry about retaliation, but many celebrate the possibility of change. The diaspora feels relief, but still unsure if a new government will appear.
