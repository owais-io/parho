Iran's recent missile strikes and the tense situation in the Strait of Hormuz have pushed oil prices higher. The strait handles about a fifth of the world's seaborne oil, so any blockage could raise prices further. Higher fuel costs mean more for groceries and transport, putting pressure on households worldwide. Markets have steadied, but inflation worries and a possible wider conflict keep the economy on edge.
