Former special counsel Jack Smith told a House committee that he pursued criminal charges against former President Donald Trump because the law requires everyone to be held accountable. He defended his work on the 2020 election interference case, saying the evidence shows Trump pushed to overturn the results and that the records he obtained helped build a timeline. Republicans accused him of political bias and questioned the use of phone‑call data. Smith said he was acting on evidence, not politics.
