Raphael Bostic, the first African‑American and openly gay president of a regional Federal Reserve bank, announced he will step down in February. His departure comes after President Trump pushed for lower interest rates and tried to influence Fed officials. Bostic’s exit creates a spot on the Fed’s 12‑member policy committee. The Fed will search for a new president, while Trump has already replaced a governor on an interim basis. Fed Chair Powell praised Bostic’s steady leadership.
