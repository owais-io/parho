Two former police officers testified in the high court that former Sinn Féin leader Gerry Adams ran the Irish Republican Army during the Troubles. They claim he was behind three bombings that injured them and are seeking one‑pound damages. Adams denies membership and leadership, but retired RUC detective Tim Hanley and another witness say intelligence proved he was the IRA’s chief until the mid‑2000s. The case resumes on Monday.
