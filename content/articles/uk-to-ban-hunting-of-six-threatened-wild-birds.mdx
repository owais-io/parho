The UK government plans to stop hunters from shooting six threatened bird species, including woodcock, pochard, goldeneye, and pintail ducks. The ban will extend the protected period across England, Wales and Scotland, with changes to the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981. A separate consultation may add the wood pigeon to the list to protect crops and welfare. The move aims to reverse steep population losses and keep Britain’s iconic birds alive.
