James Cullen, the chief of staff to New South Wales Premier Chris Minns, is fighting a court order that would make him appear before a parliamentary inquiry into leaked confidential minutes about the sale of Rosehill racecourse. He argues that the 1901 law allowing the government to compel witnesses is unconstitutional and could turn the court into a political arena. The Attorney General supports his case, but the court has issued the warrant and is considering whether to enforce it.
