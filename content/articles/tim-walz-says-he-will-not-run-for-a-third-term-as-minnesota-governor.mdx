Minnesota Gov. Tim Walz, a Democrat who ran for U.S. vice‑president in 2024, said he will not seek a third term. He blamed a spike in fraud at state social‑services programs that drew criticism from Republicans, including Donald Trump. Walz said he could not spend all his time on campaigning and instead wants to protect Minnesotans from scammers and bigotry. He will leave the race and focus on public service.
