Oil prices are climbing to nearly $110 a barrel after attacks on Middle‑East infrastructure, and Pacific island leaders are worried their economies, which depend almost entirely on imported diesel, could run short. Samoa’s prime minister asked New Zealand for extra fuel, while Tonga’s government is sharing intelligence with Australia and New Zealand. Papua New Guinea fears storage limits, and Fiji says there’s no need for panic buying. All the islands are bracing for higher costs that could hurt tourism and basic services.
