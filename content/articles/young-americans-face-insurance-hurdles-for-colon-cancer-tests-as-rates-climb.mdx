Young Americans are finding it hard to get insurance approval for colonoscopies after symptoms show up, even though colon cancer is rising in people under 45. The Affordable Care Act only covers preventive tests for those over 45, so diagnostic procedures are often denied or delayed, costing patients thousands. A 35‑year‑old paid $2,000 out of pocket after a last‑minute denial. Experts say earlier screening, stool tests, and more research are needed.
