A Kansas Native American tribe is in trouble after its development arm signed a nearly $30 million federal deal to design ICE detention centers. Other tribal groups slammed the move, saying it goes against the tribe’s values and history of forced removal. The tribe’s chair apologized, said the contract might be dropped, and fired the leaders who made the deal. The situation highlights the tension between money and culture for tribes.
