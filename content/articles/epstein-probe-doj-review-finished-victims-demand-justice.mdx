U.S. prosecutors say the review of Jeffrey Epstein’s sex‑trafficking case is finished, but victims still want full justice. Todd Blanche, the deputy attorney general, said the DOJ can’t just create evidence; they must use what’s there. Victim lawyer Jennifer Freeman slammed the department’s handling of the files as messy and accused it of hiding names. New documents suggest other men may have been involved, raising questions about broader investigations.
