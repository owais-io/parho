Boohoo Group raised £35m from investors to cut debt and support its turnaround plan, which includes cost cuts, selling a distribution centre, and turning Debenhams into an online marketplace for other brands. The move follows a £39m raise and comes amid heavy competition from rivals like Shein. The new funding could reignite tensions with Mike Ashley, owner of Frasers Group, who holds a stake in Debenhams and has previously opposed the company’s changes. Analysts warn the group still faces debt covenants.
