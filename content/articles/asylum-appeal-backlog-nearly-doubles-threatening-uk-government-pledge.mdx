More than 80,000 people waiting to have their asylum decisions reviewed is almost double what it was a year ago, a 91% jump that could break Prime Minister Keir Starmer’s promise to shut asylum hotels by 2029. The average wait to hear an appeal is now 63 weeks, and two‑thirds of appeals overturn the original refusal. Most of those waiting live in Home Office‑run hotels, adding pressure to the already strained system.
