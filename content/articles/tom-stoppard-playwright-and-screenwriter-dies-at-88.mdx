Tom Stoppard, the famous British playwright who wrote the absurdist hit *Rosencrantz and Guildenstern Are Dead*, died at 88. Born in 1937 in what is now the Czech Republic, he escaped the war to Britain, where he turned from journalism to playwriting. His works, including *Arcadia*, *The Coast of Utopia*, and the film script *Shakespeare in Love*, blend clever dialogue with political themes. Stoppard received a CBE, knighthood and the Order of Merit.
