Trevoh Chalobah was almost sold by Chelsea and spent a season on loan at Crystal Palace, where he proved himself and earned a place in the first team. He helped Chelsea beat PSG in the Club World Cup final and made his England debut. Now he is a regular defender, but Chelsea’s new owners keep looking for profit by selling academy players, causing fan frustration. Chalobah’s revival shows that investing in home‑grown talent can pay off better than constant buying.
