Rights groups in Hungary say it is risky to have Daria Boyarskaya, a former interpreter for Vladimir Putin, lead the OSCE team that will watch next month’s parliamentary election. They fear she might share sensitive info with Russia, especially as leader Viktor Orbán has close ties to Moscow. Boyarskaya says she has no links to Russian intelligence and follows OSCE rules. The OSCE says she was chosen for her experience, not for political reasons.
