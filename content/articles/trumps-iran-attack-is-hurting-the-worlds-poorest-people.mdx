The U.S. and Israel’s recent attacks on Iran sparked a chain reaction that closed the Hormuz Strait, a key oil route, and sent prices for fuel, food, and goods higher. The resulting energy shock has pushed aid costs up, disrupted shipping, and worsened food shortages in places like Sudan, Gaza, and parts of Asia. With U.S. aid cuts, many already‑vulnerable communities face famine, urging an end to the conflict.
