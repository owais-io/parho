More than 900 people have died in floods across Indonesia, Thailand and Sri Lanka after heavy monsoon rains and a tropical storm caused landslides and flooding. Sri Lanka’s capital and many towns are underwater, and thousands are still missing. Indonesia’s Sumatra island faces huge debris and food shortages, with aid ships arriving slowly. Thailand is giving compensation but faces criticism over its response. Climate change is making these storms worse.
