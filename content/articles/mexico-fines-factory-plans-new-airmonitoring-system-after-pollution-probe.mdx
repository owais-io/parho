Investigations by The Guardian and a Mexican research lab found dangerous levels of lead, cadmium and arsenic around the Zinc Nacional plant in Monterrey. In response, Mexico’s federal government has fined the company 4.8 million dollars and ordered 24 cleanup actions, including moving some work away from residential areas and building new treatment plants. It is also rolling out a first‑of‑its‑kind air‑monitoring network and tightening pollution rules, while local residents call for full public data.
