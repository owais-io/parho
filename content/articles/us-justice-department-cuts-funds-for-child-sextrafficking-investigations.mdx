The U.S. Department of Justice has slashed money and training for police and prosecutors who fight child sex crimes. A 2025 training conference in Washington, DC, was cancelled, and yearly grants to the Internet Crimes Against Children taskforce were halted. Without these resources, investigators struggle to keep up with new digital tools and feel it’s harder to bring predators to justice. Law‑enforcement officers say the cuts hurt training, funding, and morale.
