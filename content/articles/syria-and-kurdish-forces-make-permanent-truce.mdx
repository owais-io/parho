Syria’s government and the Kurdish‑led Syrian Democratic Forces have agreed to make a fragile ceasefire permanent. The deal will pull fighters from the front, let the SDF join the national army, and bring the Kurdish‑controlled cities of Hasakah and Qamishli under government control. Kurds will keep civil and educational rights, and displaced people can return. The U.S. mediator praised the deal as a historic step toward peace, ending a month of fighting with little bloodshed.
