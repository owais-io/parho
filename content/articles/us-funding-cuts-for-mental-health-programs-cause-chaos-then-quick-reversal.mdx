On Wednesday, the U.S. government abruptly pulled funding from around 2,800 mental‑health and addiction programs, including HIV counseling in Alabama, crisis‑response training in New Hampshire, and child trauma counseling in Tennessee. The move left staff scrambling over payroll, layoffs, and how to keep services running. Later that week, the cuts were reversed, but the experience shocked organizers, showing how fragile these programs are and the big risk to vulnerable people.
