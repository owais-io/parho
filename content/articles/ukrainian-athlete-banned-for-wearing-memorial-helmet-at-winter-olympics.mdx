During the Winter Olympics, Ukrainian skeleton racer Vladyslav Heraskevych was disqualified because he wanted to wear a helmet honoring 24 Ukrainian athletes killed in Russia’s war. The International Olympic Committee (IOC) said it violated its neutrality rules. Ukraine’s leaders and athletes protested, calling the ban a disgrace. The incident also hit other competitors, like a Ukrainian skater who had to cover a war‑related quote and a Haitian team whose uniform was altered.
