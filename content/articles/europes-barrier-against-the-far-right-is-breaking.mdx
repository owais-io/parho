European leaders are struggling to keep the far right at bay. Italy’s new prime minister, Giorgia Meloni, wants the right and centre‑right to unite, while France’s usual front that stops the National Rally is fraying. In the Netherlands and Germany, centre‑right parties have even talked about joining far‑right groups. Even in the European parliament, conservatives broke rules to soften climate laws. The article warns that mixing with the far right could hurt mainstream politics before the 2027 elections.
