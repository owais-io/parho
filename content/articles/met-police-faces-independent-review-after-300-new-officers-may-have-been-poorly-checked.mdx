The UK home secretary has ordered an independent review to find out if the Metropolitan Police let about 300 new officers join without proper background checks from 2016 to 2023. A Met‑run probe called Operation Jorica already flagged problems, citing high‑profile cases like the killer Wayne Couzens and the sexual‑violence officer David Carrick. The inspectorate will look at hiring practices and the force’s efforts to rebuild trust after a series of scandals.
