Health Secretary Wes Streeting has asked experts to examine why so many people are getting diagnosed with mental health conditions and claiming sickness benefits. Since 2019 the number of claims has jumped 1.2 million, especially among young adults. The review will be led by Professor Peter Fonagy and former psychiatric president Sir Simon Wessely, looking at research, patient stories and clinical practice. The aim is to give people accurate diagnoses and help cut the growing welfare bill.
