England’s special‑education services are under huge pressure. The number of children needing extra help is growing, so local councils are borrowing more. By 2030, debts could reach about £18 billion, and 60 councils might be close to bankruptcy. The government wants to change the system to curb costs, but parents fear cuts will delay support. The situation risks a financial crisis for many councils.
