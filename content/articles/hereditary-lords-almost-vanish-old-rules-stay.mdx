A vote in the House of Lords almost finished the long‑running tradition of hereditary peers, but the fight left many old rules in place. Labour promised to end hereditary seats, yet Tory lords fought back, threatening to block other bills. Fifteen peers were converted to life peers, but the Lords still has 842 members, bishops, and a donation system that many see as corrupt. Reform is stalled, raising calls for a new commission and proportional representation.
