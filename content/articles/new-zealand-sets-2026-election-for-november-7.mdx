New Zealand’s prime minister, Christopher Luxon, has fixed the next general election for November 7, 2026. He says the National party will push “fix the basics” and promise a stable economy, lower taxes and more opportunities. Key concerns for voters include the economy and cost of living. Luxon’s coalition has faced backlash over reforms to Māori rights, climate targets and mining, and polling shows Labour close behind or ahead. Labour’s leader, Chris Hipkins, is ready to challenge.
