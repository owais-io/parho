Portuguese Prime Minister‑turned‑EU Council President António Costa told Donald Trump’s team to back off, warning that the U.S. policy plan now tries to help far‑right European parties and could upset the continent’s politics. He said Europe must decide its own future. Analysts say the U.S. is moving from a partnership to a more meddling stance. The shift comes amid other tensions, like a big EU fine on Elon Musk’s X. Europe faces a test of standing up to U.S. influence.
