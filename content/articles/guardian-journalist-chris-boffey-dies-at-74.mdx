Chris Boffey, a veteran British reporter who worked for newspapers like The Guardian, The Mirror, and the Telegraph, died at 74. He led news desks at several national papers and once advised the government’s education secretary. Boffey was known for uncovering big stories, such as crimes by the Wests and the sexual offences of Paul Gadd. He also mentored young reporters and was praised as a sharp, friendly editor. He is survived by his wife and two sons.
