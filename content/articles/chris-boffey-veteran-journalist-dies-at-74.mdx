Chris Boffey, a seasoned reporter who worked for papers from the Sun to the Guardian, died at 74 from cancer. He was famous for quirky newsroom rules, like a 10‑minute limit for war‑zone stories, and for making reporters grab drinks before awards. Boffey covered dangerous stories in the 1980s and the Gulf War, even walking onto a warship gas‑masked on deck. Later he became a news editor, guided young reporters, and briefly advised a Labour education secretary.
