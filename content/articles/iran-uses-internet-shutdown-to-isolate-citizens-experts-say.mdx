Experts say Iran has intentionally cut off most of the internet to keep people from coordinating protests and to hide a violent crackdown. After a brief outage from US or Israeli strikes, the government imposed a near‑total shutdown that lasted almost three weeks in January and is happening again. Mobile phones still work, but without internet or alternative connections many Iranians are cut off, increasing confusion and a feeling of being trapped as borders close.
