A 33‑year‑old Austrian climber is accused of gross‑negligent manslaughter after his girlfriend froze to death near the summit of Austria’s tallest mountain, the Großglockner. Prosecutors say he left her exhausted and unprotected in sub‑zero temperatures to get help. If found guilty, he faces up to three years in prison, and the case raises questions about responsibility on dangerous climbs and safety standards.
