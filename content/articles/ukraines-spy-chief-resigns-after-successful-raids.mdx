Ukraine’s spy chief Vasyl Malyuk has stepped down as head of the Security Service after a year of daring raids against Russia. President Volodymyr Zelenskyy said Malyuk will stay in the agency to run special operations, praising his success against Russian bombers and command figures. Some say the move weakens Ukraine’s intelligence, while others suspect political motives as Zelenskyy refreshes his team. Malyuk’s temporary replacement is Maj Gen Yevhen Khmara.
