The US and European officials are worried that Islamic State (IS) fighters could come back to life as Syrian government troops push into the northeast, where many former IS members are held. The US moved 150 IS fighters out of a Hasakah prison to Iraq and said it could send up to 7,000 troops to stop a breakout. Meanwhile, the new Syrian government has taken control of key prisons and camps, raising fears that IS operatives could regroup.
