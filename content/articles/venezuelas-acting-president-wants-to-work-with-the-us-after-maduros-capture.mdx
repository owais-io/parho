Venezuela’s acting president, Delcy Rodríguez, said she’d like to cooperate with the United States after President Nicolás Maduro and his wife were seized and taken to New York. Rodríguez was sworn in last week and has already asked the armed forces to support her. She changed from a hard‑talking stance to a friendly one, inviting the US to build peace and development together. Trump warned the US might strike again, but many analysts say the regime’s power structure remains unchanged.
