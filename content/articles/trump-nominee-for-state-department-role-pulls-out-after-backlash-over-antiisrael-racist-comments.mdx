Jeremy Carl, a former Trump aide, was nominated to head the State Department’s international organizations office. After a hearing, he faced criticism for anti‑Israel and anti‑Jewish remarks, as well as racist comments. A key Republican senator refused to back him, and Democrats already opposed him. With no unanimous support, Carl withdrew his nomination, ending the controversy. The move was welcomed by Jewish advocacy groups who had called for his rejection.
