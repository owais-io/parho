In recent years, many pro‑Israel groups sued colleges to stop pro‑Palestinian protests, claiming the speech was antisemitic. Courts have ruled that the slogans—like “from the river to the sea” or “globalize the intifada”—are protected First Amendment speech and not hate. The decisions also say that, unless a protest targets a specific person, it can’t be shut down under Title VI. Some cases are still on appeal, but the trend is clear.
