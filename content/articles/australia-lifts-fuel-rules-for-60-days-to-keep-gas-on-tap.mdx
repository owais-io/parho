Australia’s Labor government said it will temporarily lower fuel quality standards for two months, allowing an extra 100 million litres of petrol to enter the market each month. The move aims to improve supply for towns outside major cities as global oil prices rise due to Middle East tensions and the International Energy Agency’s call to release more oil. The change will help keep gas pumps stocked and curb shortages.
