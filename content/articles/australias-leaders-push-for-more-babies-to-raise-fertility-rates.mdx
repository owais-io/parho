Australian Nationals leader Matt Canavan wants the country to have more babies as the fertility rate falls to a record low. He says families should have more children, but the push mirrors a global trend among conservative leaders. Past measures such as baby bonuses and paid leave have mixed results. Real obstacles—housing, childcare and cost—still limit family growth, and experts say true solutions need more than incentives.
