A man charged with hate speech for antisemitic comments at a Sydney anti‑immigration rally was denied bail because police say he was connected to the now‑disbanded neo‑Nazi National Socialist Network. He wore a neo‑Nazi symbol and praised former NSN leaders. The judge cited community safety and the risk of further hate offences. The case comes after new laws outlawing hate groups were passed.
