Australia used to produce most of its own petrol and diesel, but only two refineries remain today. Because of this, we import almost all fuel—mainly from Singapore, South Korea and Malaysia. The war between the US and Israel in Iran has disrupted Middle‑East oil supplies, driving up global prices. A litre of diesel is almost double its cost, and regular petrol has jumped about 40 %. Some stations may run out of fuel, adding to consumer worry.
