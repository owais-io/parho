After Iran was hit, experts warned that oil prices would jump to $100 a barrel and energy bills would rise. The article explains how this would trigger a chain reaction—higher fuel costs raise food prices, which in turn push up everything else. It also points out that we can’t really prepare for these hikes; they’re simply part of the fallout from global instability. The best we can do is use fewer goods.
