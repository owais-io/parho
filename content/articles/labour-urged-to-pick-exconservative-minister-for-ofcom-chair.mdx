UK ministers are pushing Labour to name former Conservative minister Jeremy Wright as the next chair of Ofcom, the media regulator. He is up against Labour peer Margaret Hodge, who many think is the favourite. The post matters because Ofcom is struggling to enforce the Online Safety Act, which tackles harmful content online. A new chair could give the regulator fresh direction and help rebuild trust, but the delay is worrying experts and the public.
