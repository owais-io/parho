Australia’s house prices keep rising, but most people can’t afford them because government policies boost demand. The 5% deposit guarantee, first‑home grants and a generous capital‑gains‑tax discount for investors keep prices higher than incomes. Critics say Labor needs to change these rules so people can buy instead of just invest. The article argues that small tweaks won’t fix the problem; real reform is needed.
