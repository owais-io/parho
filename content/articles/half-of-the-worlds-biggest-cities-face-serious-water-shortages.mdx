A new study shows that 50 % of the 100 largest cities on the planet are under high water stress, with 39 in “extremely high” zones. Cities like Beijing, New York, Delhi and Jakarta are at risk, while others such as Chennai, Tehran and Lagos are drying up. Satellite data reveal many Asian and African cities are losing water, affecting 1.1 billion people. The UN, World Bank and UK government warn that poor management and climate change are driving a global water crisis.
