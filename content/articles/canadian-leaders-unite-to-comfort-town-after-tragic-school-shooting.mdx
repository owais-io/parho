In a small mining town, the Canadian prime minister and opposition leader came together at a candlelit vigil for the eight people killed in a school shooting. They held hands, spoke with families, and promised support. The event showed that politics can set aside differences when communities are hurt. The tragedy was caused by an 18‑year‑old transgender woman who also took her own life. Leaders said Canada would help the town heal and keep children safe.
