Southern Maine’s University of Southern Maine shut a venue for a Palestine‑focused conference after a U.S. sanctioned speaker was to appear. Local lawmakers pressured the school, and officials worried about losing federal money. Organizers say the move silences free speech and want to sue. U.S. sanctions rules are vague, but the Treasury says a speaker can talk as long as they’re not paid. The cancelation has drawn more attention to the event.
