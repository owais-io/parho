The Justice Department has released about 3 million pages from the Jeffrey Epstein investigation, but Democrats say it is still withholding roughly half of the documents it legally must share. Survivors, lawmakers, and watchdog groups accuse the department of heavy redactions and selective disclosure. Congress wants the full record to protect victims and hold powerful people accountable. The release has sparked a fight over transparency and the protection of survivors.
