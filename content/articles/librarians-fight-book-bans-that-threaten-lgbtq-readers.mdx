Librarians across the U.S. are fighting a surge of book bans that target LGBTQ+ titles. Many have been fired or threatened for keeping these books on shelves, and some are suing local governments. Courts have ruled in favor of librarians in Wyoming, Texas, and California, and the California Freedom to Read Act helps protect library collections. The American Library Association tracks over 800 attempts to ban books each year, highlighting a growing fight for free access to information.
