David Hollinger was a fearless mountaineer who climbed some of the world’s toughest peaks, from the Eiger’s north face to the Himalayas. After a career guiding and teaching, he settled in Sheffield and co‑founded a climbing school. A ski‑related fall caused a brain‑stem stroke that left him unable to speak or move. With determination, he later learned to use a powered wheelchair and eye‑tracking computer, helping shape nursing education. He is survived by his wife, children and parents.
