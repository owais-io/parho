The FAA unexpectedly ordered a 10‑day airspace shutdown over El Paso, then lifted the ban within hours. The move left the city’s airport closed, 14 flights cancelled and emergency planes diverted. Local officials, including the mayor, demanded an explanation, saying the decision risked lives and hurt hospitals. The FAA cited “special security reasons” but offered no details. The lack of warning also caused confusion among travelers and disrupted medical shipments.
