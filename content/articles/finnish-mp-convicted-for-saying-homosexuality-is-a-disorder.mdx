The Supreme Court of Finland found Päivi Räsänen, a member of Parliament, guilty of inciting hatred after she re‑posted a 2004 pamphlet that called homosexuality a developmental disorder. She was fined €1,800. The case drew criticism from her own party’s ministers, who say the law is vague and threatens free speech. Räsänen plans to appeal to the European Court of Human Rights. Supporters of Räsänen, including the U.S.-based Alliance Defending Freedom, say the verdict shows censorship.
