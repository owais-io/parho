UK science leaders ask governments to keep investing in food research. They say malnutrition kills millions of children, hurts learning, drives poverty and can spark conflict. Food policy sits between two departments, so funding is unclear. Scientists argue that steady money is needed for new crops, bio‑fortified foods and better farming. Without it, the country risks health, growth and global stability.
