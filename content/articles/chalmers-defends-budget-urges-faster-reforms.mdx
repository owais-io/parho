Australia’s Labor leader, Jim Chalmers, said the May budget will tackle inflation and strengthen the economy, arguing critics who blame the government for rising prices are wrong. He called for quick reforms, especially in tax and productivity, and highlighted a new intergenerational report to guide long‑term planning. With the Reserve Bank likely to raise rates, he stressed keeping costs low while building more homes and keeping Australia attractive to investors.
