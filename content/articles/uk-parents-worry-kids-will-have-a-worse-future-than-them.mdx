New data shows almost a million 16‑ to 24‑year‑olds in the UK are not working, studying or training. This is the biggest rise in a century and has sparked worry that young people will have fewer jobs and poorer living standards than their parents did. A former health secretary is leading a review, and the government plans a £1.5 billion plan to tackle the problem.
