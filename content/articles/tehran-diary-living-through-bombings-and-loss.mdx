She writes about waking up at 5 a.m. in Tehran, where bombings are still happening. A phone call from her younger sister tells her that a neighbour died in an explosion. The narrator describes how the war has left her without a home, with high prices for coffee and cigarettes, and with an inhaler for breathing. She sends small help to a friend’s family while feeling the city’s emptiness and constant fear.
