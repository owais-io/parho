A walk along the lower Ouseburn in Newcastle reveals a surprisingly rich plant life that tells the city’s history. The river, once used for coal transport, now borders cafés, bars and new housing. A researcher, James Common, catalogued 1,123 species in just 1 km of the river—about a third of all British plants. The mix of old native species, ancient garden weeds and modern introductions shows how people, industry and nature have shaped the area.
