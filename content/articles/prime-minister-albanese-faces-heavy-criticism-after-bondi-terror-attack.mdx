After the deadly Bondi attack, Australian Prime Minister Anthony Albanese has been heavily criticized for not calling a national royal commission. He said earlier governments handled similar events differently and is instead pushing a state commission and an intelligence review. The decision has angered Jewish leaders, opposition MPs, and many voters, and his popularity has fallen. Despite calls for a tougher probe, he continues to focus on unity and delaying a formal inquiry.
