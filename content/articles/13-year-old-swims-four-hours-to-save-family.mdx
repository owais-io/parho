A 13‑year‑old Australian named Austin Appelbee swam about 4 km (2½ miles) in rough seas and then ran 2 km (1¼ miles) to get help for his family, who were stranded offshore. The trip took four hours, during which he shed his lifejacket and used survival backstroke to conserve energy. Experts say adrenaline and mental focus can push people beyond normal limits. Austin’s swim is an example of “hysterical strength” and child endurance.
