People often set perfect goals for the new year, but that can hurt more than help. Perfectionism turns small mistakes into big failures, leading to anxiety, burnout and procrastination. In therapy, a woman named Julie learned that “good enough” is enough. Reducing the need for flawless work improved her sleep, confidence and relationships. Social media and work metrics reinforce the myth that anything less than perfect is a failure. The key is to aim for realistic progress, not perfection.
