The article questions whether Chancellor Rachel Reeves can truly improve the UK economy. It argues her plan relies on modest growth and deregulation, which past governments have failed to deliver. Critics say Britain needs big public investment in housing, infrastructure, and green tech, not more austerity or military spending. The forecast expects inflation to hit 2 % by 2026, but oil shocks and climate risks could undermine those gains. The piece calls for a bold, state‑led push for long‑term growth.
