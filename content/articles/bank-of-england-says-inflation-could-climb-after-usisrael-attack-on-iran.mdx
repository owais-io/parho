The Bank of England says that the US‑Israel attack on Iran has pushed fuel and energy prices higher, which could raise UK inflation to 3.5% instead of the target of 2%. The Bank’s monetary policy committee kept interest rates at 3.75% for now but said it might raise them later if the price shock continues. The decision means higher living costs and could affect businesses and wages.
