UN Commission on the Status of Women has, for the first time in 70 years, added women in prison to its global justice agenda. The move follows pressure from former inmates and activists. Over 740,000 women live behind bars worldwide, a 60% jump since 2000. The decision, backed by most countries, urges governments to end discriminatory laws and address poverty, violence and lack of justice. The US is the only country to vote against the conclusions.
