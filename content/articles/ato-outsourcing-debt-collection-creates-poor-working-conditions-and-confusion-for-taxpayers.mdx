At the Australian Taxation Office (ATO), many debt‑collecting jobs are done by private firms like Recoveriescorp. Workers inside the ATO office are paid less, get shorter breaks, and face tough rules—some say they’re treated like “battery hens.” Because of the outsourcing, taxpayers get mixed messages about debt relief and higher complaints. The ATO says outsourcing helps meet demand, but critics say it creates a two‑class system and hurts both staff and citizens.
