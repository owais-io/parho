Cities across the U.S. are telling federal immigration agents, especially ICE, that they won’t be allowed to operate unchecked. Local leaders in places like Philadelphia, Chicago, Boston, Minneapolis and New York are demanding that ICE officers be arrested and prosecuted if they commit crimes. They say federal agents have been given too much immunity and that states can step in when federal law is broken. This shift signals growing pressure on federal enforcement.
