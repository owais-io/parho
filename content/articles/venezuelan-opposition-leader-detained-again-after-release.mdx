Juan Pablo Guanipa was freed from prison in Caracas after 9 months, but hours later armed men seized him again. The move shows a split in Venezuela’s new government—some leaders want reforms, others cling to old power. The incident follows a day when 35 political prisoners were released, raising hopes for democracy. Human rights groups warn the country is still stuck in authoritarian rule.
