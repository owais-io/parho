Former paramilitary commander Salvatore Mancuso has been sentenced to 40 years in prison for killings, disappearances and forced displacement of Indigenous people in La Guajira between 2002 and 2006. The court said he ordered 117 crimes, but could cut his term to eight years if he helps victims. Mancuso had served time in the US for drug trafficking and was repatriated to Colombia in 2024. He now works as a peace mediator while talks continue with the Gulf Clan.
