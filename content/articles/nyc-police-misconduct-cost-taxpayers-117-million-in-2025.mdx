NYC paid more than $117 million in 2025 to settle police misconduct lawsuits. That’s the third‑highest amount since 2018 and less than the $200 million spent in 2024. The city settled over 1,000 cases, with one‑third of the money going to people whose old convictions were overturned. The biggest payouts were $13 million and $11 million for two men whose 1987 convictions were reversed. City officials say they’re tightening rules, while police unions argue the city settles too quickly and most officers are innocent.
