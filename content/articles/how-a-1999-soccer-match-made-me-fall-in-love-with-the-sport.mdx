The author recalls watching the 1999 Women’s World Cup with his family, feeling sure the US team would win because of a legendary goalkeeper, Briana Scurry, whose past fights with the writer’s relatives inspired confidence. He explains how stories, not just stats, capture people’s hearts in soccer. That memory sparked his lifelong passion for the sport, leading him to join the Guardian as a U.S. soccer correspondent based in Saint Paul.
