The Guardian article celebrates Rev Jesse Jackson’s life and impact. From his early days beside Martin King at the 1968 Memphis motel to his 1984 and 1988 presidential campaigns, Jackson is remembered for his powerful speeches, his “I am somebody” mantra and his work with Operation Black Vote. Friends, activists and politicians recount how he built multiracial coalitions, encouraged voter registration and inspired generations to keep hope alive. His legacy is a reminder of the strength and purpose that shaped civil‑rights progress.
