A charity leader says that not adopting a clear definition of anti‑Muslim hostility would send a bad message to Muslims in the UK. She points to a rise in hate crimes, low feelings of belonging, and concerns that the current laws don’t fully protect Muslims. Critics worry a new definition could limit free speech and clash with existing laws, so the government has hesitated to make a formal declaration.
