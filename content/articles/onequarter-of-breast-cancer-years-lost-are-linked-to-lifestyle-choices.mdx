A huge study of data from more than 200 countries found that lifestyle habits—like eating lots of red meat, smoking, not exercising, and being overweight—cause about 25% of the healthy years women lose to breast cancer. The research predicts new cases will rise globally by 2050. It urges people to quit smoking, cut red meat, stay active, and keep a healthy weight to help reduce this preventable burden.
