Sarah Anderson, a 69‑year‑old London entrepreneur, died of cancer after a long career helping others. She founded The Listening Place, a free suicide‑prevention service that now runs four London sites and offers 4,000 appointments a month. Before that she ran a catering agency and created a popular toddler toilet seat. Anderson also worked on government panels about small business rules and equality, and fostered children for 16 years. The King gave her a damehood two days before she passed.
