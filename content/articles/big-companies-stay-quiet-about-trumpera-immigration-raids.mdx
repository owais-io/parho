After federal agents shot a protester in Minneapolis, many U.S. CEOs stayed silent. A group of 60 Minnesota‑based CEOs issued a vague statement calling for calm, but people criticized the lack of names and detail. Even Apple’s Tim Cook said he was “heartbroken” while attending a Trump event. The story shows how corporate leaders are caught between public anger, consumer pressure and a hostile Trump administration.
