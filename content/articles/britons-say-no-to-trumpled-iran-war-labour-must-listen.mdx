Most Britons now oppose Britain’s involvement in the Iran war that is led by Donald Trump. The article argues that the UK’s political leaders, including Labour’s Keir Starmer, are ignoring public opinion and following the US too closely, despite the war’s huge costs, civilian casualties and environmental damage. It highlights how the public’s anger over past wars in Iraq, Afghanistan and Libya has made them wary of new conflicts, urging politicians to heed this sentiment.
