A new inquiry has shown that undercover police officers, who followed left‑wing protestors for years, routinely handed their reports to MI5. The Security Service used these files to build huge dossiers on peaceful demonstrators and to check people applying for government jobs. The documents cover spying from the late 1960s to at least 2010 and expose how the police and MI5 worked together, raising questions about civil‑liberty breaches.
