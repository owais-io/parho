A Russian ship captain was found guilty of manslaughter after his vessel, the Solong, collided with the oil tanker Stena Immaculate off Yorkshire on 10 March. The crash killed Filipino crewman Mark Pernia. A jury said the captain’s gross negligence caused the avoidable death. He was remanded pending sentencing. The trial highlighted the environmental danger of the collision and the captain’s claim of a “human error.”
