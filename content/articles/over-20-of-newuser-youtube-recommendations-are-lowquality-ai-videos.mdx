Researchers found that more than 20% of the videos YouTube shows to new users are low‑quality AI‑made clips, called “AI slop.” They examined 15,000 top channels and found 278 that rely solely on AI slop. Together those channels have over 60 billion views, 221 million subscribers, and earn about $117 million a year, often targeting kids with absurd, repetitive content.
