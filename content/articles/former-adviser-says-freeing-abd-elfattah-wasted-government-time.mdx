Former adviser Paul Ovenden says the campaign to free Egyptian prisoner Alaa Abd el‑Fattah has wasted the Starmer government’s time and distracted it from key domestic issues. He claims the case became a running joke in Downing Street and that officials spent long meetings on it instead of voters’ priorities. Ovenden, who left after a past scandal, also criticises other policy debates and calls for cutting regulations and welfare.
