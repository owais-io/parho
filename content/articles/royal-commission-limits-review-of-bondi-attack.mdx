A royal commission set up to look into antisemitism in Australia said it would not investigate the full details of the Bondi beach shooting that killed 15 people, because that work belongs to the criminal trial. The commission will instead focus on how antisemitism spreads and how to build social cohesion. Families of victims will be met, and the report is due by the attack’s first anniversary.
