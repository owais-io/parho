ICC judges say a UN investigation into chief prosecutor Karim Khan’s alleged sexual misconduct does not prove he was wrong. Their 85‑page report points out gaps and contradictions in the evidence and finds the case doesn’t meet the “beyond reasonable doubt” standard. The ICC’s governing group will decide whether to take disciplinary action, which could include removing Khan from his post.
