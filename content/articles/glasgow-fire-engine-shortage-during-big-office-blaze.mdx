A huge fire gutted a Victorian office building on Glasgow’s Union Street. While regular engines arrived in five minutes, the only high‑reach ladder truck for the city was in Coatbridge, 11 miles away, and only reached the scene after two hours – when it wasn’t needed yet. The incident highlighted cuts that cut Scotland’s high‑ladder fleet from 26 to 16. Fire officials say the response was fine, but critics urge an investigation.
