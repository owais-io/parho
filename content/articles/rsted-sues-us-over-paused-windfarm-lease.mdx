Denmark’s Ørsted, the world’s biggest offshore wind developer, has taken the Trump administration to court after the U.S. government stopped leasing for its $5 billion Revolution Wind project on the northeast coast. The company says the pause violates U.S. law and will halt the almost‑finished project, which is due to start supplying power in 2026. The lawsuit follows the administration’s broader move to block all offshore wind construction on national‑security grounds.
