John Radcliffe (1928‑2021) was a BBC producer who launched the 1980s computer literacy project that brought micro‑computers into UK schools and homes. He made TV series, books and adult learning programmes, helped set up the Open University’s TV studio, and even tried early subscription TV. A lifelong friend of literature lovers, he loved music and conversation, and left a legacy of making technology accessible to everyday people.
