Australia is temporarily easing its fuel standards to add 100 million litres of petrol each month for 60 days. The move, driven by rising global oil prices and a Middle East conflict, aims to keep hospitals and emergency services supplied. The new fuel, higher in sulphur, will be routed to regional towns, especially in Queensland. The NSW premier says no panic buying is needed but urges careful use.
