Richard Tice, the deputy chief of Reform UK, said he would not call out a party candidate who told MP David Lammy to “go home to the Caribbean.” He described the remark as part of normal political debate and said he was still ready to “challenge” Lammy. Tice also brushed aside accusations from former pupils of Nigel Farage about racism, calling the claims “old news.” The move has drawn criticism from Labour and other parties.
