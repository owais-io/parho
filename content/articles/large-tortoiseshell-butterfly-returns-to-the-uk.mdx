The large tortoiseshell butterfly, once thought extinct in Britain, has been spotted again across Kent, Sussex, Hampshire, Dorset, Cornwall and the Isle of Wight. Butterfly Conservation now lists it as a resident species, meaning it may stay for good. Scientists say warming temperatures and migration from the Netherlands and France helped bring it back, and recent sightings show it is breeding in the wild. People are urged to record any finds on the iRecord app.
