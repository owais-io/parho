The National Film and Television School will add nine fully accessible rooms for disabled students from 2027, covering living costs. This first‑time housing lets students stay on campus. With £10 million from government and private money, the move tackles a gap: only 12 % of TV staff are disabled, compared to 18 % in the wider job market. The school’s increased intake shows growing inclusion, and it supports industry goals for full representation by 2030.
