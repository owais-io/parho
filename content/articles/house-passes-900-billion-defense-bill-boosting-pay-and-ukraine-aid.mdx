The U.S. House voted to approve a huge defense bill worth $900 billion. It raises many soldiers’ pay by 3.8%, gives the Pentagon a new way to buy weapons faster, and provides $400 million each year for two years to make arms for Ukraine. The bill also asks for more transparency about recent boat strikes, cuts some climate and diversity funding, and ends the legal permission for the Iraq war.
