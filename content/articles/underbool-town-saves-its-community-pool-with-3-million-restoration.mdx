Underbool is a tiny town in Victoria’s wheatbelt with just 220 residents. For years its public pool has been a vital gathering place for kids, teens, adults and the elderly. After years of leaks and danger, the town’s residents, led by Sonia McVicar, lobbied the council and the state for money. In 2024 the community secured $3 million to rebuild the pool, keeping a safe water‑safety hub open.
