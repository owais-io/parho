Vladyslav Heraskevych, a Ukrainian skeleton skater, was banned from the Winter Olympics after he wore a helmet that showed pictures of athletes who died in Russia’s war in Ukraine. The IOC said it was a political statement, but he said it was a tribute. Although the ban was lifted, the decision caused hurt and criticism. The story highlights how the Olympic rules can clash with athletes’ personal memories and raises calls for an apology.
