Gold has climbed above $5,500 for the first time, driven by a weaker U.S. dollar, worries about government debt and rising geopolitical tensions. Investors are buying gold as a safe‑haven, and traders expect the dollar to stay soft after comments from President Trump and a possible Fed rate cut. The strong price move also boosts other metals like silver, showing a broader surge in precious‑metal demand.
