A court in Ecuador sentenced 11 air force personnel to long prison terms for the forced disappearance and murder of four Afro‑Ecuadorian boys aged 11‑15 in Guayaquil. The boys were taken by soldiers on street patrols, abused, and later killed; their charred bodies were found on Christmas Eve. Eleven soldiers received 34 years, five who pleaded guilty got 2½ years, and one was cleared. The case highlights human‑rights abuses tied to the president’s hard‑line security policy.
