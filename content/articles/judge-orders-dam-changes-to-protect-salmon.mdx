An Oregon judge sided with salmon in a fight over the eight hydropower dams that hurt fish in the Columbia and Snake Rivers. He ordered the federal government to change how the dams operate, keeping water levels similar to last year with a few extra miles of flow. The move reverses a Trump decision that pulled the U.S. out of a $1bn plan to help salmon recover. Environmental groups cheered the ruling.
