Four U.S. B‑1 bomber jets landed at RAF Fairford in Gloucestershire to carry out defensive missions against Iran, after UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer approved the move. The planes will help stop Iranian missiles aimed at the Middle East. The deployment comes after Washington warned of a “surge” in strikes on Iran. The UK also moved a helicopter to the region for extra surveillance. Tensions rise as Trump and others call for more force, while Starmer defends limiting attacks.
