The UK government, led by Prime Minister Keir Starmer, is now working with popular TikTok, Instagram and YouTube creators to share its policies with people who rarely watch TV news. Influencers have met ministers, joined press conferences, and posted videos about issues like climate change, childcare and taxes. While the government says this builds trust, critics worry it lets politicians dodge tough questions and may turn creators into mouthpieces.
