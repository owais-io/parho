On Tuesday, the Senate voted 51‑48 to allow debate on the Save America Act, a Trump‑backed bill that would require voters to prove citizenship and use stricter ID checks. Republicans want the bill passed, but Democrats oppose it, saying it could disenfranchise millions. The act faces a filibuster hurdle and has faced criticism for its hard‑ball provisions. If the bill passes, it would also push for stricter voter registration rules and could limit mail‑in voting, sparking a heated partisan battle.
