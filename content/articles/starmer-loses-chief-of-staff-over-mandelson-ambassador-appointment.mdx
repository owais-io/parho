Morgan McSweeney, Keir Starmer’s chief of staff, quit after the Labour leader backed the controversial appointment of Peter Mandelson as US ambassador because of links to Jeffrey Epstein. McSweeney said he took responsibility for advising the decision and that the move damaged trust in the party. His departure weakens Starmer’s grip on Labour, raising doubts among MPs and critics ahead of upcoming elections.
