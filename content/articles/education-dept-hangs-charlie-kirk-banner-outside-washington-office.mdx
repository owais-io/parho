Washington, D.C., the U.S. Department of Education has put up big banners outside its headquarters. One shows the late conservative commentator Charlie Kirk, who was shot dead in Utah last September. The display also features historic educators like Catharine Beecher and Booker T. Washington, with a message about telling America’s hero stories. Many people slammed the choice, calling Kirk a racist and sexist voice, while Trump hailed him as a martyr and gave him a post‑humous medal.
