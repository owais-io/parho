Mehr Fardoonji, a 95‑year‑old pioneer of organic farming, died after a life devoted to the Oakcroft garden in Cheshire. For six decades she grew fruit and veg on four acres, teaching yoga and running a small school. Born in Lahore, she studied economics in London, worked in India and Israel, helped landless families, and later joined peace protests. She left Oakcroft to the Soil Association so it stays organic.
