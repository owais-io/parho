Nigel Farage’s Reform UK party ran a “free energy bill for a year” lottery that asks participants to give their name, contact details, and even their voting history. Privacy experts say collecting voting preferences for a raffle breaches UK data‑protection law because it’s sensitive data and isn’t needed for the prize. The party insists the contest is legal, but the Information Commissioner’s Office warns it may need to investigate.
