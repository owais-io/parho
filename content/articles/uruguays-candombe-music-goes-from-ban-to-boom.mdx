Uruguay's Afro-Uruguayan drum music, candombe, has moved from banned underground rhythms to vibrant street festivals. Once only played in secret African neighborhoods, it now draws thousands in Montevideo's plazas and is featured on popular singer Jorge Drexler's new album. The genre gained legal protection in 2006 and UNESCO recognition in 2009. While its popularity grows, some worry about white musicians taking over the tradition. Still, candombe remains a powerful cultural bridge.
