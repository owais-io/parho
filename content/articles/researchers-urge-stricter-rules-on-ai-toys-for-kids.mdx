A Cambridge study tested AI‑powered toys like Gabbo with 3‑ to 5‑year‑olds. The toys often misread feelings, give canned responses, and fail to join in pretend play. Parents and teachers worry these errors could hurt kids’ emotional safety and imagination. The researchers want tighter regulation, clear safety labels, and better design standards so children can play safely with AI friends.
