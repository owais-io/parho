Satellite images show that oil fires set by Israeli bombings in Tehran are still burning days later, leaving the city shrouded in toxic smoke. Residents report headaches, eye irritation, and trouble breathing, and experts warn that the smoke could cause long‑term heart disease, cancer and other health problems. The bombings also produced black, oily rain that soaked streets and roofs, worsening the air quality and raising serious public‑health concerns.
