US Postal Service faces a looming cash crunch, with its chief warning it will run out of money by February 2027 unless Congress lifts a borrowing limit. The agency has operated in deficit every year since 2007, relying on stamp sales instead of taxpayer support. To stay afloat, the USPS wants to raise postage, expand its last‑mile delivery network, and get permission to borrow beyond the current $15 billion cap.
