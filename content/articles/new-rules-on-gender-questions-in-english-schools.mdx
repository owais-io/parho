The government has issued new guidance for schools and colleges on how to help students who question their gender. It will become law next September and will be reviewed yearly. Key changes: the 2023 ban on social transitioning for primary‑age pupils is lifted, but it can only happen after proper procedures and parental or clinical approval. Schools can also keep parents out of rare safeguarding cases, and the guidance explains how to handle mixed‑sex facilities for children over eight.
