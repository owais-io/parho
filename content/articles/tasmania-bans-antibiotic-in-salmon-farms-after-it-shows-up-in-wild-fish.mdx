The Australian regulator has pulled the plug on using the antibiotic florfenicol in Tasmanian salmon farms after the drug was found in wild fish up to 10 km from the pens. The move follows a huge fish‑death outbreak that forced farms to use the drug, and no evidence was supplied to show the risk could be managed. Environmental groups say the ban protects marine life from a harmful chemical.
