After a surprise raid that captured Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro, Speaker Mike Johnson told reporters that the United States is not fighting a war there and has no troops on the ground. He said the move was a demand for the government to change, not a military takeover, and that U.S. officials plan to pressure the country through its oil exports. Democrats warned the action could drag the U.S. into a larger conflict and are pushing a war‑powers vote.
