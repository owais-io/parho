A puzzle shows three logicians, each wearing a numbered hat that others can see. One hat’s number equals the sum of the other two. The logicians announce whether they know their own number. From these statements, we can deduce the hidden number. The correct answer is 4, because only that value fits all clues. The solution uses simple reasoning about sums and differences.
