Residents of Coen and Lockhart River in far‑north Queensland are getting ready for Cyclone Narelle, a category‑five storm that could hit their town. With no big shelters, people are packing loose items away, reinforcing doors, and selling canned food. Shops are preparing for power loss and no phone service. Local leaders warn that debris and broken power lines are the biggest risks, while nearby communities like Cooktown are evacuating boats.
