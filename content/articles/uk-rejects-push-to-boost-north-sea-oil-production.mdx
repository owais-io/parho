UK ministers rejected a call from Offshore Energies UK to raise North Sea oil and gas production. The industry warned that without more local supply Britain would rely more on imports and face higher prices as Middle East tensions double gas costs. The government said new licences wouldn’t cut bills and urged moving away from fossil fuels. If the North Sea falls, imports could rise to over 25 % by 2030 and almost half by 2035.
