UK chain Iceland has quit its fight over the word “Iceland.” After losing a case, the company will use the money it would have spent on court fees to give Icelandic shoppers vouchers that can be used at its stores. The government had sued Iceland to stop the chain from using the country’s name, but a court said that place names can’t be owned by a business. Iceland will keep its name and let the country’s people benefit.
