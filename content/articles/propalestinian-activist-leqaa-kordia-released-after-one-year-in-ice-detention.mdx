Leqaa Kordia, a 33‑year‑old activist from the West Bank, was arrested at a protest outside Columbia University and sent to an ICE detention center in Texas. Despite judges saying she posed no threat, she spent almost a year in custody, with doctors warning that the conditions were unsafe. On Monday she was freed on a $100,000 bond. Her case drew support from lawmakers and highlighted problems in ICE facilities.
