About fifty pigeons gather on a farm roof every day. Males bow, fan their tails, and the females sit quietly. When a male shows interest, the pair preen and kiss with their beaks, marking the start of a courtship. The article also notes that pigeons and doves are essentially the same species, and that these birds adapt well to both city and rural life, roaming roofs and trees.
