UK’s latest security assessment says loss of species, forests and rivers endangers food, water and the economy. The report, based on data from other studies, warns that Britain’s heavy reliance on imported food and fertilisers could be hit hard if nature fails. It also flags risks like “eco‑terrorism” and water wars in the Himalayas. The piece argues that protecting nature is essential for the country’s safety and urges action on food and energy security.
