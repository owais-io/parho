Three members of Iran's women's national football squad, who had sought asylum in Australia after the Asian Cup, have now flown to Kuala Lumpur and then back to Tehran. The move follows pressure from Iran, claims of intimidation, and a changing political climate. While Australia granted special protection visas, the players said they were being coerced. The story highlights the intersection of sports, asylum, and geopolitics.
