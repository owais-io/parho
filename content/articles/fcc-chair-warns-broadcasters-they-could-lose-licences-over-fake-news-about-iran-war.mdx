FCC Chair Brendan Carr warned broadcasters that spreading “hoaxes” about the Iran conflict could cost them their licences. He posted on social media, echoing President Trump’s criticism of the press, and said stations must serve the public interest or face revocation. Carr’s threat to use the FCC’s control of the airwaves could spark a fight between government regulators and the media.
