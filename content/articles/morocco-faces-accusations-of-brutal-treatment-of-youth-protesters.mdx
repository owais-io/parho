In late September and early October, thousands of Moroccan Gen Z youths protested for better health and schooling. The government arrested and beat many, with reports of beatings, missing teeth, and even shootings that killed three. Human rights groups say the arrests were arbitrary and prisoners were mistreated, with some held for months without lawyers. Morocco says it will reform and increase spending, but the crackdown comes as it prepares to host the Africa Cup of Nations, leaving many families fearful of further violence.
