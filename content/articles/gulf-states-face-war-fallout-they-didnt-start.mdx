The Gulf’s ports have fallen silent as ships wait, after two tankers were hit by Iranian missiles near the Hormuz Strait. A drone also struck the UAE’s main oil port, Fujairah. Iran has fired thousands of missiles and drones at airports, refineries and ports. The U.S. struck Iran without consulting the Gulf allies, forcing them to spend billions on defense and lose huge oil export revenue. Gulf leaders feel angry and trapped in a war they did not start.
