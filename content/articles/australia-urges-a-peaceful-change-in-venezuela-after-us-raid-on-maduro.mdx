Australian Prime Minister Anthony Albanese says the country wants a peaceful, democratic shift in Venezuela after the U.S. military seized President Nicolás Maduro and his wife. He stresses that international law must be followed and urges dialogue to keep the region stable. The government also warns Australians not to travel to Venezuela because of the tense security situation. Other world leaders and groups have mixed reactions, with some supporting the move and others calling it a breach of law.
