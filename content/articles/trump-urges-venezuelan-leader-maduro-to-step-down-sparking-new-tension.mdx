Donald Trump told Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro to resign immediately and leave the country, offering safe passage if he agreed. Maduro rejected the demand, asking for worldwide immunity and to keep control of the military. The call comes amid U.S. naval forces near Venezuela’s coast and a four‑month pressure campaign. Trump confirmed the conversation but said it wasn’t a success. The move heightens already strained U.S.–Venezuela relations.
