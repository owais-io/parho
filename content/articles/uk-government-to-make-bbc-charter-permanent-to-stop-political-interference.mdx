Lisa Nandy, Culture Secretary, said the UK will give the BBC a permanent charter. This would stop the broadcaster from having to renew its charter every ten years, which can make it vulnerable to politics. The BBC wants the charter to stay in place so it can keep operating even if lawmakers disagree. Nandy said this will protect the BBC like the NHS and keep it accountable to viewers, not politicians.
