After Block (formerly Twitter) cut about 4,000 staff, employees say AI can’t replace them. Workers argue the new AI tools still need human help and that the layoffs were more about pleasing investors than real productivity gains. The CEO says AI made the company smaller but more efficient, while employees feel they’re being asked to build the bots that will fire them. Many are worried about job loss and morale.
