Nissan has begun making the third‑generation Leaf electric car in its Sunderland factory, a key move for the UK’s shift away from petrol. The plant, the biggest in Britain with 6,000 workers, has spent over £450 million on the new model. The Leaf, which can travel up to 386 miles on a charge, will qualify for the full £3,750 electric‑car grant. The launch is seen as vital for keeping the Sunderland site open and supporting future electric car projects.
