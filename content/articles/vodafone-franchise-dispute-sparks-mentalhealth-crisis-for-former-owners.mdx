Vodafone’s shift to franchised stores has left many former owners struggling with huge debts and cut commissions. A group of 62 ex‑franchisees now sue the company, claiming it unfairly cut sales pay and left them unable to cover rent and staff costs. The pressure has led to mental‑health problems, suicide attempts and the tragic drowning of Adrian Howe. Vodafone says it’s a commercial dispute, has paid some “goodwill” sums, but families say they feel ignored and harmed.
