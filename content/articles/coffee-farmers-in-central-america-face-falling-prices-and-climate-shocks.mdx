Small coffee farms in El Salvador and Honduras are hit hard by a mix of climate change and falling world prices. Unpredictable rains, heat waves and rust disease cut yields and quality, while the cost of inputs rises. Farmers can’t afford the adaptation they need, and labour is scarce. Credit and support are limited, so many risk losing their farms to other crops or development.
