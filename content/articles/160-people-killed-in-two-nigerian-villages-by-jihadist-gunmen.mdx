Two villages in western Nigeria, Woro and Nuku, were hit by gunmen linked to a group tied to the Islamic State, killing more than 160 people and burning homes. Villagers were rounded up, tied and shot. The attacks are part of a wider crisis in Nigeria, where jihadists, bandits and other armed groups have caused hundreds of deaths. The government and military are trying to respond, but many feel security is still weak.
