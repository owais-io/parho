Iran’s president, Masoud Pezeshkian, said the country does not want a nuclear weapon and apologized for the government’s crackdown on protests. He called for unity while urging talks with the United States, even as the US hints at military action. Massive protests sparked arrests and claims of dozens of deaths, and Iran faces pressure to reduce uranium enrichment and address its missile program. The president’s remarks come amid tense talks and a looming diplomatic crisis.
