The US issued a travel alert telling its citizens to leave Venezuela right away after reports that pro‑regime militias, called colectivos, are setting up roadblocks and checking cars for U.S. nationals. The warning comes after the US captured President Maduro and a special forces raid that killed many. Venezuela’s foreign ministry called the alert false, saying the country is calm. Still, armed colectivos have been seen in Caracas and on roads to the west.
