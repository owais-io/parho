She announced she will step down as president of UVU, after personal losses and the campus shooting of commentator Charlie Kirk. Tuminez has led the university for 7½ years, boosting enrollment, endowment, and new research centres. Her decision follows the death of her husband and the violent death of Kirk, events that heightened national debate about campus safety and political speech. She plans a break and will leave in May.
