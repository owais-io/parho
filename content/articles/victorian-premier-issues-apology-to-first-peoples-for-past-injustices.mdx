Victorian Premier Jacinta Allan formally apologized in Parliament for the harms caused to Aboriginal people during colonisation. She said the state’s past decisions denied First Peoples their rights and called for truth, justice, and a shared future. The apology, linked to the state treaty, was welcomed by elders and Indigenous leaders who saw it as a first step toward healing. The motion passed 56-27, though the Liberal opposition opposed it, calling the apology political.
