Oxford University Press named “rage bait” the word of the year, showing how online posts that purposely make people angry are on the rise. The phrase has been used three times as often in the last year as it was before. Experts say this shows a shift from simply getting clicks to deliberately stirring emotions to boost traffic. The term began in 2002 and now describes viral, outrage‑sparking content.
