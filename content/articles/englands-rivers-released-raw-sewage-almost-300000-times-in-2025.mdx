In 2025 England’s water companies released raw sewage into rivers and seas almost 300,000 times. That’s a 35 % drop from 2024, but still far higher than the drought‑affected year’s expectation. The spills mainly came from storm overflows that should only happen during heavy rain. Critics say the system is broken and that the high number of illegal discharges harms wildlife and public health. Water firms are investing £104 bn, but customers face higher bills.
