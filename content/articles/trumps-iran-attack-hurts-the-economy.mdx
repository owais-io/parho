Donald Trump ordered a surprise attack on Iran, hoping to overthrow its leadership. The bombings killed many and damaged cities, but did not achieve a clear victory. The war sent oil prices up, rattled global markets, and hurt the U.S. economy. Meanwhile, Russia profited from higher energy prices, and British politicians debated whether to support the U.S. campaign. Trump’s shifting goals have damaged trust and made the conflict messy.
