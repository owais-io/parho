The article celebrates Stephen Jones, who spent 42 years writing rugby for the Sunday Times. He was known for his sharp, fearless columns and his willingness to criticize the sport from the sidelines. The piece urges rugby leaders to follow his example by standing firm on their beliefs, protecting the game’s spirit, and fixing problems like league structures, the Champions Cup, and on‑field rules. It reminds readers that good rugby needs humor, community, and courage.
