A group of 34 Australian women and children, released after years in a Syrian detention camp, were suddenly returned to the camp when the Australian government decided not to repatriate them. They had been waiting to return home, but the decision left them stuck again in a squalid facility in north‑east Syria. The move raises questions about how former detainees are treated and the legal process behind repatriation.
