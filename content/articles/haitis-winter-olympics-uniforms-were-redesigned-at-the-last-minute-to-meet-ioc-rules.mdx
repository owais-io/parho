The Haitian team’s 2026 Winter Olympics suits had to be changed on the eve of the opening ceremony because the original design, featuring a painting of the revolutionary Toussaint Louverture, broke the International Olympic Committee’s rules against political or cultural displays. Designer Stella Jean removed Louverture from the picture, keeping a riderless horse and bright sky. The new kits arrived just in time, letting Haiti compete proudly while staying within the guidelines.
