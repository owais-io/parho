Denmark and its autonomous region Greenland go to the polls today amid a tense political climate. Voters are still thinking about U.S. President Donald Trump’s warning that he could claim Greenland, which gave the country a “Greenland bump” in polls. The election is tight, with Denmark’s left‑wing Social Democrats hoping to stay in power but the centre‑right Moderates likely becoming the deciding kingmaker. Immigration and everyday issues like the cost of living are the main campaign topics.
