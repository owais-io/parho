An Anglican rector talks about a priest’s advice, a teacher who told kids Santa isn’t real, and his own experience playing Santa at a school event. He wonders if Christians should let children believe in Santa, saying it brings wonder and joy. He notes that revealing the truth can hurt kids, but some adults think honesty is better. He also points out that Santa’s male role keeps men in the spotlight, making women feel left out.
