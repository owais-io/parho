German pencil maker Faber‑Castell said it donated a factory in Costa Rica to be used as a refugee shelter, but the government turned the site into a prison for 200 people the US deported. The men, women and children were held for months without legal basis, and a court said their detention violated human rights. Faber‑Castell was surprised, while the Costa Rican police deny the claim.
