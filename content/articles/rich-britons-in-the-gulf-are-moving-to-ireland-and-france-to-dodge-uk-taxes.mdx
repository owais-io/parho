Wealthy UK residents who have been living in the Gulf are leaving the country and taking refuge in places like Ireland and France so they can stay out of the UK’s tax system. With the financial year ending soon, they’re trying to use the limited days they’re allowed in Britain to avoid income and capital‑gain tax. HMRC says extra days under an “exceptional circumstances” rule are unlikely to be granted. Advisors warn that even a few extra days back in the UK could trigger big tax bills on worldwide income and old business sales.
