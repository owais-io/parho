Spain announced it will officially pardon 53 women who were locked up by a Franco‑era institution called the Board for the Protection of Women. The board, run by religious orders and overseen by the dictator’s wife, kept girls and young women who were seen as “unruly” or “immoral” in confinement from 1941 until 1985. The pardon marks them as victims of state repression, although many survivors still seek truth and reparations.
