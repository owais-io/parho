Mani is a rugged part of southern Greece famous for its tough history and pirate past. But a visitor discovers a hidden world of colorful wildflowers that cover the rocky hills. The author walks along the coast and sees millions of blossoms turning to follow the sun, especially a white daisy called Chios chamomile that spreads like a blanket. The region’s stone walls and olive trees contrast with the soft, bright flowers, creating a stunning natural scene.
