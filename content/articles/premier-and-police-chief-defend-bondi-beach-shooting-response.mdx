After a shooting at Bondi Beach that killed 15 people, New South Wales Premier Chris Minns and Police Commissioner Mal Lanyon said the police acted bravely and quickly. Officers with handguns stopped two gunmen who had long‑range rifles. The leaders said enough police were on the beach and that the officers risked their lives to protect the crowd. They refused to give details about the exact number of officers or response times, saying an investigation was underway.
