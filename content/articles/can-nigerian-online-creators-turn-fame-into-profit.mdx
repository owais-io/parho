Creator culture is exploding in Nigeria. Skit‑makers, YouTubers and TikTok stars like Broda Shaggi have millions of followers and fancy movie‑style shoots, but most make less than $100 a month. The sector is worth $3.1 bn now and could reach $17.8 bn by 2030, yet advertising pays little and there’s little public funding. Creators want new rules that let them earn money, protect their work and join a single voice with the government.
