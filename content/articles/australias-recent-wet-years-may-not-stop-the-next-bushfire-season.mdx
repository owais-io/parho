After five years of rain, Australia’s bushfire season is now dangerous. Fires in New South Wales and Tasmania have already destroyed dozens of houses and killed a veteran firefighter. Scientists say weather is becoming unpredictable, with hot, dry conditions and strong winds making fire risk high. Climate change is making it harder to forecast. Residents in bushfire‑prone areas are urged to make a plan, check fuel loads, and stay alert.
