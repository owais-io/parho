Immigrant truck drivers keep America’s supply chains moving, but a recent ICE crackdown has taken about 9,500 drivers off the road for things like failing English tests. The move has forced trucking companies to sell trucks or stop hauling, driving up freight costs. Training schools were shut, and the industry worries about shortages as the average driver ages. With the economy depending on these drivers, the crackdown risks delays, higher prices, and job losses.
