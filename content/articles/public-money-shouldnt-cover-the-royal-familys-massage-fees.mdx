A complaint has been made that Andrew Mountbatten‑Windsor, a member of the British royal family, has charged his personal massage sessions to public expenses while serving as a trade envoy. The letter argues that taxpayers should not pay for such private benefits and calls for stricter rules, full transparency, and repayment if costs are not in the public interest. It also urges institutions to address questionable spending and restore public confidence.
