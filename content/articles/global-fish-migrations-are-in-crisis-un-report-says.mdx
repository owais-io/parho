A new UN study shows that the epic migrations of freshwater fish – like salmon, eels and giant Amazon catfish – have shrunk by about 81% since 1970. Pollution, dams, over‑fishing and warmer rivers are the main culprits. Examining over 15,000 species, the report flags 325 that cross borders, yet only 24 are protected. Countries must work together to keep rivers connected, reduce pollution, open blocked waterways, and manage fishing so the species can survive.
