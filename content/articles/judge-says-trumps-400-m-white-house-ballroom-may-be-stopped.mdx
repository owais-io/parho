A federal judge is warning that Trump’s plan to tear down the White House East Wing and replace it with a $400 million ballroom could be halted. The National Trust for Historic Preservation sued to stop the demolition, arguing it broke preservation rules. Trump’s lawyers say the project is funded privately and doesn’t need congressional approval, but the judge sees the moves as “brazen” and may issue a final order in March. The case could go to the Supreme Court.
