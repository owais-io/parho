A recent Health Survey of 10,000 adults shows that 24% of people in England didn’t drink any alcohol in 2024 – up from 19% two years earlier. Women were a touch more likely to abstain (26% vs 22%). The survey also found that 27% of drinkers are at higher risk, with men drinking more risky amounts than women. Experts warn that despite more non‑drinkers, alcohol‑related harm and costs to the NHS remain huge.
