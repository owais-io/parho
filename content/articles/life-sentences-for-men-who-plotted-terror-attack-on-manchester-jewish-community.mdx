Two men, Walid Saadaoui and Amar Hussein, were given life in prison for planning a deadly gun attack on Manchester’s Jewish community. They had been linked to the Islamic State and planned to disguise themselves as Jews, shoot at a march, and then target synagogues and schools. An undercover officer, “Farouk,” infiltrated their circle and helped stop the plot, leading to the men’s arrest and a court warning that the attack could have been the UK’s deadliest.
