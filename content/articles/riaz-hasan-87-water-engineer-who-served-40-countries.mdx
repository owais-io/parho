Riaz Hasan, who died at 87, was a water‑resources engineer who worked in 40 countries. After studying at Bradford, he joined the UK firm Halcrow and later the UN and World Bank. He helped design long‑term water and food projects in places hit by war, famine and disasters. He faced racism in Britain but spoke up for truth. Married to NHS GP Rukhsana, he donated quietly and is survived by his wife, two children and three grandchildren.
