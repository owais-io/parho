People in a small Somerset town are turning a field once nicknamed “bitey horse field” into a community woodland. The council secured the land on a 99‑year lease and hired volunteers, including school kids, to plant trees like oak, alder and poplar. Over 600 residents will help build a new green space that will be called Frome Community Woodland, a place for play, reflection and local pride.
