North‑west Labour MPs are unhappy with the government’s three‑year local‑council funding plan, saying it unfairly takes money away from northern towns. They have written to the local‑government secretary, asking for more money and even extra £400 m each year for the most deprived areas. The MPs warn they could vote against the settlement in Parliament, risking a rebellion. Council leaders fear budget cuts and may need special loans. The government says it has increased funding and will keep helping.
