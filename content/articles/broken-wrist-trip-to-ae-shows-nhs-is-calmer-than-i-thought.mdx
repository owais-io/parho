After a broken wrist, I visited A&E. I expected chaos, but it was quiet and efficient. A doctor set the cast quickly and even wrote my follow‑up note. The story shows that waiting times and violence are real problems, yet many patients still get prompt care. It highlights the gap between national statistics and everyday experience, and how people feel lucky even when services are under strain.
