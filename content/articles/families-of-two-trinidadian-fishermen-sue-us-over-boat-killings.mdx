Families of two Trinidadian fishermen killed in a U.S. military airstrike on a small boat in the Caribbean have sued the U.S. government, calling the strikes illegal and murder. The lawsuit, filed in Massachusetts, is the first federal case over the campaign that has killed about 117 people. The Trump administration says the strikes are legal under a secret DOJ opinion that treats cartels as an armed conflict. Lawyers from the ACLU and others represent the families.
