Former Labour MP Phil Woolas has died from brain cancer at age 66. He served as minister for local government, the environment, borders and immigration during New Labour’s 13‑year rule. Woolas, who joined the party as a teenager and later worked in TV and unions, was praised by former prime ministers Tony Blair and Gordon Brown. He fought a year‑long battle with glioblastoma and left behind his wife, sons and a new grandson.
