Spain is mourning for three days after a high‑speed train derailed and collided with another near Adamuz, killing at least 40 people and injuring dozens. Rescue teams are still searching wreckage for missing victims, while families use social media to ask for updates. Investigators say a broken rail joint may have caused the accident, and officials promise a full inquiry. Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez declared the national mourning.
