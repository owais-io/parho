Kharg Island is where most of Iran’s oil leaves the country. Despite heavy US‑Israel air attacks on Iran, the island has stayed untouched because striking it would push global oil prices sharply higher. The U.S. has even discussed seizing the island, but experts say an attack would damage the market and hurt the U.S. economy. Meanwhile, the Strait of Hormuz is still blocked, keeping prices up.
