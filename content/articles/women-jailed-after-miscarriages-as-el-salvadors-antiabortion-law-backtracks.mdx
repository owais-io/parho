El Salvador’s strict anti‑abortion law now criminalises even miscarriages. A 19‑year‑old nurse was arrested after losing a pregnancy, and other women have faced long prison terms. While activists freed dozens of women between 2009‑23, the president’s 2022 state of emergency has reversed progress. Police now report patients to courts, legal aid is cut, and hospitals install cameras, turning medical care into a crime scene. This shift mirrors stricter policies in other Latin American nations.
