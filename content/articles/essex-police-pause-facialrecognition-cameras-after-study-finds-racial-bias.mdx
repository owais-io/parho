A Cambridge study showed Essex’s live facial‑recognition cameras were more likely to correctly identify black people than others. Because of this bias risk, Essex police have stopped using the system while they work with the software company to fix it. The pause follows a wider review of the technology, which has raised concerns across the UK about fairness and accuracy.
