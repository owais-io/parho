Tim Wilson, a Liberal MP, still owns a risky investment that makes money when the Australian share market falls. He bought the product during the pandemic in 2020, but it has lost about 75% of its value. Even as the market hits record highs, he keeps the bet. Analysts say this kind of short‑selling product is rare, speculative, and not meant for long‑term holding. It raises concerns about conflicts of interest.
