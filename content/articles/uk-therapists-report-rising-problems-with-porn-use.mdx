More than half of UK therapists say they've seen a rise in people struggling with porn that interferes with work, relationships, and even health. A survey of 3,000 counselors found 53% saw more clients who feel out of control or turn to extreme content. Experts call for a national plan to address porn addiction, reduce stigma, and give people support before it worsens. Services like online self‑help and training for therapists are growing.
