About 3,800 workers at JBS USA’s Greeley, Colorado plant are walking out on Monday, the first strike in the industry in years. They say the company forces them to pay for pricey protective gear, discriminates against immigrant workers, and offers too‑low wages. The walk‑out could raise meat prices and embarrass the Trump administration, which received a big donation from the plant’s parent company, Pilgrim’s Pride. Workers include many refugees speaking 57 languages.
