In Nepal, a diet of lentils, rice and yogurt that people ate long ago is now being tested as a way to cure type‑2 diabetes. A pilot study in Kathmandu had 43% of patients drop their diabetes after just eight weeks on a low‑calorie version of the old menu. Similar results are coming from villages, with half of participants free of the disease after four months. The plan could replace expensive medicines and curb the rise of junk food.
