In 2009, four workers at Illinois’s Burr Oak cemetery dug up more than 100 bodies and moved them to resell burial plots. FBI investigators found a small clump of moss on the bodies. Scientists matched it to a species that only grows in a nearby area, proving the bodies were moved there. By testing the moss’s metabolism, they determined the bodies were buried less than a year old, proving the crime was recent and helping convict the robbers.
