Hedgehogs in the UK are shrinking by 30‑75% in rural areas because of farm chemicals, lost habitat, traffic and climate changes. Small towns and suburbs are showing signs of recovery, so people can help right now. Volunteer for camera‑based monitoring, add tiny gaps in fences to make “hedgehog highways,” offer simple, pesticide‑free food, build shady shelters, and call rescue centres if a hedgehog looks hurt. Share tips online and ask local councils to protect wildlife areas.
