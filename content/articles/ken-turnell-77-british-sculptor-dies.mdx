Ken Turnell, a British sculptor, artist and art teacher, died at 77. He made sculptures shown at the Serpentine and Whitechapel Galleries and created large sand drawings on Tynemouth beach. Turnell taught at Wimbledon and Central St Martins before moving to Portugal, where he lived until his death. He is survived by partner Karen and two children.
