A UK high‑court judge has halted the forced return of a 31‑year‑old Eritrean who survived trafficking to France under the country’s “one‑in‑one‑out” scheme. The judge said sending him back could harm his mental health and that France may not recognise him as a trafficking victim or provide the care he needs. The move comes after the UK swapped 370 legal arrivals for 354 returns in the scheme, which aims to curb dangerous small‑boat crossings.
