Japanese crime boss Takeshi Ebisawa was handed 20 years in U.S. prison for trying to sell uranium and plutonium to an undercover DEA agent who pretended to be an Iranian general. Ebisawa also trafficked heroin from Myanmar’s rebels and tried to buy weapons for insurgent groups in Sri Lanka and Myanmar. The case shows how nuclear material can slip out of control and be sold by criminal networks across several countries.
