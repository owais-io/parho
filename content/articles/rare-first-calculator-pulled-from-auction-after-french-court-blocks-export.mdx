Christie’s pulled the rare Pascaline – a 17th‑century mechanical calculator invented by Blaise Pascal – from its auction after a Paris court temporarily blocked its export, classifying it a national treasure. The machine, one of only eight known, was expected to fetch over €2 million. Scientists and researchers urged the court to keep it in France, arguing it should stay in a public collection for study. The sale will resume only after the court’s final decision.
