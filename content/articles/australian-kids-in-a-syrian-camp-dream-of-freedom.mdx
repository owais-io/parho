In al‑Roj, a detention camp in Syria, 23 Australian children and their mothers have lived in tents for years. The kids talk about what Australia feels like—ice‑cream, swimming, TV—things they can’t get at home. They want to leave the camp, but fear that staying will mean even worse dangers. The mothers worry about their own safety and the children’s future.
