The writer spends a pre‑dawn walk on a large Welsh moor looking for black grouse, a rare bird that has declined sharply. He explains the difference between the common red grouse and the protected black grouse, and why black grouse are only hunted in season. While hunting is largely gone in Wales, the moor is still managed for the birds’ summer life. He finally spots a black grouse, but it flies away before he can photograph it.
