The Menai Bridge on Anglesey, a world‑first suspension bridge built by Thomas Telford, celebrated 200 years this week. The bridge helped farmers, traders and travellers link Wales to Britain and Ireland, and it still attracts tourists and locals alike. Engineers are now fixing safety problems that have forced traffic restrictions, and repairs are expected to finish next year. Despite its age, the bridge is praised for its lasting strength and future prospects.
