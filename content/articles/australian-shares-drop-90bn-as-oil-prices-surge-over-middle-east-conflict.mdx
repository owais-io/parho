Australian stocks fell 2.9% on Monday, wiping about $90bn from the market. The drop came after oil prices jumped above $100 a barrel because of fighting in the Middle East, sparking fears of higher inflation. Most sectors fell, especially mining, while energy shares rose. Investors are worried about the Reserve Bank of Australia raising rates, which could hit mortgages and household costs, adding to the sell‑off.
