The judge found the Home Office had not protected vulnerable migrants in detention, those with mental health problems and suicide risk. The case involved two men from Egypt and Bangladesh held in Brook House after a BBC Panorama investigation exposed abuse. The court said the rules that require doctors to report risks had not been used for years, putting many detainees at danger. The ruling could change how the Home Office handles vulnerable detainees and pressure charities to enforce safeguards.
