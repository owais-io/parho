After the terror attack on the Jewish community at Bondi Beach, thousands of Australians left their belongings on the shore. Local residents and survivors collected them and gathered in silence, laying flowers, candles and offering prayers. Prime Minister Anthony Albanese and community leaders joined the mourners. The day also saw support from cafés, chaplains and mental‑health volunteers, while some voiced anger at the government’s response. Many people shared hugs and tissues, showing solidarity and helping each other heal.
