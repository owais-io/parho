Virginia Spiers walks through a steep Dorset orchard and describes a spring awakening: bright yellow daffodils, early cherry plum blossoms, and old narcissus still in their rows. She hears woodpeckers and the familiar chiffchaff song, sees bees on rosemary and a fluttering brimstone butterfly. The light has warmed, shadows shift, and old trees like magnolia regain life. Farmers no longer grow daffodils commercially; instead they focus on eucalyptus, strawberries and cut flowers.
