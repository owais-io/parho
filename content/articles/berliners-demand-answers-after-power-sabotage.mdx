An arson attack by a left‑wing group cut power to about 45,000 homes and hospitals in southwest Berlin, sparking the biggest blackout since WWII. The attackers set fire to a bridge carrying high‑voltage cables, and police have little evidence so far. Berlin officials say the grid was weak and needs stronger protection, while politicians and the Red Cross call for more civil‑protection investment. Residents still await answers on who did it and how to stop it again.
