Britain plans to stop the tax‑free exemption for items worth under £135, meaning small shipments will have to pay customs duty. The change could raise prices for shoppers and hurt small firms that rely on cheap overseas goods. The business lobby says the switch should happen gradually and avoid per‑item fees that could push costs up. Some retailers support the move to level the playing field with rivals from China, but many worry about the impact on consumers.
