In Syria’s Roj camp, 34 Australians – mostly women and children – are facing nightly raids, beatings and a shrinking, unsafe environment. They were forced back after a failed trip to Damascus, and now have no clear path home. Australian officials refuse to help, saying they are not responsible. Human‑rights groups, the UN and the U.S. call for immediate repatriation. The situation risks long‑term harm and fuels political debate in Australia.
