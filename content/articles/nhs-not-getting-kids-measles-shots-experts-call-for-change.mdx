The NHS is failing to vaccinate children with MMR, leading to measles outbreaks, experts warn. Rates in some English areas are as low as in countries like Afghanistan and Malawi. The Enfield outbreak shows only 64% coverage. Calls for reform: pharmacies could give shots, catch‑up clinics set up. Government says it will push to raise rates, but pressure remains on NHS to overhaul the system. WHO wants 95% coverage, but many children still miss shots. A child died last summer.
