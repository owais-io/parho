Primroses bloom across Bainton’s St Mary’s churchyard, turning the grass buttery yellow and bringing a quiet welcome to spring. Villagers gather on Palm Sunday for the annual Bainton Primrose Festival, a tradition started in 2022 by the vicar and parishioners. The flowers, cherished for their renewal symbolism, also illustrate Darwin’s dimorphism, with two flower types encouraging cross‑pollination. Conservation groups celebrate the churchyard as a wildlife refuge. The event also showcases rare garden plants like Balkan anemones.
