Simpson’s on the Strand, a 200‑year‑old London dining icon, will open again next month after a long closure. The historic restaurant, famous for its Edwardian décor and famous patrons like Dickens and Churchill, was shut during the pandemic. Renowned restaurateur Jeremy King is bringing it back, hoping to revive its grand atmosphere and return it to the city’s culinary scene. It's a tribute to a unique slice of London history.
