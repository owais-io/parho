Guardian has issued a series of corrections to recent articles. They clarified that NHS England’s bowel cancer test sensitivity, not accuracy, is being adjusted by lowering the blood‑threshold. A typo blamed Shakespeare’s play for the line “Shall I compare thee to a summer’s day?” – it actually comes from his Sonnet 18. Other updates corrected stories about Texas airspace, Palestinian solidarity, an oil refinery, and more. Readers can send complaints to the Guardian’s editorial office.
