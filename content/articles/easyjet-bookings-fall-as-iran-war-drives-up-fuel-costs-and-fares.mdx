EasyJet says the war in the Middle East and a surge in oil prices are cutting summer flight bookings, especially to Turkey, Cyprus and Egypt. Passengers are now choosing destinations like Spain, Greece and Portugal instead. The airline has hedged most fuel costs, but some price hikes will still reach travelers. EasyJet also reopened its Newcastle base to create jobs and boost local tourism, while urging reduced domestic flights where trains exist.
