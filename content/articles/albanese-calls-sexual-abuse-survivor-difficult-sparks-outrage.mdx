Australian Prime Minister Anthony Albanese was criticised after he described Grace Tame, a sexual‑violence survivor and activist, as “difficult” in a short‑answer interview. Tame said the word is a sexist label used to shut women up, and she called the comment condescending. The remark reminded people of former PM Scott Morrison’s sexist remarks, sparking a backlash from Tame’s supporters and others who say the comment undermines the government’s progress on women’s rights.
