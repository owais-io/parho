Keir Starmer has faced pressure inside his own government to abandon net‑zero goals. His earlier speech pledged a swift switch to clean energy, but advisers from a pro‑Reform faction watered down plans and even kept him out of a UN climate summit. The result is a swing to the Green Party in the Gorton & Denton by‑election, showing voters want bold climate action. Starmer now must decide whether to re‑embrace his green promises and keep the public’s trust.
