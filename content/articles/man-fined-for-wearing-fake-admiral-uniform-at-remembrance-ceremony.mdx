Jonathan Carley, 65, was fined £500 and ordered to pay extra costs after he pretended to be a Royal Navy admiral at a Remembrance Sunday service in Llandudno. He wore a uniform he claimed was given to him as a cadet but had added fake medals and a rear‑admiral ring. The judge said his act was foolish and disrespectful to veterans. Carley said he wanted a sense of belonging, and his lawyer cited health issues.
