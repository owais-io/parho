MP Jess Phillips says Britain must stop waiting for tragedies like the Epstein scandal to act on violence against women and girls. She wants a long‑term strategy that cuts the violence in half over ten years, with real funding for police, courts, schools, health services and mental‑health care. The plan would tighten laws on grooming, stop abuse at airports and ensure survivors get justice and support, not just a political response.
