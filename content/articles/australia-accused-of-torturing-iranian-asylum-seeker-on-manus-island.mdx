A UN committee says Australia violated international law by allowing an Iranian asylum seeker to be tortured and mistreated while he was held on Manus Island in Papua New Guinea and later in Australian detention. The man was transferred in 2013, spent three years in harsh conditions, had his throat slit by a guard, and stayed in Australian custody for another three years despite serious health problems. Australia claimed it had no control over the overseas facilities, but the committee disagreed and called for compensation and reforms.
