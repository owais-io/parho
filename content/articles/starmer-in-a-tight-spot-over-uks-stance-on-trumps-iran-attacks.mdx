The UK has tried to stay neutral about US bombing Iran. Prime Minister Keir Starmer, who likes to keep close to President Trump, is now stuck. Britain did not let the US use its bases for the strikes, but it also won’t call the attacks illegal. Starmer’s government is caught between supporting the US and keeping promises to Europe and critics. His next move could shift Britain’s position in the Middle East and its relationship with the US.
