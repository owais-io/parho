President Trump said commercial flights between the U.S. and Venezuela will resume almost immediately, claiming the airspace is now safe. The decision follows U.S. pressure that helped oust dictator Nicolás Maduro and the appointment of Delcy Rodríguez as interim president. Trump also announced U.S. oil companies are scouting Venezuela’s reserves, which could bring new jobs and investment. Airlines plan to restart service in the coming months.
