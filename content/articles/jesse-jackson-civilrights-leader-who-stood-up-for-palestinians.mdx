Jesse Jackson, a top U.S. civil‑rights leader, used his platform to push for Palestinian statehood, support for Nelson Mandela, and aid for U.S. hostages abroad. His talks with leaders like Yasser Arafat helped bring the Palestinian cause into the mainstream of American politics. Jackson also fought against U.S. military interventions and pushed for sanctions on apartheid South Africa. He died at 84, leaving a legacy of linking U.S. racial justice to global struggles.
