The UK government is giving £100 million to restart a carbon dioxide plant on Teesside. It had been shut after a trade deal cut tariffs on bioethanol. The restart is meant to stop shortages caused by the war in Iran, as CO₂ is used in drinks, food preservation, medicine and animal slaughter. The plant turns wheat into ethanol, releasing CO₂ as a by‑product. The move protects jobs and supply chains.
