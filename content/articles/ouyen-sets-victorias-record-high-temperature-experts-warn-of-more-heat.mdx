Farmers and residents in Ouyen, a small town in Victoria, recorded a temperature that may be the highest ever for the state, reaching almost 49 °C (115 °F). It’s been five days of heat over 40 °C with no rain, and scientists say hot days could continue until April. Farmers worry about drought and wildfires, and the community faces health risks, especially for the elderly.
