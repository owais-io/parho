A judge in Richmond has ordered Lindsey Halligan, a lawyer appointed by Donald Trump, to explain why she still calls herself the U.S. attorney for Virginia’s eastern district, even though a different judge ruled her appointment was illegal. Halligan led prosecutions of former FBI chief James Comey and New York attorney general Letitia James, both of which were dismissed. She has seven days to file a response and explain why her title should not be struck from the indictments.
