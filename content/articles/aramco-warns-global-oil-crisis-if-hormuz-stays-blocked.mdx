Saudi Arabia’s state oil company says the world’s oil markets will face a catastrophe if the Strait of Hormuz remains closed because of the US‑Iran conflict. Aramco can still ship about 70% of its usual oil, but the 20‑million‑barrel daily cut is hurting supply. Oil prices fell after Trump’s calm comments, and markets are easing. Aramco plans to move more crude through a pipeline to the Red Sea, while G7 leaders discuss emergency oil stockpiles to stabilize prices.
