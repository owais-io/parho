A 31‑year‑old man spoke at a March for Australia rally in Sydney and made antisemitic remarks that police say match neo‑Nazi language. He was arrested and charged with publicly inciting hatred, causing fear, and will appear in court on Tuesday. The incident follows a rise in neo‑Nazi activity at the rallies, with other members like Joel Davis and former leader Thomas Sewell also mentioned. The police are keeping an eye on white‑supremacist groups at the event.
