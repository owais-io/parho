Antarctic whales that were nearly wiped out by whalers are now back in huge numbers, especially humpbacks. But industrial trawlers are catching large amounts of krill, the food these whales need. Scientists fear the fishing could hurt the whales’ recovery. The UN body that protects the area, CCAMLR, is stuck in a political stalemate, so no new limits are in place. Researchers suggest a 30‑km no‑fishing buffer around key whale feeding sites.
