Spain’s government says Telegram owner Pavel Durov is lying and trying to hurt democracy after he blasted plans to ban children under 16 from social media and to make tech firms liable for hateful posts. Durov warned the new law could lead to mass surveillance and over‑censorship. Madrid calls the attack “propaganda” and stresses the need to regulate messaging apps. The clash highlights growing European tensions over tech control.
