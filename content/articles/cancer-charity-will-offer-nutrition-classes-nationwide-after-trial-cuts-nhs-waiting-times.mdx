After a trial showed they help patients with diet and reduce NHS waiting times, Maggie’s, a cancer charity, plans to roll out nutrition lessons across all its 27 UK centres. The classes, led by specialist dietitians, clear up myths like sugar feeding cancer and teach healthy eating during treatment. They also help patients get faster access to diet support, cutting wait lists. The initiative also won praise from health officials, who said partnering with charities can make care feel warmer and more welcoming for patients.
