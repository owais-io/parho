The article says that over $2bn has been spent by 11 African governments on Chinese AI cameras that can recognise faces and track movements. Experts warn that these systems are used to crack down on activists, protesters and journalists, with little evidence they actually reduce crime. The lack of laws to protect data and privacy means people may be monitored and silenced, putting civil liberties at risk.
