Meta earned more than analysts predicted in Q4, sending its stock up about 10%. The firm is pouring billions into AI data centers, including a $6 bn deal for fiber‑optic cables, and plans to spend $162‑$169 bn in 2026 mainly on infrastructure and hiring. While cutting staff from its virtual‑reality arm, Meta is advancing an AI strategy that blends recommendation engines with personal assistants. Meanwhile, CEO Mark Zuckerberg will testify in a trial over social‑media addiction.
