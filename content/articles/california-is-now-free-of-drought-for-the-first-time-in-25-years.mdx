After years of dry weather that worsened fires and hurt farming, California has finally ended its drought after 25 years. A map from the U.S. Drought Monitor shows no dry spots across the state, thanks to wet winter storms that filled reservoirs like Shasta and Oroville. While the snowpack is still below normal and some areas need more rain, the drought‑free news lifts pressure on water‑use rules and gives hope for farmers and cities.
