Brown student Mia Tretta survived a 2019 school shooting and is now facing a second one on campus. She says the latest attack is scary but not shocking, as shootings are becoming common. Tretta has turned her experience into activism, demanding better control of “ghost guns” that were used to shoot her. The article notes how many teens die from gunfire and how lawmakers haven’t acted enough.
