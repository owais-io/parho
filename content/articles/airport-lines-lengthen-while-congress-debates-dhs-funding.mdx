A funding dispute over the Department of Homeland Security has left TSA agents unpaid and some quitting. The result is longer lines at big airports, like Atlanta and Houston. Democrats want the money to push immigration reforms, while Republicans reject it. Airline CEOs ask Congress to end the shutdown. Trump urges TSA workers to keep coming to jobs. Travelers face waiting times up to two hours, and airlines warn of more disruption if the standoff continues.
