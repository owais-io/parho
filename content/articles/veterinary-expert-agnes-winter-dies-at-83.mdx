Agnes Winter, a vet who helped keep the Wensleydale sheep breed alive, died at 83 after an illness. Growing up on a Yorkshire farm, she studied veterinary science at Liverpool University, becoming one of only six women in her class. After years of practice and research, she led the Wensleydale Sheep Breeders’ Association during a crisis and later taught at Liverpool. She was celebrated with awards and an honorary professorship before retiring to York, where she enjoyed history and tours.
