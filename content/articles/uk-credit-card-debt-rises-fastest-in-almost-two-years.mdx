Credit card debt in the UK jumped 12.1% in November, the fastest rise in almost two years. Families took on extra borrowing to cover Christmas gifts and higher everyday costs, experts say. Total credit card borrowing hit £1 billion, up from £700 million the month before. While people saved more money in bank accounts, mortgage approvals slipped. Despite worries about new tax rules, households still used credit to spend.
