At the Winter Olympics, Canadian curler Marc Kennedy was accused by Sweden’s Oskar Eriksson of double‑touching the stone, a cheating move the electronic sensors at the hog line should flag. Kennedy swore in frustration, prompting World Curling to warn Canada about abusive language and add spot checks, but it left the game result unchanged. Kennedy insists he didn’t cheat and calls Sweden’s tactics unfair.
