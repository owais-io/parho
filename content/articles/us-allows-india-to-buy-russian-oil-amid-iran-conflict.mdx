The U.S. has issued a 30‑day waiver that lets Indian refiners buy Russian crude that is stuck at sea, a move aimed at keeping global supplies flowing while the Middle East crisis threatens prices. The waiver does not give Russia a big financial boost, and Washington hopes India will later turn to U.S. oil. The decision comes after Iran’s attacks on the Strait of Hormuz, which have spiked energy costs and worried the world.
