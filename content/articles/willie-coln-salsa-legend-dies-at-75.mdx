Legendary salsa trombonist and songwriter Willie Colón passed away on Saturday at 75. The Bronx‑born artist sold more than 30 million albums and shaped salsa with hits like “Aguanile” and the historic album Siembra with Rubén Blades. Global stars such as Bad Bunny and Jerry Rivera paid tribute, celebrating Colón’s blend of jazz, rock and Caribbean rhythms that made salsa a worldwide force. His music remains a symbol of pride and resistance for Latin culture.
