Violent fighting has erupted again along the 500‑mile border between Thailand and Cambodia, after a US‑backed ceasefire signed by Donald Trump a month ago was broken. The skirmish has already killed seven civilians and wounded 20 in Cambodia, and three Thai soldiers were killed. Both sides blame the other for violating the truce, accusing each other of using artillery, drones and rockets. Hundreds of thousands of people in both countries are being moved to shelters.
