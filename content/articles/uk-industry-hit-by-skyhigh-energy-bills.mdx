UK minister Rachel Reeves said the government will cut energy costs for industry, but critics say the plans are too small. Britain’s energy prices are among the highest in the world, hurting factories and chemical plants that need cheap power to compete. Reeves introduced a “supercharger” scheme and a new competitiveness plan, but only a handful of firms will benefit. The article argues the government needs a stronger, long‑term strategy to lower energy bills and keep UK manufacturing alive.
