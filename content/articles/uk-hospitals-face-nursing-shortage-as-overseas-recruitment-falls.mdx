The UK has lost 93% of the overseas nurses it used to hire, leaving hospitals and care homes almost short of staff. The sharp drop shows how much the country depends on foreign workers and how little it has invested in training and paying local nurses. Experts call for a stronger domestic workforce and a shared‑cost plan with other recruiting countries to make the system fair for everyone.
