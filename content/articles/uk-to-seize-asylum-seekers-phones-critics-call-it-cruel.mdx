Britain’s Home Office says it will start searching for phones and SIM cards from people who arrive by small boat, even looking inside mouths, without first arresting them. Human‑rights groups say the move is cruel, violates privacy and lacks independent checks. Lawyers worry it could break a 2022 court ruling. The policy is part of a push to stop illegal crossings, but faces backlash from activists and legal experts.
