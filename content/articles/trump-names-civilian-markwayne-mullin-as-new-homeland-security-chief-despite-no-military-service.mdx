Markwayne Mullin, a civilian chosen by Donald Trump to lead the Department of Homeland Security, has never served in the US military. Yet he talks as if he has war experience, citing private security jobs and vague overseas work. In interviews he claims “special assignments” outside the Pentagon but denies wearing a uniform. Senators are questioning his background as the Senate reviews his nomination, while he continues to blur the line between civilian and veteran.
