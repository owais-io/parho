After Trump’s administration cut thousands of FDA jobs, former workers warn the agency’s food‑safety checks are weaker. A listeria outbreak that killed six and sickened 27 people showed the danger. Fewer inspectors and a smaller CDC surveillance program mean problems can slip through. Fired staff say the agency is losing expertise and science is being politicised, which could hurt U.S. food safety for years.
