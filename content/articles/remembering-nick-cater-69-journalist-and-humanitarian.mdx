Nick Cater, a Guardian reporter who spent over four decades covering African crises, died at 69 after a stroke. He started in local papers, joined the Guardian in 1983, and later helped organise the 1986 Sport Aid event that raised millions for famine relief. Cater reported from 45 countries, worked with charities like Oxfam and Médecins Sans Frontières, and was known for his bravery, including surviving an ambush in Sudan. He is survived by his wife and three children.
