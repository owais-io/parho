- Big fires have burned for weeks in Victoria.   - The Longwood blaze in the north has killed at least one person.   - Over 300 buildings are gone, and about 100 homes may have been lost.   - Fire crews are still fighting, but many communities already have serious damage.   **What’s Happening in the Fire Zone** - The government is closing roads to keep people safe.   - People should not enter fire‑scarred areas.   - Recovery centres are open for anyone who needs help.   **Queensland Floods** - A former cyclone (Koji) lost power after moving up the coast.   - People in Queensland still worry about floods, but conditions are getting cooler.   **NSW Police and Laws** - The state is giving local councils powers to shut down hate‑speech venues.   - If a place ignores the law, councils can cut power and close it for good.   **Meta’s Age‑Protection Move** - Meta removed about 550,000 accounts that were thought to be under 16.   - The company says it’s working with the Australian government to find better rules.   **Adelaide Writers’ Week** - The festival’s chair has stepped down after a controversial decision to disinvite author Randa Abdel‑Fattah.   - Several board members resigned this week, putting the event in a tough spot.   **Other News** - The 2024 Golden Globes have started, and coverage is on the way.   - Keep an eye out for more updates.
