After World War II, Dushan Chimbur escaped war‑torn Yugoslavia and travelled on foot to Italy and a German camp. He came to the UK, farmed near Abergavenny, then worked for 34 years at a steel plant in Corby. Married to Milica, he had two children and was active in the Serbian community. In the 1990s he helped send medical supplies to Balkan hospitals. He beat cancer at 87 and praised NHS care. He left his wife, children, grandchildren and a granddaughter.
