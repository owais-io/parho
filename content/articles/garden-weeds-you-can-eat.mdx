Many people think weeds are just pests, but they can be edible and tasty. Plants like nettle, chickweed, dandelion, and hairy bittercress can be harvested in spring before new crops grow. They’re low‑maintenance and high‑nutrition, adding fresh flavors to salads or soups. A few simple tips help you pick and cook them safely, turning a garden’s unwanted plants into a tasty, healthy bonus.
