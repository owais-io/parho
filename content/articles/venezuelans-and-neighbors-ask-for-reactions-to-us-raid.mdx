The US recently carried out a military raid in Venezuela, sparking global worries about its legality. Venezuela’s former president, Nicolás Maduro, and his wife were brought to a US court and denied charges. The UN says the raid breaks basic international rules. The Guardian is asking Venezuelans—inside the country and abroad—and people in nearby countries like Colombia and Cuba to share how they feel about the event, how it affects them, and what they hope for next.
