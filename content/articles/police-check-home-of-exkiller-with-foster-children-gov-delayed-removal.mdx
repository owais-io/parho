New South Wales police visited a home in February where a convicted killer, Regina Arthurell, was living with two foster kids aged 12 and 14. The state government had been warned about the situation in December but only acted after a radio report the following month. The minister apologised, saying it was unacceptable for children to live with a triple killer. An urgent review of the decision‑making is underway.
