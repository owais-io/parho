The US Citizenship and Immigration Services has stopped processing immigration applications from 19 countries that were under a Trump travel ban. It also put a hold on all asylum requests, saying it needs to check for national‑security risks. The move follows a shooting in Washington DC by an Afghan asylum seeker. USCIS will review people from “high‑risk” countries in the next 90 days and may refer some to law enforcement. The pause also affects people already in the US.
