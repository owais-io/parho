Andrew Bailey, the Bank of England governor, warned that populist politics threaten better living standards and must be challenged by global institutions. He defended the US Federal Reserve’s independence after President Trump tried to interfere. Bailey said countries turning inward hurt trade and growth, and called for stronger cooperation and robust institutions to keep inflation low and protect workers. He highlighted four big hurdles for rich nations: tech slowdown, ageing people, defence costs and climate change.
