Jim Ratcliffe, co‑owner of Manchester United, said Britain had been “colonised” by immigrants, sparking outrage. He later apologized for his wording but stopped short of a full apology, saying he was just talking about immigration control. Anti‑racism groups called his language harmful, and the club issued a statement stressing diversity and inclusion. The incident highlights how words about immigration can inflame old fears and race tensions.
