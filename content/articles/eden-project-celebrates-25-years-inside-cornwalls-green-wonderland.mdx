On its 25th birthday, the Eden Project in Cornwall is a lush, green playground that shows visitors how to protect the planet. Behind the scenes, team members—called sky monkeys—climb the massive biomes to patch and water plants, while engineers pump hot geothermal water to keep the glass walls warm. The attraction has drawn 25 million visitors and helped the local economy, but last year it lost money and cut jobs.
