Tatiana Schlossberg, a journalist and the granddaughter of former President John F. Kennedy, said she has less than a year to live after being diagnosed with acute myeloid leukemia. The diagnosis came after she gave birth to her second child. She has been critical of her cousin Robert Kennedy Jr’s health‑policy decisions, saying cuts to vaccine research and medical funding hurt patients like her. Schlossberg focuses on creating memories with her two kids and staying present in her remaining time.
