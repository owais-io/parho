Nadhim Zahawi, a former Tory minister, has joined Reform UK, sparking worries that the party could turn into a new Conservative group. Other ex‑Tories have left too, and Reform is popular in polls but may lack enough seats to win. Critics say the party is losing its own identity, and Zahawi’s past tax and business problems could hurt its reputation. Tories worry about losing members, but their leader is working to rebuild.
