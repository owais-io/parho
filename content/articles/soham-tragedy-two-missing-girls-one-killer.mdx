In 2002, two ten‑year‑old girls went missing in the small English village of Soham. The police searched for days, but no trace was found. A week later, their bodies were discovered in a ditch outside the village. Investigators traced the crime back to their former school caretaker, Ian Huntley, who had taken the girls into his house and killed them. Huntley was arrested and later sentenced to life imprisonment.
