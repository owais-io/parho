Andrew Alty, a Manchester curtain‑shop owner, received a £22,000 phone bill when his daughter used data in Morocco. The bill doubled to £42,000 after he returned home. The charges were due to an uncapped roaming clause he didn’t understand. Alty fought O2 and Currys, but the Financial Ombudsman said the contract was explained by Currys. After media attention, the companies agreed to drop the fees.
