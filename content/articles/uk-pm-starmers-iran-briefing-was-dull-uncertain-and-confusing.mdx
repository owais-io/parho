Prime Minister Keir Starmer gave a 90‑minute briefing to the liaison committee about the Iran conflict. He struggled to recall details and admitted he didn’t know how long the war could last. MPs asked about Britain’s naval presence, missile threats, and defence spending. Starmer said the UK would only defend itself, not attack, and had no plans to host war criminals. The session felt dull and uncertain, with little new information.
