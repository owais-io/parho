In Melbourne, Neil Blake has been collecting plastic pieces from synthetic turf that wash into local stormwater, showing how much plastic can end up in waterways. In remote northern Australia, Sea Shepherd volunteers clean a turtle‑nesting beach, finding ghost nets and medical waste. The article highlights community science labs, like the Port Phillip EcoCentre, and national programs such as Ausmap that let ordinary people track and fight plastic pollution. Citizen science gives evidence and urges action.
