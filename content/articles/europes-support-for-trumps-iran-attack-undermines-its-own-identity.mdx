In 2003, Europe’s split over the Iraq war pushed the continent to rethink its values and strengthen multilateral action, like the 2015 Iran nuclear deal. Now, many European leaders are quietly backing or ignoring the US‑Israeli strike on Iran, claiming it violates international law but refusing to condemn it. This lets the U.S. and Russia influence Europe, weakening the EU’s collective identity and its ability to act independently.
