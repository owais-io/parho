Australia has made it illegal for anyone under 16 to use social‑media apps like Instagram, TikTok and YouTube. The ban starts on 10 December and will force platforms to shut down or block under‑age accounts, with penalties of up to $49.5 million for non‑compliance. Teens say they’ll miss chatting but some worry about lost support. Other nations, such as Malaysia, Denmark and Norway, are watching closely. Separately, the article discusses worries about AI’s market bubble.
