Derek Philpott, 89, spent most of his life in engineering and later dedicated himself to preserving the everyday stories of Manchester’s industrial past. He created the Openshaw Memories website and published a photo‑book about his childhood in the 1940s and 50s. Born in a working‑class family, he served in the RAF, studied electronics, worked 26 years at Tektronix, and loved the local Guardian. He leaves behind his wife, children and grandchildren.
