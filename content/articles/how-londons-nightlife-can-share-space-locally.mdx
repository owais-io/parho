London’s mayor Sadiq Khan wants a 24‑hour city, but pubs and clubs are disappearing. A new nightlife commission, built on a grassroots report, says decisions should stay in local hands. It suggests real‑time sound checks, seed money for non‑alcohol events like markets and art, and keeping council control. The plan balances nightlife’s economic value with residents’ peace, offering a model for other cities.
