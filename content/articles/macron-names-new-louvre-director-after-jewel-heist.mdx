France has named Christophe Leribault as the Louvre's new chief after a $102 m jewel robbery and ongoing staff strikes. Leribault, who ran the Palace of Versailles, will focus on fixing security gaps and modernising the museum. The previous director, Laurence des Cars, stepped down after criticism of the theft and poor safety measures. The Louvre faces long‑term challenges, including inadequate CCTV and financial mismanagement.
