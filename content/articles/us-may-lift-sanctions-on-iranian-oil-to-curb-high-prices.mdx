The U.S. Treasury says it may let Iran sell up to 140 million barrels of oil that are stuck on tankers at sea, a move aimed at easing price spikes after Iran shut the Strait of Hormuz. The change would let the oil reach global markets for 10–14 days, similar to how Russian oil was handled. The goal is to boost supply and keep prices down, but critics warn it could help Iran fund its war.
