A US judge has stopped the Trump administration from detaining or deporting Imran Ahmed, a British anti‑disinformation activist who runs the Center for Countering Digital Hate. Ahmed was one of five European experts targeted by the state department for trying to pressure tech firms to censor U.S. viewpoints. The judge issued a temporary restraining order, blocking any arrest until Ahmed’s case is heard. The move comes after tensions with big‑tech CEOs and concerns about U.S. and U.K. relations.
