Aidan Chidarikire, who died at 92, helped build Zimbabwe’s pharmacy system after independence. He led the creation of a medicines list and ran nationwide training for pharmacists, making drugs more available in rural areas. After studying pharmacy in Britain, he worked for Boots in London before joining the Zimbabwean Ministry of Health. He partnered with WHO and Denmark to bring resources to the new country and later ran the Pharmacists Council. A principled leader, he leaves behind children and grandchildren.
