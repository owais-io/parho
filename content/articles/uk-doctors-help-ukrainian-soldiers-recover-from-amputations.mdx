At a Ukrainian treatment centre, British doctors from Project Renovator aid soldiers who lost limbs. They provide surgery, prosthetics and rehabilitation, working closely with Ukrainian staff. Patients like Vladislav and Oleksandr share their injuries and hope to get new legs soon. The team encourages group play, such as volleyball, and offers mental‑health support. The centre treats thousands of amputees each month, showing the UK’s support during the war.
