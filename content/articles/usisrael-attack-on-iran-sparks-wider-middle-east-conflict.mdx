The US and Israel carried out a strike on an Iranian gas field, provoking Iran to hit back at regional energy sites. The backlash has damaged a Qatari LNG plant, raising gas prices across Europe and unsettling Gulf economies that depend on oil exports. Washington’s role and its political motives have drawn criticism, and the fight is now seen as a political gamble tied to Trump’s mid‑term election. The turmoil threatens stability and fuels regional tension.
