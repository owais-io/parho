At the British Academy Film Awards, a man with Tourette syndrome shouted the N‑word at actors Michael Jordan and Delroy Lindo. The BBC apologized, saying the word was an involuntary tic, not a deliberate insult. The incident highlights how racial slurs have become normalised, even in cultural events. It also shows the tension between respecting disability and protecting people from hate speech. The article urges a broader discussion about the language we use.
