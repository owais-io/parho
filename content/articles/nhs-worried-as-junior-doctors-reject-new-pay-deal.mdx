Junior doctors in England voted down a government pay proposal, sparking a planned five‑day strike that will hit hospitals stretched by a flu wave. NHS bosses say the strike will cancel tens of thousands of appointments and risk patient safety, especially before Christmas. The British Medical Association (BMA) calls for independent mediation to end the dispute, while the government insists the offer gave doctors better training spots and a pay rise. The clash highlights long‑standing pay and staffing frustrations.
