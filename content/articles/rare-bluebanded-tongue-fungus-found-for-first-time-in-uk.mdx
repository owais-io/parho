A rare blue‑banded tongue fungus, found for the first time in the UK, was spotted at Kingley Vale national nature reserve in West Sussex. Discovered by an amateur mycologist, it’s only the second such species recorded in Europe. The find shows the reserve’s high ecological quality and highlights how careful land care keeps valuable, undisturbed habitats alive and encourages continued protection of these unique ecosystems.
