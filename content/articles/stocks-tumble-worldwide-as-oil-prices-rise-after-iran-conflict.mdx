Stocks tumbled worldwide after a flare‑up in Iran, sending oil and gas prices up. Global shares fell, with the UK’s FTSE 100 dropping over 2 %, Japan’s Nikkei slipping 3 %, and South Korea’s Kospi falling 7 %. Higher energy costs hurt Britain’s plan to curb inflation, and the pound slid to its weakest level against the dollar in three months. The Bank of England is now seen as unlikely to cut rates soon.
