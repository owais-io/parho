March in the U.S. brought a strange mix of weather: heavy rain in Hawaii, snow in Alabama, hot days in California, and sharp temperature swings in the Northeast. Scientists say the climate crisis makes these extremes more common and intense. A strong jet stream lets hot air hit the West while cold air chills the East. The heatwave could have been almost impossible without warming. Experts urge people to prepare for unpredictable weather as disaster‑relief budgets shrink.
