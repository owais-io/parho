Alaa Abd el‑Fattah, a British‑Egyptian rights activist, has said sorry for tweets he posted over ten years ago that many people find offensive. The old messages, written during heated online fights, contain hateful language and criticism of Israel, Britain, and minorities. After the tweets resurfaced, politicians have called for his British citizenship to be revoked. He says the posts were taken out of context and that his real work is defending human rights.
