Politicians and experts in the UK want suicides that happen after someone has been abused at home to be looked at as possible murders. They say the police are not asking the right questions and that too many cases are missed. Up to 1,500 people may die this way each year, but only 98 are recorded. Better training and new data projects could help hold abusers accountable.
