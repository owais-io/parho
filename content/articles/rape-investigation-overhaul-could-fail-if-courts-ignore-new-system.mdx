England and Wales are trying to change how rape cases are investigated with Operation Soteria, which builds cases on the suspect’s behaviour rather than on the victim’s background. But a report says judges and lawyers haven’t been trained in the new approach, and that could lower conviction rates and undo the reforms. The justice secretary has started a review and will give victims free independent legal help later this year. A pilot trial in London will test the new rules.
