A report by former Labour minister Baroness Amos says NHS maternity units are hiding mistakes that hurt mothers and babies. Hospitals are told to keep records clean and deny families answers. Poor staffing leads to long waits, rushed births, and families often have to take legal action. The report highlights that racism and poverty worsen outcomes for minority women. The government will set up a taskforce to tackle these problems before the final report is released.
