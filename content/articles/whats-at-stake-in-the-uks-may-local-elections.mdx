On 7 May, voters across Scotland, Wales, and England head to the polls for a mix of council and parliament elections. Scotland will elect 129 members, Wales 96, and England will choose councillors and mayors in over 130 councils. The races could shift power away from Labour and the Conservatives, giving the SNP, Reform, and Greens more seats. Results may influence leadership battles and the future of the UK union.
