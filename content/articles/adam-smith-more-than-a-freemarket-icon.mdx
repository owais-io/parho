This year marks 250 years since Adam Smith wrote *The Wealth of Nations*, but many think he was just the free‑market hero. In reality, Smith warned against greed, supported public schools, and believed in fairness. Neoliberalists like Milton Friedman boiled his ideas down to “self‑interest” and the invisible hand, missing his deeper focus on social justice and market limits. His work still shapes debates on economics and inequality today.
