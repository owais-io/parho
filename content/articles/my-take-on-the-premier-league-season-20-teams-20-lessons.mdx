I spent the season watching every Premier League club, from Arsenal’s big win at Aston Villa to Sunderland’s surprising rise. I noticed football now feels more like a business than a sport. Clubs use data and set‑piece tricks, players are chosen like products, and fans are treated as customers. Fans are happy with the game, but many feel it’s lost its excitement. The sport still mirrors society, but it’s more about money and analytics than passion.
