The Liberal Party has appointed three new female leaders in South Australia, New South Wales and Victoria, a first for the party. They are young, professional and mostly from the moderate wing. Their appointments come amid calls that the party has a “women problem,” but some fear they face a “glass cliff” – leadership in tough times. The leaders promise fresh ideas and aim to win elections against popular Labor governments, while boosting the party’s appeal to voters.
