UK households spent £226bn on housing last year, up 41% over five years. Mortgage interest rose 9%, with borrowers paying an extra £13,000 a year on average. Renters also saw a 27% rise in costs. London’s costs grew the least, but it still takes the largest share of the national housing spend. New home prices rose modestly, keeping the market steady, and experts warn that mortgage costs may keep climbing if inflation persists.
