During the Olympic skeleton event, Ukrainian athlete Vladyslav Heraskevich was barred from competing because he wanted a helmet with memorial images of soldiers killed in the war. The decision came minutes before the race, leaving teammates stunned. British rider Matt Weston set two new track records and moved into gold‑medal position, while other competitors expressed sympathy for the banned skater. The incident sparked debate over protest rights in sport.
