Harry Barnes, 89, a long‑serving Labour MP for North East Derbyshire, died after a battle with cancer. He began his career as a mature student, taught at universities, and joined Labour in 1957. During his time in Parliament (1987‑2005) he championed miners’ safety, electoral reform, and peace in Northern Ireland, while staying true to his socialist roots. He remains remembered for his principled, flexible approach.
