Redhills, the old Durham miners’ union hall, has reopened after a £14 million restoration. The building, once a meeting place for miners, now offers guided tours, weddings, concerts and community events. Rather than being a museum, it’s being turned into a living hub for the local community, with plans for affordable housing, local heating and social care projects. The project keeps the spirit of the miners’ parliament alive.
