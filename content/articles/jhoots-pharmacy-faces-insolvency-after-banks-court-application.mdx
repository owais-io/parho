A national pharmacy chain, Jhoots Pharmacy, is facing insolvency after Lloyds Bank filed a high‑court request to appoint an administrator. The company, which runs over 100 outlets, has been accused of not paying freelance locum pharmacists £670,000 and has a debt of more than £5 million. MPs criticised the chain for unpaid staff, landlords and drugs handling. The filing follows a string of court judgments and shows the business may not stay afloat.
