The search for Nancy Guthrie’s mother has sparked outrage when YouTube sleuths start streaming the crime scene. The “true‑crime” livestreamers, like Jonathan Riches, post shaky footage and false tips that mislead viewers and interfere with police work. Authorities ask for no food deliveries to the house, and the sheriff says media should respect privacy. Journalists warn that clickbait can slow investigations and spread misinformation.
