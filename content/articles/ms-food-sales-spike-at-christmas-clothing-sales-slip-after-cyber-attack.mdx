Marks & Spencer sold a lot of food during the Christmas period, but its clothing sales fell because the company is still dealing with the fallout from last year’s cyber‑attack. The breach cut online orders for weeks, hurt shoppers’ confidence, and slowed foot traffic. While food items and online grocery sales grew, the company is shifting more stores to food and closing some clothing‑only shops to adapt.
