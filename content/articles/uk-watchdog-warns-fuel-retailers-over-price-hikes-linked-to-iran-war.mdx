The UK competition watchdog (CMA) has warned fuel retailers to stop raising prices after the US‑Iran war pushed oil costs above $100 a barrel. It will collect cost and sales data, review margins, and look for “rocket‑and‑feather” price spikes. Petrol rose 5.5% (7p a litre) and diesel 11% (16p) since the conflict began. Government officials say firms can’t profit from the crisis.
