Norway, with just 5.7 million people, finished top of the 2022 Winter Olympics medal table. The country won 18 gold medals and 41 overall, beating the United States, which came in second with 12 golds and 33 medals. Cross‑country skier Johannes Klæbo won six golds, the most of any athlete. Norway’s success is linked to a culture that values fun and broad participation over early competition, and a strong focus on sports like cross‑country, biathlon and ski jumping.
