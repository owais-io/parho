Economists say $5.4 trillion will move from boomers to their heirs over the next 20 years, but Australians have mixed feelings. Some are excited to receive money that could buy homes or fund futures. Others fear family conflict, worry that inheritances will end up in aged‑care costs, or feel the promised windfall is a myth, especially for millennials. Many say clear plans and patience help manage the surprise.
