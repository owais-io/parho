The UK regulator has fined two former Carillion directors for hiding serious problems from investors before the company collapsed in 2018. Richard Adam and Zafar Khan knew about deep financial trouble but didn't warn shareholders or the board, the FCA said. They will pay £232,800 and £138,900, and Khan faces an 11‑year ban. The scandal followed Carillion’s £7 bn debt, 3,000 job losses and huge overruns on public projects.
