Britain’s new prime minister, Sir Keir Starmer, finds himself at a loss as the United States pulls back from being Europe’s main protector. The article argues that Britain’s old role – relying on U.S. support and its own institutions to keep order – is fading. With a weak economy, little manufacturing, and a cautious Treasury, the UK can’t supply Ukraine or shape European security, and it needs a fresh vision for its future.
