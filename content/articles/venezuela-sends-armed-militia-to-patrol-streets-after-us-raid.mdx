After a US raid in Caracas, Venezuela’s government sent armed militia groups to patrol streets, stop cars, and check people’s phones to show control. Residents report fear and uncertainty as checks happen on motorbikes with rifles. The interim president, Delcy Rodríguez, denies US control and calls for calm, while security forces have detained journalists and shot at drones. The situation remains tense, with opposition leaders on the move and the government trying to maintain authority.
