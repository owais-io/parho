In Australia’s national day of mourning for the 15 victims of last month’s Bondi beach terror attack, the prime minister urged acts of kindness. Children in Sydney cooked 350 meals for vulnerable people, while wreaths were laid on the beach. Communities across New South Wales and Victoria marked the day with memorial services, flag‑half‑mast ceremonies, and acts of charity, showing solidarity and support for the Jewish community.
