Victoria wants to start charging visitors to see the Twelve Apostles, including parking and a visitor centre. About 2.2 million people visit the rocks each year, with the government expecting that number to rise to 3 million by 2032. The fee is meant to ease crowding and protect the environment, following similar schemes in US national parks, Stonehenge, and other Australian sites. Residents worry it may push tourists to unsafe spots, and some say the park should stay free.
