China’s annual ‘Two Sessions’—meetings of the National People’s Congress and a consultative body—start this week in Beijing. The events set next year’s budget, growth target (about 4.5%), and the 15‑year Five‑Year Plan, aiming for tech self‑reliance and a stronger army that could challenge Taiwan. Meanwhile, Xi’s recent purge of senior military leaders, including nine delegate expulsions, shows he’s tightening loyalty as the country faces US pressure. The meetings will highlight economic challenges like youth unemployment and a property market.
