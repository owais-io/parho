Japan’s first female prime minister, Sanae Takaichi, has called an early election so voters can decide if she should stay in office. She hopes to use her high approval ratings to win back the ruling Liberal Democratic Party’s lost majority. The move comes amid a tense dispute with China over Taiwan, trade bans, and domestic worries about rising costs and political scandals.
