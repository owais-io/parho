Pete Hegseth, the new U.S. defense secretary, allegedly took four names off a list of officers being promoted to one‑star generals—two women and two Black men. He did this after the army’s top chief refused to drop them, arguing they had solid, decades‑long records. Hegseth’s move may breach military rules that let the secretary approve or reject an entire list, not individual names. The change is now under review by the White House and Senate, sparking accusations of bias and political interference.
