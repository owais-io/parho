Former Venezuelan leader Nicolás Maduro, held in Manhattan jail, walked into a federal court with shackles and a smile. He is charged with narco‑terrorism, which could mean life in prison. Maduro and his wife Cilia Flores were seen together, wearing jail clothes, while supporters and opponents shouted slogans outside. The case reflects U.S. pressure on Venezuela and shows how the Trump era mixed foreign policy with domestic law.
