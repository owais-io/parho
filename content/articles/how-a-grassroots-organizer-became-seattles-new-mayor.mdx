In Seattle's 2024 mayoral race, long‑time community organizer Katie Wilson, who was little known before the campaign, won by building a volunteer army and earning trust. She focused on transit, housing and minimum‑wage wins. Her small, grassroots team outpaced a money‑heavy incumbent, Bruce Harrell, who ran attack ads. Wilson's win shows voters want leaders who reflect their everyday lives. It signals a shift toward more inclusive representation.
