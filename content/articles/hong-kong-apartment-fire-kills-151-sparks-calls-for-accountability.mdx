A blaze tore through seven nearby flats in Hong Kong’s Wang Fuk Court, killing at least 151 people. The tragedy echoes the Grenfell Tower fire in the UK and has drawn sharp criticism of the city’s building safety standards. Residents had complained months before about poor construction materials, and now the government faces accusations of negligence and corruption. Public anger is growing as citizens demand answers and stronger safety laws.
