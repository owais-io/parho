Yousef Pezeshkian, the 45‑year‑old son of Iran’s president, keeps a public diary about the war in Iran. He writes about missile sounds, internet blackouts, his own fears and hopes, and how the fighting affects everyday life. He talks about his father’s apology to Gulf states, the limits of Iran’s weapons, and the importance of national unity. The diary mixes angry, sad, and hopeful moments, showing how ordinary people feel in the conflict.
