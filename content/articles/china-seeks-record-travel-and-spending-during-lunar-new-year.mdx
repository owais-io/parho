China’s nine‑day Lunar New Year holiday is expected to spark a record 9.5 billion travel trips as millions head home for family celebrations. The government hopes the holiday boost domestic spending, issuing 360 m yuan in vouchers to spur consumer activity. While the country’s savings rate stays high, the plan aims to grow service sectors like entertainment and healthcare. Popular films and new‑year‑specific services could help lift retail sales.
