Trump has been granting many pardons to people convicted of fraud and white‑collar crimes. His recent list includes former Puerto Rican governor Wanda Vázquez, billionaire fraudster Mark Rossini, and others. The moves have cost victims $1.3 billion in restitution and fines. Democrats say the pardons give fraudsters a free pass, while Trump says he is correcting over‑prosecution. The controversy has sparked angry replies from politicians like California’s Gov. Newsom.
