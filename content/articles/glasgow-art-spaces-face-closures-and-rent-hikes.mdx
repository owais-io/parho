Glasgow’s biggest art venues are shutting or facing huge rent hikes, leaving artists and audiences in limbo. The Centre for Contemporary Arts closed after months of financial trouble, and tenants at Trongate 103 are threatened with eviction because of a four‑fold rent increase. City councils and the SNP are blamed for losing strategic support, while the Scottish government has promised more money but little action yet, and the loss of community hubs that once sparked local talent.
