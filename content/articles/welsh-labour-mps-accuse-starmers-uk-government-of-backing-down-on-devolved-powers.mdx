Eleven Welsh Labour parliament members sent a letter to Prime Minister Keir Starmer, saying his government is undoing devolved powers. They criticized the UK‑funded Pride in Place town‑centre scheme, claiming it was a devolved matter, and warned the UK is failing to move forward on devolution of justice, policing, rail and the crown estate. The attack highlights Labour’s weak position in Wales, where the party trails in polls and has lost seats.
