In a Fort Worth courtroom, nine left‑wing protestors who gathered outside an ICE detention center on July 4 face the first U.S. case that brands them as part of an “antifa” terrorist cell. The government says the group brought guns, set off fireworks, and vandalised property, while the defense argues they planned a peaceful protest and didn’t intend violence. The trial tests how far the Trump administration can go in declaring a political movement a domestic terror group.
