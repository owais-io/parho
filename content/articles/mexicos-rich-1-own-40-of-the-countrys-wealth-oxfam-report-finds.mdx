A new Oxfam study shows Mexico’s top 1% hold 40% of all wealth while nearly 19 million people struggle to afford basic needs. The richest 22 billionaires now control $219 bn, a sharp rise over the last five years. Though poverty fell 13 million during President López Obrador’s term thanks to wage hikes, the gap remains huge. The story follows residents in Santa Lucía Reacomodo, where working‑class families live next to gated luxury estates, highlighting stark social divides.
