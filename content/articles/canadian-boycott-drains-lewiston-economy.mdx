A boycott by Canadians against US border towns in New York, led by anger over Trump’s tariffs and anti‑Canadian comments, has cut tourist spending in Lewiston and other Niagara area towns. Bakery owner Aimee Loughran reports a 30 % drop in sales, and antique shop owners see a 20 % decline. Local hotels and retailers are losing business, and tourism agencies are redirecting marketing to other U.S. states. The boycott threatens the region’s economy and could shift focus to sports events.
