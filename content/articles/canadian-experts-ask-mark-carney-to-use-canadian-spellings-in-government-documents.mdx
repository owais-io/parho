Mark Carney, former Bank of England governor, used British spellings in recent Canadian government papers. A group of linguists wrote a letter to the federal government, urging that Canadian documents use Canadian English spelling. They warned that switching to British spelling could confuse people and undermine Canada’s unique language identity. The letter cited Canadian history and pride in a distinct spelling style that blends influences from Indigenous and European languages.
