A Los Angeles jury will decide whether social media giants Meta and YouTube made their apps addictive and harmed a 20‑year‑old girl. The case, part of a big lawsuit against several platforms, argues that features like endless scrolling and auto‑play keep kids glued and can lead to depression, self‑harm and body image problems. The trial included CEOs and expert witnesses, and if the jury finds the companies liable, they could face large fines and change how they work.
