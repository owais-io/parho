Berkeley has passed a new law called Zone 0 that gives about 900 homes a year to clear trees and shrubs within 5 feet of their houses to stop embers from starting fires. The city offers money and inspections to help. Some residents hate losing shade and flowers, while scientists debate how much the rule actually helps. The plan follows deadly Los Angeles blazes and aims to protect neighborhoods and lower insurance costs.
