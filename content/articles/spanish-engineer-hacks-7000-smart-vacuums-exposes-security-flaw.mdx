Spanish software engineer discovered that he could control about 7,000 DJI Romo vacuums worldwide when reverse‑engineering the device. He used a PlayStation 5 controller, found the vacuums shared credentials, and gained camera feeds and location info from each. He reported the bug to The Verge, DJI fixed it. The incident highlights that many smart‑home products have security overlooked for speed and cost. Manufacturers must enforce unique passwords and better design to protect privacy.
