Three white‑tailed eagles that were part of the UK’s re‑introduction programme have gone missing. In Sussex, Wales and Scotland, satellite tags on the birds were cut off or stopped working, pointing to possible illegal killings. Police and the National Wildlife Crime Unit are investigating, while the RSPB is offering a £10,000 reward for clues. The disappearances threaten the fragile success of the re‑introduction project and urge the public to come forward.
