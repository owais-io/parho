The role of Scotland’s top law officer, Dorothy Bain, is being questioned after it was revealed that she told the first minister about Peter Murrell’s embezzlement charges nearly a year before they became public. Bain’s dual job—leading prosecutions and advising the government—has sparked calls for her resignation and a review to split the roles. The government has delayed publishing a report on this issue, raising concerns about transparency and potential conflict of interest.
