Barry Johnson, a respected Guardian journalist, died at 67 after a battle with bowel cancer. He worked for the paper from 1986 to 2021, editing home news and overseeing subediting. Born in Liverpool, he studied science at Cambridge and had early jobs in factories and as a driver before turning to journalism. He was married to Jay Sivell, had two daughters and a grandson, and loved crossword puzzles, poetry, and cooking.
