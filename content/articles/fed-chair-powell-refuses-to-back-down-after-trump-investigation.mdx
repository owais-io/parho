Jerome Powell, the head of the U.S. central bank, said he would not give in after the Trump administration opened a criminal probe against him. The investigation is tied to Fed renovations and is seen as a political attack. Powell has led the Fed for eight years, keeping rates steady after COVID and high inflation. He stresses the bank’s independence from politics. Trump has repeatedly criticized him and wants a new chair.
