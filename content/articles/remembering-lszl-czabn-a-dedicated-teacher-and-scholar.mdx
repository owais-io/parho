Dr. László Czabán, a senior lecturer at Manchester’s business school, passed away suddenly at 62. Born in Budapest, he moved to the UK in 1993 to study Hungary’s post‑socialist economy. His book on Hungary’s 1990s crises is still the best single study of that period. László taught in Leeds, then helped launch Manchester’s popular MSc in business analysis. He left a legacy of students, colleagues, and a loving family.
