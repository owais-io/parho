A UK House of Lords committee says that Britain’s creative sector should not be left out when governments plan changes to copyright rules for artificial‑intelligence. The report warns that letting tech firms use novels, songs and pictures without paying or crediting the artists would hurt the £146 billion‑a‑year industry. It calls for a licensing system, tougher rules on AI training data, and stronger protections against deepfakes. The government is still deciding how to update the law.
