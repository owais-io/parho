The article compares the rise of Reform UK with the split from the Conservatives, noting that both parties push a similar agenda of Brexit, low taxes, and tough immigration rules. It points out that former Tories like Nigel Farage and James Jenrick now champion the same ideas that once defined the Conservative Party. While the conflict feels personal, the two groups are almost indistinguishable, and a future merger is possible.
