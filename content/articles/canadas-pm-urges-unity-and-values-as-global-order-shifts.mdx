Canada’s prime minister spoke at a fortress in Quebec City, urging the nation to act as a guiding light amid a changing global order. He stressed national unity, defended Canadian values, and criticized the U.S. for not being “enough grateful.” Facing opposition concerns over trade trips, he pledged faster infrastructure projects and a tackle on the cost‑of‑living crisis. He also acknowledged Indigenous land rights and met the city’s snowman mascot. Canada’s Liberals sit just short of a majority.
