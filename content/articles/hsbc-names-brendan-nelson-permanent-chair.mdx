HSBC finally settled on Brendan Nelson as its new chair after months of uncertainty. The bank had been searching for a successor to Mark Tucker, who stepped down in September, and even looked at people like former UK chancellor George Osborne and Goldman’s Asia boss. Nelson, who joined the board in 2023 and was acting chair, was chosen as a permanent leader. The move has sparked worries about how long the bank will keep searching and how secure its future leadership looks.
