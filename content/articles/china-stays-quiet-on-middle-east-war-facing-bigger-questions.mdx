China has long traded oil and money with Iran, but its economy is slowing and it’s staying silent about the recent Middle East conflict. The war costs China about half a billion dollars a day and shows how little influence Beijing has when it comes to security. At the same time, China sees chances in renewables, reconstruction and new Gulf ties. The war exposes limits to China’s “strategic ambiguity” and raises doubts about how it will act next, even on Taiwan.
