Letters say Welsh rugby used to be a powerhouse, thanks to small, skillful players and strong community support. Back then clubs beat the All Blacks and fans sang hymns at games. Now the sport focuses on money and big bodies, and Wales can’t supply enough big players. The result is fewer fans and empty stadiums, so editors urge rugby back into schools to revive interest.
