Many politicians register campaign web pages before their campaigns officially start. In the UK, Angela Rayner’s unfinished site appeared early, raising questions. Rishi Sunak, Liz Truss, Penny Mordaunt and Kemi Badenoch also had domains booked months ahead of their leadership bids. This can reveal intentions too soon and invite leaks. Similar blunders hit Jeb Bush and Mitt Romney in the US, showing that early online presence can backfire.
