Federal investigators examined Jeffrey Epstein’s relationship with a Customs and Border Protection officer at St. Thomas airport. Documents show subpoenas were issued for three other officers at the same airport and for two additional officers in St. Thomas and Florida who had contact with Epstein via emails and texts. No CBP officer was charged, and investigators found no evidence they were involved in Epstein’s crimes. The article also mentions calls for justice after Andrew Mountbatten‑Windsor’s arrest and survivors’ frustration.
