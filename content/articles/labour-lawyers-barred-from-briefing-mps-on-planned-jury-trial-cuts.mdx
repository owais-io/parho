Labour lawyers say they were prevented from briefing MPs about a bill that would reduce jury trials in England and Wales. MP Karl Turner, leading a group of backbenchers, says the plan would shift thousands of cases to judges and magistrates to tackle a backlog. Up to 65 Labour MPs might vote against the bill, but the government insists the changes are needed for faster justice.
