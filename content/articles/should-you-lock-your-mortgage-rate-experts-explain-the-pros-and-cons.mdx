Australia’s Reserve Bank raised interest rates, squeezing homeowners. One way to stop payments from jumping is to lock a fixed rate for 1–5 years, giving predictable bills but no benefit if rates fall. Variable rates can change up or down. Experts say swapping banks can cut rates, but you need good credit. Many borrowers are too stressed to refinance, so debt help lines and hardship plans are vital.
