The Local Government Association says councils in England will pay almost £4bn for temporary accommodation for the homeless by 2029‑30, up from about £1.5bn extra spent since 2017‑18. The gap between what councils pay and what they get back from the government will widen to £3.9bn in four years. Councils urge the government to raise housing benefit rates to cover costs, as temporary stays rise after COVID.
