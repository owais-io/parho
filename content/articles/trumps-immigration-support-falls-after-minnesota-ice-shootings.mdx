A recent NBC poll shows that 49% of U.S. adults strongly disapprove of President Trump’s immigration policies, a sharp jump from 38% last summer. The rise follows the deadly shootings of two civilians, Renee Good and Alex Pretti, by ICE agents in Minneapolis. Many voters want ICE reformed or abolished, and trust in the federal government is low. The results also reveal a growing split between traditional Republicans and Trump supporters over how to handle ICE.
