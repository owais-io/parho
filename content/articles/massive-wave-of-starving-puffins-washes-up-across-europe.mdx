Storms in the Atlantic have caused the biggest wave of dead seabirds ever seen in Europe. More than 38,000 birds, mostly puffins, have washed up on coastlines from Portugal to Scotland. They died of starvation, struggling to find food in rough seas. The event shows how climate change and heavy storms can kill thousands of birds. Conservationists worry it could hurt already threatened seabird populations.
