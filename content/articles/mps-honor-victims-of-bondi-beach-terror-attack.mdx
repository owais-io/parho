Australian MPs gathered in parliament to mourn 15 people killed when two gunmen, said to be inspired by Islamic State, attacked a Hanukah event on Bondi beach. Prime Minister Albanese moved a condolence motion, while leaders called for stronger hate‑speech laws and tighter gun control. MPs praised a Muslim by‑stander who stopped a gunman and urged the nation to unite against antisemitism and hate. The speech highlighted the lasting pain of families and the need for national solidarity.
