Vince Zampella, who helped create the Call of Duty games and led several other popular titles, died in a car crash in California at age 55. He was the head of Respawn Entertainment and had worked on games like Medal of Honor, Titanfall, Apex Legends, and Battlefield 6. Zampella started his career in the mid-1990s and was a key figure in the gaming industry. His death is a big loss for gamers worldwide.
