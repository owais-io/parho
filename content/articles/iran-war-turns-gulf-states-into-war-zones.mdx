The war in Iran has turned Gulf cities like Dubai into battle zones, with drones and missiles striking oil hubs and luxury hotels. Tourism has dropped and many workers can’t leave. The conflict shows that Gulf states’ US‑backed bases may not protect them, and oil flow through the Strait of Hormuz is blocked. The crisis also exposes the unequal impact on wealthy visitors versus migrant workers stuck in the region.
