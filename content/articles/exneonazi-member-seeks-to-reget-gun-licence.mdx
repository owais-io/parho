Joshua Ryan Hoath, 27, was part of a disbanded Australian neo‑Nazi group and had his gun licence cancelled after police found 16 firearms and Nazi‑related books in his home. He says the licence was taken away because of his political views, not because he was dangerous. Hoath is asking a Queensland tribunal to give him back the licence, claiming it violates his freedom of speech. The case may involve constitutional arguments.
