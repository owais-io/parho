A new study shows that Sudan’s Rapid Support Forces deliberately destroyed villages, farms and livestock in North Darfur to starve the people. Researchers used satellite pictures and fire‑detection sensors to map 41 attacks, finding a huge rise in fires and that most villages stopped functioning after the raids. The evidence could be used in international courts, and shows how the RSF’s tactics have turned the war into a fight over food.
