After 25 years of waiting, New Zealand finally got its first Ikea store. Thousands queued on the opening day, but traffic jams stayed mild. Shoppers cheered for the famous meatballs and even sang “meatballs” to reporters. Prime Minister Christopher Luxon cut the ribbon, but a quick customer beat him to the front. Ikea promised low prices, like the $99 Billy bookcase, hoping to give locals a budget-friendly option amid economic worries.
