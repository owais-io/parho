Gas is still the main price‑setter in Britain’s electricity market, so when gas prices jump, families see higher bills even if renewables supply most of the power. The article says that simply adding more wind and solar won’t solve the problem; the market design itself must change. A single‑buyer model that removes gas from the price mix and public investment to pay for new clean power would lower costs and stop rent‑taking profits.
