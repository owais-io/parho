After Gaza was badly damaged by Israeli strikes, the United Nations says rebuilding could cost about $70 billion. US companies and officials are already talking about who will run the logistics, trucking and construction work, even though no contracts have been awarded yet. A private firm called Gothams was seen as a front‑runner but pulled out after security worries. A top‑level “master contractor” could earn up to $1.7 billion a year from trucking fees.
