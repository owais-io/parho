South African video artist Gabrielle Goliath is suing the arts minister, Gayton McKenzie, after he stopped her from representing South Africa at the Venice Biennale. McKenzie said her work, which criticises Israel’s actions in Gaza, was “highly divisive.” Goliath wants the high court to let her show her three‑video piece, “Elegy,” that honours Palestinian victims. The case highlights tensions over art, politics and freedom of expression in South Africa.
