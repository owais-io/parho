Fake AI videos and photos of Iran‑related war events are spreading fast online. The Guardian article shows how some images claim missile blasts in Tel Aviv or soldiers captured, but they’re fabricated. Even reputable news pictures have been wrongly accused of manipulation, sparking disputes like the New York Times’ response. Experts advise readers to stay skeptical, follow trusted fact‑checkers, look for context, and pause before sharing. This misinformation can mislead people about the real cost of war.
