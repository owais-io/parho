Nigel de Gruchy, who led Britain’s largest teachers’ union from 1990 to 2002, died at 82. He championed higher pay and less testing for teachers, winning court cases and pushing the government to create a pay review body. Known for sharp speeches, he also backed teachers who refused to work with disruptive students, sparking controversy. He grew the union’s membership from 120,000 to over 200,000. After retiring, he ran for Parliament as a Labour candidate.
