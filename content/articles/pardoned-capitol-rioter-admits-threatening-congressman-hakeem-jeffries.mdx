In 2021, Christopher Moynihan stormed the U.S. Capitol and was later pardoned by President Trump. Now, he has pleaded guilty to a misdemeanor harassment charge after sending threatening texts saying he would kill House Minority Leader Hakeem Jeffries. He will be sentenced in April. The case highlights that threats against elected officials are illegal and not protected speech. The prosecutor said such threats endanger public safety. Moynihan’s actions follow a 21‑month prison term for obstruction charges.
