Palermo’s famous Mondello beach, run for a century by the private company Italo Belga, has sparked protests over high prices and limited public space. Sicily’s authorities revoked the lease after investigations revealed its maintenance subcontractor GM Edil had staff linked to the Cosa Nostra mafia. The decision ends the long‑running concession, and city officials must decide whether a new firm will manage the beach or keep it fully public.
