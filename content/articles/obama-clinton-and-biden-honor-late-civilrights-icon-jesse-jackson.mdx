Three former U.S. presidents—Joe Biden, Barack Obama, and Bill Clinton—shared heartfelt tributes after the death of civil‑rights icon Jesse Jackson, who was 84. Biden praised Jackson as a “man of God and of the people,” Obama called him a “true giant,” and Clinton spoke of decades of friendship. The former president also noted Jackson’s lifelong fight for dignity and respect, underscoring his lasting impact.
