On Tuesday, 43 women who claim they were lured into forced domestic work by Opus Dei will gather in Buenos Aires. They say they were promised schooling, but instead worked long hours, had their mail read, and could’t see family. The Vatican, under Pope Leo, is reviewing Opus Dei’s rules after these accusations, and Argentine prosecutors are investigating. The conference aims to give the women a voice and push for church reforms.
