Seven public‑housing towers for older residents in Melbourne will be torn down, so people like Lana Sawyer and Anne Downey have to leave their homes. The state said the move starts in July and will give residents help and a chance to stay close to where they are. Many residents, many of them over 70, are upset and have begun a class‑action lawsuit to stop the demolition. The government says new homes will be safer and cheaper.
