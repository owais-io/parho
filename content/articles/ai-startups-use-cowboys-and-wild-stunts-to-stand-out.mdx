In New York, AI firm Lunos hired a cowboy to lasso the Wall Street bull to get noticed. The stunt was part of a trend where 90,000+ AI start‑ups use eye‑catching tricks instead of ordinary ads to stand out. Other companies hired horses at tech conferences or even staged topless performances to create buzz on LinkedIn and at industry events. The moves bring quick web traffic, social media likes, and some early sales, but raise questions about gimmick‑based growth.
