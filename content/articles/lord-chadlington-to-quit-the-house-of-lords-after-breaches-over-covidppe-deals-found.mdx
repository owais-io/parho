Lord Chadlington, a long‑time Tory peer, will leave the House of Lords after a standards probe found he broke rules by pushing a company in which he had a stake to supply COVID‑19 protective gear to the NHS. The company got big contracts through a “VIP lane” and later failed, owing taxes and providing unusable gear. He also failed to help earlier investigations. He’s quitting the Conservative party and retiring.
