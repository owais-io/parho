Trump announced that the United States will no longer launch a second wave of military actions against Venezuela, citing the country’s cooperation on oil projects and the release of political prisoners. He highlighted U.S. naval presence in the Caribbean as a deterrent and said that the new policy comes after talks with Venezuelan officials and a brief meeting with opposition leader María Corina Machado. The move aims to improve U.S.–Venezuela relations while keeping the option to act if needed.
