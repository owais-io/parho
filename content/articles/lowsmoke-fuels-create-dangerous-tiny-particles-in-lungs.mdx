Researchers found that fuels marketed as ‘smokeless’ actually release many more ultrafine particles than regular coal or wood. These tiny particles are smaller than light waves and can lodge deep in our lungs, increasing the risk of lung damage and cancer. The study looked at Irish homes and found higher particle levels in winter evenings, even higher than in big Chinese cities. The results suggest that just swapping one solid fuel for another isn’t enough to clean up air pollution.
