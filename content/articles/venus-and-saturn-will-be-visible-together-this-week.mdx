Venus and Saturn will line up in the western sky over the next week. Venus is bright, Saturn is dimmer but visible after sunset. The pair will move closer each night and by March 8 look almost side‑by‑side as dusk deepens. A clear western horizon helps you spot Saturn as it sinks lower. Both planets shine steady, not twinkling, reflecting sunlight. Observers anywhere with an unobstructed westward view can watch this celestial show.
