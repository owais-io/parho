Yara International CEO Svein Tore Holsether warns that a prolonged war in Iran could raise fertilizer prices, hurt crop yields, and threaten food supplies, especially in poorer nations. Prices for key chemicals like urea and ammonia have spiked after attacks near the Gulf, and gas costs have surged, adding to production costs. The potential Hormuz choke could cut supply, worsening hunger risks worldwide, says the UN WFP.
