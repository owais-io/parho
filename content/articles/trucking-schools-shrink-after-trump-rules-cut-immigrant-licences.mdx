Truck driving schools in the U.S. are losing students and revenue because Trump‑era rules now require drivers without U.S. citizenship or permanent residency to meet English‑language standards and hold commercial driver licences. Start CDL, a school run by Ukrainian and Belarusian immigrants, fell from 100 students to 28 after the new regulations. The tougher rules also threaten to revoke school accreditations, pushing many schools toward closure.
