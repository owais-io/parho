After Israel and U.S. attacked Iran, dozens of Middle East countries shut their airspace, canceling over 1,000 flights and stranding hundreds of thousands of passengers. Major hubs like Dubai, Doha and Abu Dhabi were hit, with some aircraft damaged. Airlines had to reroute planes over Saudi Arabia, adding time and fuel costs, which could push ticket prices up. Travelers were left stuck, with many airlines offering no‑extra rebooking options.
