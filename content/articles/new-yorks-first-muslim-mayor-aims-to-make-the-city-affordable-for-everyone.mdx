New York’s new mayor, Zohran Mamdani, is the city’s first Muslim, first South Asian, and youngest mayor in over a century. He promised to make NYC more affordable by offering free universal childcare, boosting cheap housing and cutting everyday costs. To pay for it, he plans to raise taxes on the wealthy and big companies. His bold move could inspire other left‑wing parties, but he’ll face pushback from the federal government and business interests.
