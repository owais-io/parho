After a mass shooting at a Hanukkah event, Australia’s government wants to make hate speech harder to spread. The plan will add new penalties for people who preach hate or violence, give the Home Affairs Minister power to reject visas for hate‑speakers, and set up a system to list hate‑based organisations. The exact wording and timelines are still being worked out, but the changes aim to stop hateful language from turning into real danger.
