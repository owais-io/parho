Apple beat Wall Street forecasts in its first‑quarter earnings, mainly because iPhone sales surged. The company made $143.8 billion, up 16 % from last year, and the new iPhone 17 line sparked a 23 % rise in iPhone revenue. Sales in China were especially strong, rising 38 %. Apple also announced a refreshed Siri using Google’s AI and plans for a folding phone by 2026. Despite AI worries, the strong sales give investors confidence.
