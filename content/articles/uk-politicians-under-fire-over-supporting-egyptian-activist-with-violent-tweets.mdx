After an Egyptian activist who was freed from prison and brought to the UK, a wave of criticism hit UK politicians. The man, Alaa Abd el‑Fattah, has a history of online posts calling for violence against Jews and police. Both Labour and Conservative leaders had campaigned for his release and citizenship, but the posts revealed a lack of background checks. The government now says it condemns his remarks and is reviewing the case.
