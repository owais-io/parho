After the Bondi shooting, Australia's Labor Party says it will move faster to carry out a 49‑point antisemitism plan written by special envoy Jillian Segal. The government has already added new hate‑crime laws, banned Nazi symbols and offered $4 m to schools, but critics say it has been slow and that some measures could curb free speech. Labor says it will keep working on the plan and wants a clear timeline.
