Peru’s congress chose José María Balcázar, a 78‑year‑old left‑wing lawmaker who has openly supported child marriage, to serve as interim president until July. He won over conservative favourite María del Carmen Alva after the previous president was ousted. Balcázar’s past comments praising sexual relations between teenagers and teachers and a corruption investigation have sparked anger across the political spectrum. People fear he may push for a fast election or other changes.
