US Southern Command said its forces fired on a suspected drug‑smuggling boat in the eastern Pacific, killing two people and leaving one survivor. The Coast Guard rescued the survivor and handed the bodies to Costa Rica. Critics say the strikes may violate international law, and some argue the campaign hasn’t stopped drugs, since much fentanyl comes from land routes in Mexico. Since September, the US has killed at least 159 people on such boats.
