Families of people hurt in the 2017 Manchester Arena bombing have written to the Prime Minister, saying MI5 failed to protect them and must be held to higher standards of openness. They want MI5 fully involved in a new law that stops cover‑ups after disasters. An inquiry found MI5 could have stopped the attack if it had acted on key intelligence before the bombing. The families also seek better accountability and compensation for victims.
