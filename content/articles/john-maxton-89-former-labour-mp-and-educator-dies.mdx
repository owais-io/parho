John Maxton, a long‑serving Labour MP for Glasgow Cathcart, died at 89. He helped keep the party alive in opposition, then became a peer who pushed for new tech, electronic voting and ID cards. He fought the poll tax, backed Scottish de‑volution, and defended the purchase of Churchill papers. A teacher, jazz fan and runner, Maxton was the nephew of ILP legend James Maxton. He left his wife, three sons and three grandchildren.
