UK ministers told HS2 to consider cutting train speeds from 224 mph to 186 mph. The aim is to cut huge costs and get the line up and running sooner in the 2030s. HS2’s new chief executive, Mark Wild, is reviewing the plan. He will report on how much money could be saved by slower trains before the summer break. The government is still hoping the project stays under £100 bn.
