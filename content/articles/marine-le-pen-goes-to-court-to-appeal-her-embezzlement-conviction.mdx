Marine Le Pen, the leader of France’s far‑right National Rally, is back in court to appeal a conviction for stealing €4.8 million of EU funds between 2004 and 2016. The new trial will last until 12 February and will decide if she can run in the 2027 presidential race. If found guilty again, she may still be able to run if the ban ends before March 2027. Her party’s young leader, Jordan Bardella, could take her spot.
