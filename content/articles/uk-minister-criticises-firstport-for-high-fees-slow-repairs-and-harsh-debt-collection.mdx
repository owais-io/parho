A UK minister has raised serious concerns about FirstPort, Britain's biggest leasehold manager, after many residents complained about high fees, slow repairs, and harsh debt‑collection tactics. MPs say the company charges too much and fails to fix problems promptly, leading to flooding, mold, and costly legal battles. FirstPort says it is improving, but the minister says more must be done. The government plans new laws to regulate leasehold management and even stop selling new leasehold flats.
