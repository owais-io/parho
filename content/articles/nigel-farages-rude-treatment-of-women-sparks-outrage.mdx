Nigel Farage, leader of Reform UK, has been accused of treating female journalists harshly, calling them “silly” and dismissing their work. His comments, coupled with hiring an anti‑abortion adviser and pledging to repeal the Equality Act, have raised fears that the party is pushing a sexist agenda. Women’s rights groups and left‑wing politicians have condemned the move, warning that it could hurt women's workplace protections and widen gender inequality.
