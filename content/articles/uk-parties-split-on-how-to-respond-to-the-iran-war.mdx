UK leaders and parties are debating how the country should react to the Iran war. A recent poll shows most Britons want a defensive role—shooting down drones and protecting civilians—but only a minority want the UK to join attacks. Labour’s Keir Starmer is in line with voters, while the Conservatives and Reform are split, and the Liberal Democrats and Greens favour a non‑combat stance.
