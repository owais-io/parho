Donald Trump purchased more than $1 million worth of bonds from Netflix and Warner Bros Discovery in December, just a week after he said he would be involved in a proposed merger between the two streaming companies. The bonds were bought after Netflix agreed to buy Warner Bros for about $82.7 billion. The move raised questions because Trump said he’d help decide the deal, but his investments are managed by independent firms. The merger faces criticism from politicians and industry groups.
