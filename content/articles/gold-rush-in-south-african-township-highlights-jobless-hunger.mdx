Rumor of gold in Gugulethu, a township outside Springs, sparked a brief rush of locals and outsiders digging for treasure. Authorities quickly shut it down, arresting three people and taking away equipment. Many participants, facing 42 % unemployment and poverty, sold the small gold haul for food and school costs. The incident shows how high gold prices tempt desperate South Africans to risk illegal mining, prompting government warnings and calls for legal support.
