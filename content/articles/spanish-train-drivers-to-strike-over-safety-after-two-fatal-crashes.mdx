After two deadly train crashes that killed 46 people, Spain’s biggest train‑drivers union said it will strike for three days to push for safer railways. The incidents, one near Adamuz and another in Gelida, exposed track problems. Drivers want the government to fix these issues quickly. Transport minister says he wants to avoid the strike and says the accidents were unrelated. Politicians have also weighed in, adding to the tension.
