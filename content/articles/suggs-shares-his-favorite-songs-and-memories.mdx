Suggs, the frontman of the band Madness, tells a personal story about the music that shaped him. He remembers his first record, a ride to Salisbury Plain, and songs he still loves or secretly hates. From karaoke favourites to wild party moments, he shares the tracks that mattered most, like “Judy Teen” and “Al Capone.” He even lists the song he’d want to hear at his funeral, showing how music connects his past to his future.
