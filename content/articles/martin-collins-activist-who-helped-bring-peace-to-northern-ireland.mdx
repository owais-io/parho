Martin Collins was a 75‑year‑old activist who fought for human rights and peace in Northern Ireland. He helped build support for the Good Friday Agreement and worked on cases of injustice, like the Guildford Four and army deaths. Collins also led campaigns for Irish people in Britain and stood up for the Traveller community. He died from complications of dementia, leaving a legacy of dedication and courage.
