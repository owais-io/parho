New South Wales is rushing a bill after a Bondi beach attack that killed 15. The bill tightens gun laws, bans certain weapons, stops people from showing terrorist flags, and gives police powers to curb protests after a terror event. Farmers, opposition parties, and civil‑rights groups argue the limits are unfair or too strong. The legislation will pass the upper house soon, sparking debate about safety vs. freedom.
