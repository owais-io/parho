Pakistan is bombing Taliban targets in Afghanistan to stop militants that launch attacks from across the border. The airstrikes aim to make the Taliban pay a high price and push them to stop supporting insurgents. The Taliban say the strikes violate their sovereignty and threaten retaliation, even suicide bombings. Pakistan also closed its border and expelled refugees, while calling for a more inclusive Afghan government. The campaign risks more violence and has drawn criticism from experts.
