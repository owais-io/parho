Scientists studied pesticide use from 2013‑2019 across 65 countries and found the chemicals are harming wildlife around the globe. Insects saw the biggest rise in damage, followed by soil organisms. Europe and China saw declines, while Africa, India, the US, Brazil and Russia saw increases. Chile is the only country likely to meet the UN’s 2030 goal of cutting pesticide risk. Experts say more data and safer farming practices are urgent.
