The 1995 Bosman ruling ended transfer fees for out‑of‑contract players, freeing them to move freely and sparking higher wages for top stars. But the champion, Jean‑Marc Bosman, never profited and fell on hard times. Three decades later, the rule still shapes football: new cases, FIFA reforms, and player unions push for fairer contracts and a collective bargaining deal to balance power.
