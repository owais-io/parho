Teachers and parents often think kids repeating the nonsense phrase “six‑seven” shows they’re not thinking. The letter argues it’s a normal part of growing up. When teachers accept the trend, children feel seen and trusted, which makes learning easier. It’s harmless fun, not a sign of stupidity. Understanding this silliness can build confidence, social bonds, and a better classroom atmosphere.
