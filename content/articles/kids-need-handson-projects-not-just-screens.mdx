The article argues that the real problem with kids spending too much time on screens is the lack of purposeful, hands‑on activities. It highlights a Bulgarian program that turns children into designers and engineers, showing how real‑world projects build language, social skills, and confidence. The piece calls for new UK guidance that focuses on what kids do, not just how many hours they spend on devices. It also notes that parents need better support for early child‑rearing.
