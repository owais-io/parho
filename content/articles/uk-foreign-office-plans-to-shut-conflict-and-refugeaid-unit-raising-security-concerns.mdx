The UK’s Foreign Office is set to close its Migration and Conflict Directorate, which has about 100 staff who help governments in crisis zones like Syria and Yemen. The move is part of wider budget cuts that could affect 2,000 jobs. MPs, union leaders and experts say losing the unit weakens the UK’s ability to prevent conflict and support refugees. They urge a pause to the cuts.
