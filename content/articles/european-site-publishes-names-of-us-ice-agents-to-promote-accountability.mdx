A Dutch‑based project called ICE List started as a joke on social media but now has millions of views. It crowdsources and publishes the names, job titles, and sometimes photos of U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement agents, without revealing personal addresses. Volunteers gather info from public records, LinkedIn, and tips, aiming to expose anonymous officers and increase transparency amid growing public criticism of ICE.
