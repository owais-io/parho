NASA is looking at delaying its Artemis II mission after spotting a problem with the rocket’s helium supply. The helium gas is needed to keep the rocket’s fuel tanks cold, and a break in the flow could affect the March launch window. NASA will move the launch vehicle back to the assembly building to check the issue, and the crew of four astronauts is staying in quarantine while they sort it out.
