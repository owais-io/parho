Sydney’s beloved Lebanese spot Abdul’s has shut its doors after 60 years, leaving regulars and families stunned. The family‑run eatery was a neighborhood fixture where friends, celebrities and everyday diners enjoyed hummus, shawarma and baklava. It was a place for birthdays, anniversaries and first dates, a second living room for many. The loss feels like the end of a generation’s tradition, and the city mourns a familiar corner of its culinary history.
