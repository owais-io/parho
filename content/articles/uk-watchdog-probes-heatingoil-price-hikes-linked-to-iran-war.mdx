The UK’s Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) is investigating heating‑oil suppliers that may have raised prices sharply since the Iran war. About 1.7 million UK households—mostly in rural areas that don’t use mains gas—have complained that orders were cancelled and new quotes were twice as high. Prices have almost tripled, and the CMA will check for unlawful practices and could take enforcement action. Government officials are also reviewing the issue and could offer financial help.
