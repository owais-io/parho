Australia’s federal and state governments are giving more than $30,000 a minute to support fossil‑fuel companies this year, a new study says. The money mainly goes to a fuel‑tax‑credit scheme that refunds diesel and petrol costs for mining, farming and tourism businesses. The subsidies are rising faster than the cost of the national disability insurance plan. Politicians and unions are calling for cuts to reduce climate harm and help people switch to clean energy.
