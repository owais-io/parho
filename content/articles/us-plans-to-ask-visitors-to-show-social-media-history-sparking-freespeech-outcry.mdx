Trump’s administration plans to require visa applicants from 42 countries to provide five years of social media, phone, and email histories for US entry checks. Critics call it censorship and a threat to free speech, warning it could hurt tourism and force people to self‑censor. Human‑rights groups say the move oversteps privacy. Trump says it’s about safety, but the policy has not yet been officially confirmed.
