Essam al‑Shazly, a 28‑year‑old Egyptian fisherman, was executed in Saudi Arabia after being accused of drug smuggling. His mother, who could only speak to him by phone, says he was forced into smuggling and that his confession was obtained under duress. Saudi Arabia carried out a record 356 executions last year, most of them for non‑violent drug offences. Families get little help, and the country rarely returns bodies or burial details.
