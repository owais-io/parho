Joe Kennedy, a retired dentist who became a passionate primrose breeder, died at 90. He spent over 30 years collecting old Irish primrose varieties and breeding new hardy hybrids. His plants were even gifted to the Obamas by Ireland's Taoiseach. After a career in dentistry and volunteering during the Troubles, he devoted his retirement to horticulture, publishing books and appearing on BBC Gardener’s World. He leaves a family and a lasting plant legacy.
