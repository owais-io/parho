Najib Razak, former Malaysian prime minister, has been given 15 more years in jail after a judge found him guilty of abusing his power in the 1MDB money‑laundering case. He was already serving a six‑year sentence for another 1MDB offence. The judge said the evidence proved he steered billions of ringgit into his accounts. The ruling could strain Malaysia’s current government, as Najib still has influence in his former party.
