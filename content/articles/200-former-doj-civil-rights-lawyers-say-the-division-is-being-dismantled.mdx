200 former DOJ civil‑rights lawyers say the division is being dismantled by Trump appointees. They claim political leaders fired career staff, forced them into low‑level jobs, dropped important cases, and shifted focus to Trump’s agenda. By May the division lost about 70% of its attorneys. A letter was sent on the division’s 68th birthday, calling the changes a “near destruction” of civil‑rights enforcement.
