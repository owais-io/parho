Cloudflare, a company that helps websites stay safe and fast, had a three‑hour outage that knocked out many popular sites, from ChatGPT and X to YouTube and Google. The problem was described as an internal service issue after a sudden traffic spike. With about 300,000 customers and services to one‑in‑five websites, the glitch showed how much of the internet relies on a few big providers, raising concerns about resilience.
