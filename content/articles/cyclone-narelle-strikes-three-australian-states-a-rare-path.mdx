Narelle, a powerful tropical cyclone, made landfall in three Australian regions – Queensland, the Northern Territory, and Western Australia – for the first time in 20 years. The storm moved unusually from east to west, covering 5,500 km. Scientists say the trip was helped by record‑warm ocean temperatures in the Coral Sea, which may be linked to climate change. The cyclone caused heavy rain and damage across three coastlines.
