Jim Boyling, a former Metropolitan Police undercover officer, says his bosses let him lie to and have sex with three women while he was infiltrating activist groups. He claims senior managers knew about these relationships but did nothing, following a “don’t ask, don’t tell” approach. The claim is part of a public inquiry into 139 undercover officers who spied on left‑wing activists from 1968 to 2010. The women say the deceit broke their trust and caused deep emotional harm.
