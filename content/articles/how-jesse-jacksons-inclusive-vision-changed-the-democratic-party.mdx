Jesse Jackson, a civil‑rights leader who died last month, built a coalition that brought Black voters, working‑class people, and even LGBTQ+ groups into the Democratic Party. His 1984 campaign and the National Rainbow Coalition showed how a broad, inclusive platform could win elections and shape policy. Jackson helped register millions of Black voters, championed gay rights early, and pushed for Palestinian self‑determination. His ideas keep influencing Democrats today.
