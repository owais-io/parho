Trump secretly planned to detain Venezuelan leader Nicolás Maduro and kept Congress in the dark until the operation began. This move broke the 1973 War Powers Resolution, which requires presidents to notify Congress about military actions. Democratic leaders demanded a briefing and warned that war‑making should stay under congressional control. Most Republicans stayed quiet or backed the president, saying the action fell under his inherent authority. Critics call for stronger checks on executive power.
