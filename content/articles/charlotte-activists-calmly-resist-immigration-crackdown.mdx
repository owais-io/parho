The U.S. Department of Homeland Security sent mixed messages about an immigration sweep in Charlotte, North Carolina. While the sheriff said federal agents were still on the move, the agency claimed its operations were over. Local activists, including a group called Siembra NC, tracked and reported ICE activity, honked to warn residents, and set up a neighborhood watch. The city stayed quiet, and a new state law now forces sheriffs to hand over people ICE wants to detain.
