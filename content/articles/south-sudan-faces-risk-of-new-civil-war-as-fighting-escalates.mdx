South Sudan’s war‑like fighting has gotten worse, putting the country at risk of a full civil war again. The army loyal to President Kiir clashes with rebels tied to former Vice‑President Riek Machar, and a recent raid killed 169 people, including civilians. The UN shelters over a thousand people and doctors are trapped. Past peace talks have stalled, and Machar’s arrest has united opposition groups, driving 280,000 people from homes.
