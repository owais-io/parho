Habermas, a leading German thinker, fought against totalitarianism and built a philosophy that stresses rational debate and democratic values. His work on communicative action and the public sphere shows how open dialogue can counter power, technology, and demagoguery. Though critics say he overlooked some social inequalities, his ideas still offer a blueprint for civil discussion in an era of illiberalism and rising nationalism.
