Australia’s government is overhauling its defence department to fix cost and schedule problems. Three agencies will merge into a new “Defence Delivery Agency” that will manage big projects from July 2026 and become fully independent a year later. Minister Richard Marles also said Canberra is watching a Chinese navy group in the Philippine Sea, which could head toward Australian waters. The changes aim to speed up procurement and cut waste, though critics say leadership hasn’t changed.
