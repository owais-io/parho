UK MPs, led by Labour MP Calvin Bailey, are reminded of the 2003 Iraq war as they discuss US‑Israeli air strikes on Iran. Prime Minister Keir Starmer says Britain will only support lawful, limited actions, citing the Chilcot report. The US president and some Conservative MPs criticize Starmer’s cautious stance, while Liberal Democrat and Labour MPs warn that an escalation could repeat Iraq’s disastrous aftermath. The debate shows how Iraq’s legacy still influences UK foreign policy.
