Britain’s FTSE 100 climbed 21.5% in 2025, the biggest jump since 2009, outpacing Wall Street. Big winners included gold miners Fresnillo and Endeavour, telecom Airtel Africa, and defence shares that rose as Europe increased weapons spending. The index hit a record 9,954 points, although some firms slipped. Global markets grew 21% amid AI concerns and Trump tariffs, with gold and European stocks staying strong. US markets also rose, but AI fears and tariffs caused swings.
