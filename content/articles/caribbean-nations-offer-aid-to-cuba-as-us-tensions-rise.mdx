Caribbean countries have said they will send humanitarian aid to Cuba after a U.S. fuel embargo worsened a crisis. The promise came at a Caricom meeting in St. Kitts, where leaders were divided over U.S. policies. Some members backed the U.S., while others called for a united stance to help Cuba. The group said it would act within a month, but political differences may slow a clear statement.
