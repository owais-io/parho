Britain’s government says it will spend £8.4bn—about a third of a £27bn five‑year plan—on fixing and resurfacing major roads and motorways. The money will go to maintenance, not just new projects. But the plan still includes 16 new road schemes, such as the Lower Thames Crossing and a dual carriageway for the A66. Critics argue the new roads will add traffic and that the plan over‑spends on unnecessary projects.
