Newcastle boss Eddie Howe said he thinks James Trafford is a good goalkeeper and might move there, while Manchester City manager Pep Guardiola said Trafford could stay at City and that he wants him to stay long term. Trafford signed with City for £31m after leaving Burnley, but has only played a handful of matches because Donnarumma is the main keeper. The move is still open and both clubs are waiting to see what happens.
