Trump has taken control of Venezuelan oil, hoping to boost production and lower prices. But Venezuela’s oil fields are in bad shape, needing up to $183 billion and many years to rebuild. The heavy, sour crude is expensive to extract, and investors fear political risk and low returns as global oil prices fall. Climate change is also pushing demand down, making Venezuela’s long‑term investment risky.
