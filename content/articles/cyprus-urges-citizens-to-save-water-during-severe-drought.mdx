Cyprus is facing its worst drought in a century, with reservoirs almost empty and water use rising because of heat and tourism. The government wants everyone to cut their daily running water by 10%—about two minutes less of showering, brushing, or washing. Alongside a €31m emergency aid package, plans include fixing leaks, reusing wastewater, installing desalination plants, and a public awareness campaign to keep daily use around 140 litres per person.
