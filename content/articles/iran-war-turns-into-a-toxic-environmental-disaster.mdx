Since March, Israeli‑American bombs have hit Iranian oil refineries, gas fields, and military sites, releasing oil, heavy metals, PFAS and other toxins. The resulting fires produced black acid rain and marine pollution. Remote‑sensing data shows over 500 environmental incidents, but Iran has kept data secret, and local activists face pushback. If the war expands to more fossil‑fuel facilities, the long‑term damage to the region’s air, water and climate could be catastrophic.
