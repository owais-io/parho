Drusilla Beyfus was a trailblazing British journalist who broke into the top newspaper ranks at 21, a rare achievement for a woman in the 1940s. She became the Daily Express’s women’s editor, wrote popular etiquette guides, and taught fashion journalism at Central St Martins. A mother of three media‑industry leaders, Beyfus’s career spanned 70 years. Her books on manners reflected Britain’s social changes and were widely read. She died at 98.
