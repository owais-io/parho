A German media giant, Axel Springer, has agreed to buy the Telegraph Media Group for £575 million, beating a £500 million bid from the Daily Mail’s owners. The deal clears the UK’s new rules that restrict foreign state influence and gives the Telegraph a stable owner after years of uncertainty. Springer said it will keep editors free and invest in digital technology and AI to grow the paper, especially in the US. Staff can expect a calmer future.
