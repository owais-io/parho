Stocks fell around the world on Tuesday after President Trump announced tariffs on eight European countries over the Greenland dispute. The S&P 500 fell 2.1%, the Dow 1.8% and the Nasdaq 2.4%, wiping billions from big names like Amazon, Tesla and Nvidia. Gold and silver hit record highs as investors sought safety. Treasury and commerce officials tried to calm fears, but market anxiety stayed high.
