When my daughter fell in love with puzzles, I tried them too. After my dad died, I struggled to calm down. Finding a 1,000‑piece garden puzzle on the dining table, I started piecing it together. The steady, focused work felt like meditation, easing my racing thoughts. Now I finish puzzles, pack them up, and buy a new one, finding peace in the simple task.
