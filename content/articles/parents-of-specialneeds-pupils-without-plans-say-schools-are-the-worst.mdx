A new survey of more than 6,000 parents in England shows that those whose children have special educational needs but no legal care plan (EHCP) are the least happy with their schools. Only 57 % say the school is good, compared with 68 % of parents who have a plan and 71 % of parents of children without special needs. Parents want better resources and training for teachers, and they say schools should include these children in mainstream classes whenever possible.
