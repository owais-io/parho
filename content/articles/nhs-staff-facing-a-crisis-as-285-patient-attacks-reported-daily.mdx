Every day, NHS workers are dealing with almost 300 violent incidents from patients, a rise from 91,000 last year to 104,000 this year. Sexual assaults and harassment have also jumped, especially against female nurses. Reasons include long wait times, distrust of medicine, racism, and a lack of secure beds for violent patients. Unions say the crisis is ignored, while the government promises a review and tougher penalties for offenders.
