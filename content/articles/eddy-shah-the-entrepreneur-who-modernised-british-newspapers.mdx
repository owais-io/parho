British businessman Eddy Shah, 81, sparked a newspaper revolution. He started free local papers and launched the national titles Today and Post in the 1980s, breaking the print unions' grip and pushing computer‑based printing. Though the papers folded, his moves forced major media groups to modernise. After selling his paper chain for £40 million, Shah wrote novels and ran leisure businesses. He was acquitted of a 1990s rape charge in 2013. His wife died in 2023.
