In 2017, President Trump announced a new embargo that blocked oil and fuel supplies to Cuba. The move made life harder for ordinary Cubans—power cuts have stretched to 18 hours a day, food prices are up, and many can’t get to work or school. The writer, who has visited Cuba for 12 years, talks to locals who say they’re tired but resilient and want to fight back against U.S. pressure.
