Flu cases in English hospitals fell for a second week, dropping 13% to about 2,700 patients a day. Only 128 needed critical care. Despite the drop, the NHS says it’s not complacent because temperatures are falling, bringing more pressure. Health groups urge people, especially those with heart disease or the elderly, to stay warm and get flu shots. Ambulance handovers improved, with fewer delays.
