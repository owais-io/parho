Scientists have found genes that can turn ordinary bacteria into antibiotic‑resistant superbugs in Lough Neagh, the biggest lake that supplies drinking water to about a third of Northern Ireland. The genes can resist many drugs, including life‑saving carbapenems. Sewage, livestock waste and untreated storm‑water overflow are putting the genes into the lake. The problem shows how badly wastewater treatment and farming practices need to improve to protect public health.
