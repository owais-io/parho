Prime Minister Keir Starmer has announced new rules that force social‑media and other online platforms to take down non‑consensual intimate pictures within 48 hours of a report. The move follows a broader strategy to cut violence against women and girls. It makes such images a priority offence under the Online Safety Act and says companies must act automatically if the material is reposted. The aim is to protect victims and shift the burden from them to the platforms and offenders.
