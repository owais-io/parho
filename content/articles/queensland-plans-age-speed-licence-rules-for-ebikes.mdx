Queensland is set to introduce rules that would make it harder to ride electric bikes. Riders would have to be at least 16, hold a learner’s licence, and be medically fit to drive a car. The bill would cap e‑bike speed on footpaths at 10 km/h and allow big fines for speeding. The move could cut off many people who rely on e‑bikes for mobility, especially those with disabilities. Critics say the rules favour car users and hurt active transport.
