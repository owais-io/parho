Today the UK government finally released its plan to cut violence against women and girls, a goal Labour pledged to halve in ten years. The minister admits she caused the repeated delays. However, victims’ commissioner Claire Waxman says the strategy could suffer from insufficient funding, risking over‑stretched services and undermining its aims. Key actions include extra police teams, NHS support, and anti‑misogyny rules in schools.
