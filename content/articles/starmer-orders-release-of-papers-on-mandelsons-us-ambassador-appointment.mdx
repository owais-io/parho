Keir Starmer has told the government to make public the documents about Peter Mandelson’s appointment as Britain’s ambassador to the U.S. The move comes after a scandal that links Mandelson to the convicted sex‑offender Jeffrey Epstein. The prime minister wants to show transparency but will keep any sensitive or security‑related material hidden. The release follows a Conservative motion to force the papers out, and the papers are still being checked before they are published.
