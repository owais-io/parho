Water bills in England and Wales will rise by about £33 on average in April, raising the yearly cost to £639—5.4% higher than last year. The hike aims to fix leaking pipes and sewage works. Customers with United Utilities will see the biggest increase, while Thames Water’s jump is only £3. Critics argue the rise unfairly burdens households, and a court case is pending. Water UK says the money will improve water quality and stop sewage spills.
