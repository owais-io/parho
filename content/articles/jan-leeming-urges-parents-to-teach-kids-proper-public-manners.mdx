Jan Leeming, a former TV newsreader, says parents must teach children how to behave in public. She criticised a noisy toddler at a Canterbury restaurant, claiming many kids are treated like royalty and allowed to run wild. Leeming argues that children should learn to be polite and respectful in restaurants, on trains, and at shops. She compares good behaviour to training a dog, though critics say it’s too harsh.
