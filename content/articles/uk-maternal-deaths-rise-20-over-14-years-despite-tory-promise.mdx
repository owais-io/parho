New data shows that the number of women dying during or shortly after pregnancy in the UK has gone up by 20% in the last 14 years, even though Conservative leaders promised to cut the rate in half. Most deaths are linked to bleeding, blood clots and pre‑eclampsia, problems that can be treated if caught early. The rise is biggest for black, Asian and poorer women, and for mothers over 35.
