Pilots at New York’s LaGuardia airport had been telling officials that flights were unsafe for months. Their complaints—about confusing traffic‑control instructions, dark runways and a crowded tower—came right before a crash that killed two pilots and hurt 41 people when an Air Canada Express jet hit a fire truck on the runway. Investigators say controller shortages and equipment problems made the airport more dangerous. The NTSB will keep looking for answers.
