Japan’s ruling LDP is set to win a big lower‑house election thanks to its new prime minister, Sanae Takaichi, the country’s first woman in the job. Takaichi has built a fan base with quirky social‑media posts and promises big tax cuts, appealing to young voters. Polls say she and her party will secure a two‑thirds majority, giving them strong control. Some voters worry her tax promises won’t help tight budgets, but the election looks to favor her.
