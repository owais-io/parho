A group of think tanks – including Progress, the New Economics Foundation, Common Wealth and Women’s Budget Group – say the Office for Budget Responsibility (OBR) is stuck in a “back‑seat driver” mode. They want the watchdog to focus more on long‑term investment and give the government a clearer, less punitive framework. The push comes as Labour’s chancellor Rachel Reeves prepares her spring forecast amid recent election losses.
