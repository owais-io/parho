Students in Iranian universities have marched on campus for the third straight day, chanting slogans against the regime after a brutal crackdown left thousands dead. The protests come amid a tense US‑Iran standoff and a planned nuclear deal talk in Geneva. While the government warns of punishment, students have burned flags, hung toy mice on trees, and said they will not be silenced. The unrest shows the regime’s grip is weakening.
