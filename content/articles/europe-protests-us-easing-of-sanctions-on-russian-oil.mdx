In March 2017, President Trump temporarily lifted U.S. sanctions on Russian oil to ease price spikes caused by Iran’s blockade of the Strait of Hormuz. European leaders—UK, Germany, France, Norway—strongly opposed, saying the move weakens pressure on Russia over its war in Ukraine. They warned the oil ban must stay and Ukraine support should not be diverted by Middle East conflict. The U.S. let Indian refiners and Russian tankers trade again.
