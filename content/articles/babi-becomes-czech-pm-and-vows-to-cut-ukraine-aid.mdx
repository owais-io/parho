Babiš, a 71‑year‑old billionaire who runs Agrofert, has been named Czech prime minister after the ODS party finished second in last month’s election. He set up a blind trust for his company to avoid a conflict of interest. His new coalition includes two small parties, and he says the Czech government will cut military aid to Ukraine and may end the ammunition programme. Meanwhile, Zelenskyy is in Rome meeting the Pope.
