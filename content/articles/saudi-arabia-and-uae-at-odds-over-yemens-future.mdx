Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates are at odds over Yemen’s future, especially a plan by the Southern Transitional Council to create an independent southern state. The UAE has backed the council, while Saudi forces bombed vehicles sent to the south, calling it a security threat. The dispute risks sparking civil war in Yemen and could affect other regional conflicts. Both Gulf powers use Yemen to boost their influence over shipping routes and trade.
