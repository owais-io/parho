Gabbard, the head of national intelligence, told a Senate intelligence panel that U.S. airstrikes on Iran were a strategic win. She said the strikes weakened Iran’s conventional forces but the regime could rebuild its missile program. Gabbard also noted Iran’s retaliation killed U.S. soldiers and hit global supplies. She defended her presence at the Fulton County FBI raid and explained the report’s omission of election‑interference threats.
