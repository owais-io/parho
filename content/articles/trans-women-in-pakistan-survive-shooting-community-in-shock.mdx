A trans woman in Karachi was shot at her home but survived. This incident is part of a wave of violent attacks on Pakistan’s trans community, with 55 killings in Sindh and dozens more across the country. Many trans people work as beggars or in sex work, and are targeted when they ask for money. Activists are filing lawsuits, asking courts to stop authorities from forcing them out of homes and the country.
