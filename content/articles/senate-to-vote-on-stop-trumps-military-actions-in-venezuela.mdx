The U.S. Senate will vote on a bill that would stop President Trump from using the military in Venezuela without Congress's approval. Democrats introduced it after Trump raided Caracas, captured dictator Nicolás Maduro, and launched airstrikes that killed over 100 people. Some Republicans support Trump’s actions, but a few break ranks. The bill is part of a push to curb future military moves by the president.
