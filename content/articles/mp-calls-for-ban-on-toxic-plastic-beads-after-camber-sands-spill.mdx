An MP and wildlife experts want a nationwide ban on tiny toxic plastic beads used in sewage plants after a huge spill on Camber Sands beach in East Sussex. Hundreds of millions of beads, which contain heavy metals and chemicals, washed up, harming wildlife and the environment. Scientists found lead, arsenic, and other toxins in the beads. The MP is launching a campaign with the Wildlife Trusts to stop the use of beads and protect coastal habitats.
