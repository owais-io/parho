An ANU study shows that almost half of Australians think a foreign army could attack the country within five years, and worries are climbing, especially among teens and young adults. Over 55% of 18‑24‑year‑olds are scared of national security threats, and 69% think a foreign military conflict will happen soon. Many feel unprepared, and rising fears of terrorism and supply shocks follow the Middle East war.
