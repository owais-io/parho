The UK government wants to change asylum rules, cutting support for families with kids after a rejected claim and extending the time before permanent status from 5 to 20 years. Labour peer Alf Dubs and refugees groups say the plans use children as a weapon, hurt integration and raise danger for families risking boat crossings. UN agencies, Amnesty and the International Rescue Committee all warn the changes will worsen fear, uncertainty and community tensions.
