The European Union plans to relax its 2035 ban that would stop selling new petrol and diesel cars. A senior parliament member said the rule may allow hybrids and existing combustion engines. Car makers, German and Italian leaders, and some governments want the change, arguing EV uptake is slow. Environmental groups are angry, saying it will keep dirty cars on roads longer. The EU is still debating the final details.
