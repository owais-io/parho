Researchers examined 135,000 people in the UK, US, Norway, and Sweden and found that just adding five minutes of brisk walking a day could reduce early deaths by about 10%. Cutting sedentary time by half an hour each day could lower deaths by 7%. The biggest benefits appear for the least active 20% of people. A related study showed that small tweaks to sleep, exercise, and diet together could add a year or more to life.
