After the Bondi beach shooting, NSW’s government wants to act fast on gun limits, a $300 m buy‑back, tighter storage and even mandatory club membership. They also plan to let police block protests for up to three months after a terror event. Critics say these quick rules could breach free‑speech rights, create new lobby groups, and may not be the best way to keep people safe.
