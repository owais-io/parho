Michael Baron, a 96‑year‑old solicitor, helped change life for autistic people. He co‑founded the UK’s leading autism charity in 1962, set up the first autism school in 1965, and opened the world’s first residential community for autistic adults in 1974. His activism began after his son Timothy was diagnosed with autism. Baron won an MBE for his work and later turned to local wildlife and poetry.
