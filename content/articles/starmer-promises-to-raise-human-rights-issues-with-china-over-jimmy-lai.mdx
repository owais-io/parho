UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer is traveling to China for his first visit in eight years, vowing to bring up human‑rights concerns, especially the release of jailed Hong Kong media figure Jimmy Lai. He says he will talk about differences with President Xi, but critics claim he should have secured Lai’s freedom first. The trip will also touch on Uyghur treatment and other rights abuses, while Downing Street promises “guardrails” for trade talks.
