A deadly fire at Hong Kong’s Wang Fuk Court killed at least 159 people, the worst building blaze in 75 years. The complex was under renovation with flammable materials that made the fire spread fast. Police arrested 21 people for faulty alarms and building work, while the government also blamed “external forces” for trying to use the tragedy for political attacks. Authorities are tightening rules on scaffolding, setting up an independent review, and holding memorials for victims.
