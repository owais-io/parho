Many companies now talk directly to customers, using “you” in ads, emails and product labels. This style, called direct address, started with old slogans like Kodak’s “You press the button, we do the rest.” While research says people respond better to such personal appeals, the writer feels it comes off as insincere and patronising. He worries it turns everyday shopping into a forced chat, and wonders if brands should just address us politely instead.
