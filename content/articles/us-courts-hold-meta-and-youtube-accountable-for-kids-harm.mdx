In recent court cases, US juries found that Meta and YouTube built addictive features that harmed children. Meta was ordered to pay $375 million after a jury said its platforms helped child sex‑trafficking. The verdicts show a growing willingness to hold tech companies accountable, especially for how they target users. Regulators now face the challenge of keeping up with digital change, and governments in places like Australia and the UK are starting to enforce stricter rules to protect children’s mental health.
