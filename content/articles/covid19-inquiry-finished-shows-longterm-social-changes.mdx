The final part of the UK Covid‑19 inquiry finished after almost three years. Experts say the pandemic has left lasting changes in mental health, the treatment of key workers, homelessness and support for disabled people. Demand for mental‑health help surged during lockdown, and many frontline workers faced abuse. Hotel‑based emergency housing became normal, and disabled people struggled to return to normal life after services were shut. The findings urge better future planning.
