Sri Lanka has cut the official workweek to four days to save fuel as oil supplies shrink due to the war in the Middle East. The Hormuz strait, a key route for Gulf oil, has been blocked, leaving many South‑Asian countries with barely enough gas and LPG for cooking and power. Sri Lanka is rationing fuel, limiting public transport, and urging schools and businesses to work from home. The move aims to keep essential services running while the crisis continues.
