The author worries that border officials might ask to see her phone or even confiscate it to check her social media. She feels that the messy, personal stuff on her phone—calculations, embarrassing notes, weird searches, private chats—would make her look bad. The piece points out that many people, especially women, feel the same, and that data‑sharing by apps can expose private info. She says she would prefer to be deported than let her phone be read.
