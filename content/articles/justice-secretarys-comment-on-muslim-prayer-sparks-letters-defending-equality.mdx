In a recent Guardian letters column, several readers responded to Shadow Justice Secretary Nick Timothy’s claim that Muslim public prayer is a “domination” act. They say the remark is unfair, discriminatory, and deepens division. The writers argue that Muslims should have the same right as other faiths to pray in public spaces, and that peaceful prayer is not threatening. They call for equal treatment under the law.
