The U.S. bombing of Iran has hurt Asian countries that depend on Gulf oil, cutting shipments through the Strait of Hormuz and pushing up fuel prices. While U.S. markets stay relatively stable because of domestic gas, European stocks have fallen more than Asia’s. Trade tariffs and high energy costs hurt many nations, and the WTO warns global growth will slow. The U.S. is seen as a source of new instability, changing how allies view it.
