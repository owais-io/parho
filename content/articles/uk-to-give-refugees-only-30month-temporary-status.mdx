The Home Secretary announced that from Monday every new asylum claim in the UK will be given a 30‑month protection period, instead of the previous five years. After that, refugees can have their status reviewed and may be asked to return if their home country is considered safe. The move aims to curb the number of people staying long‑term, but critics warn it will hurt families who have already settled and worked in Britain.
