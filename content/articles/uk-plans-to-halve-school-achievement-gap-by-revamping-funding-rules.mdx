The government will reveal a new white paper that aims to cut the gap between the poorest pupils and their richer peers. It will change how schools get extra money, shifting from free‑meal counts to family income, and create special trusts for local schools. Labour wants more money for the poorest children and new programs in the North and Coast. Critics worry the money could be cut or unevenly shared.
