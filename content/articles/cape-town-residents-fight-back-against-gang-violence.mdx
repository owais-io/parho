Since 2015, families in Cape Town’s Cape Flats have lost loved ones to gang shootings. Despite rising murders and a 40% unemployment rate, residents are not waiting for the state to act. The government has sent the army to patrol, but experts say lasting change needs jobs, trust, and better youth programs. Meanwhile, local groups organise football matches, gardening projects, and dance classes to keep children away from crime.
