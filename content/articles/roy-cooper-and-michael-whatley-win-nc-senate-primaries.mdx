North Carolina’s Senate seat, held by retiring Republican Senator Thom Tillis, is set to go to a contest between former governor Roy Cooper and former RNC chair Michael Whatley after both won their party primaries. Cooper, a two‑term governor, is favored to flip the seat, while Whatley, backed by Trump, leads the GOP field. Polls show Cooper with a 10‑point advantage, and voters are focused on the economy, healthcare and federal cuts. Redistricting also shapes other state races.
