The U.S. has tightened sanctions on Cuba, cutting fuel supplies and crippling hospitals. In Havana, doctors can’t run tests, patients lack basic medicine, and even electricity is failing. A cancer patient at a maternity hospital struggles because the embargo has left the island short of diesel, medicines and even basic hospital equipment. The result is a slow, daily fight for survival, showing how the U.S. policy is hurting ordinary Cubans.
