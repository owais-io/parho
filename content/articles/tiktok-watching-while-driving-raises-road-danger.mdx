People are watching videos like TikTok while driving, raising the danger of distracted driving. A New Jersey rider saw an Uber driver scrolling, and the same driver again hours later. In 2023, 3,275 died in crashes linked to distraction, and studies show younger drivers are the biggest group. States are starting to ban livestreaming from cars, but many laws only cover texts. Car makers now use touchscreens that pull eyes off the road, adding to the risk.
