French bookshops now have a special spot for feminist memoirs, and authors are mixing real life with story in new “autofiction” books.  In 2025, most best‑selling novels talk about parents or grandparents, showing why the Goncourt prize went to a family‑focused book.  After the Nobel win of Annie Ernaux, writers are using their own stories to keep memories alive and ask questions about family and history.
