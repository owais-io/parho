Two children at a Texas immigration detention center for kids and parents tested positive for measles as the U.S. sees a surge in the virus. Experts warn that the crowded facilities can turn into fast‑moving outbreaks. A congressman and other officials have urged the center to shut down or release detainees. Meanwhile, there is no nationwide vaccination push, and many patients are unvaccinated. The health crisis highlights the need for better protection in detention centers.
