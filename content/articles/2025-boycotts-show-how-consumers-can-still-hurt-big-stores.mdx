Boycotts in 2025 show that non‑violent protests can still hurt big companies. The article reminds us that the American founders used boycotts to win the Revolution and today shoppers target Target, Walmart, Amazon and Tesla over issues like diversity and worker treatment. These campaigns have lowered sales, dropped stock prices, and forced companies to rethink their policies. The piece argues that a successful boycott needs a united community, new alternatives to the products, and long‑term commitment.
