The Department of Homeland Security has almost shut down its civil‑rights watchdog, leaving only nine staff to cover hundreds of complaints about abuse in immigration detention. Experts say this weak oversight lets the Trump administration treat detainees with little accountability. Fired watchdogs have sued the government and filed whistleblower complaints. Human‑rights groups fear unchecked power could lead to more abuse and harsher deportation policies.
