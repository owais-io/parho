Australia has made the health‑star rating on food labels compulsory, a win for public health groups after a decade‑long fight. But experts warn the system is still flawed, with companies manipulating ratings and the formula ignoring processed foods. The new law will take a year to draft, and many say clearer warning labels and more regulation are needed to curb cheap, unhealthy foods.
