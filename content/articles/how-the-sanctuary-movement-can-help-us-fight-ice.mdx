President Trump has attacked cities that limit ICE’s power, saying they are crime havens. The sanctuary movement began in the 1980s when churches helped Central American refugees and later inspired cities to stop sharing immigration data with ICE. Research shows sanctuary policies lower crime, boost labor participation, and protect women, but local police still sometimes cooperate with ICE. The movement shows how faith groups can influence policy and fight for immigrant rights.
