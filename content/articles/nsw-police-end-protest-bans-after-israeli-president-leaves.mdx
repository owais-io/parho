New South Wales police have lifted the ban that had stopped marches in key city areas after the Israeli president’s visit. The restriction, put in place after a terror attack and used to keep protest routes safe, was ended by the police commissioner. Critics say the law limits free speech and that police used pepper spray and kettling. Legal challenges will still be heard in court.
