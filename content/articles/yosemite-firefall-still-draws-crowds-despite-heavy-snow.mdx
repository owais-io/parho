Heavy snow hasn't kept people from heading to Yosemite in February to see the famous firefall, a sunset light show that turns Horsetail Falls into glowing lava for a few minutes. Winter storms made the event rarer and forced the park to close a trail and shut its gates last week. Despite the closures, visitors returned over the weekend, causing long waits and limited parking. Officials warn of freezing temperatures and slick roads.
