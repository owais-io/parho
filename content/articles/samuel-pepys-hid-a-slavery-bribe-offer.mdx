A new study shows Samuel Pepys, the famous 17th‑century naval official and diarist, removed references to a bribe that included an enslaved boy. In 1675 a fellow officer offered Pepys the boy to secure a ship command. Pepys replied that the boy was a “small” reward and later the letter was rewritten to hide the child. Historians say Pepys was more worried about being seen as corrupt than about slavery itself.
