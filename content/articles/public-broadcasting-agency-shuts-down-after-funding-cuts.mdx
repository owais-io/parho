The Corporation for Public Broadcasting, which has helped pay for NPR and PBS for nearly 60 years, voted to dissolve after Congress cut its money. Trump and a conservative plan aimed to slash funding, and lawmakers removed $1.1 billion, leaving the agency without a budget. The agency supported 544 stations, many in rural areas. Donors have stepped in with $70 million, but many local stations may still close in the coming years.
