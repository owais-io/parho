Stocks around the world fell on Thursday as the war between Israel and Iran pushed oil prices up. The UK’s FTSE 100, Germany’s DAX, and the US Dow all slipped, while the price of Brent crude rose to about $85 a barrel. Higher fuel costs hurt airlines and made investors nervous about future interest‑rate cuts. The markets might recover if shipping can resume through the Strait of Hormuz.
