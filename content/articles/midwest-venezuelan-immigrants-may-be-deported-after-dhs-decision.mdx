Venezuelan immigrants who settled in Indiana’s suburbs are now in danger of being sent back to Venezuela after the U.S. Department of Homeland Security decided to end Temporary Protected Status for 500,000 people. The move comes after the U.S. removed President Maduro and the Trump administration’s crackdown on immigrants. Local Venezuelan families run food stalls and help revive downtown areas, but many fear losing jobs, taxes and community ties if deported. Their future is uncertain.
