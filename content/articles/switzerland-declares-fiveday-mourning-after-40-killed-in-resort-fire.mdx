On New Year's Eve a fire broke out in a crowded bar in the Swiss ski town of Crans‑Montana, killing around 40 people and injuring more than 100. The blaze started early in the morning, possibly from sparklers or candles, and spread fast, trapping many inside the basement. President Guy Parmelin has announced five days of national mourning, and investigators are racing to identify the victims while checking the venue’s safety standards.
