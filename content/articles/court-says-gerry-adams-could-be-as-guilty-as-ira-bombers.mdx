A civil case in London says former Sinn Féin leader Gerry Adams should be held just as responsible as the men who set off IRA bombs in the 1970s and 1990s. The three men who were hurt by the Old Bailey, Docklands and Manchester blasts are suing him for £1 each. Their lawyers claim Adams helped run the IRA, but Adams and his team say there is no proof and the case is too old. The trial is still underway.
