The Philippines has told all government agencies, universities, and local offices to cut energy use by at least 10% because fuel prices are rising from the Middle East war. Rules include setting air‑conditioning to 24 °C, reducing travel, and encouraging flexible or remote work. The move aims to curb inflation and ease the country’s heavy reliance on imported oil and reduce costs.
