Independent bookshops in Germany are being investigated by the domestic intelligence agency. Three shops were removed from a government prize list after the culture minister asked the agency for information. The shops are anti‑fascist and community hubs. The minister, who runs a conservative magazine, has been tightening cultural rules. The bookshops plan legal action, arguing the probes unfairly punish places that simply recommend books and host debates.
