After a flare‑up between the U.S., Israel and Iran, Iran's army threatened to set fire to tankers crossing the Strait of Hormuz, the narrow waterway that ships a fifth of the world's oil. Because the strait is hard to bypass, the threat shut many ships, sent global oil prices through the roof, and forced Gulf producers to consider stopping output if the route stays blocked. China, the biggest buyer, has huge stockpiles, while other gas‑importing nations feel the pinch.
