Adam Tickell, the chief of the University of Birmingham, said that the UK system of giving every student a government loan might be broken. He thinks students who don’t have A‑levels or similar qualifications may not finish university, yet they still get the same loan money. The loans, which average £53,000 a graduate, add to student debt and hurt university budgets. Tickell wants a full review of who can borrow and how the money should be shared.
