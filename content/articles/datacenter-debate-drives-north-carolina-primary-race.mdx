In a North Carolina congressional race, two Democrats are fighting over a big data‑center plan that could use a lot of water and power. Incumbent Rep. Valerie Foushee says the decision should stay with local leaders, while challenger Nida Allam wants a national pause on new data‑centers to protect the environment. Allam’s campaign is backed by big‑tech money from the AI firm Anthropic, sparking anger from voters who fear the industry is funding the candidate they want to regulate.
