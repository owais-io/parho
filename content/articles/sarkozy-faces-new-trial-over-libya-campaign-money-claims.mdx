Former French president Nicolas Sarkozy has appeared before a Paris appeal court to start a new trial over accusations that he arranged illegal campaign money from Libya’s former dictator Muammar Gaddafi. Last year he was found guilty of one count of conspiracy and served 20 days in prison before being released on appeal. The new hearing covers all four charges, and if convicted he could face another decade behind bars. Sarkozy denies any wrongdoing.
