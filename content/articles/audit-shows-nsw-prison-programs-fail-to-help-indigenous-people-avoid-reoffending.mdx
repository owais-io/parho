An audit of New South Wales prisons revealed that programs designed to cut re‑offending rates for Aboriginal inmates are not working. Very few prisoners get culturally appropriate help before release, and more than 60% of released Indigenous adults return to jail within a year. The report also highlights a growing remand population and a lack of support for those on remand. Small, community‑run projects like Rainbow Lodge show promise, but the state must invest more in such programs.
