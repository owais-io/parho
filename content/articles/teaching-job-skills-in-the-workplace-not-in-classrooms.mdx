I argue that learning job skills should mainly happen on the shop floor, not in a classroom. I started in a printing firm apprenticeship, then studied baking at a school, but I learned most on the job. Employers today give narrow training that limits workers, while foreign bakers bring years of hands‑on experience. Schools need to partner with industry to shift training to real work settings, so young people get useful skills and employers get better workers.
