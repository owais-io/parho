The writer spent a day at Jacob Rees‑Mogg’s house where a group of hard‑line Brexiteers gathered to celebrate a new book that claims the UK has benefited from leaving the EU. They also praised Labour’s move to talk about re‑joining. The author argues the group is wrong, pointing out studies that say Brexit has hurt the economy and that the book’s claims are mostly false. The article shows how the debate is still heated and many former supporters are disappointed.
