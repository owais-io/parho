West Midlands police commissioner Simon Foster says he didn’t have the legal authority to immediately fire Chief Constable Craig Guildford after a report found the police wrongly banned Maccabi Tel Aviv fans from an Europa League match. He explains that sacking a chief constable requires a formal process with police watchdogs and panels. Guildford stayed on for two days, then retired. The incident sparked calls for accountability, anti‑bias training, and a review of police procedures.
