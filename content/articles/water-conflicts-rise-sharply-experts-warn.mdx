Water conflicts have nearly doubled since 2022, with 419 incidents in 2024 versus 235 in 2022, says the Pacific Institute. Experts point to climate change, bad governance, and infrastructure failures as causes. Recent fights include disputes over the Indus River, Russia attacking Ukrainian dams, Israel targeting Gaza’s water, and protests in South Africa. Global water demand will outstrip supply by 2030, urging new agreements.
