The UK government will invest up to £1 billion in community‑owned solar, wind, hydro and biomass projects to help local communities control clean energy and reduce bills. The money will be managed by state‑owned GB Energy and shared with the devolved administrations. Funding will support about 1,000 projects, give communities shares in larger schemes, and help install panels on public buildings. The move aims to boost local wealth and address grid upgrade criticism.
