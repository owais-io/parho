The International Energy Agency says governments should cut highway speeds, encourage carpooling, and let people work from home to lower oil demand after Iranian strikes spiked fuel prices. It also suggests odd/even license‑plate traffic rules, car‑sharing, and limits on flights and LPG use. These steps aim to help households and businesses survive a long‑term oil supply shock.
