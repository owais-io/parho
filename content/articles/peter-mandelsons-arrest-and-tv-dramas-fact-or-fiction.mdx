Journalist Simon Jenkins argues that the press and TV shows are turning Peter Mandelson’s recent arrest into sensational drama. He points out how programmes like “The Lady” and “Dirty Business” mix real facts with invented scenes, making it hard to tell truth from fiction. Jenkins warns that this trend erodes the principle that public figures are innocent until proven guilty and encourages responsible journalism instead of sensationalism.
