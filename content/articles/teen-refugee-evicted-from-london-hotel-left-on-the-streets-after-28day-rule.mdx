Teenager who had been granted refugee status in the UK was forced onto the streets after the Home Office cut his hotel stay to 28 days. He asked local council for help but was refused, then was evicted without a blanket or sleeping bag. He survived on a bush near Willesden Junction and was attacked twice. A high‑court case forced the Home Office to recognise that the short rule can leave people homeless, and it now must offer extensions.
