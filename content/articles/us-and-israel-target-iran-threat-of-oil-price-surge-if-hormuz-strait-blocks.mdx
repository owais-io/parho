The US and Israel have launched attacks on Iran, raising fears that the country might block the Strait of Hormuz – a key route that carries about 20% of the world's oil. If the strait were closed, oil prices could jump from $70 to over $100 a barrel, hurting economies and raising fuel costs worldwide. Experts warn that even small disruptions could drive prices higher and strain global trade.
