Indonesia’s floods have claimed 631 lives and forced a million people to leave their homes. Monsoon rains and storms hit Sumatra, Sri Lanka and Thailand, leaving 3.2 million affected, 2,600 injured and 472 missing. Roads blocked, bridges broken, making rescue hard. Aceh faces food shortages; charities and the government are sending rice and oil. WHO teams are on the ground. Survivors say the water came fast and without warning.
