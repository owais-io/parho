Researchers found that about 1 in 7 Australian parents have heard worrying vaccine messages, including myths linking vaccines to autism and criticism from US politicians. The latest survey shows that more parents are now hesitant because of these ideas, even though practical barriers like cost used to dominate. Full vaccination rates have slipped below the 95% target, with measles worries rising as some communities remain unvaccinated. Health officials are urging doctors to talk openly with parents.
