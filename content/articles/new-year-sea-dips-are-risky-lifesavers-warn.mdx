People who plan to jump into the chilly UK sea for a New Year swim are being cautioned. After two men vanished on Christmas Day while swimming in Devon, the RNLI says cold water, wind, and weather can cause sudden shock and drowning, especially at 15 °C or lower. The sea is usually 6‑10 °C. They advise checking your fitness, swimming with others, wearing a wetsuit, and calling 999 if anyone struggles.
