The UK watchdog, the Competition and Markets Authority (CMA), has finished its probe into big vet groups and will introduce new rules to help pet owners save money. The CMA said pet owners have paid about £1 billion too much in the last five years because large chains—many owned by private‑equity firms—have raised prices after buying smaller practices. New measures will cap prescription fees at £21, require clearer price lists, and strengthen complaint handling.
