The BBC’s World Service has only seven weeks of funding left and may shut down if the government doesn't sign a new deal soon. Director General Tim Davie warned that without fresh money, the service could collapse just as other news outlets cut international coverage and fake news spreads fast. He urged the UK government to act quickly and invest more taxpayer money to keep trusted global reporting alive.
