Federal Reserve officials will cut the amount of money banks must keep in reserve, easing the strict rules that were put in place after the 2008 crash. Big banks will see a 4.8% drop, while smaller banks could drop up to 7.7%. The move is praised by banks for giving them more money to lend, but critics say it could make the financial system riskier and hurt small businesses.
