Oil prices have jumped after Iran’s clash with the U.S. and Israel, sending stock markets lower and sparking worries that higher energy costs will drive up inflation while slowing growth – a classic stagflation scenario. Prices hit over $115 a barrel, hurting fuel, gas and fertilizer supplies worldwide. Economists predict higher interest rates and weaker growth in the U.S., Europe, and Asia if the conflict lingers.
