In 2026, many dates will affect your money. In January, mortgage rates drop after the Bank of England cut, and the energy price cap rises by 0.2 %. In February, alcohol duties go up and crypto traders need extra tax paperwork. March sees rail fares frozen in England, and the first BoE rate decision keeps rates steady. The tax year starts April 6, with living wages rising and council tax possibly going up. July 30 is the BoE’s third rate call.
