A new national body called Our Ways Strong Together will start in Canberra this week, set up to cut family and sexual violence against Aboriginal women and children. Advocates say Indigenous people face far higher rates of abuse and that help is often spot‑ty and uneven. The organisation will work across police, courts, housing, health and education to improve prevention, support and data collection, and it is part of a $218m funding package to meet Closing the Gap goals.
