Trump is expected to choose former Fed governor Kevin Warsh as the new chair of the Federal Reserve. Warsh served during the 2008 crisis and is known for pushing lower interest rates, a stance that aligns with Trump’s wishes. The nomination would give the president more influence over the central bank, sparking concerns about the Fed’s independence. The market reacted with a stronger dollar and falling gold prices.
