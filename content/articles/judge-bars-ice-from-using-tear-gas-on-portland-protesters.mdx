A federal judge in Portland has ordered the Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agency to stop firing tear gas, pepper spray and other less‑lethal projectiles at protesters for the next 14 days. The ruling follows a weekend when ICE agents used these weapons on thousands of demonstrators, including children and journalists. The judge said the force was excessive and should only be used if a person poses an immediate threat.
