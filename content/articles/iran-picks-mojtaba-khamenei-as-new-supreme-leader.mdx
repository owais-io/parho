Mojtaba Khamenei, the late Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei’s second son, has been selected to become Iran’s new top authority as the region’s tensions rise. The clerical body announced the decision amid fresh missile and drone attacks. Global leaders, including the U.S., have reacted with concern, and oil prices spiked over $100 a barrel, rattling world markets. The move could heighten the ongoing conflict and spark wider economic shock.
