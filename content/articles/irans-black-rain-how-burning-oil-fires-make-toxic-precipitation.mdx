Earlier this month, deadly black rain fell over parts of Iran after strikes on oil refineries caused large fires. When crude oil burns badly, it releases tiny particles that hang in the air and become the seeds for raindrops. The rain looks dirty and can carry harmful chemicals like benzene and toluene into water and soil, raising cancer risks and hurting crops. If more oil sites are hit, more polluted rain could keep coming for months.
