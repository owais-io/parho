The top adviser of the U.S. vaccine committee, Kirk Milhoan, says all vaccine recommendations are being re‑evaluated. He argues that shots should be optional and based on a doctor’s advice, not mandates. Experts warn that this could lower vaccination rates, risk outbreaks of polio and measles, and create confusion about school rules. The committee’s next meeting is set for February.
