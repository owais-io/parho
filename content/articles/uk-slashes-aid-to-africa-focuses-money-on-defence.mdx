Britain is cutting its aid to the world’s poorest countries, especially in Africa, by more than half. The government will give up almost £900 million in direct help by 2029, saying the money will instead boost defence spending and support big international agencies. The cuts will hit schools, clinics and emergency relief, while some funds stay for conflict zones like Ukraine and Lebanon. Critics say the move weakens stability and hurts the most vulnerable people.
