Cornwall’s Trelavour quarry, once a china‑clay pit, is being turned into the UK’s first large lithium mine. A small company, Cornish Lithium, plans to use the granite’s mica to pull out lithium, which powers batteries for phones, laptops and electric cars. The project aims to create jobs, boost the local economy and reduce the country’s reliance on imported lithium. Workers and locals hope it will bring prosperity while keeping environmental standards high.
