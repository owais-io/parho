The National Gallery is set to lose £8.2 million next year, putting its free‑entry policy and future projects in danger. Despite record crowds at recent exhibitions, visitor numbers have fallen by half since the pandemic. The museum relies on a £32 million government grant that covers only about half its costs, with the rest coming from ticket fees, shop sales and sponsorships. Experts say cutting funding would hurt the UK’s cultural heritage and tourism.
