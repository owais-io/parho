With the 2032 Olympics pushing new construction into Brisbane’s old streets, a group of university students is working fast to rescue everyday objects—ceramic plates, pipes, and even a chamberpot—displaced by floods and future building plans. In a museum workshop, they assemble fragile shards, learning how physical handling connects us to the past. Their hands‑on experience shows how archaeology is both a science and a tactile adventure.
