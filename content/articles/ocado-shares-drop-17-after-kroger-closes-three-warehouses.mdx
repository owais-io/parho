Ocado, the online‑grocer tech company, saw its shares fall 17% after Kroger, its biggest US partner, announced it would shut three automated warehouses in Maryland, Wisconsin and Florida. The closures cut a major part of Ocado’s plan to sell its warehouse software abroad. Kroger said it is moving to a mix of store‑based and warehouse solutions and is expanding ties with quick‑delivery services. Ocado will receive some compensation but still faces a hit to its revenue.
