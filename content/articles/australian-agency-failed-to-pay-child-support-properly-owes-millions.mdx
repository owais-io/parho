A report by the Commonwealth Ombudsman says Services Australia ignored the law for six years, leaving thousands of parents unpaid. The agency’s rule that parents who care for less than 35 % of a child shouldn’t receive support conflicts with the law. At least 16,600 people are owed up to $10,000 each. The ombudsman made six recommendations; Services Australia plans to amend the law and roll out a new computer system by early 2026.
