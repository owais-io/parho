Banjima Indigenous people in Western Australia are suing the state for $1.5 billion to clear a huge pile of toxic blue asbestos left by a mine that operated from 1943 to 1966. The asbestos has caused many deaths and stops the community from living on their land. The claim asks the government to close three abandoned mines, remove the waste and pay compensation. Premier Roger Cook said he will try to negotiate a fix.
