After the death of Iran’s top leader, the regime has chosen his son, Mojtaba Khamenei, as the new supreme leader. The move keeps the conservative line but raises questions because the 1979 revolution never planned for a father‑to‑son succession. Reformist politicians and some clerics worry that the new leader is inexperienced and could destabilise the country during wartime. The U.S. and Israel have criticised the decision, while Iran faces continued tensions with Gulf states over missile attacks.
