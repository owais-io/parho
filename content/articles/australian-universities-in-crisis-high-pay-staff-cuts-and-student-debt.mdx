Australia’s universities are in crisis. Over the past decades, growth, globalisation and market pressures have turned campuses into profit‑focused, bureaucratic organisations. Vice‑chancellors earn high salaries while staff are cut and student debt rises. Governance and transparency are weak, leading to staff mental‑health concerns and student worries about housing and jobs. The article calls for more public funding, a shift from market levers to public good and greater community involvement.
