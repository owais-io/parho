Axon, the biggest US body‑camera maker, posted a record $797 million in revenue—up 39% year‑over‑year—while preparing to sell more gear to the Department of Homeland Security. The company highlighted its AI‑driven features, including voice‑activated tools and real‑time analytics. Despite the boost, Axon’s CEO warned that privacy and data misuse could spark backlash. Critics fear the footage may be used for mass surveillance of immigrants and protesters. It also wants to expand into license‑plate readers, sparking community concerns about immigration enforcement.
