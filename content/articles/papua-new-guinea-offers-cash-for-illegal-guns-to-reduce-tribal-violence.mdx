Papua New Guinea launched a gun‑buyback program that pays people to hand in illegal firearms. The move aims to cut the country’s rate of tribal killings, especially in the Highlands where land disputes and political tensions have made violence deadly. The scheme runs from February to August, offering money and sometimes help to start businesses. While the government will enforce strict penalties for those who keep guns, critics say deeper law reforms are also needed.
