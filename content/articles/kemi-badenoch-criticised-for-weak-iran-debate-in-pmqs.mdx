During a recent Prime Minister’s Questions, Kemi Badenoch was widely criticised for a weak performance on Iran. She seemed unsure of the UK’s stance, struggled to answer questions about a potential war, and made off‑target remarks about the armed forces. In contrast, Labour’s Keir Starmer spoke seriously about protecting UK nationals and avoiding escalation. The debate highlighted the gap between the two parties on foreign policy.
