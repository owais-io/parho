The Trump administration has paused all visa processing for people from 75 countries, starting on Jan. 21. Officials say the move is to stop those who might rely on U.S. welfare programs. However, a study shows that immigrants use 21% less welfare than native‑born Americans. The freeze follows Trump’s earlier travel bans and other immigration restrictions, adding to the debate over immigration limits and national security.
