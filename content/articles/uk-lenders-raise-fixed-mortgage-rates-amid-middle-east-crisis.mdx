Three major UK lenders—HSBC, Nationwide and Coventry—have raised their fixed‑rate mortgage costs because the Middle East conflict could push energy prices higher, leading to more inflation and a possible rise in Bank of England rates. Brokers warn that the move will set off a chain reaction, so more lenders may hike rates soon. This means borrowers with contracts ending in 2026 may need to remortgage at higher costs, hurting homebuyers.
