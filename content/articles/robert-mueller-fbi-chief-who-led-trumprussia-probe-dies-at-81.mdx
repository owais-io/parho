Robert Mueller, the former FBI chief who led the two‑year probe into Russian meddling in the 2016 U.S. election, died at 81. His investigation, published in 2019, found Russian interference but could not prove that the Trump campaign colluded. It highlighted possible obstruction by Trump and led to indictments of Russian spies and Trump advisers like Paul Manafort and Michael Flynn. Mueller was also known for his tough stance on 9/11 surveillance and torture.
