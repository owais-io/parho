A recent night in Cartagena saw about 300 people cheering after a bloody cockfight, even though Colombia’s Supreme Court has banned the sport. The ban, announced in September, gives a three‑year transition period while the government plans new jobs for the tens of thousands who rely on cockfighting. Many breeders and workers feel the move threatens their culture and income, and some lawmakers want the law undone.
