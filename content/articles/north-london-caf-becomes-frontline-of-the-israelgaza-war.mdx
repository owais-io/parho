Faten and Mahmoud run a supper club at Café Metro in north London, serving Palestinian dishes and raising money for Gaza. The small café has become a target of pro‑Israel vandalism and of a new Gail’s bakery that critics say fuels gentrification and is linked to Israeli security firms. The attacks, stickers and council raids show how food can turn into a front line of the Israel‑Gaza conflict, even in a quiet neighbourhood.
