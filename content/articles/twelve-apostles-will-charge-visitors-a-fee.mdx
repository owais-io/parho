The Victorian government will start charging tourists to see the famous Twelve Apostles rock stacks on the Great Ocean Road. The fee, likely under $20, will help fund protection against climate change, erosion and the impact of millions of visitors each year. Locals and Indigenous people are exempt, and a new visitor centre opening in 2026 will manage bookings. The move aims to reduce crowding and preserve the site, but critics say it’s an extra cost for Victorians.
