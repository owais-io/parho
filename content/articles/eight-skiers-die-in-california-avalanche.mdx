A fresh avalanche near Castle Peak in the Sierra Nevada killed at least eight skiers. The Lake Tahoe area has been very dangerous, with more than 50 avalanches recorded since September 2025 and a current high‑risk rating of 4 out of 5. California has seen several fatal back‑country avalanches over the past decade, making this the fourth deadliest in U.S. history. The crash happened when a weak, icy layer was topped by heavy new snow.
