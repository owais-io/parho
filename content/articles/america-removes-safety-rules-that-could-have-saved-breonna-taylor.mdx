Breonna Taylor, a 26‑year‑old ER tech, was shot to death during a no‑knock police raid in Louisville in 2020. Her death sparked reforms that banned such raids in Louisville and tightened federal rules. But last year the Trump administration quietly rolled back those limits, giving federal police more freedom to use no‑knock warrants. The move shows the government is ignoring lessons from Taylor’s death, especially as Black communities remain most at risk.
