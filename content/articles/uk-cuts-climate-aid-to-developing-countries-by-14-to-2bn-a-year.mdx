The UK will trim its climate aid to poorer nations by about 14%, cutting the budget to roughly £2 billion per year. The reduction hits programs on health, education and humanitarian help and eliminates a £3 billion earmark for nature and forest projects. Treasury officials say the money is needed for defence and the economy amid the Iran conflict, but MPs and experts warn the cuts hurt global security and health.
