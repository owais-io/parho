York Minster has opened a new exhibition to celebrate the 800th anniversary of Saint William of York, a forgotten 12th‑century martyr. The highlight is a digital reconstruction of his once‑huge shrine that was buried during Henry VIII’s Reformation. Visitors can see the film, original stone slabs, and surviving shrine fragments, while a 12th‑century bible and an ivory casket are on display. The show shows how the saint’s miracles and art still inspire the cathedral today.
