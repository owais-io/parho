30,000 car‑loan borrowers are suing Lloyds’ motor‑finance arm, Black Horse, for overcharging. A law firm plans a £66 million omnibus claim, arguing that unfair dealer commissions hurt consumers. The suit comes as the FCA’s £11 bn compensation scheme looks to give customers only about £700 each, less than half the amount many say is fair. The case could set a precedent for other lenders.
