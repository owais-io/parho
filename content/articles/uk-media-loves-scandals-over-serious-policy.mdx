UK media loves scandals over serious policy. The Peter Mandelson affair showed how politicians and the press turn every incident into a leadership crisis, keeping the public on a short‑sighted, sensationalist track. Social media turns a quick tweet into a headline, and 24‑hour news stations thrive on drama. The article argues we need calm, thoughtful reporting that focuses on real issues, not instant buzz.
