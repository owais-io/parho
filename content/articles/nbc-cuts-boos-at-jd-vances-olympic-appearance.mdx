During the Winter Olympics opening ceremony in Milan, US Vice‑President JD Vance and his wife were booed by the crowd, but NBC’s TV feed omitted the sound of the boos. Canadian TV and social media showed the reaction, while the White House shared a clip that hid the dissent. The incident follows earlier moves to keep US audiences from seeing protests at sporting events.
