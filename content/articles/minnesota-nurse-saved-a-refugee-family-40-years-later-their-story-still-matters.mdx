A Cambodian refugee family fled the Khmer Rouge and found hope in Australia thanks to a kind Minnesota nurse named Sandra Evenson. Forty years later, the nurse was killed by ICE agents in the U.S. The author, who grew up in Sydney, reconnected with Sandra, revealing the lasting impact of compassion. The story shows how volunteers can save lives even as governments clamp down on migrants.
