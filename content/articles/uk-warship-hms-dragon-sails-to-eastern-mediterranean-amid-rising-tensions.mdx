The Royal Navy's destroyer HMS Dragon left Portsmouth for the eastern Mediterranean after a rapid six‑day prep. It will head to Cyprus to guard against Iranian drones or missiles and support any humanitarian aid. The move comes as tensions rise over attacks on the Strait of Hormuz and the US, France and the UK weigh protecting oil tankers. Britain says it prefers de‑escalation but will defend civilian ships if needed.
