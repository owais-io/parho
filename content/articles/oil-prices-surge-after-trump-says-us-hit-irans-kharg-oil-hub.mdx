Oil prices climbed to over $100 a barrel after President Trump said the U.S. had hit Iran’s Kharg Island oil hub. Trump told NBC that most of the island was destroyed and asked allies to help reopen the Strait of Hormuz, a key route for global oil. The strike and the ongoing tensions in the Middle East worried traders, pushing crude up and raising fuel costs worldwide.
