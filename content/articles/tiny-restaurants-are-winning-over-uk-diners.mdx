The trend of micro‑restaurants, seating fewer than 20, is spreading across the UK. They use a single communal table, making it easy for singles and strangers to connect. Owners say the closeness lets chefs see diners’ reactions, creating a personal vibe. The downside is limited space and high costs; a full house is essential, and menus are pricey. Yet the intimate setting keeps customers coming back.
