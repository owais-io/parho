Leila Cader and her friends were looking for a Christmas market in Rome’s Castel Sant’Angelo gardens. Instead they found a festival organized by the far‑right Brothers of Italy, the party of Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni. The event had a billboard showing hateful comments against her and a banner of American activist Charlie Kirk. The festival mixes politicians, celebrities and Catholic figures, and is seen as a platform for Meloni to boost her power while critics worry about rising censorship and police power.
