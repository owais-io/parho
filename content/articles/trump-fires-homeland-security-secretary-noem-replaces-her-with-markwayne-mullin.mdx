President Donald Trump fired Kristi Noem as the head of the Department of Homeland Security after weeks of criticism over her leadership, including the shooting deaths of two U.S. citizens by ICE agents. Trump said Markwayne Mullin, a U.S. senator, will take over on March 31. Noem will become a special envoy for a new security program. Democrats welcomed the change, while some Republicans also expressed concerns about Noem’s conduct.
