The UK watchdog CMA ordered vets to stop charging more than £21 for a prescription and set a £12.50 limit for extra medicines. It also wants vets to show where a practice belongs to a big chain, give clear price lists and let owners compare prices online. The aim is to make pet care costs predictable and cheaper for owners, who spent over £6.7bn on vets last year.
