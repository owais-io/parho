After the 1857 rebellion, the British started a massive photo‑survey called “People of India” to catalogue Indians by caste, occupation and appearance. The pictures, now shown in a Delhi gallery with the book *Typecasting*, label subjects with generic titles like “manure dryer” or “snake charmer.” While the photos show India’s great diversity, they also reveal how the colonial government tried to use photography as a tool of control and classification.
