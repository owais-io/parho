The Telegraph published a made‑up story about a wealthy couple and their children struggling with rising private‑school fees. A reporter claimed the family was real, but no records exist. The press watchdog IPPO found the article broke rules, and the Telegraph removed it, issued an apology, and pledged stronger checks. The case highlights the danger of publishing unverified stories.
