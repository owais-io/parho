Trump gave Iran 48 hours to open the Strait of Hormuz for shipping, threatening to blast Iranian power plants if it stayed shut. The move comes after Iran fired missiles at Israel, prompting Israeli strikes on Iranian facilities. Iran says it will hit U.S. and regional infrastructure in retaliation. The standoff has closed a key oil route, driving crude prices above $105 a barrel. Several countries call for safe passage and consider mining clearance in the strait.
