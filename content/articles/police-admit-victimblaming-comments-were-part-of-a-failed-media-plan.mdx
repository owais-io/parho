Police officers in Queensland said that hurtful remarks about Hannah Clarke, who was murdered with her children, were made as part of a planned media strategy that went wrong. Senior officer Mark Thompson was removed from the case after critics called his comments victim‑blaming. The police later presented a seminar showing they still thought Thompson was right. The Guardian’s investigation revealed mistakes before Clarke’s death and a lack of proper review.
