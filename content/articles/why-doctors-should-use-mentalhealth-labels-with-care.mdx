The piece argues that the rise in mental‑health diagnoses, like ADHD and autism, may be partly driven by social inequality and a trend toward over‑labeling. Experts say that while labels can give patients a useful framework, they can also create stigma and hinder treatment. The article calls for a balanced, flexible approach that considers both the risks of misdiagnosis and the need to support those genuinely affected.
