Labour’s Home Secretary, Shabana Mahmood, has introduced new asylum rules in the UK. The policy gives refugees a temporary 30‑month status, so they can stay only until that period ends before they can apply for permanent settlement. It also limits how long the government can keep status pending and cuts the time refugees wait for a decision. The changes have drawn criticism from Labour MPs and others worried about forced removal.
