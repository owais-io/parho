Over 2,000 ABC workers walked off the job for 24 hours, forcing the Australian broadcaster to replace its TV, radio, and digital programs with BBC World Service content and reruns. Staff protested a low pay rise that lagged inflation, poor working conditions, and the threat of AI replacing journalists. ABC's boss said the company wouldn't budge, while unions said the offer was too small. The strike shut flagship news shows, and ABC asked the Fair Work Commission for help.
