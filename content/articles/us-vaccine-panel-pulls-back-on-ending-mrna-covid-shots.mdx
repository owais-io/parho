Federal advisers who help decide U.S. vaccine rules have stopped plans to remove mRNA COVID‑19 shots from the national schedule. The move comes as Republicans worry that changing vaccine policy could hurt them in the November midterms. Health officials say the panel still keeps the shots on the list under shared decision‑making, letting doctors and patients decide individually. Critics point to past claims that the vaccines carry hidden risks.
