Peepal Tree Press, a small independent publisher from Leeds, turned a hobby into a 40‑year‑old business that champions Caribbean and Black British writers. Founder Jeremy Poynting started it after noticing a gap in the market, printing books himself in a garage. The press has published award winners like Bernardine Evaristo and Monique Roffey, and focuses on nurturing new talent while staying close to the cultures it represents. It shows how passion and persistence can keep a niche publisher alive.
