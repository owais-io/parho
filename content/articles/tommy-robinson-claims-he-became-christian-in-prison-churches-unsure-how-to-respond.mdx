Tommy Robinson, the anti‑immigration leader, says he became a Christian while in prison. After his release he joined far‑right rallies that mix politics with Christian symbols. Churches are split: some want to condemn racism and Islamophobia, while others worry that any strong reply might give the movement more publicity. Church leaders are debating whether to speak out or stay quiet as Robinson’s faith‑based message spreads in the UK.
