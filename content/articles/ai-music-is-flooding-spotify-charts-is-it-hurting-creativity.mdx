Dave Schilling argues that AI‑generated songs are climbing Spotify charts and some creators say the technology lets people who can’t play instruments make music. But he worries the flood of machine‑made tracks replaces real human effort and adds clutter. He points out that big media companies value quantity over quality, pushing more content just to keep users scrolling. In the end, the writer fears tech is stealing the joy of creating, not helping it.
