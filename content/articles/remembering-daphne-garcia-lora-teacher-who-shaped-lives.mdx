Daphne Garcia Lora, a 98‑year‑old former head of modern languages at King Edward VI in Birmingham, died peacefully. She taught French and Spanish, supported students with tough homes, and helped many reach university. Born in Manchester, she grew up in war‑time Wales, studied at Liverpool, and married Spanish exile José Garcia Lora. After retiring she travelled widely, studied cosmology, and raised three grandchildren. She is survived by daughters and grandchildren.
