Australia is moving to stop children under 16 from using social media. It began with a psychologist’s idea and a state summit, then became a fast‑track federal law. The bill bans platforms like TikTok, Meta, Snapchat, Reddit, Twitch and now YouTube after an eSafety review. Most tech firms say they’ll comply, while a court challenge is delayed. The ban will start in 2025, aiming to keep kids off screens and onto sports fields.
