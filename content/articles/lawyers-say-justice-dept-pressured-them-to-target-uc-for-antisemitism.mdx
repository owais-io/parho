Nine former DOJ lawyers say they were pushed by political appointees to file a civil‑rights case against the University of California system over alleged antisemitism during pro‑Palestinian protests. They claim the investigation started before facts were checked and that the Trump administration used funding threats to pressure universities. After the case, all of the lawyers quit. A judge recently blocked the DOJ from withholding federal money, and UCLA has already paid a $6.5 million settlement.
