Gopichand Hinduja, one of four brothers who grew their father’s trading business into a global empire, died at 85. He was Britain’s richest man, with assets worth over £35 billion. The family owned everything from car makers and banks to Bollywood films. Gopichand ran the London office, opened the Raffles hotel, and was known for lavish parties. His life was also marked by legal battles, arms‑trade rumours and high‑profile charity work. He is survived by his wife, children and siblings.
