This summer in Australia was a record‑topping mix of heat and rain. Across the country, temperatures were 1.1 °C above average, making it the eighth‑hottest summer since records began, and rainfall was 32 % higher than normal, the wettest season in almost ten years. South Australia saw huge swings – a 50 °C heatwave in January followed by February rains that were more than triple the average. Experts warn that such extreme weather is likely to get worse with climate change.
