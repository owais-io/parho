The Egyptian‑British activist Alaa Abd el‑Fattah finally landed in London after Egypt removed the travel ban that had blocked him for years. He had been jailed for nearly a decade for criticizing the government, and his family had campaigned for his release. The new deal lets him move between the UK and Egypt. His mother, a former hunger‑strike protester, and UK officials welcomed the news, hoping it marks a fresh start for the family.
