UK immigration officers have raided restaurants, demanding passports from staff, often focusing on non‑white, foreign‑born workers. In Surrey, 11 officers burst into Mandira’s Kitchen, questioning kitchen staff for almost an hour, finding no illegal workers. The raids are part of a sharp rise in Home Office enforcement, driven by anonymous tips and a law that lets officers enter licensed premises without a warrant. Victims say the tactics are intimidating and unfair.
