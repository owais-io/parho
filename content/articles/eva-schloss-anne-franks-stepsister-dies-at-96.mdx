Eva Schloss, who survived Auschwitz and became Anne Frank’s stepsister, died at 96. The former Nazi victim spent her life fighting hatred and running the Anne Frank Trust in the UK. She grew up in Vienna, moved to Amsterdam, was captured at 15, sent to Auschwitz with her mother and later reunited with her family. King Charles, who once danced with her in London, praised her courage and work to teach about the Holocaust. Her family mourns her loss.
