Arthur Bondar, a Ukrainian‑Russian photographer, has spent years buying tiny WWII photo negatives from around the world. He smuggled the collection out of Moscow in 2023, risking confiscation and jail. The archive, which shows soldiers, civilians and everyday scenes, challenges the heroic war narrative used in Russian propaganda. Bondar shares the photos online, in books and exhibitions, hoping to preserve the truth of the war.
