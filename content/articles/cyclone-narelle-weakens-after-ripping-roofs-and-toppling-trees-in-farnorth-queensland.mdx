Cyclone Narelle weakened from a Category 4 to a Category 2 storm after tearing roofs off houses and toppling trees in far‑north Queensland. It crossed the Cape York Peninsula, then moved west over the Gulf of Carpentaria, where it may strengthen again. The Bureau warned of winds up to 150 km/h, heavy rain and flooding in towns such as Coen and Lockhart River. Climate experts say the cyclone’s strength was helped by hotter seas.
