Last year Britain gave £14 bn to charity, but that number hides a fall in both how many people donate and how much they give. The new CAF report says only 55 % of Britons gave, down from 69 % a decade ago – a loss of six million donors. Rising living costs and a shift to online requests have made giving feel less normal. The charity sector warns that Britain’s culture of giving is fragile and could shrink further.
