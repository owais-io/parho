A software glitch in Lloyds’ mobile banking apps on March 12 let up to 447,936 customers see other users’ private details, like account numbers and national insurance numbers. About 114,000 people actually clicked on transactions that revealed this info. Lloyds reported the issue to regulators, paid £139,000 to 3,625 customers, and said no money was lost. The incident highlights risks as banks shift more services to digital channels.
