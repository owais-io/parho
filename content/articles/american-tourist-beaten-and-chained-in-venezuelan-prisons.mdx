American tourist was detained at a Venezuelan military checkpoint and then imprisoned in brutal conditions, suffering beatings, chaining and lack of food or water. He is one of about 700 political prisoners. The 28‑year‑old New Yorker spent a month in harsh jails, including the notorious DGCIM headquarters and El Rodeo, before being flown out in January. His story highlights the widespread mistreatment of political prisoners in Venezuela and the regime’s use of torture to silence dissent.
