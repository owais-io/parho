A brother trying to travel back to Afghanistan is stuck in Istanbul, missing family events. His home in the Paktika province is now ruins, but memories of a communal guesthouse called the bala khana remain. That space used to host village jirgas—quiet meetings where neighbors solved disputes over tea. In Afghanistan’s recent wars, staying neutral can make civilians look like enemies, putting them in danger. Global power struggles have made these local peace practices hard to keep alive.
