John Lewis, a chemist who worked for Reckitt & Colman and later universities, helped create buprenorphine, a drug that now treats pain after surgery and replaces methadone for opioid addiction. His research made buprenorphine, sold as Subutex and Suboxone, the leading medicines for drug‑addiction treatment worldwide. He earned international awards, met with high‑level leaders, and left a legacy of hope for millions who now use his drug for relief.
