During a Senate hearing, FCC chair Brendan Carr admitted the agency is not independent but follows the president’s wishes, saying it serves at Trump’s pleasure. He said he uses the “public interest” rule to silence critics of Trump. The move has worried reporters who fear censorship, and even some Republicans question his fairness. Carr also approved a big media merger after a settlement with Trump, raising doubts about his neutrality.
