Storm Goretti slammed north‑west Europe, bringing record wind speeds over 210 kph that knocked power out in about 400,000 French homes. A few people were injured, trains and schools shut, and high‑speed rail kept running but local services paused. Germany, calling the coming front “Elli,” warned of heavy snow up to 20 cm, temperatures as low as –20 °C, and rail cancellations. Authorities urged people to stay home and keep emergency supplies.
