Scientists studied data from 156 countries and found that every extra month when temperatures stay above 27.8 °C makes people less active by 1.5 % worldwide, and even more in poorer countries. Less exercise boosts heart disease, diabetes, cancer, and mental health problems. By 2050, the rise in inactivity could cause 500,000 extra early deaths and billions in lost productivity. Hot spots—Central America, the Caribbean, parts of Africa and Asia—will feel the hit hardest.
