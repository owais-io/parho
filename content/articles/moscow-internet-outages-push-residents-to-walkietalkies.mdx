Moscow residents are losing internet access in the city’s centre and in St. Petersburg, forcing them to use old‑school walkie‑talkies and pagers. The Kremlin says the outages are for “security” but experts suspect the government is testing a “whitelist” that would let only approved sites stay online. Businesses lose up to a billion roubles a day, and lawmakers complain they can’t use Wi‑Fi in Parliament. The move worries people about future censorship.
