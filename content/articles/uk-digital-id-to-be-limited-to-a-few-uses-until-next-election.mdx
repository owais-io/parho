Britain’s £1.8bn digital ID app will only be used for a few things before the next general election, says minister Darren Jones. He says only simple tasks like paying vehicle tax or checking right‑to‑work status will be available by then. Big uses such as claiming benefits or voting will wait until the next parliament. The government will consult the public for eight weeks, and any new uses will need parliamentary approval.
