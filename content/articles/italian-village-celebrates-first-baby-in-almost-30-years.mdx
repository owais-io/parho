A tiny Italian village finally welcomed a baby after almost 30 years, and the whole community—plus the cats—celebrated the birth of Lara Trabucco. The news drew tourists and highlighted Italy’s record low birth rate, driven by job insecurity, migration and poor childcare support. The couple got a small government bonus, but still juggle work and parenting. Nearby schools and maternity units risk closing as births drop, raising concerns for future families.
