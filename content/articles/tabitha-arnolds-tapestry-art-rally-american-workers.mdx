Tabitha Arnold, a textile artist from Chattanooga, makes huge tapestries that show working‑class struggles. In New York her exhibition drew young union activists, but her biggest impact was in Tennessee. She unveiled a piece called These Hands at a Volkswagen plant union hall just before workers voted to join the U.S. Auto Workers. Her art helped inspire the vote and later a wage‑increase contract. She’s won awards and keeps making art for organizers.
