In March, the Trump team fired most people who monitored how the Department of Homeland Security treats people in detention. The tiny new teams investigate far fewer complaints—just a few percent of what they used to handle. A lawsuit says the cuts break the law and let abuses slip through. Critics fear that fewer watchdogs mean more danger for detained immigrants, especially after a record number of deaths in custody in 2025.
