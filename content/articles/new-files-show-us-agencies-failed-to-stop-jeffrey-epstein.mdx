New DOJ files reveal that US authorities knew about Jeffrey Epstein’s abuse for years but failed to act, sparking calls for accountability. The documents trace reports from 1996, a 2008 plea deal, and a 2011 interview with an abused girl, yet no meaningful action was taken. Victims say the FBI and prosecutors ignored warnings about Epstein and Ghislaine Maxwell. The release highlights how institutional inaction let further abuse happen.
