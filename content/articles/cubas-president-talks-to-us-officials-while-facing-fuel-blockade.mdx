Cuba’s President Miguel Díaz‑Canel said his government has been talking to U.S. officials, even as the island faces a long‑running fuel blockade that has caused widespread power outages. The talks aim to find ways to ease tensions, but details are scarce. Meanwhile, the government plans to release 51 prisoners, though who will be freed is unclear. Protesters have been banging pans and students have staged sit‑ins, showing growing frustration at the crisis.
