The U.S. economy slipped in February, losing 92,000 jobs and raising the unemployment rate to 4.4%. That’s a sharp drop after January’s strong gains. Big sectors hit include healthcare, which swung from a big gain to a loss, and federal government jobs. The weak data comes just before the Fed meeting on March 17‑18, which could decide interest‑rate changes. Many companies are holding off on hiring because of economic uncertainty.
