When ICE started raiding Chicago in September, residents learned how to spot agents, share rights info, and use whistles to shout a warning. The whistle trick spread to New York, New Orleans, Charlotte, and other places, thanks to groups in Los Angeles and Chicago sharing tips and kits. City groups, nonprofits and vendors now run quick patrols, call hotlines, and hold trainings together. By working as a network, they make raids harder for ICE.
