Italian regulators are looking into Sephora and Benefit Cosmetics, both owned by LVMH, for allegedly using hidden marketing tactics that push skincare items onto girls under 10. The authorities say the brands use child influencers and promotions, which could fuel a new obsession called “cosmeticorexia.” They are checking if safety warnings are missing and if kids are being sold inappropriate products. LVMH says it will cooperate.
