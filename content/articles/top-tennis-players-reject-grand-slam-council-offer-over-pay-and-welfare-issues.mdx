The world's top tennis players have turned down a Grand Slam offer to create a player council, saying it comes too late without concrete pay and welfare changes. They want a 22% share of Grand Slam revenue by 2030, but current prize pools are only 12‑16% of income. The players want a meeting that also addresses salaries, health and benefit contributions before any governance talks. The dispute highlights frustration over recent camera and match‑length changes at the Australian Open.
