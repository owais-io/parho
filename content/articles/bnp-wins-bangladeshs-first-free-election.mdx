BNP wins Bangladesh’s first free election after a student‑led uprising that toppled long‑time leader Sheikh Hasina. The party took a two‑thirds majority with 181 seats, beating the Islamist Jamaat‑e‑Islami. Voter turnout was 61%, the highest in years, and the vote was seen as a hopeful step toward democracy and ending corruption. India and the US congratulated the new government for the people’s future.
