Three skiers died when an avalanche struck the French Alps, including two Britons and a lone French skier. They were in Val d’Isère on an off‑piste run with a ski instructor, who escaped injury and tested drug‑free. A manslaughter probe is underway. The area had a red avalanche alert. Similar danger has killed 11 people in Italy’s Alps, sparking warnings across the range.
