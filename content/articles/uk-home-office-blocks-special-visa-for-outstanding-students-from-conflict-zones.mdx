The Home Office has rejected a request to keep a special visa exemption for top students from countries like Afghanistan, Sudan, Myanmar and Cameroon. The Chevening scholarship program, which sends over 1,000 international students to UK universities each year, had a “carve‑out” that was cancelled. Officials say the move stops visa abuse, but critics argue it unfairly blocks vulnerable students and hurts UK ties with those nations.
