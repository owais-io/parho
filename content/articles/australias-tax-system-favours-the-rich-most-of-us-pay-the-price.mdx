The article uses new statistics to show how much Australians earn and how the tax system rewards the wealthy. A few thousand people with more than $3 million in super get $22 billion in tax breaks each year, while the government spends far less on schools and jobseeker. Most workers earn far below the median, and house prices are rising faster than wages, especially hurting women. So the rich get the biggest perks, while ordinary Australians struggle.
