UK borrowing costs have reached their highest level since 2008, pushing the 10‑year gilt yield to 5%. Markets now think the Bank of England could raise rates up to three times this year because of the Iran crisis, high inflation and a weak economy. The rise makes it more expensive for the government to repay debt, hurting the chancellor’s plans and shaking stocks and the pound.
