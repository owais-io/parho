Tennessee has started a new database that lists people who have been convicted of domestic abuse. Called “Savanna’s law,” it keeps names, birth dates, photos and the locations of up to two or more domestic‑violence convictions for up to 20 years. The registry will not hold addresses. The law was created after Savanna Puckett, a 22‑year‑old deputy, was murdered by her ex‑boyfriend in 2022. The registry is meant to help keep communities safer.
