Musician Khalidou Sy and other Mauritanians have settled in Lockland, Ohio, after a perilous 15‑day trip across Central America. They fled racism, slavery and poor conditions in Mauritania, where human‑rights abuses persist. Their arrival has sparked anger among some residents and put a strain on local housing and fire services. More than 19,000 Mauritanian cases are pending in U.S. courts, and dozens have already been sent back by ICE. Local volunteers help them get jobs and adapt.
