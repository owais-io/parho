Chapel Down, a Kent wine maker, saw sales jump 19% to £19.4 million last year, led by a 38% rise in retail and strong exports. CEO James Pennefather said the growth comes from a generational shift: millennials now favour English sparkling wines for their light, crisp style. Prices start at £15 and top over £100. Despite hospitality pressure, the firm shipped a million bottles in 2025 and plans to keep expanding.
