Jeane Freeman, a key figure in Scotland’s government, died at 72. She helped launch Scotland’s first social security system and later served as health secretary during the COVID‑19 crisis. A former nurse and activist, she was known for her fairness, courage and open debate. Freeman worked closely with First Minister Nicola Sturgeon and was praised for her support of patients and care‑home families.
