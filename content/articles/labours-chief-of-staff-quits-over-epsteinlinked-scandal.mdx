Labour’s top aide Morgan McSweeney has quit, leaving PM Sir Keir Starmer without his main strategist after a fallout over Lord Mandelson’s controversial US ambassador post tied to Jeffrey Epstein. The move has exposed cracks in the party’s leadership and raised doubts about Starmer’s control. Calls for new vetting rules and tougher lobbying rules are growing, while party members worry about division ahead of local elections and the upcoming national vote.
