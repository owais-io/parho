During the first week of the U.S. attack on Iran, the government spent about $11.3 billion on bombs and related military costs. That single week’s expense is more than the entire annual budgets of agencies like the EPA, CDC, and the National Cancer Institute. While the administration pushes big cuts to public‑health and science funding, Congress has kept those budgets near previous levels. Scientists worry the war money crowds out research and shows a shift toward militarism.
