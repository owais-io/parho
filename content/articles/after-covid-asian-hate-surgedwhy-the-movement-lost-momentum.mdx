After Covid, attacks on Asian Americans jumped. A movement called Stop Asian Hate grew quickly, gathering reports, pushing laws, and organising patrols. It got some wins—more hate‑crime rules and state funds for support—but its energy faded when the media and politics turned away. Divides inside the community over policing and anti‑Blackness also slowed progress. Today, the movement is trying to keep fighting, but it needs more long‑term, community‑driven change.
