Four shark attacks in NSW waters in 48 hours have sent Sydney residents scrambling for safe swimming spots. Popular netted beaches and ocean pools are packed, and places like Wylie’s Baths had to limit entries and saw higher entrance fees. Councils say the crowds have risen, but exact numbers are unclear. While some swimmers are still cautious, the government is funding drones and tagging to improve safety.
