Nnena Kalu, an autistic artist with a learning disability, has won the Turner Prize for her colorful, ribbon‑like sculptures made from old VHS tape and fabric. Some critics praise her talent, while others say she won because of pity and treat her work as therapy rather than art. The article argues that art is about personal expression, not medical labels, and that Kalu’s win shows a growing acceptance of diverse artists.
