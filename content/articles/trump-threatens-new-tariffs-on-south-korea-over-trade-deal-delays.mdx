President Trump said he will raise U.S. tariffs on South Korean goods from 15% to 25% because Seoul hasn’t approved a trade deal signed last year. The move sent South Korean car‑maker stocks down at first. Seoul’s government is scrambling to finish the required laws and has promised to keep the investment and tariff terms in place. The dispute shows how stalled trade talks can trigger sharp tariff hikes.
