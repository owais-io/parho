The National Police Chiefs’ Council says police will do more to hold domestic abusers accountable when victims commit suicide. Assistant Commissioner Louisa Rolfe noted that post‑suicide investigations are increasing, but police lack resources, with many cases slipping through because of digital evidence and court delays. New guidance now forces officers to look deeper into abuse links, but the workload remains a major hurdle.
