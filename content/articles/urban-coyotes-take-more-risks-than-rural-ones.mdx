Researchers across the US tested 623 camera sites to see how coyotes react to new things in cities versus the countryside. They found that city coyotes are less scared of unfamiliar objects and stay longer near food. The study shows that urban coyotes are risk‑takers but not more aggressive. The findings can help cities plan how to live safely with coyotes, and scientists are now testing whether shooing them away works.
