Target, with almost 2,000 stores, announced it will stop selling breakfast cereals that contain synthetic dyes by May 31. The move is part of a broader push to cut artificial ingredients and respond to customers who want healthier food. Food‑health activists praised the decision, and the FDA’s latest rules let brands claim “no artificial colors” if they drop petroleum‑based dyes. Several states are already banning these chemicals, and big brands plan similar changes.
