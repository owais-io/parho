Emma Beddington writes a playful list of nine science ideas she’d like to see in 2026. She wants researchers to rethink sitting, discover new insects and a cute mammal, and make live‑translation tech that tells pets how they feel. She also hopes for a cure for earworms, a way to turn scrolling or even anger into clean energy, and short exercise to fight bowel cancer. Finally, she dreams of gadgets that help people sleep on time.
