Between 1553 and 1555, Spanish and Portuguese Jews who fled the Inquisition found a safe home in Ferrara, Italy. There they printed the first full Hebrew Bible in Spanish so that their children could read it without knowing Hebrew. The book helped the community rebuild its traditions after centuries of hidden practice. A new edition has been released, showing how these refugees kept their faith alive.
