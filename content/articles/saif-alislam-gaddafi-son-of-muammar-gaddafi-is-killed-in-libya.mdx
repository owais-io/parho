Saif al‑Islam Gaddafi, the son of Libya’s former dictator, was shot dead in Zintan. He had become a popular political figure who many saw as a threat to the country's ruling elite. Libya remains split between the eastern Haftar faction and the western UN‑backed government, and Saif’s death raises doubts about the country's ability to hold free elections. His murder also closes a possible legal case at the International Criminal Court.
