Rachel Reeves, the UK Treasury boss, will deliver a low‑profile spring forecast next week, keeping things quiet to calm investors and voters after a run‑of‑the‑mill series of budget changes that drew criticism. She will stick to the Office for Budget Responsibility’s numbers, point to rising tax receipts, lower interest rates and energy‑bill cuts, and avoid new policy announcements. The move comes after a bad byelection and while her future at No. 10 hangs in the balance.
