Author swabs George, a Connemara pony, to test for tapeworm antibodies and checks his droppings for worm eggs. With rising worm resistance and environmental concerns, vets now only treat horses when needed. The test confirms whether recent treatment worked. Meanwhile, keeping pastures clean by removing dung breaks the parasite cycle but also harms beneficial dung beetles that need the dung for food and shelter. Balancing worm control and ecosystem health is the challenge.
