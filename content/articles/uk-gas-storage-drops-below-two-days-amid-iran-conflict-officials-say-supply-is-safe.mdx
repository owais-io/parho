UK gas storage fell to less than two days, but officials say other supplies keep the nation safe. The dip follows tanker routes shifting from Europe to Asia because of the Iran conflict, raising worries about gas prices. Still, Britain gets most of its gas from the North Sea and Norway, plus LNG and European links. Energy ministers say the system is flexible and plans are in place to protect supply.
