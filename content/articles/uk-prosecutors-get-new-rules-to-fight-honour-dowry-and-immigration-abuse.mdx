The Crown Prosecution Service (CPS) has issued new rules for lawyers to better spot and handle honour‑based abuse, including dowry and immigration‑related exploitation. It also covers cases where a spouse is taken abroad and left there, spiritual or ritual abuse, and banned practices like virginity testing. The guidance aims to help prosecutors build stronger cases, protect victims, and bring wrongdoers to justice. The government also plans to create a legal definition.
