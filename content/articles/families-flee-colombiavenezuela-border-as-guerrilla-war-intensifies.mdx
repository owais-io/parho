People from the Catatumbo area on the Colombia‑Venezuela border are leaving their homes because of a fight between the ELN and a break‑away group from the Farc. More than 2,000 refugees are now in the Colombian city of Cúcuta. Drones are used to shoot at civilians and the region is tied to drug production. A U.S. raid in Caracas may change the balance, but the fighting could get worse, endangering ordinary people.
