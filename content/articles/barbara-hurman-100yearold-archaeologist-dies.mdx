Barbara Hurman, a 100‑year‑old archaeologist and World War II veteran, passed away. She spent decades cataloguing artifacts for museums in Buckinghamshire, London, and even Bulgaria, and wrote books on pottery. After serving in the ATS and the Royal Corps of Signals, she taught herself archaeology, volunteering on digs and giving talks. Barbara also shared her wartime stories, speaking at Westminster Abbey and Buckingham Palace. She is survived by three children, three grandchildren and four great‑grandchildren.
